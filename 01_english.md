
# ENGLISH
## Higher Secondary School Syllabus (w.e.f. 2024–2025)
**Mizoram Board of School Education (MBSE)**

---

## Objectives

The general objectives at this stage are to enable the learners to:

- listen and comprehend live as well as recorded oral presentations on a variety of topics.
- develop greater confidence and proficiency in the use of language skills necessary for social and academic purposes.
- participate in group discussions and interviews by making short oral presentations on given topics.
- perceive the overall meaning and organisation of a text (correlation of vital portions).
- identify the central/main point and supporting details to build communicative competence.
- promote advanced language skills such as reasoning and drawing inferences.
- translate texts from mother tongue(s) into English and vice versa.
- engage in independent reflection and enquiry.
- read and comprehend extended texts in genres such as science fiction, drama, poetry, biography, autobiography, travel and sports literature.
- write text-based responses to prescribed and unseen texts.
- write expository and argumentative essays.
- write formal and informal letters and applications.
- infer meanings of unfamiliar vocabulary using contextual clues.
- select, compile and collate information for oral presentations.
- produce unified paragraphs with adequate details.
- use grammatical structures accurately and appropriately.
- write workplace-related documents such as minutes, memoranda, notices, summaries and reports.
- fill forms, prepare CVs, write e-mails, and make notes from reference materials.

# CLASS XI

## Section A: Reading Skills
**Reading unseen prose passages and note-making**

- Two unseen passages of total length **800–1000 words**
- Types of passages:
  - Factual passages (instructions, descriptions, reports)
  - Discursive passages (argumentative, persuasive or interpretative)

### Summary of Assessment
| Passage | Word Limit | Testing Areas |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | 500–600 | Short answer questions (local, global, inferential comprehension, MCQs, vocabulary) |
| 2 | 300–400 | Note-making in appropriate format and summary |

---

## Section B: Writing

1. One out of two writing tasks such as **advertisements or posters** based on verbal input (up to **50 words**).
2. One out of two compositions based on **visual and/or verbal input** (120 words):
   - Article for newspaper or school magazine
   - Report (descriptive or argumentative)
3. One out of two letters based on given input:
   - Business or official letters (enquiries, complaints, information, orders)
   - Letters to the editor
   - Job application

---

## Section C: Grammar

Different grammatical structures will be tested in meaningful contexts.
Item types include:
- Gap filling
- Sentence reordering
- Dialogue completion
- Sentence transformation

### Grammar Areas
- Tenses
- Clauses
- Reordering and transformation of sentences

---

## Section D: Literature Textbook and Supplementary Reader

Questions assess:
- Local and global comprehension
- Interpretation and analysis
- Evaluation and inference
- Appreciation and extrapolation beyond the text

### Course Book
1. One out of two extracts from poetry
2. Three short questions from poetry
3. Three questions from prose
4. Three out of four short answer questions from prose
5. One out of two long answer questions from prose

### Supplementary Reader
1. Three short answer questions
2. One out of two long answer questions

# CLASS XII

## Section A: Reading Skills

- Two unseen passages (800–1000 words)
- Passage types:
  - Factual
  - Discursive
  - Literary (fiction, drama, poetry, essay, biography)

### Summary of Assessment
| Passage | Word Limit | Testing Areas |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | 500–600 | Short answer questions (local, global, inferential comprehension, MCQs, vocabulary) |
| 2 | 300–400 | Note-making and summary |

---

## Section B: Writing Skills

1. Two short compositions (up to **50 words** each):
   - Notices
   - Formal and informal invitations and replies
2. One out of two letters based on verbal input:
   - Business or official letters
   - Letters to the editor
   - Job application
3. One out of two compositions based on visual and/or verbal input (120 words):
   - Article
   - Report

---

## Section C: Literature Textbook and Supplementary Reader

Questions assess comprehension, interpretation, inference, appreciation and critical thinking.

### Course Book
1. One out of two poetry extracts
2. Three short questions from poetry
3. Two MCQs from prose
4. Four short answer questions from prose
5. One out of two long answer questions from prose

### Supplementary Reader
1. Three short answer questions
2. Four MCQs
3. One out of two long answer questions
