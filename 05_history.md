
# HISTORY
## Higher Secondary School Syllabus (w.e.f. 2024–2025)
**Mizoram Board of School Education (MBSE)**

---

## Objectives

- Emphasize history as a critical discipline and a process of enquiry.
- Enable students to understand how historians construct history using evidence.
- Develop ability to relate developments across regions and time periods.
- Encourage analysis of social, political, cultural and economic processes.
- Build understanding of Ancient, Medieval and Modern Indian history through sources.
- Develop historical thinking and interpretation skills.

---

# CLASS XI
## Themes in World History

### Section A: Early Societies

#### 1. Early Cities  
**Focus:** Iraq, 3rd millennium BC  
- Growth of towns  
- Nature of early urban societies  
- Debate: Uses of writing  

---

### Section B: Empires

#### 2. An Empire Across Three Continents  
**Focus:** Roman Empire, 27 BC – AD 600  
- Political evolution  
- Economic expansion  
- Religion  
- Late antiquity  
- Debate: Institution of slavery  

#### 3. Nomadic Empires  
**Focus:** Mongols, 13th–14th century  
- Nature of nomadism  
- Formation of empires  
- Conquests and relations  
- Debate: Nomadic societies and state formation  

---

### Section C: Changing Traditions

#### 4. Three Orders  
**Focus:** Western Europe, 9th–16th century  
- Feudal society and economy  
- Formation of states  
- Church and society  
- Debate: Decline of feudalism  

#### 5. Changing Cultural Traditions  
**Focus:** Europe, 14th–17th century  
- New ideas in literature and arts  
- Relationship with earlier ideas  
- Contribution of West Asia  
- Debate: Validity of the term “European Renaissance”  

---

### Section D: Paths to Modernization

#### 6. Displacing Indigenous Peoples  
**Focus:** North America and Australia, 18th–20th century  
- European colonization  
- Formation of white settler societies  
- Displacement and repression of indigenous peoples  
- Debate: Impact of European settlement  

#### 7. Paths to Modernization  
**Focus:** East Asia, late 19th–20th century  
- Japan: Militarization and economic growth  
- China: Communist alternative  
- Debate: Meaning of modernization

# CLASS XII
## Themes in Indian History

### Section A: Archaeology & Ancient India

#### 1. The Story of the First Cities: Harappan Archaeology
- Overview of early urban centres  
- Discovery of Harappan civilization  
- Archaeological reports  
- Interpretation by historians  

#### 2. Political and Economic History: How Inscriptions Tell a Story
- Mauryan to Gupta period  
- Discovery and decipherment of inscriptions  
- Asokan inscriptions and Gupta land grants  
- Historical interpretation  

#### 3. Social Histories: Using the Mahabharata
- Caste, class, kinship and gender  
- Transmission of the Mahabharata  
- Use as a historical source  

#### 4. A History of Buddhism: Sanchi Stupa
- Religious traditions  
- Discovery of Sanchi  
- Sculpture as historical evidence  

---

### Section B: Medieval India

#### 5. Medieval Society Through Travellers’ Accounts
- Alberuni, Ibn Battuta, Bernier  
- Social and cultural life  
- Interpretation of travel accounts  

#### 6. Religious Histories: The Bhakti–Sufi Tradition
- Bhakti and Sufi movements  
- Preservation of compositions  
- Historical interpretations  

#### 7. New Architecture: Hampi
- Vijayanagar architecture  
- Political symbolism  
- Discovery and interpretation  

#### 8. Agrarian Relations: The Ain-i-Akbari
- Agrarian structure  
- Compilation of Ain-i-Akbari  
- Use by historians  

---

### Section C: Modern India

#### 9. Colonialism and Rural Society
- Zamindars, peasants and artisans  
- Revenue systems  
- Official records and reports  

#### 10. Representations of 1857
- Events of 1857–58  
- Visual and textual representations  
- British interpretations  

#### 11. Mahatma Gandhi Through Contemporary Eyes
- National movement (1918–48)  
- Gandhi’s leadership  
- Newspapers as sources  

#### 12. The Making of the Constitution
- Independence  
- Constituent Assembly debates  
- Interpretation of debates
