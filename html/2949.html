SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/33

Dated: 14th January, 2019.

 

Information received indicated that Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ Forum (MBDPF) leaders namely, A. Sawibunga, President; R. Laldawngliana, Vice President and Bruno Msha, General Secretary met Shri. Mahesh Kumar Singla, Adviser (Bru) yesterday, i.e. 13.1.2019 at Delhi in which they requested for extension of Central assistance like free ration, cash dole, etc. which is due to end on 15.1.2019. They also submitted a Memorandum to the Hon’ble Prime Minister of India, Hon’ble Union Home Minister, Union Home Secretary, MHA, and Joint Secretary (NE), MHA today, i.e. 14.1.2019, in which they highlighted 10 points quoted under :-

1.      “The official meetings and discussion has to be organized with the newly formed MNF Government of Mizoram on the issue of repatriation and rehabilitation of Brus to complete the pending tasks of the previous Government.

2.      The anti-Bru attitude of the civil societies of the majority people of Mizoram is yet to be removed and convince them to create peaceful atmosphere for enabling the Brus to return to Mizoram confidently.

3.      More than 5,500 (70%) of the head of the family is yet to be opened Bank Account before repatriation starts as per the quadripartite agreement.

4.      More than 30% of Bru whose names have already been enrolled but not yet generated till date is yet to be generated.

5.      Updating of Ration Cards has been conducted by the Government of Tripura based on the Identification Slips of Mizoram Government whereas previously left out 1,000 families are still remain untouched and unidentified. The new ration cards also not yet given to any household and the relief facilities are being distributed as per the figure of 2005 head counting.

6.      Identification of remaining displaced families is to be completed on or before 30.09.2018 by the Government of á¹€izoram whereas only 448 families has been identified at Kaskaopara relief camps out of 5,500 families are yet to be identified.

7.      Updating of Electoral Rolls for displaced Brus could not carried out by DCs of Mamit, Kolasib and Lunglei due to excessive objection and agitation against Sh. Bhupesh Choudhury, IAS (Mamit), Dr. Arun T, IAS (Kolasib) and Dr. Ankita Chakravarty, IAS (Lunglei) by civil societies under the banner of Joint NGO Coordination Committee of Mizoram.

8.      No initiative has been taken for reorganizing resettlement villages, spot identification of resettlement places by MBDPF leaders along with head of families and construction of houses by 2 male members prior to repatriation starts also ignored.

9.      As per the said agreement the identification of remaining more than 1,000 families are to be completed before the commencement of 8th batch repatriation starts.

10.    Families who are separated from parental houses are not yet identified and treated as a separated family by both the Government of Mizoram and Tripura.”

Highlighting the above 10 points which they claimed are yet to be fulfilled, MBDPF asked for extension of Central assistance like free ration, cash dole, etc. beyond 15.1.2019. They also claimed that MBDPF was not consulted about the physical repatriation of 42 families in September, 2018 by neither the Government of Tripura nor Mizoram. They also claimed that casting of votes in Special Polling Booths at Kanhmun in the State Assembly Election, 2018 created lots of hardships and troubles for Brus in Tripura Camps and requested the Union Government to direct the Election Commission of India (ECI) to set up Polling Booths inside Bru relief camps in the coming Parliamentary Election and to conduct the ongoing Special Summary Revision at the 7 (seven) Bru camps in Tripura.

Information received also indicated that they were also hopeful of meeting the Hon’ble Union Home Minister, Union Home Secretary, MHA and Joint Secretary (NE), MHA at the earliest opportune time.
