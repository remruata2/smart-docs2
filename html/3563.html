19.1.2022 :    Aizawl City College Joint Students' Union (ACCJSU) Meet was held on 19.1.2022 @10:00 a.m. at Cultural Centre, Vaivakawn, Aizawl. Pu Johny Vanlalmuana, Finance Secretary, ACCJSU presided the function and around 150 (one hundred fifty) students attended. Dr. R. 18.7.2022:     Youth for Environmental Justice Mizoram (YEJM) and Aizawl City College Joint Students' Union (ACCJSU) intends to organize Mass Procession for conservation of environment in Mizoram on 19.7.2022 @ 11:00 a.m. from Vanapa Hall to Sikulpuikawn, Aizawl.

19.7.2022 :    Youth for Environmental Justice Mizoram (YEJM) and Aizawl City College Joint Students' Union (ACCJSU) organized 'Environment Justice Rally' on 19.7.2022 @ 11:30 a.m. and the rally opening function was held in front of Vanapa Hall. Pu Moses Lalramhmangaihpuia, President, ACCJSU presided the function and Pi Vanramchhuangi, Convener, YEJM delivered a short speech, and around 2000 (two thousand) volunteers participated. After the short function, they proceeded towards the office of EF&CC office and NHIDCL office, where they shouted slogans and read out the below mentioned resolutions:

            (i)        Any developmental works which could hamper the ecology should only                        be executed after conducting Environment Impact Assessment (EIA) by                reliable Agency/Institution.

            (ii)       Mandatory provisions under relevant environmental laws should be                               followed before preparing any development project within Mizoram.

            (iii)     Various ecosystems viz. wetland, grasslands and forest should be                                  conserved in its origin.

            (iv)      Strongly oppose degradation of environment at the cost of development.

 

            Thereafter, they proceeded upto Sikulpuikawn and dispersed under peaceful atmosphere the same afternoon @ 1:15 p.m.

10.11.2022 :  Students' Union of Government Aizawl North College organized an indefinite Sit-in demonstration in front of their College at Ramhlun North, Aizawl w.e.f., on 10.11.2022 @9:30 a.m. in protest against the alleged failure of the State Government on their demand, i.e., allotment of land for their college. Around 800 (eight hundred) students participated in the Sit-in demonstration. In this connection, leaders of Students' Union, Govt. Aizawl North College stated that they had approached State Government authorities' viz., Chief Minister; UD&PA Minister and Higher & Technical Education Minister on several occasions for allotment of land for their college. However, no positive action has been taken till date despite the authorities allegedly giving assurance to take necessary action on their demand. They also stated that at present, there are 1560 students at the college, and since the college buidling is congested, the college started renting Ramhlun North Bazar Building under Trade & Commerce Department for class room w.e.f., 2015 to meet severe deficiency of class room and the contract period will expire this coming December, 2022. 

            In view of no positive response on their agitation, the students resorted to road blockade of Ramhlun to Bawngkawn road this afternoon @1:45 p.m. in demand of immediate response on their demand from the State Government. In this regard,
Pu Lalnunfela Chawngthu, Election Officer, Aizawl District; Pu Francis Lallawmkima, Addl. SP, Aizawl; Pu C. Lalmalsawma, SDPO, Aizawl North held a meeting with leaders of SU, Govt. Aizawl North College and the officials requested to call off their ongoing agitation as the file concerning their demand is under process. After the meeting, the SU leaders considered the request of the officials and the road blockade was called off this afternoon @ 3:35 p.m. for the time being. Information received stated that they intend to continue the Sit-in demonstration on 11.11.2022 @9:00 a.m. in front of their college.

11.11.2022 :  Students' Union of Government Aizawl North College continued their indefinite Sit-in demonstration in front of their College, Ramhlun North, Aizawl on 11.11.2022 @ 9:30 a.m. in protest against the alleged failure of the State Government on their demand, i.e., allotment of land for their college. As many as 500 (five hundred) students participated in the Sit-in demonstration.

            In this connection, a meeting was held under the chairmanship of
Pu R. Lalramnghaka, Secretary, Higher & Technical Education Department at his office chamber on 11.11.2022 @ 11:30 a.m. The meeting was attended by Dr. Lalhriatzuali Ralte, DC, Aizawl; Pu C. Lalruaia, SP, Aizawl; Pi Sangchhin Chinzah, Director, Higher & Technical Education; Pu H. Lianzela, Director, UD&PA, leaders of Aizawl North College SU led by PC Lalthansanga and concerned department officials. The meeting decided to conduct spot verification of the proposed land for Aizawl North College at Falkland near RAY (UD&PA) Building tentatively on 18.11.2022 by concern department officials and SU leaders of Aizawl North College. It also decided that the state government should consider allotment of other convenient land, in case the stated land is not suitable for their college land. It was highlighted in the meeting that the State Government intends to provide Ramhlun North Bazar Building under Trade & Commerce Department (presently on rent for classroom) as college extension building.

            Thereafter, leaders of Aizawl North College SU held a meeting at their college and the meeting decided to await necessary action to be taken by the Government on their demand. They also decided to take severe necessary action, if the State Government fails to take positive action on their demand. As such, the Sit-in demonstration was called off this afternoon @ 2:50 p.m.

18.11.2022:   Leaders of Students' Union of Government Aizawl North College and Government officials conducted spot verification of the proposed land for Aizawl North College at Falkland near RAY (UD&PA) Building on 15.11.2022, 3 days ahead of the proposed date for verification, i.e. 18.11.2022. During the spot verification it was reportedly observed that the proposed land was small for college campus. Therefore, it was proposed to set up Search Committee to search suitable land for Govt. Aizawl North College. The Search committee members will comprises of representatives from Student's Union and officials from concern department.

09.12.2022 :  A Meeting of Search Committee constituted by the State Government to identify land suitable for establishment of Govt. Aizawl North College was held on 9.12.2022 @10:30 a.m. at the Office Chamber of Secretary GAD, Chief Minister Office.
