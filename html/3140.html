SPECIAL REPORT

 

No. MSB/CB/SR/2021/819

Dated: 8th November, 2021

 

1.          Information received stated that on 6.11.2021 @10:25 a.m. a suspicious packet attached with wire (approx. 2 kgs) was found by Assam Police near the construction site of Bridge connecting Pu Chalchhunga’s farm land to
Pu Lalnuntluanga’s farm land across Saihapui river, Phainuam-Buarchep area. Information received from Shri. Shah Nawaz Khan, DIG CRPF stated that upon learning the above stated situation, Coy Commander, CRPF immediately rushed to the spot and saw the packet submerged in water and safety fuse about 3 (three) feet length was outside the water. Bloodstain was also seen at the spot. On learning the situation, Camp Commander, Mizoram Police deployed near the area was informed and enquired about the matter. However, no suspected person, who might be responsible for the stated incident was seen from Mizoram side and no person from Mizoram side has been allowed to visit the area since border tension erupted at the area.

             In connection with the above incident, bomb-squad team from Masimpur Police Camp, Forensic expert and around 20 (twenty) Assam Police Commando personnel visited the area the same day and they left the area the same evening after conducting necessary examinations.     

             It may be mentioned that the above incident may be orchestrated in an attempt to link with an explosion incident, which was occurred on 29.10.2021 @1:25 a.m. at one unoccupied Assam Forest Building, located around 100 meters southwest of Pu Ch. Chhunga’s paddy field, near Zophai, Bairabi.

  

  1.           Shri. Kuldeep Singh, DG, CRPF visited Vairengte on 6.11.2021 and held  an informal meeting with Assam and Mizoram Officials at CRPF Camp, near Auto Stand, Vairengte. The meeting was attended by DIG (SR), Assam; DIG, CRPF; SP, Kolasib and DC, Cachar. During the meeting, Assam officials pointed out that Mizoram Police border duty should be pulled back from their camp at Auto Stand, Vairengte and boundary should be maintained same as before COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. However, SP Kolasib reiterated that vacation or handing over of Mizoram Police Camp near Auto Stand (the bone of contention) to CRPF is not possible and he also mentioned that both parties should maintain peace and normalcy at the area so as to avoid escalation of border issues in future.

 

             Situation is under close watch.

 

DGP (M)
