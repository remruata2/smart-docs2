MIZORAM - TRIPURA BORDER INCIDENT

            Mizoram shares a 66-km border with Tripura. Phuldungsei village falls under the Mamit District, Mizoram and the same is claimed by Tripura as falling under the Kanchanpur sub-division in North Tripura district.

      The standoff between Tripura and Mizoram over Phuldungsei Village in the Zampui Hill range, started in August, 2020 when Tripura Administration identified that 130 (one hundred thirty) people living in Phuldungsei Village were registered both in Tripura and Mizoram, in electoral roll, ration card and other public welfare benefit schemes of both states. Later, Tripura claimed Phuldungsei and adjoining parts (Thaidawr Tlang) are parts of Tripura.

17.5.2013:     On 17.5.2013, Mizoram and Tripura BDO Level Peace Committee meeting was convened at Kanhmun PWD I.B and attended by 19 delegates from both Mizoram and Tripura side. The meeting was chaired by Pu Lalnundika, BDO, Zawlnuam.

            The meeting discussed about the following fours demands made by Langkaih Group YMA -

            i)         Bru representatives should apologise  to the people of Miozram for the crime committed inside Mizoram by  some  Bru miscreants recently.

            ii)        Those who participated in the protest rally held at Damchera  on 23.4.2013 should tender an word of apology for their wrong accusation/implication of Mizoram Police & I.R Bn.

            iii)       Bru should not continue to organise Band/Road Block in Damchera area.

            iv)       Tripura government should fulfil the demand of Muslim Association before expiry of 30th May 2013 by giving Rs 10 lakhs as a compensation to the family of Monaf Uddin.

9.10.2020:     Babujoy, Vice President, SONGRONGMA (Conglomerate of various Bru NGOs) held a Press Conference via YouTube through KOKTRIPURA NORTH-EAST INDIA channel on 9.10.2020, in which he stated their intention to construct Mandir (Sibraikhong Temple) at Thaidawr Tlang, near Phuldungsei Village, Mamit District. The place is believed to be within Mizoram side. He further mentioned that community work which was called to be organised on 15th and 16th October, 2020 for various Tripura Bru NGOs, Chakma NGO, Mog Tribe was postponed to 19th and 20th October, 2020 due to its clash with BJP Government programme.

            He further stated that all those who intend to participate in the said community work should first gather at Kanchanpur, North Tripura District and briefing on their course of action will be conducted.

            As per input, upon hearing the said news Mizo inhabitants of Zampui Hills, Tripura raised their opposition. Upon their enquiry, SDM, Kanchanpur informed Leaders of Mizo Convention that he is not aware of the said intended action nor gave any permission for their community work.

            Further, Mizo Convention intends to demonstrate their opposition, if SONGRONGMA continue to conduct the said community work. In this regard, they plan to convene a joint meeting with Tripura Group YMA in due course.

27.10.2020: On  27.10.2020 @ 3:00 p.m. Post Commander, New Kawnpui BOP, 5th IR Battalion and party, who intended to conduct spot verification at Thaidawr Tlang as per instruction of SP, Mamit were stopped by TSR personnel on their way to Thaidawr Tlang and they then returned. TSR personnel on duty told them that visit of Thaidawr Tlang is not allowed without prior permission from Tripura Government.

            On the same evening i.e., 27.10.2020 @ 7:00 p.m. Leaders of Phuldungsei Village, Mamit District organised public meeting at Community Hall, Phuldungsei.           Pu Sainghinglova Sailo, VCP, Phuldungsei presided the meeting and more than 140 (one hundred forty) members were present. During the meeting, they discussed Tripura Forest Minister and party visit of Thaidawr Tlang the previous day and their future course of actions. After long deliberation, the meeting concludes the following:

            (i)        To oppose any religious group intention for construction of Mandir/Temple at Thaidawr Tlang.

            (ii)       Not to circulate any unreliable news regarding Thaidawr Tlang through Media/WhatsApp etc.

            (iii)     Decided to wait both the State Governments’ action as Tripura Forest Minister during his short interaction with leaders of YMA at Vanghmun stated that it is better to constitute a joint verification team from both the State Governments and to set up a negotiation table.

            (iv)      To constitute a Peace Committee consisting of representatives from both Mizo and Bru communities and necessary follow up action in this regard will be taken up by Phuldungsei VC.

                        Input received indicated that student bodies like MZP and MSU may pressurise the Government to take proactive steps and to deploy a good number of Mizoram Police in the disputed area.

It further urged the Government to deploy Mizoram Police at the said area so as to indicate the area as falling within its territorial jurisdiction.

                       

12.2.2021 :  On 12.02.21 @ around 10:00 a.m. 3 (three)  Zawlnuam Vengpui JAC Duty viz., Lalfakzuala Ralte, K. Lalsangzuala and Hmangaihsanga who performed border duty at Saranathi kai, near Zawlpui Village, Mamit District were physically assaulted by around 30 (thirty) residents of Hamsapara, Tripura and took away 1 (one) Airgun and 2 Nos. of Dao from them. Due to the physical assault, they sustained minor injury and evacuated to Zawlnuam PHC and they all were released after giving First-aid. The confrontation took place, when JAC duty informed 6 (six) Bru who tried to enter Mizoram not to enter the state due to restriction being imposed on COVID-19 pandemic. After a short period, Brus from Hamsapara mobilised and physically assaulted JAC duty using bamboo stick. Upon learning the incident, around 100 (one hundred) residents of Zawlnuam gathered at Soranathi kai (Mizoram-Tripura border) and detained 11 (eleven) Brus who already entered Mizoram side earlier today.

            In connection with the above incident, Zawlnuam Vengpui JAC submitted FIR at Kanhmun Police Station on the afternoon of 12.2.2021 @ 2:00 p.m.  Mizoram Police seized the items belonging to Zawlnuam JAC took away by Bru miscreants after contacting Tripura Police today's evening @ 4:40 p.m. and subsequently released 11 (eleven) Bru detained by residents of Zawlnuam. The crowd also dispersed in the evening at 5:00 p.m.

           

15.2.2021:  According to information received, Tripura Forest Department has taken initiative for construction of Forest Check Post at Khantlang Junction (Mizoram side), Mizoram-Tripura border which is around 1 km towards east from New Kawnpui BOP on 11.2.2021. In this regard, VC Khantlang and Young Chakma Association (YCA) submitted a written petition to Mizoram Home Minister and Forest Minister on 13.2.2021 which stated their opposition for construction of Tripura Forest Check Post at Khantlang Junction as the area fell under Khantlang VC jurisdiction and requested to take necessary action.

            In connection with the above, SDPO (Civil), West Phaileng and SDPO, West Phaileng visited the area on 15.2.2021 and they held a short interaction with Tripura Forest RO and staff, who were available at the area. Officials of Mizoram informed Tripura Forest RO not to resume their initiative for construction of Forest Check Post at Khantlang Junction, Mizoram-Tripura border as the area fell under Mizoram jurisdiction. Information received indicated that officials of Tripura forest department do not have any objection and at the time, they still intended to construct Forest Check Post near the area.

 

24.2.2021 :  SDPO, Kanchanpur, North Tripura, Range Officer and officials of Tripura Forest Department, personnel of BSF and Tripura State Rifles came at Khantlang Junction (Mizoram side), Mizoram-Tripura border, which is located around 1 km towards east from New Kawnpui BOP on 23.2.2021 @ 12:00 noon with an intention to construct Tripura Forest Check-Post at the said area. Upon learning this, SI Liantluanga, Post Commander, New Kawnpui BOP rushed to the spot and informed officials of Tripura government not to take any action for construction of Forest Check Post at the area without the approval of Mamit DC. However, Tripura Forest Officials erected signboard bearing 'Forest Check Post Kawnpui, Kanchanpur Forest Sub-Division, Kanchanpur, North Tripura' at the area, and the signboard was subsequently removed by Post Commander, New Kawnpui BOP while in presence of officials of Tripura State Government. After a short period, Officials of Tripura government left the area and no untoward incident was reported.

 

5.8.2021:       Damcherra Bazar Committee, Tripura organized road blockade at Damcherra Bridge, Mizoram-Tripura connecting bridge (the main supply route to Mizoram due to blockade of roads in Assam) w.e.f., the afternoon of  Dt. 5.8.2021 @ 3:00 p.m. in demand for free movement of people of the 2 (two) States across the bridge. Earlier, Damcherra Bazar Committee, Tripura submitted a written petition to VCP, Kanhmun wherein they requested free inter-state movement across Damcherra Bridge as restriction of movement across the bridge caused severe hardship to their business. It requested for allowing people of both sides to buy and sell freely in both markets of the two sides. It also stated that only persons from Mizoram are allowed to freely enter into Tripura while restriction is imposed upon residents of Tripura for entering into Mizoram. Due to the stated blockade 31 (thirty one) MMV and 5 (five) Tankers who intend to proceed to Tripura are stranded at Kanhmun. After negotiation were conducted between Kanhmun VC/NGO/SDO(C) Kawrthah and Damcherra Bazar Committee/Panishagar SDM, vehicles stranded on both sides were released with an intention to conduct joint meeting of both the parties on the next day.

 

6.8.2021 : Pu Andy Lalfakzuala Varte, SDO (Civil), Kawrthah and Shri. Swapnajit Sarkar, BDO, Damcherra RD Block held a meeting at Damcherra RD Block Conference Hall on 6.8.2021 @ 11:00 a.m. in connection with blockade of Damcherra Bridge by Damcherra Bazar Committee. The meeting was attended by representatives of businessmen and VC of Kanhmun and Damcherra. The meeting discussed and decided the following:

  1. SDO(C), Kawrthah will discuss the matter with the higher authority of Mizoram government regarding entry of ordinary residents and small businessmen in Mizoram freely.
  2. Regarding any problem of the entry of the ordinary residents and small businessmen in Mizoram state they can contact with the administration of Mizoram government and as well as Tripura Government Administration for solving the problem.
  3. The SDO(C), Kawrthah will pursue with the higher authority for any possible relaxation regarding mandatory Medical Screening due to COVID-19 pandemic for any person entering into Mizoram. 
  4. The SDO(C), Kawrthah stated that regarding Mpass for entry into Mizoram only registration on the dedicated portal is required and no fee is needed for the said purpose.
  5. The merchant of Damcherra area agreed the request made by BDO, Damcherra RD Block to co-operate with the government and ensure free movement of goods through Damcherra and Kanhmun Bridge till solution is made from both sides.
