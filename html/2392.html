BORDER ISSUES WITH ASSAM
Three districts of Mizoram Aizawl, Kolasib and Mamit share 164.6 km border
with Assam's Cachar, Karimganj and Hailakandi districts. Residents of Mizoram
claimed that 1875 notification should be the boundary between Assam and Mizoram,
while Assam claimed 1933 notification as the constitutional boundary between the
two states.
I. KOLASIB DISTRICT:

1. Assam-Mizoram border dispute at Zophai (Kachurthal), Bairabi, Kolasib
District:
(i) On 27th February, 2018, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP), built a wooden rest house

somewhere in the Inner Line Permit Reserved Forest (509 Sq miles). The same
evening, a joint team of the Assam police and Assam Forest officials demolished the
structure.
(ii) On 8.3.2018 around 11:45 a.m., a minor scuffle broke out between the Student
union (MZP) and Assam Police where the Assam Police had resorted to Lathi-charge
as a result of which around 15 (fifteen) MZP members reportedly sustained minor
injuries.
(iii) On10.3.2018, a scuffle broke out again between Assam Police and members of
Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) at Zophai. Assam Police used force to disperse members of
MZP using Lathi, and later on resorted to firing as a result of which one MZP member
sustained gunshot injury on his right ankle and around 49 (forty nine) others sustained
injuries. It was also reported that 8 (eight) media persons also sustained injuries.
Several vehicles belonging to MZP members were also ransacked by Assam Police.
(iv) On 12.4.2018, Joint Verification Team, comprising of officials from Mizoram
and Assam started conducting spot verification of Inner Line Reserve Area along
Mizoram-Assam border.
The Council of Ministers meeting (Mizoram) held on 8.7.2019 upheld the
Cachar Inner Line, 1875 enacted as per Section 2 of Bengal Eastern Frontier
Regulations, 1873 (Regulation 1 of 1873) as the basis for settlement of border issues.
2. Mizoram-Assam border dispute at Vairengte, Kolasib District:
(i) On 6.4.2019, officials from Assam Forest Department started renovation work
of one building located at the disputed Mizoram-Assam Forest Reserve area along the
Aizawl-Silchar National Highway and reported that the building was occupied on the
same night.
(ii) On 17.10.2020 ,around 30 (thirty) residents of Lailapur, Assam pelted stones at
IR temporary duty Camp set up due to COVID-19 pandemic near Vairengte
Autorickshaw Stand at Mizoram-Assam border. There was also a scuffle between
residents of Vairengte and Lailapur, Assam where 7(seven) residents of Vairengte
sustained injury.
(iii) Residents of Lailapur organized economic blockade w.e.f. 16.10.2020 and no
vehicles were allowed to enter Mizoram. Residents of Assam also organized economic
blockade of all major road connecting Vairengte, Kanhmun, Bairabi and Phaisen and
rail road connecting Bairabi rail station.

(iv) Border Security Force (BSF) personnel were deployed along the border from
9.11.2020 as per direction of Central government.
(v) Blockade organized by residents of Lailapur at Lailapur, Assam was lifted on

the evening of 11.11.2020. The situation along the inter-state border remained tense
but under control and subsequently went normal.
3. Assam-Mizoram border dispute at Saihapui V (Khulichara/Mizo-Punji),
Kolasib District:
(i) On the evening of 16.10.2020 in between 6:00 p.m. and 7:00 p.m. 7 (seven)
Assam Police and Forest personnel visited COVID-19 duty post set up at the outskirt
of Saihapui 'V' Village, Kolasib District (around 300 meters from Saihapui V manned
by 1st IR personnel and they instructed the personnel on duty to vacate their duty post
within two hours claiming that the duty post was within Assam territory
(ii) On 17.10.2020 @ 12:00 p.m. around 200 (two hundred) civilians and 50 (fifty)
Assam Police personnel came again at 1st IR Duty Post located at the outskirt of
Saihapui 'V' and dismantled the Covid-19 duty Post.
(iii) A bomb blast occurred at SSA Lower Primary School on 22.10.2020 @ 10:15
p.m. The said school was run by Assam Government and is located at around 300
meters from Saihapui ‘V’ Village and around 400 meters from Mizoram Police Camp
at Assam-Mizoram border towards Mizoram.
(iv) 5(five) Assam Police and Forest Department officials performed patrolling at
Buarchep (Dolakhal) upto Upper Phainuam LP School (where bomb blast occurred on
22.10.2020) on 4.7.2021, and Assam officials stated that the purpose of their visit is to
conduct survey as Assam government intended to construct road upto the said school.

4. Mizoram-Assam border dispute at Aitlang (Balichhora), Vairengte, Kolasib
District:
(i) On 27.6.2021 @ 4:30 p.m., around 10 (ten) Assam Police and Forest officials

led by Mr. Nirmal Gosh, APS, Dy. SP Border, Hailakandi District and around 30
(thirty) civilians from Assam visited Vairengte PHE water source, located near Pu
Vantluanga’s fish pond, along Aitlang stream, which is around 2 km West of
Vairengte village, inside Mizoram. Assam officials stated that they visited the area in
pursuance of the complaint submitted by Assam Forest Department regarding alleged
encroachment of forest land.
(ii) Again, on 29.6.2021,around 120 (one hundred twenty) Assam Police led by
Mr. Nirmal Gosh, Dy. SP (Border), Hailakandi District visited the farm land owned
by Pu Ramhluna of Vairengte ChhimVeng, located at around 5 km North-West of
Vairengte, inside Mizoram.
(iii) On 1.7.2021, Mizoram Police erected tent at the edge of Pu Chhandama’s farm
land, towards Pu Lalramhluna’s farm land and closer to Assam Police makeshift camp
at Pu Chhandama’s farm land. On being learnt about Mizoram Police activity, Assam
Police, who heavily outnumbered Mizoram Police rushed to the spot and dismantled
the makeshift camp.

(iv) Assam started construction of tin-roof building for Assam Forest Department at
Pu Ramhluna’s farm land on 1.7.2021.
(v) On 3.7.2021 @ 10:00 a.m., a team of Assam Armed Police entered Aitlang

again and cause panic among the people residing there.
5. Mizoram-Assam border dispute at Phaisen, Kolasib District:
Residents of Dolchhora village, Assam blocked the road connecting Phaisen,
Mizoram to Dolchhora, Assam near Mizoram-Assam border at around 15 meters away
from inter-state boundary pillar at the Assam side by erecting Bamboo wall on the
road on 20.10.2020. The said bamboo wall was dismantled by residents of Dolchhora
and nearby villages on 3.11.2020.

1. Assam-Mizoram border dispute at Thinghlun (Luseicherra), Mamit
District:
Mr. Anbamuthan MP, IAS, DC, Karimganj accompanied by SP, Karimgnaj,
Forest Officials and around 60 (sixty) police personnel and civilians from Assam
destroyed plantations and also set fire to Jhum huts belonging to Mr. John Zolawma

In this regard, SDO(Civil), Kawrthah submitted FIR to Kanhmun PS and
Kanhmun PS C/No. 12/2020 Dt. 10.10.2020 u/s 506, 120 B, 434, 436, 447, 427 IPC
been registered.
In order to diffuse tension at the area BSF were deployed at the area as per
Central Government direction from 9.11.2020
2. Mizoram-Assam border dispute at Tuikhurzau (Detchhora khed),
Chuhvel, Mamit District:
1) Shri. Rohan Kumar Jha, DC, Hailakandi, Assam; Smt. Ramandeep Kaur, SP,
Hailakandi; Assam Forest Officials and around 70 (seventy) Assam Police personnel
and civilians from Gutguti village, Assam, visited Pu John Halliday’s Farm, located
around 1.7 Km from Chuhvel, Mamit District, inside Mizoram territory on 11.6.2021
They had a brief interaction with Pu John Holliday at his Farm House and informed
him that the area falls within Assam territory.
Dr. Himanta Biswa Sarma, Chief Minister of Assam in his DO letter to Chief
Minister of Mizoram vide DO No. CMO 1/2021/1890 Dt. 27th June, 2021 which
stated his desire to maintain status quo in the border areas and take every possible
steps for their early resolution of tension along the inter-state border. However, Assam
State authorities have been having activities contrary to the above statement of the
Chief Minister of Assam
