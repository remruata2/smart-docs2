DRUGS AND ILLEGAL TRADE :

 Mizoram shares an unfenced porous international border with Myanmar which is often taken advantage by drug smugglers and illegal traders. Due to the long stretch border and porosity in the absence of physical barrier combined with Free Movement Regime, people across the border can move freely cross the border at many locations resulting in an easy route for smugglers. The increase in the illegal trafficking of narcotic drugs in the recent time can be attributed due to the ethnic clash in Manipur which did not allows trafficking these contraband items through the state and increasing demand of fund by many ethnic armed groups of Myanmar who are fighting the State Administrative Council to overthrow the Myanmar Army from the current political administration.

BANGLADESH GOVERNMENT - KNF/KNA DIALOGUE :

 After a series of online meeting, "First round of Physical Peace Talk" between Kuki-Chin National Front/Army (KCNA) and Government of Bangladesh (GoB) represented by the Peace Committee was held on 5th November, 2023 at Hmunlai village, near Ruma bazar, Bandarbun District, Bangladesh. Although no definite agreements were made, the meeting, agreed to maintained cease-fire between both the parties until the next peace talk. The KNF has submitted a draft of possible and proposed peace agreement with 6(six) point demands to the Prime Minister of Bangladesh through Peace Establishment Committee.

 The second round of bilateral physical meeting between the two parties was conducted on 05.03.2024 at Hmunlai Village, near Ruma Bazar, Bandarban District, Bangladesh. In the meeting, representatives of KNF/KNA reiterated their six demands submitted in their previous meeting. However, as the points require more time, the meeting agreed to discuss and implement them in subsequent dialogues 

 One of the demands of KNF/KNA is the immediate release of 59 KNA members imprisoned in Bangladesh prisons apart from various schemes which will benefitted the Kuki Chin communities. As per the meeting, steps will be taken by Bangladesh Government as soon as possible for the release of imprisoned KNA/KNF members. A the same time, KNF agreed not to engage in any armed activities during the ongoing peace process and will maintain cooperative relationship with the security forces to maintain the stability of law and order situation.

 The next dialogue is likely to be held on 22nd April 2024.
