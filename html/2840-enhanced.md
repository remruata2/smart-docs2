****SPECIAL REPORT****

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/234

Dated: 3rd April, 2019.

 In continuation of our Special Report No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/229 dated 2.4.2019. Pu Lalnunmawia Chuaungo, IAS, Chief Secretary to the Government of Mizoram held a meeting with the leaders of NGOCC today, i.e. 3.4.2019 @ 12:00 noon at his office chamber in which he earnestly requested the NGOCC not to go ahead with their intended boycott of the upcoming Parliamentary Election (Lok Sabha) in the State informing them about the different actions that has been taken by the Government so far. The NGOCC leader reiterated their opposition of arrangement of Special Polling Stations in Kanhmun for Brus residing in Tripura Transit Camps and that they should cast their votes in the Constituency where they are enrolled individually. The NGOCC also demanded a written commitment from the ECI regarding the issue for them to reconsider their stand. The meeting was attended by top officials from Civil and Police Departments and 14 (fourteen) leaders of NGOCC.
 The Chief Secretary later convened a meeting with Mizoram Kohhran Hruaitute Committee (MKHC) @ 3:30 p.m at his office chamber in connection with the above issue which was also attended by Pu Balaji Srivastava, IPS, DGP, Mizoram, Pu Ashish Kundra, IAS, CEO, Mizoram and other top officials from the Government and 6 leaders from MKHC. MKHC leaders were expected to mediate between the two parties to find an amicable solution to the present impasse.

 Input received indicated that Central YMA issued a letter to all Sub-Hqrs. YMA, viz. Lunglei, Champhai, Serchhip, Kolasib and Mamit and informed them about the various resolutions adopted by the NGOCC in their previous meetings regarding their opposition of any special arrangement to set up Special Polling Booths in Mizoram for Bru voters living in Tripura Transit Camps and their demand of making Bru voters to cast their votes at their respective designated polling stations. In the letter, CYMA requested its Sub-Hqrs. to support their proposed State-wide Total Bandh w.e.f. 8.4.2019 to show their commitment for Mizoram state at their respective places.

 Information has been received that Sub-Hqrs. YMA, Kolasib held a meeting last night, i.e. 2.4.2019 @ 7:30 p.m at their office which was presided over by Pu C. Liankunga, President and attended by about 30 (thirty) members. The meeting deliberated on the proposed State-wide indefinite Total Bandh by NGOCC w.e.f. 8.4.2019 and resolved to request Central YMA to reconsider their decision to organise the Total Bandh. Information received further indicated that the letter might be sent today itself.

 Another information received indicated that All NGOCC, Lunglei held a meeting today, i.e. 3.4.2019 @ 2:00 p.m at SYMA Conference Hall, Lunglei. The meeting was presided over by Pu Lalrotluanga, Chairman and attended by 22 (twenty two) members including 4 (four) MSU leaders from Aizawl led by Samuel Zoramthanpuia, Gen. Secretary.

The meeting discussed about the NGOCC intention to organise State-wide indefinite Total Bandh w.e.f. 8.4.2019 and resolved to request the NGOCC to reconsider their decision to organise the Total Bandh. Information has been received that Pu Vanlalruata, CYMA President has been expected to visit Lunglei on 5.4.2019 (Friday) and hold a meeting with leaders of SYMA, Lunglei.

 Information indicated that the NGOCC representatives headed by Pu Peter Chhakchhuak, Asst. Secretary, CYMA will hold a meeting with Mara Thyutlia Py (MTP), Mara Students’ Organisation (MSO) and Mara Chano Py (MCHP) at MTP Office, Siaha tomorrow, i.e. 4.4.2019 @ 7:00 a.m.

 Central Young Lai Association (CYLA) is scheduled to hold a meeting tomorrow, i.e. 4.4.2019 @ 12:00 noon to discuss the proposed State-wide indefinite Total Bandh.

 The remaining Sub-Hqrs. YMA, i.e. Champhai and Mamit, are yet to make a decision on the issue. Champhai will deliberate during their ‘Rorel Inkhawm’ (Session) on Friday, i.e. 5.4.2019 @ 10:00 a.m to be held at Community Hall, Kahrawt. Mamit are expected to have a meeting on the issue as and when the Sub-Hqrs. President returns as he has been out of station.

 In the meantime, PAMRA Gen. Hqrs. issued a Press Release today and expressed their support of the NGOCC in their proposed indefinite Total Bandh and requested their members from different places to support the NGOCC at their respective places.
