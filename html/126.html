PROPOSAL FOR RESTRUCTURING, STRENGTHENING AND INTERNALCONNECTIVITY OF THE  STATE SPECIAL BRANCH

 

            MAN POWER

 

It is known fact that Mizoram is located in a strategically important place having 722 Kms of International Border (300 Km with Bangladesh in the West, 404 Kms with Myanmar in the east and South) which are infested by various types of insurgents outfit. It has often been alleged that gun runner, illicit drug traffickers and FICN carrier are taking advantage of the strategic location and the prevailing peaceful situation to carry on their illegal trade which could not be ruled out as illicit drugs, sophisticated arms and FICN have been seized on many occasions.

 

            Besides to keep a close vigil on the movement of the above noted undesirable elements the Special Branch has to monitor the activities of various political parties, NGOs, Student Organisation, Labour & Services on day-to-day basis. At present, there are around 100 different Labour & Services Organizations, 160 NGOs, 140 Student Organizations in the Capital alone. Present strength of Special Branch infact could not only cope up with the activities of these organization, but also most of the Head Constables posted in the Sector Office are very senior and old aged and are on the verge of their superannuation.

 

            In view of the reason stated above and the ever increasing activities of the undesirable elements and political parties, NGOs, etc. it is felt inevitable to increase manpower of State Special Branch

COMMUNICATION:

 

This unit is having 14 Out Posts mostly situated in the border areas. Up-gradation by means of communication between the Sector Officers of this Out Posts and the Headquarters is the urgent need of this Unit. Information and Intelligence, etc. are sent to the Hqrs. through W.T. Messages which are not found safe and secure. There is often delay in transmission of this messages which requires prompts supervision and direction from the Hqrs. It is, therefore, felt necessary to install telephone (Land Line) connection along with Fax Machines in CID (SB) Out Posts where these facilities are available. At present out of these 14 Out Posts, only Vairengte SB post is provided with telephone (Land Line) facility. It is proposed that telephone connection along with Fax Machines may be provided in the Out Posts, having strategic importance to facilitate quicker means of communicating vital intelligence and informations, etc. which will be more safe and secure than the normal means of communications through W.T. Messages.

 

            Hence it is proposed that Land Line Telephone Connection along with Fax Machine may be provided to this Unit for our Out Posts mentioned below:-

            Computers and its Accessories:

 

There are at present 5 Nos of Computers (one defective) in this Unit alongwith 5 Nos of Printers and 1 Duplicating Machine as under :-

 

Computer and Printers – 5 Nos. (1 defective, 2 Computers are used for preparation of Daily Summary of  Information, Special Report and other correspondences, 1 is used in the Account Branch for preparation of pay etc. and another 1 is used in the Confidential Branch for computerization of Confidential records.)

           

This Unit is also planning to computerise various Branches like the Reserve Branch, MT Branch and Foreigners Registration/Verification Branch for the smooth functioning of Office works. As such 3 more Computers with its accessories are required.    

This Office is maintaining confidential records (fact files, profiles) of militants/criminals and various organizations like political parties, Non Govt. Organization, Youths and Students Association, Labors & Services Association, Drugs/Counterfeit Currency Traffickers, Arms Smugglers etc. As such it is planning to computerize these records for the smooth functioning of this office and for easy reference in our daily activities for the safety and security of the State. For this purpose, the State Informatics Officer, National Informatics Centre (NIC), Mizoram, Aizawl was contacted and requested to develop the Software Package. Accordingly the Software was developed on the basis of Software Requirement Specification (SRS) for Record Management Information System (RMIS) and was submitted to this Office.  This is an important steps towards up gradation of technology skills and capabilities of the State Special Branch. However, in order to fully implement this programme, the present holding of Computer of this Unit are inadequate and outdated to meet the requirement. This office requires five (5) more Computers along with its accessories (Four for this Software and the other for Internet Connectivity) which will be used and kept in the Confidential Branch of this Office. The above said software package is ready for use and minimum of two computers are urgently required . This requirement along with a copy of  Software Requirement Specification, NIC-RMIS (Records Management Information System) for SP CID(SB), Mizoram, Aizawl prepared by NIC, Mizoram State Centre has already been submitted to PHQ.

            Photography and Maintenance of Records :

At least two Digital Cameras and Video Cameras/Camcorders are required for photography and recording Law & Order related incidents, meetings, criminals, etc. for the maintenance of Audio Visual Records on Major Law & Order incidents, harden criminals and insurgents, collecting intelligence pertaining to mass political events, processions, meeting, rioting and crime scenes, etc.        

 

            Enhancing Mobility/Conveyance :

 

Timely and prompt collection of intelligence requires quick and fast mobility. At present CID(SB) is having 9 Nos. of LMVs (Gypsy), 12 nos. of Motor Cycle and 1 Scooter, some of which are old and worn out and requires constant repair. This is inadequate to meet its basic requirement for operation duty. For timely and prompt collection of intelligent and for carrying out successful operation, good conditioned light vehicle are required  

 

The Unit is having 14 outposts mostly situated in the border areas. At present out of 14 out posts, only 7 out posts are provided with motor cycle. It may be noted that field officers and sector officers are the backbone of the State CID (SB) in collection of vital information. In the light of above, it is found that 13 (thirteen) motor cycles are genuinely required by this unit to facilitate quick movement of personnel in collection, collation and dissemination of information and intelligence.
