SPECIAL REPORT

 

No. MSB/CB/SR/2020/583

Dated: 19th October, 2020           

1.         A meeting regarding maintenance of peace and tranquility in Assam-Mizoram Inter-State Border was held today, i.e., 19.10.2020 @ Beat Office, Lailapur, Assam. The meeting was chaired by Shri. Sudeep Nath, Circle Officer & Executive Magistrate, Sonai Revenue Circle and it was attended by SP Cachar, SP Kolasib, SDO (C ) Vairengte, DFO Cachar and other concern Government Officials of the two States and Cachar Truck Drivers’ Association.

            The meeting held elaborate discussion on free Inter-State movement of vehicles and various apprehensions and concerns of Truck Drivers’ Association regarding inter-State movement were looked into. Both the Districts Administration and Police authorities of  Kolasib and Cachar assured that no person or vehicles will be subjected to any kind of harassment by any Civil/Police authorities or Civil Society inside Mizoram or Assam and peace and tranquility will be maintained in both sides of the border. Further, police escort will be provided to vehicles during their to and fro movement between Assam and Mizoram, if required.

            Cachar Truck Drivers’ Association pointed out that informed decision will be taken after holding discussions with its members and other stakeholders.

            Situation is under close watch.

2.         Chief Secretary, Govt. of Mizoram held a meeting with Leaders of Central Young Mizo Association, MZP General Headquarters and MSU General Headquarters today, i.e., 19.10.2020 @ 4:00 p.m. at CS Conference Hall, MINECO, Aizawl. The meeting was attended Pu Vanlalruata, President, CYMA; Prof. Lalnuntluanga, General Secretary, CYMA; Pu Peter Chhangte, Vice-President, MZP Gen. Headquarters; Pu J. Lalmuanzuala, Vice-President, MSU Gen. Headquarters and other 5 (five) Leaders and concern Government Officials. The meeting discussed about stoppage of movement of goods carrying vehicles  into Mizoram since 16.10.2020.

            In this regard, a Joint Press Release was issued by CYMA, MZP General Headquarters and MSU General Headquarters which considered stoppage of goods carrying vehicles into Mizoram as unfortunate and they assured drivers of stranded trucks at Lailapur, Cachar District, Assam that there is no reason to fear for their personal safety, vehicles or goods within the State of Mizoram and they are welcome to enter into Mizoram in the performance of their profession and activities.
