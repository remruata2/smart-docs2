          Soon after signing of agreement for permanent resettlement of Brus in Tripura, it started some tension and protest among some indigenous people in different part of the state. Zampui Mizo Convention (MC) submitted memorandum to different authorities, demanding total avoidance for resettlement Bru near Zampui hills and its surrounding areas in North District of Tripura highlighting it drawback and consequences. However, Zampui Mizo communities are in state of worry after the government of Tripura proposal for earmarking 5 (five) camps/sites for resettlement of Bru under Kanchanpur Sub-Division.

Protest and Obstruction of proposed Bru resettlement within Kanchanpur Sub-Division:

          Zampui Mizo Convention (MC) started opposing the proposal of 5 (five) camps for resettlement of Brus under Kanchanpur Sub-Division of North Tripura. It continued to demand avoidance of resettlement near Zampui Hills areas accusing it will pose threat to Zampui mizo and it traditional boundary. Mizo Convention (MC) is an organization working for the welfare and survival Zampui mizo in Tripura and protection of their land.The leaders of Zampui Mizo Convention (MC) are as follows:

          President              : Rev. David Ralte, Vanghmun

          Vice President      : Challiana, Sabual

          Gen. Secretary     : Dr. Zairemthiama, Behliangchhip

          Asst. Secretary     : Kamdingliana, Behliangchhip

          Treasurer             : Ngurdinliana, Behliangchhip

          Fin. Secretary      : Lalnunsanga, Vanghmun

 

          Nagarik Suraksha Manch (Bengali NGO) based at Kanchanpur also opposing the Tripura government proposal of resettlement of Brus by earmarking 5(five) camps under Kanchanpur Sub-Division. Zampui Mizo Convention after careful deliberation agreed to work with Nagarik Suraksha Manch in this issue with an expectation to have more impact to the government of Tripura.

          Nagarik Suraksha Manch is a non-political newly formed Bengali organization, working for the welfare of Bengali people having its headquarters at Kanchanpur. Since 1997 after the Bru fled to North Tripura District, under Kanchanpur Sub-division there were clashes between Bengali and Bru refugee in many occasions. On 10.12.2019 following the violence that had occurred during a series of anti-CAA protests, there was violence between Bengali, residence of Ananda Bazar and Brus refugees in nearby areas, several shops and houses were damaged in the violence. As many as 93(ninety three) Bengali families took shelter at Ananda Police Station. This clash sparks and it led to severe violence between Tribal and Bengali and Bengali organization named Nagarik Suraksha Manch was formed. Leaders of the organization are as follows:

Joint Protest and activities of Mizo Convention (MC) and Nagarik Suraksha Manch:

On 2.5.2020 Mizo Convention and Nagarik Suraksha Manch, Kanchanpur submitted joint memorandum to Chief Minister, Tripura throughSub-Divisional Magistrate(SDM), Kanchanpur demanding cancellation of the proposal for conduct of spot verification of 12 (twelve) proposed sites from 4.5.2020, and stating non-compliance of their demand will lead to conduct of indefinite strike after lock down. The Sub-Divisional Magistrate(SDM), Kanchanpur assured them in writing for cancellation of proposed spot verification which is to be conducted w.e.f 4.5.2020 and no new action will be taken during lockdown.

On 24.05.2020 Mizo Convention(MC) and Nagarik Suraksha Manch members stopped verification team at Satnala who will be planning to visit Manu Chailengta RF(Reserve Forest) under Kanchanpur Sub-Division to conduct spot verification as per the instruction of SDM, Kanchanpur and the team was not able to conduct verification on that day.

On the same day a joint press conference of Mizo Convention (MC) and Nagarik Suraksha Manch was held highlighting the reason of conducting picketing and constitution of Joint Movement, and also stated if the government is not paying any heed to their demand, an indefinite strike will be conducted within Kanchanpur Sub-Division. The two organizations also decided to move PIL to High Court in this regard.

 

After much opposition by Mizo Convention(MC) and Nagararik Suraksha Manch, the SDM, Kanchanpur assured them in writing to these two groups without the government order all the proposed sites except CCRF(Central Catchment Reserve Forest) (this place is the eastern side of Phuldungsei and Sabual) spot verification will not be conducted within the specified time.

 

On 25.05.2020 under the aegis of Mizo Convention (MC), youths from different villages of Zampui Hill were deployed inside the forest so as to stop any kind of action for conducting spot verification after the Mizo Convention felt doubtful for the action taken by the state government.

 

Activities and sentiments of BRU refugees in the aftermath of Agreement:

 

Regarding the final settlement for people from Bru Refugee Camp who are not repatriated to Mizoram in the final phase of repatriation, the Tripura Government and representative of different Bru NGOs are conducting spot verification at the proposed site. It is felt that some are not willing to settle at the proposed resettlement site and sought for ways to repatriate back to Mizoram

 

BRU NGO:-

 

1. MBDPF       : Mizoram Bru Displaced People Forum.

2. MBDPC      : Mizoram Bru Displaced People Coordination Committee.

3. MBIDM      : Mizoram Bru Indegenous Democratic Movement.

4. BTDS                   : Bru Tribal Development Society.

5. BDWO        : Bru Displaced Welfare Organisation.

6. BWOI                   : Bru Welfare Organization of India

 

The above Bru NGO leaders are willing for permanent resettlement at Tripura but some of the people are unwilling and accused their leaders only concerned of their own benefits and not taking any actions for the welfare of the people.

Some of the Bru refugees who are eagerly looking to repatriate back to Mizoram are planning to meet Government of Mizoram officials especially Chief Minister, Home Minister leh Chief Secretary. But due to Covid-19 pandemic they are not able proceed.

Mizoram Bru Displaced People Repatriation Committee (MBDPRC) was constituted by Bru people willing to repatriate back to Mizoam and they are preparing a letter to be submitted to Chief Minister, Mizoram and Home Mimister but not able to complete due to Convid-19 pandemic. The following are leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced People Repatriation Committee (MBDPRC):

As per report of leaders of MBDPRC there are around 100 families consisting of 500-600 members from different refugee camps willing to repatriate to Mizoram. They stated that they were afraid of their leaders and it was the reason for not repatriate back to Mizoram during the final phase of repatriation.

Leaders of MBDPRC are still planning to meet official of Government of Mizoram after the lockdown.

 

 

 

 

Picketing of SDM Kanchanpur and her team by Mizo Convention(MC) at Phuldungsei, Zampui, Tripura:

 

On 29.5.2020 around 150 Mizo Convention (MC) members gathered at Phuldungsei to stop the spot verifation team as on that day as per arrangement of Sub-Divisional Magistrate, Kanchanpur spot verification for resettlement of Brus is to be conducted at Central Catchmen Reserve Forest (CCRF).  Around 100 youths performed duty at the eastern side of Phuldungsei, within Zampui Boundary to stop the spot verification team. The same day evening Mizo Convention leaders stopped the team while they were trying to move back to Kanchanpur from Khantlang side and informed again their opposition for the proposal to resettlement Bru refugees near Zampui Traditional Boundary and also the consequences it could have tothe Mizos.  They also condemned the formation of spot verification team, which included Bru NGO leaders without the knowledge of Mizo Convention (MC) andremindhim that they will oppose this action till the end.

 

SDM, Kanchanpur was given passage after he assured them that representatives of Mizo Convention (MC) and Nagarik Suraksha Manch will be invited in the meeting to discuss Bru Resettlement issue under the Chairmanchip of District Magistrate & Collector, North Tripura to be convened on dt.03.06.2020 @3:00 PM

 

On 29.5.2020 Pu Zoramthanga, Hon’ble CM, Mizoram wrote a letter to Shri Biplab Kumar Deb, Hon’ble CM of Tripura requesting for reconsideration and cancellation of the proposal for resettlement of displacedBrus in Zampui Hills and its surrounding areas under Kanchanpur Sub-Division in North Tripura
