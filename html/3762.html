BRIEF NOTE ON THE ISSUES OF BRU REFUGEES

 

HISTORY

The Brus also called Reangs are the Nomadic Tribe living in the western belt of Mizoram, bordering Bangladesh and Tripura State. The Brus started entering into Mizoram around 1940's and engaged themselves in jhum cultivation. The Brus were living in 14 villages in Kolasib District, 11 villages in Lunglei District and 46 villages in Mamit District and spread over 9 Assembly Constituencies (i.e. Hachhek, Dampa, Mamit, Tuirial, Kolasib, Serlui, Lunglei South, Thorang and West Tuipui)

            Political awareness developed amongst the Brus since 1990 with the formation of political party called Reang Democratic Party in June, 1990. Bru Student Association and Bru Welfare Committee were also formed to promote the welfare of Brus in Mizoram. In 1994 the RDP, BSA and BWC at a joint conference formed a new political party called the Bru National Union (BNU) by merging the three organisations. The BNU conference held in September, 1997 at Saipuilui village in Mamit District adopted a resolution for creation of Autonomous District Council for Brus in the western part of Mizoram which was strongly objected by the Mizo community in the concerned area. A militant outfit called the Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF) formed in 1996 under the leadership of Vanlalliana, Tuirum village in Mamit district became active and on 21.10.1997 Lalzawmliana, Forest Game Watcher in Dampa Forest Sanctuary was killed and two of his friends were also abducted by the BNLF. The news of the killing spread like wild fire throughout Mizoram and ignited the already tension in the area due to the demand of an Autonomous District Council by BNU. Tension between the two communities escalated and communal violence broke out and being lesser in numbers, the Bru people started fleeing Mizoram towards Tripura.

The Bru people who fled Mizoram in the wake of the incident were given refugees status in Tripura. They were given shelter in six refugee camps and were also given free rations and other essential commodities at the rate fixed by the Union Government. The Mizoram Bru Displaced People’s Forum (MBDPF), headquartered at the Naisingpara camp on the eastern side of the Jampui Hills is an apex body for Brus living in 6 (six) refugees camps in Tripura. The Forum was created in 2002.

After a number of meetings between the Centre and the State authorities were held, date for repatriation of the Bru refugees was fixed i.e. 16th November, 2009. While all preparations for repatriation process were going on smoothly a Bru militant group who called themselves the Bru Revolutionary Union (BRU) killed a Mizo youth Zarzokima (17), on 13th November, 2009. It was later confirmed that killing was done deliberately by the BRU in order to deter the repatriation process.

Even though the killers of the innocent Mizo youth left a note with a letter head 'Bru Revolutionary Union', subsequent investigation and statement given by persons involved in this murder case revealed that there is no such organisation as 'BRU' but a group of Bru miscreants who had been instigated by some section of MBDPF leaders to derail the repatriation process who did such heinous crime. Nevertheless, there are two known armed groups who operated in the Mizoram-Tripura border and Mizoram-Assam border who had secret understanding with some leaders of MBDPF who could be utilised when the need arise. The existence of these armed groups was revealed based on the revelation made by one of their leaders arrested by Mizoram Police named Henry recently. Reliable information received stated that this very group are the one who gave warning to refugees not to obey the Govt's plan of repatriation and who instilled fears amongst the Bru Refugees.

CHRONOLOGY OF EVENTS (BRIEFLY)

  • 1940 (approx.) Brus started living in Mizoram.
  • 1990: Formation of Reang Democratic Party (June), Bru Student Association (BSA) and Bru Welfare Committee (BWC).
  • 1994 : Merging of RDP, BSA and BWC into a new political party called the Bru National Union(BNU)
  • 1996: Formation of Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF) at Tuirum village in Mamit district.
  • 1997, September: BNU conference at Saipuilui village in Mamit District.
  • 1997, October 21: Killing of Lalzawmliana, Forest Game Watcher in Dampa Forest Sanctuary and two of his friends were also abducted by the BNLF.
  • 1997, November onwards: Brus Refugees started fleeing Mizoram in the wake of the killing incident and fear of retaliation by the Mizo Community.
  • 2000 June, 30: Ambush of Mizoram Armed Police vehicle by BNLF in which 7 (seven) policemen were killed.
  • 2015 January, 16 : Supreme Court of India directed MHA, Govt. of India and Govt. of Mizoram to prepare time bound plan of action for resettlement and rehabilitation of Bru migrants.
  • 2015 February 3: Home Department, Government of Mizoram prepared Road Map – IV which was submitted to Ministry of Home Affairs.
  • 2015 June, 2: Fruitless repatriation exercise as no Brus turned up.
  • 2016 May,15: Submission of Revised Action Plan by Govt. of Mizoram and Identification of genuine Bru refugees in the 6 Refugees camps was carried out.
  • 2017 March, 28 : Supreme Court of India defined the status-quo of the Bru refugees in Tripura for the Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India and Govt. of Mizoram in the on-going repatriation process.
  • 2018 March: Tentative date for commencement of physical repatriation.

Latest Demands raised by Joint NGO, Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ in their memorandum submitted to the Hon’ble Union Home Minister, Government of India on 15.12. 2017 are as follows:

  1. Immediate release of cash dole of Rs.5/- per day per adult and Rs.2.5/- per day per minor, withheld since July,2017 notwithstanding any requirement as to opening of bank accounts, and continue to pay the same until further orders.
  2. Provide regular ration i.e. 600 gms per head per day for adult member and 300 gms rice per head per day for minor member at the 7 relief camps in Tripura without any delay whatsoever to avoid starvation.
  3. Enhance the assistance to Brus at relief camps as shown by the Union of India in the Affidavit dated 25.2.2015 in ‘Akhil Bhartiya Vanvasi Kalyan ashram V. State of Mizoram & Ors. WP(C) 1/2015, as under:
  1. double the cash dole to Rs.10/- from of Rs.5/- per adult per day, and Rs.5/- from Rs.2.5/- per day per minor.
  2. distribute clothes twice as instead of once.
  3. double of cash for clothes given on Durga Puja to Rs.1264/- from Rs.632.22/- per adult per year, and to Rs.930/- from Rs.465.85/- per minor per year.
  4. provide blanket/quilt,etc. once every 6 months instead of once every 3 years.
  5. provide bathing soap once every 3 months per family in place of once every year per family.
  6. construct dwelling houses for each family at 7 Relief Camps in Tripura.
  7. provide Drinking water, educational facility, health care, etc. at 7 Relief Camps in Tripura.
  1. Immediately provide all the above assistance, including cash dole, to the Brus who had migrated to Tripura post 2009 violence in Mizoram.
  2. Allow the MBDPF leaders to participate in all the meeting conducted by Union of India, State of Tripura and State of Mizoram on the matter of repatriation of Brus from Tripura to Mizoram and rehabilitation in Mizoram after repatriation.

The Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India has in principle approved the following rehabilitation package for the Bru Refugees:

  1. Rupees 1,30,000/- cash dole for each family
  2. Rupees 5,000 monetary aid per month for two year for each family
  3. Free ration for two years
  4. Free vehicular trip up-to final rehabilitation destination.
