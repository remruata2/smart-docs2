(i)  Security Scenario in Barak Valley (Assam) and Mizoram with special reference to insurgency :

HPC(D) : When a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) was signed between Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) way back in 2018, Lalhmingthanga Sanate designated his HPC(D) splinter faction as 'HPC(D) (President's Group)' and did not participated in the said MoS. During that time, his group consisted of around 13 cadres with an estimated 4/5 weapons holding. However, by February 2020, 11(eleven) of his cadres deserted him and joined HPC(D)- General Headquarters Sinlung Group, who are under the leadership of Lalkhama @ LK Hmar.

 It is believed that there are only a cadre or two under the command of Lalhmingthanga Sanate, who himself has a great inclination towards surrendering himself to Government of Mizoram.

 The other Group under Lalkhama, who designated themselves as 'HPC(D) General Headquarters Sinlung' have an estimated 20-30 and are under the command of their self-style Commander-in-chief, Lalkhama @ LK Hmar, whose family are presently residing in Saikot, Churachandpur, Manipur. Their area of operation is in and around Churachanpur town of Manipur.

 After the signing of Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) between Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) in 2018,there has been no report of insurgency related violence inside Mizoram which includes the areas bordering Barak valley (Assam).

(iv)  Human trafficking and movement of Rohingyas :

  As per the instruction of Govt. of India, four Anti-human Trafficking Units (AHTU) has been established in Aizawl District, Lunglei District, Champhai District and Kolasib District under Mizoram Police Department.

 Apart from the eight female Rohingyas who were arrested by Vairengte police at Vairengte on 23.4.2019 , there has been no report of arrest/apprehension of Rohingyas inside Mizoram. These 8(eight) illigal migrants are presently accommodated at Temporary Foreigners Detention Centre, Vairengte, Kolasib District where they are being provided with basic facilities like electricity, food, water, medical examination etc. Security is also being provided to them round the clock by women police officer and other police personnel from Vairengte police station.
