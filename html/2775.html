MYANMAR REFUGEES -

            Mizoram shares 496.531 kms. of porous International border with Myanmar as per the record of Directorate of Land Revenue & Settlement, Government of Mizoram. The Indo-Myanmar border posts are currently manned by personnel of the Assam Rifles who are thinly stretched along the whole length of the border.

            Military takeover took place in Myanmar on 1st February 2021 when democratically elected members of Myanmar's ruling party, the National League for Democracy, were deposed by Myanmar's Military.

            The escalating resistance put up against the military regime by Civil Disobedience Movement (CDM) definitely had an impact and permeate the Indo Myanmar boundary, which resulted in influx of a considerable number of refugees arriving and taking shelter in Mizoram and Manipur. 

            Many Myanmar refugees presently took shelter in various districts of Mizoram following military coup in Myanmar. These refugees are accommodated  in their relatives' house and makeshift camps set up by NGOs. Presently, the numbers of Myanmar refugees in Mizoram  are estimated to be around 15,000.

 

Mizoram-Assam border dispute -

Mizoram has a boundary of 123 kms with Assam, 66 Kms with Tripura and 95 Kms. with Manipur. Security of the inter-state border is entrusted mainly with Mizoram Police with sister agencies namely, Assam Rifles, Border Security Force and Central Reserve Police Force.

The three South Assam districts of Cachar, Hailakandi and Karimganj share border with Mizoram’s Kolasib, Mamit and Aizawl districts. Mizoram was part of Assam till 1972, when it became a separate Union Territory. In 1987, it became a full-fledged state. While the two states have sparred over the border in the past, the stand-offs have been relatively non-violent and low-key compared to Assam’s dispute with its other neighbours – Nagaland, Meghalaya and Arunachal Pradesh – on the same matter.

 

Major Incidents -

26.7.2021:    

  

            On 26.7.2021, Assam Police tried to forcefully occupy Mizo IR Bn. Camp located at Vairengte, which is the inter-state border. On the same evening, exchange of fire took place at the inter-state border as Assam Police first open fire towards Mizoram Police and civilians. As per unconfirmed input, there were reported casualties among Assam Police Personnel due to the firing. 

                 

28.7.2021 :    A meeting was held with Chief Secretaries/DsGP of Assam and Mizoram under the Chairmanship of Union Home Secretary on 28.07.2021 at North Block, New Delhi. Following decisions were taken :

            1.         Neutral force i.e. CAPF  to be deployed in the disturbed Assam and                          Mizoram border area long NH 306 under a senior officer of CAPF.

            2.         Further, in order to facilitate the functioning of neutral force, both the                     Government of Assam and Government of Mizoram will work out                            arrangement in coordination with Ministry of Home Affairs,                                          Government of India within a reasonable timeframe.

            3.         Both the State Governments should continue discussions mutually to                      resolve the border issue in an amicable manner.

 

5.8.2021 :      (a) Pu Lalchamliana, Home Minister; Pu Lalruatkima, I&PR Minister and Pu Vanlalngaihsaka, Home Secretary held a meeting with delegates of Assam comprising of Shri. Atul Bora, Minister Border Protection & Development; Shri. Ashok Singhal, Minister Urban Development and Shri. GD Tripathy, Commissioner & Secretary, Border Protection & Development on 5.8.2021 at Aijal Club, Aizawl in connection with issues pertaining to tension, law & order at the  inter-  stateborder.
The meeting made the following joint statements:

            (i)        The Government of Assam and Mizoram welcome and agree to take forward the initiatives taken by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India and Hon'ble Chief Minister of Assam and Mizoram to remove tensions prevailing around the inter-state borders and to find lasting solutions to the disputes through discussions.

            (ii)       The representatives of Government of Mizoram convey condolences for the loss of lives on 26th July, 2021 and convey best wishes for speedy recovery of those injured.

            (iii)     Both the State Governments agree to maintain peace in the inter-State border areas and welcomed deployment of neutral force by Government of India in this regard. For this purpose, both the States shall not send their respective Forest and Police forces for patrolling, domination, enforcement or for fresh deployment to any of the areas where confrontation and conflict has taken place between the Police forces of the two States during recent times. This would include all such area along the Assam - Mizoram border in the districts of Karimganj, Hailakandi and Cachar, Assam, Mamit and Kolasib districts, Mizoram.

            (iv)      Representatives of Governments of Assam and Mizoram agree to take all necessary measures to promote, preserve and maintain peace and harmony amongst the people living in Assam and Mizoram, particularly in the border areas.
