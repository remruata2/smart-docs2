****SOME ISSUES RELATING TO VOTING BY BRUS IN TRIPURA CAMPS****

** **(1)** On 24.3.2014, all NGOs (i.e CYMA, MUP, MHIP, MZP &amp; MSU) staged a Protest Rally within Aizawl city in protest against Bru voting inside the tripura transit camps during the ensuing Lok Sabha Election 2014. Around 1600 participants/protesters participated and peacefully marched from Millenium Centre to the premises of the office of Chief Electoral Officer (CEO), Khatla. During the rally, the following slogans were chanted during the rally and procession:-**

 (i) We don't want voting of Tuikuk (Bru) voters from outside the state of  Mizoram.

 (ii) Bru Tripura Transit Camps should be removed.

 (iii) We don't want/strongly opposed casting of votes by Bru displaced  voters (who refused to repatriate back Mizoram).

 (iv) Will continue to fight against the decision of ECI on arrangement of  voting by Bru from outside the state of Mizoram.

 The Protest March peacefully ended at 1:10 PM after the protestors burnt a copy of Bru Electoral Roll as a mark of protest against voting by Bru refugees from Mizoram living in Tripura as voters of Mizoram. The following Joint Resolution was also adopted on the same day:-

 (i) Strongly objected/opposed to the decision of the Election Commission of India (ECI) for voting by those Bru refugees (who refused to repatriate despite repeated appeals) living in Tripura Transit Camps as voters of Mizoram in the ensuing Lok Sabha (MP) election. Urged the ECI to review its decision and also threatened to intensify its protest against it.

 (ii) Demand for permanent settlement of adamant Bru refugees (who refused to repatriate despite repeated appeal) in Tripura and their respective Electoral Roll should also be transferred to Tripura.

 (iii) Asked/Requested all the political parties of Mizoram not to conduct poll campaign within Tripura Transit camps.

**(2)** Joint NGOs (CYMA, MZP, MSU, MUP, MHIP) meeting was held today, i.e. 26.3.2014 @ 1:00 PM at CYMA Hall, Aizawl. The meeting was chaired by Pu Lalbiakzuala, President, CYMA and was attended by 15 representatives from the said NGOs. The meeting had deliberated upon the decision of The Election Commission of India to enable Bru refugees to exercise their franchise within Tripura. The meeting resolved the following points :-

 (i) Aizawl Deputy Commissioner be urged not to deploy election officials from Mizoram to conduct the forthcoming election for Bru refugees in Tripura.

 (ii) Bru refugees within Tripura be coerced and intimated not to exercise their franchise within Tripura.

 (iii) Mizoram Kohhran Hruaitute Committee (MKHC) be urged and seek their support and intervention on the Bru issue (to object the ECI’s decision on Bru voting).

****(4)**  Pu Ashwani Kumar, Chief Electoral Officer (CEO), Mizoram held a meeting with representatives of Joint NGOs, i.e. CYMA, MUP, MZP, MHIP &amp; MSU at his office chamber today, i.e.31.3.2014 over the issue of Bru voting in Tripura Relief Camps. Informing them of the instruction of ECI to facilitate postal ballot for Mizoram's Bru refugees lodged in six camps in North Tripura, the CEO further appealed all the leaders of Joint NGOs to cooperate and contribute for peaceful conduct of poll in Mizoram. The CEO also mentioned that on 28.3.2014, a team of election officials from Mizoram with postal ballot papers reached Tripura by helicopter.**

 It is also reported that the representatives of Joint NGOs expressed their deep resentment over the ECI's decision and threatened to launch strong protest/agitation if the Bru postal ballot votes are counted inside the State of Mizoram.

**(5)**  The Election Commission of India (ECI) will conduct Lok Sabha election through postal ballots in Tripura Bru Relief Camps from 1.4.2014 to 3.4.2014 despite opposition by five NGOs in Mizoram. It is reported that the Election Commission had appointed seven observers to oversee the voting in seven Bru relief camps in Tripura. Four Nodal Officers and one ARO from Mizoram reached Tripura Relief Camp on 28.3.2014.
