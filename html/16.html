BRIEF OF CASE:

 

The enquiry resulted with the report that Shri Diwakal Tiwari received an e-mail from Bavister Picolo Colvis, reportedly senior advocate of Nigeria, Paul & Co, Legal Solicitors & Advocates Head Office No. 34 Parliament Street, Western Avenue, Nigeria, YOI B&G, NGRC 47034 asking him to deposit Rs. 49 thousand (Equivalent of GBP 600) as fees for preparing affidavit in connection with a foreign lottery which he has won. Checking revealed that the account has several intercity cash deposits and withdrawals through ATM which led to the allegation that the account of Ms. Vanlalhriati has been used as money mule account.

 

STEP TAKEN:

 

The account holder Ms. Vanlalhriati was examined. The account has been kept frozen and the bank account statement has also been taken which is submitted with this report.

 

ASSESSMENT OF THE CASE:

 

                        From the statement of Ms. Vanlalhriati and subsequent enquiry the following are ascertained.

 

                        That Ms. Vanlalhriati opened a saving account at SBI Main Branch, Solomons Cave, Aizawl. Account No. 30339919288 on 3.3.2008 which she brought to Mumbai where she found worked at a health spa. Then she was befriended through her friend by one Nigerian called Kido who, according to her, later became her boyfriend. She gave her account and ATM Card to Kido on his request which she said was to help him in his business, exact business she did not know, for easy transfer of money as he, as a, foreigner could not open an account. There was Rs. 500/- in the account at time when she handed over her account to him. On May, 2008 Kido then operated the account till end of August, 2009. She then took back her account when she heard that the account had been used by Kido on other suspicious dealings.
