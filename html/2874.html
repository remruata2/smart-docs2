A. Front page of VANGLAINI/NEWSLINK newspaper.Dt.05.12.2012

 

A woman found dead: On the evening of 3rd December 2012, Lalduhzuali(43) w/o Zoramliana of Armed Veng was found lying dead on the roadside near 1st Bn. MAP playground. Eyewitness believed that she was hit by a motor vehicle. Police have questioned two persons in connection with this death. However, no specific information has been received yet.

    

 

 

B. Front page of VANGLAINI/MIZO AW newspaper. Dt. 05.12.2012

 

Discussion on public safety during Christmas season : Under the chairmanship of mr.Niharika Rai, D.C Kolasib, Kolasib Police,I&PR,Excise & Narcotics and leaders of YMA Sub Hqrs. discussed about ways to have a peaceful christmas season,yesterday at Kolasib D.C. office. The meeting decided to open a temporary beat post at Thingdawl to monitor the highway besides intensifying the duties of the Police, Excise & Narcotics and YMA volunteers.

 

 

 

C. Front page of NEWSLINK newspaper. Dt. 05.12.2012

 

Beware of pickpockets:With Christmas festival approaching, Aizawl New Market Authority has warned shoppers to be on guard against pickpockets who are sure to increase during the rush hour for Christmas shopping, according to market officials, majority of pickpockets arrested by the market beat police were smartly dressed married women and nicely dressed girls. The Aizawl police will deploy more policemen including plain clothes to curb the increasing menace of pickpocketing and other crimes.

Luangmual Police Out Post which was located in Luangmual area was shifted to Zonuam area and will be called Zonuam Police Out Post from now.

 

 

 

E. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper. Dt. 05.12.2012

 

Vehicular accident within the city: According to the records of Aizawl City Traffic Police, 648 vehicular accidents have occurred in which 30 persons lost their lives within Aizawl city area this year. In most of these accidents, drunken driving was the main reason.

 

 

F. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper. Dt. 05.12.2012

 

Ban on fireworks: A joint meeting of Mizoram Home Minister R.Lalzirliana, leaders of CYMA and related department decided to impose a ban on fireworks during this coming Christmas season. As practiced in the past two years, plans for the State enforcement department to implement this ban strictly was also discussed in the meeting.
