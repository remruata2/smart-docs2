SPECIAL REPORT

 

No. MSB/CB/SR/2020/463

Dated: 19th August, 2020 

 

Information has been received that 1 (one) Assam Rifles personnel of 8th AR was alleged to have perpetrated sexual misconduct against 2 (two) women of Sesih village, Champhai District, on 15.8.2020. The 2 (two) women, viz., Pi Khualsiannemi (25) w/o Tinlianthanga and Pi Lalthanmawii (38) w/o Paukhupa, were returning from their paddy field located at the bank of river Tiau on 15.8.2020 @ 4-5 p.m., and the said Assam Rifle personnel, who was on duty at approximately 1 (one) km away from the village, committed sexual misconduct against the two women by way of offering them Rs.500/- and Rs.20/- notes allegedly for sexual favour. He forcefully hugged, smooched and tried to kiss                           Pi Khualsiannemi. The 2 (two) women fled to their village in fear.

The above two women informed the incident to NGO leaders of the village, who in turn, convened a meeting the same evening. The two women and NGO leaders visited AR duty post at around 9:00 p.m. the same night and informed them about their grievances over the incident. No agreement was made.

On dt.16.8.2020, 1 (one) AR Officer went to Sesih village and an agreement was subsequently reached, wherein AR will vacate their duty post from Sesih village jurisdiction on or before 20.8.2020. Hence, no FIR was lodged over the incident.

As per input, the above AR tried to shift their duty post from its present location to the bank of river Tiau today, i.e., 19.8.2020. They were, however, prevented by around 10 (ten) leaders of NGO as the new post is still falling under Sesih village area.

Information has been received that the above AR stationed near Sesih Village have stated their intention to withdraw from the area and they are in a process of evacuating the area at the time of writing the report. As per input, they will return to their Company Headquarters at Khawbung, Champhai District.

Leaders of Sesih village are keeping a close vigil on the movement of the AR and they expressed their intention to remain on duty till the AR vacated the area. They also mentioned their plan to lodge an FIR as well as to bring the matter to the notice of media persons if AR fails to vacate Sesih village area as per the above agreement.

Situation is under close watch.

DGP (M)
