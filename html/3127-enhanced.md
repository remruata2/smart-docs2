****SPECIAL REPORT****

No. MSB/CB/SR/2021/574

Dated 28th July, 2021

****1.** Information has been received that yesterday, i.e., 27.7.2021 @ around 2:00 p.m., railway tracks connecting to Bairabi, Mizoram was uprooted by local people in between Mohammadpur, Katlicherra and Lala Bazar, Assam which is approximately around 40 (forty) kms from Bairabi Railway Station. It is also learnt that Railway authorities have informed the matter to concern authorities and no repair works have been started till the time of writing the report. As per input, Railways authorities felt that there may be delay and difficulties in undertaking repair works or ensuring further safety of rail tracks if no meaningful discussion is taken up by Assam and Mizoram government with regard to ongoing border tension.**

****2.** Mizo Missionaries and their families of Garmura Mission Compound, Hailakandi district, Assam, left the Compound yesterday, i.e., 27.7.2021 @7:30 p.m. due to apprehension about their safety in view of large gatherings of crowd at various places to observe 3-day State mourning owing to the death of Assam Cops due to border clash. As per input, 4 (four) Assam policemen were deployed at the Mission Compound after the Assam Chief Minister declared State Mourning. However, at around 12:00 noon yesterday, Officer-in-Charge of Ramnathpur PS advised them to leave the Compound as the crowd may go out of control and as they have faced such brutal attack in the past and he also assured them that he can provide police escort during their evacuation. They were provided escort up to the Assam-Mizoram border and all the 44 (forty four) persons safely arrived at Bairabi @9:00 p.m. and left for their respective destinations @10:20 p.m. after clearing Covid-19 tests.**

****3.** As per input, the Administrative Secretary of Mission Compound, Silchar requested for deployment of security personnel to Deputy Commissioner, Cachar district, last evening, i.e.,27.6.2021 @around 9:00 p.m. The request was made after some unknown miscreant(s) pelted stones at the building occupied by Pastor of Mission Compound few minutes earlier. Accordingly, DC, Cachar sent 3 (three) Assam Police personnel to provide security to the Mission Compound. In addition, 5 (five) CRPF personnel were deployed w.e.f., today. It may also be added in this connection that after the incident of firing occurred at the inter-state border on 26.7.2021, authorities of Mission Compound requested DC, Cachar to provide security duty as the reaction of the public to the incident cannot be predicted. However, DC, Cachar regretted the request stating that the Compound is not in any danger and that all available forces are being deployed at the border. As the situation still remain volatile and any untoward incident occurring at any place may flare up the situation at other places, it is, therefore suggested that security deployment at the Mission Compound may be beefed up and adequate security personnel may be deployed. It may also be added in this regard that there are 1 (one) Section of CRPF and 4 (four) unarmed Assam Police personnel presently deployed at Mizoram House, Silchar and there are 28 (twenty eight) stranded guests and 2 (two) Tank-Lorries (Truck). It is also suggested that security at Mizoram House may also be strengthened as it will be the main target should the situation turn undesirable.**

**4.** As per input, Shri.Srinkhal Chaliha, Organising Secretary of an organization named ‘Veer Lachit Sena’ of Assam has stated that attack on Assam Police personnel by Mizos will not be tolerated and they are ready to fight the encroachers of Assam’s territory. They gave an ultimatum to Assam government to immediately bring to book those Mizos responsible for the death of Assam Police personnel. As per input, the organization will not hesitate to attack Mizo Police Camp located at Kaziranga and Mizoram House, Guwahati.

**5.** Around 70 (seventy) persons representing various Cachar Students’ organization gathered in front of Mizoram House, Silchar today, i.e., 28.7.2021 @12:30 p.m. in protest against the death of 7 (seven) persons due to Assam-Mizoram border clash on 26.7.2021 and condemned the actions of Mizoram Police. They shouted slogans, such as, Mizoram Police: Go Back from Assam’ and dispersed peacefully @1:30 p.m. without any untoward incident. Mizoram House, Silchar is being guarded by CRPF personnel.

****6.** 3 (three) residents of Kolalian, Mamit District who were en-route to their farm land were restrained and pushed back by Assam Police (believed to be Assam Police Commando) at Thirphun Tlang, this morning @ 7:00 a.m. at inter-state border, which is located around 1 km from Kolalian village. Information received stated that Assam Police are being deployed along Thingphun Tlang stream.**
