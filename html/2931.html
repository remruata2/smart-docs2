SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/508

Dated: 5th July, 2019.

 

            Information has been received that leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced People’s Forum (MBDPF), namely, Pu A. Sawibunga, President; Pu R. Laldawngliana, Vice President and
Pu Bruno Msha, General Secretary met Pu Lalchamliana, Hon’ble Home Minister today, i.e. 5.7.2019 @ 1:00 p.m at his office chamber. They had submitted 6 (six) proposals, which they implored the Government to address, before the displaced Brus are repatriated to Mizoram as follows:-

  1. Some Rehabilitation Centers such as Mamit, North Sabual, Kawrtethawveng, Tuidam, Chhippui, Rengdil, Zawlnuam Vengpui, Suarhliap, Kanhmun, Luangpawl, Saithah, Pukzing, West Phaileng and Lallen are to be re-arranged as there is no sufficient land for cultivation since Brus are eager to make ongoing project of SEDP successful as the Brus people are solely depending on shifting cultivation. These families can be resettled in the following places namely, Sirte Tlang in Lunglei District and Kangzau, Artepa Lungthirek, Tuilut, Zoar, Hmuitlang, Mualthuam, Zomuantlang, Khanthuam in Mamit District.
  2. The MBDPF leaders along with all head of the families should visit Mizoram and identify the selected places of Rehabilitation Centres and construct their dwelling houses prior to the commencement of repatriation.
  3. Liberty should be given to some families who have exceptional cases to settle in any districts such as Mamit, Kolasib or Lunglei.
  4. Government of Mizoram should consider taking back those families who fled Mizoram in 2009.
  5. To approve and grant compensation packages for the bifurcated families who have been separated from their parental houses due to marriage and unavoidable circumstances.
  6. The repatriation packages should directly be transferred to the Bank Account of every head of the family as enshrined in quadrilateral agreement signed on 3rd July, 2018 in New Delhi.
