A. _Front page of The Aizawl Post newspaper Dt. 15.11.2022_

****Chikungunya positive detected :******Around 50 patients have been confirmed to be infected with Chikungunya disease in Mizoram within this year. According to the records of State Vector Borne Diseases Control Programme (SVBDCP) under Mizoram Health department, till 6th November of this year, 53 patients have been suffering from this disease however without any fatalities. The majority of these patients were from residents of southern parts of the states and were detected during the month of October of this year. This sickness, which is spread by mosquitoes like dengue, was detected in Mizoram from the year 2018 with 10 person infected by it during that year while there were no cases of it during 2019. In 2022, 67 patients were again detected with no records of infected persons in 2021.**

C. _Second page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt.15 .11.2022_

**Weapons hahded over to Mizoram Police**: Yesterday (Dt.14.11.2022) Mizoram Firearms Bureau handed over several firearms to Mizoram police in a programme held at Arya Mess, Police Headquarters, Aizawl. In this function, which was graced by Home Minister Pu Lalchamliana, Mizoram Firearms Bureau handed over AK type 56 rifle, AK Type 81 rifle, M4 A1 carbine, MA 3 Mk-II rifle and other types of weapons which are 49 in total numbers. Speaking on this occasion, the Home Minister highlighted that although it is the desire of the government to armed Mizoram police with sophisticated weapons , they are sometimes hampered by insufficiencies of funds. At the same time, Mizoram government has been procuring new arms and ammunition from time to time, which are second to none comparing to weapons of other states, the Minister added.
