A. Front page of  VANGLAINI  newspaper  Dt.21 .11.2022

Corporation, board and Societies not to recruit staff : As per an Office Memorandum  issued by Mizoram Finance Department, various Corporations, Boards and Societies under Mizoram Government are instructed not to recruit any new staffs by their own, without obtaining prior approval from finance department. According to the memorandum, these corporations and Boards often created additional posts by their own with the consent of their respective Governing Board which resulted in the need to allot additional funds to them. The memorandum highlighted how these additional funds created financial problems for Mizoram Finance department and failure to comply to this memorandum will result in non allotments of funds for their salaries.

 

 

B. Front page of  VANGLAINI newspaper  Dt. 21.11.2022

380 bootleggers apprehended: Excise & Narcotics department (END) and Police department under Mizoram government seized 6,590.95 litres of illegally manufactured  alcohol along with 380 bootleggers during the month of October, 2022. According to a statement issued by Mizoram Excise & narcotics department (END), apart from the above mentioned seizure, other items seized by enforcement agencies of Mizoram include 13,040 litres of fermenting alcohol, 4 kgs. of yeast, 56 utensils used for their manufacture, 2536 full bottles of IMFL, 29 half bottles of IMFL and 4538 can/bottles of beer.

 

 

 

C. Front page of  VANGLAINI newspaper  Dt. 21.11.2022

Toy guns seized : In pursuance of a prohibitory  order issued against the selling of fire crackers and toy guns, a joint team of local police and volunteers from YMA/VC in Champhai town conducted checking operation and recovered seven toy guns which were capable of discharging plastic pellets. During this joint checking operation, various shops within Champhai town were visited and instructed the shop owners to pay heed to the prohibition against selling of such items. Before the commencement of the checking, the joint team were flagged off by DC, Champhai, Pu James Lalrinchhana who stated that the prohibition order will be enforced strictly.
