A. Front page of THE ZOZAM  TIMES newspaper Dt.14.2.2013:

 

Peaceful atmosphere :

            Inside the Chalfilh constituency area, where MLA bye election will be conducted this month, no reports of undesirable activities have been received so far. According to Aizawl SP L.R. Dingliana, security situation in these areas are under control. There are 29 polling stations out of which 13 are proclaimed as sensitive. In each sensitive polling station, 7 armed police with 2 unarmed police are stationed while 2 MRP's are deployed in the rest. A platoon of 2nd IR Bn. under Dy.SP. are also stationed in Suangpuilawn as a Rapid response team.   

 

 

B. Front page of THE AIZAWL  POST/VANGLAINI/MIZO AW newspaper Dt.14.2.2013:

 

118 Bootleggers arrested :

            According to reports from Police & Excise Deptt. 118 bootleggers were arrested during the month of January, 2013- with 9 persons charged with alcohol consumption. The reports indicated seizure of 1907 Ltrs. of Rakzu (Local wine), 3399 tins of fermenting rice, 93 Kgs of Yeast, 5335 bottles of IMFL, 650 bottle of Beer and 16 motor vehicles in this connection.

 

 

C. Front page of THE AIZAWL  POST newspaper Dt.14.2.2013:

 

Life sentence for murder :

            In connection with Sangau Police Station case No. 5/10 dt.13.7.2010 U/s. 302 IPC, the Addl. Dist. & Session Judge, Lunglei, Vanlalenmawia convicted Thawmliana (38) S/o. Bualtuma (L) of Lungzartum to life imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 5,000/- in which failing of paying the fine will result in an extension of 5 months. The convicted person murdered Mrs. F. Menthaii on 10th July, 2010 and robbed her of Rs. 30,000/- in cash.

 

 

D. Front page of VANGLAINI/THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt.14.2.2013:

 

Precautionary Notification:

            The SDPO of Vairengte requested all travelers plying towards Silchar to be more cautious as there is an ongoing conflict between party members of Congress and BJP in Dholai areas. So far, 13 persons have died due to this incident and a curfew had been imposed in the areas. The SDPO advised travelers to make a detour of these areas by using Bairabi road.
