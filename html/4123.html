vii )     Recent intelligence:

 

            (1) KNF - "Third round Peace Talk" between Kuki-Chin National Front (KNF) and Government of Bangladesh (GoB) represented by the Peace Committee was held on 5th November, 2023 at Hmunlai village, near Ruma bazar, Bandarban district, Bangladesh. Although no definite agreements were made in the meeting, the following resolutions, which were to be carried out before the next meeting, were passed -

a) Refugees who fled to Mizoram are to be repatriated to their respective villages as soon as possible.

b) Bangladesh government are to take steps to provide development/education facilities/ health facilities to six different Kuki-Chin ethnic tribes.

c) Until the next peace talk, cease-fire shall be maintained between both the parties.

d) 23rd December 2023 has been proposed as the next peace talk date.

            The meeting was attended by 5 representatives of KCNF while Bangladesh government delegation comprised of 11 members, headed by Mr. Keu Shyelah, Chairman, Peace Committee & Chairman Bandarhban Hills District Council.

           (2) Indo Myanmar border activities :         

     

            Inputs received stated that, 83 (eighty three) armed personnel of Myanmar Army who had fled Mywah/Myuukwa camp 377 also known as the Three Corners Strategic camp (India-Myanmar-Bangladesh junction), a Myanmar Military Establishment entered Tuisentlang, Lawngtlai District Mizoram, India ON. 29.12.2023 at around 01:30 p.m. in the afternoon after crossing the Indo-Myanmar international border near border pillar no. 3. These Myanmar Army personnel surrendered themselves to Assam Rifles at Tuisentlang Village. Another 68 (sixty eight) armed personnel of Myanmar Army, from the above mentioned Myanmar Military Establishment also entered Tuisentlang, Lawngtlai District Mizoram, India on the night of 29.12.2023 at around 06:30 p.m.

These 151 (one hundred fifty one) personnel of Myanmar Army along with their arms and ammunition had been transported by the Assam Rifles, the concerned Border Guarding Force to Parva Assam Rifles Camp which served as a temporary holding area for these foreign military personnel.

Further reliable inputs indicated that since 13.12.2023, anticipating military attack from the Arakan Army, personnel of Myanmar Army of several camps in different locations within the operational areas of Arakan Army had fled their camps to reorganize at the above mentioned Mywah/Myuukwa camp 377. However, the Arakan Army conducted drone attacks on the said Myanmar Military Establishment since 22.12.2023 leading to the taking over of the military establishment by the Arakan Army. It is further learnt that approximately 50 (fifty) personnel of Myanmar Army had been captured by Arakan Army and that the area between Paletwa in Myanmar and Zorinpui in Mizoram had been taken over by the Arakan Army.

            Lawngtlai District Police has been working in close coordination with the Assam Rifles in this regard and the situation is closely monitored.

 

            Due to the intense military operations conducted during the past few months by the combined resistance forces of Myanmar against Myanmar Army in several places of Chin State, Myanmar, many Myanmar Army and Police personnel posted and camped near the IMB surrendered themselves to Mizoram Police and Assam Rifles at different places. These personnel of Myanmar were evacuated to safety for their return into Myanmar. The lack of proper Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) on the issue of surrendering of Myanmar Army personnel or government servant needed a review to devise a strategy on the matter.
