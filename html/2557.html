(N. Kotiswar Singh, J)
Though no one is present for the petitioner, considering the submission made by
learned counsel for the respondents, we are of the view that it may not be necessary
to continue this PIL. The relief claim in the PIL is as to why,
“(i) The Hon’ble High Court will not issue an appropriate writ, order or direction
asking the respondents to take immediate steps for removal of the illegal and
unconstitutional blockade of National Highway, NH-306 at Lailapur and the
Inter-State Highway connecting Karimganj District, Assam and Mamit district,
Mizoram at Medili along with the blockade of the Railway line between Silchar
Page No.# 4/5
Bairabi and to ensure that such illegal and unconstitutional blockades do not
take place again and after hearing the cause that may be shown by the parties
to finally make the rule absolute and pass any other necessary orders or
directions as deemed fit by the Hon’ble Court.”
2. It has been submitted by Mr. R. Dhar, learned counsel for the State respondent
that an affidavit has been filed on behalf of the respondent Nos. 3, 5 & 7 by one Smti
Prabhati Thaosen, IAS, Secretary to the Government of Assam, Home and Political
Department, Government of Assam, Dispur stating that the situation in the Inter-State
Assam-Mizoram border at Medly in Karimganj district is under constant vigil and
District Administration had already taken all possible steps to maintain free and smooth
vehicular traffic movement from Karimganj to Mizoram and the vehicular traffic
including transportation of essential goods and items to and fro has been going on as
usual without any interruption through Karimganj district.
3. We would like to mention that it is the primary responsibility of all the States
including the State of Assam and Mizoram to ensure smooth movement of traffic both
passengers and goods on the National Highways and it has been submitted on behalf
of the State of Assam that normalcy has been restored.
4. In this regard, it has been submitted by Mr. S. Chakraborty, learned counsel for
the Railways that movement of goods train in the Bhairabi-Silchar sector has been
already restored.
5. We have also perused the affidavit filed by one Vijoy Kumar Jha, Section Officer
in the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India, New Delhi on 06.03.2021. It has
been mentioned in Para-2 (viii) of the said affidavit that the Union Home Secretary
held a meeting with the Chief Secretaries of Assam and Mizoram through Video
Conference on 08.11.2020 and it was agreed that both the State Governments should
take necessary steps for restoring peace at the Inter-State boundary and restore
normalcy and ensure smooth movement of vehicles. It has been further mentioned in
the affidavit that the vehicular movement on National Highway No. 306 connecting
Assam and Mizoram restarted on 11.11.2020 and the Ministry of Home Affairs was
constantly in touch with the Government of Assam as well as Government of Mizoram
Page No.# 5/5
and urging both the State Governments to work for restoration of complete peace and
amity and de-escalate tensions along the border areas and as a result the process to
de-escalate tension along the border was set in and movement of vehicular traffic was
restored.
6. We are of the view that if normalcy in traffic movement on the National
Highway and rail, road has been restored as mentioned above, it may not be
necessary to continue this PIL. Accordingly, we close this PIL. However, it goes without
saying that if such situation arises in future leading to disruption of smooth movement
of traffic both passengers as well as goods, the petitioner will be at liberty to approach
this Court again.
