** **AGITATIONS AGAINST BRU VOTERS OF REFUGEE CAMPS****

_31st March, 2014._

 The Election Commission of India (ECI) announced that it will conduct Lok Sabha election through postal ballots in Tripura Bru Relief Camps from 1.4.2014 to 3.4.2014 despite opposition by five NGOs in Mizoram. It is reported that the Election Commission had appointed seven observers to oversee the voting in seven Bru relief camps in Tripura. Four Nodal Officers and one ARO from Mizoram reached Tripura Relief Camp on 28.3.2014.

_2nd April, 2014._

 MSU General Headquarter Executive Committee meeting was convened at MSU office, Tuikual, Aizawl on 2.4.2014 resolved to prevent/obstruct transport of Bru Postal Ballot boxes from Tripura to Mizoram and agreed to use force or violent means for preventing counting of such Bru postal ballot papers in Mizoram.

_1st April, 2014._

 Pi Juhi Mukherjee, Aizawl D.C. held a meeting with leaders of joint NGOs and representatives of Mizoram Lok Sabha 2014 candidates at her office chamber over the issue of Bru voting in Tripura Relief Camps.

 In the meeting the leaders of the NGOs and representatives of the candidates have expressed their strong resentment over the ongoing casting of votes through postal ballot papers by Bru electors and their intention to protest counting of such Bru ballot papers in Mizoram.

 From 1.4.2014, Bru refugees, sheltered in Relief camps in Tripura began casting their votes through postal ballots for the Lok Sabha polls in Mizoram amid tight security. The balloting continued till 3.4.2014.

_4thApril, 2014._

 NGO Co-ordination Committee (i.e. Five Mizoram state's most powerful organisation, YMA, MUP, MHIP, MZP and MSU) held a joint committee meeting at CYMA Committee room .The meeting decided to call a 72 hours total bandh within the whole of state of Mizoram w.e.f Dt. 7.4.2014, 5:00 AM. The total band is called as a part of the ongoing protest against the Election Commission’s decision to allow Bru casting of votes through postal ballots from relief camps in North Tripura.

(a) Counting of votes polled by Bru/Reang voters through postal ballot papers in six Relief Camps, Tripura during 1st - 3rdApril, 2014 for the lone Lok Sabha seat of Mizoram would be conducted at Kanchanpur, Tripura on the day of counting i.e. 16th May, 2014 (Friday). The decision of Election Commission of India (ECI) in this regard was conveyed to Chief Electoral Officer (CEO), Mizoram the night of 3.4.2014 by Pu Narendra N. Butolia, Secretary, Election Commission of India (ECI)

_7th April, 2014._

 The Statewide 72 hours total bandh called by NGO Coordination Committee in Mizoram evoked an overwhelming support from the public with business establishments, educational institutions remaining closed in most parts of the State. The Bandh has affected the ongoing electoral poll process as polling parties could not leave their places of duty due to road blockade and stiff resistance from the NGOs volunteers in Aizawl, Mamit, Serchhip, Champhai, Kolasib, Lunglei. However, there was no report of any major untoward incident from any part of the State till 1:00 PM of 7th April 2014.

_8th April, 2014._

Normal life continued getting paralysed in most part of Mizoram for the second consecutive day in response to the 72-hour tolal bandh called by NGO Coordination Committee from 7.4.2014 @5:00AM. All business establishments, banks, educational institutions remained closed and all vehicles remained off the road in National Highway as well as within the District headquarters of Aizawl, Lunglei, Kolasib, Champhai, Mamit, Serchhip However, normal life is not affected in Lawngtlai and Saiha towns as Central Young Lai Association (CYLA), Lawngtlai and _Mara Thyutlia Py___(MTP), Saiha extend only moral supports to the total bandh.

From 1:30 pm,The NGO Coordination Committee has called off the ongoing total bandh with immediate effect. after getting assurance points from Election Commission of India as well as from Government of Mizoram as under :

1. ECI had decided that provision would be made during future elections to the Lok Shaba and the Legislative Assembly of the state of Mizoram for exercise of franchise within the state of Mizoram by Bru voters living in Tripura enrolled in voter list of Mizoram.
2. The Commission has re-scheduled the polling date from 9th April, 2014 to 11th April, 2014 and also reserved 14th April,2014 as re-polling date if needed.

(iii) The Government of Mizoram is committed to take necessary steps for   deletion of Bru voters from Mizoram Electoral Roll who refuse to return  to Mizoram inspite of appeal made by Government of Mizoram.

_29th May, 2018_

 The NGO Co-ordination Committee had summitted a Memorandum to the Chief Election Commissioner, Election Commission of India, Nirvachan Sadhan, New Delhi on 29.5.2018 requesting the Election Commission to conduct the forthcoming Election to the Mizoram State Legislative Assembly for Brus living in Tripura, within the State of Mizoram only. The NGO Co-ordination Committee insisted that, after repeated attempts by the Ministry of Home Affairs and Government of Mizoram, there is no justification to provide special facilities in terms of Revision of Electoral Rolls and Right to Franchise to those Brus who refused to return to Mizoram as provided by Sub-Clause (b) of Clause 19 under Part-III of the Representation of the Peoples Act, 1950, and that they would oppose any such special arrangements for Brus.

_30th May, 2018_

 The NGO Co-ordination Committee, after meeting the Chief Election Commissioner on. 29.5.2018, met Shri. Satyendra Garg, Joint Secretary, MHA in his office chamber on 30.5.2018 again apprising him of their request not to provide special facilities to the Bru refugees in Tripura in terms of Revision of Electoral Rolls and rights to franchise any more as they refused to return to Mizoram.

_8th August, 2018_

 A joint meeting of Government officials with all Political Parties and Joint NGOs Coordination Committee, viz. CYMA, MHIP, MUP, MZP, MSU and MJA was held on 8.8.2018 at Secretariat Conference Hall, New Secretariat Complex, Khatla which was presided by Pu R. Lalzirliana, Hon'ble Home Minister and attended by 31 (thirty one) persons. Pu R. Lalzirliana, Hon’ble Home Minister informed the meeting that Summary Electoral Roll Revision has to be conducted at Bru Refugee camps as per the instruction of ECI. After threadbare discussion, representatives of all NGOs and Political Parties reiterated their stand that Summary Electoral Roll Revision should be conducted in Mizoram and not at Bru Refugee Camps in Tripura.

_9th August, 2018_

 Leaders of Central Young Mizo Association (CYMA) are upset with the conduct of Electoral Roll Revision of Bru voters at Refugee Camps in Tripura as per the direction of Election Commission of India. Leaders of CYMA highlighted that Government of Mizoram and Election Commission of India have previously expressed their assent to conduct Electoral Roll Revision of Bru Refugees in line with the Repatriation. Leaders of CYMA also held Shri. S.B. Sashank, Chief Electoral Officer, Mizoram responsible for initiating the on-going Electoral Roll Revision of Bru Voters at Refugee Camps in Tripura.

 

 _13th August, 2018_

 (a) MSU Hqrs. Zamuang, Sub-Hqrs. Bungthuam and Sub-Hqrs. Rengdil issued a joint statement dated 11.8.2018 in the wake of an attempt by Election Department to conduct Electoral Roll Revision inside Bru Refugee Camps in North Tripura. The Joint MSU, in its statement, appealed to the Election Department officials to respect the resolution adopted in this regard by All NGOs who had opposed such exercise, and warned that they would adopt any means to oppose conducting of E/Roll Revision inside Tripura.

 (b)MZP Sub-Hqrs., W. Phaileng in a statement issued dated 11.8.2018, also stated that officials who had attempted to conduct E/Roll Revision inside Bru Refugee Camps in Tripura were deterred by MZP Hqrs. Zawlnuam and YMA, and appealed to the general public to support the resolution adopted by All NGOs in this regard.

_14th August, 2018_

 (a) In a Press Note issued on 14.8.2018, NGO Coordination Committee (MUP, MHIP, MZP, MSU and CYMA) urged the Election Officials not to conduct 2018 MLA Election related matters anywhere outside Mizoram. NGO Coordination Committee lauded the initiatives of the State Government to repatriate Bru Refugees from Mizoram and also expressed their hope for the success of the ‘Four Corner Agreement’ signed on 3.7.2018 by the Union Home Ministry, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF).

 (d) Executive Committee Meeting of Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ Coordination Committee (MBDPCC) was held on 13.8.2018 at Naisingpara, Tripura. The meeting was chaired by Pu T. Laldingliana, President, MBDPCC and the following resolutions were passed by the meeting :-

 i) MBDPCC strongly condemned the Government of Mizoram for allowing NGOs to block Election officials from conducting E/Roll Revision at Refugee Camps in Tripura.  MBDPCC alleged the Government of Mizoram of instigating the communal based NGOs in derailing the process of E/Roll Revision.
 ii) MBDPCC strongly resolved to organise a long march from Kanhmun to Zawlnuam on or before 22.8.2018 in protest against the attitude of Government of Mizoram towards the Bru refugees.

_20th August, 2018_

 NGO Coordination Committee meeting was held on 20.8.2018 @ 2:30 p.m at CYMA Committee Room, Tuikhuahtlang, Aizawl.. The meeting elaborated the reports on conduct of Electoral Roll Revision at Bru Refugee Camps in Tripura. The meeting expressed serious concern over the alleged inclusion of fresh voters in the Electoral Roll Revision and will take necessary steps for the cancellation thereof. NGO Coordination Committee also expressed disappointment with Office of the Deputy Commissioner, Mamit in lodging FIR against leaders of Langkaih GroupYMA &amp; MZP and in this regard, NGO Coordination Committee urged the authorities to post a Mizo for the post of Deputy Commissioner, Mamit District.

_24th August, 2018_

 In a Circular issued on 24.8.2018, NGO Coordination Committee urged its affiliated NGOs to render assistance to the Government authorities in the undergoing Electoral Roll Revision being conducted at Tripura Transit Camps under the instruction of Election Commission of India. The Circular urged the affiliated NGO Coordination Committee members within Lunglei, Mamit and Kolasib Districts to monitor Roll Hearing dates for the detection of bogus voters and their subsequent removal from Electoral Rolls.

_4th September, 2018_

 (a) NGO Co-ordination Committee (CYMA, MUP, MHIP, MZP, MSU) held a meeting on 4.9.2018 in which they expressed expressed serious concern over Deputy Commissioner, Mamit conducting Electoral Roll Revision at Tripura Transit Camp. NGO Coordination Committee highlighted that they have previously urged the concerned officials not to conduct election related matters outside Mizoram before the completion of the repatriation process. In this regard, NGO Co-ordination Committee alleged Shri. Bhupesh Chaudhary, Deputy Commissioner, Mamit of disrespecting the sentiments of NGO Co-ordination Committee and the Mizo public in general and urged Shri. Bhupesh Chaudhary to leave Mizoram. NGO Co-ordination Committee also urged the Election Commission of India to fulfill its commitment made in 2014 for taking necessary initiatives to stop polling at Bru Refugee Camps at Tripura. A Press Note is issued in this regard.

(b) All NGOs &amp; Political Parties, Mamit held a meetingon. 4.9.2018 @ 7:00 a.m at Mamit YMA Sub-Hqrs. Office Committee Room. The meeting elaborated on the transfer of Deputy Commissioner, Mamit District out of Mamit. In this regard, 7 (seven) member representatives of Mamit NGO Coordination and All Political Parties left Mamit for Aizawl on the same day to submit their application regarding the transfer of Shri. Bhupesh Chaudhary, Deputy Commissioner, Mamit. The application highlighted several points including : -

 i) Accusation against Shri. Bhupesh Chaudhary of intentionally conducting Bru Electoral Roll Revision at Refugee Camps in Tripura.

 ii) Failure to protect the safety of important documents relating to Bru Repatriation Process at the Deputy Commissioner's Office, Mamit.

 iii) Disrespecting the Mizo community by taking initiatives for the arrest of innocent NGO Coordination Committee leaders at Zawlnuam on 18.8.2018.

 iv) His alleged indifferent attitude towards Mizo Community at Mamit.

 v) His alleged unprofessional attitude against the staff of Deputy Commissioner's Office, Mamit.

_5th September, 2018_

 All NGOs &amp; All Political Parties, Mamit took out a procession on 5.9.2018 @ 12:00 noon from Charkawn, Mamit to Deputy Commissioner Complex, Mamit in demand for transfer of Shri. Bhupesh Chaudhary, Deputy Commissioner from Mamit District. Around 800 (eight hundred) persons took part in the procession. Leaders of All NGOs &amp; All Political Parties, Mamit including leaders of CYMA, MZP, etc. delivered speeches during the procession which ended at around 1:50 p.m. The following resolutions were passed by All NGOs &amp; All Political Parties, Mamit :-

 i) All NGOs &amp; All Political Parties, Mamit protest the discreet conduct of  Electoral Roll Revision at Tripura.

 ii) All NGOs &amp; All Political Parties, Mamit will not allow polling of votes by the Bru voters outside of Mizoram in the upcoming MLA General Election -  2018.

 iii) All NGOs &amp; All Political Parties, Mamit expressed doubt over Shri.  Bhupesh Chaudhary to conduct free and fair MLA Election-2018 at Mamit  District.

 iv) All NGOs &amp; All Political Parties, Mamit demanded the State Government  to issue transfer order for Shri. Bhupesh Chaudhary out from Mamit District on  or before 10.9.2018.

_17th September, 2018_

 A Joint Meeting of all NGOs, all Political Parties and VC Association of Mamit District under the aegis of Sub-Hqrs. YMA, Mamit held a meeting on 15.9.2018 @ Sub-Hqrs. YMA Mamit Committee Room which demanded the transfer of Pu Bhupesh Chaudhary, IAS &amp; DC, Mamit before 18.9.2018. The joint meeting had decided to picket the Office of DC, Mamit from 18.9.2018 onwards if his transfer order out of Mamit District is not issued till 17.9.2018.

_18th September, 2018_

 NGO Coordination Committee, Aizawl held a meeting on the night of 17.9.2018 @ 7:30 p.m at CYMA Office which was followed by CYMA CEC Emergency Meeting @ 8:30 p.m at the same venue. CYMA came to know that Mamit DC had left Mamit for Aizawl @ 6:30 p.m the same evening and spread emergency notice for public gathering to receive him. The public gathered at Aizawl Tennis Court (around 150), New Secretariat Gate-1 (around 50) and Gate-2 (around 70) under the aegis of CYMA and later dispersed @ 12:15 to 12:30 a.m after the DC did not arrive as expected.

 All NGOs Mamit picketed Mamit DC Office and Mamit DC Office was closed today. More than 1,000 (one thousand) persons gathered in front of Mamit DC Office gate. The public gathering dispersed @ 10:50 a.m after passing the following resolutions:-

 i) We don't want Pu Bhupesh Chaudhary, Mamit DC, who   insulted the Mizos to return to Mamit and he should leave   Mizoram at the earliest.

 ii) Transfer order of Pu Bhupesh Chaudhary, Mamit DC should   be issued at the earliest.

_19th September, 2018_

 NGO Coordination Committee, Mamit had stopped picketing of Mamit DC Office and the Office is opened normally as Mamit DC, Pu Bhupesh Chaudhary left Mamit.

_3rd October, 2018_

 NGO Co-ordination Committee was held on 2.10.2018 @ 2:00 p.m at Central YMA Committee Room.. The meeting decided that as the deadline of Brus repatriated from Tripura Transit Camp had ended on 30.9.2018, it should not be allowed to continue repatriation even if there is a Bru Refugee who wants to repatriate after the period fixed as per the agreement of MHA, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and MBDPF which was signed on 3.7.2018. The meeting also stated that those who do not want to return to Mizoram should be deleted from Electoral Roll as already promised by the Government (vide No.32020/19/2012-HM Govt. of Mizoram, Home Department dated Aizawl the 8thApril, 2018)
