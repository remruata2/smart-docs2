A. Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt.14.1.2013

 

Bye-election to be held: Election Commission of India announced that bye-election to Chalfilh constituency (as its MLA Chawngtinthanga, Parliamentary Secretary died) will be held on 23.2.2013.

               Information regarding this bye-election will be announced on 30.1.2013. Nomination can be filed till 6.2.2013 which will be examined on 23.2.2013 and withdrawal time will be open till 9.2.2013. Press conference will be called by Chief Electoral Officer, Pu K. Riachho, IAS today regarding the upcoming bye-election.

 

B. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES/NEWSLINK/THE AIZAWL POST newspapers Dt. 14.1.2013

 

Drowned in Tuivai river: Two persons of Daido village in Aizawl district - Laltanpuia(33) S/o Lalthlamuana and Lianngomanga(13) S/o Gunchizama drowned in Tuivai river on 11.1.2013.

              These two people alongwith two children boarded a bamboo raft to cross Tuivai river and as they fell the raft was going to be thrown over by the strong currents of water, they jumped out of it and drowned. The two children went to the village and informed people about it, they searched for their corpses which were found on saturday.

 

C. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt. 14.1.2013

 

3854 persons without ILP were sent back: In 2012, 37,600 persons entered Mizoram through Vairengte out of which 3854 people without ILP were sent back.

              Vairengte Check-gate regtistered 72 foreigners who came to Mizoram through Vairengte last year.

              Everyday, many people come to Mizoram through Vairengte where they were checked if they have ILP or not, but many also come to Mizoram through Phaisen and Serkhan to Bagha road where ILP has never been checked which makes it impossible to count the numbers of outsiders who entered Mizoram.

              Workers employed by company working in Mizoram are issued group ILP and many people entered at the source time.

 

D. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt. 14.1.2013

 

Two persons accused of shooting animals detained in jail: Remsangpuia and Biakchungnunga of Phuldungsei entered Dampa Tiger Reserve area and were accused of killing two deers, one capped langur and one grey peacock. They were caught by Dampa Tiger Reserve staff on the night of 10.1.2013, they were caught with eight guns and smoked meat of animals they shot. The culprits were taken to Aizawl and produced before Court on 11.1.2013 and sent to jail.
