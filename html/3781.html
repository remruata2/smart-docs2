            The 4th round of Peace Dialogue was held on 28th April, 2017, and the 5th Round of Peace Dialogue was held on 21st & 22nd September, 2017 where in the framework of agreement was worked out. The Peace Dialogue was concluded under conducive atmosphere. As scheduled, the Political Level Peace Talk between Mizoram State Government and HPC-D was held under a cordial environment at the State Guest House, Aizawl during 7th & 8th March, 2018 and the Peace Talk ended with a logical conclusion on 8.3.2018. The State Government and the HPC-D were represented by 10 (ten) members each. While the delegates of Mizoram Government were led by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Political Adviser to Chief Minister; Pu L.T. Hmar, Working Chairman, HPC-D led the HPC-D delegates during the two-day Peace Dialogue.

            On 2.4.2018 @ 4:30 p.m, Government of Mizoram and HPC-D signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) at the function held at the State Guest House, Aizawl. Shri. Arvind Ray, Chief Secretary of Mizoram and Pu H. Zosangbera, Chairman, HPC-D signed the MoS on behalf of the State Government and HPC-D respectively. Pu Lalbiakzama, Addl. Secretary, Home gave reports on the peace process and speeches were delivered by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Pu Lalthalien, Spokesperson, HPC-D during the function. The signing of MoS will bring to an end, the over two-decades armed conflict of the HPC-D. The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC-D was held at CTI, Sesawng on 13th April, 2018. Around 104 Cadres and 12 Council Members of HPC(D) come to over ground at this ceremony. 44 different Firearms with 48 magazines and 2892 rounds of different ammunitions along with Radio Sets with charger were surrendered by HPC(D) at this ceremony.

* The Notification issued by Government of Mizoram with regard to appointment and extension of the term of Interim Sinlung Hills Council (SHC) vide No.B.12012/1/2018-GAD(SHC) Dated 18.1.2019 has evoked much criticism from HPC(D) and amongst Hmar community as well since the newly appointed members allegedly included names of former HPC/SHDC leaders and MNF workers while dropping others who they claimed were integral to the party responsible for the signing of Memorandum of Settlement (MoS). Input received indicated that the 3 (three) appointed SHC members, namely, Lalthlamuana Hmar, Chief Executive Member, Lalbiaknunga, Deputy Chairman and Ms. Melody Lalchhuangzeli, Executive Member representing HPC(D) may be asked to decline their appointment pending decision by the HPC(D) high command whose intended meeting had been stalled due to Total Bandh called by Young Paite Association in Churachandpur on 22.1.2019. Input indicates further that Lalthlamuana Hmar, who is appointed as CEM, seemed prepared to accept rather than decline, albeit the HPC(D) high command’s pending decision.

 

            * Executive Committee meeting of the Ex-Hmar Volunteer Association (HPC) held on 23.1.2019 at Sakawrdai had adopted a resolution criticising the Government of Mizoram for extending the interim period of Sinlung Hills Council (SHC) and expressed their disapproval of the newly appointed members of the Council describing it as a total disregard of the Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) signed between the State Government and the HPC(D). The Association demanded the State Government to respect the MoS and to rescind its appointment order immediately. In the meantime, the Ex-HVA (HPC) also appealed the appointed members to decline the appointment.
