****Action Plan (Road Map – IV) for repatriation and Rehabilitation of Reang (Bru) migrants from Tripura to Mizoram in terms of the direction issued by Social Justice Bench of the Hon’ble Supreme Court****

**A. Identification Of Reang (Bru) Families:**

 All concerned Deputy Commissioners with the assistance of District Authorities in Tripura should assess the number of families who already figured in the Electoral Rolls of Mizoram for their repatriation to Mizoram. For those who claimed to be of Mizoram origin but does not figure in the Electoral Roll of Mizoram, the concerned Deputy Commissioners should devise mechanism for their identification. As past repatriation endeavours of the State Government has been thwarted by Reang leaders for one reason or another, it is imperative that the activities and movement of these leaders be curtailed for successful repatriation.

**B. Arrangement of Logistic:**

 All concerned Deputy Commissioners with the assistance of District Authorities in Tripura should arrange transportation for conveyance of repatriated Reang (Bru) families. They should also set up Facilitation Centres for reception of repatriated at location suitable to the repatriated families. Appointment of Administrative Officers for all villages selected for resettlement along with arrangement for their training should be made on expeditious basis. Arrangement of security for conveyance transporting the repatriated families and villages selected for resettlement should be made by Superintendent of Police of the concerned District. The concerned Deputy Commissioners should appoint Liaison Officers in all the camps in Tripura to facilitate and oversee the arrangement of transport. For Lunglei and Kolasib District, they should identify designated staging area for night halt during for the repatriated families.

**C. Allotment of House Site:**

 All concerned Deputy Commissioners should issue appropriate instruction to all the President of Village Council and Chairman of Site Allotment Advisory Board (SAAB) in which the repatriated are to be resettled to apportion house-site for the repatriated families.

**D. Construction And Arrangement of facilitation centre, Transit Facility And Temporary Shelter:**

 All concerned Deputy Commissioners should issue appropriate instruction to all the Administrative Officers, Presidents of Village Councils and Chairmans of Village Committees to construct sanitary facilities for the repatriated families along with temporary shelters for their accommodation until they construct a house of their own. And on the day of their repatriation, the Administrative Officer should ensure that a hot meal is provided to them on their arrival and the sick and vulnerable attended to by the medical personnel. All appropriate measures to facilitate their entry to societal mainstream should be undertaken to avoid alienation and discrimination.

**E. Repatriation of Reang (Bru) Families:**

******All concerned Deputy Commissioners should ensure that repatriation of Reang families is undertaken in an organised manner and on expeditious basis. It should be ensured that least time is spent on transit and that full attention is given to the focused responsibility. Deputy Commissioners should make arrangement for running of community kitchen as per entitlement with provision apportioned for the purpose for at least one week until Free Ration card is issued to the repatriated family.**

****F. Disbursement of Free Ration:****

 Free Ration Management Committee under the Chairmanship of Administrative Officer should be constituted in every village selected for resettlement. The constitution and maintenance along with disbursement of Free Ration to the repatriated families should be made as per the procedure described in the Road Map-I.

**G. Disbursement of Cash And Housing Assistance:**

 To ensure that the repatriated families gainfully utilized the assistance, cash and housing assistance should be disbursed in instalment after assessment of implicit needs of the repatriated families. Prescribed formalities for construction of house by repatriated family as described in Road Map-I should be followed and deviation should be made only with the approval of stakeholder and leaders of village for resettlement.
