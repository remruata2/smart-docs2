**Agenda:**

Discussion on Entry-Exit Points along the Indo-Myanmar Border.

**Proceedings:**

1. _Opening Remarks:_

  The meeting was chaired by Pu Anil Shukla, DGP Mizoram. He explained that the meeting was called to study the Entry-Exit points along the Indo-Myanmar Border.

2_. Presentation:_

  Pu Rohit Meena, IPS, DIG (Eastern Range), presented a detailed study on the Entry-Exit points along the Indo-Myanmar Border through a Powerpoint presentation. This was followed by a discussion on the viability of the proposed entry-exit points along Indo-Myanmar border.

**Resolutions:**

1. _Identification of Villages within the 10-Kilometer Border Zone:_

 - Suggestions will be sent to the Government of Mizoram, Home Department, regarding the identification of villages within the 10-kilometer border zone of the Indo-Myanmar Border.

- The Home Secretary will be requested to instruct the concerned District Collectors (DCs) to take necessary action.

- The identified list of villages will be shared with Mizoram Police, SIB, and Assam Rifles.

**-Action: IGP (Hqrs)/SP, CID (SB)**

2) _Finalization of Entry-Exit Points:_

  The latest proposals on Entry-Exit points along the Indo-Myanmar Border were discussed one by one, based on the proposed list prepared by AR. 1. **Thingsai entry-exit points**

 SP Hnahthial raised concerns about the lack of an all-weather road to the Thingsai Entry-Exit point. With the upcoming monsoon season, communication and accessibility to the point will be severely affected. It was decided to inform the concerned authorities about the lack of an all-weather road and the anticipated difficulties during the monsoon season.
