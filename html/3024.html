SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/665

Dated: 6th September, 2019.

 

 

            Reference to our DSI dated 5.9.2019, Item No. E(a). Information has been received that about 130 (one hundred thirty) persons comprises of members of Bharat Hit Raksha Abhiyan (BHRA) and Bru NGO leaders proceeded to Mizoram after visiting Bru Transit Camps in Tripura. They will be accommodated by Pu S.P. Singh, Director (Retd.), Fisheries Deptt., Govt. of Mizoram at his building in Tlangnuam, Aizawl. Pu S.P. Singh is one of the leaders of the BJP, Mizoram Kishan Morcha and appointed as liaison officer for any visiting party leaders from outside the state.

            BHRA is aiming to meet the Chief Minister and the Governor during their stay in Aizawl. Even if Governor is not available, they will try to meet him at Guwahati to discuss about Bru refugees. Input received indicated that BHRA is intending to submit memorandum to the Chief Minister urging him to provide security to the Bru refugees, to allow settlement of repatriated Brus with a minimum of 500 (five hundred) families in a village and allotment of at least 5 acres of land to every Bru family for their survival.

            BHRA cancelled their previous planned night halt at Bawngva village and they are on their way to Aizawl while writing this report. They are expected to leave Aizawl for Guwahati on 7.9.2019 after meeting the Chief Minister.
