**Assessment of Present Security of Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram:**

****Pu. Lal Thanhawla, the incumbent Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram had received a threatening/malicious short service message (SMS) from unknown person on 11.12.2013 and accordingly Aizawl Police Station Case No.373/2013 dt.13.12.2013 u/s 171G IPC r/w 66 A (a)(b) IT Act was registered. The investigation established that Pu. K. Chhawnthuama, Durtlang, Aizawl was the main accused in this case and on 6.3.2014 the case I/O had submitted charge sheet against the main accused. On 4.4.2014 &amp; 8.4.2014, while he was on bail, Pu. K.Chhawnthuama had uploaded another controversial video clips on youtube accusing the incumbent Chief Minister of Mizoram as the main architect in rigging of Electronic Voting Machines (EVM) during the Mizoram General Election-2013. Subsequently, two of the alleged co-partners of K.Chhawnthuama namely R.Lalmuanhlua, Durtlang, Mual veng and C.Lalbiakmawia @ Michael Chhakchhuak, Pukpui, Lunglei uploaded series of controversial videos on youtube on 10.4.2014,14.4.2014, 15.4.2014 and 27.4.2014 with an apparent attempt to undermine the image of Pu. Lal Thanhawla, Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram.

The series of controversial video clips uploaded by K. Chhawnthuama and his co-partners had triggered a law and order problems in some part of Mizoram and opposition parties have also used this issue as an opportunity to malign the personal image of Pu. Lal Thanhawla. In connection with all these issues, Aizawl PS Case No.107/2014 dt.13.04.2014 u/s 109/505 (1)(b) /120 B IPC r/w 66 A (a) (c) IT Act 2000 has also been registered and Special Investigation Team was set up to investigate this sensational case.

The main accused Pu. K.Chhawnthuama and two of his alleged co-accused persons namely R. Lalmuanhlua and K. Lalhruaitluanga were already arrested by Mizoram Police and all of them were kept under Judicial Remand.

 In view of the above stated sensitivity of the cases, SP CID (SB) Field staffs have conducted fresh assessment of security of Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram. During the process, inspection of the surveillance system installed at the Chief Minister‘s Secretariat and Chief Minister’s Bungalow was conducted and Security in-charge/SO to Chief Minister and Personnel Secretary to Chief Minister were also consulted. The following are the outcomes/findings of the current threat perception assessment.

(i)No imminent /urgent serious direct threat to the personnel security of Ho’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram and his family members are perceived at the moment due to the alleged EVM rigging case. However, the apprehension raised by Hon’ble Home Minister on the security of Hon’ble Chief Minister due to the recent allegation made by some elements on EVM rigging is found appropriate and should not be sidelined.

 (ii) It is found that all the CCTV (Closed-circuit television) installed in and around the Chief Minister’s Secretariat and Chief Minister’s Bungalow are non-functional and require immediate activation for the interest of an overall security of the Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram.

 (iii) It is also learnt that a strict access control measures is not taken up at the entrance of Chief Minister’s Secretariat, especially during ‘General public interview session due to sharing of Chief Minister’s Secretariat by Pu. Lal Thanzara, Hon’ble Minister, Health,etc

(iv) It is also found that a strict access control measures is not enforced at the Main Gate of the Chief Minister’s Secretariat. Strict access control measures are to be enforced to ensure that no unauthorised person will have access to the Chief Minister’s Secretariat and Chief Minister’s Bungalow.

 (v) Door Frame Metal detector, etc installed at the main entrance of the Chief Minister’s Secretariat is also non-functional.
