REPATRIATION OF DISPLACED BRUS’ VIS A VIS MBDPF ACTIVITIES

 

After signing of Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with BNLF for their return to mainstream and resetling them the GoM was taking step towards the repatriation of  Bru Refugees as part of the agreement (Para 9 of the MoU between the GoM and the BNLF). However right from the beginning, the MBDPF leaders started taking steps which came in the way of the repatriation process. The first step of such kind was the awareness campaign in the refugee camps like Naisingpara, Asapara, Hazachera on 8.1.2006 by leaders viz. Laldawngliana, A. Sawibunga and Bruno Msha to convince the general public to object repatriation unless the GoM agreed to the following demands :-

 

  1. 1 year Free Ration
  2. Establishment of cluster villages to accommodate at least 500 Bru repatriated families.

 

After campaigning in the six camps, a procession was organized on 27.1.2006 from Naisingpara Refugee Camp to Dosda R.D Block Office. The MBDPF leaders who delivered speeches viz. Elvis Chorky, Chairman MBDPF, Laldawngliana, Gen. Secy. and A. Sawibunga, Treasurer, among many others points stressed 1 year free ration, repatriation not to be under dictated terms of GoM, MoU between Mizoram and BNLF was not enough to safeguard the Bru people and their strong objection to be resettled in their respective old villages. A memorandum containing 16 charters of demand was submitted to Prime Minister of India through BDO Dosda, N. Tripura giving a copy to Union Home Minister Shivraj Patel, Chief Minister Mizoram, Joint Secretary NE Affairs Unit, India.

 

Inspite of the negative response of the MBDPF leaders, the Govt. of Mizoram was actively considering the repatriation of the displaced Brus and examine their 16 points charter of demands. Meanwhile it was felt necessary to strongly deal with the then militant outfit, the BLFM which posed threat and hardship for the smooth flow of repatriation. There was internecine rivalry amongst the leadership of BLFM which led to the successful operation leading to the arrest of the top brass leaders like President Vanlalliana and others on Dt. 20.4.2006 which was closely followed by surrendering of the remaining leadership led by Zoramthanga V/President and Lalrinkima Molshoy, Gen. Secretary and other high ranking officers of the army setup. The intelligent operation against the BLFM finally culminated into the surrendering of the 804 strong BLFM cadre led by Lalthazuala, then communication secretary of BLFM on 26th October, 2006.

 

After clearing the main stumbling block of the smooth conduct of repatriation the Govt. of Mizoram again initiated repatriation process. Contrary to the initiative of the Govt. of Mizoram for repatriation, the MBDPF also revived their struggle for early repatriation in a negative manner i.e one week sit-in-demonstration in front of Raj Bhawan, Agartala with effect from 22.2.2007 which was followed by indefinite hunger strike with effect from 2.3.2007. The hunger strike was called off on 6.3.2007 only after the visit of Naveen Verma, Joint Secretary, MHA who conceded most of the demands amongst the 16 points of their charter of demand. Four points were kept pending to be discussed with Govt. of Mizoram.

 

On 30.1.2007, the MBDPF leaders submitted a memorandum containing 26 points of demands to Naveen Verma, Joint Secretary, MHA. In the memorandum submitted to Naveen Verma, the MBDPF not only added 10 points of demand in addition of 16 points charter of demand already submitted to Prime Minister but also categorically demanded that 1 lakh compensation to each of the Bru displaced family coupled with Rs. 30,000/- each as housing assistance requiring fresh examination of feasibility of sanctioning such huge amount of money.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Cont…… next

 

On 13th April 2007 a meeting with the MBDPF was held under the chairmanship of Shri C. Ropianga the then Home Commissioner, Govt. of Mizoram. The MBDPF leaders submitted fresh demand to the Chief Minister, Govt. of Mizoram containing 17 points which are divided into two (2)  1-8 concerning Govt. of Mizoram, 9-17 Govt. of India. The points of demand were somewhat similar with their demands already submitted to Govt. of India. However there are some fresh demand like :- 

 

  1. Formation of one permanent autonomous body exclusively for Bru community of Mizoram.
  2. Immediate functioning of special development project as envisaged in the MoU appointing chairman and secretary from the Brus community.
  3. Repatriation of all displaced Brus living in 6 refugees camp without identification.

 

The points submitted by the Brus were discussed and were left for further examination by the Govt. of Mizoram. Another round of meeting was held on 21.11.2008 in the office Chamber Mr. C. Ropianga the then Home Commissioner Govt. of Mizoram. The meeting has decided to have  identification of bonafide residence of Mizoram Brus among the displaced Brus living in the 6 different camps to be conducted by the Govt. of Mizoram at the earliest possible time by giving advance information.

 

On 7th February, 2008 a meeting with the MBDPF leaders held under the chairmanship of C. Ropianga then Home Commissioner discussed wherein the MBDPF leaders were apprised of the decision of the Govt. of India and Govt. of Mizoram pertaining to the demands submitted to the Chief Minister of Mizoram on April 2007. In this meeting leaders of the MBDPF categorically stated that leaders of Ex-BNLF cannot represent the cases of Bru refugees at any level as they do not accept their agreed terms. They further made it clear that they do

not like to have piece meal repatriation until and unless all their demands are fulfilled or MoU signed between the Govt. of Mizoram and MBDPF leaders for implementation of their demand. The process of identification may not be taken up at the moment.

 

Since the process of identification of bonafide resident of Mizoram Brus in the Bru refugee camp was objected by the MBDPF leader the first step of repatriation process was stalled resulting into the delay of repatriation process.

 

Meanwhile attention was given for the settlement of the 804 strong BLFM surrenderees who had been patiently waiting for their resettlement. After carefully conducting identification of Mizoram Brus the genuine Mizoram Brus were re-settled in several selected sides of the district they belonged before migration to Tripura. The re-settlement process of the BLFM surrenderees was completed in the month of March, 2009.

 

Inspite of the objection of repatrition by the MBDPF leaders in the meeting on 7th February, 2008 the Govt. of Mizoram again initiated repatriation process of the Bru refugee and prepared road map for the repatriation of the Bru refugee, a meeting was held in the office chamber of Pu Lalmalsawma, Home commissioner, Govt. of Mizoram on On 4.11.2009 and discussed about the repatriation and rehabilitation of Bru migrants from Tripura. Road map for their repatriation prepared by the Govt. of Mizoram was discussed at length which the MBDPF raised objection on some points which were also discussed points by points. However, when process for their repatriation was due to start on 16.11.2009, Zarzokima s/o Lalngilneia of Bungthuam was shot dead about 1 Km north of Bungthuam village by unidentified persons on 13.11.2009. The culprit left note in which they claimed themselves as Bru Revolutionary Union (BRU) and that they did not want their repatriation without proper agreement and would fight the State Govt. for their right.

Cont…………. next

 

The brutal killing of Zarzokima by some Bru militants and the content of the note left behind by his killers was a matter of great concern for the Govt. Widespread communal violence in the western belt of Mizoram causing many people homeless prompted the Govt. to form a Special Investigation Team (SIT) to investigate this sensational case. The SIT successfully unearthed the reason behind the killing due to which the alleged mastermind of this sensational case. R. Laldawngliana, V/President, MBDPF was arrested on 23.1.2010. The arrested person did not deny that the incident was made to stall repatriation process to kickstart from 16.11.2009.

 

The MBDPF leaders objecting the road map prepared by the Govt. of Mizoram during their last meeting started awareness campaign and on 5th and 6th November, 2009, they visited six refugee camps by holding a public meeting and tried to convince the people not to accept the road map for repatriation. Apart from awareness campaign to the general public, some extremist leaders instructed the so called Bru militant group to cause incident either in the district of Mamit or Kolasib which could prevent the smooth process of Bru refugee repatriation culminating in the unprovoked firing of one innocent Mizo youth causing major law and order problem in the western belt of Mizoram just before the road map was activated.

 

            The MBDPF leaders, who always appeared to have been sincere in their resolve to repatriation process, are in reality acted in a manner contrary to what they used to say in the meeting. Whenever the Govt. proposed to start the process, all kinds of fresh demands used to be added which call for fresh discussion not only for the Govt. of Mizoram but with the Central Govt. Based on intelligence sources and visible material evidence, the reason behind the reluctance of MBDPF leaders to welcome repatriation process is simple; they amassed wealth at the expense of Bru refugees and majority of their leaders were purchasing one or two vehicles including luxury car and commercial vehicles like Car or Jeep and even 207 model for their business.

 

            In the light of the above few lines, it could clearly be seen that the MBDPF leaders while projecting themselves as very serious and sincere  in the repatriation and resettlement of the Mizoram displaced Brus were used to making fresh demands in every meeting which could well be presumed as a delaying tactics on the part of the MBDPF leaders. No one could be blamed for making such a conclusion considering the standard of living of the leaders in the Refugee camp.
