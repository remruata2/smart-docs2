****MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER SITUATION REPORT****

No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/617

Dated 3rd November, 2020

In continuation of our report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/614 Dt. 2.11.2020, Situation Report on Mizoram-Assam Border is as under:

1. **Thinghlun, Mamit District Report** **:**

 No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

1. **Kanhmun, Mamit District Report :**

 There is no movement of vehicles across Medili Bridge, Mizoram-Assam border today i.e., 3.11.2020 due to roadblock organized on either side of the border.

1. **Saihapui ‘V’, Kolasib District :**

 No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

1. **Bairabi, Kolasib District Report :**

 There is no movement of vehicles across Bairabi border today i.e., 3.11.2020.

 Information received indicated that more than 200 (two hundred) members of Krishak Mukti Sangram Samiti (KMSS) who intended to proceed to Masjid near Zophai, Mizoram-Assam border assembled near Ramnathpur Police Station around 1:00 p.m. today and they are prohibited by Assam Police. In this regard, leaders of Krishak Mukti Sangram Samiti (KMSS) are detained at Ramnathpur Police Station and they were released later this evening.

1. **Phaisen, Kolasib District Report :**

Today i.e., 3.11.2020 @ 2:30 p.m. residents of Dolchhora and nearby villages dismantled the bamboo wall which was constructed by them on 20.10.2020 after they had negotiation with leaders of Kolasib Sub-Headquarters YMA and leaders of Phaisen Village. Further, residents of Assam side assured that there will be free flow of vehicles and people at the border if Kolasib District Administration makes arrangement for Covid-19 Screening/Testing at Phaisen.

1. **Vairengte, Kolasib District Report :**

****Road block organized by Zoram Drivers’ Union (ZDU) at Tawngtaina Hmun, Outskirt of Vairengte is still continuing today i.e., 3.11.2020. There is no vehicular movement across Mizoram-Assam border at Vairengte and 162 (one hundred sixty two) vehicles are stranded due to the said road block till today.

Vairengte Joint Village Council and All NGOs Vairengte Emergency meeting was held last evening i.e., 2.11.2020 @ 8:00 p.m. at SDO (Civil) Conference Hall, Vairengte. During the meeting, they discussed border disputes and their future course of action in this regard, and the meeting decided the following:

1. Appealed residents of Vairengte not to allow harvest of their betel nuts by any non-mizos.
2. Not to have alliance with non-mizos who are engaging in betel nut business.
3. No initiative should be taken by any individual association in circulating news relating to Vairengte border issues through social media without Vairengte Joint VC and Joint YMA consent.
4. Expressed gratitude to NGOs and other individuals for extending financial and other assistance during recent border disputes.

The meeting expressed deep regret regarding the death of Imtyaz Ali (48) S/o Khobor Ali, Peshnagar, Lailapur at CHC, Vairengte who was a drug peddler who used to supply drugs in Vairengte.

Further, the dead body of Imtyaz Ali (48) S/o Khobor Ali, Peshnagar, Lailapur was handed over to Ali Hussain Laskar (brother of the deceased) by Mizoram Police at Vairengte Auto Stand today i.e., 3.11.2020 @ 10:30 a.m. after observing all legal formalities. Shri Jakir Das Addl. SP, Cachar, Shri Vikash Chetri, Circle Magistrate and around 20(twenty) Assam Police personnel accompanied brother of the deceased. At the same time, Pu Vanlalnghenga, Dy. CO, 3rd MAP, SDPO, Vairengte, OC, Vairengte PS and Pu Nengthianlala, Executive Magistrate were present at the time of handing over the dead body. It is reported that the dead body will be taken to Silchar Medical College for Autopsy. Around 100 (one hundred) persons assembled at Lailapur, Assam today due to the above incident and there is no report of Law and order situation in the area.

Further, Shri. Jishnu Baruah,the newly appointed Chief Secretary of Assam and Shri. Bhasker Jyoti Mahanta, Director General of Police visited Lailapur today at around 2:00 p.m. to take stock of the situation.

Situation is under close watch.
