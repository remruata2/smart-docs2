A. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt. 30.5.2013

Lungsen village wants to wipe-out Sibinasora before the end of June : Sibinasora hamlet 23 kms away from Lunglei occupied by Chakma immigrants has not been left by any though the government notified the village to be wiped out. Different groups of Lungsen community decided to let the people leave Sibinasora before the end of June. 182 occupants of Sibinasora were notified by Tlabung DFO to leave the place before 15th May, 2013 but to no avail. Joint meeting of Lungsen VC, MZP and MSU felt that their lack of response shows their disrespect for the government.

B. Front page of  of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt. 30.5.2013

30 days closure of bridge between Kanhmun and Damcherra : The bridge connecting Kanhmun and Damcherra has been closed for 30 days. As reported by YMA President of Langkaih group J.Malsawma, though the self-repatriation plan of Bru has been delayed or cancelled for the time being by the government, some family wanted to come back to Mizoram, as such ration card was given to them by their leaders and on 25th May, 2013, six families consisting of 40 members decided to come back to Mizoram instead of dying from hunger in Tripura, they were first placed at Zawlnuam and later joined their families at Zamuang.

C. Front page of  VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 30.5.2013

4 jailed for FICN : 2 men and 2 women have been arrested and detained at Kolasib Dist. Jail by Kolasib Police regarding the seizure of FICN. On Tuesday i.e., 28.5.2013, Rintluangi w/o Ponalalboro, a resident of New Diakkawn, who is involved in the crime was arrested by Kolasib Police. Before this, Ginlala of Manipur, Khualzagaua of Khuangpuilam, Kolasib and Niangneui of Rengtekawn, Kolasib were arrested. Police assumed that around ` 2, 50,000/- fake currency notes have been spread at Kolasib, out of which ` 2,18,500/- was recovered. An amount of fake currency notes of ` 17,500/-was received by Forest Dept., Kolasib and another ` 28,500/- by P&E Dept., Kolasib. Sources said that the bank refused to accept the money back.

D. Front page of  THE AIZAWL POST newspaper Dt. 30.5.2013

Court registered case ‘Laipuitlang landslide’: Gauhati High Court, Aizawl Bench registered a case – WP(C) 5/5/2013 Laipuitlang Landslide and opened it at High Court Yesterday. In the motion stage, Addl. Advocate General, AK Rokhum ordered that steps taken by government regarding the landslide be reported and informed to the court. Court assigned Senior advocate Lalramzauva to assist the court as Amicus Curiae. Hearing will be held in the court on 3rd July, 2013.
