MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER SITUATION REPORT

No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/624

Dated 6th November, 2020

 

          In continuation of our report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/622
Dt. 5.11.2020, Situation Report on Mizoram-Assam Border is as under:

 

  1. Thinghlun, Mamit District Report :

          No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

 

  1. Kanhmun, Mamit District Report :

          Today i.e., 6.11.2020 @ 10:00 a.m. 1(one) company of SSB(CAPF) replaced one platoon of Assam Police deployed at Assam side near Medili Bridge, Assam-Mizoram border. At present, one section of 4th IR Battalion and one Platoon of 5th IR Battalion are deployed at Medili Bridge.

          Further, Pu K. Lalrinliana, Supply Minister visited the ongoing construction of Damcherra Bridge which connects Kanhmun, Mizoram and Damcherra, Tripura today i.e., 6.11.2020 @ 3:30 p.m. and later inspected the proposed place for construction of Kanhmun Supply godown and left Kanhmun at 4:40 p.m. this evening.

          There is no movement of vehicles across Medili Bridge, Mizoram-Assam border today i.e., 6.11.2020 due to roadblock organized on either side of the border.

 

  1. Saihapui ‘V’, Kolasib District :

No new development has been reported during the last 24(twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

 

  1. Bairabi, Kolasib District Report :

          Today i.e., 6.11.2020 @ 11:45 a.m. Pu K. Lalrinliana, Supply Minister visited Bairabi Rail Station and inspected the on-going preparation for unloading of Oil when Train carrying Oil arrived at Bairabi. He left Bairabi for Kanhmun at 12:40 p.m. this afternoon.

There is no movement of vehicles across Bairabi border today i.e., 6.11.2020.  Situation is normal.

 

  1. Phaisen, Kolasib District Report :

Blockade of vehicles bound to Assam via Phaisen road at Zukbual Mual, Bilkhawthlir by Buhchangphai MZP is still continuing today i.e., 6.11.2020 and 12 (twelve) vehicles are stranded due to the said roadblock till today.

Today i.e., 6.11.2020 @ 6:00 a.m. 5(five) leaders of MZP General Headquarters led by Pu B. Vanlaltana, President proceeded to Buhchangphai and Phaisen area to take stock of the situation. They are at Bilkhawthlir at the time of writing the report i.e., 6.11.2020 @ 6:00 p.m. and they will halt tonight at Buhchangphai.

  1. Vairengte, Kolasib District Report :

          Today i.e., 6.11.2020 @ 1:10 p.m. Pu Vanlalfaka Ralte, SP, Kolasib searched for a suitable place of deployment of BSF in between Vairengte Auto Stand and Lailapur, Assam. He was accompanied by Pu Vanlalnghenga, Dy. CO, 3rd MAP, Pu Isaac C. Lalrempuia, SDO (Civil), Vairengte and SDPO, Vairengte. When they reached a place near Hawaithang Playground/Lailapur field/Kawngthar field they found OC, Dholai and party proceeding towards Vairengte and later learnt that they intended to erect Tents for SSB Camp (to be deployed by Assam government) just below Mizoram Police place of deployment at Vairengte Auto Stand. SP, Kolasib informed OC, Dholai PS that the place for setting up of SSB Camp or BSF Camp has not been finalised and no action shall be taken at this juncture. After a while Shri Bhanwar Lal Meena, SP, Cachar reached the area and he informed SP, Kolasib that the place for setting of SSB Camp was identified yesterday i.e., 5.11.2020 by IGP (L&O), Assam and DIG (NR), Mizoram. However, SP, Kolasib informed SP, Cachar that the place which they had visited was a different location and that is also not yet approved by both the state governments. Both SsP left the area without making a definite conclusion as no action for setting up of BSF or SSB Camp can be taken up by Kolasib and Cachar District Authorities before approval of the proposed sites by both state governments. One platoon of 225 Bn. CRPF personnel is deployed at the site where SP, Cachar intended to set up SSB Camp to prevent escalation of tension.

         

          Road  block organized by  Zoram Drivers’ Union (ZDU) at Tawngtaina Hmun, Outskirt of Vairengte is still continuing today i.e., 6.11.2020. There is no vehicular movement across Mizoram-Assam border at Vairengte and 178 (one hundred seventy eight) vehicles are stranded due to the said road block till today. Information received indicated that SDPO Vairengte proposed releasing of
non-Assam registration stranded vehicles (at present 55 (fifty five) Assam registration vehicles are stranded) but ZDU leaders have not yet agreed to the proposal and they decided to hold a meeting this evening.

         

          Situation is under close watch.
