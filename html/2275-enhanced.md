1. **Saikhawthlir, Mamit District Report:**

 No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

1. **Kanhmun, Mamit District Report :** 

 Ref. to Mizoram-Assam Border Situation Report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2021/605 Dt. 05.08.2021. Pu Andy Lalfakzuala Varte, SDO (Civil), Kawrthah and Shri. Swapnajit Sarkar, BDO, Damcherra RD Block held a meeting at Damcherra RD Block Conference Hall today i.e., 6.8.2021 @ 11:00 a.m. in connection with blockade of Damcherra Bridge by Damcherra Bazar Committee. The meeting was attended by representatives of businessmen and VC of Kanhmun and Damcherra. The meeting discussed and decided the following:

1. SDO(C), Kawrthah will discuss the matter with the higher authority of Mizoram government regarding entry of ordinary residents and small businessmen in Mizoram freely.
2. Regarding any problem of the entry of the ordinary residents and small businessmen in Mizoram state they can contact with the administration of Mizoram government and as well as Tripura Government Administration for solving the problem.
3. The SDO(C), Kawrthah will pursue with the higher authority for any possible relaxation regarding mandatory Medical Screening due to COVID-19 pandemic for any person entering into Mizoram.
4. The SDO(C), Kawrthah stated that regarding Mpass for entry into Mizoram only registration on the dedicated portal is required and no fee is needed for the said purpose.
5. The merchant of Damcherra area agreed the request made by BDO, Damcherra RD Block to co-operate with the government and ensure free movement of goods through Damcherra and Kanhmun Bridge till solution is made from both sides.

There was no entry of vehicle at Medili Bridge from Assam today i.e., 6.8.2021 as blockade of road backed by Assam Police is still continuing at Assam side of Medili Bridge.

96 (ninety six) vehicles (HMV-18, MMV-75, LMV-1, LPG-1, Maxi Cab-1, Oil Tanker-Nil, Bus-Nil) entered Mizoram from Tripura through Damcherra bridge today @ 3:30 p.m.

1. **Phaisen, Kolasib District Report :**

Sad Guru Ltd. Company (the company has Camp at Buhchang, Kolasib District and presently engaged in construction of Bikhawthlir to Khuangpuilam Road and Kawnpui to Khamrang Road) hire vehicle Mahindra Bolera B/R No. TR-01 AT-0384 driven by Ranzon of Dolchhera, Hailakandi, Assam along with two passengers viz., Shri. Ashok Poder, Director, Sad Guru Ltd. and Shri. Subas Biwas, Supervisor while proceeding towards Phaisen, Kolasib District from Bilaipur was stopped by civilians of Dolchherra near Dolcherra BOP.

Upon checking the stated vehicle, 1(one) bag of Dal, 1(one) bag of Potato and 3 (three) cases of liquor were found and in this regard, the civilians of Dolchherra accused them of trying to supply essential commodities to Mizoram, and they informed the civilians that they intend to proceed to Phaisen following the reported lifting of road blockade in Assam and if the roadblock is still in force they are willing to return back. However, the civilians didn’t pay any heed to their statement and later turned violent and vandalized their vehicle. Assam Police at Dochhera BOP tried to control the situation and they even opened fire towards the sky to control the crowd as the situation continued to worsen. Upon learning the stated incident, Shri. Gayrav Upadhyay, SP, Hailakandi and a large number of Assam Police promptly arrived at the scene. It is reported that the Police even used tear gas in their attempt to control the situation. The situation went under control around 1:20 a.m. this morning and subsequently dispersed the crowds.

In connection with the stated incident, a case has been registered at Lala Police Station, Hailakandi District and 7 (seven) civilians of Dolchherra were arrested. 

1. **Vairengte, Kolasib District Report :**

Today i.e., 6.8.2021 @ 1:30 p.m. 2 (two) Journalists of Dainik Bhaskar, Hindustan Times proceeded to Lailapur and Major Carolyne Zonunmawii (Rtd.), DSW &amp; RO Kolasib escort them up to Lailapur. In the meantime, 2 (two) Champhai Sumo carrying 20 (twenty) Myanmar refugees (adult -12, minor - 8), who intend to proceed to Delhi also proceeded along with them. However, the 2 (two) Champhai Sumo carrying Myanmar refugees were not allowed to pass through barricade installed by Assam Police at Lailapur citing security as the reason. The 2 (two) Sumo returned to Mizoram and as per arrangement of DC, Kolasib the stated Myanmar refugees would be accommodated at Circuit House, Kolasib till the situation return to normalcy.
There was no entry of vehicle from Assam today due to reported blockade of NH-306 in Cachar District of Assam.
