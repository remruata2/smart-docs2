BRIEF NOTE ON THE ISSUES OF BRU REFUGEES & REPATRIATION PROCESS

 

HISTORY

The Brus also called Reangs are the Nomadic Tribe living in the western belt of Mizoram, bordering Bangladesh and Tripura State. The Brus started entering into Mizoram around 1940's and engaged themselves in jhum cultivation. The Brus were living in 14 villages in Kolasib District, 11 villages in Lunglei District and 46 villages in Mamit District and spread over 9 Assembly Constituencies (i.e. Hachhek, Dampa, Mamit, Tuirial, Kolasib, Serlui, Lunglei South, Thorang and West Tuipui)

            A militant outfit called the Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF) formed in 1996 under the leadership of Vanlalliana, Tuirum village in Mamit district became active and on 21.10.1997 Lalzawmliana, Forest Game Watcher in Dampa Forest Sanctuary was killed and two of his friends were also abducted by the BNLF. The news of the killing spread like wild fire throughout Mizoram and ignited the already tension in the area due to the demand of an Autonomous District Council by Bru National Union (BNU). Tension between the two communities escalated and communal violence broke out and being lesser in numbers, the Bru people started fleeing Mizoram towards Tripura.

The Bru people who fled Mizoram in the wake of the incident were given refugees status in Tripura. They were given shelter in six refugee camps (Kaiskau, Khakchangpara, Hamsapara, Naisingpara, Asapara and Hazacherra) and were also given free rations and other essential commodities at the rate fixed by the Union Government. The Mizoram Bru Displaced People’s Forum (MBDPF), headquartered at the Naisingpara camp on the eastern side of the Jampui Hills is an apex body for Brus living in 6 (six) refugees camps in Tripura. The Forum was created in 2002.

After a number of meetings between the Centre and the State authorities were held, date for repatriation of the Bru refugees was fixed i.e. 16th November, 2009. While all preparations for repatriation process were going on smoothly a Bru militant group who called themselves the Bru Revolutionary Union (BRU) killed a Mizo youth Zarzokima (17), on 13th  November, 2009. It was later confirmed that killing was done deliberately by the BRU in order to deter the repatriation process.

BRU MILITANCY

                                   Since 1997, a group of Brus youth took up arms and started demanding a separate Autonomous District Council for the Brus residing in Mizoram and formed an armed group to support their demand. This armed group threatened the Brus of Mizoram to flee Mizoram. The fleeing Brus from Mizoram were designated in various refugee camps in Tripura. Inspite of an all out effort made by the Central Government and Mizoram Government to repatriate these Bru refugees, they are still reluctant to return to Mizoram.

                        To end the Bru militant activities, the Government of Mizoram initiated Peace Talk with leaders of Bru militants; 195 BNLF cadres surrendered on 25.7.2005 and 804 strong members of BLFM also surrendered before the Government of Mizoram on 26.10.2006 with their arms and ammunition.

 

BRU DEMOCRATIC FRONT OF MIZORAM (BDFM):

Different armed groups of the Brus- BNA/BRA, BNLF or BLFM and BNCT are not active anymore. However, a new group Bru Democratic Front of Mizoram (BDFM) was again formed under the patronage of NLFT. These BDFM cadres are mainly responsible for the heinous crimes which occurred in the western border areas of Mizoram during the recent past which included kidnapping for ransom, burglary of Government offices, etc.

            Though exact time of its formation is not known, BDFM are the remnants of the then BLFM who refused to surrender at the time of the latter's mass surrender on 26.10.2006. They formerly call themselves as Bru Revolutionary Army(BRA), Bru Liberation Army(BLA) etc. They seem to have no concrete aims and objectives and are more likely to be an armed gang rather than a well organised militant group.

The Bru insurgency has been contained to a great extend so much so that this insurgent Group has become more or less a group of Bandits without any definite demand or objectives.

QUADRIPARTITE AGREEMENT

1) Bru Refugee Repatriation agreement was made by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and the Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) on 2.7.2018.

2) However, this Quadripartite Agreement was later opposed by different sections of the Bru society and formed various organizations to derail the repatriation Viz. EPDC (Equal Package Demand Committee), MBRPAIDC (Mizoram Bru Returnees' Peace Accords Implementing Demand Committee), and MBDPCC (Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Coordination Committee).

3) These Bru organizations, so as to prevent the repatriation at all cost, keeps on making fresh demands some of which are completely unacceptable by the Mizo community, for instance, the creation of Autonomous District Council for Brus.

 

PROBLEMS AFFECTING THE SUCCESS OF BRU REPATRIATION

1.         Different organization of Mizoram insisted on repatriating Mizoram Brus origin only, which can be traced from the records of Mizoram Electoral Roll of 1995 and their genuine descendants.

2.         Educated and enlightened leaders of Brus, insisted at all cost, to prevent the repatriation as they are benefited by their fellow Brus being given a refugee status. Donations/Contributions so collected amounted to huge amount which are worth something greatly desirable by those in high position. Hence, demanding various points completely unacceptable by Mizo community and even the government, most notably having Autonomous District Council of Brus.

3.         While these are the purviews of both the Brus and the Mizo communities, solving the Bru Crisis is an important agenda for the Central Government as the Brus, who have settled in Mizoram (India) before Independence have to be considered as Indian Citizens with Minority Status in the State of Mizoram.

4.         The main Law & Order issue which often sprang up regarding Bru refugees is the intentions of Election Commission of India to let these refugees to cast their votes (State Assembly and Parliamentary) during the elections at their respective refugee camps, which has been strongly opposed by the Mizos. In previous years, this objection had let to the near boycotting of the complete election process itself.

 

LOK SABHA MP ELECTIONS BOYCOTTED BY NGOCC

*          An emergent meeting of NGO co-ordination committee held on 4.4.2014 at Central YMA committee room, Tuikhuahtlang, Aizawl which was attended by representatives from CYMA (Central Young Mizo Association), MZP (Mizo Zirlai Pawl), MSU (Mizo Students Union), MHIP (Mizo Hmeichhe Insuihkhawm Pawl), MUP (Mizoram Upa Pawl) resolved to organize a 72 hours Total Bandh with effect from 5:00 AM of 7th April, 2014 to 5:00 AM of 10th April, 2014 throughout the State in protest against casting of votes by Bru refugees in their respective refugee camps, Tripura and to prevent counting of Bru refugee votes. The meeting further decided to request MP candidates not to accept the result if Bru refugee votes is the deciding factor in the election. (The polling date for Lok Sabha election in Mizoram was fixed by ECI as 9th April 2014)

 

*          The State wide 72 hours total bandh called by NGO Coordination Committee in Mizoram in demand of ‘complete cancellation of votes already cast by Reang (Bru) at six (6) relief camps in Tripura and re-polling of Bru votes at special booths on the soil of Mizoram’ evoked an overwhelming support from the public with business establishments, educational institutions remaining closed in most parts of the State. The Bandh affected the electoral poll process as polling parties could not leave their places of duty due to road blockade and stiff resistance from the NGOs volunteers in Aizawl, Mamit, Serchhip, Champhai, Kolasib, Lunglei. However, there was no report of any major untoward incident from any part of the State till 5:00 PM on 7th April 2014.

*         The NGO Co-ordination Committee called off the then ongoing total bandh on 8.4.2014 at 1:30 PM after getting the following assurance points from Election Commission of India (ECI) as well as from Government of Mizoram :-

(i)          That the Election Commission of India ‘decided that provision would be made during future elections to the Lok Sabha and the Legislative Assembly of the State of Mizoram, for exercise of franchise within the State of Mizoram by Bru (Reang) voters living in Tripura enrolled in voter lists of Mizoram’.

(ii)         That the Election Commission of India ‘decided that the date of poll shall be re-scheduled from 9th April, 2014 to 11th  April, 2014 with the date of re-poll, if any, on 14th April, 2014’.

(iii)        That the Government of Mizoram is committed to take necessary steps for repatriation of genuine Bru refugees residing in Transit Camps, Tripura within 3 (three) months after election process is over i.e. 16th May, 2014.

(iv)        That the Government of Mizoram is committed to take necessary steps for deletion of Bru voters from Mizoram Electoral Roll who refused to return back to Mizoram inspite of appeal made by Government of Mizoram.

This 72 hours Total Bandh received an overwhelming support from the general masses in all the districts of the state except Siaha district where the total bandh was not observed on 7.4.2014 and all the 52 polling parties left for their respective places of polling stations on 7.4.2014 as per schedule.

In another incident, on 6th November, 2018, NGO Co-ordination Committee (NGOCC) organised ‘Hnam Chhan Hnatlang,’ an agitation picketing CEO, Mizoram Office. The crowd started gathering from 8:00 a.m. onwards which swelled up to more than 40,000 at its peak. The normal programme started at 10:00 a.m. which was presided by Pu R. Lalngheta, CYMA Vice- President. Speeches were delivered by different Presidents of various organisations participating in NGOCC. In their speeches,  condemning the recent conduct of Shri. S.B. Shashank, CEO, they highlighted the following points:-

 

  1. S.B. Shashank should be transferred out of Mizoram as the people had lost their confidence in ECI because of his conduct of the Election process.

 

  1. The Brus in the Transit Camps, Tripura should cast their votes in the Polling stations where they are enrolled. No special arrangement should be made under any circumstances.

 

  1. ECI should stick to its commitment of 2014 that the Brus in the Tripura Transit Camps should cast their votes within the state of Mizoram.

The agitation was called off @ 12:15 p.m. It was reported that all political parties, labour unions, service associations, private schools and business associations have also voiced their full support of the NGOCC. Many Government servants/officials also participated in the agitation who later resumed their duties after the agitation was called off.

AGREEMENT BETWEEN GOVERNMENT OF INDIA, GOVERNMENTS OF MIZORAM AND TRIPURA AND MIZORAM BRU DISPLACED PEOPLES' FORUM (MBDPF) – QUADRIPARTITE AGREEMENT

            Bru Refugee Repatriation agreement was made by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and the Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) on 2.7.2018.

            Government of India has been making efforts in consultation with Governments of Mizoram and Tripura and MBDPF for repatriation of Brus displaced from Mizoram and presently temporarily living in camps in Tripura. In this regard, all the related issues have been discussed with the relevant stakeholders and the following has been agreed :

 

1.     Those Bru Displaced Persons having been identified by the Government of Mizoram as Displaced Persons (5407 families comprising of 32876 persons) will repatriate to Mizoram as per schedule of repatriation before 30th September, 2018. Temporary camps in North Tripura will be closed in the 1st week of October, 2018.

 

2.     To rehabilitate these Brus in Mizoram, following assistance will be provided :

 

(i)    Each Family will be given one time Financial Assistance for Rehabilitation of Rupees 4 Lakh (Rupees Four Lakh only) to be kept in Fixed Deposit in the name of the head of the family within one month of its repatriation. The money will remain in Fixed Deposit for Two Years and the money will be given to the family on its having lived continuously in Mizoram for three years.

(ii)   Each Family will be given Rupees 5,000/- (Rupees Five Thousand only) per month as Cash Assistance for a period of Two Years from the date of repatriation.

(iii)  The cash assistance will be provided through DBT.

(iv)  Each Family will be given Rupees One Lakh and Fifty Thousand for House Building Assistance. This will be disbursed in three installments: Rs.25,000 at the time of repatriation, Rs.50,000 on completion of 50% of House Construction and the remaining Rs Rs.75,000 on the completion of House.

(v)   Each person repatriating will be given Free Ration for two years at the present rate applicable.

(vi)  Each Family will get Free Transportation to be arranged by Government of Mizoram. The agreed cost of Transportation will be reimbursed to Government of Mizoram by MHA.

       

            Government of India will endeavor to address the following suggestions of Displaced Brus, in consultation with Government of Mizoram:-

(i)        Sanctioning of 4 SPOs for a period of Three Years for each village where Displaced Persons are to resettle.

(ii)   Reorganizing resettlement in Villages having less than 50 Displaced Families.

(iii)  Arrangements for availability of jhum cultivation land for livelihood of Displaced Persons at par with other villagers. If a new NLUP Scheme is undertaken by Government of Mizoram and NITI, these repatriated families will be included as beneficiaries in the new scheme.

(iv)  To establish Eklauya Tribal Residential Schools in villages of Resettlement.

(v)   Resettled Families to get the benefit of Centrally Sponsored Schemes at par with other villagers.

(vi)  Provision of additional land for the houses for toilet and cattle shed, apart from 30'x40'    house site for the resettled family.

(vii) Identification of remaining Displaced Persons to be completed expeditiously.

(viii) PRC and Tribal ST Certificate to be issued to all displaced persons within one month from the date of repatriation.

(ix)  Special Development Project for Brus through Govt. of Mizoram and Ministry of DoNER.

(x)   Families which are separated from parental house to be identified and accommodated in the same village.

                   

Government of India will also endeavor as follows :

(i)    Get Biometric Aadhar Card issued to each resettled Person within one month.

(ii)   Opening of Bank accounts and updating of Ration Cards.

(iii)  Updating of Electoral Rolls before 31 August 2018.

(iv)  Conducting of a Special Socio Economic Caste Census of the repatriated Displaced Persons.

           

Government of India will constitute a Monitoring committee to Coordinate for implementation of this agreement. This Committee will consist of :

1. Special Secretary (Internal Security), MHA                : Chairperson

2. Adviser (Bru), Ministry of Home Affairs                    : Member

3. Joint Secretary (North East), MHA                               : Member

4. Principal Secretary (Home), Govt. of Mizoram          : Member

5. Principal Secretary (Home), Govt. of Tripura             : Member

6. Two representatives of MBDPF                                                : Member

7.  Bru representatives from Mizoram                              : Member

     to be nominated by Govt. of Mizoram

8. One representative from civil society                          : Member

     organization in Mizoram, to be nominated

     by Govt. of Mizoram

           

This Committee will also consider any issues that might arise related to the families which had repatriated earlier.

           

The above Agreement is signed in presence of Hon'ble Union Home Minister of India, Hon'ble Chief Minister of Mizoram and Tripura. 

 

REACTION OF NGO COORDINATION COMMITTEE ON QUADRIPARTITE AGREEMENT

            In a Press Note issued on 12.7.2018, leaders of NGO Coordination Committee (CYMA, MUP, MHIP, MZP and MSU) lauded Bru Refugee Repatriation agreement made by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and the Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) on 2.7.2018 and also appealed the Mizo public to support the agreement. Leaders of NGO Coordination Committee was optimistic that all the 1995 Electoral Roll registered Bru Refugees and their descendants lodged at various Transit Camps in Tripura will peacefully return to Mizoram and also lauded the decision to permanently close down Refugee Camps in Tripura. NGO Coordination Committee also stated that the Committee will oppose conduct of Elections or its related matters outside Mizoram.
