MIZO ZIRLAI PAWL

( MIZO STUDENTS' ASSOCIATION)

 

            Mizo Zirlai Pawl ( Mizo students' Association) is a Mizo multi-national student organization and apex students body in Mizoram state. Established on 27th October 1935 in Shillong by Mizo earlier educated students. Originally as the Lushai Students Association (LSA), this was later replaced with the “Mizo Zirlai Pawl” (Mizo Students Association) on 1st September 1946. As Mizoram State Government notified MZP raising day as 27th October, it has been observed as "Zirlaite Ni" (Students' Day) from 2008 in Mizoram. MZP is registered as SR No. 35 of 1969–70 under Indian Societies Registration Act. with its General Headquarters  in the state capital of Mizoram, Aizawl. It has 12 Headquarters inside and outside  Mizoram state, such as Churachandpur (in Manipur), Behliangchhip (in Tripura), and inside Mizoram - Serchhip,  Biate,  Champhai, Darlawn,  Kolasib,  Mamit, Zawlnuam, Saitual,Lunglei and Khawzawl. It has 50 Sub-Headquarters and numerous branches.

            The following Mizo (used as an umbrella term for all peoples who speak one of the Mizo languages) student organizations are affiliated to it:

  • Hmar Students Association (HSA)
  • Lai Students' Association (LSA)
  • Mizoram Bawm Students' Association (MBSA)
  • Pang zirlai Pawl (PZP)
  • Mara Students' Organization (MSO)
  • Ranglong Students' Union (RSU)
  • Thado-Kuki Students' Union (TKSU)
  • Siamsinpawlpi (SSPP)

            It is itself a constituent unit of North-East Students' Organisation (NESO).

            Most of College Students Union in Mizoram, Women Polytechnic, Aizawl and Mizoram University Students Council; Chairman/Vice President and General Secretary are the Ex-Officio Members in General Headquarters.

            In different parts of India, Cities and Institutions based Mizo Students Associations are also affiliated to Mizo Zirlai Pawl such as Mizo Students Union Pune (MSUP), Delhi Mizo Zirlai Pawl (DMZP) and like wise.

History:

The Lushai Students Association, previously founded in 1926 at Shillong and which had almost collapsed was revived in October 1935. Their main objectives were to look after the welfare of the Lushai Students, to prepare them for future leaders, and to preserve the Lushai traditional and cultural values. In its first General Election held on 24th October 1939, R. Buchhawna was elected as President, Lalhmuaka as General Secretary, Hrawva as Treasurer and Saptea as Student Secretary. The General Meeting at Aizawl on 1st September 1946 changed its name into Mizo Zirlai Pawl, in short MZP and shifted its base from Shillong to Aizawl.

BRIEF CHRONICLE OF MZP

 

1926

Lushai Students Association was formed in Calcutta, Gauhati and Shillong. Unfortunately, the organisation collapsed and did not continue to exist.

 

 

 

 

1935

Lushai Students Association was again established in Shillong. Pu R. Buchhawna, B.A as President, Lalhmuaka as General Secretary and Hrawva B.A, as Treasurer.

 

 

 

 

1938

Published its newspaper 'LSA CHANCHINBU' for the first time.

 

 

 

 

1939

World War II effects the association. It could not exist properly and so was its newspaper.

 

 

 

 

1945

When World War II ended, it reformed and the constitutions were ammended.

 

 

 

 

1946

Lushai Students Association changed its name into Mizo Zirlai Pawl, in short MZP and shifted its base from Shillong to Aizawl. The Magazine, ‘LSA Newsletter’ was also chanced to ‘MZP Chanchinbu.’

 

 

 

 

1959

After the shocked of Mautam (famine), MZP was reformed again. At that time, it had its office in Mizo District Council Office and in Govt. Mizo High School,Aizawl. MZP demanded Special Scholarship for Mizo Students to the Assam Government. For the first time in Mizoram and to promote Mizo literature and songs, MZP organised 'Hla Kutpui' (Song Festival) in Aizawl. Formed the committee of Mizo Vernicular.

 

 

 

 

1966

Mizo National Front uprising and Mizo Independence Movement influenced most of MZP leaders. Many leaders joined the movement which greatly weakened the organisation as a student association.

 

 

 

 

1970s

MZP became stronger and experienced rapid growth in Mizoram. Around 1000 branches in Mizoram and outside Mizoram state existed. Inside Mizoram, most of the high schools and villages run the organisation.

 

 

 

 

1973

MZP revived and re-organised Mizo Traditional festival such as 'Chapchar Kut'. This festival is one of the most valued festival which is still celebrated today .

 

 

 

 

1978

MZP Conference held at Kolasib decided to extend the term of MZP Leaders from one year to two years.

 

 

 

 

1979

MZP supported the MNF-India peace process and even submitted a memorandum to the Govt. of India regarding peace in Mizoram.

 

 

 

 

1981

Protested against the selection of non-mizos in the state quotas of Technical Courses. Protested and demanded the abolishion of Chakma District Council.

 

 

 

 

1982

MZP took an action for peace in Mizoram. The Union Territory Government blamed MZP as anti-national and BANNED the organisation. This issued shocked the MZP and it became weaken.  The organization, however, ran secretly by  few leaders.

 

 

 

 

1985

Although the new Government withdrew the banned of MZP, it could not function properly as an organization since  various students association had sprung up in Mizoram during the banned period.

 

 

 

 

1993

On 14 August, meeting of leaders of seven students association decided to disband their respective organisation and amalgamated into MZP once again and reformed it again. In September of the same year, Special Assembly ammended the constitution and elected the new leaders. The Constitution added the Affiliation rules for Mizo Tribe-based students organisation and Federal Council System. The NECC also changed the name to NESO.

 

 

 

 

1993–1995

Emphasized the theme of (Mizo hnam inpumkhatna) 'Mizo Unification'.

 

 

 

 

1994

MZP strongly against the issue to abolish  ILP.

 

 

 

 

1995

Proclaimed the Chakma Foreigner Quit Order. Most of Chakma foreigners deleted from electoral roll.

 

 

 

 

1996

Organised the issue of Chakma Foreigners. Longmarch from Aizawl to Chawngte.

 

 

 

 

1995–1998

Proposed and used the theme of (ram leh hnam humhalh) 'Mizo tribe preservation year'.

 

 

 

 

1997

For the first time, MZP General Headquarters falls into 'Adviser Rule'. Constitution amended which extended the term of elected Office Bearer to two years and the President not to be elected more than one term.

 

 

 

 

2002

Organised ZOFEST in Churachandpur for the unification amongs Zo-hnahthlak of Zofa.

 

 

 

 

2007

Demanded an apology from the President of India to the people of Mizo for the bombing of Mizoram during the MNF insurgency

 

 

 

 

2008

The Govt. of Mizoram notified MZP rising day to be observed as 'Zirlaite Ni' (Students' Day). Anniversary of Mizoram bombing day by the Indian Government during MNF Independent movement was celebrate as "Zoram Ni'.

 

 

 

 

2010

Celebrated its Platinum Jubilee.

 

 

 

 

2013

MZP main office building, 'MZP PISAPUI' was inaugurated by Mizoram Chief Minister Lal Thanhawla in Aizawl.

 

 

 

 

2014

The MZP General Assembly  held at Farkawn passed the following agendae :- 

 

(i)        Minimum qualification for Office Bearers of MZP General Headquarters is set as Graduate level and above.

(ii)       Any Government servant is barred for contesting election of leaders of MZP General Headquarters.

 (iv)     Efforts be made to conduct career guidance/awareness campaign for students in various parts of Mizoram.

(v)       Efforts be made for renovation of colleges as well as setting up of new colleges in various parts of Mizoram according to the requirements of students.

(vi)      Initiatives be taken up for setting up of ILP Checking Cell at various inter-state borders/international boundaries.

(vii)    Agree to make corrections in Political Map of Mizoram (since the actual Mizoram area is not wholly included as shown in such political maps).

(viii)   Agree to ask the Government to enhance the strength of security deployment in Mizoram border areas.

(ix)      Initiatives be taken up to identify non-Mizo merchants doing business illegally and also to take up further necessary action. 

 

 

 

 

2015

Mizo Zirlai Pawl Assembly  convened on 15.10.2015 passed the following agendae after it was thoroughly deliberated :-

o To encourage Mizo youths to clear UPSC and SSC examinations.

o Uniform distribution of teachers in Government Schools.

o Setting up of CSIR UGC NET Examination Centre in Mizoram.

o To urge the Government to make recruitment only through advertisement.

o Drive against illegal traders in Mizoram.

o Conservation and enrichment of Mizo Language.

o Conduct of Zofest in 2016.

o Shifting of Office Timing of Government offices in Mizoram. 

 

 

 

 

2016

Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) Assembly held at Kawrthah passed the following resolutions :-

(i) To take preventive measures against admission of illegal immigrant/foreigner   students in educational institutions in Mizoram.

(ii) To take steps for proper implementation of Mid-Day Meal programme in Mizoram.

(iii) To take initiave for replacing the word 'Mizo' by the word 'Lusei' in all new edition of Mizo History subject incorporated in the school syllabus, etc.

(iv) To take measures for immediate setting up of Lok Ayukta in Mizoram.

(v) MZP should take initiative for safeguarding the Mizo community from economic assimilation from non-indigenous residents of Mizoram.

 

 

 

 

 

 

2017

MZP Assembly held on 24.10.2017 passed the following agenda :-

i)   To organise ZOFEST during 2018.

ii)  To take initiative for the deportation of illegal migrants from Bangladesh.

iii) To implore the Government to be more vigilant in registration of births & deaths in Chakma inhabited areas as all the Registrars of Birth & Death are from Chakma community.

iv) To take measures for expediting the process for recruitment of Teachers.

v) To promote Zo Nationalism through Social Media.

vi) To establish Sub-Committee to monitor movement of Chakma and Bru.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Aims and Objectives :

The motto is ‘anrual hi chakna’ (Union is strength) and the theme is ‘Serve for Mizo Nation’ (Mizo zirlaite kan ram leh hnam tan). The main aims is 'to prepare all Zohnahthlâk people to be valuable citizens for the Mizo nation and Zoram ' and 'to help the government in the development of Zoram and Mizo nation'. The following are some of the main aims and objectives:

  1. to safeguard the rights and unity of all Mizo students
  2. to prepare Mizo people to become helpful citizens of Zoram
  3. to do its best to unite all Mizo people and create an independent Mizo state out of all the territories historically occupied by Mizo peoples
  4. to prevent and attack corruption in Mizoram
  5. to conserve traditional Mizo values.

 

Functions :

The association is administered by the General Headquarters. The General Headquarters is formed by 6 (six) elected Office Bearers. The Office Bearers were elected for 2 (two) years by Electoral College after every 2 (two) years who then appointed not more than 50 (fifty) Executive Committee Members for a term of 1 (one) year. 10 (Ten) appointed Secretaries were selected from the Executive Committee Members and they in-charge of various posts apart from advising and assisting the Elected Office Bearers.

            The Executive Committee appoints Advisers and NESO Council members for a term of 1 (one) year. Various Sub-Committees and Clubs are also formed in the General Headquarters.

            MZP Assembly is the top authority in the association, and Federal Council Sitting is the second highest authority to rule the association.
