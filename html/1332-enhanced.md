****THE BIRTH OF BRU MILITANT GROUP****

****BNLF: The Brus (otherwise Riangs) are the Nomadic tribe inhabiting the area scattered in the western belt of Mizoram, bordering Bangladesh and Tripura State. As far as their history is concerned, they entered into Mizoram since 1940's moving from one place to another in search of jhum cultivation.****

****The on-going episode of Bru crisis which has given rise to by the killing of Pu Lalzawmliana, Forest Game Watcher at Persang village in Dampa Forest Sanctuary on 21.10.1997 has had a long impact on social, economic and political condition of the Brus. He was shot dead by Bru militants called the Bru National Liberation Front(BNLF), who snatched away .22 Rifles and WT Set Pu Lalzawmliana had carried. The news of the killing of Pu Lalzawmliana by BNLF shook and engulfed Mizoram and ignited the already volatile atmosphere prevailing in the area due to the demand for Autonomous District Council for the Brus. As a result of this brutal killing, tension began to envelope and escalate in the surrounding areas, which further snowballed into burning down of Bru houses at Tuipuibari village by the mob on 24.10.1997 evening itself. In the meantime, the BNLF had also threatened the Bru Community to leave Mizoram for their operational convenience. The Bru populace had little choice in the process and thousand of them fled to Tripura State. This was followed by massive burning down of Bru houses, for which the deployment of Police and BSF was re-quisitioned to prevent the Brus from leaving Mizoram and the mob setting Bru houses on fire, but in vain.****

**This incident (killing of Lalzawmliana by Bru militants, BNLF) has resulted in the large scale migration of Brus from Mizoram to Tripura for fear of possible repermission. Following this, there are 31919 Bru refugees presently living in Tripura in the following 6(six) Refugee camps:**

**1) Naisingpara 2) Hazachera**

**3) Ashapara 4) Kaiskau**

**5) Khakchang 6) Hamsa**

****Contrary to the members of Bru refugees as furnished by Tripura State as mentioned above, so far only 10688 Bru refugees have been identified to be genuine Brus of Mizoram by Deputy Commissioner, Aizawl (as on May 2001). At the same time, 1617 Bru refugees have already returned to Mizoram(as on October 2000) and certain number of Bru refugee families have also returned to Mizoram on their own will(As reported by Bru VCP of Tuirum and Tuipuibari on 3.5.2000).****

****Later on, Govt. of Mizoram initiated peace talk with leaders of BNLF w.e.f. 7.9.2001 and after 12 rounds of peace talk between government of Mizoram and BNLF, MOU was signed between BNLF and Govt. of Mizoram on 26.4.2005. And in pursuance of MOU signed on 26.4.2005, the 195 strong BNLF cadres and their family members have been temporarily sheltered at Sidon Transit Camp, Tuipuibari. Altogether a total members of 950 including all BNLF cadres and families have been provided free ration at the rate of Rs.10/- per adult and Rs.5/- per minor per day.****

**Rehabilitation and Housing Assistance: Govt. of Mizoram had sanctioned Rs. 50,000/- and Rs. 30,000/- as rehabilitation package and Housing Assistance respectively for each BNLF cadre from the fund received from central government.**

****Ration Card, etc: These ex-BNLF cadres were issued ration card in their respective villages and also enrolled in the electoral roll and also issued Electoral Photo Identity Card (EPIC) excepting those resettled in Tumpanglui. Schools under SSA Scheme were also opened particularly in Pathiantlang and Tumpanglui.****

**BLFM: The Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram (BLFM) was formed in December 2001 at Naisingpara refugee camp reportedly at the instance of NLFT(BM) under the leadership of Vanlalliana, founder president of BNLF but overthrown by his men.**

**The first ever major activity launched by BLFM was kidnapping of one Maxi Cab driver Lalfamkima on 1.4.2003 for ransom. With the signing of memorandum of understanding(MOU) between BNLF and Govt. of Mizoram, the BLFM cadres were also expected to come overground and join the mainstream. Contrary to his hope, BLFM cadres further intensified their activities and resorted to forcible collection of donations from innocent civilians.**

**Surrendering of BLFM Sprinter Group: A sprinter group of BLFM headed by A. Zoramthanga, former vice president and Lalrinkima, General Secretary with cadres numbering 38 members with weapons surrendered to Govt. of Mizoram on 16.3.2006 followed by 4 cadres on 6.7.2006 and another 11 cadres on 17.8.2006. Altogether 53 cadres of a factional group of BLFM headed by A. Zoramthanga surrendered before Govt. of Mizoram by the task of September 2006. Meanwhile BLFM headed by Vanlalliana continue to remain operational, functional and active.**

**Rehabilitation and resettlement: Govt. of Mizoram rehabilitated this group with cast assistance and housing assistance at pas with the BNLF surrenderees. They were resettled at Pangbalkawn village. However, most of them have returned to refugees camps in Tripura, except for 6(six) of them who resettled at Chhimluang, Saihapui and Pangbalkawn itself.**

**Arrest of President etc: In the aftermath of the surrender of 38 BLFM cadres, four top brass leaders viz. 1) Vanlalliana, President 2) Vanrama, Vice President 3) Romawia Meska, COAS and 4) Lallawma, SS Capt. were arrested on 20.4.2006 in a joint operation launched by Mizoram Police SB and Assam Rifles, Kanchanpur, code name operation Black Hound which dealt a severe blow to the BLFM.**

****Surrender of 804 Strong Cadres of BLFM: Due to the arrest of their top leaders and surrender of 53 cadres of BLFM earlier, the remaining 804 strong members of BLFM have surrendered to the Govt. unconditionally and laid down their arms on 26.10.2006 and were temporarily sheltered in two transit camp - Damparengpui and Tuipuibari. Free ration have been provided under Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India.****

**Identification of BLFM surrenderees: Identification of bonafide Mizoram Bru was felt necessary for the resettlement of BLFM surrenderees in Mizoram, Govt. officials and NGO's have been sent to their respective transit camp and out of 804 BLFM cadres, 731 cadres were identified as genuine Mizoram Brus. ******Resettlement: Govt. of Mizoram took all possible steps to resettle the BLFM surrenderees identified as a bonafide resident of Mizoram with their family members at the places selected by concerned District Authorities. Housing Assistance to the tune of Rs. 30,000/- each was disbursed to them in two installments. However, the BLFM surrenderee were reluctant to be resettled unless they were given -**

**1) Rs.50,000/- each as Rehabilitation Package.**

**2) Unconditional release of their jailed leaders.**

**3) Resettlement in one or two places only in Mamit District.**

**4) Withdrawal of pending case against BLFM members in other States.**

**5) Hike of transportation cost from Rs. 2,000/- to Rs.3,000/-and construction of temporary hut from Rs. 3,000/- to Rs.5,000/-.**

****6) One year free ration after resettlement.****

**7) Five bundles of GCI Sheets.**

**8) Immediate solution of Bru issues.**

****Inspite of the above demand, Govt. of Mizoram has been taking various steps for permanent settlement of BLFM surrenderees. The BLFM surrenderees in the transit camp were issued Job Card under NREGS scheme as far as practicable.****

****RECENT ACTIVITIES OF THE SO CALLED BRU MILITANT GROUP****

****LED BY SS SGT. MOIKAU****

**SS Sgt. Moikau who became very infamous for his recent involvement in various cases of abduction, kidnapping and murder has been found out to be the kingpin in the following events:**

**i) He was responsible for the killing of Thankima (the main suspect of the murder of Zarzokima) on 31.3.2010 at Khakchangpara Refugee Camp.**

**ii) On 10.4.2010 at around 6:30 pm, Bru Armed gang suspected to be Moikau and his party abducted one, Roton Joy@ Tiger of Naisingpara Refugee Camp and murdered him at the outskirt of Anonda village. His dead body was recovered at around 2:00 pm on 11.4.2010(Sunday).**

**iii) From reliable information received, the following persons are being targeted by Bru militant led by SS Sgt. Moikau:-**

**a) MS. John@ Vanlalzawna, Higher Secondary School teacher, Naisingpara.**

**b) Elvis Chorkhu, President MBDPF(Mizoram Bru Displaced People Forum).**

**iv) One, SS Lt. Henry, Deputy Commander in Chief has instructed SS Sgt. Moikau to take action against one, Lalhmingthanga, former SS Army Chief of BLFM on the suspicion that, Lalhmingthanga is the agent of Mizoram CID.**

**v) A group of militants led by SS Sgt. Moikau has decided to collect Rs.20/- from each household of Nigli as donation to meet the expenses incurred for organizing the procession in protest against the arrest of 5(five) persons on suspicion ground by Assam Police.**

**vi) Reliable information received indicated that Bru militant under the command of SS Sgt. Moikau had abducted an unidentified non-Mizo recently in Kutkuthi - Pasakhauma (Gurguthi) area and demanded Rs.5/- lakhs ransom for his release. Later, the militant released the abductee after payment of about Rs. 1/- lakh ransom. It is learnt that SS Lt. Thomas, Dy. Commander in Chief had been monitoring their activities from Kaisakau Refugee Camp.**

**SS Sgt. Moikau has left no stone unturned to derail the repatriation process of Bru refugees who fled to Tripura from Mizoram in the aftermath of the killing of Pu Lalzawmliana, Forest Game Watcher at Persang (Dampa Rengpui reserve) on 21.10.1997.**

****KILLING OF ZARZOKIMA(17) AT BUNGTHUAM****

****On 13.11.2009 @ 10:00 am, one, Zarzokima (17) S/o Lalngilneia of Bungthuam was shot dead by Bru militant. The culprit left a note in which they claimed themselves as Bru Revolutionary Union (BRU) and they didn't want their repatriation without proper agreement and would fight the State Govt. for their rights.****

**As a result of this incident, Mizo community around this village had set on fire several Bru houses in several Bru inhabited villages. Around 329 houses were reported to have been burnt down. Many had fled to Tripura. So far, around 263 families have been repatriated from Tripura (Refugee Camp) to Mizoram as on today.**
