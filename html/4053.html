  1. Brief of the cases and comments :

 

  1. Sangau PS Case No.3/24 dt.05.02.2024 u/s 4 of Explosives Substances Act r/w  34 IPC and 14 Foreigners Act:

 

  (a) Gist of the case: On dt.05.02.2024, 42nd Assam Rifles, Sangau post intercepted one Bolero B/R No. MZ01 B 6369 driven by Siamthanga (50) S/o Chhanerha, Sentetfiang village, Lawngtlai District along with his wife Chimengi (40) near Pangkhua village, Lawngtlai District from where they seized  6 Boxes (1200 nos.) of gelatine sticks, 1800 nos. – detonators and 3750 mtrs detonating fuse which were handed over to Sangau PS.  

These items were transported from Sentetfiang village at the instance of one Suichung @ Pasuia, Salen village, Mynamar. It was found that the explosives items were purchased by Suichung @ Pasuia and TunTun(32) S/o Chhanawl, both belonging to Salen village, Myanmar from Chawnpui Veng, Aizawl.

            It was further revealed that the contraband items were purchased from Pi Lalsangpuii w/o Lawmzuala of Chawnpui, Aizawl and the couple were later arrested.

During investigation, the following persons involved in the case were   arrested and remanded in Judicial custody.

 

  1. Siamthanga(50) h/o Amengi, Sentetfiang, Lawngtlai District
  2. Amengi(40) w/o Siamthanga, Sentetfiang, Lawngtlai District
  3. Suichung(50) @ Pasuia s/o Hrangthanga, Salen village, Myanmar
  4. Tun Tun(32) s/o Chhanawl, Salen village, Myanmar
  5. Bawihleiuka(28) s/o Siamthanga, Sentetfiang, Lawngtlai District
  6. Lalsangpuii(60) w/o Lawmzuala of Chawnpui, Aizawl
  7. Lawmzuala (58) S/o Lalzama of Chawnpui, Aizawl

 

      Chargesheet was submitted in this case vide Sangau PS CS No.4/24 Dt.25.03.2024.

 

(b) Suspected origin and destination : Upon enquiring into the matter, it has come to light that the explosives were bought from M/S Trustwell, Thakthing Veng, Aizawl, which is the sole explosives dealer in Mizoram which is licensed to possess as well as sale of explosives by Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisations (PESO) and the explosives are destined for Myanmar.

 

  1.  Bawngkawn PS Case No.53/24 dt.15.02.2024 u/s 5(a) of the Explosives   Substances Act, 1908:

 

(a) Gist of the case: On dt.15.02.2024, Assam Rifles had seized 165 boxes (16500 nos.) of detonators and safety fuse of 993 cm from the possession of Lalrunmawia (30) s/o Seibula, Durtlang Mual Veng, Aizawl at Bangkawn Sairang Road and then handed over to Bawngkawn Police Station. The accused Lalrunmawia is a driver by profession who handles his own vehicle-Tata Yodha (Pick-up) B/R No. MZ 01 Y 6016.

      Chargesheet was submitted in this case vide BKN PS CS No.228/24 Dt.22.10.2024.

 

 

(b) Suspected origin and destination : Interrogation report of the accused reveals that on dt.15.02.2024 he had received phone call from an unknown number who asked him to collect a certain commodity from Phulpui/Sateek village. On arriving Phulpui/Sateek village, one stranger loaded 2 packets of certain commodity at the back seat of the vehicle and instructed him to deliver to Falkland-Zemabawk junction where it would be pickup by a 3rd party. He then headed towards Aizawl, on reaching Falkland-Zemabawk junction, no 3rd party was available, he then proceeded to pickup stand, Bawngkawn (Sairang road), Aizawl where he was apprehended by Assam Rifles team and was later produced to Bawngkawn-PS.

            It is pertinent to be mentioned that despite conducting joint interrogation by various agencies and rigorous investigation by the Police, the source of explosives nor the destination as well as the identities of the persons involved could not be sorted out as of now.

  1. Ngopa PS case No.1/2024 dt.26.02.2024 U/S 5 Explosive Substances Act:

  1. Kulikawn PS Case No. 41/24 dt.01.05.2024 U/S 5 (a) Explosives Substances Act:

 

(a) Gist of the case: On 01.05.2024, W/FIR submitted by Naib Subedar Chamanlal, 2 Assam rifles, Aizawl that on the same night @ 8:00 pm, as per their inputs received, strongly suspecting transportation of war like stores,  a Mobile Vehicle Check Post was temporarily established by the Assam Rifle Quick Reaction Team  near Trinity Hospital, Aizawl intercepted one Maruti Eco Van BR/No. MZ-01/AA-0517, checked and recovered suspected gelatine sticks and special ordinary detonators inside the vehicle and detained the driver Lalfela (45) s/o Vanneia of Vaivakawn Zohnuai.

             Later the Assam Rifles informed the matter to Kulikawn PS regarding the recovery of the explosives, Kulikawn Police party went to the spot and seized 1622 nos. of gelatine sticks and 200 nos. of detonators total v/o Rs. 47600/-  from the illegal possession of the  vehicle driver Lalfela (45) s/o Vanneia of Vaivakawn Zohnuai.

 

 

Investigation reveals that Lalfela was approached through whatsapp by one who called himself Jacob and claimed himself to be a resident of Lunglei. Jacob requested him to collect 10 boxes of gelatine (1 box @ Rs 8000) and 2 boxes of detonators (@ Rs 3500 per box). Lalfela then proceeded to Sakawrtuichhun and procured 8 boxes of gelatine, 2 boxes of detonators and another 218 gelatine sticks. Lalfela while intending to hand him the said explosives at the place ie. Near Trinity Hospital, Melthum, he was arrested by Assam Rifles personnel and detained him. Later, Kulikawn PS was informed and arrived at the spot and seized the explosives items.  

            The accused driver Lalfela was arrested and produced to Judicial custody.

            Case is lying pending for awaiting prosecution sanction.

 

  (b) Suspected origin and destination: Interrogation of Lalfela revealed that he had procured the seized items from Rammawii, Sakawrtuichhun. Rammawii had leased the stone quarry of Pu Antony Chhuanvawra H/o Margaret Zohmingthangi, Chawlhhmun at Sakawrtuichhun. Rammawii do not possess explosive purchase permit to be used in their quarry but, Pu Antony Chhuanvawra, who possessed explosive purchase license  provided them 2 boxes of explosives per month for their quarrying purposes. These explosives stored by Rammawii was sold to her acquaintance Lalfela who had urged her to let him procure.

  1. Action taken by Mizoram Police to curb the menace of smuggling of explosives and detonator in Mizoram:

 

The following are the follow up action taken by Mizoram Police to curb the menace of smuggling of explosives and detonator in Mizoram:

 

(1)   Field I/C, CID (SB) conducted detail enquiry into the seizures of explosives in Mizoram and conducted joint interrogation of the arrested accused persons to determine the forward/backward linkages and identification of origin and destination.

 

(2)  On 30th May 2024, a meeting was conducted for all Arms Dealers and Explosive Magazines License Holders of Aizawl District at DGP Conference Hall, PHQ, Aizawl under the Chairmanship of Pu Lalbiakthanga Khiangte, IGP (L & O) which was attended by senior Police Officers of Mizoram – DGP (M), DIGP (NR) and DIGP (ER) and 35 members of Arms and Explosive License holders.

 

         The meeting highlighted the responsibilities of the dealers/license owners and the consequences they could face if found to be involved in illegal trade. It also highlighted the  necessity for collaborative efforts between Arms dealers, Explosive magazine license holders and Police in curbing the illegal possession and pilferage of arms and explosives which the dealers expressed their willingness to cooperate with Mizoram Police.

 

 (3)   Careful analysis of CDR’s of accused/persons involved in the illegal trade of explosives/detonators is being conducted by CID (SB) which contributes a major role in curbing and identification of forward/backward linkage.
