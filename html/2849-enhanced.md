****SPECIAL REPORT****

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/635

Dated: 26th August, 2019.

 DC to DC Meeting was held between DC Kolasib and DC Hailakandi today, i.e. 26.8.2019 @ 11:00 a.m at PWD IB, Bairabi, Kolasib District, Mizoram. The meeting was attended by SP Kolasib and SP Hailakandi, DFO Kolasib and DFO Hailakandi, a total of 12 (twelve) officials of both Kolasib and Hailakandi districts. The meeting discussed 3 (three) mains issues concerning both the districts :-

1. Inner Line Pass.
2. Free Movement of local farmers.
3. Reconstruction of Masjid at Zophai/Kachurtol.

 After careful deliberations, the meeting resolved the following points :-

1. The committee cannot come to conclusion as to whether the Masjid construction has to be stopped or removed as both the parties have their own concerns. However, it was decided that the matter be taken up at the Government level. In the meantime, construction work of permanent nature is to be discontinued/not taken up until decision is taken at the Government level.
2. In case any new activity is proposed to be taken up in the disputed area, both the districts should be informed prior consent and no action is to be taken without the consent of both the districts.
3. Coordination Committee comprising of members from both the districts be constituted involving officials including Panchayat/Village Council Members and NGOs.
4. Free movement of local farmers in the disputed area should be ensured by both the districts.
5. The committee agreed to meet after 3 (three) months to review the progress of the decision arrived at today’s meeting.
6. Both parties agreed to proceed to visit the bomb blast site after the meeting.

 After the meeting, officials of both sides visited Masjid reconstruction site wherein it was reiterated by Kolasib District officials that reconstruction of Masjid is a clear violation of an agreement to maintain Status Quo in the disputed area. DC, Hailakandi is however reported to have stated that reconstruction of Masjid is not a violation of Status Quo as the Masjid has always been standing in the location even during the visit of Jt.Secretary, MHA(NE), Govt. of India and that it is neither erection of new structure, nor venturing into new areas to build the Masjid and therefore, not a violation of Status Quo.

 Situation under close watch.
