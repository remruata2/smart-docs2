SPECIAL REPORT

 

No. MSB/CB/SR/2021/699

Dated: 15th Sept., 2021

 

          As per input, there has been a large-scale influx of Myanmar refugees into Mizoram after the recent intense fighting between the Chinland Defense Force (CDF) comprising of armed civilians led by Chin National Army (CNA) and Myanmar army at Lungler village, Chin State, Myanmar. Around 400 (four hundred) armed CDF members attacked Myanmar army camp (Lungler Camp manned by 266 Bn. Myanmar Army) on the early morning of 9.9.2021 @7:00 a.m. The army sent helicopters and jet fighters and launched counter-attack. The aerial attacks could be seen with naked eyes from Thingsai village, Mizoram, located at around 12 (twelve) kms from the Indo-Myanmar border. CDF again attacked the camp on 10.9.2021 @8 :00 p.m. and captured the camp. 1 (one) Myanmar army personnel was killed in the attack and 12 (twelve) were captured and they are presently lodged at Camp Victoria (CNF Camp), Myanmar. 8 (eight) nos. of assault rifles, 6 nos. of G-3 automatic rifle, 1 no. of Uzi sub-machine gun, 1 no. of 70mm Mortar, 2 Pistols,
3 Shotguns and huge cache of ammunitions were seized by the CDF. The camp was destroyed and burnt to the ground and 6 (six) CDF members also suffered physical injuries. Lungler villagers and CDF members completely deserted Lungler village after the incident was over.

 

          As per input, 1548 (one thousand five hundred forty eight) Myanmar refugees entered into Mizoram in the aftermath of the above incident, i.e., w.e.f., 10.9.2021, which brings the total number of Myanmar refugees presently staying in Mizoram to 11035 (eleven thousand and thirty five). The refugees are from Lungler and its nearby villages, i.e., Dawn, Ralkel & Bungkhua. They are mainly accommodated at refugee camps constructed by the villagers, available community halls, and other empty buildings.
