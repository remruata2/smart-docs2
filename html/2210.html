Brief history :

 

1. Dt.20.4.2016 : Darlawn District Demand Committee (DDDC) issued a Press Release reiterating their commitment on their demand, i.e. creation of Darlawn District. DDDC demanded the Government to declare Darlawn District within 2016.

 

2. Dt.4.7.2016 : Darlawn District Demand Committee (DDDC) sent a letter to the Hon'ble Chief Minister of Mizoram vide letter No. 101/DDDC-DLN/2016/053 dated 4th July, 2016 in which they conveyed the need for the creation of Administrative District at Darlawn. They also expressed their optimism for the declaration of Darlawn Administrative District through the District Re-organisation Committee which was set up by the Government.

            The meeting decided to discuss their demand with the Hon'ble Chief Minister of Mizoram and in this regard.

 

4. Dt.22.8.2017 : Darlawn District Demand Committee (DDDC) met Pu Lal Thanhawla, Hon'ble Chief Minister of Mizoram on 22.8.2017 with regard to Darlawn District Demand. After the meeting, leaders of DDDC  hold a Press Conference at Aizawl Press Club in which they highlighted the reasons and benefits for setting up of Darlawn district.

 

5. Dt 17.4.2018 : A procession organized by Darlawn District Demand Committee (DDDC) was held at Darlawn on 17.4.2018 @ 10:30 a.m. About 1,000 (one thousand) people participated and all Government offices, Educational Institutions and Shops remained closed during the procession.

The processionists adopted resolutions demanding the Government to immediately take steps to create a new Administrative District for the Northern Hill area which they claimed is far lagging behind in terms of developments compared to other parts of Mizoram, and that the 41 (forty one) villages within their demand area should not be divided or included in other districts. The procession was peaceful and there was no untoward incident during the procession.
