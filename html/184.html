ASSESSMENT OF THREAT PERCEPTION IN RESPECT OF HON’BLE SPEAKER, MIZORAM LEGISLATIVE ASSEMBLY, HON’BLE HOME MINISTER AND COUNCIL OF MINISTERS.

 

Sl. No.

Name & Designation

Assessment of Threat Perception.

1.

Pu Hiphei

Hon’ble Speaker

No immediate threat has not been perceived till date. Pu Hiphei is also comfortable with the present level of security provided to him. However, the security of Hon’ble Speaker needs to be strengthened during his visit to his constituency as certain insurgent outfits of Myanmar origin occasionally enter some part of his constituency.

2.

Pu R.Lalzirliana

Hon’ble Home Minister

The Hon’ble Home Minister who is presently residing at Armed Veng locality did not have any known coercion or imminent threat from any person. It is also affirmed that in spite of dealing with all internal dilemmas of the state, it has been legitimately opinioned that major NGOs of Mizoram such as MZP, MSU, CYMA, ZDU, etc are by and large contented with his administrations at present. Duty performance of each House guards and PSOs are also found dependable.

 

3.

Pu R. Romawia, Hon’ble Minister

With three (3) Personal Security Officers (PSOs) and proper House guards, security arrangement for the moment is found satisfactory in respect to Pu R. Romawia. Nevertheless, it is opinioned that improvements in a way of smartness, general conduct and efficiency of PSOs are still required.

 

 4.

Pu Lalsawta, Hon’ble Minister

Pu Lalsawta, Hon’ble Minister who is presently residing at Mission Vengthlang, Aizawl and who is attending his office at Govt. Secretariat, Aizawl is not having any illegitimate pressure from any individual or group. His personal guards and House guards are found to be efficient in performing their duties. Present Security arrangement has been observed to be sufficient for the time being.

 

 5.

Pu H. Rohluna, Hon’ble Minister

Presently, three (3) Personal Security Officers are allotted to him. His resident which located at Ramthar Veng is also continuously guarded by static armed guard. Although his security arrangement has been found acceptable for his movement inside the city area, it is felt that intensification of security arrangement is necessary during visits to his constituency since some part of the east side of the constituency which is bordering Myanmar falls within the operational area of Zomi Revolutionary Army, an underground militant group of Myanmar origin.

 

 

 6.

Pu Zodintluanga, Hon’ble Minister

Pu Zodintluanga currently having three (3)PSOs and armed guards is not under any threat and his security arrangement has been found sufficient for the time being. However, it is suggested that the general conduct and efficiency of PSOs be improved. Moreover, small arms i.e. Pistols/Revolvers alloted to PSO were reportedly old and unreliable. As a result, inspections of such arms were conducted and the report about which was found correct. Hence, it is suggested that dependable arms be allotted to the PSOs.

 

 7.

Pu John Rotluangliana, Hon’ble Minister

Pu John Rotluangliana, Hon’ble Minister who was elected from Mamit Constituency do not have any known direct or indirect threat at present. He is presently having three (3) PSOs who are following a daily duty schedule from 9:00 Am to 6:00 Pm. His resident is also guarded by static armed guard continuously. In his case, it is desirable and recommended the PSOs to perform their duty even at night and be available for the Minister. It is also highly recommended to strengthen the security of the Minister when he is visiting his constituency since some part of which are pronounced sensitive areas.

 

 8.

Pu PC Lalthanliana, Hon’ble Minister

With his armed house guard including three (3) dynamic PSOs, Pu PC Lalthanliana’s security arrangement was found fitting. Moreover terrorization or intimidation of any kind has never been received by Pu PC Lalthanliana till date. Nonetheless, it is proposed that visitors at his office and residence be more carefully checked before entering the Minister’s room.

 

 9.

Pu Lal Thanzara, Hon’ble Minister

He is provided with armed house guards and three (3) PSOs who are staying with him during office hour i.e. 9:00 Am – 5:00 Pm including holiday. Armed house guards are on duty around the clock while PSOs performed their duty only during office hour which is due to inconveniency of accommodation/quarters for them. Armed house guard as well as PSOs are believed to be more efficient if quarters or accommodation is provided.

 

 10.

Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, Hon’ble Minister

Till today, threat has not been received by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, Hon’ble Minister. Through night and day his resident is guarded by armed police and he is as provided with three (3) PSOs. But since the PSOs are usually do not turn up at the Minister’s resident while he is on tour, it has become highly debatable whether the PSOs will stay at Minister’s resident to guard his family members while he is on tour. It is further suggested that continuous protection towards the Minister must be carried out by the PSOs even on Sunday morning i.e. the time in which the PSOs are reportedly leaving the Minister unattended.

 11.

Pu

C. Ngunlianchunga, Hon’ble Minister

Pu C. Ngunlianchunga is currently provided with three (3) PSOs and static House Guard. The threat level perceived with regard to the Minister is minimal. The PSOs are constantly guarding the Minister i.e. through night and day, atleast one PSO always stays at his resident even when he is on tour. At present, his security arrangement has been found satisfactory.

 

12.

DR. B.D. Chakma, Hon’ble Minister

Dr.BD Chakma has never encountered with threat intimation. His security arrangement which comprises of armed house guards and three (3) PSOs is found sufficient for the time being. It is recommended that the Minister's guard be strengthened when he is in and around Chawngte due to the recent outbreak of violence in Chawngte.

 

 

Note :

 

In general, the security conditions and arrangement towards the above mentioned Hon’ble Speakers, Hon’ble Home Minister and other Council of Ministers are found to be satisfactory. Since direct/indirect threat and intimidation had never been heard or received by anyone of them, it is felt unnecessary to bring up new level of security arrangement in terms of armed house guard and PSOs. But as indicated above, some PSOs guarded the officials round the clock while some PSOs guarded them only during office hours. In some cases, PSOs left the officials every Sunday morning leaving them unprotected and some PSOs never show up at the official’s resident while the official is on tour. It was then learnt that such dissimilar pattern of duty deployment had caused unspoken problems towards the officials also. It is hence highly recommended that a clear-cut Duty guidelines/protocols for the PSOs be made/provided, which may as well contain general rules of conducts (i.e. Dos and Don’ts for PSOs) so as to maintain similar pattern of duty for every PSOs who are protecting officials.

 

In regard to the VIPs’ General Security in and around their office i.e. Govt. Secretariat Complex, Aizawl, it is felt that the armed guard sentry post should be increased by erecting at least three more sentry posts at suitable corners outside the office which now have only one sentry gate at the main-gate. If possible, allotment of one platoon of armed police for this purpose is suggested. It was also learnt that there are four (4) exits at the secretariat building’s ground floor each of which are equipped with DFMD, but as a matter of fact, only the main entrance i.e. the main door is guarded with police personnel, the other three minor exits are not specifically guarded. It is strongly suggested that static armed guard as well as security police personnel be posted at each of three unguarded exit.

 

The vehicles parking arrangement at the New Secretariat Complex is also highly unsystematic and could become huge trouble in case of emergency situation. It is hence suggested that more systematic arrangement of vehicles parking and entry at the main entrance be chalked out. Smooth flow of traffic should also be kept which is necessary for swift and speedy evacuation of the building’s premises in an emergency situation.
