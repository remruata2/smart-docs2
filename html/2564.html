9.10.2020(SR):

1.         Information has been received that Babujoy, Vice President, SONGRONGMA (Conglomerate of various Bru NGOs) held a Press Conference via YouTube through KOKTRIPURA NORTH-EAST INDIA channel on Dt. 9.10.2020, in which he stated their intention to construct Mandir (Sibraikhong Temple) at Thaidawr Tlang, near Phuldungsei Village, Mamit District. The place is believed to be within Mizoram side. He further mentioned that community work which was called to be organised on 15th and 16th October, 2020 for various Tripura Bru NGOs, Chakma NGO, Mog Tribe was postponed to 19th and 20th October, 2020 due to its clash with BJP Government programme.

            He further stated that all those who intend to participate in the said community work should first gather at Kanchanpur, North Tripura District and briefing on their course of action will be conducted.

            As per input, upon hearing the said news Mizo inhabitants of Zampui Hills, Tripura raised their opposition. Upon their enquiry, SDM, Kanchanpur informed Leaders of Mizo Convention that he is not aware of the said intended action nor gave any permission for their community work.

            Further, Mizo Convention intends to demonstrate their opposition, if SONGRONGMA continue to conduct the said community work. In this regard, they plan to convene a joint meeting with Tripura Group YMA in due course. 

2.         Information has also been received that one farm house belonging to Pu John Zolawma of Thinghlun, located at Zaite Road Assam Side around 150 meters from Tlangte Lui (state boundary river) was allegedly set ablaze by Assam Forest Officials on Dt. 9.10.2020 @ around 2:40 p.m., and due to this the said farm house was burnt down to ashes.

It also stated that Ministry of Home Affairs sent reminder letter to Government of Mizoram on 26.11.2018 and 20.2.2019 but till date no comment has been made.  It further stated that remaining silent despite Central Government initiative on the matter cannot be understood and the only way to resolve Assam-Mizoram Border dispute through a peaceful means is to follow the suggestions made in their memorandum. It also stated that after careful deliberation they urged Mizoram government to immediately submit comment to Union Home Ministry. They further urged Mizoram government to submit comment between Dt. 14.10.2020 @ 12:00 midnight and Dt. 21.10.2020 @ 12:00 midnight failing which they intend to resort to state wide indefinite Total Bandh.

            Further, JAC on Inner Line Forest Demand Committee submitted a written petition to Chief Secretary on Dt. 14.10.020 with a request to submit comment to Union Home Ministry regarding their memorandum submitted to Prime Minister between Dt. 14.10.2020 @ 12:00 midnight and Dt. 21.10.2020 @ 12:00 midnight, failing which they intend to organise state wide indefinite Total Bandh.

28.10.2020(SR): In continuation of our Special Reports vide No. MSB/CB/SR/2020/559      Dt. 9.10.2020 and No. MSB/CB/SR/2020/569 Dt. 14.10.2020, Information has been received that Shri. Mevar Kumar Jamatia, Tripura Forest Minister; District Magistrate, North Tripura; SP, North Tripura; SDM, Kanchanpur; SDPO, Kanchanpur; DFO, Kanchanpur; SDFO, Kanchanpur and Leaders of Bru Hindu religious community visited Thaidawr Tlang, under Phuldungsei VC, Mamit District on 26.10.2020 @ 2:00 p.m. Upon learning their visit, leaders of Mizo Convention of Zampui Hills, Tripura rushed to Thaidawr Tlang, but the Minister and party already left when they reached the area. During their visit, the Minister and party performed religious rites at the area.

                        Further, around 50 (fifty) Tripura State Rifles (TSR) personnel are deployed at Thaidawr Tlang and they are stationed at old BSNL Office and PRJB School at Phuldungsei (both buildings are within Mizoram side). Earlier, they were performing duty at the approach road of Thaidawr Tlang, however, on Dt. 26.10.2020, around 20 (twenty) TSR personnel were deployed at Thaidawr Tlang/Hill peak.  

                        In this regard, Mizo Students’ Union (MSU) General Headquarters issued a press release on Dt. 27.10.2020 which condemned the visit and performing of religious rites at Thaidawr Tlang by Tripura Forest Minister and party. It further urged the Government to deploy Mizoram Police at the said area so as to indicate the area as falling within its territorial jurisdiction.

                        Yesterday i.e., 27.10.2020 @ 3:00 p.m. Post Commander, New Kawnpui BOP, 5th IR Battalion and party, who intended to conduct spot verification at Thaidawr Tlang as per instruction of SP, Mamit were stopped by TSR personnel on their way to Thaidawr Tlang and they then returned. TSR personnel on duty told them that visit of Thaidawr Tlang is not allowed without prior permission from Tripura Government.

                        Yesterday evening i.e., 27.10.2020 @ 7:00 p.m. Leaders of Phuldungsei Village, Mamit District organised public meeting at Community Hall, Phuldungsei.           Pu Sainghinglova Sailo, VCP, Phuldungsei presided the meeting and more than 140 (one hundred forty) members were present. During the meeting, they discussed Tripura Forest Minister and party visit of Thaidawr Tlang yesterday and their future course of actions. After long deliberation, the meeting concludes the following:

            (i)        To oppose any religious group intention for construction of Mandir/Temple at Thaidawr Tlang.

            (ii)       Not to circulate any unreliable news regarding Thaidawr Tlang through Media/WhatsApp etc.

            (iii)     Decided to wait both the State Governments’ action as Tripura Forest Minister during his short interaction with leaders of YMA at Vanghmun stated that it is better to constitute a joint verification team from both the State Governments and to set up a negotiation table.

            (iv)      To constitute a Peace Committee consisting of representatives from both Mizo and Bru communities and necessary follow up action in this regard will be taken up by Phuldungsei VC.

                        Input received indicated that student bodies like MZP and MSU may pressurise the Government to take proactive steps and to deploy a good number of Mizoram Police in the disputed area.

Lalsangzuala and Hmangaihsanga who performed border duty at Saranathi kai, near Zawlpui Village, Mamit District were physically assaulted by around 30 (thirty) residents of Hamsapara, Tripura and took away 1 (one) Airgun and

Upon learning the incident, around 100 (one hundred) residents of Zawlnuam gathered at Soranathi kai (Mizoram-Tripura border) and detained 11 (eleven) Brus who already entered Mizoram side earlier today.

            In connection with the above incident, Zawlnuam Vengpui JAC submitted FIR at Kanhmun Police Station today afternoon @ 2:00 p.m.  Mizoram Police seized the items belonging to Zawlnuam JAC took away by Bru miscreants after contacting Tripura Police today's evening @ 4:40 p.m. and subsequently released 11 (eleven) Bru detained by residents of Zawlnuam. The crowd also dispersed today's evening at 5:00 p.m.

15.2.2021(DSI):

            Information has been received that Tripura Forest Department has taken initiative for construction of Forest Check Post at Khantlang Junction (Mizoram side), Mizoram-Tripura border which is around 1 km towards east from New Kawnpui BOP on 11.2.2021. In this regard, VC Khantlang and Young Chakma Association (YCA) submitted a written petition to Mizoram Home Minister and Forest Minister on 13.2.2021 which stated their opposition for construction of Tripura Forest Check Post at Khantlang Junction as the area fell under Khantlang VC jurisdiction and requested to take necessary action.

            In connection with the above, SDPO (Civil), West Phaileng and SDPO, West Phaileng visited the area today, i.e., 15.2.2021 and they held a short interaction with Tripura Forest RO and staff, who were available at the area. Officials of Mizoram informed Tripura Forest RO not to resume their initiative for construction of Forest Check Post at Khantlang Junction, Mizoram-Tripura border as the area fell under Mizoram jurisdiction. Information received indicated that officials of Tripura forest department do not have any objection and at the time, they still intended to construct Forest Check Post near the area.

24.2.2021(DSI)  :

            SDPO, Kanchanpur, North Tripura, Range Officer and officials of Tripura Forest Department, personnel of BSF and Tripura State Rifles came at Khantlang Junction (Mizoram side), Mizoram-Tripura border, which is located around 1 km towards east from New Kawnpui BOP on 23.2.2021 @ 12:00 noon with an intention to construct Tripura Forest Check-Post at the said area. Upon learning this, SI Liantluanga, Post Commander, New Kawnpui BOP rushed to the spot and informed officials of Tripura government not to take any action for construction of Forest Check Post at the area without the approval of Mamit DC. However, Tripura Forest Officials erected signboard bearing 'Forest Check Post Kawnpui, Kanchanpur Forest Sub-Division, Kanchanpur, North Tripura' at the area, and the signboard was subsequently removed by Post Commander, New Kawnpui BOP while in presence of officials of Tripura State Government. After a short period, Officials of Tripura government left the area and no untoward incident has been reported.
