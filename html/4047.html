Agreement between MARA TERRITORIAL COUNCIL (MTC)/MARA DEFENCE FORCE (MDF) and INTERIM MARALAND COUNCIL (ITC)/CHINLAND DEFENCE FORCE (CDF MARA) :

            As a result of the efforts given by Mara Evangelical Church Headquarters, Myanmar, an agreement was signed  between MTC/MDF and ITC/CDF Mara during the period between 22nd -23rd May 2024 at Zawngling village, Siah district, Mizoram. For this agreement to materialised, Mara Evangelical Church Headquarters, Myanmar had created Peace commission on 1st February 2024 to act as mediator between the two parties and conducted several meetings with which were attended by representatives of both the parties as well as leaders of Mara Evangelical Church Headquarters, Myanmar.

            The talks held from 22nd -23rd May 2024 at Zawngling village was presided over by Rev.C.Sitlo, Chairman Peace Committee and the following points were approved by all the members which includes representatives of both MTC/MDF and ITC/CDF Mara apart from members of the Peace Committee  for the reasons for their unification :

1. For the benefits of future Mara politics, there has to be only a single Mara Army within Maraland.

2. There should be no conflicts between Mara brethrens

3. To avert any kinds of conflicts which can cause poverty and hardships among the Mara population.

4. For the arrangements of proper educational system and developments of future Mara generations.

5. For promotion of supply of sufficient daily needs and proper communications in the Mara inhabited areas.

5. To establish a prominent Church service within Maraland.

            The two different army factions also agreed upon the following points for their future undertakings :

1. Both the representatives of MTC/MDF and ITC/CDF Mara will present their unity agreement in their respective meeting, seeking their approvals.

2. As a result of their unity, they will notify all their previous alliance in and around their inhabited areas. As from now on, only cadres of Mara Army will operate in their areas. However, if entering or passing of their land is required by other personnel, prior information has to be given.

3. Adequate arrangements has to be made with these personnel so as to enable Mara subjects to live a peaceful lives within Mara dominated areas.

4. The garrison of CDF-Lautu located at Sapaw areas will be considered when all the Mara Armies are under one command.

5. For better unity, Reunification Committee will be created under the guidance of Peace Committee which will comprise of five members respectively from each groups with another five persons from within their dominated areas.

6. The list of appointed/recommended members for Reunification committee will be submitted to Peace Committee within two days i.e from 24th May 2024.

7. Future course of actions will be determined by Reunification committee and if necessary, will raise Special Mara People Congress, the election of which will be conducted under their supervisions.

8. As the two different Mara faction has become united, all prisoners detained by each groups will be released within 14 days from 24th May 2024.
