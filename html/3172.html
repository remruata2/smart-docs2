A.Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES, VANGLAINI & THE AIZAWL POST newspaper Dt.7.2.2013:

Two women who set fire to a house and vehicles at Bethlehem Veng caught: Lallawmkimi (20) d/o Lalsangliana (L) and Lalchhandami (24) d/o K.Lalbiakdika accused of burning several vehicles and a house at Bethlehem Veng on the night of 29th Jan. 2013 were caught by the police yesterday and sent to Central Jail at 6:30pm last night. People of Bethlehem Vengthlang and Bethlehem Veng gathered due to this incident.

            The two women were already suspected and were taken into police custody for interrogation. Besides burning vehicles and a house on 29th Jan., the women confessed that they burnt several bikes at different localities of Aizawl on 21st Jan. said the police.

            The motive behind the crime was not that of anger or revenge said the accused, they said it was done out of drunken excitement. The women found kerosene in a place where pigs feed were cooked, took the kerosene and started to burn whatever they want as they roamed around. Police would not give proper reply to the media about the crime or the culprits.

            In the meanwhile, some locals of Bethlehem Veng expressed their disbelief that only two women could not be responsible for burning those numbers of vehicles and a house, they believed more people could be involved.

            Meeting between Local Council and Branch YMA of Bethlehem Veng and Bethlehem Vengthlang was held immediately to prevent an outbreak of public anger and the residences of the accused were guarded by some people. Many youth of Bethlehem Veng gathered at Sihlui and they were prevented from proceeding to Aizawl PS by local leaders. Local leaders were working hard to keep peace and were able to prevent local people from proceeding to Aizawl PS.

            Several personnel from 1st Bn. MAP were on duty at Aizawl PS and did their best and prevent angry people from doing harmful things. Police are also ready for security measures.

           

 

B.Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt.7.2.2013:

DIG(Southern Range) intend to visit all the Police Station and Outpost: Mizoram Police DIG  (SR), LH Shanliana intends tio visit all the PS and Outpost in Lunglei, Lawngtlai & Saiha district to know about the conditions of police public relationship.

            The DIG told media that Southern range was open on 13th Oct., 2008 and has 3 IPS till now; there are about 10,000 police personnel in Mizoram and out of which 1,660are in DIG Southern Range. Depending on the number of people living in a State, Mizoram has the second highest number of police personnel. In Lunglei district, there is one police personnel for 400 persons and in Lawngtlai district, one police personnel for 228 persons and in Saiha district, one police personnel for 554 persons.
