(a) Updates on development in Myanmar and Bangladesh having security implication in our territory (AR,BSF,SB/CID, Special Bureau, Military Int)

 

          * Myanmar : CNA/CDF - Chinland Joint Defence Committee (CJDC) was formed on April 4, 2021 by the 17 (seventeen) factions of the Civil Defence Forces (CDF) in Chin State, Myanmar. In its initiative for the formation of Interim Government of Chinland, CJDC had decided to prepare a Constitutional Draft. In pursuance of CJDC decision, 'Future Chinland Constitutional Planning Workshop' was held during 7th - 8th September, 2023 at Central YMA Hall, Aizawl, Mizoram. The programme, which was presided over by Mr. Zing Cung, CNF Chairman, was attended by representatives from 16 (sixteen) factions and they had formed a Constitutional Drafting Committee comprising of 31 (thirty one) members. Mr. Ramkul Cung, CNF Assistant General Secretary, Mr. Tial Ling, MP and Salai Paul, CDF Paletwa were the Conveners of The Drafting Committee and the members consisted of 5 (five) representatives from CNF, 9 (nine) Members of Parliament, and 1 (one) representative each from CDF/PDF.

          The Final Draft of the Constitution, drafted by the Constitution Drafting Committee was approved by the Working Committee for Chinland Council at a Conference held at Camp Victoria between 27.11.2023 to 1.12.2023. As mandated by the Constitution, Chinland Council was formed with the election of the following as Chinland Council Leading Members before the commencement of Chinland Government :-

1.       Chairman                       -        Zing Cung (CNF)

2.       Vice Chairman (1)          -        U Min Nyo (MP-Kampalet)

3.       Vice Chairman (2)          -        U Bawi Kung (CDF-Mara)

4.       Secretary                         -        Salai Plau (CDF-Paletwa)

5.       Joint Secretary (1)           -        Salai Htet Ni (CNF)

6.       Joint Secretary (2)           -        Pu Tial Ling (MP-Hakha)

 

          During 30th and 31st January 2024, Chinland Council Meeting was held at Camp Victoria, Chin State Myanmar. The Meeting was presided over by Chinland Council Chairman, Mr. Zing Cung (KNF Chairman) and was attended by representatives of 17 (seventeen) CDF factions; (1. CDF-Halkha, 2. CDF-Thantlang, 3. CDF-Sentlang, 4. CDF-Zotung, 5. CDF-Zophei, 6. CDF-Lautu, 7. CDF-Mara, 8. CDF-Dai, 9. CDF-Hualngoram, 10. CDF-Sihjang, 11. CDF-Zahngiat, 12. Peoples Defence Army (PDA)-Tidim, 13. CDF-Kampalet, 14. CDF-Matupui, 15. CDF-Paletwa, 16. KKG, 17. CDF-Zaw) and about 100 (one hundred) observers from another 2 (two) organisations (Mindat MP and All Burma Student Democratic Federation (ABSDF).

The Chinland Council meeting announced the establishment of Chinland Government on February 1, 2024 and designated its leaders to form the newly formed Chinland Government.

 

Chin National Army (CNA) led ethnic armed groups under Chinland Council reportedly has been trying to capture the remaining Myanmar Army camps under the State Administrative Council (SAC) inside Chin State, Myanmar. Further inputs stated that the issue of non alliance between Chinland Council and Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA) Eastern Command, Myanmar as well as Chinland Brotherhood Alliance which comprises Chinland Defence Force (CDF) Mindat, Peoples Defence Force (PDF) Zoland and Chin National Defence Force (CNDF) which did not accept the Chinland Council was discussed by the Chinland Council meeting on many occasions but Chinland Council reportedly decided not to wage war against these other arms ethnic groups.

Information received in this regard stated that around 100 (one hundred) cadres of CNA and 30 (thirty) cadres from other ethnic armed groups under Chinland Council, namely; People Defence Army (PDA) Tedim, Chinland Defence Force (CDF) Tonzang, CDF Hualngoram, CDF Zangiat, CDF Thantlang, CDF Halkha, CDF Matupi and CDF Kalay Kabaw Gangaw totally more than 250 (two hundred fifty) cadres led by S/S Brigadier Pantui of CNA proceeded towards Tedim Township to attack the 259th Battalion Myanmar Army stationed and camped at Tedim town on 30th April, 2024. It is reported that on their way to the town, the CNA led armed groups were ambushed by ZRA Eastern Command near the Bridge of Manchaung River at the outskirt of Tedim town. The ambush conducted by ZRA Eastern Command reportedly led to the death of 1 (one) CNA Cadre and 1 (one) cadre of CDF Matupi and they exchanged fires which lasted for about 5 (five) minutes. Subsequently, CNA led ethnic armed groups reportedly retreated to Laitui village, Tedim Township.

On 03.05.2024 at around 02:00 p.m. in the afternoon, CNA led ethnic armed groups under Chinland Council retaliated against ZRA Eastern Command by attacking Mount Carmel  Camp, the designated camp of ZRA Eastern Command at Muallum, Tonzang Township and lasted till around 06:30 p.m. in the evening in which one cadre of ZRA was reportedly killed. The Mount Carmel Camp was subsequently overran and captured by the CNA led armed groups on 04.05.2024 leading to seizure of arms and ammunitions left by ZRA Eastern Command including 60 mm mortar shells and local made mortars, 40 mm grenades, 7.62 mm and 5.56 mm cartridges and mine detectors. Further inputs indicated that CNA led armed groups had conducted a surveillance of Mount Carmel before the attack by using drones.

          Reliable information received stated that 1(one) cadre of ZRA and 2 (two) cadres of CNA led armed group died while 4 (four) cadres of ZRA Eastern Command and 3 (three) cadres of CNA led armed group suffered injuries during the attacked of Mount Carmel Camp. Further inputs indicated that around 10 (ten) cadres of ZRA Headquarters, Manipur are scheduled to leave Manipur tomorrow, i.e. 09.05.2024 to provide logistic support to its Eastern Command.

 

Latest inputs regarding the situation in Myanmar indicated that a military operation has been conducted since 15.05.2024 by Chin National Army (CNA) led ethnic armed groups under Chinland Council against the State Administrative Council’s Myanmar Army and the Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA) Eastern Command camps inside Tonzang Township. The CNA led armed groups reportedly captured most of the camps allegedly occupied by Myanmar Army and ZRA Eastern Command including Dampui Camp (ZRA), Nakzaang Camp (Myanmar Army), Chikha Camp (Myanmar Army), Eliza Camp, Suansang (ZRA) and Tonzang Town Camp (Myanmar Army) on 19.05.2024. During these clashes, the Myanmar Army retaliated by conducting airstrikes over Tonzang town, Nakzaang and Lungtak villages which led to desertion of these villages by the civilians. Some of these civilians who had left their villages and houses due to fear of another airstrikes by the Myanmar Army have entered the adjoining villages inside Mizoram for shelter and asylum.

 

On 27.05.2024 @04:00 AM three Chin  Brotherhood alliances (CNDF, PDF – Zoland and CDF –Mindat)  attack Tedim town and captured Police Station. However, as the Myanmar Army was supported by airstrike, the rebellion group could not captured the Myanmar Army Camp yet till date.

 

          Further, information received also stated that a number of Myanmar Nationals had entered Siaha District during the second week of May 2024 due to the increase tension between Maraland Defence Force (MDF) and Chinland Defence Force (CDF-Mara) in Chin State, Myanmar. These Myanmar Nationals who are seeking temporary shelter inside the Mizoram are mainly from different villages of Myanmar including Vairum, Salachhuah, Laty, Bakla, Piedo, Bakalei and Tisavai villages and are staying with their friends and families.

 

          At the same time, Khumi community of Myanmar’s Paletwa District had entered Khawmawi Village last week allegedly due to the atrocities of the Arakan Army against the Khumi community as well as the conscription law reportedly imposed by Arakan Army upon every man under the age of 40 years of age within the administrative area of Arakan Army.

 

          Due to the ongoing political turmoil in Myanmar, Chinland Defence Force (CDF) Matupi and its allied ethnic armed groups, conduced military operation against the 2 (two) State Administrative Council’s (SAC) Myanmar Army Battalion stationed at Matupi Township, Myanmar. It is reported that 304 Battalion Myanmar Army was attacked by CDF (Matupi) Brigade - I and its allied Chinland Brotherhood and Arakan Army since 09.06.2024 while CDF (Matupi) Brigade - II and its allied Chin National Army (CNA) had conducted an operation against the 140 Battalion Myanmar Army since 13.06.2024. The military operations conducted in Matupi Township led to another wave of Myanmar refugees from the township and its adjoining villages into Mizoram. These Myanmar refugees reportedly had entered Mizoram through Zyhno (Zawngling) Village of Siaha District and further entered Lawngtlai District, Lunglei District and Aizawl District.

 

          Further report stated that as many as 113 (one hundred thirteen) Myanmar refugees from Matupi District of Myanmar had entered Lunglei District since 09.06.2024 till 17.06.2024. Out of these latest refugees, 55 (fifty five) refugees stays with their relatives at Lunglei Ramthar and Farm Veng, 4 (four) refugees stays at Nghasih camp, Lunglei Ramthar, 34 (thirty four) refugees stays at Thaizawl refugee camp, 15 (fifteen) refugees at Saichhoh camp, Haulawng and 5 (five) refugees at Vanhne Village. It is also reported that 108 (one hundred eight) Myanmar refugees who had recently entered Lawngtlai on the night of 11.06.2024 and early morning of 12.06.2024 were accommodated at Lawngtlai Chanmari - IV Camp while 32 (thirty two) Myanmar refugees were reported to be temporarily sheltered at Upper Saikah Village. It has also been learnt that as many as 164 (One hundred sixty four) Myanmar refugees from Matupi District, Myanmar has reached Aizawl District in the aftermath of the military operation conducted by CDF (Matupi) and its allied armed groups. These refugees who recently seek shelter in Aizawl are currently accommodated at Tuirial camp (6), Sairang camp (68), Sihhmui camp (76) and Durtlang Leitan camp (14).

These refugees reportedly fled Samang Village due to the alleged attempt of forceful conscription of civilians into Arakan Army. Further inputs suggested that these Bawm refugees were originally from Bangladesh who had fled to Myanmar in the past as well as recently.  The refugees in Hmawngbu village are reportedly not accommodated in a designated refugee camp but currently live with their friends and relatives.

         

          In another incident, 127 (one hundred twenty seven) Bangladesh Nationals from Bawm community seek refuge inside Mizoram and entered Chamdur Project Veng, Lawngtlai District on the early morning of 10.05.2024. These refugees are reported to be civilians from Ruma Upazila and its surrounding Lairunpi and Bethel villages who reportedly tried to escape the atrocities of Bangladesh Army in the ongoing combing operation conducted by the Bangladesh Government in the aftermath of the bank robberies alleged to be committed by Kuki Chin National Front (KNF) on 02.04.2024 and 03.04.2024. (b) Current trends in smuggling of narcotics drugs, arms, explosives, WLS and other contrabands :

 

Drug Smugglings

         Mizoram being geographically close to the Golden/Death Triangle and due to the porous unfenced international border with Myanmar became a frequented route for drug smugglers in recent years. Mizoram which also shares inter-state boundary with three other Indian states is also used as a gateway for trafficking of drugs and other contraband items from Myanmar to Mainland India. Also, the recent unrest in Myanmar causing the decline in the number of military check gates on the Myanmar side and the Myanmar Nationals seeking asylum in India acting as a peddlers/dealers to support themselves financially resulted in the increase of drugs trafficking in Indo-Myanmar border. As communal violence occurred in Manipur, which was reportedly one of the major smuggling routes, smugglers could no longer used Manipur as their route; the peaceful state of Mizoram become more vulnerable to such smugglers.
