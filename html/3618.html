A. Front page of  VANGLAINI  newspaper  Dt.12.10.2022

 Teak and other wooden  products not in restricted list :  In a reply to the request of Mizoram Governor Pu Hari Babu Kambhampatil, Union Minister of commerce & industries Dr. Piyush Goyal stated that teak and other wooden products were not included in the restricted list. According to the statement released by the government, Mizoram Woodbased Industry Owners Association (MWIOA) had on 21st June 2022 submitted in writing, the difficulties faced by them in importing teak and other wooden related products through Zokhawthar from Myanmar to Governor of Mizoram who in turn relayed the plight of MWIOA to the Union Commerce Minister. In the reply letter, the Union Minister mentioned that only Article HS 44039918 (rough wood of red sanders) and HS 44039922 (rough wood of Sandalwood) are prohibited under the import policy while the rest of wood planks and wooden products can be imported through Land Custom Station located in Zokhawthar village.

 

 

B. Front page  of  VANGLAIN newspaper  Dt.12.10.2022

No complaint from AR in connection with Temple clock : Maj. Praveen Kumar, Public Relations Officer of 2nd Bn. Assam Rifles told VANGLAINI that there has been no complaint to the police from the Assam Rifles in connection with the noise created by the clock of Salvation Army Temple, Aizawl. The PRO further added that such complain were unthinkable by them as the Assam Rifles respected all religions and their activities. The complaint made by AR commandant as published in yesterday's VANGLAINI edition was due to misinformation received and all the readers are requested for their understanding.
