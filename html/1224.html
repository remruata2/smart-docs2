BOMB BLAST DURING 2013

 

I       On 23.5.2013, a powerful bomb exploded inside the premises of 26th Assam Rifles Mandir near Zodin Square, Aizawl. The blast damaged 26 window glasses and crater of 6 inches deep was created by the blast. Fortunately, there is no human casualty due to the blast. In this connection, Aizawl PS C/No.169/13 dt.23.5.13 u/s 295/436 IPC r/w 3 of Explosive Substances Act has been registered.

 

II     On 21.7.2013@ 3:15am, there was an explosion of carbide within the premises of New Capital Complex, Khatla, Aizawl. It was immediately confirmed that three youths namely David Lalhmingsanga S/o Rohnuna of Champhai, C/117 Robert Lalmuanpuia S/o K. Rozama, Champhai DEF and Michael Lalnipuia, Champhai were carrying carbide, gas welding equipments like oxygen cylinder, welding rod, pipe and face mask with them. They also parked an Alto car(black) B/R No.ML-05H/1638 utilized by them at the place of explosion. One David Lalhmingsanga was injured in the blast and is undergoing treatment at Civil Hospital, Aizawl.

 

BOMB BLAST DURING 2014

 

I       On the night of 30.1.2014 @ 10:55 am, an unknown type of explosive bomb was exploded from one vegetable shop at Treasury's Square market. Destroying some shops into bits, and caused severe damages to the shop itself from where the blast took place as well as its adjacent shops while other nearby shops also are reportedly heavily damaged.

             All the window panes of Assembly secretariat and the Information and Public Relations office were smashed under the impact, window glasses of other nearby buildings including private residences were also shattered. The whole walls of two rooms, housing drivers and guards, in the Assembly Secretariat were also severely damaged by the blast. Neither the type of bomb/explosive used nor the culprits and their intention were not yet known however the case is being thoroughly investigated by Police and Forensic personnel. Fortunately, there is no human casualty due to the blast. In this connection Aizawl PS Case No. 28 dt. 31.1.2014 u/s 436 IPC r/w 3 of Explosive Substances Act has been registered.

                        Damages caused by the explosion are listed below:

       (1) 266 window panes, 65 ventilation glasses and 6 nos. of tube light frames of Assembly House and 1 extended room used as Driver' room of Assembly House were badly damaged.

       (2) 231 window panes,168 ventilation glasses and 22 nos. of window frames of Information & Public Relations New building.

       (3) 135 window panes and 5 nos. of window frames of Information & Public Relations Old building.

       (4) 72 window panes, 12 ventilation glasses and 25 nos. of wall tiles of Printing & Stationery building.

       (5) 8 window panes, 2 nos. of window frames and 5 nos. of wall tiles of Pi Thanzami Tochhawng's house.

       (6) 7 window panes of MJA Office/Press Club.

       (7) 7 nos. of hawkers shed of Treasury Bazaar were totally damaged and 14 nos. of hawkers shed were partially damaged.

 

II    On the night of 20.2.2014 at 11:45pm, some unknown miscreants had blasted an explosive suspected to be gelatin at the entrance of New Market meat shop (old building). The explosion had caused damages such as: (i) Main gate of old building of New Market meat shop, (ii) some portion of the retaining wall near the main gate, (iii) 3 window frame, 14 window glass, 4 ventilator and ceiling tile of teachers' room of SSA girls hostel, (iv) 11 window panes and wall tiles of Trade and Commerce Deptt. Quarters. Fortunately, there is no human casualty due to the blast. The case is being investigated by Aizawl Police Station Staff. In this connection, Aizawl PS C/No.48/14 dt.21.2.14 u/s 436 IPC r/w 3 of Explosive Substances Act has been registered.

 

III  A powerful bomb exploded near the residence of Pu Hmingchungnunga, Chairman, Sinlung Hills Development Council (SHDC), Bungkawn Vengthar on the morning of 22.3.2014 @ 3:50 AM damaging over 220 numbers of window panes/ventilation glasses and some household properties of the targetted building and the adjoining private buildings at Bungkawn Vengthar, Aizawl. However, there was no casualty due to the explosion. Pu Hmingchungnunga is currently residing in the third floor of the building of Pu Hmingdailova Colney (L) with his eleven family members on rental basis. In this connection Aizawl PS C/no.84/14 Dt. 22.3.2014 u/s 447/427 IPC r/w Sec 3(a) E.S Act have been registered.

It may be mentioned that there was a strong rivalry between HPC(D) and Pu Hmingchungnunga since long time back and an attempt to assassinate Pu Hmingchungnunga by HPC(D) cadres was also foiled during October, 2007 by arresting 2 (two) HPC(D) cadres along with geletine sticks, detonators, pistol, remote control, etc. Aizawl Police have so far detained one person, namely Lalchungnunga (23 yrs) S/o Lalbiaksanga, Zemabawk, Aizawl.

      

 

IV  On the night of 18.4.2014@11:00pm, there was a bomb blast within the vicinity of Chaltlang Mual Veng on a path way leading westward of Chaltlang Mual Veng near Chaltlang High School resulting in partial damage to one Assam type building belonging to  Pu R.Lalchhanhima, EE P&E Department as well as one RCC building belonging to Dr.Ramfangzauva of Chaltlang Mual Veng which were situated on both side of the pathway. The blast also left a dent on the surface of the pathway. However, luckily no human being was hurt in the blast. Expert team from FLS Aizawl, on being requisitioned, also visited the place of occurrence and all requisite formalities have been observed and according to the experts, explosive used in the blast was apparently Gelatin explosive. The total value of damage inflicted due to the blast is estimated to be around rupees one lakh. In this regard, Bawngkawn PS C/No.78/14 Dt. 19.4.2014 u/s 3(a) E.S Act has been registered.

BOMB BLAST DURING 2018

 

I.       On 15.1.2018 @ 10:00 a.m. inside Tarum forest (near India - Myanmar border), one person namely Jutia Tongchangya (26) S/o Chondro Lal of Bundukbanga village was seriously injured by an explosion suspected to be a landmine blast.  Jutia Tongchangya sustained grievous injury as his left leg was completely blown off by the blast. He was shifted to District Hospital, Lawngtlai in the morning of 16.1.2018 for medical treatment. Jutia Tongchangya and his friend had gone to Tarum forest to harvest brooms. In this connection, Vasei PS Case No. 1/2018 dt. 21.1.2018 u/s 326 IPC r/w 4(a) Explosive Substances Act has been registered.

 

II.     Mrs. Khusoma Bru (37yrs) W/o Nbauh Krauhha of Kawnkhua, Lawngtlai District was severely injured by a landmine blast on 18.1.2018@ 8:00am near Indo-Myanmar border as she was on her way to harvest chilli from their jhum. She was succumbed to her injuries on 19.1.2018@ 9:30am. In this connection, Vasei PS Case No. 2/2018 dt. 21.1.2018 u/s 302 IPC r/w 4(a) Explosive Substances Act has been registered.

 

III.   On the night of 10.3.2018 @ 11:00 pm, an explosion occurred within the premises of Director of Transport office, Chaltlang, diligent enquiry was conducted into the matter by an officer of Bawngkawn Police Station, during the course of enquiry, it substantiated that on the night of 10.3.2018 @ 11:00pm, some unknown miscreants hurl suspected gelatin explosive into the premises of Directorate of Transport office, Chaltlang from the sidewalk located beside the office. The suspected gelatin explosive went off causing colossal damage to window pane, etc. The explosion seems to be done with intention to cause damage to property.  In this connection, Bawngkawn PS Case No. 64/2018 dt. 19.3.2018 u/s 3(a) Explosive Substances Act has been registered.

 

IV.    On 7.9.2018 @ 8:45 pm, Abednego Lalhruaitluanga S/o Lalchungnunga of Hlimen had blasted gelatin in public place i.e, near the residence of Pu Zosangzuala of Hlimen Dam veng. In this connection, Kulikawn Police Station Case No. 120/18 dt. 7.9.2018 u/s 506 IPC r/w 3 of Explosive Substance Act has been registered.

 

V.     On the night of 5th December, 2018, Lalchhanhima (26) s/o Lalthlamuana of Sairang, Aizawl District locked himself inside their house and ransacked house-hold items as a result of his intoxication. As the police arrived at the house after they were summoned by his relatives, the said Lalchhanhima blew himself up using a stick of gelatin bomb before police personnel could break inside the house. The explosion blew away his head and his left hand, killing him instantly. In this connection, Sairang Police Station UD Case No. 15/18 dt. 6.12.2018 u/s 174 Cr.PC has been registered.

 

BOMB BLAST DURING 2019

 

I.         On 18.2.2019, some unknown miscreants damaged one water pump and two water storage tanks, total value of Rs. 50,000/- located at College of Vetenary Sciences and Animal Husbandry, Selesih by using explosive materials. In this connection, Bawngkawn Police Station Case No. 43/19 dt. 19.2.2019 u/s 3 Explosive Substance Act 1908 r/w 427 IPC has been registered.

                                          

 

II.       On 11.3.2019 @ 7:00 am, one civilian from Bandukbanga named Birondro Kumar (40) s/o Peleya had gone to the Indo-Myanmar border near Border Pillar No. 2 for agricultural work. Unfortunately, he stepped on a landmine and his right feet to knee was blown off with his left leg badly burnt due to the explosion of the landmine. The victim was further taken to Lunglei Hospital by ambulance of Assam Rifles for further medical treatment under proper escort. Hence, Vasei Police Station Case No. 1/19 dt. 12.3.2019 u/s 307/326 IPC r/w 4 ES Act has been registered.

 

 

III.      On 13.12.2019 @ 1:00 am there was a blast inside the fencing of Aizawl Centenary Monument near Raj Bhavan, Aizawl. The sound of the blast was heard by the sentry duty of the CRPF posted at the Raj Bhavan. Upon hearing the incident Pu S.B.K. Singh, IPS, DGP (M); Pu L.H. Shanliana, IPS, IGP (L&O); Pu John Neihlaia, IPS (Hqrs. & Intel) and other senior Police Officers besides staff from Aizawl Police Station reached the spot immediately. Experts from Forensic Science Laboratory also meticulously examined the spot and had collected samples and other important evidences for further examinations. Fortunately there was no casualty caused by the blast. A small crater near the monument and some window-panes at the adjacent building, i.e. Civil Aviation Wing office were broken. The exact blasting materials used for the purpose of the blast can be ascertained only after conducting necessary examination. However, it seems to have done using some easily available materials in local.

                                    In this connection, Aizawl PS Case No 674/19 of 13.12.2019 u/s 120B/435/434/427 IPC R/W Sec 3 of Explosive Substances Act/Sec 3 of Damage to Public Property Act has been registered and Special Investigation Team constituted by Mizoram Police to investigate the case.
