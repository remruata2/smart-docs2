****LAW & ORDER SITUATION, ACTIVITIES OF ANTI-SOCIAL/ANTI-NATIONALS, ETC. ALONG THE BORDERING AREAS WITH TRIPURA (WEF 2017 TILL 3.2.2018)****

**I.**  Information has been received that representatives of _Mizoram Bru Displaced People's Forum_(MBDPF), Mizoram Bru Displaced Welfare Organisation (MBDWO) and NGOs from all Bru Refugee Camps, Tripura held a joint meeting at Naisingpara Refugee Camp, Tripura on Dt. 5.8.2017. Pu Lalfamkima Apeto chaired the meeting and it was attended by around 70 (seventy) members. The meeting decided to form a new forum known as ‘Mizoram Bru Indigenous Democratic Movement’ (MBIDM) and elected the following 3 (three) persons as leaders for MBIDM.

 President  : G.B. Herbert Reang, P/A Naisingpara, Tripura

 Vice President  : Lalfamkima Apeto, P/A Naisingpara, Tripura

 General Secretary : Philip Apeto, P/A Naisingpara, Tripura

 The three elected leaders will further appoint members for Executive Committee, Treasurer, Finance Secretary, etc. New branches of MBIDM will be set up in all Bru Refugee Camps, Tripura.

 The aim of MBIDM is to protect the welfare &amp; interest of the displaced Bru families living in Refugee Camps, Tripura. MBIDM also decided to revive the demand for creation of a separate Autonomous District Council for Bru community and in this regard, they decided to submit a Memorandum, ‘_Creation of Autonomous District Council for Bru Indigenous Group of Mizoram under 6th Schedule of Indian Constitution’_ to the Hon’ble Prime Minister of India and to the Government of Mizoram after 15.8.2017 (i.e. after celebration of Independence Day-2017 is over).

****II.**  In a Press Release issued on 24.10.2017, Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) expressed their anguish over the irregular distribution of relief rations to the Brus living at 7 (seven) Relief Camps in North Tripura District, Tripura. MBDPF further expressed their apprehension of casualties in the camps due to starvation unless ration is distributed immediately. They also stated their intention to organise road blockade and raid Government storehouse for their survival. In a statement, MBDPF further alleged that this could be a 'tactical ploy' to force them to accept the present package of repatriation prepared by Government of Mizoram.**

**III.** Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ Forum (MBDPF) Headquarters and its branches held a joint meeting on 8.11.2017 at Naisingpara, Tripura. Pu A. Sawibunga, President, MBDPF presided over the meeting and 29 (twenty nine) members were present. Consequent upon the meeting, MBDPF sent a letter to Shri Mahesh Kumar Singla, IPS, Adviser (NE), Bru, Ministry of Home Affairs wherein they conveyed their grievances over his alleged refusal to meet leaders of MBDPF during his visit of Kanchanpur, Tripura on 4-6.11.2017 and his meeting with some section of Bru refugees at Kaiskau, Khakchangpara and Asapara and his distribution of unsigned circular for repatriation in Bru language. In the letter, they reminded Shri Mahesh Kumar Singla that Bru refugees are willing to repatriate to Mizoram and start a normal life if their demands are met by Government of India and Government of Mizoram. They also highlighted about irregular and delay in distribution of rations to Bru refugees. They also appealed to take immediate steps for peaceful repatriation and to bring pragmatic solution for the Bru refugees.

**IV.** A Joint Meeting of all NGO’s - Mizoram Bru Displaced People Forum (MBDPF), Young Bru Association (YBA), Bru Tribal Development Society (BTDS), Mizoram Bru Indegenous Democratic Movement (MBIDM), Bru Displaced Welfare Oranisation (BDWO), Camp Defence Committee (CDC), All Church Committee (ACC), representative of Religious Organisation from Christian and Hindu from all seven Relief Camps in North Tripura was held on 22.11.2017 at Multipurpose Centre, Naisingpara Relief Camp, North Tripura. Pu A. Sawibunga, President, MBDPF presided over the meeting and about 135 (one hundred thirty five) members attended.

 The meeting appreciated the decision of Ministry of Home Affair. Government of India to repatriate all Bru Refugees before the commencement of 2018 Assembly Election in Mizoram. However, the meeting strongly condemend Shri. Mahesh Kumar Singla, IPS, Adviser, (NE) Bru over his alleged intention to divide the displaced Bru and over his sidelining of MBDPF leaders and his meeting with some leaders of Internally Displaced People (IDP).

 The meeting stated that the decision of Shri. Mahesh Kumar Singla could result panic, chaos, enmity among the inmates and create law and order problems among Bru Refugees. The meeting also criticised Shri. Mahesh Kumar Singla over his alleged direction to officials of Government of Tripura on 5.11.2017 to stop distribution of Cash-dole of Rs. 5/- per adult and Rs. 2.50/- per minor until and unless the Head of family opens Bank Account for transferring of cash-dole into their Account. The meeting also held that as per the ongoing process, enrollment in Aadhar for EPIC holders of Bru Refugees will take another 1 (one) year.

 The Joint Meeting strongly affirmed that the decision of Shri. Mahesh Kumar Singla is nothing but to harass the Internally Displaced Bru and to reptriate Brus forcefully into Mizoram without considering their legitimate demands. The meeting also demanded Shri. Mahesh Kumar Singla to re-consider his decision and distribute cash-dole as per the ongoing norm (once in two months) until and unless all Heads of the household open Bank Account.

 The meeting also decided to launch a series of agitation as under if their prayer is not re-considered by Shri. Mahesh Kumar Singla and the Government of Tripura : -

i) Mass procession from Naisingpara to BDO Office, Dasda, N. Tripura on  1.12.2017.

ii)  Long March from Naisingpara to DM &amp; Collector Office, Dharmanagar, N.Tripura during Second Week of December, 2017.

iii) Poster campaign along National Highway from Guwahati to Mizoram and Tripura during January, 2018.

iv)  One week sit-in demonstration in Agartala during February, 2018.

v)  Indefinite Sit-in demonstration in Ramlila Maidan, New Delhi during March, 2018.

**V.** On 15.12.2017, around 1500 members of Brus living in different Refugee Camps within North Tripura took out a procession under the aegis of Joint NGO Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF). They marched from Naisingpara Camp and Ashapara Camp to RD Block, Dasda, Kanchanpur. They chanted slogans and carried placards written 'We are Indian, We love Peace', 'Distribute immediate Cash Dole for Five months’, 'Avoid forceful repatriation, Don't do forceful repatriation', etc.

 They dispersed peacefully after they submitted a Joint Memorandum to Hon'ble Union Home Minister, Government of India through BDO, Dasda R.D Block, Kanchanpur, North Tripura. In the Joint Memorandum, they requested the Hon'ble Union Home Minister to take immediate measure for (i) Immediate release of cash dole withheld since July, 2017. (ii) Provision of regular ration at the 7(seven) relief camps in Tripura without any delay whatsoever to avoid starvation. (iii) Enhance the assistance to Brus at relief camps as sworn/warranted by the Union of India. (iv) Allow the MBDPF leaders to participate in all the meetings being conducted by the Union of India, Mizoram and Tripura on the matter of repatriation of Bru from Tripura to Mizoram and rehabilitation in Mizoram after repatriation.

**VI.** Information has been received that Joint NGOs of Bru Refugees led by Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) decided to await the response of the Hon’ble Union Home Minister till end of December, 2017 with regard to the Joint Memorandum submitted during their protest march on 15.12.2017. Joint NGOs reportedly decided to launch ‘Long March’ from Naisingpara Refugee Camp to Dharmanagar, North Tripura during January, 2018 if positive response is not received from the Hon’ble Union Home Minister within the specified time.

 In the Joint Memorandum submitted on 15.12.2017, Joint NGOs of Bru Refugees led by Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) requested the Hon'ble Union Home Minister to take immediate measure for (i) Immediate release of cash dole withheld since July, 2017. **VII.** Information has been received that on 15.1.2018 @ 7:00 a.m. leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples Forum (MBDPF) organised a Public Meeting at Naisingpara Refugee Camp. The public meeting was presided over by A. Sawibunga, President, MBDPF and it was attended by about 1,000 (one thousand) members of MBDPF. The meeting discussed topic regarding the proposed repatriation process to be undertaken by the Govt. of Mizoram. As per information received, no hand-votes were cast in support of the proposed repatriation by the Govt. of Mizoram. Members of MBDPF voiced that they will oppose the repatriation process until their demands from the Central Govt. and Mizoram Govt. are met. They also stated their intention to wait for the results of their appeal pending in the Supreme Court. Leaders of MBDPF stated that public meeting will also be conducted in the other Bru Refugee Camps.

 Officials from Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India are likely to hold a Joint Meeting with leaders of MBDPF and prominent citizens on 8.2.2018 at Dharmanagar, Tripura on the subject of repatriation. The following officials from MHA are expected to attend the joint meeting :-

 (i) Smt. RinaMitra, Special Secretary (Internal), MHA

 (ii) SatyandraGarg, Joint Secretary (North East), MHA

 (iii) Mahesh Kumar Singhla, IPS Retd., Adviser to MHA i/c North East.

****VIII.** The Long March, scheduled to be organised by Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) from Naisingpara Refugee camp to Dharmanagar, North Tripura on 1.2.2018 and 2.2.2018 in protest against the irregular distribution of ration since September 2017 and suspension of payment of cash-dole since July 2017 was indefinitely postponed. The postponement was due to non-receipt of permission from the Tripura Government in which State Legislative Assembly Election is scheduled to be held on 18.2.2018.**

**IX.**  Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) submitted Memorandum to the Shri. Narendra Modi, Hon'ble Prime Minister of India on 3.2.2018 through the District Collector, Dharmanagar, North Tripura. The Memorandum alleged that the repatriation process undertaken by the Government of Mizoram and Central Government as fruitless and completely baseless in finding solution to the 20 (twenty) years long pending issue. The following points were highlighted in the

 Memorandum submitted to the Hon'ble Prime Minister :-

(i) To give direction to the Central Government and Government of Tripura to distribute cash dole of Rs. 5/ day per adult and Rs. 2.5/day per minor which were withheld since July 2017 and to resume distributions of ration.

(ii) To construct dwelling house for each family at 7 (seven) relief camps in Tripura.

(iii) To direct the Government of Tripura to update the population of refugees and to issue New Ration cards for the refugees.
(iv) To direct the Election Commission of India for conducting special/intensive summary revision of Electoral Rolls in the Relief Camps.
 Pertaining to repatriation and rehabilitation :-

(i) Allotment of 5 (five) acres of land per family for agriculture and sustenance.

(ii) Formation of cluster/model villages comprising of not less than 500 families with basic amenities like education, healthcare, communication, electricity, security, etc.

(iii) To increase cash assistance from Rs. 80,000/- to Rs. 10 (ten) lakhs per family.

(iv) To conduct identification of 1,000 families left out due to non-availability of EPIC of Mizoram during the identification conducted during 2.11.2016-23.11.2018.

(v) To give additional cash assistance of Rs. 5 (five) lakhs to each families for construction of dwelling houses.
(vi) To deploy Central Security Forces for security of Repatriated Brus and to recruit Bru Youths in Special Police Officers (SPOs) under provisions of Indian Police Act, 1861.

(vii) To provide Government jobs for at least one person in each Bru family as per qualification.

(viii)To create separate administrative council for the Bru community in Mizoram.
