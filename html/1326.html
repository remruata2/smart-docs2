FREE MOVEMENT REGIME (FMR) INSIDE MIZORAM ALONG INDO-MYANMAR BORDER

 

  1. Free Movement Regime (FMR) is a bilateral agreement between India and Myanmar that allows free movement of Indian and Myanmarese citizens within 16 kms. of the border without Visa restrictions.

 

  1. Mizoram shares 404 kms. long border with Myanmar.

 

  1. Police Stations and Out Posts existing within the Indo-Myanmar Free Movement Regime (FMR) are as under:

  1. There is a Committee on FMR along Indo-Myanmar Border under the Chairmanship of Special Secretary (Internal Security), MHA which was constituted vide Communication No. 1/13/2017-BM.II dated 11th August, 2017 for effective implementation of Free Movement Regime along the Indo-Myanmar Border comprising 11 (eleven) committee members.

 

  1. The Committee met on 15.09.2017, 16.10.2017, 08.11.2017 and 03.01.2018 and based on discussions of the Committee, the final comprehensive formulation for the policy and the issue of FMR is as follows :

I.        Border Pass

1.  A Border Pass is to be a restricted travel document issued by designated authorities of bordering states to Indian Nationals who are local original residents of the area falling within 16 kms. of International Border to enable them to enter the border areas of Myanmar.

 

2. An applicant for Border Pass may apply for inclusion therein, the name(s) of his/her child (children) accompanying him/her subject to the child not having attained the age of twelve years.

 

3. The following authorities will be authorized to issue the Border Pass :

      a) Deputy Commissioner of the District.               b) Superintendent of Police / FRO.

      c) Authorised Officer of Indian Mission or any other officer specifically authorized by the   State Governments of Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur and            Mizoram.

 

4. Border Pass will be a bilingual document which can be issued in any local language with compulsory English translation of the same.

 

5. The Border Pass will be valid for a period of one year from the date of issue and its validity may be extended upon renewal, for a subsequent period of one year at a time. It shall be valid for multiple entries with the holder permitted to stay in the border areas of Myanmar for up to two weeks per visit. In case anything adverse comes to the notice of authorities, Border Pass may be cancelled any time by the issuing authority.

 

6. The Border Pass holder will be able to cross International Border from any crossing points and also any immigration check posts notified under relevant Act/Rules. The entry and exit at the International Border will be from the areas of the State which issues the Border Pass. To illustrate the holder of a Border Pass issued by Government of Arunachal Pradesh, entry and exit of the holder of this Border Pass will be from Indo-Myanmar International Border falling within the State of Arunachal Pradesh.

 

7. Every Border Pass will carry a unique number. Foreigners Division, MHA will devise a format of Border Pass in the form of a booklet with all relevant details regarding purpose of visit, place to be visited, entry/exit points/persons to be visited, period and place of stay in all Indo-Myanmar Border (IMB) States for uniformity.

 

8.  A citizen of Myanmar holding a Border Pass issued by authorities in Myanmar will be able to enter and stay within 16 kms. of International Border on Indian side.

 

9. The State Government will ensure that no Border Pass holder from Myanmar goes beyond 16 kms. of International Border. In case of violation by a Border Pass holder, prosecution under relevant Act(s) and law(s) will be done by the State Government.

 

10. The issuing authorities in India shall make available to the authorities in Myanmar through diplomatic channels, the names, specimen of those who are authorized to issue the Border Pass and data of all Border passes issued.

 

11. MEA will take up with the issuing authorities in Myanmar to make available to authorities in India, the names, specimen of those authorities, who are authorized to issue Border Pass in Myanmar and the data of all Border Passes issued to the authorities in India through diplomatic channels, so as to prevent forgeries and misuse.

 

II. Enumeration of Villages within 16 kms. of International Border : The Border States on International Border will draw up a list of villages falling within the aerial 16 kms. of International Border. This list is to be made available to Border Guarding Forces, State Police and the issuing authorities of Border Pass.

 

III. Bio-Metric Cards to eligible persons within 16 kms. from International Border : The State Governments concerned will endeavour to issue Aadhar identification card to the residents of villages within 16 kms. by 31st March, 2018.

 

IV. Movement of Vehicles across International Border : At present, there is no agreement between India and Myanmar on Motor Vehicles crossing International Border. Therefore concerned State Governments/Border Guarding Forces must ensure that there is no unauthorised crossing of vehicles at International Borders, except with specific approval of competent authorities.
