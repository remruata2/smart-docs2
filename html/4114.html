FMR New Guidelines (Free Movement Limit On Indo-Myanmar Border Tightened To 10 Km, With Border Pass) :

 

As per MHA Notification on dated December 24, 2024, the FMR range has been reduced from 16 kilometers to 10 kilometers on either side of the international border. The revised regulations, which came into effect on December 30, 2024, mandate the issuance of a Border Pass for residents within the 10-kilometer zone, permitting a stay of up to seven days. 

 

1. Exemption under G.S.R. No. 1265: 

   The Central Government, vide G.S.R. No. 1265, has exempted members of hill tribes, whether citizens of India or Myanmar, residing in the border areas under the Free Movement Regime, from the requirement of Rule 3 of the Passport (Entry into India) Rules, 1950, subject to specified conditions. 

 

2. Meeting chaired by Secretary (BM), MHA: 

   On December 16, 2024, a meeting was convened under the chairmanship of the Secretary (Border Management), MHA, wherein State Governments were informed of the Government of India’s decision to regulate the FMR through the issuance of Border Passes. The Border Pass will be issued by the Assam Rifles based on the following criteria: 

   -Proof of Identity (POI): A certificate issued by OC/SHO of Local Police Station (PS) or the local village chief/authority, confirming that the individual belongs to a village within 10 kilometers on either side of the international border. 

   -Passport: The passport of the individual must indicate the address and name of the village falling within the 10-kilometer zone on either side of the international border.

BANGLADESH:

 

            On 7th January, 2025, at approximately 8:30 AM, a group of 28 individuals from Bandarban District, Bangladesh, entered Hruitezawl (112.7 km from Lawngtlai Town) in Lawngtlai District, Mizoram.  Upon receiving information about their arrival, personnel of the 28th Assam Rifles, stationed at Tuithumhnar, promptly responded. The Assam Rifles transported the group back to Tuithumhnar within the same district. The Assam Rifles sought the assistance of the Village Council of Tuithumhnar to provide temporary shelter for the refugees. On 11th January, 2025, these 28 refugees were pushed backed by  team of Assam Rifles, BDO Office, Bungtlang South and Vasei PS, they were believed to proceed Myanmar via Zorinpui, Lawngtlai.

 

            On 18th January, 2025, 05 (five) members Bawm community of Bangladesh origin   reached Vathuampui, Lawngtlai from Sameng Village, Myanmar by crossing Myanmar Border. On 21st  January 2025 they were taken into Assam rifles custody and were then pushed back by the Assam Rifles on  22nd January 2025 at 10:24 am from Kakichhuah village by Boat.

Drugs seizures:

 

The modus operandi and ways of concealment of smuggled narcotics used by smugglers is constantly evolving. The past seizures of narcotics in the state revealed that these drugs are often concealed in sealed food packages, vegetables, electrical goods and more commonly inside a car door panel or costumed concealed compartment inside a vehicle.

Further, the emerging trend of using end to end encrypted messaging applications and compartmentalizing activities like logistics, finances or intelligence gathering by different groups or units complicated the interception of modern narcotics syndicates.

During this month till 24.02.2025, most of the drugs seized i.e. 80 % (approx.) are destined to Assam and other outerstate whereas 20 % (approx.) are destined for local consumption which are mostly transported from Myanmar.
