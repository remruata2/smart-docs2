i) Security scenario in Mizoram with special reference to insurgency and smuggling :

            Mizoram itself experiences low level insurgencies in the North Eastern and the North Western regions. However, Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) (the insurgent group from the northern parts) had signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) on 2nd April 2018. The signing of MoS brought about an end to over two-decade armed insurgency of the HPC(D). The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC-D was held on 12th April, 2018 where around 102 (one hundred two) Cadres and 12 (twelve) Council Members of HPC(D) came to over ground at the ceremony. However, the other faction of HPC(D) led by Lalhmingthanga Sanate have started resurfacing and reorganise themselves. They have been indulging in collection of donation and extortion during the closing months of 2022 and the opening months of 2023.

(iii) Indo Myanmar Border  update and influx of Myanmarese  nationals :

            Mizoram shares 496.531 kms of international border with Myanmar as per the record of Directorate of Land Revenue & Settlement, Government of Mizoram which is unfenced. Moreover, the Free Movement Regime allows people of the adjoining countries to enter 16 kms. into the neighbouring state. The Indo-Myanmar border security is currently handled by Assam Rifles who are thinly stretched along the whole length of the border.

            Military rule which took place in Myanmar on 1st February 2021 deposed the democratically elected  National League for Democracy and  the escalating resistance put up against the military regime by Committee Representing Pyidaungsu Hluttaw (CRPH) and Civil Disobedience Movement (CDM)  resulted in the influxes of  considerable number of refugees from Myanmar in general and Chin community in particular seeking asylum and taking shelter in Mizoram. These refugees presently took shelter in various districts of Mizoram and are accommodated in their relative’s houses and makeshift camps set up by NGOs. According to the latest information received, makeshift camps for Myanmar refugees are constructed at Zawlsei, Vaphai, Farkawn and Chawngtui Villages in Champhai District and at Thingsai, Bualpui 'H', New Ngharchhip and Cherhlun Villages by in Hnahthial District by their respective local NGOs.

vi) KNF/KNA activities across the border in Bangladesh :

            After a series of  online meeting, "First round of Physical Peace Talk" between Kuki-Chin National Front/Army (KCNA) and Government of Bangladesh (GoB) represented by the Peace Committee was held on 5th November, 2023 at Hmunlai village, near Ruma bazar, Bandarbun District, Bangladesh. Although no definite agreements were made, the meeting, agreed to maintained cease-fire between both the parties until the next peace talk. The KNF has submitted a draft of possible and proposed peace agreement with 6(six) point demands to the Prime Minister of Bangladesh through Peace Establishment Committee.        

            The second round of bilateral physical meeting between the two parties was conducted on 05.03.2024 at Hmunlai Village, near Ruma Bazar, Bandarban District, Bangladesh. In the meeting, representatives of KNF/KNA reiterated their six demands submitted in their previous meeting. However, as the points  require more time, the meeting agreed to discuss and implement them in subsequent dialogues                                                                                                                                                                                                      

            One of the demands of KNF/KNA is the immediate release of 59 KNA members imprisoned in Bangladesh prisons apart from various schemes which will benefitted the Kuki Chin communities. As per the meeting, steps will be taken by Bangladesh Government as soon as possible for the release of imprisoned KNA/KNF members. A the same time, KNF agreed not to engage in any armed activities during the ongoing peace process and will maintain cooperative relationship with the security forces to maintain the stability of law and order situation.

            The next dialogue is likely to be held on 22nd April 2024.
