WRITE-UP ON AGENDA FOR

CHIEF MINISTER’S CONFERENCE ON INTERNAL SECURITY

TO BE HELD ON 16th APRIL, 2012 AT NEW DELHI

 

 

1.  Internal security situation in Mizoram.

 

Mizoram is one of the most peaceful states in the country. In fact, Mizoram can boast of being the least militant infected state in the militant strife-torn North-East states mainly because of the budding militancy being checked by Mizoram Police at its infant stage. The last of such militant group was the 64 strong SPLA cadres who came overground just after completion of their training somewhere in the border of Manipur-Myanmar under the aegis of PREPAK in 2009.

 

The overall security situation in Mizoram state by and large is peaceful. However, various militant groups from neighbouring states and foreign countries like Myanmar and Bangladesh, taking advantage of the peaceful situation, used to pose a threat to the people living in the border areas. The recent glaring example felt in the Manipur-Mizoram border recently was HPC-D, a Manipur based militant group of Hmar community who threatened the electorates of SHDC area by way of giving diktat and the kidnapping of 6 ABCI officials from Indo-Bangla border by a Tripura based militant group, NLFT.

 

It is often said that the above noted militant groups and other militant outfits of North East are using Mizoram for trafficking of arms and ammunitions. Considering frequent seizure of arms and ammunitions in the State, this notion could not be totally ruled out. However, it could also be boldly said that trafficking of arms and other contraband goods have been successfully contained during the last 3 years.

 

2.  Intelligence Wing-Strengthening

 

                        During the last three years, the Special Branch have been strengthened to meet the requirement of the ever increasing duties and responsibilities of the Special Branch. During the year 2008 to 2010, surveillance equipment of the following were given to this unit while other proposals are also being made for procuring other equipments from 5% of MPF Scheme.

 

                        1) Digital Voice Logger with SMS retreaver

                        2) 2(two) nos. of Bushnell Binoculars

                        3) Bushnell Telescope with Digital Video Recorder

                        4) Pen Style Video Recorder

                        5) Computer with accessories

 

                        As the above surveillance equipments were given to this unit, Police personnel especially having a computer and technical know how who will be looking after the Technical Branch to work in three shifts for 24 hrs. is currently required. The process for centralized procurement of Telephone Call Interception System (TCIS) for State Special Branch is most welcome as modern criminals are more and more advanced in their activities with the advancement of technology in modern world. The present technical equipments being utilized by the Special Branch Mizoram needs to be improved and upgraded so as to meet the current challenges being faced for prevention of criminal activities.

 

 

ANALYSIS GROUP:         

                       

                        The Hon'ble Home Minister Shri P. Chidambaram, in his letter to the Hon'ble Chief Minister, Mizoram Pu Lalthanhawla dt. 17.12.2008 suggested that the State Intelligence Wing must have an Analysis Group that will receive, collate, analyse, link up with previous inputs and disseminate intelligence input which should work in two shifts totalling 16 hrs. Requirement of man power for Analysis Group comprising Dy.SP -1(one), Inspector -2(two), Sub-Inspector -4(four) was also submitted, but not yet sanctioned.

 

 

PAID SOURCES:

 

                        Inspite of the strengthening of Special Branch staff in the level of Sub-Inspector, Head Constable and Constable, the strength of this unit is falling short of the requirement to collect advance information particularly on the movement of insurgent outfit, arms smuggler and so on. To make wider covering of the areas bordering Myanmar and Bangladesh and other strategic areas and also for the timely collection of intelligence, the need to engage paid sources could not be over emphasised. It may be suggested that budgetary provision may be made available for the engagement of atleast 30(thirty) nos. of paid sources under the discretionary power of Special Branch.

 

 

 

3.  Strengthening of Counter Terrorism capabilities (NCTC)

 

Terrorist activities and immediate threat from Terrorism in the case of Mizoram State as on today is found to be almost negligible. But this doesn't mean we can sit idly waiting the incident to happen. Owing to its strategic location and peacefulness, the Terrorists may take advantage of the situation which would be detrimental to the security of the country in general, the state of Mizoram in particular.

 

It is therefore suggested that the existing SMAC being run by State Special Branch be given more attention and utilized with other sister intelligence agencies of both Central and State Govt. without compromising the autonomy of the State and its intelligence wing. The duties and functions of NCTC as proposed in the agenda would be effective taking into the confidence of State Intelligence Branches through sharing of intelligence and information by means of SMAC.           

 

SMAC in Mizoram was fully functional since May, 2011 and all equipments were installed in the SP, CID (SB) office compound, Mizoram. Since then, the agency functions smoothly and effectively by receiving and uploading important messages from the system. Unfortunately, the agency used to face some problems during the last 10 months like loss of link and other technical problems which caused the deficiencies for the maximum utilization of the system and to receive and upload required data on many occasions.
