WRITE UP ON RECENT ARREST OF FOREIGNERS (NEPALEASE & ROHINGYAS) INSIDE MIZORAM:

 

1.         On 23.4.2019@ 11:30pm, vehicle B/R No.AS 01/BC-0951 Sumo Maxi Cab carrying 8(eight) females, proceeding towards Aizawl was detained at Vairengte Police Check Gate, Sojol Roy(38) S/o Kutinenu Roy of Kabuganj, Cachar, Assam, driver of the vehicle stated that on the evening of the same day, Amrul, owner of the vehicle directed him to carry the girls from Sumo counter, Silchar and drop them at Zemabawk Petrol Pump, Aizawl from where one unknown person will pick them up.

 

            Particulars of the girls are as under :

                        1. Toyaba(22) D/o Soyid Salam

                        2. Rubina(20) D/o Soyid Salam

                        3. Tasmin(19) D/o Dilma Ahmed

                        4. Sokhu(18) D/o Shehel Ahmed

                        5. Noor Kayas Bengum(18) D/o Karim Mulla

                        6. Atariza(18) D/o Mutar Ahmed(L)

                        7. Samwar Begum(17) D/o Abu Subinyan(L)

                        8. Sominara Begum(15) D/o Hasim Mulla(L)

 

            All of Kutupalong refugee camp, Bangladesh.

 

            The system of transporting the girls as per version of the driver is so controversial which raised suspicion and the girls alongwith the driver are taken to Vairengte Police Station.

 

            On the morning of 24.4.2019, the girls were examined through interpreter as they spoke Rohingya language which is Eastern Indo-Aryan language similar to Bengali to some extent.

 

            Toyaba(22) D/o Soyid Salam, the eldest one stated that all of them are refugee from Myanmar and resided at Kutupalong refugee camp, Bangladesh. Sometime about 5 months ago, Nurkayas and Toyaba both residents of the same camp who used to talk Anamal Hussain who live in Malaysia enticed them to go to Malaysia through India and his brother will find suitable jobs for them. At first, they declined this offer but as the two men promised prosperous future and as their living condition in Bangladesh is so poor, the eight of them agreed to go to Malaysia.

 

            She and her sister Rubina left the camp on 17.4.2019 and when they reached Silchar, the six other girls were already available at the house, the area and owner they did not know.

 

           

 

 

On the 23rd April, 2019 evening, they were taken to MM Sumo counter, Silchar from where they boarded the Sumo. When they reached Police Check Gate, they were intercepted and taken to Police Station.

 

            Vairengte P/S case no. 42/2019 Dt.24.4.2019  u/s 14 Foreigners Act has been registered in connection with this incident. These eight(8) female Rohingyas were forwarded to District Court Kolasib  on 25.4.2019 for deportation. However, the exact time and how this will be implemented is not yet finalized. Presently, the 8 Rohihgyas are detained at Vairengte police station by improvising suitable accommodation for them.

            The above mentioned persons are suspected to be proceeding towards Myanmar but even after thorough interrogations were conducted they claimed that all of them are heading towards Hyderabad and even refused to disclose identity of sumo driver who brought them to Aizawl. Pi Denghliri of Edenthar Veng stated that within 2019, around 15/16 persons in groups (suspected to be victims of human trafficking), comprising of both males and females used to stay overnight at her residence 5 (five) to 6 (six) times already and she further stated that one person named Madiki of Tahan, Myanmar is the main culprit in facilitating these movements. Interrogations are going on at the time of writing this report to find out the real reasons behind these movements and all the 8 (eight) female suspected victims  are kept in Protective Home, Hunthar while the other 4 (four) Male suspected victims are kept in Bawngkawn Police station. Bawngkawn PS Case No. 117/19 U/S 370/IPC (3)(5)/34 IPC Dt.6.5.2019 was registered in this regard.
