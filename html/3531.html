A. Front page of  VANGLAINI  newspaper  Dt. 02.12.2022

High demand for Baktawng town utensils products: Chhuanthar, which is a sub village within Baktawng town area has been producing aluminium utensils for a considerable period of time. Previously, there were  dozens of families in Chhuanthar who were engaged in the manufacturing of these products, however, presently, there are only five families who still retained the trade. One of their products in particular, "Baktawng Cooker" is being demanded in great numbers especially from the neighbouring states of Nagaland and Meghalaya. However, the output of these cooker cannot meet the require demands as some of the craftsmen who were occupied in the moulding of these cookers shifted to furniture works after the Covid-19 pandemic hampered their business connection with other states for two consecutive years.

 

 

B. Front page of  VANGLAINI newspaper  Dt. 02.12.2022

Meeting in connection with G-20 meeting, 2023:  For the G-20 meeting which will be hosted by Mizoram during the month of March 2023, a meeting under the Chairmanship of Mizoram Chief Minister, Pu Zoramthanga  was held yesterday (1.12.2022) at CM Conference Hall, Aizawl. In this meeting, discussion was held on the consultation between G-20 Secretariat and Mizoram Home department, steps taken by the state in connection with its preparations, efforts to be undertaken and creation of various Sub-Committees. This upcoming G-20 meeting in Mizoram, which is an Engagement Group Meeting has been designated as - 'Start Up 20' and a core team has been created with the Secretary of Mizoram Home Department as its Chairman.

 

 

 

C. Front page of  The Aizawl Post newspaper  Dt. 02.12.2022

No VIP during the festive season : In a letter to all the Secretaries of the Central government, Chief Secretaries of states/UTs and state Governors, General Administration Department of Mizoram stated the inabilities of the state to have State Guest  which includes VIPs and dignitaries during the festive period of December i.e 15th December to 3rd January. In their letter, Mizoram GAD explained that the reason behind these inconveniences were because of the practice of Christmas and new Year celebrations by the entire population of Mizoram and their related programmes. However, it does not mean that no VIP and dignitary cannot visit Mizoram, only they cannot be receive as a State Guest during these period. This practice has also been often followed by other states when they are about to enter the most important  festival season of their respective states, officials from GAD added.
