CHRONOLOGY OF EVENT/ISSUES RELATING TO MIZORAM (SELECTION OF CANDIDATES FOR HIGHER TECHNICAL COURSES) RULES

                 The State of Mizoram has no facilities for Higher Technical Studies within the State and the seats allotted to the State of Mizoram for Higher Technical Studies by the Central Government are insufficient to meet the requirement of technical manpower of the State.

                 The state Government had so far rendered its best to ensure free and fair selection of candidates for the allotment of Professional and Technical courses using the Central guidelines notified. Alleged discrepancies and illegitimate practices however do took place in the past selection processes. With the increasing consciousness and better awareness on rights and privileges besides developing the concept of nationalism among the Mizos, at times the State Government have had witnessed successive upheavals since 1981 with regard to the alleged discrepancies or illegitimate practises on the part of the State Government in selecting candidates for these courses. These upheavals involves demonstrations and strong protest mainly spearheaded by the Students Organisations. The timeline of events/issues since 1993 may be highlighted as under:

 

 

6.7.1993 :   The Governor of Mizoram made Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1993 on 6.7.1993  to ensure fair and just selection of candidates for Higher Technical Studies in respect of Degree Courses of various disciplines in Engineering, Medical, Agriculture, AH and Veterinary etc.,”. The rules envisaged that Candidates will be selected in order of merit or mark percentage in PUSc. or Class XII (Sc.) exams as well as marks scored in personal interview and preference to  be given to those who can speak in Mizo language. (Annexure – I)

 

7.6.1999 : The State Government deems it expedient to have fair selection of promising candidates only for those limited seats offered to the State of Mizoram on merit by means of certain aptitude test.

                     Therefore, the Governor of Mizoram made the consolidated Rules ie., Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999 which was laid on 7.6.1999 for conducting Selection Test Examination to ensure the fair selection of candidates for such higher technical studies in respect of Degree Courses in various disciplines in Agricultural Sciences, Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Sciences, Medical Sciences and all branches of Engineering Science.

                     Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999 completely repealed Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1993. Written examination ie, entrance test was to be conducted through this rule and candidates are to be selected in accordance with their performance in the entrance test to qualify for studies in medical and engineering (technical studies). So, merits or percentage in PUSc. or Class – XII (Sc.) do not have much importance, performance in entrance test becomes the most important step to pursue technical studies. Sub-rule (3) of Rules 5 and its explanation are as under :-

 

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999 

Rule 5(1) All applicants are classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats

(a) Children of the local permanent resident of the Mizoram state as category – I.

(ii) Children of the non- local permanent resident of the Mizoram state as category – II.

(iii) Children of the Central/other state Govt employees not permanently serving in Mizoram as category – III.

 Explanation: For the purpose of this Sub-rule, the term ‘Local permanent Residents’ means those who are indigenous people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently  in the state.

(2) To be eligible to be considered under the Category – II and the Category – III, a Government servant should have completed at least two years of service in Mizoram and posted in the Offices of the State Government whether located within the State or not.

(3) On the basis of marks obtained in the Selection Examination, Selection shall be made in order of merit from amongst eligible candidates from category –I first. In case sufficient number of eligible candidates are not available from category-I, the remaining seats shall be filled in, in order of merit by the eligible candidates from Category-II and Category-III likewise. (Annexure – II)

26.9.2000 : The Hon’ble Guwahati High Court  in the Bhanu Choudhury vs State Of Mizoram And Ors cases(vide Civil Rule(PIL) No.1239 of  1994)   held Sub-rule (3) of Rules 5  of  Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999   to be ultra vires and  it had  struck  down. (Annexure – III)

 

15.7.2002 : As the State Government of Mizoram deems it expedient to have fair selection of promising candidates only for those limited seats offered and to strike some reasonable balance of reservation of seats for the different categories of candidates ;

                     Therefore, the Governor of Mizoram made amendment i.e   Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Amendment) Rules, 2002 which is written as under :-

 

Amendment to Rule 5(3): The whole portion of Rule 5(3) of the principal rule shall be deleted and replaced by the following

 

‘Allotment of seats shall be made in the following order

(i)Category-I : Children of local permanent resident-85%

(ii) Category-II: Non-Local permanent residents-10%

(iii)Category-III: Children of Central and State Government  employees posted in the state who have  completed  not less than 2 years in Mizoram on the last  day of submission  of application-5%. (Annexure – IV)

25.9.2014:             Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) General Headquarters organized a series of protest against the inclusion of Chakmas and other non-indigenous students under Category-I in the State Technical Entrance Examination (STEE). The MZP General Headquarters claimed that Chakmas are not indigenous people of the State of Mizoram and therefore should be excluded from the Category-I in the State Technical Entrance Examination (STEE).Due to this protest movement 13 (thirteen) student leaders were arrested by Mizoram Police.         

                     On 25.9.2014, thousands of students took out a protest rally within Aizawl City and in different part of Mizoram forcing the government to reach an agreement with the agitating students’ leaders.

23.4.2015   Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) and Government of Mizoram reached an agreement and made an amendment to the STEE Rules (ie, Mizoram Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Course Rules (Sixth Ammendment) Rules, 2015) which was published by Government of Mizoram in The Mizoram Gazette on 23rd April, 2015  as follows :-

 

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Sixth Amendment) Rules, 2015.

 

Amendment of Rule 5

Rule 5 of the Principal Rules shall be substituted by the following,

Sub rule (1 ) namely -(Method of Selection)

“(1) All applicants shall be classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats, viz :-

(a) Children of local permanent residents of the State of Mizoram, as Category – I.

(b) Children of other permanent residents of the State of Mizoram as Category – II.

(c) Children of Central/Other State Government employees staying in Mizoram as Category – III.

 

Explanation :

For the purpose of this sub-rule

(a) The term “Local permanent Residents” means those who are indigenous (Zo-Ethnic people who are native inhabitants) people of the State of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the State.

 

(b) The term “Other Permanent Residents” means those who are Non-indigenous (Non-Zo-Ethnic people who are non-native inhabitants) people of the State of Mizoram and those who are serving under the Government of Mizoram or Government Corporation or Autonomous Bodies under the State of Mizoram, or other recognised organisations and in whose case, it can safely be inferred that they would reside permanently in Mizoram. (Annexure – V)

 

16.7.2015 : Mizoram Chakma Students Union (MCSU) had submitted PIL(No.39/2015.Dt 23.6.2015) in Gauhati High Court challenging the  Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Sixth Amendment) Rules, 2015. On 16.7.2015, the Hon’ble Gauhati High Court disposed of the PIL after the Government of Mizoram withdrew the Sixth Amendment Rules of 2015 and put the back the Chakmas under Category I.

 

20.4.2016 : The Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules 2016 was again published on 20th April, 2016 wherein Category – I having 95 % of seats were reserved again for the permanent resident Zo ethnic tribes, Category – II having  4 % seats were reserved for the non Zo ethnic and the remaining 1 % seat was reserved for the children of Central Government Service personnel staying in Mizoram ie, Category – III.

                     With the commencement of this Rule, Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999 and its subsequent amendments stand repealed.

         Mizoram Chakma Students Union (MSCU) submitted a PIL again to Gauhati High Court as Chakmas were placed in Category – II. After hearing the petition on 24th June, 2016, Gauhati High Court passed a stay order on the functioning of Sub-rule (3) of Rule 5 of the Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 2016.

Sub-rule (3) of Rule 5 is written as under :-

    

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 2016

 

Methods of Selection : Rule 5

(1) All applicants shall be classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats, viz :-

(a)   Children of local permanent residents of the State of Mizoram as Category – I.

(b)   Children of other permanent residents of the State of Mizoram as Category – II.

(c)    Children of Central/Other State Government employees staying in Mizoram as Category – III.

 

Explanation :  For the purpose of this sub-rule :

(a) The term ‘Permanent residents’ means those who are Zo-ethnic people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the state.

(b)    The term ’other permanent residents’ means those who are (i) Children of non-Zo ethnic people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the state. (ii) Children of parents who are serving under the Government of Mizoram or Government corporation or Autonomous Bodies under the State Government who are regular employees of such organisation and in whose case, it can safely be inferred that they would reside permanently in Mizoram including children of deceased employees of the State Government whose families are living in Mizoram.

(c)    To be eligible to be considered under Category – III, either of the parents of the candidate(s) should have been residing and posted in Mizoram for at least three years.

 

(2) Percentage of seats for Category – I, II and III. 95 % of the total seats shall be observed for Category - I, 4 % of the Total seats shall be reserved for Category - II and 1 % of the total seats shall be reserved for Category - III. Determination of percentage for each Category has been made on the basis of average result of STEE (State Technical Entrance Examination) for the last 5 (five) years.

                           The disciplines in which seats are to be allotted to Category – I, II and III will be decided by the Selection Board by preparing seat matrix.

                     Vacant seats, if any due to unavailability of suitable candidates from amongst the Category-II and III shall be reverted back to Category – I.  (Annexure – VI)

12.5.2017 : On 12th May, 2017, Department of Higher  Technical Education, Mizoram amended Rule 5 of The Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 2016. The notification is meant only for the purpose of State Medical And Technical Entrance Examination (SMATEE), 2017 and it is a temporary measure. These Rules is called as The Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Amendment) Rules, 2017.  

                     The definition of Local Permanent Resident under Category – I as Zo-ethnic in Rule, 2016 (which Chakmas were opposed to it and caused them to submit PIL on High Court) is reverted to indigenous people of the State of Mizoram as was used before in the former Rule (Rule, 1999). (Annexure – VII)

 

20.7.2017 :  On 20.7.2017 @ 9:00 a.m, around 1,800 (one thousand eight hundred) students activists under the aegis of MZP General Headquarters gathered in front of the Office of Jt. Director, Higher & Technical Education (Technical Wing), Government of Mizoram at Chaltlang, Aizawl in protest against the allotment of Chakma students as against the Mizoram State quota for MBBS and BDS course besides to prevent Four Chakma students who had qualified the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)-2017 from entering  the Office building of Jt. Director, H&TE (Technical Wing) @ 9:20 a.m for Counselling Session. The four Chakma students however managed to enter the building  under the escort of Mizoram Police. Consequence upon the entering of the office building by Chakma students, MZP activists tried to break the Police barricade to enter the Counselling venue resulting in a brief tussle between the agitating students and Police. However, Police managed to contain the situation and few Police personnel and students sustained minor injuries during the preventive action of Police. The injured persons were brought to Civil Hospital, Aizawl for medical treatment.

                     Subsequently, an Emergency Meeting of MZP was held near Sericulture Office, Chaltlang and the meeting passed the following resolutions :-

 

  1. Counselling for filling up of seats in MBBS and BDS under Mizoram State quota be cancelled by Government of Mizoram.
  2.  State-wide Total Bandh be organised on 21.7.2017 if Counselling is not cancelled.
  3. Demanded the removal of Pu R. Romawia, Hon’ble Minister, H&TE, etc. and Pu B.D. Chakma, Hon’ble Minister, AH&Vety, etc. if Chakma students are not withdrawn from the Counselling. 

 

                     MZP General Headquarters had called off their agitation for the day @ 12:50 p.m. Altogether, 54 (fifty four) students have turned up for the Counselling session/class.

 

                     Meanwhile, leaders of MZP General Headquarters met Pu R. Romawia, Hon'ble Minister, H&TE, etc. on the evening of 20.7.2017 in his office chamber. Leaders of MZP demanded the Hon'ble Minister to take initiative for the removal of Chakma from the list of Category - I of the Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules and for removal of selected Chakma students from filling up of seats in MBBS and BDS under Mizoram State quota. An agreement was reached with the MZP according to which the State authority declared that despite the stay order of the Gauhati High Court on the functioning of Sub-rule (3) of Rule 5 of the Mizoram (Selection of candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules 2016, Category-I status would solely be availed by the Zo-ethnic tribe of the state of Mizoram in the concluded selection for Higher technical Courses.

                     The meeting decided to conduct fresh Counselling Session as soon as confirmation of seats allotment by Medical Colleges is received. However, the meeting agreed to let the Higher & Technical Department to go ahead with the allotment of MBBS and BDS seats already confirmed for the following Medical Colleges :-

         i)         RIMS              -           7 seats

         ii)        NEIGHRIMS -           3 seats

         iii)       Dibrugarh      -           1 seat

        

                           Subsequently, MZP General Headquarters held an Executive Meeting in the evening at MZP Pisa Pui, Aizawl and after a long deliberation, the meeting agreed to cancel the proposed state-wide indefinite Total Bandh @ 8:50 p.m.

 

26.7.2017:  Pu R.Romawia, Minister, Higher & Technical Education Department made a press statement declaring that the State Government is committed in safeguarding the rights and privileges of the indigenous Zo-ethnic people and will ensure that the Category-I status will be availed by them only.

2.5.2018 :   Government of Mizoram and Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) have been taking necessary actions regarding the PIL against them in Gauhati High Court by Mizoram Chakma Student Union (MCSU). Pu JC Lalnunsanga, Legal Adviser of MZP represented the Government of Mizoram. Non-appearance of Lawyers of Mizoram Chakma Students Union (MCSU) in the Court even after intimation over telephone made Gauhati High Court dismissed the PIL against Government of Mizoram by MCSU on 2nd May, 2018. Hence, the stay order passed on 24.6.2016 on the functioning of The Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 2016 by Gauhati High Court stands vacated. (Annexure – VIII).      

                     Information received from Higher Authority of Higher & Technical Education Department indicated that The Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 2016 shall be used for Higher Technical Studies in respect of Degree Courses of various disciplines from this year onwards.
