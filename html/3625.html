31.5.2022 : National Students' Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram State in its Press Release issued on 31.5.2022 congratulated all successful students in the Higher Secondary School Leaving Certificate (HSSLC) 2022 Result which was declared today. It also stated that the pass percentage was lower compared to last year and in this regard, it urged all teachers and students to review their performance. It further encouraged all unsuccessful students to try much harder.

3.6.2022  : National Students' Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram State in its Press Release issued on 3.6.2022 stated that leaders of NSUI met Pu Ramhmangaiha Ralte, Chairman MSSSB today in connection with their recent recruitment. It also stated that 3 (three) non-Mizos General category candidates were selected for appointment under Mizoram government by MSSSB, which was allegedly created to show favouritism to party workers in its recent recruitment. It also stated that non-Mizos (General Category) candidates are allowed to apply for job in the guidelines being in used by MSSSB, which was prepared by DP&AR. It further urged the state government to review the said guidelines and prepare proper criteria. It also stated that the said guideline prepared by DP&AR is not safe for the mizo nation and in this regard, it expressed their desire to strengthen Mizoram Public Service Commission (MPSC) so as to conduct more recruitment.

14.6.2022 :    National Students Union of India (NSUI) District Headquarters Lawngtlai in its Press Release issued on 14.6.2022 stated that necessary enquiry was conducted in connection with alleged presence of irregularities in Primary School Teachers Recruitment Examination conducted by the Education & Human Resource Development Department, Lai Autonomous District Council (LADC) during 7-8.6.2022 and found existence of various irregularities viz., some candidates brought mobile phone in the examination hall, leakage of question papers, illegal assistances given to the candidates by invigilators etc. It considered the said exam as not reliable/valid and requested LADC Executive to cancel the examination. It further requested Pu V Zirsanga, CEM, LADC and Pu L. Daniala, Executive Member i/c Education, LADC to constitute an Enquiry Committee to enquire into the alleged presence of irregularities in the stated examination and to take necessary legal action against the offender(s).

28.6.2022 :    National Students' Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram State in its Press Release issued on 28.6.2022 expressed regret and strongly condemned Pu Lalrintluanga Sailo, MLA for allegedly threatening Headmaster, Govt. Middle School, West Lungdar and Senior Teacher over Telephone. It considered the same as deprivation of other's right. It also stated that the school was constructed using NABARD loan of Rs.29 lakhs, and the Office and Kitchen were not constructed and at present, toilets are in dilapidated condition. It further stated that on previous occasion, MZP Sub-Headquarters, West Lungdar dismantled the said school due to its sub-standard construction, and the school building has not been occupied as it was considered not fit for occupation. It strongly condemned the MLA for his intention to transfer teachers of Govt. Middle School, West Lungdar and threatening them over Telephone, and considered the same as disrespect of the teachers, students and the general public.

5.7.2022 :      National Students' Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram State submitted a written petition to the School Education Minister on 5.7.2022 wherein they stated that the existing Govt. West Lungdar Middle School building was constructed in the year 2002 and the school office building in 2013-2014, and both the buildings are still fit for occupation. It also stated that the new school building was constructed at another location using NABARD loan of Rs.29 lakhs and it is reported that Pu Lalrintluanga Sailo, constituency MLA also contributed Rs.7 lakhs for construction of the new school building. At the new location, office and kitchen are not constructed and all students could not be accommodated as the building is too small. Further, there is no playground for students. It further stated that school authority and leaders of the village desire for reconstruction of the old school building from NABARD loan, and as they are not in a position to occupy the new school building due to the stated reasons, it is perceived that the MLA threaten 2 (two) school teachers for transfer, viz., Pi Roliankimi, Headmistress and Pu Khawlrochhunga, School Teacher. In this regard, they requested the Minister not to transfer the said teachers.

18.7.2022 :    National Student Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram in its Press Release issued on 17.7.2022 stated that as per RTI information, fund received by the Sports & Youth Services Department under PMJVK project fund for development of sports infrastructure has been withheld by Finance Department and it urged the Chief Minister and authority of Finance Department to immediately release the said fund. 22.8.2022 :    National Students' Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram state in its Press Release issued 22.8.2022 stated that North East National Student Union of India Conclave (meeting of NSUI leaders of various Northeast states) was held yesterday i.e., 21.8.2022 at Rajiv Bhavan, Guwahati, Assam. The conclave was attended by 3 (three) NSUI Mizoram leaders led by Pu RB. Lalmalsawma, President NSUI. The various difficulties faced by students of North-eastern states were highlighted in the meeting and they decided to submit a proposal to NSUI Headquarters, Delhi concerning setting up of a dedicated team to cater the needs of NE students, who are facing difficulties. It also decided to take necessary action to increase the number of entry into Civil Services by north-eastern youths. It further decided to organize cultural exchange program so as to familiarize the north-eastern states by Central mainland.

16.9.2022 :    NSUI issued a Press release on 16.9.2022 in which they appeal to the contractors under RUSA for the timely completion of construction works in different colleges in Mizoram under RUSA project. NSUI alleged that they have conducted verification of construction works under RUSA and found that many construction works are at slow pace. They expressed their serious concern over the slow progress of constructions and urged the contractors to refrain themselves from delaying construction and waiting for revised estimate of their project.

3.11.2022 :    National Students' Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram in its Press Release issued today, i.e., 3.11.2022 expressed regret over imposition of economic blockade by Kolasib District Land Owners Association on 3.11.2022 due to alleged delay in payment of land compensation for the proposed four lane road. It also expressed regret over delay in payment of compensation despite the alleged depositing of money to be paid for compensation into the joint account of NHIDCL and Competent Authority for Land Acquisition in July, 2022. In this regard, Pu RB Lalmalsawma, President NSUI contacted DC, Kolasib and requested to take necessary action for stranded students due to the road blockade.

7.11.2022:     National Students' Union of India (NSUI) Mizoram State in its Press Release issued on 7.11.2022 stated that leaders of NSUI submitted a written petition to the concerned authorities for repair of the dilapidated Aizawl to Lunglei road near BN Hospital on several occasions.
