****BRU NATIONAL LIBERATION FRONT (BNLF)****

1. On 21.10.97 BNLF militants killed Pu Lalzawmliana, Wild Life Game Watcher of Forest Department near Persang village. West Phaileng PS Case No. 15/97 dt. 22.10.97 u/s 302 IPC.

2. Confirmed information indicated that between 19th and 21st Oct '97, members of BNLF visited Bru villages of Tuirum, Khamrang, Falkawn, Hnahthialzawl and Damparengpui and threatened the villagers to leave Mizoram immediately, stating that they are going to attack Mizoram Police Forces.

3. On 27.10.97 suspected BNLF militants coming from Tripura State visited Zawlpui village and threatened Bru families to leave Mizoram on the night itself. Accordingly 30 Bru families left for Tripura.

4. On 23.11.97 BNLF verbally declared all Brus from Mizoram should leave Mizoram before 26.11.97.

5. On the night of 30.11.97, 3 BNLF militants visited Khawhnai village and threatened the Bru families that they should leave Mizoram immediately. Accordingly, 6 Bru families left the village the same night.

6. Two Bru persons armed with gun, shot at Ohenrai s/o Kalicharan of South Chhimluang and forcibly took away Rs. 2,900/- from his wife.

7. On 3.3.1998, between 6:00 AM and 7:00AM two Mizo women viz. Zikpuii and Zairemi both of Kanhmun village (Mizoram) were assaulted and robbed near Zamuang village. The culprit snatched away wrist watches and some money from them. The culprit was identified as Lalsanga Bru s/o Goti Bru of Zamuang village. The infuriated mob from Rengdil, Zamuang and Zawlnuam, who were in search for the culprits torched some Bru houses on 3.3.1998 and 4.3.1998. Kanhmun PS Case No. 5/98 dt. 4.3.1998 u/s 394 IPC was registered.

8. On 27.3.98 at about 2:15 PM, suspected BNLF extremist threw hand Grenades at a Police Truck bearing Regn. No. ZRP-463 carrying water in between Tuipuibari and Chikha. As a result, 3 (three) Syntex tank carried by the truck were completely damaged. However, there was no loss of life or injury. West Phaileng PS Case No. 5/98 dt. 1 .4.98 u/s 307/358/427 IPC r/w 3 of ES Act.

9. One person named Kristiana (Bru) of Sesih village near Buhchang under Aizawl District was shot dead by 2 suspected Bru militants using SBBL gun on the night of 19.4.98.

10. On 11.4.98 at about 5:30 PM, BNLF militants ambushed the Mizoram Police Truck (Water Carrier) at a place 4 Kms west of BSF Camp at Tuipuibari. The militants threw a number of Grenades and thereafter resorted to discriminate firing at the Police Water Carrying Truck. As a result the Police driver Hav. Vanlalnghaka and Constable Lalnunngila were seriously wounded. A bullet passed through the heel of the Commander of the Police Party. As the Police Party returned the fire, the BNLF militants escaped into the thick jungle under the cover of darkness. They left behind one .303 Rifle Mark-III, containing 5 live rounds, one misfired H/E Hand Grenade (KF) and one SBBL gun and empty cases. It was later on confirmed from the villagers who assisted the militants in digging trench at the ambush site, the BNLF militants were 10/13 in numbers and they had escaped into Bangladesh. West Phaileng PS Case No. 6/98 u/s 3007/353/427/121(A)/34 IPC r/w 25(1-B)(a) 27 Arms Act was registered.

11. On 11.4.1998, militants ambushed Mizoram Police Truck near Tuipuibari. Two Police personnel seriously injured. One SBBL gun and one Rifle were captured by Police.

12. On 17.4.98, 3 (three) unidentified BNLF militants entered Thangnang village located in Sajek range of Bangladesh and forcibly abducted one Lalbiakpuia (20) s/o Rothanga of South Dampui, Lunglei District who had gone to visit his ailing grandfather-Laldova. The abductors discussed in Bru/Riang dialect and decided to kill Lalbiakpuia, incidentally the victim understood the Bru dialect. On the pretext of attending nature's call abductors agreed to untie him. Taking advantage of the situation, he jumped down from the road and managed to escape under the cover of darkness in the jungle.

13. As a result of frequent threat and feeling of insecurity, quite a few Mizo families living at scattered places in North Tripura have recently left their homes and orange gardens and they have come to Zampui Hills of Tripura where Mizos are in good numbers. The report indicates that 35 Mizo families with 271 members have come to Zampui Hills.
