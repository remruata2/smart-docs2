**10.3.2022 :** Mizoram Kohhran Hruaitute Committee (The Mizoram Church Leaders Committee) submitted a written representation to Shri. M. Venkaiah Naidu, Hon'ble Vice President of India on 10.3.2022 wherein they stated the following :-

 (i) Requested to take necessary action so as to ensure intervention of Union  Government in providing necessary assistance to Myanmar Refugees,  who are presently taking shelter within the State.
 (ii) Requested his intervention for amicable settlement of long inter-state  boundary disputes.

 (iii) Requested to take necessary action for re-consideration of the stringent  provisions of Foreign Contribution Regulation Act (FCRA) so as to  encourage more donations from abroad to NGOs, religious  establishment and institutions in India.

**14.3.2022:** Mizoram Kohhran Hruaitute Committee (MKHC) submitted a written petition to the Chief Minister of Mizoram on 12.3.2022 wherein they requested the following in connection with COVID-19 SOP :-

 (i) Restriction on singing hymns and guidelines for church service should  be left at the hands of the respective Churches in the SOP.

 (ii) Restriction on night church service during church Assembly/Conference  should also be lifted as the same was permitted in normal church  service.

****22.9.2022 :** United Pentecostal Church of North East India - North Mizoram District in its Press Release issued on 21.9.2022 stated that Supreme Court of India has settled 27 (twenty seven) years long dispute between United Pentecostal Church of North East India and Mizoram UPC. It also stated that the Supreme Court of India had dismissed the Special Leave petition (SLP) submitted by Mizoram UPC against the judgment of Gauhati High Court on 7.9.2022. It further stated that the Apex Court upheld the Gauhati High Court judgment which stated that Mizoram UPC has no right to claim the property owned by UPC denomination upto the year 1995 as Mizoram UPC left the said church in the year 1995 and hence, dismissed the SLP submitted by Mizoram UPC. Such being the case, all properties (before occurrence of dispute between the two parties) under the custody of Mizoram UPC should be handed over to UPC NEI. It also stated that in pursuance of the SC Judgment, UPC NEI North Mizoram District held a meeting on 13.9.2022 regarding procurement of properties under its jurisdiction and necessary action is being taken for immediate handling over of all the properties, which are under the custody of Mizoram UPC.**
