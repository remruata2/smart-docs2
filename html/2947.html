SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/04

Dated: 6th January, 2019.

 

Information has been received that North East Students’ Organisation (NESO) is intending to organise a North East wide Total Bandh on 8.1.2019 (Tuesday) in protest against the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 purportedly to be passed by the Parliament. Consequently, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP), which is affiliated to NESO, called an Emergency meeting today, i.e. 6.1.2019 @ 12:00 noon at their office which was attended by 43 (forty three) members including representatives from NESO (Mizoram), Hmar Students’ Association (HSA), Mizoram Bawm Students’ Association (MBSA) and SU leaders from Govt. Aizawl West College, Govt. J. Thankima College and Govt. Johnson College. The meeting resolved to support the decision of NESO and intended to organise a state-wide Total Bandh on 8.1.2019 between 5:00 a.m to 4:00 p.m in Mizoram as well.

In a Press Release issued today, i.e. 6.1.2019, MZP Gen. Hqrs. highlighted their intention to organise a state-wide Total Bandh on 8.1.2019 and appealed the people of Mizoram to support the Total Bandh. The Press Release further highlighted that the following personnel will be exempted during the Bandh :-

  1. Media persons
  2. Police/Magistrate
  3. PHE Emergency services
  4. Hospital staff
  5. Milkmen
  6. P&E Emergency services

MZP will deploy volunteers at the following places within Aizawl :-

1.    Kulikawn                      2.         Sikulpuikawn                3.         Treasury Square

4.    Khatla kawn                 5.         Tennis Court                 6.         Temple Square

7.    Vaivakawn                   8.         Company Peng 9.         Bawngkawn

10.  Zemabawk                   11.       Chaltlang                      12.       Ramhlun Industry peng

13.  Chanmari                      14.       Zarkawt                       15.       Bazar Bungkawn

16.  College Veng   

 

Information is received that MZP Gen. Hqrs. further notified its Headquarters, Sub-Headquarters and Branches to organise the Total Bandh in their respective places and also requested Lai Students’ Association (LSA), Lawngtlai District and Mara Students’ Organisation (MSO), Siaha District to organise the same in their respective places. It is reported that the 2 (two) Student bodies are likely to render moral support at the time of writing this report.
