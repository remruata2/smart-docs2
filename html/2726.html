NATIONAL REGISTER OF CITIZENS (NRC)

 

1.    An Executive meeting of MZP General Hqrs was held on 3.8.2018 @ 12:00 noon at MZP Conference Hall which was presided over by L. Ramdinliana Renthlei, President and attended by about 30 (thirty) members. The meeting discussed issues pertaining to Mizoram-Assam boundary and felt that the Core Committee which had been set up to conduct survey on the actual boundary, in which MZP is also a member, is unable to make any conclusive report within the stipulated time, i.e. July, 2018, and hence decided to pull out of the Core Committee. The meeting also resolved that they are committed to constructing 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' at Zophai. The meeting also discussed about National Register of Citizens (NRC) in Assam and resolved that steps should be taken for implementing the same in Mizoram and they intended to call a Press Conference at Aizawl Press Club tomorrow, i.e. 4.8.2018 as a pre-step awareness for the public.

 

2.    BJP, Mizoram in their Press Release issued dt. 3.8.2018 highlighted that detection of illegal migrants in Mizoram in line with the National Register of Citizens (NRC) followed by Assam Government would be carried out if they are elected to power. Pu Clement Liankunga (IAS, Retd.) also stated that lack of Administrative and Financial Reforms have stood in the way of all-round development, the Press Release added.

 

3.    MZP Gen. Hqrs. called a Press Conference at Aizawl Press Club on 4.8.2018 @ 12:00 noon. About 10 (ten) MZP leaders and 10 (ten) media persons were present at the conference. MZP President, L. Ramdinliana Renthlei, who conducted the press conference, expressed fear of possible influx of illegal migrants from neighbouring Assam State following the release of draft report of NRC on 30th July, 2018 which left more than 40 lakhs people as illegal migrants. Alleging the Government as being indolent while other neighbouring states, viz. Meghalaya and Nagaland in particular, have adopted pre-emptive measures by deploying additional Police force to prevent possible infiltration, the MZP in its Press Statement dated 4.8.2018 stated that they would initiate action themselves by constructing a 'check gate' if the Government fails to deploy additional Police force along Assam border areas before 15th August, 2018.

 

4.    MNF in their Press Release issued on 6.8.2018 highlighted that a meeting was convened today at Mizo Hnam Run, Aizawl which was chaired by Pu T.C. Pachhunga, Gen. Secretary and attended by around 160 (one hundred sixty) party workers. Speaking in the meeting, Pu R. Tlanghmingthanga, Vice President accused Pu Lal Thanhawla, Hon'ble Chief Minister of uttering baseless allegations saying that he would not have to quit the post of Chief Minister had he been afraid of Vai (non-Mizos); MNF have surrendered to embrace the Indian culture and religion and that MNF had sold Mizo Nationalism to Arjun Singh. The only way to assimilate Mizo Nationalism is embracing Indian National Congress, he added. He further urged the Government to take drastic steps to block possible influx of foreigners resulted from the National Register of Citizens (NRC) followed by Assam Government and at the same time, supported MZP's intention of constructing infiltration gates at strategic locations if the Government failed to act, the Press Release added.

 

5.    In the wake of recent publication of Final Draft of NRC by Government of Assam, leaders of MZP Gen. Hqrs., Aizawl urged the State Government to prevent the influx of foreigners and other non-indigenous persons into Mizoram and also expressed their readiness to assist the concerned authorities.

 

6.    MPCC in their Press Release issued on 10.8.2018 highlighted that a Political Session, attended by about 180 (one hundred eighty) party workers, was convened today at Congress Bhavan and it was chaired by Pu C. Chawngkunga, Gen. Secretary, MPCC. Speaking in the meeting, Pu Lal Thanhawla, Hon'ble Chief Minister stated that they have not disbursed money arbitrarily but they rather helped the needy workers/farmers to have a meaningful lives and that they will not end their flagship programme, i.e. NLUP despite strong criticisms from the opposition parties. He further added that Mizoram has shown progressive trend in Gross State Domestic Product (GSDP) and Per Capita Income due to various commendable steps taken by the Congress Government. He went on to say that Brus fled Mizoram on their own volition and they keep on attacking Mizos based on false allegations at the Central Government on many occasions and they are embraced by the Central Government as they followed similar religion, i.e. Hindu. He further added that he had demanded similar rehabilitation packages for the victims of ethnic clashes between Kukis and Paihte in the past which was turned down by the Central Government. He reiterated that he is in favour of equal rehabilitation packages for the already repatriated families with those in line for repatriation. Advanced precautionary measures have been carried out to block influx of illegal foreigners into Mizoram as a result of the publication of Final Draft of National Register of Citizens (NRC) by Assam Government before it surfaced in the media, the Chief Minister added.

 

7.    In a Press Release on 16.8.2018, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) highlighted that MZP has strongly urged Government of Mizoram to take necessary steps in checking illegal entry of non-indigenous persons from entering Mizoram in the wake of publication of National Register of Citizens (NRC) by Government of Assam. MZP alleged that the State Government has not taken necessary initiatives and in this regard, leaders of MZP stated that they will take necessary initiatives in checking non-indigenous persons from entering Mizoram illegally. MZP urged its Headquarters, Sub-Headquarters and Branches from inter-state borders to establish check gates and start checking of illegal entry into Mizoram. MZP also urged the authorities not to issue Inner Line Permit (ILP) to non-indigenous persons from Assam who are not registered under NRC.

9.    In a Press Release issued on 20.8.2018, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) Gen. Hqrs., Aizawl strongly urged the Hon'ble Education Minister of Mizoram and Education Department officials for proportionate Transfer and Posting of School Teachers in Urban and Rural areas of Mizoram. Leaders of MZP highlighted that as many as 158 (one hundred fifty eight) Teaching Staff were engaged in non-teaching duty at various Education Department offices in Aizawl and urged the Education Department to address the issues of shortages of Teaching Staff at rural areas. Leaders of MZP stated that more than 40 (forty) lakhs people left out in the National Register of Citizens (NRC) published by Govt. of Assam have migrated to neighbouring states and in this regard, MZP stated that they have urged the Government of Mizoram to conduct checking of illegal entry of non-indigenous persons into Mizoram. MZP alleged that since the State Government has not taken necessary initiatives, leaders of MZP stated that they have conducted a State-wide checking of non-indigenous persons staying illegally in Mizoram on 17.8.2018. MZP highlighted that as many as 20 (twenty) non-indigenous persons who are not listed under Assam NRC were pushed back from Mizoram. The Press Release also added that leaders of MZP Gen. Hqrs., Aizawl will conduct checking of NRC excluded non-indigenous persons who are illegally staying at Bairabi, Vairengte and Saiphai areas of Mizoram w.e.f. 20.8.2018.

 

10. In a Press Release issued on 21.8.2018, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) Gen. Hqrs. Aizawl highlighted that MZP have detected more than 200 (two hundred) 'foreigners' illegally trying to enter into Mizoram. Leaders of MZP stated that these 'foreigners' are the 40 (forty) lakhs or more people who are not included in the list of Government of Assam publicised final draft of National Register of Citizens (NRC). MZP also stated that other Assam neighbouring states have taken proactive measures to counter the migration of these foreigners into their states and in this regard MZP have also urged the Government of Mizoram to conduct checking of illegal foreigners in Mizoram. MZP alleged that since the State Government has not taken necessary initiatives, checking of foreigners staying illegally was conducted by MZP in Mizoram since 17.8.2018. Leaders of MZP also highlighted that, MZP will continue checking of foreigners who are illegally trying to enter into Mizoram through Bairabi, Vairengte and Saphai (in collaboration with Hmar Students' Association Unit, Saiphai). MZP Press Release also appeals the Mizo Public to support their cause.

 

11.  It has been reported that leaders of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) Gen. Hqrs., Aizawl who have engaged themselves in checking of illegal non-indigenous from entering Mizoram have withdrew themselves from Bairabi, Vairengte and Saiphai on 23.8.2018. Checking of non-indigenous persons from entering Mizoram will be continued by MZP Sub- Hqrs. of Vairengte, Bairabi and Saiphai at their convenient time. It is also reported that leaders of MZP Gen. Hqrs., Aizawl are likely to hold a meeting in the near future to discuss their further course of action. Situation is under close watch.

 

12.  ZPM in their Press Release issued on 23.8.2018 highlighted that a public meeting was held yesterday, i.e. 22.8.2018 at Khuangleng village in East Tuipui Constituency and it was graced by Pu Lalduhoma, ZPM Chief Ministerial Candidate and Pu Lalduhawma, Incharge, East Tuipui constituency who were on a political tour. Pu Lalduhoma stated that National Register of Citizens (NRC) would be given a priority if they are elected to power while condemning the Government for not doing enough to prevent possible influx of foreigners into Mizoram as a result of  Draft List of NRC published by Assam Government. He further reiterated ZPM's commitment to allocation of separate budget for purchasing local agricultural and allied products and adoption of 'Hand Holding' policy, the Press Release added.

 

13.  In a Press Release issued on 27.8.2018, leaders of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) Gen. Hqrs., Aizawl stated that the non-indigenous persons of Assam who are excluded in National Register of Citizens recently published by Assam Government are foreigners. MZP stated that these foreigners are migrating to the neighbouring states. In the Press Release, leaders of MZP highlighted that since 16.8.2018, they have established check posts to prevent foreigners from illegally entering Mizoram and also stated that as many as 424 (four hundred twenty four) foreigners were detected from check gates at Bairabi, Vairengte and Saiphai. MZP also highlighted that the detected foreigners trying to illegally enter into Mizoram were pushed back in collaboration with Mizoram Police.
