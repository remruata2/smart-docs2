SPECIAL  REPORT

 

                                                                                    No.MSB/CB/SR/2012/980

                                                                                    Dated Aizawl, Friday,

                                                                                                the 7th December, 2012.

 

1.         HPC(D) leader arrived Guwahati :

            Reference our previous S/R dated 06.12.2012 para (1), input received that S/S Acting Chairman HPC(D) left Imphal by flight today, i.e. 07.12.2012 and arrived Guwahati @2:30pm. His purpose, as reported earlier, is to visit Lalropuia S/S Army Chief who is now undergoing medical treatment in GNRC hospital, Guwahati, Assam.

2.         Update on Abduction of three Bengali traders :

             Reference our previous S/R dated 26 & 29 Nov, 2012, information received stated that the three Bengali Teak (timber) traders abducted by a combined group of NLFT and BNCT on 25.11.2012 from Tuipuibari-II were held at NLFT camp known as “Hnahbok Camp” located inside Kasalong forest west of Zopui village in Sazek hills of Bangladesh. It is learnt that families of the abductees were last contacted on 02.12.2012 through mobile phone by the abductors urging them to immediately pay up their ransom demand of Rs 30 lakhs.

3.         Sighting of unidentified armed group near Kawrtethawveng:

            Unconfirmed information received stated that movement of an unidentified group numbering approximate 20 (twenty) carrying weapons were seen at a location 1 (one) km west of Kawrtethawveng, Mamit District of Mizoram last night i.e. 06.12.2012 @10:00pm. They were sighted by villagers who were out for hunting when this armed group wearing civilian dress were moving towards Mualthuam, a small Bru village bordering Tripura.

            Identity of this group though not immediately known, were suspected to be elements of either NLFT or BNCT as they reportedly spoke a dialect closely similar to that of Bru, and a Police team had been sent to the area to ascertain the information and take necessary steps.
