JUDGEMENT & ORDER

 

  1. The prosecution story of the case in brief is that on 12.3.2012, an FIR was lodged by SI Rokima Chhangte S/o C. Vanlaldika of Ramthar Veng, Aizawl with the O/C, Aizawl PS stating that on 12.3.2012 at 7:00 am, in pursuance of their information joint operation with BSF, Aizawl Sector Staff, CID(SB) and DSB, Aizawl was held and they apprehended Vanlalpeka (27) S/o H.Linga of Tuithiang Veng, Aizawl and seized two firearms i.e. 1 no. of 9mm Pistol (fabrique nationale) B/R. No. W9A143 and 1 no. of .38 Revolver (Smith & Wesson) B/R No. 896299 from the residence of S. Noel Lalrintluanga. In this regard, he requested for necessary action. Hence, Aizawl PS C/No. 107 dt. 12.3.2012 u/s 25(1A)(1AA)/25(1B)(A) Arms Act was registered and duly investigated into.

 

  1. During the course of investigation, the accused Vanlalpeka was arrested and informing his ground of arrest also  seized 1 no. of 9mm Pistol (fabrique nationale) B/R. No. W9A143 and 1 no. of .38 Revolver (Smith & Wesson) B/R No. 896299 and all available witnesses were examined. The property was seized in presence of reliable witnesses and their statements were recorded. The said seized items were also forwarded to Forensic Science laboratory to prove that the firearms were genuine, serviceable etc. and the result came back positive. The accused  was thereof kept at PS lock up for further interrogation. A prayer for 72 hrs remand into police custody was sent to court and 48 hrs remand was allowed. During interrogation, he admitted his guilt and confessed that he did it for his personal gain. Prayer for prosecution sanction was accorded by the honourable magistrate. However, his counter-partner Liankhuma of Myanmar who he stated as the owner of the seized firearms could not be found after giving several efforts to locate him. From the above facts and circumstances, a prima facie case u/s  25(1A)(1AA)/25(1B)(A) Arms Act was found well established against the accused Vanlalpeka and charge sheet was submitted accordingly.

 

  1. The defence case was that the accused was innocent.

 

  1. During the course of trial, the prosecution examined 5 witnesses.

 

  1.  PW-1 Lalsawmliana stated that on 12.3.2012 the police invited him as he was the Assistant Secretary of YMA, Chanmari Branch to witness recovery and seizure of some suspected illicit article. Accordingly,  they proceeded towards Mag. C Agency at Chanmari. When they reached, the police searched within the store. In the meantime the police asked to the shopkeeper namely Pu Noel-a whether the accused put two pistols within his shop and Pu Noel-a took out one .38 revolver and 9mm pistol from the rack inside his store and he stated that that the said revolver and pistol belonged to the accused Vanlalpeka. He further stated that the accused took in the store the day before. Thereafter, the shop keeper Pu Noel-a contacted the accused through telephone. After sometime, the accused appeared and tried to take out his .38 revolver and 9mm pistol. So the police arrested him. The accused also admitted that the said revolver and 9mm pistol were belonged to him. So police seized revolver and 9mm pistol in his presence and he also stood as one of witnesses. He put his signature on the body of the seizure memo. Exbt. P-1 was the seizure memo and Exbt. P-1(a) was his signature. Exbt. M-1 was the seized revolver and Exbt. M-2 was 9mm pistol.

 

  1. PW-2 Lalrokima Chhangte, CID(SB) identified the accused person. On the second week of March 2012, he received information from reliable source stating that one person kept two prohibited bore pistol and the same was trying to sell within Aizawl city. As per information, they conducted investigation within Aizawl city for searching the said prohibited arms and the accused. On 11.3.2012 they conducted joint operation with the SB and BSF G but they could not find positive result. On the same day as per information received by different unit they learned that the probable name and possible location of the person involving in this illegal possession and on the same day the location, the name of the suspected person was confirmed. It was further confirmed that the suspected person was keeping arms in his possession and the same was also trying to sell within Aizawl city. But they could not know the exact location where the arm was kept. On the following day after going through countless difficulties it was by noon time when the location of suspected person and the place where he kept the arms became secretly known and they proceeded towards the PO i.e. Chanmari Ramhlun Rd. When they reached the shop of Mr. Noel Lalrintluanga, his brother Mr. Eddy and two employees of the said shop were present. After waiting for sometime the shop owner Noel Lalrintluanga returned to the shop and he and Eddy stated that two persons tried to sell the said prohibited arms. But they did not know the name of the owner of the said prohibited arms. They also further stated that the accused and Lalsawmkima kept and hide a small bag inside his shop. Eddy took out small bag and they checked and found one 9mm pistol (made in Belgium) with empty magazine) and .38 Revolver ‘Smith and Wesson’ (made in USA). In the meantime as per advice by him Mr. Noel contacted through telephone to the accused to take out the said arm and thereafter the accused tried to take out the said arm and they apprehended the accused and he seized the two prohibited arms in the present of two civilian witnesses and he prepared seizure memo and he lodged the FIR to the O/C, Aizawl PS. Exbt.P-1 was seizure memo, Exbt. P-1(b) was his signature, Exbt. P-2 was FIR and Exbt. P-2(a) was his signature. Exbt. M-1 was the seized article in respect of .38 Revolver and Exbt. M-2 was the seized article in respect of 9mm pistol. The place where the seized articles were recovered as stated in the FIR and seizure memo was the same building as well as the same floor. They used for residence and as a shop. When the accused tried to take out the said two arms he also claimed ownership and he stated that the said two arms belonged to him.

 

  1.  PW-3 Jonathan L. Ralte, SI of Police identified the accused and stated that on 12.3.2012 a written FIR was received from SI Lalrokima Chhangte stating that on the same day at around 7:00am in pursuance of their information they conducted joit operation with BSF, Aizawl, CID and DSP, Aizawl. They recovered two firearm i.e. one number of 9mm pistol B/R No. W9A143 and one .38 Revolver B/R No. 896299 from the residence of  S.Noel Lalrintluanga. They also apprehended the accused person. The case was registered as Aizawl PS C/No. 107 dt. 12.3.2012 u/s 25(1A)(IAA)/25(1B)(A) Arms Act. The case was endorsed to SI Lalbuatsaiha for further investigation. During his investigation he obtained prosecution sanction from the Dist. Magistrate, Aizawl and he sent the said the two firearm to the FSL for further investigation. He also arrested the accused person. The said two firearms were also already seized by SI Lalrokima. FSL report was also received back by SI Lalbuatsaiha. Unfortunately, SI Lalbuatsaiha was transferred to Darlawn PS and he took up the case by instruction of O/C. From the investigation conducted by SI Lalbuatsaiaha and materials available on record, he found prima facie u/s 25(1A)(IAA)/25(1B)(A) Arms Act against the accused person and he submitted charge sheet accordingly. Exbt. P-3 was charge sheet submitted by him and Exbt. P-3 was his signature.

 

  1. PW-4 Lalbuatsaiha, SI of Police, identified the accused person. He stated that on 12.3.2012 a written FIR was received from SI Lalrokima Chhangte stating that on the same day at around 7:00am while he was at Aizawl PS. The case was registered as Aizawl PS C/No. 107/12 u/s 25(1A)(IAA)/25(1B)(A) Arms Act against the accused person. Accordingly he arrested the accused person and he recorded his statement. Thereafter, he also packed and sealed the seized article and the seized article was sent to the FSL, Aizawl Mizoram for further examination. Unfortunately, he was transferred to the Darlawn PS. Exbt.P-4 was arrest memo submitted by him and Exbt.P-4(a) was his signature. Exbt.P-5 was forwarding note Exbt.P-5 (a) was his signature.

 

  1.  PW-5 R. Sangzuala stated that on 29.3.2012 FSL Mizoram Aizawl received 2 Exbt. For further examination which were packed in 1 card board paper. Accordingly, he examined the said Exbt. Such as one 9 MM Pistol and one .38 Revolver. The weapon 9mm pistol was in working condition. The manufacturer and country of origin was Fabrique condition and the manufacturer and country of the origin is Smith & Wessen, USA. Exbt. P-6 was FSL report and Exbt. P-6(a) was his signature.

 

  1. The accused was examined u/s 313 Cr.PC and he was asked the question that from the evidences available on record, on 12.3.2012 exbt.M-1 and M-2 was recovered from the shop of Noela which was belonged to him and he put the arms to the shop and what he wanted to say and replied in negative.

 

  1. I heard the ld. PP and Addl. PPs who submitted that from the evidences available on record the prosecution proved the guilt of the accused and prayed to convict the accused accordingly.

 

  1. I also heard the id. D/L who submitted that exbt.M-1 and 2 were not recovered from the possession of the accused and that from the evidences it was very clear that the exhibits were recovered from the shop of Noel Lalritluanga but Noel Lalrintluanga was not examined by the prosecution and the prosecution failed to prove the guilt of the accused and prayed to acquit.

 

  1. PW-1 Lalsawmliana is the only independent witness and from his statement it appears that on 12.3.2012 the police asked the shop keeper Noela whether the accused put two pistols in his shop and Noela took out one .38 revolver and 9mm pistol from his shop and stated that the two arms were belonged to the accused Vanlalpeka and the accused put the day before. Further it also appears that the shop keeper telephoned the accused and the accused appeared and tried to take out the arms and the Police arrested him and the arms. He identified exhibit P-1 the seizure memo and exbt.M-1 and 2 the seized materials.

 

  1. In his cross examination he did not deny the suggestion that he was not sure whether the accused is the person who was alleged to be the owner of exbt.M-1 and 2 who was present at the time of recovering the articles.

 

  1. From his cross examination it appears that the witness did not identify the accused who came to the PO at the time of recovering the exbt.M-1 and 2 and the witness was not sure whether the accused was the person who was alleged to have put the exbt.M-1 and 2 to the shop of Noel.

 

  1. The statement of PW-2 Lalrokima, SI, CID(SB) was in para 6 above and from his statement it appears that at the time of recovering exbt.M-1 and 2 Noel Lalrintluanga and his brother Eddy and two other employees of the shop were present and Eddy took out small bag wherein the two exhibits were put in. He also identified exbt.P-1 the seizure memo and he was also the complainant who lodged the FIR and he proved the FIR exhibit P-2 and he also identified exbt.M-1 and 2. The seizure memo and the FIR were proved.

 

  1. PW-1 stated that the two exhibits M-1 and 2 were taken out by Noela who stated that it was put by the accused. However, PW-2 stated that the two arms were taken ,,out by Eddy. There is discrepancy and I am unable to know who took out the two arms.

 

  1. In his cross examination, PW-2 did not deny the suggestion that the two arms were seized from the shop of Noel Lalrintluanga and did not recover from the possession of the accused and he also did not deny the suggestion that seized articles were not seized by him.

 

  1. PW-4 Lalbuatsaiha, SI of Police was the Case i/o who obtained the prosecution sanction from the District Magistrate and he also sent the two arms to the FSL however before his completion of investigation he was transferred to other place. He arrested the accused and he proved the arrest memo exbt.P-4. In his cross examination he stated that he was not the seizing officer and PW-2 also stated in his cross examination he was not the seizing officer but in the seizure memo exbt.P-1 the seizing officer is PW-2 Lalrokima.

 

  1. PW-3 Jonathan L. Ralte, SI of Police continued the investigation after PW-4 was transferred and he filed the charge sheet exbt.P-3 and exbt.P-3 the charge sheet was proved.

 

  1. PW-5 R. Sangzuala, Asst. Director, FSL examined the two arms and exbt.P-6 is his report and he proved the report and from his report and statement it appears that the two arms were working condition and serviceable.

 

  1. In this instant case the prosecution did not examine Noel Lalrintluanga in whose shop the two arms were recovered and they did not also examine the brother of Noel Lalrintluannga Eddy who was also allege to have taken two arms from the shop.

 

  1. In view of the above facts and circumstances, I hold that the prosecution is unable to prove the guilt of the accused beyond a shadow of all reasonable doubts and the accused is acquitted accordingly.

 

  1. The seized materials exbt.M-1 and 2 vide CMR No. 65/12 shall be confiscated.

 

  1. The case stands disposed off accordingly.
