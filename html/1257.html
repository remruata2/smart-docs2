i) Security scenario in Barak Valley (Assam) and Mizoram, particularly areas bordering with Bangladesh: The security scenario of the western part of Mizoram bordering with Bangladesh is comparatively peaceful. However, due to the occasional incident of kidnappings/abduction occurred in the border areas because of the porousness of the Indo-Bangladesh border required more attention. The border between Mizoram and Bangladesh, especially Mamit District side of Mizoram and Bangladesh, deserves special attention in and around Dampa Wildlife Sanctuary Abduction cases have had occured in which 5 (five) persons have been abducted by NLFT on the month of Feb.2013. Subsequently, 3(persons) have been abducted by the combined team of NLFT and BDFM in the later part of the same year, out of which 2 Mizos have been released on 20.01.2014 and then Deep Mandal, Airtel Surveyor was also released on 22.3.2014. In this regard, an outpost of IR Bn. had been established at Chikha in Dampa Tiger Reserve area, Mamit District. The security condition of the said areas is being kept under constant watch.

iv) Trans-border crimes and illegal movement through border:   

In the end of January 2014, about 30 armed gang of Shantibahini were seen near the river of Keisalam inside Dampa Tiger Reserve area by civilians. One of them wore camouflage and the others were in civil dress. All of them carried AK-47 rifle and green bag with gun barrel and butt in it. They came from the upstream of Keisalam and talked/spoke to the civilians, they also bought a long tailed lizard (Tangkawng in Mizo) for ` 250/- in Indian currency and 4 kgs. of fish for 300 Taka of Bangladesh currency. Then they left towards Indo-Bangladesh Border following the river.

 

There is also a foot path from the main road at 2 kms. to Phuldungsei village which reach the forest. Therefore, it is believed that the arm smugglers and the militants used this route to transport their arms and ammunitions through Dampa Tiger Reserve towards Bangladesh.

vii) Miscellaneous/ Any other issues:    

          Input received on 19.02.2014 regarding the presence of a group of Armed HPC (D) cadres numbering approximate 10 to 13 at Parvachawm and Senvawn villages located south-west of Parbung, Churachandpur District had corroborated with debriefing of two HPC (D) deserters who recently surrendered before SP, CID (Special Branch). This HPC’D’ group had been intensifying their unlawful activities such as, Extortion, Intimidation, etc.. ever since electioneering of the recently concluded Legislative Assembly Election in November, 2013. The group is also responsible for the shooting of Pu B. Zahunga of Khawlian on 20.11.2013.

 

After preparing an elaborate plan for operation, a joint team of Mizoram Police assisted by Major H.Lalnunsangi, Officer Commanding of Team 2/1 CTIS Det, Eastern Command Counter Intelligence Unit stationed in Aizawl and 34th Assam Rifles (having AOR of the area in Manipur) conducted simultaneous raids on Parvachawm and Senvawn villages during 2:00 AM – 9:00 AM of 19.02.2014.
