**I. RETALIATION AGAINST INDEFINITE POSTPONEMENT OF WRITTEN EXAMINATION BY PRESTA**

 Over 200 members of Pre-Service Teachers Association (PRESTA) gathered in front of Chief Minister’s bungalow gate on 20.6.2013 at around 9:30am. The PRESTA resorted to picketing the Hon’ble Chief Minister in retaliation to indefinite postponement of written examination slated to be conducted on 20th June, 2013 for filling up of 169 posts of teachers on account of voluntary retirement of teachers. The police dispersed the agitated members of PRESTA. According to Pre-Service Teachers Association’s report, two of their members namely, Malsawmtluanga Colney of Chhipphir and Emily Lalthankungi of Lunglei Farm Veng were injured in the scuffle. The injured persons were taken to Aizawl Civil Hospital and were discharged after administering first aid.

 The PRESTA leaders subsequently issued a press statement and stated the following points :-

 (i) Extremely saddened by the sudden cancellation/postponement of the written examination on the evening of 19.6.2013 which is slated to be conducted on 20th June, 2013 for filling up of vacant posts of teachers on account of Voluntary Retirement Scheme (VRS) and urged the Government to conduct the said examination at the earliest.

 (ii) Eligibility for the examination does not demand TET and over 800 applicants tried to appear for the written exam. Therefore, PRESTA demand an early conduct of examination.

 (iii) The PRESTA strongly condemned the initiative of MSU.

 (iv) The PRESTA would oppose to the conduct of TET exam for those SSA teachers who were engaged after the year 2010.

 (v) MTET and VRS Exam Centre should be set up at Aizawl and Lunglei.

 (vi) Government should arrange Rs. 8 lakhs for the traveling expenses already incurred to by over 600 applicants coming from different parts of Mizoram.

 (v) PRESTA condemned the injuries inflicted to its members.
