SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/92

Dated: 12th February, 2019.

 

 

In continuation of our Special Report No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/90 Dated 11.2.2019. National Trade Union of Mizoram (NTUM) held a peaceful Sit-in demonstration outside the main entry gate of New Secretariat Office today, i.e. 12.02.2019 @ 11:00 a.m. Around 200 (two hundred) Muster Roll workers participated in the demonstration headed by
Pu C. Remtluanga, President NTUM, which was conducted in support of ‘Proposal for revision of Minimum Wages rates in Mizoram’, one of the agenda of the Cabinet Meeting held today @ 11:00 a.m at Chief Secretary’s Conference Hall. During a short interview by reporters at the spot, Pu C. Remtluanga claimed that they had gathered peacefully without any intention to cause trouble in anticipation of receiving a positive outcome on the proposal laid before the Cabinet which they had been demanding for quite some time. The meeting approved the revision of Minimum Wages rates and the gathering dispersed peacefully @ 12:45 p.m after they had been informed about the approval accorded to the proposal by the meeting.
