A. _Front page of VANGLAINI /THE AIZAWL POST newspaper Dt. 17.6.2022_

**Meeting between Chief Minister and Assam Rifles****:******Yesterday i.e 16.6.2022, Pu Zoramthanga,Chief Minister held a meeting with senior officers of Assam Rifles in Mizoram at CMO Conference Hall, Aizawl on relevant matters relating to the shifting of Assam Rifles from the heart of Aizawl city to Zokhawsang (Located at the outskirt of the city).

 According to Government of Mizoram press release, Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) had notified the Assam Rifles back in the year 2019 to vacate their cantonment in Aizawl. However, in spite of this, only a fraction of them had moved to Zokhawsang.

 The meeting decided Assam Rifles to take necessary action for immediate shifting. The meeting also highlighted measures taken by Mizoram Government development of infrastructures at Zokhawsasng.

 The Chief Minister was accompanied by Dy. Chief Minister, Home Minister and Land Revenue &amp; Settlement Minister in this meeting while the Assam Rifles were represented by Brigadier of 23 Sector DIG and other senior staff.

B. _Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 17.6.2022_

**No rules for disposal of confiscated grape wine:******According to information obtained by Vanglaini newspaper, meeting of Council of Ministers held under the Chairmanship of Chief Minister, Pu Zoramthanga on 25th March 2022 had approved Mizoram Liquor (Prohibition) Draft Rules, 2022 submitted by Mizoram Excise &amp; Narcotics Department which prohibited liquor and all its related substances.

 However, this MLP Draft Rules, 2022 can be implemented only after it is publicised in the government gazetted , which is not yet done. As such, for the time being, Mizoram Government has no legal means on how to dispose the confiscated grape wine which were seized by Excise Department from Millennium Centre and other parts of Aizawl city on 27th May 2022 and are still in their custody.
