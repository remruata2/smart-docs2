15.2.2022:            Provisional Employees Association of Mizoram (PEAM) held its Executive Emergency Meeting today, i.e., 15.2.2022 @ 1:00 p.m. at PWD Site Office, MINECO, Aizawl. Pu Lalhruaitluanga Sailo, President PEAM, presided the meeting and 20 (twenty) members attended. As per input, the meeting decided to resort to Mass Casual Leave on 17-18.2.2022 as no favorable information regarding their various demands has been received from the State government till date.

15.2.2022      In continuation of our DSI Part-VI Dt.15.2.2022, Provisional Employees Association of Mizoram (PEAM) served a written petition to all Head of Departments, Government of Mizoram on 16.2.2022 wherein they requested not to create any difficulties to provisional employees under their respective departments in connection with their intention to resort to Mass Casual Leave on 17-18.2.2022.

17.2.2022 :    Ref. to DSI Part-VI (a) Dt. 16.2.2022, Provisional Employees under various departments across the state availed Mass Casual Leave on 17.2.2022 in demand of immediate fulfilment of their various standing demands viz. resumption of Regularisation of Provisional Employees Scheme, which is being under suspension due to enforcement of austerity measures by the state government; implementation of National Pension System (NPS) for provisional employees. They intended to continue to avail Mass Casual Leave on18.2.2022.

                                    In connection with the above ongoing agitation, Provisional Employees Association of Mizoram (PEAM) General Headquarters held a meeting on 17.2.2022 @ 1:30 p.m. at Directorate of Agriculture Office, Tuikual South, Aizawl. Pu Lalhruaitluanga Sailo, President PEAM presided the meeting and 32 (thirty two) members attended. The meeting expressed regret regarding suspension of Regularisation of Provisional Employees Scheme due to enforcement of austerity measures, and decided to take necessary action, if their ongoing agitation by availing Mass Casual Leave bore no fruitful result. The meeting also urged the government to take measures for implementation of New Pension Scheme (NPS) for provisional employees. Information received stated that leaders of PEAM met Pu JC Ramthanga,
Addl. Chief Secretary on 17.2.2022  @ 12:00 Noon at the latter's invitation and it was reported that the meeting could not arrive at amicable agreement on the demands of the PEAM. Input received also stated that Master Roll (MR) employees also extended their support to PEAM ongoing agitation.

 

 

 

 

18.2.2022:     Reference to our DSI Part-VI (b) Dt. 17.2.2022, Provisional Employees under various departments across the State continued to avail Mass Casual Leave today, i.e., 18.2.2022. Leaders of PEAM stated that at present, there are around 9,000 provisional employees under various departments across the state.

            9 (nine) leaders of Provisional Employees Association of Mizoram (PEAM) led by Pu Lalhruaitluanga Sailo, President PEAM met the Chief Minister of Mizoram today @ 11:00 a.m. at the latter's office. It was reported that the Chief Minister requested them to await up to April, 2022 regarding their demand for resumption of Regularisation of Provisional Employees Scheme and advised them to meet Finance Commissioner regarding implementation of New Pension Scheme (NPS) for provisional employees.

            In this connection, a Meeting was held today @ 1:00 p.m. at PWD Site Office, MINECO, Khatla, Aizawl. Pu Lalhruaitluanga Sailo, President PEAM presided the meeting and 36 (thirty six) members attended. The meeting decided to call off their ongoing agitation w.e.f. from today and to meet Finance Commissioner in the coming week regarding implementation of New Pension Scheme (NPS) for provisional employees.

01.8.2022 :    National Trade Union of Mizoram (NTUM) General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 1.8.2022 stated that the State government has prepared Rules in the year 2011 in order to enforce The Shop and Establishment Act 1948, Central Act within the State and Minimum wages could be fixed in accordance with the Act. However, due to various difficulties, the stated Act could not be enforced till date. It also stated that they expected the Cabinet Committee to pass the Minimum wages under The Shop and Establishment Act which will be placed before the committee considering the work condition of marginalized workers. It further appealed the competent authority to take necessary action so as to make the stated Act enforceable within the state.
