Mizoram shares 318 Km long international boundary with Bangladesh, These porous international borders with Bangladesh served as important routes for Insurgent Groups to procure arms and ammunition and also to smuggle contraband goods and illicit drugs. The porous border areas have also widely encouraged other criminal activities like abduction/ kidnapping of Indian citizens by Bangladesh based insurgent groups.

 

In order to curb the infiltration, smuggling and other anti-national activities from across the Indo-Bangladesh border, the Government has undertaken the construction of fencing along this border.

 

The Indo-Bangladesh Border Fencing was started in Mizoram in the year 2005. The total length of international border fencing proposed along Mizoram- Bangladesh border was 344.41 Km out of which about 70% work have been completed.

 

According to Mizoram State Boundary Committee, at least 15 Indian villages in Mizoram are now within Bangladesh side of the border due to irresponsibility of the construction companies who are fencing the Mizoram- Bangladesh border. The Boundary Committee also found that the fences pass through the middle of 20 other villages.

 

Proper management of borders, which is vital to the national security, presents many challenges and includes coordination and concerted action by the administrative, diplomatic, security, intelligence, legal, regulatory and economic agencies of the country to secure the frontiers and serve its best interests.

 

Approach and practices of border management vary from one border to another, based on the security perceptions and relationship with the neighbouring country.

Mizoram has a long international boundary with two countries (512 kms with Myanmar and 318 kms with Bangladesh). There are 3 (three) Battalions of BSF deployed at Indo-Bangladesh border. In addition, the State government has deployed Mizoram Police (MAP/IR Bn.) along the border supplementing the central armed forces.

Factors affecting Indo (Mizoram)-Bangladesh Border Management

(i) The porous international borders with Bangladesh served as an important routes for Insurgent Groups to procure arms and ammunition and also to smuggle contraband goods and illicit drugs.

(ii) The porous border areas have widely encouraged other criminal activities like abduction/kidnapping of Indian citizens by Bangladesh based insurgent groups.

(iii) The porous border area also open for influx of foreign nationals from Bangladesh i.e Chakmas/Bengali Muslim and Myanmarese from Myanmar into the state of Mizoram. The junction of Mizoram, Bangladesh and Myanmar is also vulnerable for infiltration point of Muslim Rohingyas from Myanmar.

(iv) The recent increase in the smuggling of Gold and contraband drugs like Pseudoephedrine, Methamphetamine across the border has called for immediate and proper attention. In this regard, Mizoram Police has made several recoveries.

(v) Identical tribes live on both sides of the boundary pose difficulty in restricting the movement of the people.

(iii) Border Roads:

Along Mizoram-Bangladesh Border, a stretch of 303.97 km. length of border patrol-roads has also been constructed out of the sanctioned length of about 602. 28 km.

(iv) Border Out Posts (BOPs)

Out of the total of 91 Border Out-Posts (BOPs) approved, the construction in respect of 18 BOPs has been completed and the work in other 73 BOPs are yet to be established along Mizoram-Bangladesh Borders

(v) Special Task Force (STF)

In order to detect and deport settling down of illegal immigrants of Bangladesh nationals in Mizoram, 3(three) Special Task Force (STF) had already been set up in three district of Mizoram namely Lunglei District, Mamit District and Lawngtlai District as desired by Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. Each STP is headed by Deputy Commissioner of the concerned District.

(vi) Arrest of Bangladesh nationals:

Mizoram Police have so far arrested 59 Bangladesh nationals illegally entering Mizoram during 2008-2016(till November, 2016).

(vii) Recovery of Arms & Ammunition:

Due to the sustained efforts by Mizoram Police, during the last two year (Le. 2015-2016) as many as 57 sophisticated weapons and about 8212 ammunitions were recovered from various arms smugglers and insurgent groups operating along Indo- Bangladesh borders.

Indo-Bangladesh Border-Issues relating to Mizoram

 

1). Bawm/Bangladesh Refugee:-

 

 Brief history :-

                 A military operation against Kuki Chin National Army (KNA) by Bangladesh Army on 15.11.2022 at Chhaihkhiang Village, Bangladesh resulted in the mass exodus of civilians, mainly the Bawm community during which 272 civilians including women and children fled their villages to take shelter inside Mizoram.

Subsequent military operations led to 3 major influxes of Bawm civilians into Lawngtlai District of Mizoram which briefly halted during the Election process of Bangladesh in January 2024 and the peace talk between the KNA and Bangladesh Government. However, the looting of Banks and arms by KNA on 2nd and 3rd April 2024 resulted in an intensified operation against the KNA leading to more influxes of Bawm community into Mizoram.     

The latest Bawm refugees numbering 93 persons who entered Damdep-II Village, Lawngtlai District, Mizoram on the night of 04.07.2024 were evacuated by BSF personnel to Crocodile Camp, Kamtuli in an attempt to deport the refugees. However, with the intervention of the State Government, the 93 Bawm refugees who were joined by another 33 Bawm refugees at Chhippui BSF BOP were safely transported to Chamdur Village on the morning of 08.07.2024.

2). Bangladesh Internal Conflict and recent clashes in Chittagong Hill Tracts (CHT) area:-

            The reservation of 30% of Government Job for descendants of freedom fighters in Bangladesh sparks a students’ protest across universities in Bangladesh. As the protest intensified, thousands of protesters stormed and ransacked the official residence of the then Bangladesh Prime Minister, Sheikh Hasina demanding the resignation of Seikh Hasina who subsequently fled the country on 5th, August. Three days later, an interim government  under   Nobel

Prize winner Mohammad Yunus was formed in the violence torn Bangladesh to bring back normalcy.

            On 18.09.2024, a Bengali man named Mohamed Mamun, who was suspected of theft was lynched to death in Khagrachhari District of Chittagong Hill Tracts of Bangladesh by a mob consisting of Chakma and Marma communities. The situation escalated leading to violent clashes between the Bengali settlers and the minority community in the CHT which reportedly resulted in the deaths of at least three people and destruction of numerous homes of the Chakma community. Acknowledging the reported atrocities of the indigenous Chakmas in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, the Chakma community of Mizoram under the aegis of Central Young Chakma Association along with all the civil society organizations of the Chakma community organized a protest rally at Kamalanagar, Mizoram on 22.09.2024 followed by a candle lighting rally on the same night. The protest rally was participated by almost 2500 protesters who demanded the intervention of the Indian Government in the reported atrocities against the indigenous Chakma community of Bangladesh.

            The border guarding forces as well as the state police were alert to check any possible influx of the Chakma community of Bangladesh who had fled their homes. However, there is no report of any illegal entry of the Chakma community from Bangladesh into Mizoram, India due to the incident.

 

3). Allegation made by BGB regarding availability of Training Camps of Bangladeshi Insurgent within Mizoram :-

            There have been instances of allegations by Border Guard  Bangladesh (BGB) that miscreants of Chittagong Hill Tracts (CHT) particularly Parbatya Chattagram Jana Samhati Shamiti (PCJSS), United Peoples Democratic Front (UPDF), Kuki Chin National Front (KNF) are either staying inside India or establishing their camps inside India which was last highlighted in the Veracity Report of BGB Booklet during the DG, BSF and DG, BGB Level Border Conference held at Dakha, Bangladesh between 5th to 9th March 2024. However, District Police and CID (SB) of Mizoram Police independently conducted spot verification/inspection during the month of August and October 2023 as well as in the month of January 2024 and found no such miscreants or their camps inside Mizoram, India particularly in Lunglei and Mamit Districts.

 

4). Border Trade at Tlabung :-

            Since there is no infrastructure at the Integrated Check Post (ICP), Kawrpuichhuah (Tlabung, Lunglei District), there is no manpower posted at Kawrpuichhuah from Mizoram Police. However, as envisaged by MHA, minimum required posts were created vide Home Department’s notification no. A.11013/3/2019- HMP dt.08.09.2022, but approval for recruitment or filling of posts not yet sanctioned by the Government.

            Since there is no infrastructure, there is no immigration counter manned/available at ICP, Kawrpuichhuah.

             Commercial Vehicle Terminal Building was inaugurated on 12th April, 2023 at Tlabung, Lunglei District, Mizoram by the then Hon’ble Minister of State (Transport) Pu TJ Lalnuntluanga. However, due to non-availability of manpower, it is non-functional.

5). Indo-Bangladesh border fencing:-

 Mizoram shares 318 Km long international boundary with Bangladesh. These porous international borders with Bangladesh served as important routes for Insurgent Groups to procure arms and ammunition and also to smuggle contraband goods and illicit drugs.  

              In order to curb the infiltration, smuggling and other anti-national activities from across the Indo-Bangladesh border, the Government has undertaken the construction of fencing along this border.

             The Indo-Bangladesh Border Fencing was started in Mizoram in the year 2005. The total length of international border fencing proposed along Mizoram-Bangladesh border was 318 Km.

              According to Mizoram State Boundary Committee, at least 15 Indian villages in Mizoram are now within Bangladesh side of the border due to irresponsibility of the construction companies who are fencing the Mizoram- Bangladesh border. The Boundary Committee also found that the fences pass through the middle of 20 other villages.

             Border fencing work could not be undertaken along a 60-km stretch in the Dampa Tiger Reserve owing to pending clearances from the National Board of Wildlife, which has demanded that the State transfer of around 1,520 hectares of non-forestland in compensation.

             Mizoram Chakma Development Forum (MCDF) claimed that about 49.7% of the total Chakma population in Mizoram has lost their houses, lands, cultivation fields and others properties due to the ongoing India-Bangladesh border fencing in Mizoram. On 21 April 2006, the State Government constituted a “State Level Co-ordination Committee on Border Fencing” to ensure that the victims received proper compensation and rehabilitation.

            In order to seal the Indo-Bangladesh border, the Government has adopted multi-pronged approach which include construction of fencing, floodlighting, Border Out Posts (BoPs), induction of latest surveillance equipments like Hand Held Thermal Imagers (HHTI), Night vision Goggle/Devices, etc.

 

6). FICN/Drugs smuggling/Arms smuggling:-

 

            (a) FICN – There is no report of smuggling or seizure of Fake Indian Currency notes (FICN) across the Indo- Bangladesh Border within Mizoram.

 

(b) Drugs – Smuggling of Narcotic Drugs from Bangladesh side into Mizoram is not reported. However, smuggling of Narcotic drugs reportedly Methamphetamine tablets (Yaba tablets) from Myanmar into Bangladesh through Mizoram cannot be ruled out.

 

  (c) Arms – Smuggling of arms from Myanmar to Bangladesh particularly after the capture of Myanmar Army Camps/base by the resistance forces is reported and confirmed with the recent interrogation of accused person arrested on 17.09.2024 in c/w BKN PS C/no. 302/24 Dt.17.09.2024 u/s 25(1A)/25(1AA) Arms Act r/w Sec.14 Foreigner Act in a seizure of 1000 rounds of live 7.62 ammunitions at Zuangtui, Aizawl by Mizoram Police.
