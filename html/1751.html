Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP)

 

The Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project is a project that will connect the eastern Indian seaport of Kolkata with Sittwe seaport in Rakhine State, Myanmar by sea. In Myanmar, it will then link Sittwe seaport to Paletwa, Chin State via the Kaladan river boat route, and then from Paletwa by road to Mizoram state in Northeast India. Originally, the project was scheduled to be completed by 2014 but it has not been completed till today.

Route

The project has several sections combining multi-modes of transport:

  1. 539 km shipping route from seaport of Kolkata in India to Sittwe seaport in Myanmar via Bay of Bengal. This sea route has been operational for several decades.
  2. 158 km river boat route from Sittwe seaport to Inland Water Terminal (IWT) at Paletwa Jetty via Kaladan river in Myanmar, (June 2017)
  3. 110 km road route from IWT Paletwa to Indo-Myanmar border in Myanmar. Construction contract awarded in June 2017.
  4. 90 km route from Indo-Myanmar border at Zorinpui to Aizawl-Saiha National Highway at Lawngtlai District, Mizoram in India by road on National Highway 54 (India) (NH-54), which then continues further to Dabaka in Assam via 850 km long NH-54 which in turn is part of the larger East-West Corridor connecting North East India with the rest of India.

 

History

This project will reduce distance from Kolkata to Sittwee by approximately 1328 km and will reduce the need to transport good through the narrow Siliguri corridor, also known as Chicken's Neck.

            Initially India had tried to persuade Bangladesh to offer transport and transit rights to the north eastern states. However, Bangladesh has consistently refused to grant such rights, including access to its Chittagong port, which is less than 200 km away from Agartala, the capital of Tripura.

            The project is being piloted and funded by the Ministry of External Affairs (India). The preliminary feasibility studies were carried out by Rail India Technical and Economic Services (RITES). Construction work on Sittwe port and the boat jetty in Paletwa, as well as the dredging work, will be executed by the Inland Waterways Authority of India (IWAI), with Essar Projects Ltd, a division of the Essar Group appointed in May 2010 as the main contractor.

            Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project initially faced problems such as underestimation of the road length in Myanmar and plans to construct hydro-electric projects — Chhimtuipui River and Lungleng River — on two tributaries of the Kaladan River followed by another project downstream. That the first two projects are being built by one public sector undertaking and the third is being constructed by another PSU (Public Sector Unit) has also led to coordination issues. Due to construction of this hydro electrical project, Navigation of boats could be effected.

            The project includes a waterway component of 158 km on Kaladan River from Sittwe to Paletwa in Myanmar and a road component of 109 km from Paletwa to Zorinpui along the India-Myanmar border in Mizoram.

Development

On 19th April 2016, Indian Minister General VK Singh informed that 66% of the new 2-lane-per-direction 12-meter-wide 99.83 km National Highway 502A (NH502A) starting from NH-54 at Lawngtlai to Zorinpui in Mizoram on Indian side is done and will be completed by June 2018. Other aspects of the project including the port, waterways and 109 km Zorinpui on the India-Myanmar border to Paletwa will be completed by April 2019. Mr Lalrinngheta, a Project Site Engineer on the India side, also reported the work is ongoing.

            In April 2017, India handed over the operation of completed Sittwe port and Inland Water Terminal at Paletwa to Myanmar. In June 2017, India handed six gas tanker cargo vessels worth US$81.29 million (K110.08 billion) to the Myanmar government to transport gas to north-east India via Manipur. Work on the port in Sittwe and the IWT in Paletwa, Chin State, is in its final stages, and the six cargo vessels are meant to facilitate transportation of goods from Sittwe to Paletwa. The $81.29 million cost of the vessels was met through a grant from India. The construction work was assigned to IWT in October 2012, the keels were laid in March 2013 and the vessels were launched between April and December 2016. On completion of the tests and trials at Yangon, the vessels reached Sittwe in March 2017. Acceptance trials were completed in April 2017 in Sittwe.

             

Activities of Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLAO) on Land Compensation of KMMTTP

1.    Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLOA) held its General Meeting today, i.e. 24.5.2016 @ 11:30 AM at J.H. Lorrain Hall, Lawngtlai Bazar under the chairmanship of Pu H. Remmawia, President, LDLOA. The meeting passed the following resolutions :-

       i)  Condemned the undue delay in distribution of compensation to land      owners under Kaladan Multi Modal Transit Transport Project          (KMMTTP) and determined to organize picketing against the      companies under KMMTTP from 1st June, 2016 if the State      Government fails to take up the process allocation of fund for payment           of compensation to them.

       ii) The rate of compensation under KMMTTP be equivalent and in par       with compensation rate of other districts in Mizoram.

       iii)Demanded the authority to give compensation prior to the          commencement of any project in Lawngtlai District in future.

2.    Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLOA) commenced picketing against staff/workers of RDS Company and ARSS Company, Contractors engaged under KMMTTP from 1.6.2016 in protest against the undue payment of compensation to LDLOA members. Due to this picketing called by LDLOA, it was reported that workers under RDS Company and ARSS Company stayed away from their work.

3.    Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLOA) continued their picketing against staff/workers of RDS Company and ARSS Company, Contractors engaged under KMMTTP for the 23rd consecutive day i.e. 23.6.2016 in protest against the undue payment of compensation to LDLOA members. Due to this picketing called by LDLOA, it was reported that workers under RDS Company and ARSS Company continued to stay away from their work and Mizoram Police has been performing their duties to control law and order situation.

4.    Lawngtlai District Land Owner Asociation (LDLOA) had called off their indefinite picketing against staff/workers of RDS Company and ARSS Company, Contractors engaged under KMMTTP on 8.7.2016 as their demand for land compensation has been processed by the Government. In this regard, LDLOA had issued a Press Release on 8.7.2016 in which they expressed their gratitude to Pu H. Zothangliana, Parliamentary Secretary; Dr. A. Muthamma IAS, Dy. Commissioner, Lawngtlai District; Pu H. Vanlalthaliana EM, i/c Revenue, LADC; Pu H. Chhuanawma EM, i/c Forest, LADC; Pu V.L. Hmuaka CEM, LADC and Pu Lalsanglura, SP Lawngtlai District for taking initiative to fulfill their demands.

5.    Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLOA) held a meeting on 29.8.2016 @ 12:30 PM at their Office, Lawngtlai. Pu H. Remmawia, President chaired the meeting and 9 (nine) members were present. The meeting rejected the proposed lists of beneficiaries of compensation which were not included in the earlier Land Revenue Survey. They also decided to hold General Meeting if the DC, Lawngtlai did not complete draft award lists before 5th September, 2016.

6.    Office Bearers Meeting of Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLOA) was held on 7.9.2016  and the meeting decided to issue  stay order against the on-going works of KMMTTP if the DC, Lawngtlai District could not complete all the necessary works for the awarding of compensation to the land owners before 20th September, 2016. The meeting also demanded not to include any new beneficiaries whose names were not included in the earlier Land Revenue Survey.

7.    Lawngtlai District Land Owners’ Association (LDLOA), Lawngtlai under Kaladan Multi Modal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) held a meeting on 3.6.2017 at Merchant Office, Lawngtlai. Pu H. Remmawia, President, LDLOA presided over the meeting and the meeting deliberated upon their grievances over the delay in payment of compensation by PWD, KMMTTP Division, Government of Mizoram. The meeting decided to await the response of the Government with regard to their demand till 7.6.2017 and resolved to organise picketing against workers under RDS Company and ARSS Company, contractors under KMMTTP and staff of PWD, KMMTTP Division, Government of Mizoram if the Government did not give positive response.

8.    Information has been received that Lawngtlai District Land Owners’ Association (LDLOA) decided to hold indefinite blockade of the Kaladan Multi-Modal Transport Transit Project (KMMTTP) Road from tomorrow, i.e. 20.2.2018. The demonstration will be held in demand of payment of compensation which has been pending since the last 9 (nine) years. LDLOA is likely to appeal public support in their agitation and to avoid plying along KMMTTP Road during their agitation.

9.    Dr. Arun T, Deputy Commissioner, Lawngtlai District held a meeting with leaders of Lawngtlai District Land Owners’ Association (LDLOA) on 22.2.2018 at Lawngtlai DC Office Chamber. The meeting was attended by Pu Lalsanglura, Superintendent of Police, Lawngtlai District; Pu C. Lalhumhima, EE, PWD, MMPD-II and other officials. Dr. Arun T informed the meeting that necessary measures have been undertaken by the DC Office with regards to the pending compensations. Leaders of LDLOA appreciated the actions taken by the authorities and with immediate effect, LDLOA relaxed their blockade of KMMTTP Road for all other user except workers under M/s Atlanta - ARSS (joint venture) and RDS Project Ltd. who was engaged in the construction works. It is reported that no vehicle has been plying on this road albeit the relaxation by LDLOA and no untoward incident reported till today.

10. Lawngtlai District Land Owners’ Association (LDLOA) held a meeting at their office, Lawngtlai on 2.4.2018 @ 7:00 am under the chairmanship of Pu H.Remmawia, President, LDLOA. The meeting decided to stage indefinite blockade of a highway constructed under the Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) from 2.4.2018 in protest against alleged non-payment of compensations for their lands to be acquired for the ongoing highway construction works. The LDLOA slammed the Central Government for turning a deaf ear to their demands even after the Deputy Commissioner of Lawngtlai had reportedly approved as many as 303 (three hundred and three) compensation applications. They passed a resolution that  LDLOA will not allow any constructions works to be carried out by RDS and ARSS companies at the site and that any violations of the diktat will invite stern reaction from the LDLOA.
