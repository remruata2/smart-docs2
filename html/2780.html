                 Further information received indicated that the seized materials were suspected to be belonging to the Arakan Liberation Army (ALA), one of the Myanmar militant groups alleged as having their camps inside Bangladesh territory. Input has been received that about 12 (twelve) ALA cadres along with their commander, namely, Kioksu arrived Ajasora (a tiny hamlet of about 14 houses) i.e. ½ km away from Bandukbanga village,  Lawngtlai district from Bangladesh territory on 23.12.2019 to spend Christmas festival. They were hiding their equipments at one abandoned hut. Upon receiving information about the movement of the ALA cadres, BSF staff posted at Parva BOP conducted search and recovered the mentioned item and apprehended 3(three) cadres of ALA including Kioksu, Commander but the detained cadres were released on the night itself.

 

                 Criminal case is not yet registered and is being prepared at Vasei Police Station. In pursuant of an instruction by Pu Neihchungnunga, IPS, DIG (SR), SDPO Lawngtlai & party moved to the PO today, i.e. 25.12.2019 to do necessary further investigation and legal formalities.
