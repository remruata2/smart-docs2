****SPECIAL REPORT****

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/743

Dated: 2nd October, 2019.

 Central Young Mizo Association (CYMA) convened its Executive Committee Meeting yesterday, i.e. 1.10.2019 @ 7:00 p.m at their office. The meeting was chaired by Pu Vanlalruata, President, CYMA and around 40 (forty) members were present. The meeting discussed about the upcoming visit of Shri. Amit Shah, Union Home Minister and BJP National President to Mizoram on 5.10.2019 and the intention of the Central Government to reintroduce Citizenship Amendment Bill -2016 (CAB-16) in the Parliament.

 The meeting, as a mark of protest and opposition against the Central Government’s intention to revive CAB-16, resolved that Mizo people under the banner of NGOCC will assemble at the following places in Aizawl during the visit of Shri. Amit Shah, Union Home Minister :-

1. Thuampui Helipad Junction
2. Bawngkawn Traffic Point
3. Chanmari Junction
4. Zarkawt Junction
5. Treasury Square (infront of Raj Bhavan)

 It also decided that ‘Pawndum’ (Mizo traditional shawl) will be worn by all and placards condemning and opposing CAB-16 will be widely displayed. Information received indicated that placards condemning CAB-16 will be displayed in three languages, viz. Mizo, English and Hindi, on the following lines :-

1. Down with CAB.
2. Mr. Amit Sir, do not introduce CAB in the Parliament.
3. Mizoram against CAB.
4. CAB for Chakma Buddhist and against Mizo Christians.

 Recapping recent development, the NGO Co-ordination Committee (CYMA, MUP, MHIP, MZP &amp; MSU) meeting held on 27.9.2019 @ 3:00 p.m at CYMA Committee Room, Aizawl resolved to protest and condemn the Central Government’s intention to revive CAB-16 during the visit of Shri. Amit Shah to Mizoram on 5.10.2019. In this connection, Pu Lalnunmawia Chuaungo, IAS, Chief Secretary held a meeting with leaders of NGOCC on 30.9.2019 @ 8:00 a.m at Chief Minister's Conference Hall. The Chief Secretary requested NGOCC leaders to cancel their intention to receive the Union Home Minister by wearing 'Pawndum' and placards condemning and opposing CAB during his visit to Mizoram.
 Situation is under close watch.
