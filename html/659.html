BRU REPATRIATION

The 9th and Final Phase of the repatriation process of  Brus living in Tripura Relief Camps was commenced on 3.10.2019. On the first day, 48 families with 194 family members were repatriated. As many as 212 families with a total of 833 family members were repatriated as on 6.11.2019.

On 25.9.2019, Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ Forum (MBDPF) sent a letter to Pu AP Maheshwari IPS, Special Secretary (IS), MHA and mentioned their various demands such as-

  1. Insufficiency of land at the selected rehabilitation centres and proposed the following places for rehabilitation, namely, SirteTlang in Lunglei District; Mualmam, Artepalungthirek, Kangzau, Tuilut, Zoar and Hmuitlang in Mamit District.
  2. Spot visit of the rehabilitation centres by the concerned stakeholders and construction of dwelling houses before commencement of the repatriation.
  3. To give liberty to displaced families to select their place of resettlement as per their own conveniences.
  4. Acceptance of displaced families who left Mizoram in 2009 and grant of equal compensation to families displaced in 1997
  5. To conduct re-identification of approx. 1281 families who did not turn up in the 2nd phase identification conducted in July, 2019.
  6. Reconsideration of the families who were rejected families in the identification conducted in July, 2019 and extends the same compensation.

On 7.10.2019, Bru Internal Displaced People (BIDP) and Mizoram Bru Displaced Women Organization (MBDWO) organized agitations at Kaskau, Khakchangpara and Asapara and demanded the following:

  1. To create an Autonomous District for Brus community.
  2. Formation of cluster/model villages for Brus in Mizoram etc.

A mass rally was organized on 21.10.2019 by four Bru NGOs viz, i) MBDPF, ii) Mizoram Bru Indigenous Democratic Movement (MBIDM), iii) Bru Tribal Development Society (BTDS) and iv) Bru Displaced Women Welfare Committee (BDWWC) and submitted a memorandum to Hon’ble Union Home Minister demanding the following:

  1. Removal of Dampa Tiger Wildlife Sanctuary for the resettlement of Brus.
  2. Formation of ‘Permanent Development Project’ exclusively for the Brus in Mizoram.
  3. Immediate release of free ration and cash dole.

            The on-going Bru repatriation has been stalled on 31.10.2019 to 7.11.2019 due to road blockade between Kanchanpur and Ananda Bazar organised by various NGOs which was spearheaded by MBDPF in protest of stoppage of ration and cash-dole since 1st October, 2019. Placards containing the following slogans were displayed during the agitation -

                        i)         Stoppage of ration is inhumane.

                        ii)        Stoppage of ration is unconstitutional.

                        iii)       No ration no rest.

 

            However, on 7.11.2019, after 8 (eight) consecutive days road blockade, it was called off after leaders of Bru NGOs held a meeting with Jishnu Dev Verma, Deputy Chief Minister, Tripura and other dignitaries from Tripura as the latter informed them that necessary steps will be taken for resumption of ration and cash dole till the completion of repatriation, ie, November, 2019.
