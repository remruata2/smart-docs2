Point No.6 NRC/CAB Likely Fall Outs:

 

NATIONAL REGISTER OF CITIZENS

 

            Government of Assam published Final Draft of National Register of Citizens(NRC) on 30th July,2018 which left more than 40 lakhs population as illegal foreigners. Following this, the Government of Mizoram immediately took pro-active measures by deploying additional Forces along Mizoram-Assam border and other strategic points to prevent influx of illegal foreigners into Mizoram. But, unhappy with the actions taken by Government, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) issued a Press Release on 16.8.2018, in which they highlighted that MZP has strongly urged Government of Mizoram to take necessary steps in checking illegal entry of non-indigenous persons from entering Mizoram in the wake of publication of National Register of Citizens(NRC) by Government of Assam. MZP alleged that he State Government has not taken necessary initiatives and in this regard and stated that they will take necessary initiatives in checking non-indigenous persons from entering Mizoram illegally. MZP urged its Headquarters, Sub-Headquarters and Branches from inter-state borders to establish check gates and start checking of illegal entry into Mizoram. MZP also urged the authorities not to issue Inner Line Permit(ILP) to non-indigenous persons from Assam who are not registered under NRC.

            In the Press Release, leaders of MZP highlighted that since 16.8.2018, they have established check posts to prevent foreigners from illegally entering Mizoram and also stated that as many as 424 (four hundred twenty four) foreigners were detected from check gates at Bairabi, Vairengte and Saiphai. MZP also highlighted that the detected foreigners trying to illegally enter into Mizoram were pushed back in collaboration with Mizoram Police.

            Central Young Mizo Association(CYMA) held a meeting on the topic of ‘Safeguarding Mizoram’ with 6(six) members representatives each from all YMA Branches within Aizawl City on 30.8.2018 @ 7:30 pm at Vanapa Hall, Aizawl. Pu R.Lalngheta, Vice President, CYMA presided over the meeting which was attended by about 400(four hundred) YMA representatives. The meeting elaborated on the need to prioritize a careful and co-ordinated enforcement of Inner Line Permit by all YMA Branches in the wake of publication of Final Draft of National Register of Citizens by Government of Assam.
