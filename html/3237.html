                  Threat perception assessment of AR Ground and Raj Bhavan and Protected Persons

 

                Assessment of threat perception in respect of AR Ground and Raj Bhavan premises which are the proposed venues for the Independence Day celebration, 2022 scheduled to be held on 15th August, 2022 by Mizoram State Government along with all the protected persons within the state is hereby furnish as follows.

 

            During the course of threat assessment, sources and contacts, links and informants from insurgent groups having operating area adjoining to the interstate and international border were contacted besides constantly monitoring their activities and movements. Despite the existence and presence of several insurgent groups having base and operating areas alongside interstate and international border, it is learnt that at this moment none of these underground insurgents pose any threat to disrupt or thwart the scheduled celebration of the said National Day. It is also further learnt that the threat perceived as against various protected persons of the state Government from insurgent groups is minimal at the moment. Impending issues undertaken by NGOs, student bodies, civil society organisation were also taken into account.

            It is concluded that the threat perceived as against various protected persons of the state government as well as the venues for the proposed National Day from insurgent groups, NGOs, student bodies, civil society organisations, political rivalry or even from personal animosity is considered minimal at the moment.

 

 

 

 

                        Threat perception assessment outside Aizawl City

 

                        Presently, CID (SB) is having 17 sectors, spreading throughout Mizoram viz. Lunglei, Lawngtlai, Siaha, Serchhip, Champhai, Saitual, Kolasib, Vairengte, Mamit, West Phaileng, Bairabi, Chawngte, Sakawrdai, Tlabung, Khawlian,Hnahthial and Zawlnuam. Threat perception assessment  was conducted by Sector Officers in their respective jurisdiction and it is perceived that (except Chawngte) there is no imminent threat to the forthcoming Independence Day celebration, 2022.

 

                        At the same time, information has been received that Chawngte Joint NGOs consisting of Group YMA, Central Young Chakma Association and Mizoram Chakma Students' Union had submitted their demand to Mizoram Chief Minister on 21.7.2022 which are as under :

 

            (i)        The state government should post regular Doctor at CHC (Community                         Health Centre) Chawngte, Lawngtlai District as there has been no                                  regular Doctor in the said CHC.

            (ii)       Regular staff should be posted at SDO (C) Chawngte as there has been                         no regular staff in the said office.

           

 

 

            (iii)     Separate Officers for SDO (C), Chawngte and BDO, Chawngte should              be posted.

            (iv)      Women Police should be posted at Chawngte Police Station.

 

            A 'Peaceful Procession' was held on 8.8.2022 at Chawngte  under the aegis of Chawngte Joint NGOs in support of the above mentioned demands and urged the Chief Minister to fulfil their demands before the end of 14.8.2022.

            Input received stated that Chawngte Joint NGOs intend to boycott the upcoming Independence Day Celebration at Chawngte in case of no positive response from the State government on their demands.
