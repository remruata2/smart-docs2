**A. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES, THE AIZAWL POST & VANGLAINI newspapers Dt. 16.5.2013**

**Arms worth****`****7.5 lakh seized******: The joint team of Champhai Police, SIB and 14 AR seized five (5) 40 mm Rifle grenade believed to be smuggled from Myanmar and arrested four (4) alleged owners.

 Champhai police reported that on the evening of Tuesday, one (1) 40mm Rifle grenade (MG/2) was seized from Lalnuntluangi d/o Hrangliana, Tahan 'A' Group, Myanmar who was lodging at a Hotel Holiday Home in Champhai by the Joint Operation Team. Following the information received from Lalnuntluangi, another four (4) 40 mm Rifle Grenades were seized from LH Nanglama (50) s/o Sawngnanga (L) of Champhai Vengthar.

 The alleged owners, Lalnuntluangi and LH Nanglama, Lalengkimi (32) d/o Biakngura of Vaphai and Thangkhenmang @ Manga s/o Ginkhanthanga of Champhai Bethel Veng were arrested and registered cases against them. The arms seized cost ` 1.5 lakh each and are to be sold at ` 7.5 lakh.

**B. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES & VANGLAINI newspapers Dt. 16.5.2013**

**All foreigners left Rangvamual and Phunchawng******: The deadline set by Central YMA for foreigners to leave Rangvamual and Phunchawng was over yesterday and all foreigners are believed to have left these two (2) places.

 R. Lallianchama, leader of Rangvamual YMA said that all the foreigner families at Rangvamual had left Rangvamual. B.Nothanga, leader of Phunchawng YMA said that 95 foreigner families at Phunchawng had left Phunchawng while two families left without their properties.

 There has been no untoward report due to the ultimatum of Central YMA which informed foreigners to leave Phunchawng and Rangvamual, there is scarcity of alcohol, said YMA leader.
