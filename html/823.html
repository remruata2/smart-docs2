The Project :           Indo-Bangladesh at the initial total 656 crore

 

Border Fencing Project estimated cost of Rs.

 

 

 

Objective:

 

 

 

 

 

Scheme:

 

To put up physical barrier along 336.62km long Indo-Bangladesh border for internal security of the country.

 

The scheme comprised of four components

 

 

(1) Fencing (2) Road (3) BOP

(4) Floodlighting

 

Modalities for execution of work :

The physical topography of the international border with Bangladesh is placed below -

As per the scheme, fencing is to be erected at a distance of 150 yards from the International Border but in a situation where, owing to inhospitable terrain / topography , it is not possible to adhere to this stipulation, the guidelines permit that in such cases, the fencing could be erected within as much as 500 yards inside Mizoram from the zero (0) line. However, these guidelines have not been uniformly followed by the Executing Agencies and alignments were made as per convenience. In some locations, entire villages are outside the fencing; in some cases, the fencing cuts through the middle of existing villages creating problems in the entire border areas of Mizoram. VSELECTIVE FENCING OF INDO-MYANMAR BORDERS AND OTHER ISSUES :

 

• The 404 km long Indo-Myanmar border in Mizoram sector is manifested by inhospitable terrain.

 

• If fencing is to be erected from 150 - 500 yards from the ‘Zero Line’, a large number of villages located close to the border and all fertile plains will be fenced out.

 

• The State Government does not want to repeat the compensation problems faced and experienced with the Indo-Bangladesh Border Fencing.

 

 

• The finalisation of Border Pass scheme would present system which will be more effective for cross border challenges

 

supersede the preventing any

 

 

 

• The Indo-Bangladesh Border fencing has in many instances caused havoc to the livelihood of the border populace and the State Government does not want to repeat this again. Hence, the State Government is not in favour of constructing                        Indo-Myanmar     Border     fencing.                        The decision/view of the state Government regarding the same has been conveyed numerous times to MHA vide Home Department letter No.F.22015/14/2005-HM-Vol-II dated 11.5.2012, 14.5.2013, 8.8.2013, 2.11.2016 and 8.12.2016
