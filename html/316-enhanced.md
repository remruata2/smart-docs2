****REPORT ON THREAT AGAINST HINDU LEADERS****

****AND THEIR ESTABLISHMENTS****

 Discreet enquiry had been made regarding possible strikes on Hindu leaders and their establishments within Aizawl city by religious or political parties. Enquiry revealed that Hindus in Mizoram have eleven(11) temples/mandirs in Aizawl at the following localities:-

 1. Maubawk Shiv Mandir

 2. Khatla Shiv Mandir

 3. Dinthar Hari Mandir

 4. Bawngkawn Shiv Mandir

 5. Zotlang Shiv Mandir

 6. Thuampui Krishna Temple (under BRTF)

 7. Sairang Shiv Mandir (under BRTF)

 8. Armed Veng Shiv Mandir (under 1st Bn. MAP)

 9. Shakti Mandir, Babutlang

 10. Krishna Mandir Bazar (under 10th AR)

 11. Khatla Shiv Mandir (under 23 Sector AR)

 The above eleven Mandirs/Temples in different parts of Aizawl and some are located within the premises of security agencies like AR, 1st Bn. MAP and BRTF. Each mandir is looked after by a Pandit.

 In addition, the Hindu community in Aizawl has the following religion based Club/Centre:-

1) **Hindustan****Club**: It organizes the celebration of various Hindu festivals for the Hindu community by chalking out programme for recreation/amusement and holding arts exhibition for children etc. The club usually holds meeting at Shakti Mandir, Babutlang. The club leaders are:-

 President : A.K Chakravarty

 Secretary : Dr. Maity

 Treasurer : S.K Dey

 Adviser : Mr. Goswami

2) **Rajapita Brahma Kumaris Rajyoga Education Centre**: The centre was set up in April 1966 at Zemabawk and currently occupies B. Daniela's building, Tuikhuahtlang paying the monthly rent of Rs.6000/-. The centre is currently being run by four workers, namely -

 1. Deva Sis Brother - Guwahati

 2. Jyanto Brother - Guwahati

 3. BK. Narmada Sister - Silchar

 4. BK. Deepa Sister - Silchar

 The centre aims at spreading the awareness of Hinduism and fulfilling the spiritual needs of the devotees. It also provides counselling to people needing help such as drug addicts. The centre welcomes people of all faiths. Till date, the centre is running smoothly and has not encountered any kind of threat or untoward incident from outside neither from the locals nor the Muslim community. The centre has another branch at Kolasib, Tumpui Veng under the care of two sisters.

****Threat Perception**: Till date, communal and religious harmony still prevails there. There is no reported attack on the Hindus inside Mizoram by people from other faiths. Interaction with the Hindu community in Mizoram reveals that they do not perceive any kind of threat to their lives or property on account of religions. There is no report on the presence of anti-social elements/fanatics from Muslim fundamentalist organisations inside Mizoram warranting action from law enforcing agency.**
