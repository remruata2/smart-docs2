A. Front page of  VANGLAINI  newspaper  Dt. 06.12.2022

AMC locked a house : Enforcement Committee under Aizawl Municipal Council (AMC) locked one building which was alleged to have been constructed in violation of the AMC Building Regulation, 2012 at Chanmari Veng, Aizawl. While a G6 Residential Building permit (six floors, apart from the ground floor) had been issued for the construction of this house, seven floors have been construction by its owner. According to the Chairman of this Enforcement Committee, Pu John Zosangliana (Councillor), six neighbours of this house have submitted complaints against the construction of this house, which belongs to Pu Dinary T Azyu . Even after receiving repeated warnings to halt the construction of the building from AMC, the owner paid no heed to them and continued the construction. The Enforcement Committee of AMC had no choice, but to locked the said building, the Chairman added. This house is currently rented by Agape Multi-speciality Hospital and have just started to function.

 

 

 

B. Front page of  VANGLAINI newspaper  Dt. 06.12.2022

No tolerance drive: In a public noticed issued by Pu Rodingliana Chawngthu, SP Aizawl Traffic Police, starting from 12th December 2022 Aizawl Traffic Police will be conducting 'No Tolerance Drive/Checking' within the city. The notice requested the public to obey traffic rules and possess valid documents as there will be no  leniencies against violators of traffic rules during this period.

 

 

 

C. Front page of  VANGLAINI newspaper  Dt. 06.12.2022

Gradings of clean city/towns to be started:  The panel of Judges, who will be inspecting various towns of the state before giving them grades were flagged off by UD&PA Director, Pu H.Lianzela in front of Vanapa Hall yesterday. These panel of judges consisted of six persons, one each from - I&PR Department, Mizoram Pollution Control Board, Mizoram Journalist Association,  Central YMA, Science Teacher Association of Mizoram and Higher Secondary Lecturers Association of Mizoram. The judges were divided into two groups and will inspect 28 towns approved by UD&PA from 5-20, December 2022.
