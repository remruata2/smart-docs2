****Brief Note on KMMTTP****

 As part of India’s LOOK EAST POLICY to establish a close relationship between Government of India and Myanmar - Kaladan Multimodal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) is an infrastructure development project initiated by Ministry of External Affairs. A Multi Modal Transit Transport Route has been proposed composition of land, river and sea routes through Mizoram State along the Kaladan River up to Myanmar, Sittwe(Akyab) seaport in Bay of Bengal _to connect landlocked Northeast from Kolkata through Sittwe seaport, Myanmar via Kaladan River route._The New 2-Lane Highway takes off from 473.40 kmp of NH-54 within Lawngtlai Town at AOC filling Station.

 The KMMTTP comprises land, river and sea routes connecting Mizoram with Kolkata Sea Port –  (a) Lawngtlai to Zorinpui (Mizoram) Land Route - 87.51 Km

(b) Zorinpui Border to Kaletwa (Myanmar) Land Route - 45 Km

(c) Kaletwa to Paletwa (Myanmar) Land Route - 60.70 Km

(d) Paletwa to Sittwe (Myanmar) River Route - 150 Km

(e) Sittwe to Kolkata Port (Bay of Bengal) Sea Route - 539 Km.

The total length of the route is 882.21 Km which is far shorter than the existing land link between the endpoints of the project at 1764 Km.

Mizoram PWD is entrusted to look after the construction of a new 2-lane Highway to support KMMTTP under Phase A of Special Accelerated Road Development Programme (SARDP)-NE within Mizoram from Lawngtlai to Zorinpui, the Indo-Myanmar border point. The remaining sector i.e. beyond the border of Mizoram (India) is being taken up by the Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India.

KMMTTP consist of 2 (two) components - Port &amp; Inland Water Transport and Highway component –

(a) Inland Waterways Authority of India (IWAI) is the Project Development Consultant (PDC) appointed by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) in March 2009 for implementation of the Port &amp; IWT components. M/s Essar Projects India Limited, Mumbai is the main Contractor for this components and the contract value is Rs. 342 crores.

(b)  Construction of a highway from Paletwa to India-Myanmar border for 110 kms and M/s. IRCON is the agency appointed to execute Road component.

**1. Commencement of the project inside Mizoram:**
Construction of this project was started during October 2010. The project is of 100% central funding through MoRT&amp;H, GoI funding scheme for SARDP -NE. The whole project is divided into three packages:

1. The first package from 0/00 and end at 38/00 kmp. - RDS, Project Ltd, New Delhi
2. Second package stretches is 38/00 – 71/00 kmp - ARSS (Atlanta) (JV), Orissa
3. The last package is from 71/00-99/830 kmp- RDS, Project Ltd, New Delhi

**3. Completion of the project inside Mizoram:**

The project was schedule to complete during 2014 but could be completed during June 2023 due to land issue problems which has been aroused right from the commencement of the project. The project had been revised three times due to omission of essential item of works which was not reflected in the DPR:

**4.** **Current progress of work:**

All works as per Bill of Quantities are completed on June 2023. Meanwhile, due to the dumping of debris by NH 54 Widening contractors, the smooth plying of the road hampered in certain locations. Due to cyclone _Remal_happened on May 2024, road formations at 2 locations were collapsed and several locations were fully blocked by landslides. Debris are partially cleared for plying of vehicles. Despite the partial clearance of such landslides, the constant fall of mudslides at certain location can block the road at any time.

**5. Security aspect of the Project:**

Lai, Bru, Bawm, Rakhine and Chakma tribes are the major tribes living along the coverage area of the KMMTTP inside Mizoram and only small villages are found along the KMMTTP’s highway. The Arakan Army which is fighting to uproot the military dictatorship in Myanmar is the major armed group operating within the KMMTTP area inside Myanmar. The spokesperson of United League of Arakan (ULA) or Arakan Army in his interview to Indian Media reportedly said that Kaladan Multimodal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) is safe and it would not come under any harm and added that they have no problem with India and expressed their readiness to cooperate with India for mutual benefit.
