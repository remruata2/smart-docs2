****Threat perception assessment report.****

1. **Shri S.B. Shashank, IAS**

Being the present Chief electoral Officer of Government of Mizoram, he is greatly observed by political parties and NGOs in a state like Mizoram where it is well known that CEO’s responsibilities starts from drafting/finalizing of electoral roll till processing of Elections. It is therefore considered that the said official be continued to stay in a protected list and that provision of PSO also be continued.

1. **Lalnunmawia Chuaungo, IAS**

Pu Lalnunmawia Chuaungo, IAS is presently holding the post of Principal Secretary, Home &amp; Finance in Government of Mizoram. Since his post comes with upholding Law and Order within the State, it is necessary to continue to include the said official in a protected list even though a direct or indirect threat is not known in the past and present. As such, provision of PSO may be continued.

1. **Pu Lalduhawma, President ZNP**

He is the president of ZNP and the Chief Ministerial candidate of ZPM for the upcoming MLA election. It is known that he frequently travelled around Mizoram state, campaigning for his party and for himself as he will also be contesting in the said upcoming election. It is, therefore considered that it is the state government’s responsibility to provide PSO to the said person although direct or indirect threat is not known.

1. **Prof. J.V. Hluna, President, BJP**

Since he is the present leader of BJP Mizoram state, he regularly travels around Mizoram state. It is also known that he will be contesting in the upcoming MLA election and for these reasons, provision of PSO be continued although presence of direct or indirect threat is not known.

1. **Dy. Residence Commissioner, Silchar, Mizoram House**

Pi Saizikpuii, MCS is presently posted as Dy. Residence Commissioner having two PSOs. It is known that unforeseen communal tension between Mizoram state and Assam state occasionally flared up in the past recent years. Though peace between the two states has been retained at present, the said official’s intervention in the activities of NGOs and other criminals was often needed. Moreover, the said official often needed to travel in and out of the two states- Mizoram and Assam since their office used to do Bank Transaction only at Treasury Office, Kolasib, Mizoram which is hectic for only one PSO. Therefore it is considered that the said official be continued to provide with two PSOs.

1. **Pu Hmingchungnunga, Chairman, SHDC**

Pu Hmingchungnunga, the Chairman of SHDC tenure has ended on dt. 31.5.2018. Apart from this, the underground outfit, HPC(D) who often posed threats for him had recently signed MoS with the Government of Mizoram and his previous direct/indirect intimidations from the said outfit no longer exist as of now. Because of these factors, it is no longer necessary to provide him with PSO and House Guard as he himself also felt it unnecessary for the continuation of such securities.
