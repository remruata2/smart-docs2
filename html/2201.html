GENESIS OF THE FORMATION OF

MIZORAM PEOPLE FORUM(MPF)

 

            Following the expectations set by the Indian Electoral Laws and System for the efficient running of a democratic government, the different churches of Mizoram have been taking active part in the process by issuing election messages before the election and offering prayers for peaceful conduct of the poll; the YMA and other voluntary groups have also taken certain measures in this regard. Although it can be said that these messages, to a certain extent, improved the efficiency of the elections it was, however, not deemed fully satisfactory. During these elections the amount of money that was utilized for the election purposes increased significantly. The amount of money spent in the 1978 MLA Election and the amount spent in the 2003 MLA Election has increased by manifolds, as per the research. This is expected to drastically increase the practice of corruption in Mizoram.

 

            The Mizoram Presbyterian Church Synod Meeting (vide Synod 2004 Gen. 11&Gen.58) resolved to rectify and reform the state of politics in Mizoram. Following this resolutions(SEC 201:13, 206:45), the Synod Executive Committee held talks with leaders of different churches of Mizoram, different prominent NGO’s, intellectuals and respected individuals of Mizoram. Ensuing with the different views and advice of these groups and individuals a concrete action plan was made.

 

            Adhering to this plan of action, the different churches and prominent NGOs of the state held a deliberation on July 5th, 2005 where they decided on the need to improve the state of politics in Mizoram and adopted a proposal for the same. However, it was resolved that each church and each group have a discussion on the proposal within their own church or group. On September 1st, 2005, the representatives from the different churches and prominent NGOs came together and formally accepted the formation of the Mizoram People Forum(MPF) so that a greater initiative can be taken towards the improvement of politics within the state, wherein a Working Committee was set up to chalk out the modalities and working plan, as well as the objectives. After careful consideration deliberation and assessment of these objectives, it was deemed satisfactory and so the Mizoram People Forum was formally formed on June 21st, 2006.

 

Organisation :

            As this organization was set up to look after the entirety of Mizoram, it has been divided into different sections.

a.         Annual General Meeting : This is the highest authority within the MPF; this meeting is called every year by the Governing Board.

 

b.         Central Forum : The Central Forum is also the Governing Board. It will have two meetings annually.

 

c.         Board Executive Committee : Due to the magnitude of the MPF headquarters, the Board Executive Committee is in charge of maintain and looking after the headquarters.

 

d.         District Forum : There will be a District Forum present in every Administrative/Political District. At present, there are 6 (six) District Forum in Mizoram, such as, (1) Champhai District Forum, (2) Kolasib District Forum, (3) Serchhip District Forum, (4) Mamit District Forum, (5) Siaha District Forum, and (6) Lawngtlai District Forum

 

e.         Constituency Forum : There will be a Constituency Forum in each Assembly Constituency. At present, there are 28 (twenty eight) Constituency Forum in Mizoram.

 

f.          Local Forum : There will be a Local Forum in each VC/LC Area. However, if the locality covers a large area more than one Local Forum can be form. At present, there are about 800 (eight hundred) Local Forum in Mizoram

 

g.         General Headquarters Office : The General Headquarters Office is located at Tuikhuahtlang, Aizawl.

 

 

            Ever since its establishment, MPF has taken great efforts for holding clean, free and fair election in various elections within Mizoram. For this, an agreement, commonly known as “Articles of Agreement” was signed between MPF and all the political parties of Mizoram. The joint efforts these political parties and the Mizoram People Forum in implementing this agreement have been a great success in the past elections.

            Mizoram will be facing Legislative Assembly General Election again in 2018. Buoyant by the past achievements and success, and the desire to uphold the great election model, political parties of Mizoram and the Mizoram People Forum have come together again and agreed to adhere to the articles of agreement again, the main points of which are highlighted below –

 

General Conduct :

(1)       For holding a clean, free and fair election every precaution and measure should be taken.

 

(2)       All the rightful citizens of the country enrolled in the electoral roll must cast their votes.

 

(3)       The conveyance and traveling expenditures of electoral voters, who are stationed in places other than the constituency where they have enrolled, shall not be borne by others such as political parties, candidates or their workers.

 

Election Expenditure :

(1)        No party or candidate shall indulge in huge and wasteful election expenditures. Election expenditures should be kept to the minimum. MPF functionaries shall not accept money or donations from the political parties and the candidates.

 

(2)        Activities which are ‘corrupt practices’ and offences under the election law can crop up during the house to house campaign. Therefore, house to house visits by candidates and their supporters must be conducted in the presence of MPF local forum leaders.

 

(3)        Influencing of voters through money or other incentives and inducements must be avoided. General public, organizations and individuals are requested not to ask or expect any favour from the candidates. Similarly, candidates and political parties are also requested to refrain from announcing any financial grants, distribution of inducement articles.

 

(4)        Feasting, picnic and procession escalate the election expenditure unnecessarily; they must be avoided.

 

(5)        Banner, flag and poster unnecessarily escalate the election expenditure; therefore the maximum number of banners, flags and posters is restricted within each locality.

 

Free and fair election and security issues :

(1)         Use of force, intimidation and undue influence to the voters to secure their votes must be avoided.

 

(2)         Securing the support of underground armed groups and use of arms shall be prevented at all cost.

 

(3)         Take all necessary actions for security and peaceful election, especially along the border and sensitive areas.

 

Policy implementation :

(1)         Only realistic and do-able policies and programmes must be included in the Party manifesto.

 

(2)         More than the personal and individual life of the candidates, election campaigning should revolve around the party policy land programme. Campaigning by use of false propaganda, unproven truth and scandalous issues must be avoided.

 

Candidates :

            While making the nomination, the political parties are requested to consider the following qualities in their prospective candidates -

 

(1)       Stable and upright person.

 

(2)       Honest and hard working.

 

(3)       Person with great integrity.

 

(4)       Free from the influence of alcoholic and other drugs.

 

(5)       Abstain from fornication.

 

(6)       Having great respect for law of the land.

 

(7)       Dedicated to the welfare of the society and fellow human beings.

 

(8)       Committed to one’s faith and religion.

 

Joint electioneering effort :

(1)         As and when possible, joint election campaign platform will be organized for various candidates, under the guidance of MPF Constituency Forum. These programmes may be widely publicized in the local TU and media; other than these, joint election campaign platform must be avoided.

 

(2)         All public meetings, even those organized by individual party, may be held under the guidance of MPF. In the absence of MPF, individual political parties may make suitable arrangement.

 

(3)         No tea or eatables shall be served on the polling day. Giving out of election numbers or slips shall be undertaken by the MPF duty or in a joint effort with the party workers.

 

Conclusion :

(1)         MPF District Forum, Constituency Forum and Local Forum shall neither make additional agreement nor deviate from this ‘Articles of Agreement’.

 

(2)         If any political parties or candidates default or goes against the spirit of this ‘Articles of Agreement’ and if there is solid proof or documentary evidence to prove the same, it shall be widely publicized within the MLA Constituency where it has been committed.

 

(3)         This ‘Articles of Agreement’ shall be widely publicized and circulated by the Mizoram People Forum and Political Parties.

 

(4)         For having free and fair election, this agreement will also include Independent Candidates, if there is any.
