WRITE UP FOR DGPs MEETING -2014 AT GUWAHATI

 

1. INTERNATIONAL BORDERS-CROSS BORDERS ISSUES–OUR SECURITY CONCERNS:

 

(i)Mizoram is having approximately 512 kms and 323 kms porous international borders with Myanmar and Bangladesh respectively which served as an important routes  for  Insurgent Groups to procure arms and ammunition and also to smuggle contraband goods and illicit drugs.

 

(ii)The porous border areas have widely encouraged other criminal activities like abduction/kidnapping  of Indian citizens by Bangladesh based insurgent groups.

 

(iii)The porous border area also open for influx of foreign nationals from Bangladesh i.e Chakmas/Bengali Muslim and Myanmarese from Myanmar into the state of Mizoram.

 

(iv)The recent increase in the smuggling of contraband drugs like Pseudoephedrine, Methamphetamine across the border has called for immediate and proper attention and Mizoram Police has made constant efforts to effect several recoveries.

 

        (v)It is imperative, therefore, that fencing of all the border areas with Bangladesh and Myanmar needs to be expedited on an urgent basis to prevent illegal infiltration and cross border criminal activities.

 

 

2. INTERSTATE COORDINATION:

 

 Border issues with Assam:

The Assam-Mizoram border has remained relatively calm despite the disputed nature of the border. However, tension along this border flared up in the month of April, 2014 at the inter-state border between Assam’s Hailakandi district and Mizoram’s Kolasib district after Assam Police and Forest Department personnel allegedly dismantled the farmhouse of Mizo farmers near Buhchangphai village in Mizoram. A meeting has been held in Shillong on 24th September 2014 between Mizoram and Assam Officials to resolve this burning issue.  On June,2014 Pu. Lal Thanhawala, Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram met Pu. Rajnath Singh, Hon’ble Union Home minister and requested him to ensure settlement of the Mizoram-Assam boundary dispute.


 

3. TALK WITH INSURGENT LEADERS:

 

(i) The Government of Mizoram and HPC –D (H.Zosangbera faction) held the first round of Peace talk on 11th November,2010 and subsequently on 31st January 2013, 18th July 2013 and 14th August 2013. However, the last round of talk held in Aizawl on 14th August 2013 ended in a deadlock as both the parties could not agree on the conditions and duration for further extension of Suspension of Operation (SoO).

 

(ii) Government of Mizoram has asked the Union Home Ministry to declare Manipur-based H.Zosangbera faction of Hmar People's Convention-Democrats as unlawful organisation under the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967. 

 

(iii) Meanwhile, leaders of another faction of HPC (D) led by Lalhmingthanga  Sanate  suggested their keenness for revival of the peace talks. On 20.10.2014, a meeting was held in the office Chamber of Home Minister under the chairmanship of Pu.R.Lalzirliana,  Hon’ble Home Minister and the meeting entrusted CID (SB) with the preliminary task of holding the parley with the said  leaders of HPC-D.
