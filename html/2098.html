POINT NO. (viii) :    DETAILS  OF  CEASEFIRE  AGREEMENT  AND  OTHER                                                 AGREEMENTS

 

I.         PEACE TALK AND SIGNING OF MEMORANDUM OF     UNDERSTANDING WITH BRU NATIONAL LIBERATION FRONT      (BNLF)  :

           

            To end mistrust amongst different communities and militant activities of BNLF, Govt. of Mizoram initiated Peace Talk with leaders of BNLF w.e.f. 7.9.2001 and after 12 rounds of peace talks between Govt. of Mizoram and BNLF, an MoU was signed between BNLF and Govt. of Mizoram on 26.4.2005.

 

ARMS LAYING AND TEMPORARY SHELTER FOR BNLF AND FAMILY MEMBERS :

            In pursuance of MoU signed on 26.4.2005, the 195 strong BNLF cadres and their family members have been temporarily sheltered at Sidon Transit Camp, Tuipuibari. Altogether a total members of 950 including all BNLF cadres and families have been provided free ration at the rate of Rs. 10/- per adult and  Rs. 5/- per minor per day.

 

RESETTLEMENT :

            Various problems created by the BLFM outfit came in the way of resettlement of BNLF as a result of which the BNLF and their family members had to stay in the Camp beyond the stipulated time.

Finally the Govt. of Mizoram had resettled BNLF and their family members in the villages in Mizoram.

 

II.        SURRENDER AND RESETTLEMENT OF BRU LIBERATION FRONT MIZORAM (BLFM)

           

SURRENDERING OF BLFM SPLINTER GROUP :

            The BLFM is a very ill-organised militant group from the very outset. A splinter group of BLFM headed by A. Zoramthanga, former Vice President and Lalrinkima, Gen. Secretary numbering 38 surrendered to the Govt. with weapons on 16.3.2006 followed by 4 cadres on 6.7.2006 and 11 cadres on 17.8.2006. Altogether 53 cadres of A. Zoramthanga faction of BLFM surrendered before Govt. of Mizoram by the turn of September, 2006.

 

REHABILITATION AND RESETTLEMENT :

            Govt. of Mizoram rehabilitated this group with Cash Assistance and Housing Assistance at par with the BNLF surrenderees. They were resettled at Pangbalkawn village by purchasing a plot of land for them. However, most of them have returned to refugee camps in Tripura except 6 of them who resettled at  Chhimluang, Saihapui and Pangbalkawn itself.

 

 

SURRENDER OF 804 STRONG CADRES OF BLFM :        

Out of frustration due to arrest of their top leaders and surrender of 53 cadres of BLFM, the remaining 804 strong members of BLFM decided to lay down their arms and surrender to the Govt. unconditionally. As such, the remaining BLFM cadres, with the active participation of Assam Rifles laid down their arms on 26.10.2006 and were temporarily sheltered in two Transit Camps viz. Damparengpui and Tuipuibari. Free ration have been provided as per scale which was sanctioned by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India. However, the state Govt. has also supplement the sanction as the surrenderees have to stay in the transit camp over and above the anticipated period.

 

RESETTLEMENT :

            The Govt. of Mizoram took all possible steps to resettle the surrendered BLFM identified as a bonafide resident of Mizoram with their family members at the places selected by concerned District Authorities. Housing Assistance to the tune Rs. 30,000/- each was disbursed to them in two installments.

 

III.      SIGNING OF PEACE ACCORD BETWEEN GOVERNMENT OF            MIZORAM AND HMAR PEOPLES CONVENTION (HPC) :

                        The Government of Mizoram eventually managed to contain the activities of the HPC militants through the courageous and dedicated efforts of Mizoram Police which resulted into the signing of a Peace Accord between the Government of Mizoram and HPC on 27th July 1994. The HPC militants surrendered formally to the Govt. of Mizoram as per the provision of this Accord and these persons were given financial grants for their rehabilitation etc. The Govt. of Mizoram also agreed to set up the Sinlung Hills Development Council (SHDC) for the socio-economic upliftment of the people living in the Hmar dominated areas. Accordingly, Adhoc Chairman and members constituting the council were even appointed by the Govt. of Mizoram from among the leaders of the erstwhile HPC set up. Peace was thus restored in Mizoram after the signing of the Accord.

 

IV.      PEACE TALK AND SIGNING OF MEMORANDUM OF     SETTLEMENT (MOS) BETWEEN GOVERNMENT OF MIZORAM            AND HMAR PEOPLES CONVENTION (DEMOCRATIC) [HPC(D)]

            The 4th round of Peace Dialogue was held on 28th April, 2017, and the 5th Round of Peace Dialogue was held on 21st & 22nd September, 2017 where in the framework of agreement was worked out. The Peace Dialogue was concluded under conducive atmosphere. As scheduled, the Political Level Peace Talk between Mizoram State Government and HPC(D) was held under a cordial environment at the State Guest House, Aizawl during 7th & 8th March, 2018 and the Peace Talk ended with a logical conclusion on 8.3.2018. The State Government and the HPC(D) were represented by 10 (ten) members each. While the delegates of Mizoram Government were led by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Political Adviser to Chief Minister; Pu L.T. Hmar, Working Chairman, HPC(D) led the HPC(D) delegates during the two-day Peace Dialogue.

            On 2.4.2018 @ 4:30 p.m, Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) at the function held at the State Guest House, Aizawl. Shri. Arvind Ray, Chief Secretary of Mizoram and Pu H. Zosangbera, Chairman, HPC(D) signed the MoS on behalf of the State Government and HPC(D) respectively. Pu Lalbiakzama, Addl. Secretary, Home gave reports on the peace process and speeches were delivered by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Pu Lalthalien, Spokesperson, HPC(D) during the function. The signing of MoS will bring to an end, the over two-decades armed conflict of the HPC(D). The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC(D) was held at CTI, Sesawng on 13th April, 2018. 102 Cadres and 12 Council Members of HPC(D) come to over ground at this ceremony.

 

REHABILITATION PACKAGE :

            The Govt. of Mizoram had sanctioned Rs. 3 lakhs as rehabilitation package for the surrendered HPC(D) Cadres and Council Members of HPC(D). Rehabilitation package was distributed in two (2) installment - First installment ie, Rs. 20,000/- was distributed on 13th April, 2018 at CTI, Sesawng (Arms laying Ceremony) and the Second installment ie, Rs. 2.8 lakhs was distributed on 13th June, 2018 at Badminton Hall, PHQ, Khatla.

 

SINLUNG HILLS COUNCIL (SHC) :

            Due to the consequences of MoS signed by Government of Mizoram and HPC(D), the Governor of Mizoram constituted an Interim Council of the Sinlung Hills for a period of 6 (six) months and appointed 14 (fourteen) persons as members of the Interim Sinlung Hills Council with immediate effect on 9th July, 2018 vide  No. B.12013/1/2018-HM(SHC).

            On 13th July, 2018 @ 10:00 a.m, the Oath Taking ceremony of members of the Interim Sinlung Hills Council was held in the office of Deputy Commissioner, Aizawl District. Dr. A. Muthamma, DC, Aizawl District administered the oath of affirmation to the appointed 14 (fourteen) members of the Interim Sinlung Hills Council.
