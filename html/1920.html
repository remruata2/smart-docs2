Information from sources (a joint operation team of BSF, Aizawl Sector and staffs of DSB, Aizawl) was received by CID(SB) on Dt. 11.3.2012. The said information contained that some unidentified persons were illegally possessing and trying to sell some unidentified weapons/firearms and that two BFS’s sources were already engaged to snare the illegal possessor for apprehension and seizure. It may be noted that the said party (BSF and DSB) further requested CID(SB) to join the operation party which was accepted by the latter after the matter was thoroughly discussed by senior officers of CID(SB).

A joint operation party was then formed on the same day and as engagement with the illegal possessor was already made by BSF sources, no new strategy was planned. On the same day, the joint operation team were trying to locate and apprehend the illegal possessor but to no success. On the next day i.e. dt. 12.3.2012, the operation was continued since morning time. On the said day, the mentioned two civilian sources were trying to lure out the illegal possessor which was identified as Peka of unknown residence, the sources disguise themselves as an Arms buyer who are looking for arms to buy. They were also secretly followed and watched by the operation team. As the suspect (illegal possessor) was a very clever fellow, he kept on changing places where he and the sources will meet to discuss business. From the sources it was also known that the suspect’s was called Peka but by that time his residence was not yet known. As the suspect often suddenly switching places where he will meet the sources, the joint operation party failed to locate/intervene while the sources meet the suspect but the place where the suspect took the sources was later on revealed by the sources through phone call. The sources also said that they have seen the arms which the suspect person tried to sell and also that they examine it thoroughly. They also stated that the arms were reportedly kept inside the shop where they suspect brought them as they saw him again (after the meeting) going out from the shop empty handed. On receiving the said information, the joint operation team immediately proceeded towards the said shop which is located at Ramhlun road, Chanmari. On reaching the shop, the co-owner of the shop Mr. Eddie told the operation team that the suspect had left the shop a few minutes then. The owner of the shop Mr. Noela who is also an older brother of Mr. Eddiea had also ccme in after awhile and when the two brothers were questioned they said that one of their distant friends came to the shop that day and that one of them had kept his bag at one corner of the shop. On hearing the information, the operation team called on civilian witness Pu Lalsawmliana of Chanmari and in his presence Mr. Eddie-a, took out the bag from one corner of the shop as demanded. When he opened the bag two small arms (9mm pistol and .38 Revolver) were found inside the bag. The recovered articles were seized in the presence of the mentioned civilian witness. At the time of making seizure, statements of witness, accused and seizing officer were recorded. The brother claimed that they did not know the content of the bag and that they were not their property. They further mentioned that they would be able to help the operation party in arresting the real owner of the bag by insisting him (the owner of the bag) to come to their shop to take his bag. Since the information obtained by the operation party also confirmed that the brothers were not the owner of the arms and that they were not the one who was selling the arms, attempt was continued to be made to lure the suspect into the shop. Mr. Noela called the owner and demanded him to take away his bag from their shop. Soon after, the owner of the bag came to the shop and he was immediately apprehended by the operation team. In the presence of civilian witness, the bag’s owner was asked to take out his bag from  the place where he hid it. As demanded, he opened the bag and took out two small arms which he had tried to sell earlier that day. The owner of the bag (the accused) also admitted that the bag and its content were his property and that he was the one who kept/hid the bag inside the shop. Upon hearing the accused confession, the accused was apprehended. The accused and the seized articles were then brought to Aizawl P/S and handed them over to O/C of Aizawl P/S along with a written FIR. The accused was also formally arrested by the case I/O at the P/S.

 

The accused Vanlalpeka of Tuithiang Veng was at first apprehended at the place of occurance and he was later formally arrested by the case I/O when he was produced at the P/S. All the seized articles were also brought and produced to the P/S along with the accused.

7,8 &9.

As CID(SB) personnel are not empowered to take up cases, this particular case was also taken up by one officer from Aizawl P/S for further investigation and also to conclude the case by submitting charge sheet to the court. There are certain limitations of power for some unit like CID(SB) etc., as in the said cases also, only, the operation, apprehension, seizing, recording statement of accused/seizure witness/seizing officer, lodging F.I.R could be carried out, and after the accused and seized articles were handed over to O/C of a P/S, the case was taken up by the concerned P/S staff for further investigation and also to conclude the case. It is not the duty of seizing officer of CID(SB) unit to submit the chargesheet or to further call other witness. However, in this particular case, the mentioned shop owners who present at time of seizing Mr. Noela and his brother Mr. Eddie were called to present themselves for questioning several times at CID (SB) office at Bungkawn. On that occasions also, no direct involvement was not seen from the brother’s side, they themselves also accepted to appear before the court to confess the truth as it was (i.e. against the accused Vanlalpeka) if needed. The case I/O was also hinted to include the brother as prsecution witnessess.
