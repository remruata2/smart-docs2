RESTRICTED  CIRCULATION

action and Movement plan FOR the final and  8th batch of Reang (Bru) repatriation & Rehabilitation from tripura to mizoram as per Road Map - IV prepared by home department, government of mizoram, 2018.

 

 

  1. introduction : The State Government has successfully repatriated 1648 Reang (Bru)  families to Mamit and Kolasib District since November, 2010 till December, 2017.

The Action & Movement Plan of Reang (Bru) repatriation and rehabilitation has been prepared as per Draft Agreement between the Government of India, Mizoram, Tripura and MBDPF which was conveyed to Government of Mizoram on 28th June, 2018.

The tentative date for commencement of physical repatriation has been earmarked to be completed on 10th September, 2018. The Action & Movement Plan has been prepared by Home Department, Government of Mizoram in the absence of  consultation with official stakeholders, without convening the State Level Core Committee (SLCC) for Reang (Bru) repatriation and rehabilitation and more importantly without any assurance for the sanction of the revised fund requirement from MHA, allocation and procurement of free ration and repairing of roads within the concerned districts which were not repaired due to heavy monsoon experienced by the State in 2017 and 2018.

The physical repatriation which is to commence from 14th August, 2018 shall target the families identified during the identification exercise to be completed on 10th September, 2018.

The Reang (Bru) families who are proposed to be repatriated to Mizoram are those who have at least one member of the family registered in the E/Roll, 2014 of Mizoram. As per this criteria, the district-wise break-up for the proposed 8th batch is as follows -

  1. Financial Position as per Road Map – IV:

In line with the directions of MHA and Hon'ble Supreme Court, a total projection of Rs. 68,60,50,978.08 was made in 2015 as per Road Map - IV for the final repatriation and rehabilitation of Reang (Bru) migrants from Tripura to Mizoram.

Out of the total projected amount, the Ministry of Home Affairs had so far sanctioned Rs. 4,70,41,765.00; Rs. 5,00,00,000.00 and Rs. 7,50,00,000.00 in financial year 2015 - 2016 and 2016 - 2017. To enable the State Government to complete the repatriation and rehabilitation process, the State Government had submitted revise fund requirement amounting to Rs. 1,21,30,04,641.55/- based on the identification of Reang (Bru) families to be repatriated.

The sanction of the revised fund projection is urgently required to initiate and complete the repatriation and rehabilitation process. And also to revise sanction orders of funds available with the State Government i.e. Rs. 4,70,41,765/-; Rs. 5,00,00,000/-;  Rs. 7,50,00,000/-  and Rs. 7,80,00,000/- sanctioned during 2015 – 2016; 2016 – 2017 and 2017 - 2018 to enable their utilisation and inclusion of the component for all additional Financial Assistance in the revision.

 

  1. PROPOSALS FOR REPATRIATION :

Depending upon the timely sanction of the entire projected revised funds by the Ministry, law & order situation and willingness to be repatriated to Mizoram by the Reangs(Bru) in the Relief Camps, Tripura, the State Government has finalised the following Movement Plan, which is given below -

The exercise to be completed on 10th September, 2018 up to the closure of the R/Camps i.e. 1st week of October, 2018 as targeted by MHA.

Identification & Repatriation Process

( 2nd phase – for those not in the E/Roll of Mizoram but claim to be from Mizoram )

 

  1. An exercise to identify the number of Bru families , in which at least one member of the family is included in the Electoral roll of the State, was earlier undertaken by the respective District Administration in November,2016.
  2. The target families for identification in the 2nd Phase would involve those Bru families left over from the above exercise in the 6(six) recognised transit camps.
  3. As a first step of identification, there may be stray instances in which any member of the left over families of Phase 1 of the Identification process are otherwise enrolled in the Electoral roll of the State, in which case the families may be identified for repatriation. It is not necessary that all eligible persons of a family are registered in the Electoral roll.
  4. Otherwise, the existence of any relatives/direct ancestors  (grand parents, parents, uncle, aunt etc) in the electoral roll of the State may be taken as an enabling criteria for identification, for which documentary proof shall be available.
  5. Given that regular revisions of electoral rolls have been conducted for the Bru families in the camp, the reason for non-inclusion in the electoral roll of the State may  be explained by the family members to the satisfaction of the Identification Officer.
  6. The above explanation may be correlated with the duration of their stay in the Transit camp, for ascertaining which documentary proof  like ration card issued by the Govt. of Tripura etc or summary enquiry on the spot may be of help.
  7. The Ration Card issued by the R&R Dept of Government of Tripura shall be invariably be demanded for considering the claim of the families for identification for repatriation. However, freshly issued ration cards i.e after the date of signing the Agreement for repatriation may not be given due weightage.
  8. There may be instances in which the list of family members submitted for identification does not match with the list of members indicated in the Ration Card. If the list of members does not match/ is more than what is indicated in the ration card, reasons for the same should be explained to the satisfaction of the Identification Officer, who shall accordingly indicate in the proforma his observation/recommendation ( like exclusion of excess family members from identification process, etc)  against  such discrepancy.
  9. There may also be instances in which the identity of the family members could not be ascertained either from the electoral roll or ration card, in such cases, any other documentary evidence issued by the State Govt. officials, Village Councils etc  may be reasonably considered.
  10. The identification process of the families could also rely on other circumstantial evidence like knowledge of the family members about their village of origin, village folks, adjacent village, terrain, rivers, localities, local practices etc of their place of origin.
  11. In all the above cases, the onus of proof to establish their place of origin shall lie with the family members.

  • Those identified to be genuine Reang (Brus) from Mizoram shall be repatriated to their respective districts by concerned DCs after finalization of village of resettlement, transportation and security arrangements.

  1. CONCLUSION :
  • The proposed dates for specific actions are subject to alteration depending upon the response from Reang (Bru) leaders within Tripura, NGOs and political parties within Mizoram regarding the proposal of the repatriation process.
  • The actual physical repatriation shall commence from the 14th August, 2018 on the condition that the entire revised fund projection are sanctioned to the State Government by MHA. But, if MHA sanctions the projected amount in parts, the repatriation dateline shall be revised.
  • The actual physical repatriation shall commence from the 14th August, 2018  if Free Ration allocation is made to the State Government within July, 2018 by Department of Food and public Distribution so that free ration can be procured from FCI by 15th July, 2018 by the State Government.
