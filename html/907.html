ARAKAN ARMY

 

            The Arakan Army is a Rakhine insurgent group in Myanmar (Burma), founded on the 10th April 2009. It is the armed wing of the United League of Arakan (ULA) and is currently led by Major General Twan Mrat Naing. The purpose of the AA is to protect Arakan people and to establish peace, justice, freedom and development.

            The AA is a participant in the Kachin conflict, fighting alongside the Kachin Independence Army (KIA) against the Tatmadaw (Myanmar Armed Forces). Most AA soldiers were originally trained at the KIA Military Academy; however, the AA has additional training camps in Rakhine State. According to the Myanmar Peace Monitor, the AA had more than 1,500 troops in 2014, including personnel stationed in the Rakhine State near Myanmar's border with Bangladesh. The Irrawaddy (A website by the Irrawaddy Publishing Group founded in 1990) has stated that, as of September 2015, the AA has 2,500 troops and 10,000 supporters

            The Arakan Army (AA) was founded on 10 April 2009 along with its political wing, the United League of Arakan (ULA), in what it describes as its "temporary headquarters" in Laiza, Kachin State. The group has stated that it advocates for:

  • self-determination for the multi-ethnic Arakanese population
  • the safeguarding and promotion of the national identity and cultural            heritage of the Arakan people
  • the "national dignity" and best interests of the Arakan people

            Following training, the group had planned to return to Arakan State and fight for self-determination; however, with the outbreak of fighting in Kachin State in June 2011, they were unable to return. As a result, they took up arms against the Myanmar Army in support of the KIA. In 2014, the AA started a settlement in Rakhine State (home land) near the border with Bangladesh and another near the border of Thai-Myanmar with which it has become much stronger and its combat abilities have been positively impacted.

(d)       Dt. 22.8.2017 : Information has been received that on 22.8.2017 @ around 9:00 a.m, a group of Arakan Army (AA) numbering around 120(one hundred twenty) attacked the Arakan Liberation Army (ALA) camp at Lungka du, Tri-junction inside Myanmar. There was an intermittent encounter between the two groups which lasted for about 27 hours and Arakan Army finally took over the camp. It was reported that 1(one) cadre of ALA succumbed to bullet injury and three others including 2nd Lt.Thon Lang sustained injuries. It was also learnt that 1(one) cadre of Arakan Army was killed while 1(one) was severely injured. The injured ALA cadres were taken to Bandukbanga village, Lawngtlai District for medical treatment. AA also reportedly took away all the domestic animals from Lungkadu camp.

            Meanwhile, on 23.8.2017 @ around 1:00 p.m, 2(two) groups of Arakan Army numbering around 50 each entered Refugee Basti village, Lawngtlai District and Sangara Sora, a sub-village in Lawngtlai District. AA reportedly killed 2(two) villagers at Refugee Basti namely, Nay Aung (54) and Hna Aung(32) and they burnt down all houses (45 approx.) of Refugee Basti village and took  away domestic animals, etc. of the village. AA also destroyed a Clinic at Refugee Basti which was jointly established by Arakan Liberation Party (ALP) and Burma Medical Association (BMA), an NGO. Consequence upon this, all the villagers of Refugee Basti numbering about 300 (three hundred) fled and took shelter at Bandukbanga village leaving behind all their properties. AA left the two villages at around 4:30 p.m and they proceeded towards Tri-Junction on the same day.

            It was also reported that Arakan Army also took away all the properties of 12(twelve) families of Siminasora villages, Lawngtlai District from their jhum land at Lungkadu forest inside Myanmar.

            1st Assam Rifles, Lunglei and Lawngtlai Police sent a team to Refugee Basti area on 24.8.2017 to enquire upon the incident.

16. Dt.16.10.2018 : Information has been received that, on 16.10.2018 an encounter occurred between Myanmarese Army (MA) and Arakan Army (AA) near Ohm Thu Wa village, Myanmar in which one S/S Pvt. Maung Lung @ Maung Lone (26), S/o Senga of Kyaw Ktaw village, Myanmar suffered a head injury. He was brought to Lawngtlai Kakichhuah village from which he was further transported to Aizawl on 17.10.2018 under proper escort and admitted at Greenwood Hospital, Bawngkawn @ 10:10 p.m. Bawngkawn PS Case No. 242/18 dt. 18.10.2018 u/s 14 Foreigners Act was registered against him. Maung Lung stated that AA killed 3 (three) MA personnel in the encounter.

 

Input received also stated that MA and AA also had an encounter on 17.10.2018 @ 1:10 p.m to 3:45 p.m near Varang village, Myanmar close to the Indian border in which 3 (three) Myanmarese officers including LIB 41 Battalion Commander were reportedly killed while AA personnel suffered only minor injuries. Input further indicated that they are still on the point of gun-battle at any time.
