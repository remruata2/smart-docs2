vii.       Recent intelligence -

 

                A thorough inquiry was conducted on the allegation made by Peace Accord MNF Returnee Association (PAMRA), General Headquarters through its Press Release issued on 24.01.2024 that Parbatya Chattagram Jana Samhiti Samiti (PCJSS) also known as JSS (Santu Group), a Chakma ethnic base underground organisation operating in Chittagong Hill Tract, Bangladesh has military training camps inside Lunglei and Mamit District of Mizoram. A detail report on the allegation is as under.

 

            Parbatya Chattagram Jana Samhiti Samiti (PCJSS) or United People’s Party of the Chittagong Hill Tracts was formed on 17th February, 1972 with the aim of establishing the right of self-determination of the Jumma peoples (tribal group of people of the Chittagong Hill Tracts).  Its military arm, the Shanti Bahini was used to fight the government forces in the Hill Tracts. A Peace Accord was later signed on 2nd December, 1997 which led to the disarmament of the Shanti Bahini and enabled the PCJSS to return to mainstream politics. However, dissatisfied with the non implementation of the agreement between PCJSS and the Bangladesh Government, some members of PCJSS started fighting with the Bangladesh Government since 2008 which often involved arms conflicts. The alias of PCJSS mentioned in the Press Release, JSS (Santu Group) is not an official organisation but merely the same political party, PCJSS is currently headed by Jyotirindra Bodhipriya Larma alias Santu Larma. The present status of the mentioned villages alleged to have military training camps of PCJSS inside Mizoram is as follow:-

 

I. Lunglei District:

 

(a)       Salmore (Salmur) – A temporary transit camp for procurement of essential     commodities and supply of ration to its members in Bangladesh at the        vicinity of Salmur village. Khawthlangtuipui/ Karnaphuly River flows    between the transit camp and Salmur village. Further, there is no report of       presence of arms and ammunitions or members of PCJSS undergoing training       at the transit camp.

 

(b)       Tara Banya (Terabonia) – No transit camp.

 

(c)       Chumochumi (Sumasumi) – No transit camp.

 

(d)       Malchari/Malsuri – Family members of some members of PCJSS are camped            at the so called Family Quarters at the vicinity of Malsuri village. There are a     total of 20 houses out of which 2 are unoccupied.

 

(e)       Bhalukkyachari (Balukiasuri) – No transit camp.

 

(f)        Andar Manek Village (Undermanik) – No transit camp.

 

II. Mamit District:

 

(a)       Silsury village (Silsuri) – No transit camp. Further, the alleged Company Commander at Silsuri camp, Alo Chakma is a 55 years old resident of Barkal, Rangamati District, Bangladesh who occasional visited his Uncle-in-Law, BN Lolit Ranjan, a Village Council Member of Silsuri Village.

 

(b)       Amchari (Amsuri) – No transit camp.  Further, the alleged Company Commander at Amchari camp, Binanta Chakma (Binanda Chakma as per PARMRA Press Release) is a 36 years old resident of Amchari (Amchurimukh) locality of Rajiv Nagar. Binanta Chakma (36) s/o Bhubonjoy Chakma possessed an Indian Voters’ EPIC Card and lives with his wife and children at the aforementioned address.

 

            Further inquiry revealed that a medical centre with around 10 houses was run by PCJSS at the vicinity of Tipperaghat village, Lunglei District. The medical centre has around 15 workers including a doctor and caters the needs of members of PCJSS as well as civilian patients. Also, a temporary rest house (transit camp) of PCJSS is present at Nunsury Village, Lunglei District and around 5 members of PCJSS are currently staying at the rest house.

3. FIR against Teressa, Ph.D scholar of Mizoram University:

            On 30.1.2024 , H.Lalthianghlima, President, MZP Gen. Headquarters submitted an FIR against Miss Teresa Ningthoujam, Ph.D scholar, Mizoram University at Vaivakawn Police Station. In the FIR, MZP President accused Miss Teresa Ningthoujam, who is staying at Kumtluang Women Hostel, MZU of playing active role in raising funds in Mizoram for procuring of arms and ammunitions to help Meitei armed groups and alleged that the viral video shows that the fund are to be used for killing Kuki tribes. MZP insisted appropriate legal proceeding against Miss Teresa Ningthoujam. 

            A Press Release was also issued by MZP Gen. Headquarters in this regard on the same day wherein they strongly condemned Miss Teresa Ningthoujam for allegedly collecting donation for Arambai Tengol and for playing active role in ethnical violence in Manipur. MZP also mentioned that they had met MZU authority the day before and requested them to immediately terminate the Ph.D programme of Miss Teresa Ningthoujam.

 

4.  Bangladesh- KNF dialogue :

            After a series of  online meeting, "Third round Peace Talk" between Kuki-Chin National Front/Army (KCNA) and Government of Bangladesh (GoB) represented by the Peace Committee was held on 5th November, 2023 at Hmunlai village, near Ruma bazar, Bandarban district, Bangladesh. Although no definite agreements were made, the meeting, agreed to maintained  cease-fire between both the parties until the next peace talk. The next talks between the two parties are scheduled to conducted during the month of February 2024.The understanding between Bangladesh Government/Army and KCNA could be temporary as it could be a short term strategy of Bangladesh Government  for conducting a peaceful General Election which however was concluded on 07.01.2024.
