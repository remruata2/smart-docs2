Point No. 8. Groups agitating against developmental projects

 

Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) is a project that will connect the eastern Indian seaport of Kolkata with Sittwe seaport in Rakhine State, Myanmar by sea. It covers a total length of 87.180 kms between Lawngtlai and Indo-Myanmar Border. The project work in Mizoram commenced on 10.10.2010 and was scheduled to be completed by 2014, but it has not been completed till today.

It is funded by Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH) with the initial Project cost of Rs. 792.62 Crores and Mizoram PWD functions as the implementing agency. The cost of the project was revised and approved with a total amount of Rs. 1,011.52 Crore.

The project is divided into 3 (Three) packages - package I, II &III. The construction of packages I & III are under RDS Project Ltd and that of the package II is under Atlanta ARSS (Joint Venture).

Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLAO)

            As a result of the commencement of the Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) one association called Lawngtlai District Land Owners Association (LDLAO) sprung up on the issue of land compensation. Some of the activities of this LDLOA are as under:-

1.    LDLOA held its General Meeting on 24.5.2016 at J.H. Lorrain Hall, Lawngtlai Bazar under the chairmanship of Pu H. Remmawia, President. The meeting resolved the following resolutions:-

  1. Condemned the undue delay in distribution of compensation to land owners under KMMTTP and determined to organize picketing against the Companies under KMMTTP from 1st June, 2016 if the State Government fails to take up the process of allocation of fund for payment of compensation.
  2. The rate of compensation under KMMTTP be equivalent and at par with compensation rate of other districts in Mizoram.
  3. Demanded the authority to give compensation prior to the commencement of any project in Lawngtlai District in future.

2.    LDLOA conducted picketing against contractors/staff/workers engaged under KMMTTP from 1.6.2016 which lasted till 8.7.2016.

3.    LDLOA held a meeting on 29.8.2016 @ 12:30 PM at their Office and rejected the proposed lists of beneficiaries of compensation which were not included in the earlier Land Revenue Survey.

4.    Office Bearers Meeting of LDLOA was held on 7.9.2016 and the meeting decided to issue stay order against the on-going works of KMMTTP if the DC, Lawngtlai District could not complete all the necessary works for awarding of compensation to the land owners before 20th September, 2016. The meeting also demanded not to include any new beneficiaries whose names were not included in the earlier Land Revenue Survey.

5.    They organized indefinite blockade of the KMMTTP Road w.e.f., 20.2.2018 in protest against unduly long delay in  payment of compensation for 9 (nine) years since the commencement of the work.

 

6.    Altercation occurred between Officials and LDLOA President PuRemmawia during spot verification with regard to compensation claim of LDLOA carried out on 20.5.2019 @ 7:00 am as the LDLOA had objected to the verification insisting that it was unnecessary exercise.

8.    In pursuant of the Executive Meeting held on 9.7.2019, LDLOA submitted a letter to the Hon'ble Chief Minister and the DC, Lawngtlai on 10.7.2019 requesting them to revoke the cancellation order of the Supplementary Draft Award No.1 of 2006 dt.20.10.2016 for KMMTTP Road issued by DC, Lawngtlai on 4.7.2019.
