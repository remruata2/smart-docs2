A. _Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt.10 .11.2022_

****Female voters outnumbered male voters in Mizoram**: As per the instruction of Election Commission of India (ECI), Special Summary Revision of Photo Electoral Rolls,2023 is to be conducted in Mizoram. In this connection, Draft Electoral Roll was released in the state yesterday (9.11.2022) wherein female voters outnumbered male voters. According to this Draft Electoral Roll, out of a total voters of 8,31,272 - 4,03,927 were males while the remaining 4,27,345 were female voters. There are 9 out of 11 districts in Mizoram where the number of female voters are higher than their male counterparts while Mamit and Khawzawl are the only districts where the numbers of male voters are higher than females. Among all the districts of Mizoram, Aizawl district is having the highest number of voters with 2,76,657 voters while Hnahthial district with 15,694 voters is the district with the lowest. For the said Special Summary Revision of Photo Electoral Rolls, 2023, addition, deletion and corrections of names will be conducted during the period between 9th November - 8th December, 2022 with the release of Final Electoral Roll scheduled to be held on 15th January, 2023.**

B. _Front page of The Aizawl Post newspaper Dt. 10.11.2022_

**Seizure of Heroin and Meth:******Mizoram police seized Heroin and Methamphetamine in two different locations and booked their suspected owners under appropriate section of law under ND&amp;PS Act.

 On 8.11.2022, a joint operation team of Special Narcotics Police Station and 2nd Assam Rifles who conducted a raid in Aibawk/Sialsuk areas recovered 342 grms. of Heroin and 6.37 Kgs. of methamphetamine from the possession of Sangchhunga (37), Thaltlang village, Lawngtlai district at the outskirt of Sialsuk village. Meanwhile, acting on a tipoff received, personnel of Lunglei District Special Branch seized 1.747 kgs. of Heroin on 9.11.2022 (Wednesday) from Khupkhankual (25) and Haudeihchin (25) Champhai Vengthar in an area between Tlawng river bridge and Pukpui village.
