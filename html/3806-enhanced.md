PREFACE

The “Statistical Handbook of Mizoram 2020”, which is the 23rd (twenty third) in the series presents statistical data of the State in a consise form, covering various sectors of an economy. Data were collected from various Central and State Departments, Directorates, Offices, various Non-Government Organisations, Churches and other official documents and publications. It seek to provide statistical information on Socio-Economic aspect of the State. Efforts have been made to incorporate upto-date information for the benefit of interested individuals, policy makers, administrators, bureaucrats, planners, scholars, research scholars and tourists.

I gratefully acknowledged the kind cooperation extended by all data suppliers and hope continuous cooperation in future. Even though all possible efforts have been made for publishing this Publication before the end of the Fiscal 2020-2021; Covid-19 Pandemic delayed collection of statistical data.

I expressed my sincere gratitude to all Deputy Directors, Research Officer, Inspector of Statistics, Sub-Inspector of Statistics, Field Assistant of Statistics, Data Entry Operators of Technical Wing of this Directorate for bringing out this invaluable Publication.

Any suggestion and advice for further improvement in terms of Content, Presentation, Quality, etc. for the next publication would highly be appreciated.

(B. LALRINHLUA) Director

State Information

1. MIZORAM : Mizoram is situated in the North

Eastern part of India. It shares 404

km and 318 km long international

borders with Myanmar and

Bangladesh respectively.

2. State Capital : Aizawl. It is located in the northern

part of Mizoram at an altitude of

1132 meters above sea level

3. State Animal : Saza (Serow)

4. State Bird : Vavu (Pheasant)

5. State Tree : Iron Wood (Herhse)

6. State Flower : Senhri (Red Vanda)

7. Highest Mountain: Phawngpui, in Lawngtlai District,

with an elevation of 2157 metres

8. Longest River : Tlawng River, measuring 185.15 km.

It originates in Zopui Hill (Near

Zobawk in Lunglei) at an elevation

of 1,395 metres (4,577 ft).

9. Important Festival : Chapchar Kut, Christmas Festival,

New Year Festival, Anthurium Festival, Lyuva Khutla Festival,Winter Festival

(xi)

10. National Parks : Murlen National Park, Phawngpui National Park

11. Important Wildlife: i) Animals :Serow, Hoolock Gibbon,

Binturang, Indian Bison, Clouded

Leopardii) Birds :Hume’s Pheasant,

Great Indian Hornbill, Hornbill,

Peacock Pheasant, Kalij Pheasant

12. Main Rivers : Tlawng, Tiau lui, Chhimtuipui, Tut,

Tuivai, Tuichang, Tuirial, Mat,

Tuivawl, Tuirini, Serlui

13. Forest Area : 18748 km2 (88.93% of the state

geographical area) as per Forest

Survey of India Report, 2015

14. Historical Places : Rih Dil, Lianchhiari Lunglen Tlang, Thasiama seno neihna, Lamsial Puk, Sibuta Lung, Kawtchhuah Ropui

15. Tourism Interest : State Museum, Reiek Tlang, Hmuifang, Sialsuk Tlang, Tam Dil, Vantawng Falls, Serlui B, Dampa Reserve, Zokhua, Hlimen Park etc.
