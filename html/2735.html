DETAIL REPORT ON KNA/AA CONFLICT

 

           No. MSB/CB/SR/2022/916

           Dated: 21st November, 2022

 

             Reliable information received indicated that on 15.11.2022, there was speculation among Kuki Chin National Army (KNA) that their camp ‘Capital Camp’ situated in between Salopui village and Chaihkhiang village of Bangladesh was approached by Arakan Army (AA) by means of Bangladesh Army vehicles and plan to attack. Consequence upon this, KNA had informed villagers of Sunsong, Salopui, Thingdawl and Chhaihkhiang villages of Bangladesh to leave their villages and took shelters. On 16.11.022, AA cadre numbering about 60 (sixty) entered Khaihkhiang village and send emissary from civilian to inform KNA that they wanted to hold talk with them. In response to this, KNA had reportedly sent 4 (four) KNA cadres and 9 (nine) civilians as delegates for the talk with AA. But it was reported that AA had detained all the 13 (thirteen) KNA delegates. Consequence upon this, encounter between Bangladesh Army backed AA and KNA started taking place. It was also reported that the main cause of the encounter between Bangladesh Army backed AA and KNA was that a few days back KNA had allegedly killed 1 (one) AA cadre and they also took away 1 (one) machine gun. In retaliation to this incident and to counter KNA from entering inside Banderban District, Bangladesh, the claimed operation area of AA, AA started the encounter. It was also reported that AA demanded KNA to return the seized machine gun and promise to release the 13 (thirteen) delegates of KNA detained by them in exchange of the machine gun.  However, it was learnt that KNA have not yet return the machine gun to AA nor AA have release the 13 (thirteen) delegates detained by them. Information received also indicated that AA had given an ultimatum that they will take appropriate action against the 13 (thirteen) detained persons if KNA did not return the machine gun on or before 6:00 pm today, i.e. 21.11.2022. It was also learnt that both AA and KNA agreed to hold peace dialogue today, i.e. 21.11.2022 @3:00 pm. and both party will send 3 (three) civilians each as delegates.

 

             Due to the encounter between Bangladesh Army Backed AA and KNA, around 300 (three hundred) residents (mostly Bawm tribe) including children and women of  Sunsong, Salopui, Thingdawl and Chhaihkhiang villages of Bangladesh   has fled their villages to take shelter inside Mizoram. As many as 272 refugees entered Bandukbanga village, Lawngtlai District yesterday evening i.e., 20.11.2022 @ 4:00 p.m. and residents of Bandukbanga and BSF personnel are providing food/relief to them. Mr. Sitro Mohon, MDC, LADC who was available at the village also held talks with BSF for provision of shelter to the refugees.

 

             Kuki-Chin National Front (KNF) issued a reported Press Statement wherein they alleged that on 14.11.2022, a secret meeting was held between senior officers of Arakan Army (AA) and Bangladesh Army and Directorate General of Forces Intelligence (DGFI) officials, Border Guards Bangladesh (BGB) officials at Tamra at Bangladesh-Myanmar border. Some senior officers were present on behalf of the AA Army in this secret meeting. The Meeting reportedly made the following resolution:

    i.        Kuki-Chin National Front (KNF) should be dispersed  from the soil of
             Bangladesh through the Arakan Army. As the price, the Bangladesh
             government will pay 10 million taka to the AA army.

    ii.       Arakan Army and Bangladesh Army will jointly conduct operations
             against KNF. If the campaign is successful, the Bangladesh government
             will provide all the necessary expenditure to the Arakan Army.

    iii.      Bangladesh will allow to use its territory for AA until Arakan becomes
             independent.

 

             They also stated that on the way back from the above meeting, a high-ranking officer of the DGFI was shot dead in a sudden attack by a guerilla. According to the decision of the said agreement, on 16.11.22, about 60-70 cadres of AA army with sophisticated weapons went near Cheikhiang village to launch attack against KNA. In pursuance of the stated decision, AA cadres came towards Cheikhiang Para, and as there was no KNF member in Cheikhiang Para, they started harassing the villagers in various ways. As soon as they came to the village, they openly admitted that they had an agreement with the Bangladesh Army to fight against KNA. Some innocent villagers, including unarmed KNF members, were caught and tied up, and they were reportedly handed over to the Bangladesh Army.

 

             In this regard, leaders of Mizo Students' Union (MSU) led by Samuel Zoramthanpuia, President MSU held Emergency Meeting at MSU office in the wee hour today, i.e. 21.11.2022 @1:00 a.m. The meeting strongly condemned the alleged refusal of Zo-ethnic tribes, who fled their home from entering Mizoram by BSF.

                   Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) General Headquarters  also issued a Press Release today, expressing their regret over the problems being faced by Zo-ethnic tribes in Bangladesh due to the operation launced against KNA by Bangladesh Army and Arakan Army (AA). In this regard, leaders of MZP and Mizoram Bawm Students Association (MBSA) also met Dr. Lalthangliana, Minister today @ 12:30 p.m. and  requested him to provide necessary assistance to 271 refugees (may be read as 272), who are taking shelter inside Mizoram. Zofa Tangrual Pawl (Zofa Allied Organisation) and Mizoram Bawm Students Association also issued a Press Release today condemning the operation launched against KNA by Bangladesh Army and AA.

             Detailed information regarding the encounter between AA and KNA has been collected and situation is under close watch.
