10.2.2022  : Mizoram Merchant Association (MIMA) General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 10.2.2022 stated that some shop owners are running their shops by incurring losses since 2020 due to imposition of Covid-19 induced Lockdown.  In this regard, it highlighted the following :-

(i)        Shop owners are severely affected as they could not open their shops properly
since March, 2020. At the same time, they have to pay salary to their workers,
electric bills, rent etc. In this regard, they expressed their desire to open their
shops by strictly following Covid Appropriate Behaviour (CAB) and opined that closing of business shops is not an effective way to combat COVID-19 pandemic.

(ii)       They also opined that opening of business shops is not the reason for surge in
COVID-19. It further stated that no contamination of COVID-19 occurred from
their shops as they are very careful and strictly followed CAB.

(iii)     Medical and essential shops are opened daily during enforcement of opening of
shops in staggered manner and there has been minimal report of COVID-19
contraction case from such shops. The same clearly depicted that shop is not a
place of spreading the disease. They considered issuance of order which directed
opening of shops in staggered manner only deprived and caused hardships to
them.

(iv)      Expressed their desire to run their business regularly by strictly following CAB
and SOP, and if the same is not allowed, the state government should provide relief to them.

            It further stated that if opening of shops in staggered manner clause is not amended in the present SOP, MIMA Executive Meeting will be held to discuss their course of action.

14.3.2022:     Mizoram Merchant Association (MIMA), General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 11.3.2022 stated that MIMA Executive Meeting after close scrutiny of AMC Licensing Regulations, 2021 opined that there is no provision for the safety of indigenous/local shop owners as the documents required for obtaining shop licence could easily be obtained by non-Mizos. It also stated that MIMA found that a good numbers of non-Mizo shop owners got registered under the Aizawl Municipal Council (AMC) and they still stand firm on their decision that all traders/shop owners should not get registered under AMC. It also urged the authorities of AMC not to coerce business owners to get registration under AMC. It further urged all Banks not to demand AMC registration while applying loan.

 

27.5.2022 : Mizoram Merchant Association (MIMA) General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on the evening of 26.5.2022 stated that leaders of MIMA met Pu David N Lhouvum, SBI Chief Manager the same day and submitted a written petition in connection with the difficulties faced by the general public due to shortages of small changes in the market viz., Re.1 and Rs. 2, 5, 10 and 20 and the difficulties faced in exchange of Rs.10 coins. 20.6.2022:     Mizoram Merchant Association (MIMA), General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 18.6.2022 stated that immediate issuance of POL control/rationing order due to occurrence of road damages or other natural disaster in other states caused panic among the general public, which led to long queue in the petrol pump. It also stated that during such a situation, the public are restricted from collecting petrol using gallon/container from Petrol Pump while the state government officials are allowed to collect petrol as much as they want using coupon. It further stated the need to curb illegal selling of petrol in places other than petrol pumps as petroleum blackers (one who sells petrol without permission) engaged in hoarding which allegedly led to immediate depletion of stock of POL in the petrol pumps.

22.7.2022 :    Mizoram Merchant Association (MIMA) General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 22.7.2022 stated that MIMA General Headquarters Executive Meeting held yesterday discussed the proposal intended to be submitted in the Cabinet Committee regarding enforcement of Minimum Wages as per Shops and Establishment Act for shops and establishment within the state. It also stated that no state in India used such kind of law and the same was enforced only in big industry, private hospital and well established factory and insurance company. It further expressed disappointment over the said initiative and the same will not be accepted at any cost. It further stated that they intend to convey the matter to Chief Minister and other concern authorities, and appealed the Cabinet Committee not to approve the said proposal.
