general and armed ethnic groups of Chin State, Myanmar to overthrow the military

number of refugees particularly the Chin Community into Mizoram. Since the Military

 

Coup, there has been a reported 8 (eight) major influxes of Myanmar Nationals into

 

the State. Leaders of these ethnic groups held a workshop at Aizawl during 7th – 8th

 

Sept.2023 in the goal of forming an interim Government at Chinland .

 

Apart from the Chin community, due to the arm clashes between the State

 

Administration Council’s (SAC) Myanmar Army and Arakan Army in Rakhine State

 

and the subsequent capturing of almost all important cities by the Arakan Army,

 

some communities of Khumi tribe are also seeking shelter into the Southern part of

 

Mizoram reportedly due to the atrocities of the Arakan Army.

C. Bawm/Bangladesh refugee :-

 

1) Brief history :-

 

A military operation against Kuki Chin National Army (KNA) by Bangladesh

 

Army on 15.11.2022 at Chhaihkhiang Village, Bangladesh resulted in the mass exodus

 

of civilians, mainly the Bawm community during which 272 civilians including women

 

and children fled their villages to take shelter inside Mizoram.

 

Subsequent military operations led to 3 major influxes of Bawm civilians into

 

Lawngtlai District of Mizoram which briefly halted during the Election process of

 

Bangladesh in January 2024 and the peace talk between the KNA and Bangladesh

 

Government. However, the looting of Banks and arms by KNA on 2nd and 3rd April 2024

 

resulted in an intensified operation against the KNA leading to more influxes of Bawm

 

community into Mizoram.

 

The latest Bawm refugees numbering 93 persons who entered Damdep-II

 

Village, Lawngtlai District, Mizoram on the night of 04.07.2024 were evacuated by BSF

 

personnel to Crocodile Camp, Kamtuli in an attempt to deport the refugees. However,

 

with the intervention of the State Government, the 93 Bawm refugees who were joined

 

by another 33 Bawm refugees at Chhippui BSF BOP were safely transported to

 

Chamdur Village on the morning of 08.07.2024.

D. Brief Note on KMMTTP :

 

The Kaladan Multimodal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) is a strategic

 

infrastructure initiative by the GOI involving multimodal transport route combining land,

 

river, and sea segments to link Northeast India with Myanmar and the Bay of Bengal. The

 

route spans 882.21 km and comprises :

 

Lawngtlai to Zorinpui (Mizoram) : land route 87.51 kms.

 

Zorinpui Border to Kaletwa (Myanmar) : land route 45 kms.

 

Kaletwa to Paletwa (Myanmar) : land route 60.70 kms.

 

Paletwa to Sittwe (Myanmar) : 150 km river route.

 

Sittwe to Kolkata Port (Bay of Bengal) : 539 km sea route.

 

As of June 2024, the KMMTTP land route construction within Mizoram is

 

completed which however is occasionally hindered by landslides caused by heavy rainfall.

 

In the meantime, due to the ongoing clashes in Myanmar, only around 10 kms earthwork

 

between Zorinpui and Paletwa land route in Myanmar is executed by the assigned

 

company for the project which is Myanmar New Power Construction Company.

 

The Arakan Army, comprising the major ethnic Arakanese in the Rakhine State of

 

Myanmar has reportedly controlled most of Rakhine State including some parts of Chin

 

State since February 2024 thereby controlling the proposed route of KMMTTP within

 

Myanmar except Sittwe. However, with the ongoing clashes between the Myanmar Army

 

and resistance forces in other parts of Myanmar, the Arakan Army as well as the civilians

 

in Rakhine State heavily depended upon goods and petroleum oils transported through

 

Mizoram mainly Lawngtlai and Siaha District. At the same time, the tension between the

 

Arakan Army and the Chin ethnic armed groups in Myanmar led to the banning of

 

transport of essential goods and petroleum oil into Myanmar by Central Young Lai

 

Association (CYLA) which sided with their Chin brethren since April 2024. However, the

 

banning of transportation of essential goods into Myanmar is now limited only to

 

petroleum oil and fertilizers through Lawngtlai District.

 

E. Arecanut Smuggling:-

 

Smuggling of illegal arecanut through Mizoram took place since the year 2016

 

mainly to evade the high tax imposed on the import of arecanut by GOI. Since then, as

 

on 30th September 2024, Mizoram Police had seized 45,194 bags (3,176,830 kgs.

 

approx) worth a total of Rs. 50,09,91,750/- which were further handed over to

 

Custom Dept. and concerned DC’s for appropriate legal action or disposal.

 

The Aizawl Bench of Hon’ble Gauhati High Court in its order dated 16th July

 

2024 has directed the CBI to probe the smuggling of arecanuts into Mizoram following

 

a PIL filed by social activist Pi Vanramchhuangi (Ruatfela Nu) on 29th April 2024.

H. Mizoram-Assam Boundary Dispute:

 

1) Brief history :

 

The Mizoram-Assam border, defined by the Constitution of India 1935, spans

 

164.6 km. Historically, Mizoram and Assam have contested the exact demarcation of

 

this boundary. Mizoram claims a 1875 boundary marked by the British-era Inner Line

 

Reserve Forest, while Assam adheres to a 1933 Survey of India map as its

 

constitutional boundary.

 

2) Bone of Contention

 

The crux of the dispute lies in differing interpretations of the border's

 

demarcation. Mizoram asserts that the Inner Line Reserve Forest should be the

 

boundary, while Assam upholds the 1933 map. This fundamental disagreement has led

 

to a series of confrontations and skirmishes over the years.

 

Recent Events

 

The conflict has intensified with notable incidents:

 

ï‚· 27.02.2018: The Mizoram Zirlai Pawl (MZP) constructed a rest house at Bairabi

 

Zophai, which was demolished by Assam Police and Forest Officials, claiming it was

 

within Assam’s territory.

 

ï‚· 30.06.2021: Tensions peaked when approximately 60 youths from Vairengte, aiming

 

to set up a camp at Aitlang, Vairengte for Mizoram Police, were stopped by Assam

 

Police. This led to a standoff involving around 200 Assam Police personnel and 10

 

Mizoram Police. Assam Police, heavily outnumbering Mizoram forces, occupied the

 

disputed area, prompting Mizoram's retreat to maintain status quo.

 

ï‚· 26.07.2021: The situation escalated further when around 200 Assam Police, led by

 

IGP Anurag Agarwal, attempted to forcefully remove Mizoram Police from their camp

 

located at Vairengte Auto Stand. The confrontation turned violent with Assam Police

 

using tear gas and smoke bombs. A series of exchanges of gunfire ensued, resulting in

 

injuries on both sides. Properties, including vehicles, were damaged or set ablazed by

 

unknown miscreants.

 

High-Level Meetings

 

Efforts to resolve the dispute include several high-level meetings:

 

ï‚· 27.07.2021: Chief Secretary level meeting alongwith DGP’s of both States chaired by

 

Union Home Secretary at New Delhi.

 

ï‚· 05.08.2021: A Ministerial level border talks meeting in Aijal Club, Mizoram.

 

ï‚· 09.08.2022: Another Ministerial level meeting in Aijal Club.

 

ï‚· 17.11.2022: Ministerial level Discussions in Guwahati, Assam.

 

ï‚· 09.08.2024: Ministerial level latest talks in Mizoram’s State Guest House.

 

The Mizoram-Assam boundary dispute remains a complex issue, marked by historical

 

grievances and sporadic violence. Continued dialogue and negotiation are crucial for

 

achieving a lasting resolution.

 

I. The Free Movement Regime along the Indo-Myanmar Border:

 

1) Brief history:

 

The Free Movement Regime (FMR), is a bilateral agreement between India and

 

Myanmar allowing citizens to travel freely within 16 km of the border for up to 72 hours

 

without a visa. This initiative aims to maintain traditional ties and alleviate insecurity for

 

tribals residing along the border. The FMR covers 1,629.5 km of unfenced border shared

 

between four Indian states viz. Arunachal Pradesh Nagaland Manipur and Mizoram with

 

Myanmar.

 

Page 5 of 8

 

In January 2018, the Union Cabinet approved a Memorandum of

 

Understanding to enhance economic interactions through this regime. However,

 

concerns have arisen about its misuse. The Ministry of Home Affairs reported that

 

militants and criminals exploit the FMR to smuggle weapons, narcotics, and counterfeit

 

currency, taking advantage of the regime's leniencies to cross borders and evade

 

capture.

 

2) Enhancing Security and Mobility:

 

To address these issues, a committee led by the Special Secretary (Internal

 

Security) was established in August 2017. The committee's mandate includes creating

 

standardized procedures and guidelines for the FMR, delineating roles for central and

 

state agencies, and developing a uniform Border Pass system. This pass will enable

 

secure movement within the 16 km border area, with strict controls to prevent misuse.

 

The committee also plans to implement biometric cards for local residents

 

and ensure that unauthorized vehicle crossings are prevented. The new measures are

 

designed to balance the benefits of the FMR with enhanced security to safeguard both

 

nations and their border communities.

 

Mizoram shares 496.531 kms long border with Myanmar which is unfenced

 

and porous, Six Districts of the State share border with Myanmar. Out of the six

 

Districts, Champhai shares the longest border with Myanmar.

 

3) Border Fencing and Free Movement Regime Abolition

 

The Mizoram Assembly recently passed a resolution unanimously opposing

 

the Indian government’s proposal to construct a fence along the Indo-Myanmar border

 

and abolish the Free Movement Regime (FMR). Introduced by Home Minister K.

 

Sapdanga, the resolution highlights concerns that these measures would adversely

 

affect the Zo ethnic communities, historically divided by the border imposed during

 

British colonial rule. The Assembly argues that reinforcing this division through new

 

barriers would disrupt livelihoods and social ties of border communities.

 

Note: Indo-Bangladesh Border Fencing

 

The Indo-Bangladesh border, spanning approximately 4,096 kilometres, is one of

 

the world’s longest international boundaries. This extensive border has seen

 

significant efforts to bolster security and control migration through a comprehensive

 

fencing project. The fencing along the Indo-Bangladesh border is largely completed,

 

with over 90% installed. High-priority areas vulnerable to illegal crossings have been

 

addressed, though some sections remain incomplete or need maintenance due to

 

geographical challenges. The fencing primarily consists of barbed wire, supplemented

 

by floodlights and surveillance systems.

 

J. Insurgency in Mizoram :

 

Mizoram itself experiences low level insurgencies in the North Eastern and

 

the North Western regions. However, Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) (the

 

insurgent group from the northern parts) had signed a Memorandum of Settlement

 

(MoS) on 2nd April 2018. The signing of MOS brought about an end to over two-decade

 

armed insurgency of the HPC(D). The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC-D was held on

 

12th April, 2018 where around 102 (one hundred two) Cadres and 12 (twelve) Council

 

Members of HPC(D) came to over ground at the ceremony. However, the other faction

 

of HPC(D) led by Lalhmingthanga Sanate have started resurfacing and reorganized

 

themselves. They have been indulging in collection of donation and extortion during

 

the years 2022 and 2023.

K. Surrendering of Myanmar Army:

 

The increase military operation conducted by the armed ethnic groups in

 

different parts of Chin State and Rakhine State of Myanmar led to surrendering of

 

Myanmar Army to Mizoram Police as well as Assam Rifles since the last part of 2023.

 

In the month of November and December last year, as many as 255 personnel of

 

Myanmar Army surrendered inside Champhai, Siaha and Lawngtlai District of

 

Mizoram while in the month of January, 2024, 380 Myanmar Army personnel

 

surrendered themselves inside Lawngtlai and Siaha District of Mizoram. These army

 

personnel were at first airlifted back to Myanmar by Assam Rifles using helicopter

 

but later were evacuated by military aircraft of Myanmar from Lengpui Airport. The

 

Myanmar aircraft which is scheduled to evacuate the last 92 personnel crashed

 

landed at Lengpui airport on 23.01.2024 injuring 6 crews on board but without any

 

serious injuries or casualties. The last batch of the Myanmar Army personnel were

 

finally evacuated by two Myanmar Airforce Special Flight on 24.01.2024. The total

 

number of Myanmar Army personnel surrendered to Mizoram Police/Indian Security

 

personnel in Mizoram is 635 as on 30.08.2024.

 

L. Biometric enrolment of refugees:

 

The State Government under the leadership of the Hon’ble Chief minister

 

decided to proceed with the biometric data collection of Myanmar and Bangladesh

 

refugees in Mizoram provided that these refugees are not deported until and unless

 

peace prevails in their respective countries. The State’s Home department has write a

 

letter to the Union Ministry to examine the Foreigner’s Identification portal(FIP)

 

which is meant for capturing the particulars of illegal immigrants and subsequent

 

deportation requesting a change in the FIP to allow the refugees to remain in the

 

State, and a reply on the matter is still awaited by the State. The centre has extended

 

the time of Biometric enrolment till December, 2024 and Union Home Minister has

 

reportedly assured the Hon’ble CM that the refugees will not be deported till peace is

 

restored in the neighbouring countries.
