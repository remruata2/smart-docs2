Notes on Mizoram Police CID (Special Branch):

A. BRIEF HISTORY OF THE UNIT:

          The CID Special Branch housed in CID Complex Bungkawn, Aizawl was created and started functioning in the year 1973. Pu A.K. Roy, APS was the first Superintendent of Police of CID(SB). Being an intelligence agency of the state it is playing an important role in the collection and dissemination of intelligence pertaining to the movement of insurgents, illegal trafficking of arms and other anti-social elements bearing security of the state on law and order problem.

 

1. Functioning and responsibilities of the Department   

1. The main functions of the Special Branch is to promptly communicate to Government and to the Director General of Police informations on all matters of political and public importance.

 

2. The Special Branch also deals with matters of national security and security of individuals who are important to the nation.

 

3. Related to above, the following are also the duties of State Special Branch :

    1. to communicate to the Special Branch of others States information which is of interest to them;
    2. to communicate to Deputy Inspector General of Police, Range and Superintendent of Police of the District and other specified officers important informations which is of interest to them;
    3. to prepare periodical reports on matters falling within its purview and to forward copies to those specified;
    4. to make secret enquiries into important matters of political nature or those affecting national interest;

(e)       to help in the investigation of those crimes which have a political purpose or motive and in which political suspects are involved.

 

Scope of functions:

The scope of functions of the Special Branch and the subject dealt with includes the following: -

(i) Political movements and associations, their inception, progress and development – this includes societies having any political purpose avowed or concealed.

(ii) Political activities, open as well as secret and subversive.

(iii) Labour Unions, their formation and development and labour activities.

(iv) Volunteer organisations and their activities.

(v) Youth movements and students associations and their activities.

(vi) Religious and communal activities and religious activities having a political complexion.

(vii) Social and economic matters having political significance.

(viii) Movements and activities of foreigners believed to be politically motivated.

(ix) Anti-national activities or those likely to affect the security and safety of the State or of India generally.

(x) Security schemes, including security of vital installations and key industries.

(xi) Movements and activities of political suspects and surveillance and watch over them.

(xii) Censorship of newspapers, periodicals, pamphlets, party literature, etc.

(xiii) Loyalty and reliability of Government services particularly of employee in defence services, transport and communications, services charged with the maintenance of law and order and those connected with vital installations and industries.

 

Special Functions:

The following matters are dealt exclusively by the State Special Branch:

(a) The existence of any strong and widespread popular feeling on political matters of importance.

(b) The circulation of rumours, newsletters, posters, leaflets or other publications likely to disturb the public peace or to create disaffection, communal strife or disturbances.

(c) Attempts to tamper with the discipline, performance or loyalty of the Armed Forces or of the Police.

(d) Attempts to suborn the loyalty of other services of Government.

(e) Espionage and Fifth Column activities.

 

 

 

2. An over view of organizational structure, including key positions and their roles.

1.         Head of the Branch:  The Deputy Inspector General of Police, Special Branch is the head of the Special Branch subject to the general control and supervision of the Director General of Police. He is the functional and administrative head of the Special Branch organisation. He is the head of office of the State Special Branch. He is responsible to the Director General of Police and to the State Government for efficient performance of duty by the State Special Branch and by the District Special Branch. He exercises full administrative control over the entire organisation of the Special Branch at the State and issue instructions to the Superintendent of Police of the district in regard to the functioning of District Special Branch. He is responsible for organising training both for the State Special Branch Staff as well as the District Special Branch staff. He has been authorised to send informations and reports on matters which come within the purview of the Special Branch direct to the Government.

Reports to Government: Under the general guidance and control of the Director General of Police, the Deputy Inspector General of Police, Special Branch, is to send reports and information direct to Government, of course, with intimation to the Director General of Police. Such direct correspondence with Government is restricted to matters which come within the purview of the Special Branch. In all services matters and in those involving police discipline references should definitely be made to the Director General of Police.

 

2. Superintendent of Police, Special Branch at the Headquarters:

(i)        The Superintendent of Police, Special Branch at the Headquarters is wholly responsible to the Deputy Inspector General of Police, Special Branch for the efficient working of the State Special Branch in all its aspects. He must keep the latter posted with all information received at the State Special Branch Headquarters forwarding the files and reports for orders except in trivial matters or where the Deputy Inspector General of Police expressly directed as such.

            (ii)       He shall deal with the diaries and reports received from the District Superintendents of Police of the districts and other field officers of the District Special Branch.

(iii)     He shall prepare the daily and the periodical reports and also publications issued from the State Special Branch.

(iv)      He shall keep in close touch with the Criminal Investigation Department and the Superintendent of Police of the districts in connection with the enquiries relating to offences of a politico-criminal nature and shall render such assistance as may be necessary.

(v)       He shall guide, supervise and control officers subordinate to him at the Headquarters, whether working in the field or at the Headquarters.

(vi)      He shall maintain liaison with other intelligence organisations including military intelligence.

(vii)    He shall carry out all the administrative works assigned to him by the Deputy Inspector General of Police, Special Branch.

 

3. Officers under Superintendent of Police, Special Branch at  Headquarters:

(i)        Working under the Superintendent of Police, Special Branch Headquarters, there are a number of Additional Superintendents of Police, Deputy Superintendents of Police, Inspectors and other Subordinate Officers, Under Officers and Constables according to requirement.

(ii)       The entire work at the Headquarters is divided into a number of Sections, each Section being headed by a Deputy Superintendent of Police, an Inspector or a Sub-Inspector; one or more such Sections being placed under a Superintendent of Police, Additional Superintendent of Police or Deputy Superintendent of Police.

 

 

4. Field staff of State Special Branch:

a)         The State Special Branch have its own field staff for collecting information. The state is divided into 17 sectors and  are headed by their respective Sector Officers.

b) Duties of Field Staff:      The functions and duties of the Field Staff of the State Special Branch are to collect information and gather intelligence on all matters of interest to the State Special Branch. They also carry out such other tasks as falling within the purview of the Special Branch.

 

5. Confidential Branch:

            The Confidential Branch is synonymous with the information centre of the unit. All information either general or intelligence collected are processed, analysed and maintained in this Branch.

 

6. Foreigners Registration Office (fro)/Verification Branch:

            FRRO deals with registering of Foreigners entering into Mizoram and subsequent movement inside Mizoram and their time of departure of Mizoram. It also deals with verification of character and Antecedents (C/A) of Passport applicants from Mizoram and also newly appointed persons to Govt. jobs. The FRRO/VB is having a direct link with Foreigners Division, Ministry of Home Affairs, Delhi for on-line verification to Pakistan nationals and post verification of overseas citizens of India (OCI).

 

7. Technical Branch:

            Technical Branch came into existence as a separate entity from 16th April, 2010 having its own technical works which yielded immeasurable results in giving timely information and detection of crime and criminal to Police Department. Apart from their technical works, this branch also rendered technical assistance to other sister units.

8. Subsidiary Multi Agency Centre (SMAC)

            The Subsidiary Multi Agency Centre is established in the Special Branch as one of the Centres amongst various Intelligence Agencies under Ministry of Home Affairs for effective means of sharing intelligence report between the state Agencies.

 

9. MT Branch:

            MT Branch under Motor Transport Officer is looking after power generator and vehicles of the unit.

 

10. Reserve Branch:

            Reserve Branch mainly looks after administration, service matters of staff, deployment and discipline of the unit. It is also functioning as Armourer of the unit maintaining the Arms and ammunitions.

 

11. Establishment and Account Branch:

            This Branch is looking after all financial Accounts and transaction of unit, stationery, pension files and service book of Ministerial staffs.

12. Personal Branch:

            The main function of Personal Branch is publication and dispatch of Daily Summary of Information (DSI), Special Report (SR) and Confidential letters purported to the knowledge of SP. It also looks after the library of the unit and other important documents.
