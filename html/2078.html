BRIEF NOTE ON MIZORAM INSURGENCY PROBLEMS

Introduction:

In term of Insurgency related incidents, Mizoram can undoubtedly  be regarded as one of the most peaceful state in india. The 20-year long insurgency in Mizoram (1966-86) led by the Mizo National Front (MNF) was resolved  way back in 1986.  However, after the end of 20-year  insurgency of Mizoram  some  armed  groups of  Hmars  i.e Hmar People’s Convention (HPC) and  armed group of Brus (reang) i.e Bru National Liberation Front(BNLF), Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram(BLFM) took armed  struggle movement in Mizoram undermining the peaceful atmosphere of the state of Mizoram.

Bru Militancy in Mizoram

Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF) :

The BNLF was formed in the month of April 1997 with an objective of creating an Autonomous District Council within the Bru inhabited areas of Mamit District  in the western part of Mizoram. The first heinous crime committed by the BNLF was killing of Lalzawmliana, a Forest Game Watcher at Persang village in Dampa Forest Sanctuary in Mamit District. The BNLF thereafter resorted to violent activities such as ambush of Security forces, extortions, kidnappings and arsons. However, to end mistrust amongst different communities and militant activities of BNLF, Government of Mizoram initiated Peace Talk with leaders of BNLF w.e.f. 7.9.2001 and after 12 rounds of talks, Memorandum of Understanding was signed on 26.4.2005. The BNLF surrendered 9 different types of arms and 661 nos. of ammunitions.

Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram (BLFM) :

The BLFM was formed in December 2001 due to differences in ideology amongst BNLF members under the auspices of NLFT (BM) while negotiation was going on between Government of Mizoram and BNLF. The main objective was repatriation of Bru refugees staying at various Refugee Camps in Tripura. In the middle part of 2002, another outfit called Bru National Front (Independence Movement) was formed by dissidents of BNLF/BLFM. After functioning for over a year, the BNLF merged with BLFM in October, 2003. The first crime committed by the BLFM was kidnapping of a Mizo youth F. Lalfamkima, Sumo driver, near West Phaileng, Mamit District. However, after committing several subversive activities, the BLFM numbering 804 members surrendered to the Government of Mizoram on 26.10.2006 with 66 arms of different types of and 45 rounds of ammunitions

Bru Democratic Front of Mizoram (BDFM)/ BNDLFM

            Though exact time of its formation is not known, BDFM are the remnants of the then BLFM who refused to surrender at the time of the latter's mass surrender on 26.10.2006. They formerly call themselves as Bru Revolutionary Army(BRA), Bru Liberation Army(BLA) etc. They seem to have no concrete aims and objectives and are more likely to be an armed gang rather than a well organised militant group. Bru Democratic Front of Mizoram (BDFM) was formed under the patronage of Tripura based insurgent group i.e National Liberation Front of Tripura(NLFT)

Hmar Insurgency in Mizoram

The Hmar People Convention (HPC) was formed on 3.7.1986 with an objective of creating Hmar Autonomous Council in the northeast areas of Mizoram and some portion of Cachar District of Assam. The HPC resorted to violent activities like extortion, kidnapping, blasting of bridges and power line and ambush of security forces. The HPC had killed 8 Police personnel between 1989 to 1994. After 13 rounds of Peace Talks 331 cadres of HPC surrendered to Government of Mizoram and Memorandum of Understanding was signed on 27.7.1994. The HPC surrendered 40 nos. of different types of weapons and 190 rounds of ammunitions.

The HPC-D was formed in 1996 by remnants of the HPC who were disgruntled with the MoU signed between HPC and Govt. of Mizoram in 1994. The HPC-D resorted to violence from the outset of its formation like extortion, kidnapping and ambush of security forces. They had killed 13 Mizoram Police personnel till 28.3.2015. The HPC-D had split into two factions on 29.9.2011. One faction is led by the outset President Lalhmingthanga Sanate and the other faction led by H. Zosangbera. HPC-D H. Zosangbera faction is more active and carry out  their criminal  activities like killing of security personnel ,abduction, collecting donations, extortion, intimidation within Mizoram (along Mizoram – Manipur border). They operated from the state of Manipur.
