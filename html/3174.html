A.Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt.11.2.2013:

Police plans to take steps in reducing motor accidents by 75%: Acknowledging the huge responsibilities of the drivers as lives of passengers greatly depend on them and as many of the vehicles used to carry loads worth million rupees, Mizoram Police will not go soft with those drivers who are neglectful and are not complying with the traffic rules, Pu C.Zorammuana, DIG(NR) said. It is also planned to take steps in reducing the number of motor accidents by 75% in comparison with the previous year, the DIG added.

   The DIG has spoken about the plans of the police on how to take action to disobedient drivers, while addressing the closing function of the 24th Road Safety Week at Chanmari YMA Hall on Friday. The DIG has also mentioned that most of the motor accidents in Mizoram in the last two years occurred between 12 noon and 6:00pm. Given that most of the two wheeler drivers used to indulge in drunken driving in the evening, for which most of the accidents had indeed occurred, a surprise checking may need to be carried out. And, Zero Tolerance may also be carried out not only throughout a week, but may be even for a month, the DIG added.  

  

 

B.Front page of THE AIZAWL POST newspaper Dt.11.2.2013:

3 years Jail for 3 corrupted SI (M):  Three (3) Sub-Inspectors (Ministerial) of Mizoram Police accused of misappropriation of Rs. 91,19,751/- of public money were sentenced to 3 years imprisonment  and a fine of rupees 1 lakh each by the court. If they cannot pay the money, the duration of their imprisonment will be extended to 6 months in simple imprisonment.

   From May 1995 to July 1998, the three SIs (M), namely – Accountant Lalsanglawra s/o Lalruma(L) of College Veng, Cashier Laldinliana s/o Tlangchawma of Venghlui and Bill Accountant Annie Zosangliani d/o C.Sawiluaia of Chanmari were colleagues in the office of Superintendent of Police, Aizawl district, and while on duty in this office, the three Sub-Inspectors used to draw public money for their own share while drawing salaries for 300 to 340 constables every month.

   Accountant General, Shillong found out that there was misappropriation of money at Aizawl SP Office by the end of 1998 and informed Aizawl SP that money was drawn out more than necessary, then Aizawl SP lodged an FIR at Aizawl Police Station, enquiry team was formed to investigate the case. Enquiry team checked Treasury transit; Payment register and Bill register and found the amount drawn out was more than necessary for the salary of the constables. The three accused were immediately suspended.

On 8th Feb. 2013, T.Saikunga, Special Judge (Prevention of Corruption Act) proved the guilty of the three Sub-Inspectors (M), they were not to be forwarded to Central Jail at the moment and were given time to appeal within 3 months u/s 389(3) Cr.PC.
