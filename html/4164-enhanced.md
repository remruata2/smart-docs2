****CHRONOLOGICAL REPORT ON THE CRPF-HVV ENCOUNTER****

**Background**

Ongoing ethnic violence between the Meitei and Kuki communities in Manipur has so far resulted in at least 237 deaths and the displacement of over 65,000 people. The conflict and violence which began in May 2023, for over 17 months, rooted in long-standing issues related to land rights, political representation, and ethnic identity, with both communities accusing each other of targeting civilians.

**November 11, 2024: CRPF-HVV Confrontation**

A clash in Jiribam between CRPF and Hmar Village Volunteers (HVV) resulted in 10 HVV deaths, including their commander, Pu Lalsiemlien Hmar. Kuki-Zo Council called for a full-day shutdown on November 12, 2024, to mourn and demand justice.

**November 12, 2024: Delayed Post-Mortem**

Six bodies underwent post-mortem at Silchar Medical College; four were delayed due to the delay of Jeribam Police team under the instruction of Manipur police authorities.In the meantime, CYMA and Branch YMA organizations planned to honor the deceased along their transportation route.

**November 13, 2024: Continued Delays**

Four post-mortems were still pending despite readiness by the doctor and legal formalities being completed. Manipur authorities denied road transport through Mizoram, requesting airlifting instead.

**November 14, 2024: Negotiations and Progress**

Post-mortems for four additional bodies were completed by 5:00 PM.

Hmar leaders requested immediate handover; however, authorities insisted on DNA verification. ITLF opposed airlifting, demanding road transport through Mizoram.

**November 15, 2024: DNA Tests and Resistance**

DNA testing for six remaining bodies was pending. Barak Valley Hmar Coordination Committee urged action; the Cachar DC supported dead body removal to maintain peace.

**November 16, 2024:**

After a meeting involving Assam Police authorities and Hmar leaders, it was decided to airlift the bodies due to rising tensions. At 2:08 PM, the deceased were flown from Masimpur Helipad to Churachandpur for proper burial ceremonies. The situation remains calm, but inter-community tensions persist.
