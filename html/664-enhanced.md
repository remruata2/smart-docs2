****CENTRAL YOUNG LAI ASSOCIATION (CYLA) ON CHAKMA CENSUS****

4th Central Young Lai Association (CYLA) 'Rorel Inkhawmpui' was held on **27.3.2019**at YLA Hall, Chawnhu which was presided by Pu J. Sangthangpuia, President and about 110 (one hundred ten) members attended the function. The meeting, inter alia, resolved the following :-

i) To take steps for moral reformation.

ii) To request all the Churches to hold a prayer for the land and tribe of Lai on every first Sunday of September.

iii) To take initiative to change the name of Chakma inhabited villages in Lawngtlai District.

A team of Central Young Lai Association (CYLA) led by Pu J. Sangthangpuia, President, numbering 12 (twelve) persons left Lawngtlai on **30.5.2019** to visit the following Chakma villages in western belt of Lawngtlai District. Their purpose was to conduct census and detect foreigners having doubtful residential documents :-

i) Pandawnglui ii) Sakeilui One iii) Sakeilui Two iv) Karlui One

v) Jognasury vi)Sumsilui  vii) Sedailui  viii) Tuikhurlui

ix) Karlui Two x) Sedailui

**On **13.6.2019**, Central Young Lai Association (CYLA) issued an appeal to all its branches to select volunteers (5-10 members from each branch) to carry out the final phase of drive on identification of illegal Chakma foreigners and to report themselves to CYLA before the end of 13.6.2019. The appeal reiterated the importance of their action for safeguarding the land and nation and requested all leaders and members of its branches to participate selflessly.**

 Earlier, on **30.5.2019**, a team of CYLA led by Pu J. Sangthangpuia, President, numbering 12 (twelve) persons left Lawngtlai and carried out the first phase of drive against illegal Chakma foreigners in western belt of Lai Autonomous District Council (LADC).

 In this connection, it was also reported that 8 (eight) members representing Village Councils and Young Chakma Association (YCA) had submitted a joint application to the Deputy Commissioner, Lawngtlai District on **3.6.2019** with the following request :-

1. Order investigation to find out the facts about the ongoing ‘Local area population census-2019’ being conducted by CYLA and fixed accountability for such illegal activities which vitiate communal harmony.
2. Seize and destroy all the data gathered or collected through this illegal population census by NGO.
3. Ensure such illegal census/head counting are not conducted anywhere in future and take strict actions against those who conduct such activities.

 A team of Central Young Lai Association led by Pu J. Sangthangpuia, President numbering 30 (thirty) members left Lawngtlai on **14.6.2019** to carry out the final phase of drive on identification of illegal Chakma foreigners within Lai Autonomous District Council (LADC), Lawngtlai District. Information received indicated that the said final phase of the drive was conducted in the following villages :-

1. Jognasuri ii) Pandawnglui iii) Karlui-I

iv)  Karlui-II v) Bolisora

 Necessary security arrangement was made by Lawngtlai Police by deploying Police personnel in the above mentioned villages respectively.

A team of Central Young Lai Association (CYLA) numbering 30 (thirty) members led by Pu J. Sangthangpuia, President returned to Lawngtlai on **17.6.2019** after carrying out drive on identification of illegal Chakma foreigners within Lai Autonomous District Council (LADC), Lawngtlai District. CYLA teams conducted the drive in the villages like Fangfarlui, Damlui, Pandawnglui, Karlui-I, Karlui-II, Jognasury, Nghalimlui and Vaseikai during **14 to16.6.2019**. CYLA expressed their gratitude to all other NGOs and individuals who extended their support.

CYLA issued a Press Release on **18.6.2019** wherein they highlighted a Local Area Population Census drive they had conducted in the western area of Lai Autonomous District Council (LADC) recently and expressed their gratitude to other NGOs and individuals who extended their support. The PR further highlighted their gratitude to all the Village Councils where they had conducted the drive for giving their cooperation. CYLA also expressed their commitment towards safeguarding the land and nation.
