MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER SITUATION REPORT

No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/631

Dated 10th November, 2020

 

          In continuation of our report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/628
Dt. 9.11.2020, Situation Report on Mizoram-Assam Border is as under:

 

  1. Thinghlun, Mamit District Report :

Public Meeting was held today’s morning i.e., 10.11.2020 @ 7:30 a.m. at Community Hall, Thinghlun. Pu Hmingthanpuia, Vice President, VC Thinghlun presided the meeting and around 100(one hundred) members attended. The meeting decided the following regarding deployment of BSF at Thinghlun:

  1. In view of Covid-19 pandemic and as a precautionary measure BSF temporarily stationed at Govt. Comprehensive Primary and Middle School, Thinghlun should be isolated for at least 7(seven) days and during this, their requirement will be procured through VC Thinghlun.
  2. All NGOs meeting will decide their place of deployment.

 

  1. Kanhmun, Mamit District Report :

Kanhmun VLTF held a meeting yesterday i.e., 9.11.2020 @ 6:30 p.m. and Pu PC. Lalremmawia, VCP, Kanhmun chaired the meeting. The meeting discuss scarcity of essential commodities due to blockade of road at Tripura and Assam and decided to find a way to import goods from Tripura through Langkaih river. It also decided not to continue imposition of blockade of vehicles bound to Mizoram from Assam.

          There is no movement of vehicles across Medili Bridge, Assam-Mizoram border today i.e., 9.11.2020.  Situation is normal.

 

  1. Saihapui ‘V’, Kolasib District :

          Pu H. Lalthianghlima of Phainuam submitted FIR to Vairengte Police Station today i.e., 10.11.2020 wherein he stated that on 9.11.2020 under the initiative DC, Cachar, Police authorities and civilians dug his paddy field for proposed SSB Camp without his consent and destroyed 30(thirty) Oil palm, 10(ten) Areca nut, 4(four) Sandalwood and 3(three) Orange trees and alleged Assam Police for committing theft of 9(nine) chicken from his farm during
20-28th October, 2020, and requested to take necessary legal action. However, site preparation of SSB proposed Camp within his land is still continuing today. Residents of Phainuam organized a procession today i.e., 10.11.2020

Lalrinmawia Hmar, Phainuam Pastor, Mizoram Synod stated their disappointment regarding economic roadblock organized by Bangladeshi refugees and if the Central Government does not clear the said roadblock, they will considered it as disregard of the mizo people by the Central Government and they will attain independence. He further stated that they will continue to protect their land from Bangladeshi immigrants. They shouted slogan condemning road block organized by Bangladeshi immigrants, their commitment to stand their land etc. at the border and during this, around 40 (forty) civilians from Assam assembled at Assam side of the border. They dispersed at 12:30 p.m. under peaceful atmosphere.

Further, site preparation for BSF Camp at Saihapui ‘V’ was started today at 11:00 p.m. under the supervision of Pu Isaac C. Lalrempuia, SDO(Civil), Vairengte inside the farm land of Pu Lalmalsawma of Saihapui ‘V’, which is beyond the bridge towards Assam side around 30 meters from BSF place of deployment (earlier Mizoram Police was stationed). However, SSB personnel deployed by Assam objected and claimed that the area fell within Assam territory and the work could not be continued. In this regard, SDO(Civil) Vairengte submitted a report to DC, Kolasib.

 

  1. Bairabi, Kolasib District Report :

Bairabi Joint VC and Joint NGOs Committee was held yesterday i.e., 9.11.2020 @ 7:30 p.m. at MZP Pisa, Bairabi. Pu B. Lalhriatpuia, President, MSU Sub-Headquarters Bairabi chaired the meeting and 9(nine) members from Bairabi Joint VC, Branch YMA attended the meeting. The meeting after deliberation decided that the on-going enforcement of prohibition of Non-Mizos from venturing out from their home due to blockade of road organized in between Ramnathpur, Assam and Bairabi, Mizoram will be continued to enforce strictly until withdrawal of the said road block and if anyone violate their decision a fine of Rs.500/- will be imposed.

-3-

There is no movement of vehicles across Bairabi border today i.e., 10.11.2020 and blockade of Mizoram bound freight train organized at Muhamadpur Rail Station and Jamira Rail Station is still continuing today. Situation is normal.

 

  1. Phaisen, Kolasib District Report :

          No new development has been reported during the last 24(twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

 

  1. Vairengte, Kolasib District Report :

          A community work for construction of BSF Camp organized by Joint VC Vairengte and Joint YMA is continued today i.e., 10.11.2020 @ 10:00 a.m. and around 200(two hundred) volunteers participated. They have completed construction of 2(two) 20 men barrack and concluded for today at 5:00 p.m.

          2(two) Trucks carrying balu and 1(one) Maxi cab (sumo) entered Vairengte from Assam today i.e., 10.11.2020. Blockade of vehicles carrying essential commodities at Lailapur is still continuing and around 120 (one hundred twenty) vehicles carrying essential commodities are stranded at Lailapur.

          Situation is under close watch.
