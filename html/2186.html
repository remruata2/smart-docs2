Irregular Recruitment of Staff under CADC

            Information has been received that Chakma Joint NGOs like Young Chakma Association (YCA), Chakma Mahila Samiti (CMS) and Mizoram Chakma Students Union (MCSU) in a joint meeting held at Kamalanagar on 10.7.2015 (Friday) accused Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) of indulging in irregular recruitment of 101 (one hundred and one) staff under CADC. The Joint NGOs demanded the CADC to rectify such irregularity within 15 (fifteen) days.

 

            Chakma Joint NGO, a conglomeration of Central Young Chakma Association (CYCA), Chakma Mahila Samiti (CMS) and Mizoram Chakma Students Union (MCSU) organised a procession within Chawngte Town on 30.7.2015 in protest against inaction of the authority of Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) towards their demands, i.e. cancellation of appointment order of 101 (one hundred and one) staff recruited without floating advertisement under CADC during 2015. About 250 (two hundred and fifty) volunteers took part in this procession.

 

            The authority of Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) convened a meeting on 31.7.2015 @ 11:00 AM at CADC Office, Chawngte. The meeting was attended by Executive Members and Ruling Members of District Council (MDC). The Meeting refused to fulfill the demands of Chakma Joint NGO's for cancellation of the appointment order of 101 (one hundred and one) staff recruited by them reportedly without floating advertisement.

 

            As there was no positive response from the CADC authorities, the leaders of Joint Chakma NGOs decided to intensify their agitation by launching ‘Total Bandh’ today, i.e. 4.8.2015.

 

            On 4.8.2015 @ 6:30 AM, a mob of around 150-200 people attacked Chawngte/ Kamalanagar Police Station, Lawngtlai District after the following 9 (nine) leaders of Joint Chakma NGOs have been arrested in the morning of 4.8.2018 (4:30 AM to 5:30 AM) at Chawngte Police Station on the ground of preventive measures for the ‘Total Bandh’ called by Joint Chakma NGOs (Central Young Chakma Association, Mizoram Chakma Students Union & Chakma Mahila Samiti) w.e.f.  6:00 AM on this day.

 

            The angry mob pelted stones and damaged some of the properties of Chawngte Police Station like window panes, Solar plates, etc and to disperse the irate mob, the Police resorted to lathi charge and eventually opened fire (around 8 rounds) as ordered by Executive Magistrate, Shri. Lallawmawma. Unfortunately, one Class-XI student identified as Agosh Chakma (16 yrs) S/o Nomuni Chakma, Kalamanagar Higher Secondary School (HSS) succumbed to injury suspected to have been caused by a deflected bullet. Three Policemen also sustained minor injuries.

     The arrested 9 (nine) leaders of Joint Chakma NGOs were released at 10:15 AM and the violent mob also dispersed at around 12:00 noon after the authority of Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) and the leaders of Joint Chakma NGOs reached the following agreements :-

 

  1. CADC will give an ex-gratia payment of Rs.10 lakhs to the bereaved family of Agosh                 Chakma (L).
  2. CADC agreed to cancel/revoke all the appointments of 101 jobs made under CADC                     during January-July, 2015.
  3. To meet the medical treatments of 2 (two) civilians who sustained minor injuries.

 

     In view of the incident at Chawngte, around 30 Chakma students also assembled infront of Chakma House, Hunthar Veng, Aizawl today, i.e. 4.8.2015 and met Pu Dangu Budalila Chakma, CEM, CADC and urged him to immediately solve the ongoing crisis at Chawngte. In this regard, the CEM briefed the media in which he clarified the response of CADC over grievances and demand of the Joint Chakma NGOs. He also accused the MNF and BJP party of instigating the leaders of Joint NGOs in spearheading movement against incumbent CADC. He also expressed the need to provide adequate security to all members of CADC.

 

     Government of Mizoram has ordered Lawngtlai District Magistrate to conduct Magisterial Enquiry into the incident at Chawngte on 4.8.2015. One PSO each has also been provided to all the 17(Seventeen) MDCs of Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) to ensure their security and safety.

 

     On 21.9.2015, Pu Buddha Lila Chakma, Chief Executive Member (CEM), Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) submitted his resignation from the post of CEM, CADC to Chairman, CADC. In the backdrop of his resignation, Congress MDC (Congress Legislative Party) held emergent meeting this afternoon @ 1:00 PM at Congress Bhawan, Chawngte and the meeting unanimously elected Pu Kali Kumar Tongtongia (incumbent Chairman, CADC) as party leader.

 

     On July, 2017, Pu Kali Kumar Tongtongia, CEM CADC had reportedly agreed to cancel the appointment orders made during September, 2015 till date (without floating an advertisement) in view of the demand made by Chakma NGOs of Chawngte.

No Confidence Motion in CADC

     On 4.7.2017, the Hon'ble Governor of Mizoram placed Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) under animated suspension following a political crisis within the ruling members of MDC. Deputy Commissioner, Lawngtlai District was appointed as a Caretaker of the CADC with immediate effect. As 12 (twelve) District Council Members (MDCs) out of 20 (twenty) have proposed 'No Confidence Motion' to the present Chief Executive Member, free and fair floor test will be conducted within a week from today.

 

     On 10.7.2017 @ 10:30 a.m, a Special Session of CADC for Floor Test/Vote of Confidence against Executive Committee of CADC led by Pu Kali Kumar Tongchongya was conducted at Session Hall, CADC, Chawngte. The Special Session dissolved the Executive Committee of CADC as Pu Kali Kumar Tongchongya failed to prove majority of the House.

    

     It was reported that Pu Amit Kumar Chakma is proposed to be the leader of the Congress Legislature Party of CADC.

 

CADC under Governor’s Rule

     On 22.3.2018, the Government of Mizoram issued a Notification placing Chakma Autonomous District Council under Governor's Rule. The Deputy Commissioner, Lawngtlai will assume administration of the District Council on behalf of the Governor of Mizoram for a period not exceeding 4 months or till re-constitution of the said Council whichever is earlier. It may be recalled that on 26.2.2018, the Government appointed Dr. Arun T, IAS, Deputy Commissioner, Lawngtlai to examine and report on any matter relating to the administration of the Chakma Autonomous District Council after several complaints regarding malfunctioning of the District Council. The Commission had since then submitted a report indicating that the administration in the Chakma Autonomous District Council could not be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the Sixth Schedule to the Constitution of India under the prevailing circumstances.

18-Fultuli Constituency of CADC

     The Candidate of Mizo National Front (MNF) Party, Shri. Sumir Tongchongya who had filed his nomination at 18-Fultuli MDC Constituency for the upcoming 10th Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) MDC General Election, 2018 was disqualified by the Returning Officer, (RO), Shri N.Chakhai, Deputy Commissioner of Lawngtlai District due to the seconder he had used on his nomination paper is a Government servant.

 

     In the wake of this, on 9.4.2018, Legal Board of the Mizo National Front (MNF) Party had submitted a complaint addressed to the Commissioner, State Election Commissioner, Mizoram, Aizawl regarding the disqualification of the official candidate of MNF party. In their complaint, they alleged that the Returning Officer of the said Election illegally disqualified Shri. Sumir Tongchongya in contrary to the relevant provisions of the Chakma Autonomous District Council (Constitution, Conduct of Business) Rules, 2002 and the Chakma Autonomous District Council (Constitution, Conduct of Business) Rules, 2012 and that the impugned order made by the Returning Officer has no legal stand. They further demanded that the State Election Commission may take appropriate action against the Returning Officer and that the Certificate of Scrutiny and Certificate of Election issued by him upon 18-Fultuli MDC Constituency should be suspended within a day, failing which, the MNF party will approach a Court of Law.

 

     Mizo National Front (MNF) Party had submitted a complaint to the Gauhati High Court, Aizawl Bench about this matter, and the Gauhati High Court issued Stay order on the Election at 18-Fultuli Constituency on 11.4.2018. The date of election at this constituency will be announced after the Judgement of Gauhati High Court is declared.           

 

Misappropriation of Fund at CADC

     It has been reported that, there has been misappropriation of public fund by the then CEM and other Executive Members of the recently suspended CADC, Mizo National Front Party sent a letter to the Hon’ble Governor of Mizoram reiterated their demand for proper and thorough investigation into the alleged large scale misappropriation of public fund amounting to Rs. 1,020.60 lakhs by the then CEM and other Executive Members of the recently suspended CADC. They urged the Hon’ble Governor of Mizoram to engage independent agency, i.e, the Office of the Accountant General to take up investigation so that fair, speedy and thorough investigation could be carried out.
