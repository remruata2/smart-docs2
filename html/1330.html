BRIEF NOTE ON THE ISSUE OF ANTI-BRU REPATRIATION PROCESS

 

The Bru people who fled Mizoram due to communal tension in 1997-1998 were given shelter in 6 refugee camps at the following places in North Tripura and were given free rations and others essential commodities at the rate fixed by the central government.

Name of Refugee Camps:

(i) Naisingpara                                  (ii)Asapara

(iii)Khakchangpara                          (iv)Hazacherra

(v)Kaiskau                                        (vi)Hamsapara

 

 

  1. Activities of Anti-Repatriation Process   before 13.11.2009 (Bungthuam Incident)

 

 

Date

 

Remark

 

8.1.2006

 

        MBDPF leaders viz. Laldawngliana, A. Sawibunga and Bruno Msha  conducted Awareness campaign on anti-repatriation process in the refugee camps  like Naisingpara, Asapara, Hazachera on 8.1.2006 and asked  the general public to object repatriation process unless the Government of Mizoram  agreed to the following demands :-

 

(i) 1 year Free Ration

(ii) Establishment of cluster villages to accommodate at least 500 Bru repatriated families.

(Enclosed in Annexure - I)

 

 

27.1.2006

              

             Procession was organized on 27.1.2006 from Naisingpara Refugee Camp to Dosda R.D Block Office. The MBDPF leaders who delivered speeches viz. Elvis Chorky, Chairman MBDPF, Laldawngliana, General Secretary and A. Sawibunga, Treasurer emphasized that MoU signed between Government of Mizoram and BNLF(Bru National Liberation Front) was not enough to safeguard the Bru people. A memorandum containing 16 charters of demand was submitted to Hon’ble Prime Minister of India through BDO Dosda, N. Tripura giving a copy to Union Home Minister, Chief Minister Mizoram, Joint Secretary NE Affairs Unit, India.

(Enclosed in Annexure - II)

 

 

   30.1.2007

 

          On 30.1.2007, the MBDPF leaders submitted a memorandum containing 26 points of demands to Shri Naveen Verma, Joint Secretary, Ministry of Home Affairs(MHA). In the memorandum submitted to Shri Naveen Verma, the MBDPF not only added 10 points of demand in addition to 16 points charter of demand already submitted to Prime Minister but also categorically demanded that 1 lakh compensation to each of the Bru displaced family and  Rs. 30,000/- each as housing assistance .

(Enclosed in Annexure - III)

 

22.2-6.3.2007

 

 

         MBDPF staged one week sit-in-demonstration in front of Raj Bhawan, Agartala with effect from 22.2.2007 which was followed by indefinite hunger strike with effect from 2.3.2007 and the hunger strike was called off on 6.3.2007  after the visit of Shri Naveen Verma, Joint Secretary, MHA who conceded most of the demands amongst the 16 points of their charter of demand.

(Enclosed in Annexure - IV)

 

 

13.4.2007

 

       On 13th April 2007 a meeting with the leaders of MBDPF was held under the chairmanship of Shri C. Ropianga, Home Commissioner, Government of Mizoram. The MBDPF leaders submitted fresh demand to the Chief Minister, Government of Mizoram containing 17 points which are divided into two (2)  1-8 concerning Govt. of Mizoram, 9-17 Govt. of India. The points of demand were somewhat similar with their demands already submitted to Govt. of India. However there are some fresh demands like :- 

 

                              (i) Formation of one permanent Autonomous Body exclusively for Bru community of Mizoram.

                              (ii) Immediate functioning of Special Development Project as envisaged in the MoU appointing chairman and secretary from the Brus community.

(iii) Repatriation of all displaced Brus living in 6 refugees camp without identification.

(Enclosed in Annexure - V)

 

 

 

 7.2.2008

 

            On 7th February, 2008 a meeting with the MBDPF leaders was held under the chairmanship of C. Ropianga Home Commissioner . In the meeting leaders of the MBDPF categorically stated that leaders of Ex-BNLF could not represent the cases of Bru refugees at any level as they did not accept their agreed terms. They further made it clear that they did not like to have piece meal repatriation until and unless all their demands are fulfilled or MoU signed between the Govt. of Mizoram and MBDPF leaders for implementation of their demands. Since the process of identification of bonafide resident of Mizoram Brus in the Bru refugee camps was objected by the MBDPF leader, the first step of repatriation process was stalled resulting into the delay of repatriation process.

(Enclosed in Annexure - VI)

 

 

  4.11.2009

 

       On 4.11.2009 meeting was held in the office chamber of Pu Lalmalsawma, Home commissioner, Government of Mizoram with MBDPF leaders. The meeting discussed about the repatriation and rehabilitation of Bru migrants from Tripura. Road map (Road Map-I) prepared by the Government of Mizoram was discussed at length in which the MBDPF raised objection on some points which were also discussed points by points.

(Enclosed in Annexure - VII)

 

 

5-6.11.2009

 

  The MBDPF leaders objecting the road map (Road Map-I)  prepared by the Government  of Mizoram during their last meeting started awareness campaign  on 5th and 6th November, 2009, they visited six refugee camps by holding a public meeting and tried to convince the people not to accept the road map for repatriation.

(Enclosed in Annexure - VIII)

 

 

13.11.2009

      

       Zarzokima(17) s/o Lalngilneia of Bungthuam village, Mamit District  was shot dead about 1 Km north of Bungthuam village by Bru militants  on 13.11.2009. The culprit left note in which they claimed themselves as Bru Revolutionary Union (BRU) and that they did not want their repatriation without proper agreement and would fight the State Government  for their right.  In this connection, Kanhmun PS C/No. 23/09 dt. 13.11.09 u/s 302 IPC r/w 25(1B)(a)/27(1) Arms Act had been registered and the Special Investigation Team(SIT) successfully unearthed the reason behind the killing due to which the alleged mastermind of this sensational case. R. Laldawngliana, Vice President, MBDPF was arrested on 23.1.2010. The arrested person did not deny that the incident was made to stall repatriation process to kickstart from 16.11.2009.

 

       Outraged by this brutal incident, 387 huts/houses in 9 (nine) villages within Mamit District and 12 huts/houses of Bukvannei village within Kolasib District belonging to the Bru community and other communities were torched by unknown miscreants bringing the whole process of repatriation and rehabilitation to an abrupt halt.

(Enclosed in Annexure - IX)

         

 

 

 

 

  1. Activities of Anti-Repatriation Process aftermath of Bungthuam Incident

 

 

24.1.2010

 

      On 24.1.2010, a procession organized by MBDPF was carried out from Naisingpara to Asapara Relief Camp in protest against the arrest of Mr. R. Laldawngliana, Vice President, MBDPF on 23.1.2009 by Mizoram Police, who was suspected to be the kingpin in the murder of Zarzokima (17) S/o. Lalngilneia of Bungthuam, Mamit District. The processionists passed resolutions in which they would refrain from the ongoing talks on their repatriation with the Government of Mizoram until Mr. R. Laldawngliana was released and should further talks be held, its should be outside Mizoram and should be a tri­partite one, with representatives from the Central Government.

(Enclosed in Annexure - X)

 

27.4.2010

           On 27.4.2010, it was learnt that Mr. Elvis Chorkhy, President, MBDPF who was in favour of the repatriation was targeted by Bru militants led by Moikau believed to be at the instances of Mr. Bruno Msha, Asstt. General Secretary and Mr. R. Laldawngliana, Vice President, MBDPF to derail the repatriation process. In the meantime, Tripura Police also received inputs that there was presence of potential threat against Mr. Elvis Chorkhy and asked him whether he would be requiring security or not.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XI)

 

 

30.4.2010

 

        

          On 30.4.2010, a meeting of MBDPF leaders was held at Naisingpara Relief Camp in the absence of Mr. Elvis Chorkhy, President, MBDPF. The meeting was attended by the following leaders who all opposed the repatriation.

          Mr. R.Laldawngliana, Vice President.

          Mr. Sawibunga, Gen. Secy.

          Mr. Bruno Msha, Asst. Gen. Secy.

          Mr. Vansanga,Jt. Secy.

 

          The meeting decided to lobby with an influential Hindu NGO, the Shiv Mondali to oppose the repatriation process.  It was also learnt that the displaced Brus were willing to be repatriated as per the Government of Mizoram’s proposal but discouraged by some sections of MBDPF leaders and some refugees were even warned of dire consequences if they obey the Government's proposal.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XII)

 

 

24.8. 2010

      Leaders of MBDPF in its meeting held on 24th August, 2010 at Naisingpara relief camp had decided to desist from participating in  the repatriation process. This decision was taken in the absence of Mr. Elvis Chorkhy, President, MBDPF. The meeting was conducted hastily as the members feared that Mr. Elvis Chorkhy might over rule such decision to desist from participating in the repatriation process. Further, members of the MBDPF have even spread rumors about possible disruption of the repatriation process by militant groups in order to instill fear in the minds of the displaced Brus.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XIII)

 

16.6.2014

 

   Leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples Forum (MBDPF) called a public meeting at Naisingpara on 16.6.2014 and had dissuaded the public to be repatriated unless the Government has conceded to their demands submitted earlier such as two years free ration, raising repatriation package to Rs.1.5 lakhs per family etc.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XIV)

 

26.8.2014

       Central team headed by Mr.Rajiv Gauba, Additional Secretary, Ministry of Home Affairs visited Bru Refugee Camp in Naisingpara, Tripura. A Joint memorandum was submitted by leaders of MBDPF demanding for supply of better facilities such as ration money, power supply, increase of rehabilitation package from Rs.85,000/- to Rs.1,50,000 for repatriated refugees into Mizoram.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XV)

 

14.2.2015

 

           Shri Rajnath Singh, Hon’ble Union Home Minister visited Kanchanpur,Tripura where he interacted with the representatives of Bru refugees living in relief camps in Tripira.  A 10(ten) point charter of demand was handed over to him by Pu Bruno Mesha, leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples Forum (MBDPF). The Charter of Demand included -

  1.   Allotment of sufficient land to each repatriated family for their survival & livelihood.
  2.   Formation of Grouping/Model villages involving not less than 500 families with all civic amenities like electricity, drinking water facility, school, health, Anganwadi, road connectivity, etc.
  3.   Ensuring proper security to the Bru people by deployment of Central Para Military Force(CPMF) or Special Police Officers(SPOs) on the pattern of Tripura by recruiting from the Bru community.
  4.   Indira Aawas Yojna(IAY) should be provided to all repatriated families.
  5.   Cash grant at least at par with the Kashmiri Pandits and Tamil refugees.
  6.   Electoral Roll of 1995 should not be the only baseline for identification of Bru families, all kinds of Government documents issued by Government authorities of Tripura during their stay in relief camps should be taken into consideration.
  7.    Provision of seeds to start cultivation.
  8.    Free food rations at least for 2(two) years from date of repatriation.
  9.    Primitive Tribal Group(PTG) which is under Ministry of Tribal Affairs should be implemented exclusively for the Bru people in Mizoram for their speedy development.
  10.    Formation of Permanent Development Project for the Brus in Mizoram as was committed by Mr. RCA Jain, IAS, the then Special Secretary of MHA at Raj Bhavan, Agartala, Tripura in 2001 and Mr. Naveen Verma, IAS, the then Joint Secretary(NE) MHA at State Guest House, Aizawl in 2009.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XVI)

 

20.6.2015

        On 20.6.2015 the citizen of 6(six) Relief Camps of Naisingpara, Asapara, Hazachera, Kaiskaupara, Khakchangpara and Hamsapara including Naisaupara/Nobajoy (where surrenderees of erstwhile BLFM are settled) had adopted a resolution unanimously to boycott the repatriation process.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XVII)

 

25.6.2015

 

       On 25.6.2015 the citizen of 7 (seven) Relief Camps of Naisingpara, Ashapara, Hazachera, Naisaupara, Kaskaopara, Khakchangpara and Hamsapara submitted a Public consensus in regard to identification of the families in relief camps for repatriation to Register  of the Supreme Court of India, Hon’ble Union Home Minister, Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram, Hon’ble Chief Minister of Tripura. The inmates of all  the 7 (seven) Relief Camps called boycott of the identification process under Road Map-IV and requested four party agreement involving Central Government, State Government of Mizoram, State Government of Tripura and Bru leaders for repatriation and settlement of repatriated Bru families.

(Enclosed in Annexure - XVIII)

 

 

It may not be out of place to mention here that Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples Forum (MBDPF) leaders are trying their best to stall any repatriation process citing various flimsy grounds and raising endless demands. In addition, the leaders of MBDPF have pocketed huge sum of money from the cash dole meant for the displaced Bru refugees lodged in different relief camps thereby forming a thriving business for the few.  As per the latest intelligence input, some of the leaders of MBDPF  have maintained  close nexus with Bru militants(i.e United Democratic Liberation Army(UDLA)) led by Shri Doinoram Reang operating from Hailakandi District/Cachar District, Assam with an aim to stall any repatriation process, etc should the need arises and for the reasons best known to them.

 It is also a fact that the leaders of MBDPF have taken advantage of various Bru NGOs in the Refugee Camps like Young Bru Association (YBA), Bru Displaced Women Welfare Committee (BDWWC), All Dealers Association(ADA), Bru Displaced Welfare Organisation (BDWO), All Chaudary Committee(ACC), Bru Snungnai Mthoh(BSM),Bru Chaudry Council(BCC), Camp Defence Committee(CDC) for realization of their vested interests and to derail any Repatriation process.
