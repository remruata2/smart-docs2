****RIGHT TO INFORMATION ACT,2005****

****(RTI MANUAL OF CID(SB)****

Sec.4.(1)(b) of RTI Act, 2005

(i) The organization is called Special Branch

Functions :-

 1) The main functions of the special Branch is to promptly communicate to Government and to the Director General of Police informations on all matters of political and public importance.

 2) The Special Branch also deals with matters of national security of individuals who are important to the nation.

 3) Related to above the State Special Branch has to discharge other functions as follows :-

 a) to communicate to the Special Branch of others States information which is of interest to them;

 b)  to communicate to Deputy Inspector General of Police, Range and Superintendent of Police of the District and other specified officers important informations which is of interest to them.

 c) to prepare periodical reports on matters falling within its purview and to forward copies to those specified;

 d) to make secret enquiries into important matters of political nature or those affecting national interest;

 e) to help in the investigation of those crimes which have a political purpose or motive and in which political suspects are involved.

** **Scope :-** The scope of functions of the Special Branch and the subject dealt with includes the following :-**

 i) Political movements and associations, their inception, progress and development - this includes societies having any political purpose avowed or concealed.

 ii) Political activities, open as well as secret and subversive.

 iii) Labour Unions, their formation and development and labour activities.

 iv) Volunteer organizations and their activities.

 v) Youth movements and students associations and their activities.

 vi) Religious and communal activities and religious activities having a  political complexion.

 vii) Social and economic matters having political significance.

 viii) Movements and activities of foreigners believed to be politically  motivated.

 ix) Anti-national activities or those likely to affect the security and safety of  the State or of India generally.

 x) Security schemes, including security of vital installations and key  industries.

 xi) Movements and activities of political suspects and surveillance and   watch over them.

 xii) Censorship of newspapers, periodicals, pamphlets, party literature etc.

 xiii) Loyalty and reliability of Govt. services particularly of employee in   defence services, transport and communications, services charged with  the maintenance of law and order and those connected with vital  installations and industries.

** **Special Function :****

******The following matters are dealt exclusively by the State Special Branch :-**

 a) The existence of any strong and widespread popular feeling on political  matters of importance.

 b) The circulation of rumours, newsletters, posters, leaflets or other   publications likely to disturb the public peace or to create disaffection,  communal strife or disturbances.

 c) Attempts to tamper with the discipline, performance or loyalty of the   Armed Forces or of the Police.

 d) Attempts to suborn the loyalty of other services of Government.

**(ii) **Powers and duties of officers & employees** :- The Sr. Superintendent of Police CID (SB) is the head of the Special Branch, subject to the general control and supervision of the DGP. He is responsible to the DGP and to the State Govt. for efficient performance of duty by the State Special Branch. He exercises full administrative control over the entire organization of the Special Branch at the State. He is authorised to send information and reports on matters which come within the purview of the Special Branch direct to the Govt. In all administrative matters, including discipline and service matters the SrSP, CID(SB) shall however, take up the matter with the DGP.**

 Under the administrative control of the Sr.Superintendent of Police, there are various Police functionaries starting from rank of Constable to Additional Superintendents of Police. They are governed by and exercise the powers and duties conferred by the Mizoram Police Special Branch Manual, Mizoram Police Manual, various special and minor acts which are adopted by the Government of Mizoram apart from the CCS &amp; CCA Rules and Financial Rules.

 The functions and duties of various Police functionaries under the Superintendent of Police, Special Branch are as follows :-

(a) Addl.S.P :- He is the over all in-charge of all Branches of CID(SB). All the files are routed through him by concerned Dy. SP. All the correspondences relating to Monthly, Quarterly &amp; Half yearly return are signed by him. He is authorized to sign Contingency Bills, Pay Bills and functions as DDO. He is the in-charge of Estab. &amp; Account Branch and DSI.

(b) Dy.SP (Hqrs) :- He is the in-charge of Reserve Branch, Field Branch, Confidential Branch, Technical &amp; SMAC Branch and all Sector Officers &amp; men. He also assists Addl.S.P in compilation of DSI.

(c) Dy.SP (Field) :- He supervised day-to-day works of Field staff, all Sector officers &amp; men. He is the in-charge of Foreigner and Passport Branch and MT Branch. He also assist Addl. SP in compilation of DSI.

(d) Reserve Officer and other Officers with Constabulary in Reserve Office :- Reserve Officer (R.O) is in-charge of Reserve Office which is one of the branches of the office of the Superintendent of Police CID(SB). He is concerned mainly with the discipline and management of the forces and manpower available in the Unit. He and his staff are responsible for maintenance of Service books of all staff of CID(SB) excluding G.Os and Ministerial staff, and also proper maintenance and custody of Office equipments and monitoring correspondences.

(e) Motor Transport Officer :- A Inspector of Police is placed as in-charge of M.T Branch. The sanction strength of driver for the Unit is 20 and is filled up at present. Their duties and responsibility are to look after and maintain the vehicles allotted to the Unit. The MT Branch is responsible for drawing of POL and issue to vehicles and taking of defective vehicles to workshop for repair.

(f) Head Assistant (H.A) :- A ministerial Inspector is placed to look after all ministerial staff and deals with the general establishment and management of the account of the office. There are ministerial SI and ASI under the HA who function as Accountant, Pay bill Assistant, Cashier, Dealing Assistant etc. who are under the control of the Superintendent of Police.

(g) One Inspector each are also posted as In-Charge of four other Branches of the office - viz. Field Branch, Verification Branch, Technical Branch and Confidential Branch.

**(iii) The procedure of decision making process, including channels of supervision and accountability :-**

 As the head of the office, the decision of the Sr.Superintendent of Police is final in cases concerning transfer and posting, distribution of works, granting of leave and sanctioning of rewards etc. Channel of supervision is as per the chain of command i.e. in order of rank in the official position. The accountability of each rank differs and it depends on posting and work assign to each.

**(iv) Norms for discharge of functions :-**

 The various functions of the Special Branch are governed by the Special Branch Manual, the Mizoram Police Manual, 2005, CCS and CCA Rules, Police Act and Cr.PC. Local laws, being in force in the State and other special laws as notified and amended from time to time by the Central and State Legislative Assembly.

**(v) The rules, regulations, manual and records, held by it or under its control or used by its employees for discharging its function :-**

 Mizoram Police Special Branch Manual, 2005 and MIzoram Police Manual, Mizoram Police Act and CCS &amp; CCA Rules, Financial rules, Local and other special laws as notified and amended from time to time by the Central and State legislators.
