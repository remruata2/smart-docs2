A. Front page of Vanglaini newspaper Dt. 27.9.2012.

 

Mizoram economic lifeline choked : A student body called All Barak Youth Students Association(ABYSA) started an indifinite blockade of NH-54 from 0600 hrs. dt. 26.9.2012 in protest against the existing practice of Inner Line Permit(ILP) and demanding immediate solution to Mizoram-Assam border issue. The Young Mizo Association(YMA), the largest NGO in Mizoram and the State's apex students' body Mizo Zirlai Pawl(MZP) and other NGOs appealed the ABYSA to end their indefinite blockade of NH-54.

 

 

 

B. Front page of Vanglaini newspaper Dt. 27.9.2012.

 

NGO appeal High Court on corruption: 10(ten) NGOs of Mizoram had knocked on the door of the Guwahati High Court to nail the 3(three) accused in the Multi Crore Road fund scam i.e. construction of Serkhan to Bagha road after losing hope on the Special Court of Prevention of Corruption Act in Aizawl.

 

PRISM supported by 10 like-minded organisations submitted a PIL to the Aizawl Bench of Guwahati High Court against the Special Court's recent hearings on the bail pleas of the 3 accused Public Works Department(PWD) officials.

 

According to PRISM, the ACB alone has filed 19 chargesheets in the Special Court since anti-corruption movement gained momentum from 2006. In connection with the chargesheets, 57 persons charged with corruption cases have applied for anticipatory bails and the Court has granted each of them.

 

The 10 NGOs who has put signature on this PIL are:

 

1) PRISM(People Right to Information and Development Implementing Society of Mizoram)

2)     SOSA(Society for Social Action)

3)     FOMTU(Federation of Mizoram Trade Union)

4)     HZP(Hmarphei Zirlai Pawl)

5)     CITUM(Centre of Indian Trade Union Mizoram)

6)     HR&LN(Human Rights and Law Network)

7)     GDN(Grassroot Development Network)

8)     AIMS(All Mizoram Iron-Scraps Marketing Society)

9)     MTP(Mipui Tangrual Pawl)

10)   MBCS(Mizoram Blind Care Society)

 

 

 

 

C. Front page of Vanglaini newspaper Dt. 27.9.2012.

 

More recovery made on burglary of Zawlnuam BDO Office: On September 24th-28th 2012, the Police recovered the sum of Rs.4,38,370/- in Tripura which was stolen from Zawlnuam BDO Office and arrested 5 people alongwith it.

 

Mamit SP, Rodingliana Chawngthu told Vanglaini newspaper that out of the 5 arrested people, 2 people were found not involved and that the Police has not yet found one of the main accused in this case.

 

With the sum or Rs.4,38,370/- recovered on 24th-26th September, the sum total amount of money recovered has come to Rs.84,51,100/-. The Police are working hard to seize the rest of the money from Zawlnuam BDO Office and to arrest the burglars.

 

 

 

D. Back page of Vanglaini newspaper Dt. 27.9.2012.

 

Sending two children abroad; Police and Community Health Action Network(CHAN) on the pursuit : Ralkapthanga of Champhai lodged FIR at Aizawl Police Station on 25.9.2012 in connection with her missing daughter Lalruatmawii(11) and Chhunglawmzuali(3) both of Champhai.

 

The complainant handed over the two missing children to one C.Sangchia, Zarkawt on 15.10.2001 and were sent abroad and they had never been heard since.

 

The complainant approached C.Sangchia on June 2012 and when the latter could not give satisfactory answer to their whereabouts, he approached CHAN and with their advice lodged the FIR.
