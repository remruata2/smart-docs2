A. Front page of VANGLAINI/THE ZOZAM TIMES/THE AIZAWL POST/TAWRHBAWM/MIZO AW/NEWSLINK newspapers Dt.15.1.2013

 

Crime rate falls in north of Mizoram: Crime review meeting was held yesterday i.e. 14.1.2013 at Arya Mess in Police Headquarters led by Pu Zorammuana, DIG(NR) and attended by SP of each district and each unit. The meeting reviewed crimes committed and registered in 2012 and compared it with the crimes committed and registered in 2011.

               Eventhough Mizoram recorded a comparative less number of cases registered against criminal offences during the last year as compared to 2011, cases of rape and murder were on the rise.

               According to Police records, a total of 2117 IPC and non-IPC cases were registered in 2012 compared to the previous year's 2238 cases. These were cases registered in northern range, including Aizawl, Champhai, Serchhip, Mamit and Kolasib districts. Crime reports from Saiha, Lawngtlai and Lunglei districts which fall under southern range were not available.

               While IPC cases increased by 34, which accounted for 2.28% of the total IPC cases, non-IPC cases fell by 115(20.61%) resulting in an overall decrease in crime rate.

               However, major crimes like rape and murder were still on the rise at 33.15% and 18.18% respectively. As many as 79 rape cases were registered during last year, compared to 59 in 2011, while 26 murder cases were registered against 22 in 2011.

               Theft and burglary cases decreased by 6.47% and 1.09% respectively. It has been reported that 18 attempt to murder cases were registered last year, a decline from 23 cases in 2011.

Model Code of Conduct in force ahead of Chalfilh bye-election: On 14.1.2013 i.e. yesterday, Mizoram Chief Electoral Officer, Pu K.Riachho, IAS said that the State Election Department was ready to hold the bye-polls to the Chalfilh assembly seat on 23.2.2013.

              Addressing a press conference at Aizawl Club, Pu K.Riachho said that provisional electorate of Chalfilh assembly seat was 10,754.

              The security situation in the constituency dominated by the Hmar community and by and large controlled by the Hmar People's Convention-Democrats(HPC-D) would be assessed by the State Home Department, he said.

              He said that the election model code of conduct was in place from friday i.e. 11.1.2013, the day the Election Commission of India announced the elections.

              The Chalfilh seat was lying vacant after the demise of Parliamentary Secretary, Pu Chawngtinthanga of the ruling Congress on 16.9.2012.
