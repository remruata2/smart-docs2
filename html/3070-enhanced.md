****SPECIAL REPORT****

No. MSB/CB/SR/2020/492

Dated: 14th September, 2020

 As per input, COVID-19 Rapid Antigen Test (RAT) was conducted among stranded truckers of Kolasib District at the initiative of District Administration w.e.f., 6.8.2020 and on that day 18 (eighteen) Truck Drivers and Handymen were tested positive and all of them were admitted to Covid Care Center (CCC), Polytechnic Building, Thingdawl, Kolasib on 7.8.2020 for necessary medical treatment. In light of the above test where COVID-19 positive cases were detected among truck drivers, RAT has been conducted at Vairengte w.e.f., 10.8.2020. So far, a total of 202 (two hundred and two) Non-Mizo Truck Drivers and Handymen were tested COVID-19 positive and admitted at CCC, Thingdawl and 111 (one hundred eleven) were released from CCC after being tested negative. As on today i.e., 14.9.2020, 91 (ninety one) Truckers are presently accommodated at CCC, Thingdawl and another 69 (sixty nine) Truckers are about to be released today, i.e., 14.9.2020, after they are tested negative.

In this connection, on 10.9.2020 Silchar Truck Driver’s Association (STDA) submitted a written petition to Liaison Officer, Mizoram House, Silchar wherein they stated their displeasure over the manner of treatment being given to Truckers at CCC, Thingdawl, Kolasib stating that they were deprived of proper fooding and lodging facility at CCC, Thingdawl and they also suffered continuous ill-treatment at the hands of persons engaged at the Center. They also conveyed their displeasure against the alleged physical assault received from workers when complaints were made about their ill-treatment. It further alleged that some of them are treated for more than 45 (forty five) days at Quarantine Center without taking any step for their release by District Administration and due to which their families suffered financial hardship. They requested for necessary action for immediate release of Non-Mizo Truckers quarantined at CCC, Thingdawl, Kolasib, failing which they will resort to agitation.

In this regard, Silchar Truck Driver’s Association (STDA) resorted to road block w.e.f., 12.9.2020 @ 5:00 a.m. at Lailapur, Assam in demand for immediate release of Non-Mizo truckers who are quarantined at CCC, Thingdawl, Kolasib.

On 12.9.2020 @ around 10:30 p.m., 11 (eleven) Mizo Tanker Drivers and Handymen who were proceeding towards Mizoram were physically assaulted at Lailapur, Assam by the above agitators. All the Drivers and Handymen sustained minor injuries and they were evacuated to Hospital for medical treatment and were subsequently released after receiving medical treatment. They were given shelter at Dholai PS and they safely entered into Mizoram under Police escort on 13.9.2020.

In this connection, All Mizoram Petroleum Dealers’ Association (AMPEDA) issued a Press Release yesterday i.e., 13.9.2020 wherein they condemned the physical assault of Mizo Oil Tankers Drivers and Handymen at Lailapur, Cachar, Assam on 12.9.2020. It also demanded the Government take immediate action against the offenders and to award suitable punishment. It further stated that all Petrol Pumps throughout Mizoram will be closed on 15.9.2020 in protest against the said incident.

Further, Mizoram Oil Tanker Drivers’ Association (MOTDA) also issued a Press Release yesterday i.e., 13.9.2020 which stated their regret and anguished over physical assault of 11 (eleven) Oil Tankers Drivers and Handymen and damaged caused to their Trucks while they are on their way back to Mizoram on 12.9.2020 at Lailapur by around 80 (eighty) members of Silchar Truck Drivers’ Association. It also urged the State Government to immediately detect the person(s) responsible. It further urged the following:

1. Immediate medical care to be given to the victims.
2. To take necessary action to repay expenses necessary to cover damaged caused to 11 (eleven) Oil Tankers due to the incident.

Today, i.e., 14.9.2020 @ around 6:00 a.m., 4 (four) members of MOTDA led by Pu Romel Lalruatdika, President, MOTDA proceeded to Vairengte from Aizawl to submit FIR against physical assault of their fellow members on 12.9.2020 at Lailapur by members of Silchar Truck Driver’s Association. Similarly, 4 (four) members of Zoram Drivers’ Union led by Pu F. Dosanga also proceeded to Vairengte from Aizawl today @ 7:00 a.m.

Situation is under close watch.
