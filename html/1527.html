REPORT ON SEEKING OF ASYLUM BY CHIN NATIONAL ARMY (CNA) DUE TO MILITARY COUP IN MYANMAR

No.MSB/CB/B-4/2021/105

Dated: 11th February, 2021

 

          Military takeover took place in Myanmar on 1st February 2021 when democratically elected members of Myanmar's ruling party, the National League for Democracy, were deposed by Myanmar's Military. The Myanmar Military proclaimed a year-long state of emergency and declared that power had been vested in Commander-in-Chief of Defence Services General Min Aung Hlaing. It declared the results of the November 2020 general election as invalid and stated its intent to hold a new election at the end of the state of emergency. In opposition of Military takeover that ousted the country’s democratically elected leaders, thousands of protesters took to the streets in cities across Myanmar.

          In the wake of the above Military takeover, Pu Hlathun, Army Chief of Myanmar-based armed insurgent group Chin National Army (CNA), the armed wing of the Chin National Front (CNF) came to Farkawn, Champhai District from Camp Victoria (CNA Camp, Myanmar) on 2.2.2021 and met
Pu Lalramliana, VCP Farkawn.  He sought asylum for their families in Farkawn VC area in the wake of the military coup in Myanmar and in case, the situation turns violent. Pu Lalramliana, VCP Farkawn communicated the matter to
Pi Maria CT Zuali, Deputy Commissioner, Champhai District over telephone, and she then informed him that she will inform the matter to higher authorities as the matter was beyond her jurisdiction.

Further, so far, there is no report of any action taken by Myanmar Military against CNA and leaders of CNA approached VCP Farkawn and sought asylum in advance in Farkawn VC area, in case the situation turns violent. 

Camp Victoria (biggest Camp of CNA) is located around 3 (three) Kms at Myanmar side of Tiau River (Indo-Myanmar boundary river) from Farkawn, Champhai District. Dr. Suikhar, Asst. Secretary General, CNA is presently stationed at the Camp along with around 100 (one hundred) CNA cadres, and around 40 (forty) families of CNA cadre are also living in the Camp.

Peace Talks between CNA and Myanmar government were conducted since 5.1.2012, and since then cease fire has been declared between CNA and Myanmar Army. Further, cadres of CNA are allowed to roam freely anywhere in the country without arms as per their Memorandum signed with Myanmar Union Level Peace Negotiation Team on 7.5.2012. They also set up Liason Office at Tahan and Matupui Township.

Situation is under close watch.
