AGENDA POINTS FOR ONLINE/VIRTUAL DIRECTIONAL LEVEL CO-ORDINATION MEETING OF LIA ON  08th DECEMBER, 2021

(i)      Security Scenario in Barak Valley (Assam) and Mizoram with special reference to insurgency:

          After the signing of Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) between Government of Mizoram and HPC (D) on  2.4.2018  , there has been no report of any active insurgency within the state.

(ii) Yaba Tablets : As far as Yabla tablets are concerned, there has been no records of its manufacturing and processing inside the state of Mizoram. The seizure of these contrabands by Mizoram Police are mainly during on its way to other states from Myanmar through Mizoram.

Seizure made by CID SB  during 2021 :

(1)       On 18.3.2021 CID(SB) staffs had recovered and seized 5.856 kgs (50,000 tablets) of suspected Methamphetamine value of Rs.1,00,00,000/- in the local smuggling rate at New Capital Complex (Near Directorate of sports & Youth Services) from the join possession of (1) Pekliansanga(30) s/o Ngunthanga of Zokhawthar (2) Kajal Paul(42) s/o MC Paul of Sonai road, Silchar, Assam and (3) Bappu Choudhury of GC Collage road, Silchar, Assam P/A New Capital Complex, Aizawl. Vehicle (TATA 207) B/R MZ 01-E9399 which was used for transporting the suspected Methamphetamine and Rs.50,000/- found to be sale proceed money were also seized. Hence, Spl. Narcotic PS C/No.10/21 dt.18.3.2021 u/s 22(c)/29/25/62 ND&PS Act was registered.

            Regarding this, one of these accused, Kajal Paul(42) s/o MC Paul of Sonai road, Silchar, Assam is believed to be the main kingpin in the trafficking of Methamphetamine from Mizoram to Assam state and his arrest is considered to minimised the flow of these narcotics from Myanmar through Mizoram to Assam at the moment.

(2)       On 26.8.2021 , a Joint Operation team of CID (SB) and SIB, Mizoram recovered and seized 5 lakhs tablets of Methamphetamine (v/o Rs. 25 crores at international smuggling rate and Rs. 10 crores at the local smuggling rate) on 26.8.2021 @ 3:15 p.m. The contraband items were seized from 1 (one) heavy vehicle CRX 1512, BR/No. NL - 01A E-0243, driven by Mantu Kumar Dep (45) S/o MK Deb of Karimganj, Assam and second driver Subhas Das (33) S/o Sumesh Das of Karimganj, Assam near Buichali Bridge, Sairang, Aizawl District. In this connection, the above 2 (two) persons were arrested after observing all formalities and they were produced at Special Narcotic Police Station along with the seized contraband items. Accordingly, Special Narcotic PS Case No. 20/21  Dt. 26.8.2021 u/s 22(c) /29 ND & PS Act has been registered.

(iii)                 Trans-Border Crimes and Illegal Movement through border including present status of Myanmarese refugees:

 

                        Myanmar refugees have started entering Mizoram since 12th February, 2021 in the wake of Military Coup in Myanmar. The first batch of Myanmar refugees entered Mizoram on 12th February, 2021 and accommodated at Sangau-II, Lawngtlai Disitrict, Mizoram. These Myanmar refugees are presently staying at different Districts of Mizoram, most of the refugees are staying with their Indian relatives while others stayed at Refugee/Relief Camp.

 

                        As per our office record, as many as 15,500 Myanmar refugees had already entered the state of Mizoram, out of which (as on 6th December, 2021) 14,557 refugees are currently staying within the state of Mizoram, 923 refugees had already returned to their country of origin and 20 refugees had left Mizoram and proceeded towards Delhi.

 (iv)     Human Trafficking and movement of Rohingyas and other Foreign          nationals:

 

                        As per the instruction of Govt. of India, four Anti-human Trafficking Units (AHTU) has been established in Aizawl District, Lunglei District, Champhai District and Kolasib District under Mizoram Police Department.

 

B.                    Present Scenario:

 

                        Action taken by Govt. of Mizoram and Mizoram Police Department in the field of Anti-Human Trafficking are as follows:

 

            1)        Superintendent of Police CID (Crime) was appointed as state Anti Human Trafficking Nodal Officer by Police Headquarters, Mizoram vide order No. CB/PHQ/C/307/2010/75 Dtd. 02.11.2010 and email ID of Nodal Officer is ahtumizoram@gmail.com .

 

            2)        Police Headquarters order No. CB/PHQ/C/307/08/311 Dtd. 12.07.2010 appointed all Addl. SP and Dy. SP as Anti-Human Trafficking Nodal Officers in their respective District.

 

            3)        Whenever information is received regarding Human Trafficking, the case will be taken up by Anti-Human Trafficking Unit (AHTU) Team set up at Aizawl, Lunglei, Champhai and Kolasib District along with representative of State Department and NGOs, within their respective jurisdictions under the supervision of concerned District Superintendent of Police and the Nodal Officer, Anti-Human Trafficking, Mizoram. However, in regards to remaining District where AHTUs are not set up viz. (Serchhip, Mamit, Lawngtlai and Siaha, Saitual, Khawzawl and Hnahthial), if such report is received, AHTU Team will temporarily set up by the concerned District Superintendent of Police in consultation with the Nodal Officer, Anti-Human Trafficking, Mizoram and the Team so setup will take up the Human Trafficking cases.

 

            4)        Recently, Mizoram State has some inter-state Human Trafficking cases. The Investigating Officer/Senior Officer along with lady officer is usually dispatched to conduct rescue operation in such cases. Co-operation with NGOs is required for rescue and  rehabilitation of trafficked victims and to have a successful combat against Human Trafficking.

Workshop for Police Officers and NGOs:

 

                        It is proposed to conduct Workshop and Training to Sensitized Mizoram Police Officers and NGOs in connection with Laws regarding Human Trafficking. Fund required for this is being sought from the Central Government. Training/Seminar proposed to be conducted are as follows:

 

                        Awareness Campaign: Under the overall guidance of Nodal Officer, State Anti-Human Trafficking Unit, awareness is generated through TV talk shows in local channels, radio talk show and live phone in programmes.Circulars have been issued to the public through Young Mizo Association (YMA), the largest NGO in Mizoram and awareness campaigns have been conducted in schools and colleges.

 

                        Sensitizing Police personnel posted on Bus Stands, Bus Terminals and Railway Stations (if any) on human trafficking and also to keep a close watch on suspicious activities has been made recently. Similarly, police personnel posted at Police Stations and at various check posts and borders of the State are being sensitized in issues relating to human trafficking.

 

                        State Level Conference on Human Trafficking for Judicial Officers and Police Officers was proposed to be held on March 2020 but was cancelled due to Covid -19 pandemic.
