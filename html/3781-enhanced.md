****PEACE DIALOGUE BETWEEN GOVT. OF MIZORAM AND HPC(D)****

****

**Due to the initiative of SP CID (SB), **5(five) rounds of Peace Dialogue** between Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) were conducted within the year 2016 and 2017. The **1st round of Peace Dialogue was held on 10th August,2016** and the **2nd round of Peace Dialogue was held during 5th to 6th October,2016**, the **3rd round of Peace Dialogue was on 16th December, 2016 @ 11:00am at State Guest House, Aizawl**. ** The **4th round of Peace Dialogue was held on 28th April, 2017**, and the **5th Round of Peace Dialogue was held on 21st & 22nd September, 2017** where in the framework of agreement was worked out. The Peace Dialogue was concluded under conducive atmosphere. As scheduled, **the Political Level Peace Talk between Mizoram State Government and HPC-D was held under a cordial environment at the State Guest House, Aizawl during 7th & 8th March, 2018** and the Peace Talk ended with a logical conclusion on 8.3.2018. The State Government and the HPC-D were represented by 10 (ten) members each. While the delegates of Mizoram Government were led by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Political Adviser to Chief Minister; Pu L.T. Hmar, Working Chairman, HPC-D led the HPC-D delegates during the two-day Peace Dialogue.**

** **On 2.4.2018 @ 4:30 p.m, Government of Mizoram and HPC-D signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) at the function held at the State Guest House, Aizawl**. Shri. Arvind Ray, Chief Secretary of Mizoram and Pu H. Zosangbera, Chairman, HPC-D signed the MoS on behalf of the State Government and HPC-D respectively. Pu Lalbiakzama, Addl. Secretary, Home gave reports on the peace process and speeches were delivered by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Pu Lalthalien, Spokesperson, HPC-D during the function. The signing of MoS will bring to an end, the over two-decades armed conflict of the HPC-D. **The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC-D was held at CTI, Sesawng on 13th April, 2018**. **Around 104 Cadres and 12 Council Members of HPC(D) come to over ground at this ceremony. 44 different Firearms with 48 magazines and 2892 rounds of different ammunitions along with Radio Sets with charger were surrendered by HPC(D) at this ceremony.****
