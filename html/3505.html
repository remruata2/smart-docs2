A. Inside page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 27.5.2014

Police Press Statement on Selam Incident: Police made a press statement on 26th May, 2014 regarding the law & order problem that arose between Forest Dept. staff and Selam villagers stating that case had been registered at Ngopa Police Station vide Ngopa PS C/No. 11/14 dt. 24.5.2014 u/s 186/353 IPC r/w14 Foreigners Act.

          The police statement said that when government employees do their duty, it is illegal to disturb them with weapons/tools and violence and that it is a very serious matter not to be deal with lightly. As such the police deal with Selam incident seriously and doing its best in finding the wrongdoers to get punishment for their deeds. The police sent to Selam were prepared and armed in accordance with the action and possible reaction of Selam people.

          Trying to stop the police who escorted the arrested 14 persons responsible for the incident by using women and children and blocking the road with logs and felled trees were all illegal, and that the police are doing everything according to the law, said the police statement.

          Women and children put in the forefront are regarded as ‘mob’ when problem occurs and if the police do anything or use any action to disperse the mob, and if women and children are injured or hurt, it is not the police's responsibility but the people who put them in that forefront position, also said the police statement.         

 

B. Front page of THE AIZAWL POST newspaper Dt. 27.5.2014

Selam Community Leaders Sent to Jail: 10 Selam community leaders and 4 persons arrested for illegal timber cutting and for torching E&F Department's truck were sent to court on 26th May and hearing will be will be held on 29th May, 2014.

Injured Man - Dead: Lalbiakmawia (30) who was found unconcious at Reiek road near Haukhuma Hauzel's farm succumbed to his injury at around 5:30am yesterday after Maubawk YMA and police took care of him. Lalbiakmawia was injured on his head.

Opium Seized: On Sunday morning at 1:30am, Lawngtlai Police seized 520 grams of Opium and arrested the alleged owner Lalneiha of Sihtlangpui. Sources said that Lalneiha was also involved in the robbery of Lawngtlai SBI in the year 2000.
