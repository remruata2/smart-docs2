STATUS REPORT OF BRU REFUGEES VIS A VIS REPATRIATION PROCESS

 

 

 

              The Brus also called Riangs are the Nomadic Tribe living in the western belt of Mizoram, bordering Bangladesh and Tripura State. As far as history goes the Brus started entering into Mizoram around 1940's moving from one place to another for jhum cultivation.

 

              Political awareness gradually emerged amongst the Brus and formed a political party called Riang Democratic Party in June, 1990. At the same time Bru Student Association and Bru Welfare Committee were also formed to promote the welfare of Brus in Mizoram. In 1994 the RDP, BSA and BWC at a joint conference formed a new political party called the Bru National Union(BNU) by merging the three organisations. The BNU conference held in September, 1997 at Saipuilui village in Mamit District adopted a resolution for creation of Autonomous District Council for Brus in the western part of Mizoram which was strongly objected by the Mizo community in the concerned area. At the same time a militant outfit called the Bru National Liberation Front(BNLF) which was formed in 1996 under the Presidenship of Vanlalliana, Tuirum village in Mamit district became more and more active and on 21.10.1997 Lalzawmliana, Forest Game Watcher in Dampa Forest Sanctuary was killed and two of his friends were also abducted by the BNLF. The news about the killing had spread like wild fire throughout Mizoram and ignited the already tension in the area due to the demand of an Autonomous District Council by BNU. Tension between the two communities escalated and communal violence broke out and being lesser in numbers, the Bru people started fleeing Mizoram at the instance of the BNLF for their operational convenience.

 

              The Bru people who fled Mizoram in the wake of the incident were given refugees status in Tripura. They were given shelter in six refugee camps and were also given free rations and other essential commodities at the rate fixed by the Union Government.

 

              After a number of meetings between the Centre and the State authorities were held, date for repatriation of the Bru refugees was fixed i.e. 16th November, 2009. While all preparations for repatriation process were going on smoothly a Bru militant group who called themselves the Bru Revolutionary Union(BRU) killed a Mizo youth on 13th November, 2009. It was later confirmed that killing was done deliberately by the BRU in order to deter the repatriation process.

 

              Even though the killers of the innocent Mizo youth left a note with a letter head 'Bru Revolutionary Union', subsequent investigation and statement given by persons involved in this murder case revealed that there is no such organisation as 'BRU' but a group of Bru miscreants who had been instigated by some section of MBDPF leaders to derail the repatriation process who did such heinous crime. Nevertheless, there are two known armed groups who operated in the Mizoram-Tripura border and Mizoram-Assam border who had secret understanding with some leaders of MBDPF who could be utilised when the need arise. The existence of these armed groups was revealed based on the revelation made by one of their leaders arrested by Mizoram Police named Henry recently. Reliable information received stated that this very group are the one who gave warning to refugees not to obey the Govt's plan of repatriation and who instilled fears amongst the Bru Refugees.

              The aftermath activities of the anti-repatriation process may be summarized as under:-

 

              On 24.1.2010, a procession organized by MBDPF was carried out from Naisingpara to Asapara in protest against arrest of R. Laldawngliana, Vice President, MBDPF on 23.1.2009 by Mizoram Police, who is suspected to be the kingpin in the murder of Zarzokima (17) S/o Lalngilneia of Bungthuam, Mamit District. The processionists adopted resolutions that they would refrain from the ongoing talks on their repatriation until R. Laldawngliana was released and should further talks be held, it should be outside Mizoram and should be tri-partite with representatives from the Central Govt.

 

              On 27.4.2010, it was learnt that Elvis Chorkhy, President, MBDPF who was in favour of the repatriation was targeted by Bru militants led by Moikau believed to be instigated by Bruno Msha, Asstt. General Secy. and R. Laldawngliana, Vice President, MBDPF to derail the repatriation process.

              In the meantime, Tripura Police also sensed the presence of threat against Elvis Chorkhy and asked him whether he required security or not.

 

              On 30.4.2010, a meeting of MBDPF leaders was held at Naisingpara in the absence of Elvis Chorkhy, Pesident, MBDPF. The meeting was attended by the following leaders who all opposed the repatriation.

 

                          1) R. Laldawngliana, Vice President

                          2) Sawibunga, Gen. Secy.

                          3) Bruno Msha, Asst. Gen. Secy.

                          4) Vansanga, Jt. Secy.

 

              The meeting decided to lobby with an influential Hindu NGO, the Shiv Mondali to oppose the repatriation process.

 

              On 9.5.2010, a joint meeting of MBDPF leaders and Presidents of MBDPF Branches representing six refugee camps was held at Naisingpara and discussed the Govt. of Mizoram's proposal for repatriation of Bru families who fled Mizoram in the wake of Bungthuam incident in the last part of 2009. The meeting demanded the Government to fulfill the following points:-

 

              1) The Government should pick them up from where they were temporarily staying at different refugee camps upto their final destination.

 

              2) They could not accept 284 family list prepared by D.C, Mamit and that 564 families with a population of more than 5000 prepared by Tripura Govt. should be accepted.

 

              3) Joint spot verification should be made by representatives of Govt. of Mizoram and MBDPF to ascertain the true identity of the recent refugees.

 

              It was learnt that the refugees were willing to be repatriated with the Government's proposal but were discouraged by some section of MBDPF leaders on the pretext of the above points and some refugees were even warned not to obey the Government's proposal or else they would be targeted by Bru militant group as soon as they boarded in the vehicle to return to Mizoram.

 

              A group of Bru militant under the leadership of Moikau, encouraged by some section of MBDPF leaders such as Bruno Msha, Sawibunga and Vansanga, till 10.5.2010, tried to persuade the people to oppose the Government's plan and even threatened them of dire consequences if they proceed to Mizoram as planned. The group numbering around 9 with arms was also learnt to be in readiness to target the refugees who returned to Mizoram.
