Subject:          Report on seizure of 1980 nos. of 7.56x39mm live Ammunitions

 

Sir,

On dated 07.06.2024 @ around 09:00am, a specific information was received from a reliable source stating that some unknown person(s) were in possession of live round Ammunitions in contravention to The Arms Act, 1959 in the area of Serchhip town. As such, self with CID (SB) operation team left Aizawl and proceeded towards the said location.

                        On reaching Serchhip town, information was given to the Officer in charge, Serchhip Police Station. On being accompanied by staff from Serchhip Police Station, two male persons who were acting suspiciously carrying Blue rucksack and light white colour polythene bag were spotted entering the residence of Pi Lalduhzuali of Darnam veng, Serchhip. After discreet enquiry was conducted and after ground of belief was recorded under section 165 Cr.PC., the two male persons who were later identified as (i) Lalchhanhima  (44yrs.) S/o Zabiaka (L) of Leisen, Thantlang District, Myanmar and (ii) Lalnunnema (31yrs.) S/o Lalkima (L) of Thantlang, Thantlang District, Myanmar were approached.

                        On checking their Blue rucksack, a light white colour polythene bag was found, inside which 1980 nos. of live rounds ammunitions, 7.62 x 39mm caliber, meant for AK 47 rifle. The value of the ammunitions is estimated to be @ Rs. 10,89,000/- (Rs. 550 perpiece) in the illegal market rate. The illegal 1980 nos. of live rounds Ammunitions, 7.62x39mm and CANDA 125 Bike (Registration no. - NIL) used for transportation are seized in the presence of reliable witnesses and after attaining all legal formalities.

                        The arrested accused and the seized items were then produced to Serchhip PS. Serchhip PS Case No. 56/2024 Dt. 07.06.2024 u/s 25(1-A) Arms Act 1959 r/w 34 IPC / 192 MV Act r/w 14 Foreigner Act was registered.
