A. Front page of VANGLAINI  newspaper Dt. 23.6.2022

10 Mizos arrested for drugs;  BMA urges Mizo community to be vigilant :    Seven Mizos were arrested in Bengaluru and three Mizos in Rwanda in a large-scale heroin smuggling operation in Bengaluru recently. Bangalore Mizo Association (BMA) on 21st June 2022 called on Mizo community to be vigilant against deception and drug trafficking in Bangalore.

          BMA  in a statement on Tuesday night said that some people are being lured to import goods into India by inviting them to travel abroad for money .

          Five Mizos, who were lured by a Mizo woman, returned to India from South Africa at the end of May 2022. After Customs officials searched and found heroin in their luggage at Bangalore airport, they were arrested and put in jail.  Four others were also arrested in Rwanda before entering India.

          According to BMA president Lawmna Kima, the main accuse behind this deception is Lalngaihsaki d/o Laltlanthanga, Bawngkawn,Aizawl who is a pay guest in Mumbai.  Her main accomplice, a Nigerian on the other hand lives in Delhi,  according to the Times of India.

          BMA president said that five heroin smugglers coming from South Africa were arrested at Bangalore airport on 24th  May 2022. Lalngaihsaki and her husband were also arrested on 25th May on charges of Heroin and are being tried for bail.

          Lawmna Kima said that four people, three Mizos and one non mizo were also arrested in Rwanda for drug trafficking.  The three Mizos are from Kolasib, Khawzawl and Tuikual.

          BMA said that it is difficult for the BMA to deal with and assist drug-related victims and appeal to the Mizo community to be vigilant against such types of deception.

 

 

B. Front Page  of VANGLAINI  newspaper  Dt. 23.6.2022

Rationing of Petrol  is necessary; no shortages with other supplies: Food, Civil Supplies and Consumer Affairs (FCS&CA) Secretary Ramdinliani expressed the need to rationed petrol for the time being due to heavy rains in Assam and Meghalaya.

          Ramdinliani said, "Mizoram's main source of petrol is Silchar depot, Assam where sufficient amount could not be stored as train loads could not reach it due to the floods of Silchar. Diesel, however, is not affected by the situation in Silchar."

           "Despite various difficulties, petrol is being transported to meet the daily needs of the people, however, controlling its sales is necessary to avert hoarding by some people" FCS&CA Secretary further said.
