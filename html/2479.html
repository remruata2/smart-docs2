MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER SITUATION REPORT

No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/633

Dated 11th November, 2020

 

          In continuation of our report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/631
Dt. 10.11.2020, Situation Report on Mizoram-Assam Border is as under:

 

  1. Thinghlun, Mamit District Report :

          No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

 

  1. Kanhmun, Mamit District Report :

          15 (fifteen) vehicles entered Mizoram via Medili Bridge, Assam-Mizoram border today i.e., 11.11.2020 and there is free movement of vehicles across the border.

          Renovation work of Damcherra Bridge (Connecting Bridge of Mizoram and Tripura) is underway and it is expected to complete the work within November, 2020.

 

  1. Saihapui ‘V’, Kolasib District :

          Shri Chander Shekhar, who claimed himself to be IED Adviser (MHA), Jorhat visited bomb blast sites at Upper Painom (Phainuam) Lower Primary School and SSA Lower Primary (Dolakhal) School, Saihapui ‘V’ today i.e., 11.11.2020 @ 11:50 a.m. and after taking photograph/video of the site he left the area at 12:05 p.m. today’s afternoon.

          Some non-mizos are collecting betel nut from Pu Jacob-a of Phainuam farm at Saihapui ‘V’ area today i.e., 11.11.2020 @ 11:00 a.m. Upon learning this, leaders of Saihapui ‘V’ Branch MZP led by Pu Joseph Lalherliana, their President went to the spot and apprised the owner Pu Jacob of Phainuam, who was at the spot regarding their decision for temporary suspension of engaging business with non-mizos due to the prevailing border standoff and then they discontinued collection of betel nut without any misunderstanding.        

          Further, Shri Arunab Saikia, Scroll.in News, Guwahati arrived Saihapui ‘V’ today at 12:50 p.m. to prepare news report but he was not allowed to visit
Pu Lalchawimawia’s paddy field allegedly destroyed by SSB personnel deployed by Assam. However, he interviewed Pu Lalchawimawia of Saihapui ‘V’ and he left the area at 1:57 p.m. today’s afternoon.

          Pu Lalbiakthanga Khiangte, DIG (NR), Dr. H. Lalthlangliana, DC, Kolasib and Pu Vanlalfaka Ralte, SP, Kolasib visited site preparation of proposed SSB Camp deployed by Assam at the spot today i.e., 11.11.2020 @ 3:44 p.m. and left the area at 4:53 p.m.

 

  1. Bairabi, Kolasib District Report :

          There is no movement of vehicles across Bairabi border today i.e., 11.11.2020. Freight train carrying rice arrived Bairabi Rail Station today’s early morning i.e., 11.11.2020 @ 1:30 a.m. and information received indicated that with the intervention of Assam Police blockage of Mizoram freight train bound to Mizoram at Muhamadpur Rail Station and Jamira Rail was lifted since yesterday’s evening. Situation is normal.

  1. Phaisen, Kolasib District Report :

5(five) Oil carrying Trucks and 7(seven) Trucks carrying LPG entered Mizoram from Assam via Phaisen last evening i.e., 10.11.2020 and situation is normal.

 

  1. Vairengte, Kolasib District Report :

          Construction for BSF Camp beyond Vairengte Auto Stand is continued today i.e., 11.11.2020. P&E Department provided electric connection at the site and it is expected that PHE Department will also provide water connection. No essential carrying vehicles entered Mizoram today i.e., 11.11.2020.

          Dr. H. Lalthlangliana, DC, Kolasib and Smt. Keerthi Jalli, DC, Cachar held a meeting with Drivers’ Union at Forest Beat House, Lailapur today i.e., 11.11.2020 @ 12:30 p.m. Pu Vanlalfaka Ralte, SP, Kolasib, Shri Bhanwar Lal Meena, SP, Cachar, Pu Lalfamkima, Chairman, Vairengte Joint VC, leaders of Lailapur Panchayat, Leaders of Silchar Truck Owners Association, Media persons and other officials attended the meeting. During the meeting, DC, Kolasib assured complete safety of drivers within Mizoram and press release issued in this regard by government of Mizoram was also distributed to leaders of Drivers’ Union.  As per report of DC, Kolasib, the drivers had no issue concerning their safety within Mizoram and it was found that blockade was organized by only a few persons of Lailapur. Some drivers expressed their desire to go to Mizoram to continue their normal business. However, lifting of road blockade is still strongly opposed by some hardline elements of Lailapur.

         

          Situation is under close watch.

(K. LALREMSANGA)

Sr. Superintendent of Police

CID (SB) : Mizoram : Aizawl.
