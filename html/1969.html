A GLIMPSE AT THE NBFCs AND UIBs OPERATING  IN MIZORAM

 

 

As many as 30 Non-Banking Financial Companies and Un-incorporated Bodies had been established and operating as Financial Institution in contravention with Chapter III C Rule No. 45S  of  Reserve Bank of India Act (Act 2 of 1934)  in Mizoram, most of them at Aizawl since 1996 till date. Majority of these companies are sponsored by persons from Assam and Kolkata and many Burmese nationals permanently settled in Mizoram and Manipuries are also involved in this operation. Lists of NBFCs and UIBs are tabled and attached  in a separate sheet .

 

Though some of the Companies claimed to have registration under SEBI and IRDA none of them have been registered under RBI.

 

MODUS OPERANDI: These firms/Companies are advertising themselves to the public as being legal financial institutions and lured them in the form of engaging agents to deposit money with fat interest ranging from three percent (3%) to thirty five percent (35%).

 

MONEY INVOLVED: The amount of money deposited by the customers which these companies are unable to repay is about Rs. 3067.4 lakhs.

 

CURRENT STATUS: More than half of these companies (in bold in the list)  ceased to operate at present. No complaint has yet been received by Police against these companies till date.
