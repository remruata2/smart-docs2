REPORT ON INCIDENT AT BORDER AREAS                                                                                                                              SECRET

 

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2022/68                                                                              Dated: 31st January, 2022

 

                  Information received stated that People Defense Force (PDF) launched an ambushed against around 30 (thirty) Myanmar Army personnel (who are stationed at Rihkhawdar Camp), who proceeded to Khawmawi (Tiau) village, Chin state, Myanmar by foot through forest on 28.1.2022 @ 2:30 p.m., in between Khawmawi (Tiau) and Haimual villages, Chin state of Myanmar. It was followed by exchanged of fire between the two parties which lasted for about half an hour and subsequently the Myanmar Army retreated to their camp at Rihkhawdar. Input received also stated that as a result of the above incident, 1 (one) Myanmar Army personal suffered bullet injury and no casualty was reported from PDF side. In this connection, PDF alleged Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA) of assisting the Myanmar Army. 

             

                  Following the above incident, around 2250 (two thousand two hundred and fifty) residents of Khawmawi village, which is situated near Tiau river (Indo-Myanmar boundary river) and its surrounding villages, Chin State, Myanmar are taking refuge at Zokhawthar and Melbuk villages, Champhai District (Zokhawthar - 2,000 and Melbuk - 250), Mizoram. Leaders of Zokhawthar village and Melbuk village accommodated the above mentioned refugees at available Community Halls, Schools building, Godowns etc. Around 200 (two hundred) Myanmar Refugees are accommodated at temporary shelter at the bank of Tiau river, Indian side. Further, influx of refugees is continuing in the above mentioned two villages till date.

             

                  In pursuance of instruction received from PDF, leaders of Khawmawi village, Myanmar and its surrounding villages imposed curfew within their respective villages due to the possible clash between Myanmar Army and PDF as tomorrow, i.e., 1.2.2022 is the first anniversary of Military Coup in the country.

             

                  Recapping earlier development, Myanmar Army, who are stationed at Rihkhawdar Camp exploded bombs four times on the evening of Dt. 25.1.2022 @ around 8:00 p.m. and in fear of this, around 400 (four hundred) residents of Rihkhawdar and Khawmawi, Myanmar rushed to Zokhawthar village, Champhai District the same night for refuge.
