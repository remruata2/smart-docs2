            The joint meeting lo Law Enforcement Agencies (LEAs) in hybrid mode was chaired by Pu Anil Shukla IPS, DGP Mizoram. The chairman warmly welcomed the officers and explained that the meeting was convened to examine the proposed entry – exit points along the Indo-Myanmar Border by the various agencies and come to a reconciliation and decision for officially designated entry-exit points along the IMB.

            At the invitation of the chairman, Pu Rohit Meena, IPS, DIG (Eastern Range), gave a detailed presentation on the Entry-Exit points along the Indo-Myanmar Border which was followed by a discussion on the viability of the proposed entry-exit points along Indo-Myanmar border and the following decisions were made by the meeting.

 

  1. Identification of Villages within the 10-Kilometer Border Zone:

 i.  Suggestions will be sent to the Government of Mizoram, Home Department,   regarding the identification of villages located within the 10-kilometer FMR zone of the Indo-Myanmar Border. 

ii.   The Home Secretary, Mizoram shall be requested to instruct the concerned District Collectors (DCs) to take necessary action. 

iii.   The identified list of villages will be shared with Mizoram Police, SIB, and Assam Rifles. 

 

 Action: IGP (Hqrs)/SP CID (SB) 

 

2.  Issuing authority for Proof of Identity:

            At present, the SHOs/OCs of Police Station are designated as the issuing authority for proof of identity to residents of villages falling within the FMR zone. The meeting is of the opinion that SHOs/OCs of PS are not he appropriate issuing authority as police stations do not maintain database of citizens. It was decided that proposal shall be sent to the state government to designate a civil authority as the issuing authority for proof of identity.

 

    Action: IGP (Hqrs)

3. Thingsai entry-exit point:

 

            SP Hnahthial raised concerns about the lack of an all-weather road to the Thingsai Entry-Exit point. With the upcoming monsoon season, communication and accessibility to the point will be severely affected. It was decided to inform the concerned authorities about the lack of an all-weather road and the anticipated difficulties during the monsoon season.
