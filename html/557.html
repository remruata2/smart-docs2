FUNCTIONS OF THE UNIT :

           1)        The main functions of the Special Branch is to promptly communicate to    Government and to the Director General of Police information on all matters of          political and public importance.

           2)        The Special Branch also deals with matters of national security of individuals       who are important to the nation.

           3)        Related to above, the State Special Branch has to discharge other functions as       follows :-

            a)        to communicate to the Special Branch of others States information   which is of interest to them;

            b)        to communicate to Deputy Inspector General of Police, Range and    Superintendent of Police of the District and other specified officers          important information which is of interest to them.

            c)        to prepare periodical reports on matters falling within its purview and to     forward copies to those specified;

            d)        to make secret enquiries into important matters of political nature or           those affecting national interest;

            e)        to help in the investigation of those crimes which have a political     purpose or motive and in which political suspects are involved.

SCOPE :      

            The scope of functions of the Special Branch and the subject dealt with includes the following :-

            i)         Political movements and associations, their inception, progress and             development - this includes societies having any political purpose     avowed or concealed.

            ii)        Political activities, open as well as secret and subversive.

            iii)       Labour Unions, their formation and development and labour activities.

            iv)       Volunteer organizations and their activities.

            v)        Youth movements and students associations and their activities.

            vi)       Religious and communal activities and religious activities having a political complexion.

            vii)      Social and economic matters having political significance.

            viii)    Movements and activities of foreigners believed to be politically      motivated

            ix)       Anti-national activities or those likely to affect the security and safety of   the State of India generally.

            x)        Security schemes, including security of vital installations and key    industries.

            xi)       Movements and activities of political suspects and surveillance and            watch over them.

            xii)      Censorship of newspapers, periodicals, pamphlets, party literature etc.

            xiii)    Loyalty and reliability of Govt. service particularly of employee in defence services, transport and communications, services charged with the maintenance of law and order and those connected with vital            installations and industries.

 

SPECIAL FUNCTION :

            The following matters are dealt exclusively by the State Special Branch :

            a)        The existence of any strong and widespread popular feeling on political     matters of importance.

            b)        The circulation of rumours, newsletters, posters, leaflets or other      publications likely to disturb the public peace or to create disaffection,         communal strife or disturbances.

            c)        Attempts to tamper with the discipline, performance or loyalty of the           Armed Forces or of the Police.

            d)        Attempts to suborn the loyalty of other services of Government.

            e)        Espionage and Fifth Column activities.

  1. Branches

Special Branch is divided into various Branches for the smooth functioning of its day to day works. The important Branches are as follows:

  1. Reserve Branch
  2. Foreigner Cell/ Verification Branch
  3. Technical & SMAC Branch
  4. General & Account Branch
  5. Confidential Branch
  6. Field Branch
  7. MT Branch

 

 

  1. Verification Branch

 

  1. Foreigners’ Registration:

            Visa related services were done by this Branch. In September 2018, Foreigner Registration System was changed by the Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India. In this new system, foreigners who have visited Mizoram have to register themselves in the online portal. However, as the system is complex, this Branch serves as a facilitator and conduct interview and field enquiry whenever requested by the Foreigners Regional Registration Office, Kolkata.

  1. Verification of Passport applicants:

            Personal Particular Form (PPF) of Passport applicants within the state of Mizoram were downloaded, printed and send out to all concerned district SsP for police verification by Verification Branch, CID (SB). Upon receiving completed Police Verification Form (PVF) from concerned district SsP, PVR were scanned, entered and uploaded in the PSP system by Verification Branch.

            However, as per instructions received from Mizoram PHQ vide No.DGP(M)/SO-MISC/2019/545 Dt.25.09.2019, Passport Verification process shall be done by Special Branch only and concerned DEF shall no longer take part in the verification of Passport applicants with effect from 01.11.2019. This new system is implemented for reducing the time taken for verification of Passport applicants.

            Besides this, appointment verification, verification of character and antecedents of Para-Military personnel were also done by this Branch.

           

 

  1. Confidential Branch

            The main duty of the Confidential Branch is to deal with all the correspondences of intelligence input received from various sources relating to the security of the States in particular and the nation as a whole, law & order situation, movement and activities of insurgents outfits in and around the State, kidnapping incidents, arms smuggling, etc. It is the duty of this Branch to disseminate such inputs to the concerned as and when called for. It also deals with queries under RTI Act, compilation of weekly, monthly and half yearly returns of CID (SB), furnishing of some specific required information during the sitting of Parliament and State Legislative Assembly is also the duty of the Confidential Branch. 

  1. Field Branch

            The main task of Field Branch is intelligence collection and information collection covering the whole of Mizoram. It established as many as 15 (Fifteen) Sector Offices throughout Mizoram deploying officers and other ranks. Headquarters Field Staff are further divided into different groups to cover the activity of political parties, various NGOs, various Organisations within Government services, movements of students’ union bodies etc.

            Field staff prepared daily reports covering the activity of the stated various organisations. The reports are further submitted to the higher authority in the form of Daily Summary Information or Special Report as and when necessary.

            Besides the day to day normal works, it pursued different works such as assessment of security threats perception, verification of character and antecedents’ of any individual etc. which are assigned by higher authorities.

            Field Branch further diligently watched and closely monitored the activities of suspected persons on smuggling of Arms, Drugs etc. It closely watched the behavior of history sheeter. It also monitored the movements of the underground cadres in and outside the state of Mizoram.

  1. Technical Branch

            Technical Branch of CID (SB) started functioning from 16th April 2010. The main function of this branch is collection of intelligence through technical surveillance viz. analysis of mobile telecommunication voice and data.

                SP, CID(SB) acts as Nodal Officer (Law Enforcement Agency), Mizoram Police, any correspondence relating to tele-communication are handled and managed by Technical Branch. Initially, it was equipped with Voice Logger which is capable of monitoring 16 mobile numbers at a time. Better equipment called Verba Centre was later installed and started functioning since 24th September, 2014. Working system of technical branch is on round-the-clock basis. The system can connect 8 Service Provider (300X8=240 targets) and can support upto 30 concurrent calls (real time) per Service Provider  (30X8=240 concurrent calls) at a time.

 

  1. Subsidiary Multi Agency Centre (SMAC)

            The Subsidiary Multi Agency Centre (SMAC) started functioning at the Office of the Superintendent of Police CID (SB) from the 1st of May, 2011. The main function of this agency is the sharing of intelligence inputs with the different intelligence agency around the country. This sharing of intelligence with other agencies is done through a secured channel called TMS (Threat Management System), developed by Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA). Meetings are held monthly on regular basis wherein inputs are shared amongs various intelligence agencies.
