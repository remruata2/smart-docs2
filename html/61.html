WRITE UP ON THE INFLUX OF ROHINGYAS INSIDE MIZORAM

(AS ON Dt.22.11.2019)

 

 

 

1.         On 3.2.2018 @ 12:00 p.m 2 (two) Rohingya boys, viz (1) Rahamed Tullah, 14 yrs s/o Jahet Hussain and (2) Faizal 13 yrs s/o Jania Allam, both resident of Butinaung, Rakhaine District, Myanmar were apprehended by Champhai Police at Melbuk enroute to Zokhawthar while trying to cross the international border to Myanmar without valid Travel Documents/Passport. They were being forwarded to Juvenile Justice Board, Aizawl District as they were minors while  arrangement was being made for conducting thorough interview in Aizawl.

 

            Preliminary interview has revealed that there were also 4 (four) others, all minors of whom two are females, who were still untraced as they were at different location at the time of the apprehension. It was also revealed that they have been brought to Aizawl by one person named Johny, whose identity is yet to be ascertained, from Bangladesh refugee camp via Lawngtlai and Aizawl Districts.

 

 

 

2.         On 23.4.2019@ 11:30pm, vehicle B/R No.AS 01/BC-0951 Sumo Maxi Cab carrying 8(eight) females, proceeding towards Aizawl was detained at Vairengte Police Check Gate, Sojol Roy(38) S/o Kutinenu Roy of Kabuganj, Cachar, Assam, driver of the vehicle stated that on the evening of the same day, Amrul, owner of the vehicle directed him to carry the girls from Sumo counter, Silchar and drop them at Zemabawk Petrol Pump, Aizawl from where one unknown person will pick them up.

 

            Particulars of the girls are as under :

                        1. Toyaba(22) D/o Soyid Salam

                        2. Rubina(20) D/o Soyid Salam

                        3. Tasmin(19) D/o Dilma Ahmed

                        4. Sokhu(18) D/o Shehel Ahmed

                        5. Noor Kayas Bengum(18) D/o Karim Mulla

                        6. Atariza(18) D/o Mutar Ahmed(L)

                        7. Samwar Begum(17) D/o Abu Subinyan(L)

                        8. Sominara Begum(15) D/o Hasim Mulla(L)

 

            All of Kutupalong refugee camp, Bangladesh.

 

            The system of transporting the girls as per version of the driver is so controversial which raised suspicion and the girls along with the driver are taken to Vairengte Police Station.

 

            On the morning of 24.4.2019, the girls were examined through interpreter as they spoke Rohingya language which is Eastern Indo-Aryan language similar to Bengali to some extent.

 

            Toyaba(22) D/o Soyid Salam, the eldest one stated that all of them are refugee from Myanmar and resided at Kutupalong refugee camp, Bangladesh. Sometime about 5 months ago, Nurkayas and Toyaba both residents of the same camp who used to talk Anamal Hussain who live in Malaysia enticed them to go to Malaysia through India and his brother will find suitable jobs for them. At first, they declined this offer but as the two men promised prosperous future and as their living condition in Bangladesh is so poor, the eight of them agreed to go to Malaysia.

 

            She and her sister Rubina left the camp on 17.4.2019 and when they reached Silchar, the six other girls were already available at the house, the area and owner they did not know.

 

            On the 23rd April, 2019 evening, they were taken to MM Sumo counter, Silchar from where they boarded the Sumo. When they reached Police Check Gate, they were intercepted and taken to Police Station.

 

            Vairengte P/S case no. 42/2019 Dt.24.4.2019  u/s 14 Foreigners Act has been registered in connection with this incident. These eight(8) female Rohingyas were forwarded to District Court Kolasib  on 25.4.2019 for deportation. However, the exact time and how this will be implemented is not yet finalized. Presently, the 8 Rohihgyas are detained at Vairengte police station by improvising suitable accommodation for them.

Interrogations are going on at the time of writing this report to find out the real reasons behind these movements and all the 8 (eight) female suspected victims  are kept in Protective Home, Hunthar while the other 4 (four) Male suspected victims are kept in Bawngkawn Police station.             They were detained while boarding in one Sumo bearing Reg. No.MZ-01/K-4816 which was coming from Aizawl and was driven by H.Lalzawmliana s/o Lalkamlova of Leithum village. It was ascertained that they were all Rohingyas and are being transported from a refugee camp in Bangladesh namely, Kutupalong. They were suspected to be the victims of Human Trafficking being transported to Myanmar. Police personnel at Dungtlang OP apprehended two persons namely, Ngutindawng (25) s/o Thabuantai of Tahan G Group, Myanmar and Jonathan (23) s/o Thalawmlian of Khawthlir, Myanmar from Saikhumphai village and were suspected to be the receiver of the said minor girls and transporter to Myanmar. Tasmila (15), one of the minor girls managed to slip away from Dungtlang village while they were being brought to Champhai. The two stated suspected persons also managed to escape from Police custody at the outskirt of Khuangleng village while being brought to Champhai PS. The seven minor girls and the Sumo driver were produced at Champhai PS.

 

            In this connectioin, Champhai PS C/No. 59/2019, dt 17.6.2019 u/s 370(3)(4)(5) IPC is being registered. SDPO and party proceeded to the spot to arrest the two escaped accused persons and also to recover one victim. The 7 (seven) minor girls were forwarded to CJM Court, Champhai today, i.e. 18.6.2019 for further directions regarding their welfare. According to Information  received, a meeting to chalk out the programme for deportation of the refugees at Hmawngbuchhuah was held on 18.6.2019 @ 11:00 a.m. at DC Office, Lawngtlai. The meeting, chaired by DC, Lawngtlai, was attended by representatives of District Police, Assam Rifles, LADC and CYLA numbering about 10 (ten) persons. The meeting resolved to commence the deportation on 25.6.2019. The necessary internal arrangements and other logistics to be done by DC, Lawngtlai and the Assam Rifles were also endorsed to liaise with Myanmar Army.
