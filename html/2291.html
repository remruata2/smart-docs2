MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER SITUATION REPORT

No.MSB/CB/B-4/2021/117

Dated 18th February, 2021

 

          In continuation of our report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2021/114
Dt. 17.02.2021, Situation Report on Mizoram-Assam Border is as under :-

 

  1. Thinghlun, Mamit District Report:

          No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

 

  1. Kanhmun, Mamit District Report :

21 (twenty one) vehicles (HMV-10, MMV-6, LMV-5) entered Mizoram from Assam via., Medili Bridge today i.e., 18.02.2021 @ 3:30 p.m. and 2 (two) vehicles (HMV-Nil, MMV-2, LMV-Nil) transited their goods at Medili Bridge.

28 (twenty eight) vehicles (HMV-6, MMV-19, LMV-3) entered Mizoram from Tripura via., Damcherra bridge today i.e., 18.02.2021 @ 3:30 pm.
4 (four) vehicles (MMV-4, LMV-Nil) transited their goods at Damcherra Bridge.

 

  1. Saihapui ‘V’, Kolasib District :

          No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

 

  1. Bairabi, Kolasib District Report :

Pu Pabindra Kumar Nath, SP Hailakandi visited Assam Police duty near the site of incident at Assam-Mizoram border, where a conflict occurred on 9.2.2021 today i.e., 18.2.2021 @ 12:48 p.m. and left the area this afternoon around 1:03 p.m.

85 (eighty five) vehicles (HMV-60, MMV-Nil, LMV-20, LPG-Nil, Maxi Cab-5) entered Bairabi from Assam today i.e., 18.02.2021 @ 3:30 p.m. Situation is normal.

 

  1. Phaisen, Kolasib District Report :

          No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

 

  1. Vairengte, Kolasib District Report :

155 (one hundred fifty five) vehicles (HMV-51, MMV-43, LMV-33, LPG-2, Maxi Cab-5, Oil Tanker-21, Bus-Nil) entered Vairengte from Assam today i.e., 18.02.2021 during 8:00 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. Situation is normal.

         

  1. Saiphai, Kolasib District Report :

          No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

         

          Situation is under close watch.

(K. LALREMSANGA)
