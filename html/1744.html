1.

 

HmarPeoples’ Convention-Democrat (HPC-D) 

 

1996

 

To create Autonomous District Council in Hmar tribe inhabited areas in north and northwestern part of Mizoram bordering Manipur and Assam states.

  

(i)Raising funds by collecting the so-called donation through threat,intimidation and extortion.

 

 (ii)Ambush of security forces as and when they have an opportunity to pressurize Government of Mizoram regarding their demand.

 

 (iii)Involved in widespread intimidation during Mizoram Legislative Assembly  Election in their demand area.

 

(iv)Blasting of Bombs.

2.

 

Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA)

 

1988

 

To protect the interests of the Paite community from the ‘onslaught of any community or group’. It further attempts to bring all the Zomi people, divided by artificial State boundaries in various countries, specifically in Myanmar (Chin State), India (Manipur and Mizoram) and Bangladesh (Chittagong Hills Tracts), together under one administrative unit, a ‘Zogam’, which means ‘land of the Zomi’ under the Indian Union.

 

Raising fund by collecting donation and tax in their operational areas inside Manipur and boardering area of Manipur-Mizoram.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

3.

 

Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF)

 

1996

 

To create Autonomous District Council for the Brus residing in western  belt  of Mizoram.

 

Note: To end the militant activities of the BNLF, the Government of Mizoram initiated Peace Talk with leaders of BNLF w.e.f 7.9.2001 and after 12 rounds of peace talks, MoU was signed between BNLF and Governmnet of Mizoram on 26.4.2005. Due to the signing of MoU, altogether 195 cadres of BNLF surrendered before the government on 25.7.2005 along with their arms & ammunition.

 

 

(i)Raising fund by collecting donation and tax in their operational areas on western belt of Mizoram.

 

(ii) Ambush of security forces as and when they have an opportunity.

 

(iii) Kidnapping/Abduction.

 

4.

 

Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram( BLFM)

 

2001

 

To create Autonomous District Council for the Brus residing in western  belt  of Mizoram.

 

Note: 804 strong members of BLFM surrendered before the government on 26.10.2006 with their arms and ammunitions. Government of Mizoram took all possible steps to resettle the BLFM surrenderees identified as bonafide residence of Mizoram. Housing Assistance to the tune of Rs.30,000/- each was disbursed to them in two installments.

 

 

(i)Raising fund by collecting donation and tax in their operational areas on western belt of Mizoram.

 

(ii) Kidnapping/Abduction.
