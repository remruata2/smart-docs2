****EFFECTS OF CAA IN MIZORAM****

**I. BURNING OF POSTERS:**

 As intended by MZP General Headquarters, about 300 (three hundred), including onlookers, gathered in front of Vanapa Hall on 12.12.2019 @ 2:00 p.m. and burnt the posters of Shri. Amit Shah, Hon’ble Union Home Minister and Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP (LS). The MZP President, B. Vanlaltana delivered a short speech denouncing the passage of the Citizenship Amendment Bill and sharply criticised Pu C. Lalrosanga for supporting the bill against the wishes of Churches and the people who had voted him to power.

**II.****SIT-IN PROTEST:**

1. A group known as 'Zoram Revolutionary Movement' (ZRM) staged a Sit-in Protest in front of Vanapa Hall on 13.12.2019 @ 11:00 am in which about 150 (one hundred and fifty) persons participated. The protesters were mostly activists of Students’ Union leaders of various Colleges in Aizawl. This group (ZRM) is known to be in its fledgling stage without elected leaders.

 The protesters carried placards as noted below:-

i) I don't plan on sharing my only home with some strangers.

ii) No Citizenship based on Religion.

iii) We stand with All Anti-CAB Protesters.

 The protesters also shouted slogans demanding resignation of Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP.

2. Zoram Revolution Movement (ZRM), in continuation of its protest of Citizenship Amendment Act (CAA) had called for Sit-in protests in Aizawl and other District Headquarters on 16.12.2019. In Aizawl, about 300 (three hundred) protesters, mostly College students staged a demonstration in front of Vanapa Hall from 11:00 a.m. Slogans were raised demanding resignation of Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP (LS) and also displayed placards denouncing the CAA. Demonstrations were also carried out elsewhere in Lunglei, Serchhip and Kolasib.

3. A Sit-in demonstration organised by Zoram Revolution Movement (ZRM) was carried out at Darthangvunga Traffic Point, Champhai Vengthlang on 17.12.2019 @ 11:30 a.m. About 80 (eighty) mostly College students participated in this demonstration. Protesters displayed placards and slogans were raised deploring the CAA during demonstration.

4. A Sit-in demonstration organised by MZU Students’ Council was held at MZU Campus on 18.12.2019 @ 2:45 p.m about 300 (three hundred) students participated. Preamble to the Constitution of India was read out during the protest and slogans were raised as follows:-

i)  What do we want? - Secular India.

ii) We opposed. - CAA

iii) We condemned. - Delhi Police

iv) Justice for - Jamia Students

**III. SUBMISSION OF****LETTER TO THE HON’BLE PRESIDENT OF INDIA:**

****ZPM Youth led by Pu Malsawmzuala, President submitted their disapproval letter of Citizenship Amendment Act, 2019 (CAA) to the Hon'ble President of India through the Hon'ble Governor of Mizoram on 19.12.2019 @ 12:15 p.m.

**IV. RECEIVED OF PU C.LALROSANGA, MP (LS)****:**

 A demonstration organised by MZP was held at Lengpui Airport to receive Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP (LS), who arrived from New Delhi on 7.1.2020. About 50 (fifty) members of MZP Gen. Hqrs., NESO and ACCJSU, clad in ‘Mizo Pawndum’, gathered outside the Airport at around 1:30 pm. Speeches were delivered by - (i) B. Vanlaltana, President, MZP; ii) Lalnunmawia Pautu, Gen. Secretary, MZP; iii) Ricky Lalbiakmawia, Fin. Secretary, NESO and iv) Lalruatpuia, President, ACCJSU.

 Placards containing anti-CAA slogans and mockery purportedly directed at Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP(LS) were displayed by the protestors.

**V. DISPLAYING OF BLACK FLAGS****:**

******MZP Gen. Hqrs. organized 'MZP Hnatlang' on 9.1.2020 by displaying black flags at various conspicuous places in Aizawl between Sikulpuikawn and Bawngkawn to show their support and solidarity to fellow students who were being victimized in the nationwide protests against CAB/CAA. In a short function held in front of MZP office, Lalnunmawia Pautu, Gen. Secretary, MZP; Ricky Lalbiakmawia, Fin. Secretary, NESO and Lawmsangzuali, student of JNU addressed the volunteers and expressed their support and solidarity to their fellow students. About 20 (twenty) members participated.**

 It may also be mentioned that some political parties and Church organisations also expressed their opposition of CAA in their Press Conference and Press Release.
