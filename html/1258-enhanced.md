****REPORT ON ALLEGED SETTING UP OF CAMP(S) BY ARAKAN ARMY (AA) INSIDE MIZORAM****

Information has been received from Sister Intelligence Agency that the AA (Arakan Army) is setting up camps at Kakichhuah areas, along the Indo-Myanmar border on Mizoram soil. In this regard, an enquiry was conducted to ascertain the stated input. But after conducting thorough enquiry by engaging reliable sources and others means, it is reported that there is no evidence to suggest the existence of AA camp inside Mizoram.

Known AA leaders were also contacted and they reported that they have no camp inside Mizoram and reiterated that they have no intention to establish camp(s) in Mizoram in the foreseeable future. As per input, the nearest AA camp inside Myanmar is around 15 kms from the Indo-Myanmar border.

**RECENT MOVEMENT OF ARAKAN LIBERATION ARMY (ALP)/**

**ARAKAN LIBERATION PARTY (ALP)**

 Around 15 (fifteen) cadres of ALA/ALP left their camps located Bangladesh territory on 23.12.2019 for Refugee Basti (Ajasora) village, Lawngtlai District to spend Christmas festival. Before leaving their camps, they received information about the possibility of attack from AA cadres. As such they brought their arms &amp; ammunitions but they had not seen any AA movement on the route. They had arrived Ajasora on 24.12.2019 @ 9:30 a.m. but their belongings were seized by the BSF personnel posted at Parva after their arrival. Police case was also registered at Vasei PS. The ALA cadres fled Ajasora after the incident and their where about are not known and arrest was not be made till today.

**NGHALIMLUI INCIDENT**

 NGOs of Bungtlang South organized procession on 14.1.2020 @ 2:30 p.m from Vaseikai to Nghalimlui, Lawngtlai District in protest against the alleged murder of Lalrawnliana (32) S/o A. Lalhmuaka of Bungtlang South, whose dead body was found on 11.1.2020 near Nghalimlui. The procession, wherein about 500 (five hundred) people from Bungtlang South participated, ended @ 3:00 p.m. They were also joined by around 10 (ten) leaders of Central Young Lai Association (CYLA) led by Pu J. Sangthangpuia, President, CYLA and around 5 (five) Lai Student Association (LSA) leaders.

 During the procession, the crowd chanted slogans expressing their demand for arrest of the real culprit(s) at the earliest and vehemently condemns the incident. In the meantime, the crowd suddenly attacked the houses of Nghalimlui by pelting stones and as per information received, as many as 47 (forty seven) out of 260 (two hundred and sixty) households were damaged by the crowd. These houses were mostly private owned Assam Type buildings and thatched roof and are located near the road side. Fortunately, there was no reported human casualty due to the incident.

 The crowd left Nghalimlui village after the procession without any further incident.
