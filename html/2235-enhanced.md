**Concise story of the case :**

On February 2021, 10 (ten) students from different parts of Mizoram who had enrolled for B.ed course at NMR college of Education, Kurnool, Andhra Pradesh had faced problems in obtaining their examination admit card upon arrival at Kurnool while intending to appear for their 1st semester examination. Out of the 10 (ten) students, only 1(one) was issued admit card by the University while the rest were rejected citing incomplete documents submitted by the students. The students had enrolled in the said college through Dr.Deb Consultancy Regional office, Khatla, Aizawl. The students had faced severe hardships physically and mentally as well as resulted in financial loss as a results of the misfortune.

Citing Covid pandemic, the University had not conduct semester examination on December 2019 and further no examination was conducted during the year 2020.

However, upon receiving information that the 1st semester examination is to be conducted in the month of February 2021, 10(ten) out of 15(fifteen) students had left for Kunoor, Andhra Pradesh to appear for their 1st semester examination.When they reached Kunoor,out of ten Mizo students only one was able to get admit card as the University authority claimed that incomplete documents was the reason for non issuance of admit card. Meanwhile the college authority had maintained that all the application form duly filled along with necessary documents of the students were submitted to the University authority. From the accounts of the Mizo students, it is learnt that there were also several students from other north eastern states who had enrolled through Dr.Dep Consultancy firm in their respective state but unable to obtain their examination admit card too. Despite, earnest attempts from Dr.Sujal Debnath himself to sort out their problems, the students were unable to sit for their examination. At one point of time the students even intended to lodge First Information Report (FIR) at the concerned Police Station. However, the issue gets settled after Dr.Subal Debnath (Dr.Deb Consultancy) and the Mizo students made a written agreement in which six of them were refunded of the course fees ie,Rs35,000 they had already payed for their cource.The rest of the students preferred to get readmitted at other convenient colleges and were arranged an admission for B.ed course in two colleges in West Bengal which are :

**Dr.Dep Consultancy**

Dr.Deb Consultancy is a registered proprietorship consultant (Registration No.10419/03, Certificate No. In-DL98489038587345L) registered and certified by the the Govt.of National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT of Delhi) under Societies Registration Act XXI of 1860. Its corporate office is located at 12 Navodaya Path, Dwarakanagar, Khanapara, Guwahati, Assam. It has its regional offices at Agartala (Tripura), Kohima (Nagaland), Pelling (Sikkim), Itanagar (Arunachal Pradesh), Aizawl (Mizoram) and Imphal (Manipur).

In the course of enquiry, it is revealed that Dr.Deb consultancy had linked with several colleges in different parts of the country and mainly deals in arranging admission for students in these colleges depending on their choice of course. In the course of arranging admission for students, they inturn received a certain amount of money as consultation charge from the colleges. However, the consultation charge vary upon different colleges.

It is pertinent to mention that with the introduction of The National Council for Teacher Education (Determination of minimum qualifications for recruitment of teachers in schools) Regulations 2001, Dr.Deb Consultancy through its Regional Office at Khatla had provided admission to hundreds of teacher aspirants and in service teachers for B.ed course etc. as demand for persuing such training courses is very much in demand. Many have had obtained their degrees from these colleges and got themselves recruited as school teachers under the Government of Mizoram. However it is to be mentioned that the students who had enrolled through this consultancy firm for B.ed course in particular studied through distance learning /correspondence which is not permissible nowadays. The students are enrolled however as regular students without the knowledge of the university to which they are affiliated.
