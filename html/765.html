YOUNG MIZO ASSOCIATION (YMA)
(S.R. No.4 of 1977 under Indian Society Registration Act(XXI of 1860) on 14th May,1977)
Formation :
Young Mizo Association was established on 15.6.1935 originally as Young
Lushai Association (YLA), which was later replaced with the “Young Mizo
Association” in 1947. It was initiated by the Welsh Christian Missionaries who
understood the need of cultural conservation of the Mizo tribe, who were under
pressure of Political and social modernization. YMA is the largest and most
comprehensive non-profit, secular and non-governmental organisation of the Mizo
people. It was registered as SR No. 4 of 1977 under Indian Societies Registration
Act (XXI of 1860) on 14 May 1977 to the Government of Mizoram.
1. Aims and Objectives :
(a) Good used of leisure
(b) Development of the Mizo Society
(c) Revere Christian ethics
2. Type : Young Mizo Association is a voluntary organization without any Political
Party interference
3. Motto : “Help the needy”

9. General Conference & Election :
a) Office Bearers of CYMA are elected by the General Conference for a term of 2
years.
b) For a member to be elected as Office Bearers, he/she must be a resident of
Mizoram capital, Aizawl.
c) Same post as an Office Bearers cannot be held for more than 2 consecutive
terms.
d) Guide lines for election :
i) Central Executive committee, according to the situation, may nominate not
more than 5 persons.
ii) Executive committee of each Sub-Headquarters may nominate not more
than 3 persons.
iii) Not more than 2 members may also be nominated by Group Executive
Committee.
iv) All the Branches within Aizawl City may nominate 1 candidate each. This
nomination is not necessarily to be a member from within their respective
Branches.
v) These nominated persons should at least be a member of YMA for 5 years
provided they have been a member as an Office Bearer in SubHeadquarters/ Groups or Branches.
