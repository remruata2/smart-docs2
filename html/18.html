STATEMENT OF Pu V.LALCHAWIMAWIA, ADJUTANT 3rd Bn. MAP (PROSECUTION WITNESS) IN CONNECTION WITH DE AGAINST

Inspr. TIALTHAWNGA OF 3rd Bn. MAP

 

            My particulars given above is correct I was posted at 3rd Bn. MAP from September 2008 and posted as Adjutant.

 

            On 22nd April 2009, I was staying late at my office due to pressing works and went home late. After dinner, while I was resting in my home, at around 7:00 – 8:00 PM, I was called by ADM (I) Biaknunsanga through telephone informing me about the incident that while Inspr. Tialthawnga and C/- Biakzauva had an altercation about the purchase of motorcycle, one Hav. Lalengmawia of training branch had interfered and because of this Inspr. Tialthawnga hit or push Hav. Lalengmawia who fell to the ground and became unconscious. The Havildar was brought to Bn. MI for medical treatment but the medical staff, after seeing the gravity of his injury referred him to Civil Hospital. Then I went down to ADM (I) office and I asked about the details of the incident, and I was informed about the reported incidents. I informed this incident to my superior officers and I sent someone to call Inspr. Tialthawnga. But they came back without the officer saying that he was not at home and were told by his relatives that he (Tialthawnga) came home earlier but quickly went out again and did not know where he had gone. I instructed my men to lookout for him. Then I went to the place of incident which is in between Trainers’ Barrack and temporary bathrooms.

 

            After this, we stayed at the ADM (I) office and later came to know that Inspr. Tialthawnga had come home. Then I sent someone to call him. Inspr. Tialthawnga arrived and I asked him about the incident. He (Tialthawnga) replied that he did not clearly know what happened, but as things happened, he have to bear the  responsibilities. We held a discussion among the officers present and we felt necessary to medically examine Inspr. Tialthawnga and I filled up and signed the medical requisition form and sent the office to Civil Hospital, Aizawl for medical examination to ascertain whether he consume alcohol or not. He was escorted by SI H. Lalrinsanga and some Jawans. I also informed Aizawl PS about this as I was asked earlier about the whereabout of Inspr. Tialthawnga. During this period our officers who escorted the injured Hav. Lalengmawia to the Hospital reported that he (Lalengmawia) was still unconscious. After this, as there was nothing much to do, we dispersed and I went home. I was informed about the death of Hav. Lalengmawia, immediately after his death and made necessary preparations for his burial. I have nothing more to say.

 

            EXAMINATION BY PRESENTING OFFICER:

 

Q1. Did you know Inspr. Tialthawnga personally?

 

-       Yes, I know him. He belongs to our unit (3rd. MAP)

 

Q2. Where did he stay?

 

-       He stayed at Govt. Quarter at 3rd MAP Complex.

 

Q3. On 22nd April 2009, while this incident occurs, where did you stay?

 

-       I stayed in Govt. Quarter at 3rd MAP Complex during this period.

 

Q4. While you are preparing for sending Inspr. Tialthawnga for medical examination, did he appear drunk in your opinion?

 

-       Yes, according to appearance, he seems to be a bit drunk. His movement was not normal.

 

Q5. Did you know about the result of medical examination of Inspr. Tialthawnga after he was examined at Civil Hospital?

 

-       Yes, I received the Xerox copy of the medical report on the next day i.e. 23.4.2009 where the MO opined that the officer Inspr. Tialthawnga had consumed alcohol.

 

 

 

Q6. When did you know that Hav. Lalengmawia who become unconscious after being reportedly beaten by Inspr. Tialthawnga has expired?

 

-       I was informed soon after he expired, may be around 4:00 AM.

 

Q7. Then what happened to Inspr. Tialthawnga?

 

-       As I said earlier, I sent Inspr. Tialthawnga for medical examination to Civil Hospital and also informed officers of Aizawl PS about this as I know that Aizawl PS has conducted inquiry on this incident.

 

            EXAMINATION BY DEFENCE ASSISTANT Pu C. VANRAMLAWMA :

 

Q1. I want to know how you receive the information about how Inspr. Tialthawnga has beaten Hav. Lalengmawia?

 

-       From what I hear from others, Inspr. Tialthawnga has hit or push Hav. Lalengmawia around his head/throat which led him to fell down on the ground and became unconscious.

 

Q2. You said that you have visited the place of incident, did you see any hard substance in the area?

 

-       I did not find any particular hard substance but the ground where the incident took place was mud-plastered hard soil.

 

Q3. In your opinion, as per the charge article that Inspr. Tialthawnga gave fistful blow 2/3 times on Hav. Lalengmawia, do you think that this blows if it happened will be able to cause the death of Hav. Lalengmawia?

 

-       In my opinion, giving 2/3 blows is not enough to cause the death of a person but could happen in a rare case if one is an expert combat fighter.

 

Q4. When Inspr. Tialthawnga was produced before you, was he, in your opinion, is a drunken mood?

 

-       From his appearance, it can be presumed that he drank alcohol. His movement is not normal.

 

Q5. Did you know that it is necessary to get approval of the Government to purchase vehicle for Government Servant?

-       No.

Inspr. TIALTHAWNGA (u/s) OF 3rd Bn. MAP

 

            My particulars given above is correct.

 

            On 23.4.2009,  I was posted as Officer-in-Charge of Aizawl PS and I received written FIR from Pu S. Lawmsanga s/o Taihranga of Zemabawk Vengthar stating that on 22.4.2009 at 6:00 PM his younger brother Hav. Lalengmawia of 3rd Bn. MAP who attend roll call was beaten by Inspr. Tialthawnga of 3rd Bn. MAP resulting in his unconsciousness and admitted to Civil Hospital and was treated at ICU and he succumbed to his injuries on 23.4.2009 at 3:10 AM.

 

            Accordingly, case was registered against Inspr. Tialthawnga of 3rd Bn. MAP i.e Aizawl PS Case No. 129/09 Dt. 23.4.09 u/s 304 IPC and Inspr. O.U. Majumdar was endorsed to investigate the case I have nothing more to say.
