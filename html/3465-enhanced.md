****14.1.2022 :** Mizoram Police Sub-ordinate Officers' Association (MPSOA) held its General Conference on 14.1.2022 @10:00 a.m. at the Conference Hall of MPSOA Guest House, Bungkawn, Aizawl. Pu Lalchamliana, Home Minister graced the function as Chief Guest and Pu John Neihlaia, IGP as Guest of Honour. During the conference, they passed the following agendas concerning the state government:**

 (i) MPSOA should take necessary action for filling up of huge existing Police vacant posts under Home Department.

 (ii) Necessary action should be taken for granting of promotion on time to serving Police personnel.

 (iii) MPSOA should take necessary action for filling up of all vacant ACB deputation posts on time.

****16.2.2022 :** 5 (five) leaders of Mizoram Police Subordinate Officers' Association (MPSOA) led by Pu JH Chunglianthanga, President, MPSOA met Pu Lalchamliana, Home Minister on 16.2.2022 @2:00 p.m. at his office chamber. During the meeting, leaders of MPSOA raised issues about slow process of promotion of their members due to the transfer of promotion consideration power/authority from Police Establishment Board (PEB) to DP&amp;AR, Govt. of Mizoram. MPSOA leaders also pointed out that the above move is in violation of Hon'ble Supreme Court Judgment in Prakash Singh &amp; Ors vs Union of India, 2006 which directed that the PEB shall consider all transfer, postings, promotion and other all service-related matters of officers below the rank of DySP. In this connection, the Home Minister assured to look into the matter.**

****20.5.2022 :** Mizoram Armed Police Association (MAPA) held its 2nd General Assembly 2022 on 20.5.2022 @ 11:00 a.m. at Aizawl Club. Pu HC Sangzuala, President MAPA presided the assembly and around 120 (one hundred twenty) members attended. Pu Robert Romawia Royte, Sports Minister graced the function as Chief Guest and Pu John Neihlaia IPS, IGP (Hqrs.) as Guest of Honour. They passed the following resolutions concerning the state government:**

 (i) The state government had already increased Arms Allowance from Rs.4 to Rs.40, and the same should be further increased at a reasonable rate.

 (ii) The state government should again confer authority to Police Establishment Board (PEB), Police Headquarters, regarding consideration of promotion in respect of Constable to Sub-Inspector so as to solve problem of long pending promotion cases.

 (iii) The state government should recruit as many police personnel as possible against the vacant post so as to meet acute shortages of manpower in the department.

 (iv) Necessary action should be taken so as to revoke order circulated to all  Commandants and Principal, PTS which notified Armed Police Personnel, who are matriculate and willing to submit their willingness for attachment to Traffic Police.
