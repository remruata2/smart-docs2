****1.** CNC held a meeting on 16.8.2010 at their office Upper Republic (Pu Hiphei Building) presided over by Ralhnin, General Secy. CNC, 5 Office Bearers attended the meeting. The meeting decided that CNC should conduct seminar on 'Civil society based organisation' for a period of 5 days starting from 27th - 31st August 2010 at the CNC office, Upper Republic.**

 During the meeting, lectures on the following topics will be delivered by eminent Chin intellectuals:-

 1. Importance of Civil Society by Mr. Thanglianpiang, ZRO/ZRA.

 2. Constitution governing CNC by Mr. Khua Uk Lian, CNF.

 3. Gender Equality by Mrs. Lalhmangaihzuali(Tei), CNC.

 4. Environment by Mrs. Lalzenghaki, Women League of Chinland.

 5. News reporting by Mr. Thatni, CNF.

 6. Financial Management/Report by Mrs. G.S. Mang, President, Chin  Student &amp; Youth Federation.

 The seminar is being sponsored by Luxemburg based donor agency 'Euro-Burma Office' which also sponsored earlier seminar in the post. Lianhmung Sakhong, Chairman CNC Thailand based is expected to attend this seminar.

 CNC plans to conduct the training under a veil of secrecy after the State Government earlier refused permission for training of FBR.

 In this connection, Lianhmung Sakhong, Chairman CNC Thailand based arrived at Aizawl from New Delhi on 22.8.2010. In view of recent heavy rainfall and floods, it is not clear whether the delegates from Myanmar would be able to attend the training in full strength.

**2.**On 16.8.2010, the Free Burma Ranger(FBR) had started training from 10:00 am dividing the trainees into two groups i.e. Medical group and Counselling &amp; Photography group. Leaders and Trainers from abroad had given lectures and teachings.

 The Medical group is having training in the office of Women League of Chinland located at Mc Hill, Zarkawt in which Monica Jane Parker, a nurse from Australia had given lectures on orthopaedics/orthotics, pregnancy and delivery, first-aids etc. Sixteen(16) persons had attended the training and will be concluded on 30.8.2010. There are ten(10) trainees at present (as on 24.8.2010) and Dr John Brian North Cote Shaw from USA, who arrived in Aizawl on 22.8.2010 is giving lectures.

 The Counselling&amp;Photography group had training in the house of Rozathanga, Secretary, Chin Humanitarian Relief Committee (CHRC), Dawrpui Vengthar (Pu C. Chawngkunga building) at which David Eubank, Director, FBR, Chiangmai, Thailand had given lectures on how their missions/operations shall be done once inside Burma. Ramlianmawia@ B-a, working at a photo studio 'Rama Photo' at Chanmari, Aizawl and Lalthanzama C/o Pi Sapthangi, Chanmari West, Aizawl, owner of a photo studio 'Zodi Photo' located at Kulikawn, Aizawl had given teachings on handling and operation of different types of cameras. One Nema, brother of said Ramlianmawia was engaged as interpreter. Training of this group was concluded on 17.8.2010 and fourteen (14) persons attended the training.

 The training process of the FBR has been watched closely and as far as known, there was no mentioning of Burma political issues by leaders and trainers while delivering lectures. After completion of the training, different intermixed-groups i.e. Medical and Non-medical persons will be formed and will start their operation in the Chin State of Burma.

 The report about meeting of His Excellency the Governor of Mizoram by leaders of FBR and co-ordinators was also enquired and it was ascertained that Rozathanga, Secretary, CHRC had personally met the former on 10.8.2010. Rozathanga said that the Governor told him he did not think the Mizoram Government would permit them to conduct training and also directed him not to do such thing, if so.

 All the leaders and trainers except Dr. John Brian North Cote Shaw of USA had already left Mizoram.
