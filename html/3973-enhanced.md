****Notes for Mizoram – Assam Boundary meeting on 16.04.2025****

1. **Introduction (History)** :

  The present boundary between Mizoram and Assam as enshrined in the Constitution of India 1935 is that Mizoram shares a total of 164.6 km boundary with Assam, 66 km border with Tripura and 95 km with Manipur.

**2. Bone of Contention :**

  (i) Mizoram claims the 509 square miles (1,318 sq.kms) of the inner line reserved forest notified in **1875** under the **BEFR 1873** as its actual boundary,

  (ii) Assam on the other hand, regards the map prepared by Survey of India in **1933** as its constitutional boundary.

**3. Recent event in chronological order:**

**A.MAMIT DISTRICT:**

**2020 : (1) Number of small incident : 1**

**(2) Number of major incident (in brief) : 1**

**(a) Dt.9.10.2020 :** Mr.Anbamuthan MP, IAS, DC, Karimganj accompanied by SP, Karimgnaj, Forest Officials and around 60 police personnel and civilians from Assam destroyed plantations and also set fire to Jhum huts at Thinghlun, Mamit District belonging to Mr. John Zolawma and Mr. Ben David. In this connection, Mizoram Police was deployed at Zaite road, Thinghlun to maintain order in the area.

**2021 :** **(1) Number of small incident : 1**

**(2) Number of major incident (in brief) : 1**

**(a) Dt.27.4.2021 :** On the evening of 27.4.2021 @ 9:20 p.m. Kanhmun VLTF Duty Post located at Kobangsang, around 1 km from Kanhmun Village was allegedly set ablaze by unknown miscreants and it was immediately extinguished. Later around 10:30 p.m., the cultivated land of Pu Rozamliana of Kanhmun located around 500 meters from the said Kanhmun VLTF Duty Post and Forest Teak Plantation located near VLTF Duty Post were burned and the fire was extinguished by volunteers from Kanhmun Village @ 12:00 a.m. the same night.

**B. KOLASIB DISTRICT:**

**2018 : (1) Number of small incident : Nil**

**(2) Number of major incident (in brief) : 2**

   **(a) Dt.27.02.2018 :** MZP (Mizo Zirlai Pawl) Gen. Hqrs. constructed a wooden rest house (Zofate Chawlhbuk) at Bairabi Zophai, it was demolished by Assam Police and Assam Forest Officials on the same evening claiming that it was built within Assam's territory.

   **(b) Dt.10.03.2018 :**The MZP's with an intent to reconstruct 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' at Bairabi Zophai proceeded to the same spot and were intercepted by Assam Police and subsequently used lathi and firearms to disperse them.

1 MZP member sustained gunshot injury, 49 others with 8 media persons sustained minor injuries. The Assam Police burnt down the temporary shed constructed by the MZP near the disputed site, the following vehicles belonging to MZP members were also ransacked by Assam Police :-

(1) Sumo - 1 no. (2) Taxi -1 no. (3) Car (Nano) - 1 no. (4) M/Cycles -3 nos.

**2019 : (1) Number of small incident : 2**

**(2) Number of major incident (in brief) : 1**

   **(a) Dt. 23.05.2019 :**There was bomb blast at a Mosque named ‘Bhaithul Mukharram Jamma Masjid’ located near Bairabi Zophai in the disputed Inner line Forest Reserve area. The blast had caused partial damage to the mosque building without casualty.

**2020 : (1) Number of small incident : 5**

**(2) Number of major incident (in brief) : 2** ****

**(a) Dt.17.10.2020 :** On 17.10.2020 @ 12:00 p.m. around 200 Assam civilians and 50 Assam Police personnel came at 1stIR Duty Post located at the outskirt of Saihapui 'V' and forcefully dismantled the duty Post outnumbering the Mizoram Police on duty.

   **(b) Dt.17.10.2020 :** On 17.10.2020 @ around 6:30 p.m., around 30 persons, allegedly residents of Lailapur, Assam pelted stones at IR temporary duty Camp set up due to COVID-19 pandemic near Vairengte Autorickshaw Stand at Mizoram-Assam border. Enraged over the incident, around 300 (three hundred) residents of Vairengte armed with sticks, dao etc. rushed to the spot and allegedly set ablaze 1 (one) house and around 7 (seven) temporary shops set up by Assam at the disputed Mizoram-Assam border. There was scuffle between residents of Vairengte and Lailapur leaving several injuries.

**2021 : (1) Number of small incident : 6**

**(2) Number of major incident (in brief) :****3******

******(a) 9.2.2021:** One Bairabi North VC member Pu H. Vanlalrova, Bairabi North VC Member was physically assaulted by residents of Assam at Zophai while he and 2 (two) staff of P&amp;E Department conducted survey for erection of electric poles at Zophai, Pu H. Vanlalrova sustained injuries on his back. IR personnel of Zophai BOP immediately rushed to the area and SDPO Kolasib, OC Bairabi PS and party also reached the spot to control further escalation of tension. OC, Ramnathpur PS and staff, Assam also arrived at the spot to control the situation. Around 45 (forty five) youths of Assam carrying bamboo sticks gathered at the area and similarly around 100 (one hundred) residents of Bairabi gathered at Mizoram side and pelted stones towards each other but no serious physical injury has been reported from either side. The crowd from either side were dispersed at around 6:00 p.m. 

****

**(b) 30.6.2021:** There was a stand-off between around 200 Assam Police Personnel, which was later reinforced by 2 Companies of CRPF from Bilaipur, Assam and 10 Mizoram Armed Police personnel and 3 MRP personnel led by Pu David J. Byhnadaolai, Addl. SP Kolasib at Pu Ramhluna's farm land, Aitlang Vairengte as the Assam Police tried to proceed to Pu Chhandama's areca nut plantation to set up makeshift camp there.

 In the early morning of 30.6.2021 @ 3:00 a.m. SP, Hailakandi gave an ultimatum to Addl. SP, Kolasib to retreat from the area within 10 minutes. The stand-off lasted for several hours and later, as per the order of DC and SP, Hailakandi, Assam Police, who heavily outnumbered Mizoram Police at the area, forcefully cleared their path and stationed themselves at Pu Chhandama's areca nut plantation. 

 Dr. H. Lalthlangliana, DC Kolasib also arrived at Pu Chhandama's areca nut plantation and held a brief meeting with DC Hailakandi, who was already stationed at the area and both parties agreed to maintain status quo at the area at least till the Chief Secretaries of both states held a meeting on 9.7.2021.

****

**(c) 26.7.2021:** On 26.7.2021 @ 11:30 a.m. around 200 Assam Police led by Shri. Anurag Agarwal, IGP, Assam arrived at Vairengte Auto Stand, near Vairengte (inside Mizoram territory) and tried to forcefully push back 1st Mizo IR Battalion Personnel, who are on duty at the area. Pu Vanlalfaka Ralte, SP Kolasib and party immediately rushed to the area and tried to have a dialogue with Assam Police, but the Assam Police refused and informed him that the area falls within Assam territory and they intend to construct camp at the area.

  Later, Assam Police, who heavily outnumbered Mizoram Police at the area forcefully pushed back Mizoram Police and stationed themselves at Mizo IR Bn. camp. Civilians from Vairengte also arrived at the area, and a confrontation and scuffle took place between Assam Police and residents of Vairengte. Assam Police fired a number of Tear Gas and Smoke Bomb towards civilians of Vairengte and Mizoram Police and due to this, 7 (seven) persons sustained minor injuries.

  One Mizo couple, who were proceeding towards Mizoram by their "Swift" vehicle car were physically assaulted and attacked by unknown miscreants from Assam, and as a result of which, they sustained injury on their persons and their vehicle was badly damaged.

  Tension between the 2 (two) sides continued to escalate as Assam Police continued to forcefully occupy Mizo IR Bn. Camp and on the same evening @ around 4:40 p.m. several exchange of gunfire took place at the inter-state border as Assam Police first open fire towards Mizoram Police and civilians. The firing lasted for about 45 minutes and subsequently Assam Police abandoned the Camp and withdrew from the area.

There are reported casualties among Assam Police Personnel due to firing and there is one Hav. Lalbuatsaiha of 5th IR who sustained a minor injury of grazed gunshot wound on his arm among Mizoram Police. PC Lalmuansanga of Vairengte sustained bullet injury on his thigh and was evacuated to District Hospital, Kolasib.

  ****
