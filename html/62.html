WRITE UP ON RECENT ARREST OF FOREIGNERS (NEPALESE & ROHINGYAS) INSIDE MIZORAM  (Dt.8.5.19) :

 

1.         On 23.4.2019 @ 11:30pm, a Sumo Maxi cab bearing registration No. AS 01/BC-0951, carrying 8(eight) females passengers, proceeding towards Aizawl was stopped and detained at Vairengte Police Check Gate along with the driver. Upon questioning, the driver, Sojol Roy (38) S/o Kutinenu Roy of Kabuganj, Cachar, Assam told Vairengte police that one Amrul, owner of the vehicle directed him to pick up the girls from Sumo Counter, Silchar and drop them at Zemabawk Petrol Pump, Aizawl from where one unknown person was to pick them up.

 

            Particulars of the girls are as under :

 

                        1. Toyaba(22) D/o Soyid Salam

                        2. Rubina(20) D/o Soyid Salam

                        3. Tasmin(19) D/o Dilma Ahmed

                        4. Sokhu(18) D/o Shehel Ahmed

                        5. Noor Kayas Bengum(18) D/o Karim Mulla

                        6. Atariza(18) D/o Mutar Ahmed(L)

                        7. Samwar Begum(17) D/o Abu Subinyan(L)

                        8. Sominara Begum(15) D/o Hasim Mulla(L)

 

            Preliminary investigation revealed that all the detained Rohingya girls were refugees, staying in Kutupalong refugee camp, Chittagong Hills Tract, Bangladesh. The Rohingya girls were examined through an interpreter as they spoke Rohingya language which is Eastern Indo-Aryan language similar to Bengali.

 

            During de-briefing, Toyaba(22) D/o Soyid Salam, the eldest of them disclosed that all of them are refugee from Myanmar and stayed at Kutupalong refugee camp, Bangladesh. They were induced by two other refugees viz. Nurkayas and Toyaba, both staying at the same camp who are having contacts in Malaysia, with a promise of a suitable jobs. Though they at first declined the offer, they later agreed to go to Malaysia with a hope of getting better living conditions.

 

            She and her sister, Rubina left the camp on 17.4.2019 and when they reached Silchar, the other six girls were already there at the house, the area and owner of which they did not know.

 

            Vairengte Police Station case no. 42/2019 Dt.24.4.2019  u/s 14 Foreigners Act has been registered in this connection and the eight (8) female Rohingyas were forwarded to District Court, Kolasib  on 25.4.2019.They are now kept in a makeshift accommodation at Vairengte Police Station awaiting Court's/Government's direction for their deportation.

 

2.         Bawngkawn PS C/No.107/19 dt.24.4.2019 u/s 370(3)/468 IPC has been registered on the basis of FIR submitted by Dy.SP, CID(Crime) F. Lalthlamuana to the effect that based on his source and enquiries it was found that 69 Nepalese women stayed in different hotels within Aizawl city on 14.4.2019, twelve(12) Nepalese women were stopped at Zokhawthar by Assam Rifles on 13.4.2019. Nine(9) Nepalese crossed Indo-Myanmar border at Zokhawthar and three(3) Nepalese women stayed at Zokhawthar hotel. These Nepalese women are highly suspected to be a victim of human trafficking. During enquiry, it was found that some of them possess Nepal passport while some of them do not and are planning to cross Myanmar and seek employment in the Middle East countries. As per SDPO Champhai, it was learnt that two hundred and ten(210) Nepalese women already crossed Indo-Myanmar border at Zokhawthar in between 14.3.2018 and 13.4.2019 in different batches that possess valid Nepali passport and e-visa for visiting Myanmar. During enquiry, it is suspected that their movement into Mizoram is illegal trafficking into Myanmar for further employments in Middle East countries, those who do not possess Nepali passport in Aizawl were found to possess Nepali passport at Zokhawthar. Immigration seal bearing Sunauli Check Post located at Uttar Pradesh was forged at Aizawl. So, it is suspected that these women are being recruited for the purpose of exploitation in various forms. In the meantime, one Bal Bahadur was apprehended by Bawngkawn PS on 23.4.2019 and is highly suspected to be involved in human trafficking of these girls.

            All together, twenty three(23) Nepalese women were arrested in connection with this case. However, all these foreigners were deported on 7.5.2019.

Pi Denghliri of Edenthar Veng stated that within 2019, around 15/16 persons in groups (suspected to be victims of human trafficking), comprising of both males and females used to stay overnight at her residence 5 (five) to 6 (six) times already and she further stated that one person named Madiki of Tahan, Myanmar is the main culprit in facilitating these movements. Interrogations are going on at the time of writing this report to find out the real reasons behind these movements and all the 8 (eight) female suspected victims  are kept in Protective Home, Hunthar while the other 4 (four) Male suspected victims are kept in Bawngkawn Police station.
