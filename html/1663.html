MEASURES TO STRENGTHEN THE NATIONAL ANTI-TERRORISM FRAMEWORK

 

  1. Identification of the Institution and Structures required for an efficient anti-terrorism mechanism-deficiencies in the existing institutions and structure required.

 

  1. Counter Insurgency & Anti Terrorist School (CIATS)

Ministry of Home Affairs, Bureau of Police Research & Development (BPR&D), New Delhi may be asked to set up Counter Insurgency & Anti Terrorist School (CIATS) in Mizoram. CIAT School will provide training to all police personnel of the State on counter insurgency & jungle warfare with a view to tackle effectively the menace of insurgency and terrorism. 

 

(ii) Anti-Terrorist Squad (ATS)

ATS may be created by Government of Mizoram to counter the menace of terrorism in Mizoram. The ATS teams will be selected from highly trained personnel from Mizoram Police and must have a thorough knowledge of department policies and procedures. They will be trained to deploy against threats of terrorism, for crowd control, etc.  

 

The ATS will be equipped with specialized firearms including submachine guns, assault rifles, sniper rifles, riot control agents, etc. The ATS will be provided with specialized equipments like heavy body armour, ballistic shields, armoured vehicles, advanced night vision optics, etc

 

(iii)Counter-Insurgency and Jungle Warfare School (CIJW) in Vairengte, Mizoram

 

CIJW School, Vairengte is considered  as one of world's most prestigious anti-terrorist training institution. CIJW School is having trainees from several countries receiving counter insurgency and counter terrorist training.  It is suggested that all newly recruit armed police personnel and officers of ASI, SI, DySP of  Mizoram Police may undergo  regular  training course at CIJW School, Vairengte.

(b)Efficient Consequence management- The Coordinating mechanism at State-level that presently exists could be outlined and suggestions to improve the system may be made.

(a)Lead Intelligence Agency (LIA) Coordination/ Directional  Meeting:

 Assam Rifle and Border Security Force (BSF) have regularly conducted LIA Coordination Meeting in Aizawl. LIA Directional Meeting is also regularly conducted by BSF in Silchar, Assam. Officers from State Police, Security forces, paramilitary forces and intelligence agencies deployed within the state of Mizoram and Assam have attended such meetings.

(a)Subsidiary Multi Agency Centre (SMAC):

 (i)Mizoram Police have operationalised a Subsidiary Multi-Agency Centre (SMAC) since May, 2011. Regular SMAC meetings of Nodal Officers of the State police, security forces, paramilitary forces and intelligence agencies deployed within the State of  Mizoram are being conducted regularly at SIB Hqrs, Aizawl.

 

(ii)Intelligence inputs are being shared with various intelligence agencies all over India through MAC Threat Management System.

 

 

Suggestion to improve the System of SMAC in Mizoram:

 

 Real times sharing of intelligence inputs through Threat Management System (TMS) with other intelligence agencies are hampered due to frequent disruption of connectivity (internet) of the system. It is therefore suggested that suitable measure may be taken to plug such loophole.
