(ii) Recent development in IBB and influx of BD nationals/impact

 (a) Consequence upon the alleged encounter between Bangladesh Army backed Arakan Army (AA) and Kuki Chin National Army, inside Bangladesh since 16.11.2022, villagers (mostly Bawm tribe) from Sunsong, Salopui, Thingdawl and Chhaihkhiang villages of Bangladesh had fled their villages to take shelter inside Mizoram. As many as 272 refugees entered Bandukbanga village, Lawngtlai District on 20.11.2022. The total nos. of refugees from Bangladesh who are taking shelter inside Mizoram increased to 304 according to latest report.

 (b ) Pu JH. Pianga, President, United Bawm, Pang, Lau Association (UBPLA) held a Press Conference on 3.12.2022 at Lunglei Press Club and stated that BSF should not deport/push back refugees from Bangladesh as they are Indian citizen, who migrated to Bangladesh due to the resultant crisis of Independence movement in Mizoram, and appealed the State Government and residents of Mizoram to extend necessary assistance to Bangladesh refugees. He also stated that 6 (six) Mizo sub-clans in Bangladesh are fighting for statehood in Bangladesh due to alleged discrimination and deprivation of their various rights. In this regard, Kuki-Chin National Army (KNA) was formed to take necessary action on their demand, and incident of exchange of fire also took place between KNA and Bangladesh Army backed Arakan Army. He strongly condemned BSF for allegedly denying Bangladesh refugees from entering into Mizoram, and expressed his desire to look into the matter by authorities of Indian and Mizoram Government. He also appealed the State Government to provide assistance to Bangladesh refugees and to accommodate them at Uiphumtlang under Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC) area.

 (c) A meeting was held on 4.12.2022 at CYMA Committee Room to discuss matters relating to Bangladesh Refugees. The meeting was attended by leaders of Central Young Mizo Association (CYMA), Pu TJ Lalnuntluanga, Hon'ble Minister of State, Law &amp; Judicial, etc., Pu Lalrintluanga Sailo, Govt. Deputy Chief Whip and Pu D. Thangliana ex-MLA. The meeting strongly condemned the reported deportation of Bangladesh Refugees, who had already entered the State by BSF. The Meeting also expressed their desire to discuss the matter with concerned authorities of Central Government by Pu Zoramthanga, Hon'ble Chief Minister of Mizoram. The meeting further decided that necessary assistance should be given to the Bangladesh refugees by District authorities and various NGOs in Lawngtlai District.

(iii) KNF/KNA affairs

_kuki chin national front (KNF) :_

 This organisation was founded in the year 2008 by elements of Zo- ethnic living in Bangladesh as Kuki-Chin National Development Organisation (KNDO).

 Two years after its establishment, leaders of this organisation sought assistance from Kachin National Army (KNA), a militant outfit operating in Myanmar, for training which resulted in about 20-40 KNDO cadres receiving training from KNA during the year 2017-2020. After these cadres completed their training, their organisation was renamed as Kuki Chin National Front (KNF).

 The main objective of this organisation is to unite Zo-ethnic tribes including CHIN tribes living in Myanmar - Bangladesh bordering areas into one autonomous region having a development budget of their own from Bangladesh Government.

CAMPS: The Head Quarters of KNF is situated at SALOPI, Bandarbham district, near RUMA BAZAR in Bangladesh. Other camps are also established at Chittagong and Khakrasuri areas.

(ii) Indian insurgent groups (IIGs) in Myanmar and Bangladesh

 A couple of years back, Mizoram itself experiences low level insurgencies in the North Eastern regions. However, government of Mizoram and HPC(D) (the insurgent group form the northern parts) had signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) on 2nd April 2018. The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC-D was held on 12th April, 2018. Since then, Mizoram has been a peaceful state with no insurgent group of the state worth mentioning. As such, it may be stated that there are no insurgent groups of Mizoram in Myanmar and Bangladesh for the time being. However, below are the insurgent groups of neighbouring states who are having bases in Myanmar and Bangladesh -

1. National Liberation Front of Tripura (NLFT)

2.Various factions of National Socialist council of Nagalim (NSCN)

3. Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA)

NATIONAL LIBERATION FRONT OF TRIPURA (NLFT) :

 NLFT(BM) : With the arrest of Jacob Hrangkhawl S/S President NLFT(BM) on dt.06.07.2022, no arrangement had been made so far to take charge of the post of President or to replace him either. There had not been any recent activities or movements within Sazek area. However, it is learnt that at present they are stationed within Masalong area. It is further learnt that Bomton S/S Army Chief had expressed his anger with the arrest of Jacob Hrangkhawl from Aizawl while blaming the Mizoram Police over their alleged involvement with the Assam Rifles. Inputs was also received indicating that they may intend to engaged in subversive activities against the government of Mizoram in retaliation to the arrest of their President.

 NLFT(PD) : It may be mentioned that an operational team of CID (SB) apprehended PARIMAL DEBBARMA, @ Phankrak who is Commander-in-chief of NLFT (Parimal Debbarma faction) on 12.03.2021 in Aizawl and was produced at Aizawl Police Station on 13.03.2021. After observing all the required legal formalities, he was handed over to Tripura Police on 14.03.2021.

 Apart from the above mentioned incident, there has been no report of any NLFT (BM)/NLFT (PD) activities inside or across the bordering areas of Mizoram.

ZOMI REVOLUTIONARY ARMY (ZRA) EASTERN COMMAND :

 The operational areas of ZRA Eastern Command, who are commanded by SS Colonel Kham Min Thang includes the areas around Tidim,Tonzang and Chikha inside Myanmar.

 For a long period, ZRA had prohibited the establishment of any armed group within their operational areas. However, after the creation of Civil Disobedience Movement as a result of the 2021 military coup of Myanmar, several armed groups have sprung up which includes People Defence Force (PDF) Zoland inside the operational areas of ZRA. This greatly displeased ZRA who strongly opposed the activities of PDF Zoland, often resulting in threats and even violence against these PDF Zoland members.
