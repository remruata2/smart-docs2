ROHINGYA REFUGEE AT LUNGPUK AND KHAIKHY

 

            Information has been received that the number of Ralie villagers of Myanmar crossing the India-Myanmar international border to Lungpuk and Khaikhy villages on the evening of 21.5.2017 was 335 people (261 persons at Lungpuk village and 74 persons at Khaikhy village including 11 pregnant women and 7 malaria diagnosed patient). Ralie villagers, particularly Matu tribes had approached other neighbouring/nearby villages in Myanmar. As per information received, Ralie villagers staying at Lungpuk and Khaikhy villages intended to return to Ralie village as soon as possible. Ralie village is located 9 Kms. (approx.) from Khaikhy village and 18 Kms. from Lungpuk village.

            Mara Autonomous District Council (MADC) is set to provide four-days ration for these Ralie villagers who took shelter at Lungpuk and Khaikhy villages.

 

 

 

 

            On  24.5.2017 @ 9:00 a.m, a farewell function for refugees from Ralie village, Myanmar was organised at Lungpuk Community Hall. The farewell function was attended by Tuipang BDO, Police officers, Assam Rifles, Village Council and Lungpuk villagers. After the function, all the refugees staying in both Lungpuk and Khaikhy villages were dropped off at the Indo-Myanmar border by availing the vehicles of Mizoram Police, Assam Rifles, BDO Tuipang, etc. numbering about 10 (ten) vehicles @ 3:30 p.m and they returned to Ralie village. Representatives of refugees signed a document declaring that they all returned to Ralie village at their own will and that they were not subjected to any kind of mistreatment during their stay in India.
