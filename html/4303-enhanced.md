**05.03.2004****:** The second round of bilateral physical meeting between the two parties was conducted at Bethel Para Communiuty Centre in Ruma Upazila of Bandar district. This meeting marks a pivotal step towards resolving a conflict that has displaced hundreds and stoked tension in the region.

**02.04.2024****:** Around 25 (twenty five) armed cadres of Kuki-Chin National Army/ Kuki-Chin National Front (KNA/KNF) led by S/S Major Zorina Bawm of Sunsawng Village robbed Sonali Bank (one of the leading banks in Bangladesh) of Ruma Branch, Bandarban, Bangladesh. They looted about 1 crore 58 lakhs Taka from the bank. The Bank Manager, Nizam Uddin was abducted while the bank security personnel's arms consisting of 8 SKS rifles (also known as Chinese rifle), 2 Sub Machine Guns, 4 Shotguns and 415 rounds of ammunition were snatched away by the KNA/KNF cadres. The abducted Bank Manager, Nizam Uddin was released on 04.04.2024 at around 7:30 p.m.

**03.04.24 :** At around 12:08 p.m. (BDT), Sonali Bank and Krishi Bank of Thanchi Branches, Bandarban District were also robbed by a group 25 (twenty five) armed cadres of KNA/KNF led by S/S Major Sangpa Bawm. They looted 17 lakhs 50 thousand Taka from the two banks

**Myanmar issues :**

****26.3.2024**_:_Report has been received lately that the number of Myanmar Nationals seeking refuge in Mizoram due to the order of compulsory joining of the Myanmar Army by the State Administrative Council (Myanmar Army) have significantly increase in the past week. It is reported that as many as 763 (seven hundred sixty three) Myanmar Nationals had fled Myanmar and entered Zokhawthar as of 18.03.2024.**

**03.04.2024** : On 25.03.2024 and 26.03.2024 reports has been received that a number of Myanmar Nationals from Khumi community had entered Mizoram and reached Khawmawi Village, Lunglei District It is further learnt that during the same time, another group of Myanmar Nationals from Khumi communities had reached Diltlang South Village, Lawngtlai District. A total of **87** person stayed at Khawmawi village and**24** persons at Diltlang. These Myanmar Nationals are staying with their friends and families who had already settled in Khawmawi Village as well as Diltlang South Village.

**16.04.2024 :**On 16.04.2024 CYLA, Lawngtlai Kumpuan Committee issued a circular stating that the export of essential commodities including fuel into Myanmar had disturb the market of these essential commodities in Lawngtlai district and urged traders and shopkeepers to discontinue supply of essential commodities from Lawngtlai District to Myanmar.

****22.04.2024**_:_ On 19.04.2024 at around 04:30 p.m., Assam Rifles authorities of Zokhawthar Company Operating Base (COB) had reportedly informed the general public that the Friendship Bridge over Tiau River which connects India and Myanmar will be sealed indefinitely. Upon receiving the information, people on both sides of the bridge (India and Myanmar) had gathered near the bridge which however, was immediately dispersed by the district police without any major law and order issue. The bridge was later opened on 20.04.2024 from 01:00 p.m. even though movement of the public/residents across the gate is regulated by the Assam Rifles. Further information received stated that 23rd Sector Assam Rifles DIG and other officials who visited Zokhawthar on 20.04.2024 interacted with Zokhawthar Village leaders, during which the Assam Rifles officials reportedly informed the residents of Zokhawthar that the Friendly Bridge will temporarily stays open till the end of April 2024 after which it will be sealed from 1st May, 2024 as per instruction from higher authorities. In this regard, there is no report of protest or agitation from the public at the moment and the situation is closely monitored.**
