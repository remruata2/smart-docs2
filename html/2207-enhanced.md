**A. Historical Background Of KHAWZAWL**

  Khawzawl was first inhabited by the Hmar sub-clan of Khawzawl and it was named after them. Khawzawl is a grouping centre wherein the following villages of were grouped together since 20thNovember, 1968 as a result of declaration of Independence by Mizo National Front:

 1) Darngawn

 2) Zaingen

 3) Tuisen

 4) Arro

 5) Zuchhip

 6) Hmuncheng

 7) Neihdawn

 8) Ngaizawl

 9) Tualpui

 10) Khualen

 The first elected Village Council came into existence in 1969, soon after grouping of villages was completed. In the first elected Village Council of Khawzawl, there were 10 elected members with Pu Darleta as its first President. As time goes by and with the growth of population, at present Khawzawl is divided into eight (8) Village Councils.

  Khawzawl was under the administration of Administrative Officer (AO) for five (5) years from September, 1969 to July, 1974. In the year 1974, Community Development Block was inaugurated in Khawzawl which is still in existence till date. Prior to establishment of Khawzawl Civil Sub-Divisional Office in March 1999, Block Development Officer was the Administrative Head and at present Khawzawl area is administered by Sub- Divisional Officer (Civil).

**B. Demography of Khawzawl DISTRICT**

  As per 2011Indiacensus, Khawzawl had a population of 11,022; of which 5,616 are males while 5,406 are females. Population of Children with age of 0-6 years is 1746 which is 15.84 % of total population of Khawzawl (NT). In Khawzawl Notified Town, Female Sex Ratio is of 963 against state average of 976. Child Sex Ratio in Khawzawl is around 938 compared to Mizoram state average of 970. Literacy rate of Khawzawl city is 96.64 % higher than state average of 91.33 %. Male literacy is around 97.39 % while female literacy is 95.86 %.As per 2011 Census there were 2,306 households.

 At present, the number of households in the proposed district area is 11,387 and the strength of population within the proposed district is also 56,133.

**C. GEOGRAPHICAL BOUNDARY OF THE PROPOSED DISTRICT** 

 The geographical area of the proposed District is comprises of Tuichang and Lengteng Constituencies as well as part of Champhai North, Tuikum and Hrangturzo Assembly Constituencies.

**D. EXISTING GOVERNMENT OFFICES**

 Khawzawl is Sub-Divisional Headquarters in Civil Administration and is also Block Headquarters. As of now, there are three (3) offices which have been functioning in the capacity of the district level, namely they are Soil &amp; Water Conservation Department, Jawahar Navodaya Vidhialaya and Krishi Vigya Kendra (KVK). Apart from these offices, there are five (5) Division level offices within Khawzawl town namely; PWD, PHE, P&amp;E, Environment &amp; Forest (Wildlife), Horticulture. In addition to the aforementioned offices, There are also Sub- Divisional Offices of Agriculture, Minor Irrigation and Education, Police Department etc. Khawzawl is also a Headquarter of 2ndIR Battalion.

**F. CHRONOLOGICAL EVENTS :**

**1.** Dt.12 Sept 2008 : Declaration of Khawzawl District by Government of Mizoram vide Notification No. A.60011/21/95-GAD/pt

**2.** Dt.1.6.2017 : A team of Joint Students’ Union, Khawzawl (MSU Khawzawl, MZP, Khawzawl and Khawzawl Students’ Association) led by Pu Malsawmkima, President met Pu Zodintluanga, Hon’ble Minister; Pu R. Romawia, Hon’ble Minister and other officials on 1.6.2017 with regard to their demand for the implementation of Khawzawl District.

**3.** Dt.7.8.2017 : On 7.8.2017 @ 10:30 a.m, a Joint Meeting of all NGOs within Khawzawl town was held at BRC Training Hall, Khawzawl Vengthar under the aegis of Khawzawl District Implementing Demand Committee (KDIDC). Pu M.S. Dawngliana, Chairman, KDIDC chaired the meeting and around 56 (fifty six) members were present. The meeting passed the following resolutions :-

i) Demanded the Government to immediately implement Khawzawl District as a full fledged District as declared by Government of Mizoram in 2008.

ii) Demanded the Government to initiate the implementation of Khawzawl District before 12.8.2017 and threatened to organise Total Bandh on 14.8.2017 if positive response is not given by the Government.

 KDIDC submitted a copy of the Resolution to the Government of Mizoram through SDO(C), Khawzawl on 7.8.2017.

**4.** Dt. 14.8.2017 : Khawzawl District Implementing Demand Committee (KDIDC) and All NGOs, Khawzawl organised a Total Bandh on 14.8.2017 from 5:00 am to 5:00 pm within Khawzawl town. All commercial establishments, Educational Institutions within Khawzawl town were closed during the Total Bandh. However, around 25 (twenty five) vehicles were stranded along the National Highway connecting Khawzawl town till 4:40 pm. The Total Bandh was peacefully called off at 5:00 p.m.

**5.** Dt. 1.9.2017 : Khawzawl Welfare Committee (KWC) organised a discussion programme on 'Implementation of Khawzawl District' on 1.9.2017 @ 1:00 noon at Aijal Club. Pu Lalpianfela Chawngthu, President, KWC chaired the programme and around 50 (fifty) KWC members attended the programme. KWC also expressed their full support to the initiative taken by Khawzawl District Implementation Demand Committee (KDIDC).

**6.** Dt. 9.4.2018 : Khawzawl District Implementation Demand Committee (KDIDC) in a Press Release issued on 9.4.2018 highlighted that they had organised a public rally at Dinthar Bazar, Khawzawl, Champhai District on the morning demanding immediate implementation of Khawzawl as a full-fledged District. Around 300 (three hundred) residents of Khawzawl participated in the rally and passed the following resolutions:-

(i) The State Government must declare Khawzawl as a full-fledged District before a Model Code of Conduct is enforced in the State.

(ii) If the State Government implements Khawzawl as a full-fledged District, only Congress party may contest Tuichang Constituency for the upcoming Mizoram Legislative Assembly General Election, 2018. 

(iii) If Khawzawl is not declared as a full-fledged District along with other towns, Congress party may not take the trouble of contesting Election at Tuichang Constituency and KDIDC volunteers will take all necessary steps towards implementation of its demands.

****7.**  Dt. 17.8.2018 : In a meeting of Khawzawl District Implementation Demand Committee (KDIDC) on 17.8.2018 urged the ruling party ie, Congress party not to nominate/made their candidates at Tuichang Assembly Constituency for the forthcoming Assembly General Election, 2018 if Khawzawl District is not implemented as a full fledged district.**

8. Khawzawl District Implementation Demand Committee (KDIDC) organised Khawzawl District Implementation Demand Procession on 31.8.2018 @ 9:30 a.m. Around 600 (six hundred) people participated in the procession. A short function was held at Dinthar Bazar which was presided over Pu H. Chalkhuma, Sr. Vice President, KDIDC.

 Pu Malsawmdawngliana, Chairman, KDIDC in his speech highlighted that KDIDC will continue to take all the necessary initiative for the implementation of Khawzawl District. The procession ended at around 11:40 a.m.
