COMMENTS ON MEMORANDUM/ PUBLIC CONSENSUS SUBMITTED BY REPRESENTATIVES OF REANG (BRU) MIGRANTS

 

1.The MBDPF Charter of Demands is not considered by the Government of Central and State Government of Mizoram

Comments:

The State Government of Mizoram is initiating step to consider The Charter of demand of Mizoram Bru Displaced People Forum(MBDPF). However, without the financial & logistic support from the Central Government such demand cannot be considered during a short period of time.

 

2. The Rehabilitation Centre were selected by the Government of Mizoram only without consulting MBDPF leaders  and  90%  of the selected  resettlement villages are in  Mizo dominated towns and cities.

            &

3.The Repatriated  families are subjected to dump in scattered way in various remote place in three districts of Mamit, Kolasib and Lunglei.

 

Comments:   

The Bru people who fled Mizoram due to communal tension in 1997-1998 were given shelter in 6 refugee camps at the following places in North Tripura and were given free rations and others essential commodities at the rate fixed by the central government.

Name of Refugee Camps

(i) Naisingpara                                 (ii)Asapara

(iii)Khakchangpara                         (iv)Hazacherra

(v)Kaiskau                                         (vi)Hamsapara

The number of Bru migrants who are lodged in the above six camps in Tripura as per enumeration of the Bru Coordination Committee as on 30th August,2010 is 29625 (adult+minor)

It may be mentioned that before they left Mizoram in 1997, the Brus were living in 14 villages in Kolasib District, 11 villages in Lunglei District and 46 villages in Mamit district, spread over 9 Assembly Constituencies. (i.e Hachhek, Dampa, Mamit,Tuirial, Kolasib, Serlui, Lunglei South, Thorang, West Tuipui)

As per information available with Home Department, Government of Mizoram, the number of electors in the three districts of Lunglei, Mamit and Kolasib is 12,538, out of which 9,933 electors have been registered in 41 polling stations in Mamit District.

            It is, therefore natural that all displaced Brus be repatriated in their respective erstwhile villages which is scattered in three district of Mizoram viz. Mamit, Kolasib and Lunglei.

After considering all aspects of the issues involved, the State Government has decided to rehabilitate /resettle in the same village where they had originally settled; if that village is abandoned one, they would be rehabilitated/resettled in the Assembly Constituency where they were previously registered as Voters. And to the extent possible, they would be accommodated within the jurisdiction of the Poling Stations to which they once belong.

                                    &

6. Untimed season for cultivation of Jhuming and also a season of storm, rain, and landslide.

                                    &

7. No livelihood opportunity for survival after exhausting two years free rations and 85,000/- cash grant.

Comments:

 Livelihood opportunity for survival of the Bru migrants is adequately addressed in the Action Plan (Road Map-IV) for Repatriation & Rehabilitation of Reang(Bru)  prepared by Home Department, Government of Mizoram. However, the Bru migrants after they resettle in Mizoram should also work/fight for their own survival.  Arrangement of Perpetual supply of free rations to Bru resettled in Mizoram would be a huge challenge for a state like Mizoram.
