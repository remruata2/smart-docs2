                        The 4th round of Peace Dialogue was held on 28th April, 2017, and the 5th Round of Peace Dialogue was held on 21st & 22nd September, 2017 wherein the framework of agreement was worked out. The Peace Dialogue was concluded under conducive atmosphere. As scheduled, the Political Level Peace Talk between Mizoram State Government and HPC-D was held under a cordial environment at the State Guest House, Aizawl during 7th & 8th March, 2018 and the Peace Talk ended with a logical conclusion on 8.3.2018. The State Government and the HPC-D were represented by 10 (ten) members each. While the delegates of Mizoram Government were led by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Political Adviser to Chief Minister; Pu L.T. Hmar, Working Chairman, HPC-D led the HPC-D delegates during the two-day Peace Dialogue.

                        On 2.4.2018 @ 4:30 pm, Government of Mizoram and HPC-D signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) at the function held at the State Guest House, Aizawl. Shri. Arvind Ray, Chief Secretary of Mizoram and Pu H. Zosangbera, Chairman, HPC-D signed the MoS on behalf of the State Government and HPC-D respectively. Pu Lalbiakzama, Addl. Secretary, Home gave reports on the peace process and speeches were delivered by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Pu Lalthalien, Spokesperson, HPC-D during the function. The signing of MoS will bring an end to the over two-decades armed conflict of the HPC-D. The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC-D was held at CTI, Sesawng on 12th April, 2018 where around 104 Cadres and 12 Council Members of HPC(D) come to over ground in this ceremony.

(v) Need to strengthen mechanism of State Police to monitor activities and Visa violations by foreigners during their visit to the region :

(i)  All the District Foreigner Registration Officers i.e  Superintendent of Police  (SP) of all district of Mizoram(SP Mamit, SP Lawngtlai, SP Champhai, SP Lunglei, SP Siaha, SP Serchhip & SP Kolasib) except Superintendent of Police (SP) CID(SB), Mizoram (i.e., State Foreigner Registration Officer) should be made functional/operational for  providing services  to Foreign nationals like  issue of  Registration of Foreigner’s Certificate and also to enable them  to monitor the movement and activities of foreign nationals visiting their respective district. All the offices of the District SPs should also be strengthen with facilities (online/internet connectivity, etc.)  and trained manpower.

(ii) Foreigner Detention Centre should be set up in Mizoram to enable State authorities to house the deportees (overstaying of Foreigners, etc) and to ensure that they are kept in check until the deportation process is completed.
