AGENDA POINTS FOR ONLINE/VIRTUAL DIRECTIONAL LEVEL CO-ORDINATION MEETING OF LIA ON  8th   SEPTEMBER, 2021

(i)      Security Scenario in Barak Valley (Assam) and Mizoram with special reference to insurgency:

          After the signing of Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) between Government of Mizoram and HPC (D) in 2018, there has been no report of any active insurgency within the state.

 (iii)    Trans - border crimes and illegal movement through border including present status of Myanmarese refugees:

Myanmar refugees have started entering Mizoram since 12th Feb, 2021 in the wake of Military Coup in Myanmar. The first batch of Myanmar refugees entered Mizoram on 12th Feb, 2021 and accommodated at Sangau-II, Lawngtlai District, Mizoram. These Myanmar refugees are presently staying at different Districts of Mizoram, most of the refugees are staying with their Indian relatives while others stayed at Refugee/Relief Camp.

          As per our office record, as many as 10280 Myanmar refugees had      already entered the state of Mizoram, out of which (as on 7th September, 2021) 9485 refugees are currently staying within the state of Mizoram, 775 refugees had already returned to their country of origin and 20 refugees had left Mizoram and proceeded towards Delhi.

(v) Jehadi/Muslim Fundamentalist activities in Barak Valley and Movement of JMB and IIG groups across border and Indian Border States:

                   There has been no report of Jehadis or Muslim Fundamentalist activities inside Mizoram during the reported period i.e.,  w.e.f. 24th June, 2021 to 5th September, 2021.

(vii) Inter -agency co-operation:

          Mizoram police has been maintaining close coordination and connection with the various agencies operating within the state. Apart from LIA meetings, Subsidiary Multi Agency Centre (SMAC), official letters, phone calls, e-mail, manual contact, joint operation etc. are often utilised which strengthens the relations between all agencies involved.

          It may be worth mentioning that Joint Operation of CID (SB) and Sister Agency recovered and seized 5 lakhs tablets of Methamphetamine (v/o Rs. 25 crores at international smuggling rate and Rs. 10 crores at the local smuggling rate) on 26.8.2021 @3:15 p.m. and in this connection, Special Narcotic PS Case No. 20/21 Dt. 26.8.2021 u/s 22(c)/29 ND&PS Act was registered. In another joint anti-narcotics operation conducted by CID (SB) and Sister Agency on 5.9.2021 seized 25 Hawng of suspected Heroin No.4 kept inside soap cases (weighing 296.6 grams & value of Rs.12.5 lakhs in local market) from one Tata Sumo BR/R/No.MZ 02 A 0318, from the illegal possession of Lallianmawia (30) s/o of Roliana,  Khawmawi, Myanmar and in this regard, Bawngkawn PS C/No. 510/21 Dt.5.9.21 u/s 21 (c)/25 ND&PS Act, r/w Foreigners Act has been registered.
