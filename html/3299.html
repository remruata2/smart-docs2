A. Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt.14.3.2013

Truck service resumed at Bairabi: A halt in truck service caused by a scuffle between truck drivers and some youths of Bairabi was called off yesterday following peace talk held to solve the problem yesterday attended by leaders of MTOA, Aizawl, NGOs of Bairabi and Sub Dy. Commissioner of Bairabi and SDPO Kolasib.

 

B. Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt.14.3.2013

SP, Traffic Notice: The Traffic police notify that Fire Service vehicles are often late as some vehicles parked on the roadside blocked the way to reach the place of occurrence. In case of emergency, it reminds each Local Council to make sure that vehicles are parked in garages not on roadsides or non-parking areas. As such, lost of lives and properties will be reduced in case of outbreak of fire.   

 

C. Front page THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt.14.3.2013

3 years imprisonment for attempting to molest a child: Lalhuapzauva s/o Zatluanga of Saron Veng, Aizawl accused of attempting to molest a 9 years old girl on 22nd Feb. 2008 was sentenced to 3 years imprisonment by Gauhati High Court, Aizawl Bench.

            The girl was rescued by her family when Huapzauva attempted to molest her and a complaint was lodged to the Police. Regarding the case, Addl. Dist. & Session Judge-I K.L Liana had made an order sentencing the culprit 2 years  imprisonment and fined Rs. 2,000/- on 17th July, 2012. Huapzauva made an appeal on the court's verdict to Gauhati High Court, Aizawl Bench while the prosecution felt                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           the punishment too benign and made an appeal to the same court. Lalhuapzauva’s appeal was not accepted by Gauhati High Court, Aizawl Bench, but Government appeal was accepted and the imprisonment is extended from 2 years to 3 years.
