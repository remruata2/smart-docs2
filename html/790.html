WRITE UP ON CITIZENSHIP (AMENDMENT) BILL, 2016

CITIZENSHIP (AMENDMENT) BILL, 2016

            The main points of the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 may be highlighted as follows:

The Bill is intended to amend the Citizenship Act, 1955 to make illegal migrants who are Hindus, Sikhs, Buddhists, Jains, Parsis and Christians from Afghanistan, Bangladesh and Pakistan, eligible for citizenship.

Under the Act, one of the requirements for citizenship by naturalisation is that the applicant must have resided in India during the last 12 months, and for 11 of the previous 14 years.  The Bill aims to relax this 11 year requirement to six years for persons belonging to the above mentioned six religions and three countries.

The Bill provides that the registration of Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) cardholders may be cancelled if they violate any law.

 

ARTICLE 371G IN THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA, 1949:

371G. Special provision with respect to the State of  Mizoram Notwithstanding anything in this Constitution,

(a) no Act of President in respect of

(i) religious or social practices of the Mizos,

(ii) Mizo customary law and procedure,

(iii) administration of civil and criminal justice involving decisions according to Mizo customary law,

(iv) ownership and transfer of land, shall apply to the State of Mizoram unless the Legislative Assembly of the State of Mizoram by a resolution so decides: Provided that nothing in this clause shall apply to any Central Act in force in the union territory of Mizoram immediately before the commencement of the Constitution (Fifty third Amendment) Act, 1986 ;

(b) the Legislative Assembly of the State of Mizoram shall consist of not less than forty members.

            Referring to the above Article 371G of the Indian Constitution, it most likely appears that the article still prevents any individual from enjoying the above provisions under the Article though it may not prevent them from acquiring citizenship under the provisions of the Bill if it becomes an Act.

1. TOTAL BANDH IN PROTEST AGAINST CTIZENSHIP  (AMMENDMENT) BILL, 2016 ORGANISED BY MZP:

MZP Gen. Hqrs. organized a state-wide Total Bandh on 8.1.2019 from 5:00 a.m to 4:00 p.m along with Student Unions of other North East states under the umbrella of North East Students' Organisation (NESO) in protest against the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 purportedly to be passed by the Parliament. MZP Gen. Hqrs. deployed volunteers at 16 (sixteen) junctions/ points within Aizawl. Elaborate and systematic security arrangement was made by Mizoram Police. Information has been received that Government Offices, Educational Institutions, Shops, NGO’s offices, Oil filling stations, etc. remain closed during the bandh.

A copy of the said Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 was burnt by L. Ramdinliana Renthlei, President, MZP at Treasury Square, Aizawl @ 1:35 p.m.

It is also reported that as many as 37 (thirty seven) non-Mizos who were found loitering during the Total Bandh were produced by MZP volunteers at the nearest Police Stations for their safety till the bandh was called-off.

MZP Hqrs., Lunglei and Champhai also expressed their gratitude to the people of Lunglei and Champhai respectively for their cooperation and support during the Total Bandh by issuing a Press Release today. It is also reported that as requested by Mara Students' Organisation (MSO), Siaha, Government Offices and Educational Institutions in Siaha remain closed during the bandh.

Information has been received that the Total Bandh was also observed by Headquarters, Sub-Headquarters and Branches of MZP at their respective places. The bandh was called off by Darlawn Sub-Hqrs. @ 1:30 p.m, Khawbung and Dungtlang Sub-Hqrs. @ 1:40 p.m, Lungsen Sub-Hqrs. @ 2:30 p.m, West Phaileng Sub-Hqrs. @ 3:20 p.m at their respective places and at all other places @ 4:00 p.m.

MZP Gen. Hqrs. called off the Total Bandh @ 4:00 p.m as per schedule. There was no untoward incident reported during the bandh.

2.      PROCESSION IN PROTEST AGAINST CTIZENSHIP (AMMENDMENT) BILL, 2016 ORGANISED BY MZP:      

Thousands took to the streets in Aizawl and elsewhere in major towns of different districts across the State in protest against the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 on 23.1.2019. The procession was organised in pursuance of NESO's 'Protest Rally' programme earlier scheduled to be carried out in all North-east states before 30.1.2019. In Aizawl, about 15,000 (fifteen thousand) protesters, most of them students in their uniforms, participated in the procession. The procession, as earlier scheduled, started @ 11:00 a.m from Khatla and Zarkawt, and converged in front of Vanapa Hall. Slogans were raised and placards displayed denouncing the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016.
            Pu Vanlalruata, CYMA President in his speech, expressed fear that Mizos and other religious minorities of the NE states could easily be assimilated if the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 is passed by Rajya Sabha. The MZP President, L. Ramdinliana Renthlei who also addressed the crowd, threatened to take up arms and revive underground movement if the CAB is not passed in accordance with the wishes of the Mizo people. Announcing the NGOCC's decision to boycott the Republic Day, the MZP President also asked the students to stop coming to Lammual (AR Ground) to practice parade from tomorrow onward. Prominent among participants of today's procession were Pu Lalchhandama Ralte, Minister of State, i/c Education, etc.; Pu C. Lalsawivunga, MLA, ZPM; Pu V.L. Zaithanzama, MLA, ZPM and Pu Zoram Sangliana, Ex-Minister, INC. Pu Lalruatkima, Minister i/c Rural Development, etc. also participated in the procession at Champhai.

The Procession adopted  the following resolutions :-

i)         To safeguard our land against the foreigners, we the Mizos will fight till our last breath.

ii)        The Central Government should not implement the Citizenship(Amendment Bill), 2016 as it is unsafe for the people of North East.

iii)       If the Government implements Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016, we will assume that the Government is favouring the foreigners over its own people living in the North East.

iv)       If the Government turns deaf ears to our plea, we the Mizos will not sit idle.

                                    Effigies of Shri. Narendra Modi, Prime Minister of India and Shri. Rajnath Singh, Union Home Minister were burnt at the end of the procession and the crowd dispersed at 1:05 p.m without any untoward incident during the procession.

3.      BOYCOTT OF REPUBLIC DAY, 2019 BY NGOCC :

The celebratory function of the 70th Republic Day celebration, 2019 was boycotted by NGOCC in protest against the Citizenship (Ammendment) Bill, 2016 on 26.1.2019. The protestors, estimated to be around 250, gathered outside the 3 (three) entries of AR Lammual preventing civilians, students and others not listed in the discussion between the Government and the NGOCC held on 25.1.2019 from entering the venue.

The 70th Republic Day celebration, 2019 was held peacefully on 26.1.2019 @ 8:00 a.m at AR Lammual. The Hon’ble Governor of Mizoram, Pu Kummanam Rajasekharan unfurled the National Flag at 8:00 a.m as scheduled. The ceremonial parade was concluded without any untoward incident despite the strong demonstration by the NGOCC in their boycott of the celebration in protest against the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016. Dignitaries and Government officials who were listed to attend the function by the meeting between the Hon’ble Chief Minister and NGOCC attended the programme.

At other places in Mizoram, Republic Day celebration programmes were also carried out peacefully and no untoward report received.
