**INSURGENCY :**

_1.Hmar Peoples Convention (Democratic) -_

On 2.4.2018 @ 4:30 p.m, Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) at the function held at the State Guest House, Aizawl. The Arms Laying Ceremony of HPC-D was held at CTI, Sesawng on 13th April, 2018. Around 104 Cadres and 12 Council Members of HPC(D) come to over ground at this ceremony.

The rival faction of HPC(D), Lalhmingthanga Sanate’s faction designated themselves as ‘HPC(D) – President’s Group’ are still active outside Mizoram, but they have no activities inside Mizoram as of now. There are around 12 cadres/members in HPC(D)-President’s Group. Unconfirmed information indicated that their President is planning ways and means to co-operate with Jeliangrong United Front (JUF), a Naga outfit.

This splinter HPC (D) group operates in the bordering areas of Mizoram (Aizawl and Kolasib Districts) and Manipur/Assam states. However, they are hardly a threat for the ensuing MP election, 2019.

2. In the eastern side of Mizoram, CNF and ZRA are the main insurgent groups. These groups operates around the indo-Myanmar border areas,particularly Myanmar side. There has been no information of their intention to interfere in the coming election.

In the southern parts of Mizoram, under the jurisdiction of Siaha and Lawngtlai Districts, Myanmar insurgents such as the Arakan Army,Arakan Liberation Party and Chin National Front are operating in the indo-Myanmar border areas. Mizoram MP 2019 election :

1.CYMA Central Executive Committee (CEC) meeting held on 30.1.2019 at their office resolved to boycott the upcoming General Election of MP (Lok Sabha) 2019 if the authorities make any arrangement for Special Polling Booths at Kanhmun for Bru voters living in Tripura Transit Camps without paying heed to the demand of NGOCC. The NGOCC has earlier expressed their opposition to any arrangement other than making Bru voters cast their votes only at their native villages where they are enrolled in the Electoral Roll.

**In pursuance of CYMA Central Executive Committee (CEC) meeting held on 30.1.2019, _NGO Coordination Committee (NGOCC)_ meeting was held on 1.4.2019 at CYMA Office . The meeting resolved to organise State-wide indefinite Total Bandh w.e.f. 8.4.2019 to press the authorities for the implementation of their demand.**

NGOCC held a meeting again on the night of 3.4.2019 @ 8:30 p.m at CYMA Office and deliberated on the letter of the Chief Secretary to the NGOCC over their intended boycott of MP Election, 2019. However, the meeting expressed its gratitude towards the Government of Mizoram and the Ministry of Home Affairs for their commitment to complete the Bru repatriation process in 2019 and to take initiatives to end the special assistance given to the Brus who do not want to be repatriated. The Committee expressed its confidence towards the efforts proposed to be taken by the Government and hence called-off its proposal to boycott MP Election, 2019 along with an indefinite Total Bandh w.e.f. 8.4.2019.

2_._A statement was recently issued by Serhmun Relocation Committee about their intention to boycott the coming 2019 MP election.

According to the statement, unless they received assurance from the Government before the date of MP election 2019 that allocation funds for 225 house of their village will be release, families of these houses will boycott the election by not participating and casting their votes.

 Bru refugees :

 After an agreement was reached between Mizoram NGOCC and Election Commission of India, Bru refugees from the various transit camps inside Tripura will cast their votes in Kanhmun village (Mizoram). There are around 11086 Brus voters living in the transit camp out of which around 6000 voters are estimated to turnup on the polling day. However, it is believed that only around 500 light vehicles are allotted for the transportation of these Brus Voters which may be in adequate. Apart from these, unless sufficient water food and lodging for these Brus voters from the transit camp are organized, many of the Brus expressed the possibilities of hardship which can be faced on the polling day.

Mizoram People Forum :

 One of the unique features of Mizoram politics and Election is the presence of Mizoram People Forum, commonly known as MPF. This MPF is formed as a watch dog meant for any election process by the mutual agreement of all the political parties of Mizoram different church denomination and various NGO’s. The main task MPF is to have a clean and fair election and to prevent excessive expenditure and activities of all the political parties involves.

 In the past elections such as – Mizoram Legislative Assembly General Election 2008,2013, 2018; MP election 2009,2014; Village Council Election 2009,2012,2015 and Aizawl Municipal Corporation Election 2010,2015 and various district Council election, Mizoram people Forum and different Political parties have made an agreement which bore positive result. The MPF became know to people within and outside Mizoram because of these achievements as elections process became much more satisfactory, thanks to the cooperation of all involved.
