**B. Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 24/1/2013**

**2.Drug addiction caused an increase in burglary case**: According to Police, the increase of burglary and other crimes in Lunglei Dist. was due to the numbers of drug addicted person in the area.

 In a crime meeting held yesterday at his office for Lunglei District, The S.P of Lunglei Dr. VJ Chandran mentioned this and further expressed the need for the police to increase their efforts. He also emphasized the need for having good co-operation with the community to check the rise of rape case.

 During the year 2012, Lunglei Police registered 153 cases which is the lowest in a span of 10 years. Among these cases, burglary/theft occupied the highest percentage, with 8 rape cases. No murder case was registered during the year.

**Top of the table clash - Police versus Dinthar FC :**In the ongoing Mizoram Premiere league, The Mizoram Police and Dinthar FC will play a match today at 2:45 PM at A.R. playground which will decide the champion of this season. The Mizoram Police, with a point of 19 is leading by 4 points over 15 points of Dinthar FC. The Mizoram police will add 3 more points if they won the match, which will be rather difficult to compete for Dinthar in this season. Both the team coaches are confident of victory. The coach of Dinthar FC further claims that the police had never defeated them in their previous matches.

**C. Front page of MIZO AW newspaper Dt.24.1.2013**

****

**Achievement of Police :** The culprit in the house breaking case of Bawngkawn Chhim veng, Lalthlamuana (21) of Durtlang (Present address- Aizawl Venglai) was arrested as a result of police investigation. All the stolen articles including a laptop worth Rs. 20,000, a pair of shoes and a photon device were also recovered.

 The MNYF Gen. Hqrs. issued a press release yesterday i.e. Dt. 23/1/2013 highlighting an office bearers meeting of MNYF on Dt.22/1/2013 at MNYF office which was presided over by PC Laltlansanga President MNYF Gen. Hqrs.

 In their Press Release, the MNYF allegedly indicted the Congress ministry for not paying heed on the development of Mizoram during their current tenure and accused the ministry of incompetence. In the case of boundary between Mizoram and Assam, the MNYF mentioned their appreciation on the action of ZNP President Pu Lalduhawma in having the guts to resign from "Boundary Committee" which was not a genuine committee as it does not have a representative from MNF party and accused the same committee as ceding Mizoram area to Assam in their findings.

 The Press release further mentioned that for the purchase of computer tables, a sum of money amounting Rs. 6 lakhs was sanctioned to the police department. Instead of buying from an approved company, the police department manufactured these computer tables themselves by employing police constables. A bill of Rs. 5,000 per table was then submitted which was rather highly expensive for such an inferior hand-made articles. In this connection, MNYF asked the Hon'ble Home Minister if he is aware of this supply of low-grade hand-made articles, if so, the MNYF strongly condemned it, and if not aware, the MNYF allegedly accused the Home Minister of being not competent enough to look after his departments.

 The MNYF further demanded the Home minister to look into this matter as to identify the supplier and also to disclose the exact truth on this.
