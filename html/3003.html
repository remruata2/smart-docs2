SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/816

Dated: 4th November, 2019.

 

 

Information has been received that leaders of Bru NGOs claimed the following persons to have died due to starvation as a result of stoppage of free ration and cash dole by the Central Government:-

  1. John Chongprengh (2 yrs), s/o Dolendro Chongprengh, Naisingpara Camp, on
                      31.10.2019.
  2. Makato Reang (60 yrs), w/o Binoda Reang, Naisingpara Camp on 31.10.2019.
  3. Bristirung (1 yr), d/o Lomborai, Naisingpara Camp on 3.11.2019.
  4. Akosha (1 yr), s/o Moninam, Naisingpara Camp on 3.11.2019.
  5. Ojitrai (3 mnths), s/o Mergonam, Naisingpara Camp on 3.11.2019.
  6. Pigili Reang (4 mnths), d/o Khondno Reang, Hamsapara Camp on 4.11.2019.

 

In addition, the following persons were reported to be hospitalized at Dasda PHC, allegedly due to starvation:-

  1. Ramjoy Reang (65 yrs), s/o Mohanjoy Reang, Ashapara Camp on 1.11.2019.
  2. Khumbarboti (60 yrs), s/o Porbamoni Molshoy, Ashapara Camp on 3.11.2019.
  3. Dobokumar (55 yrs), Ashapara Camp on 4.11.2019.

 

Information has also been received that two dead bodies, viz. Bristirung (1 yr), d/o Lomborai, Naisingpara Camp & Akosha (1 yr), s/o Moninam, Naisingpara Camp, who both died on 3.11.2019, were exhumed today, i.e. 4.11.2019, for Post-Mortem Examination to ascertain the real cause of their death.

 

As per input, various Bru NGOs of Tripura and individuals have collected and distributed rice and cash assistance to inmates of Brus in 6 (six) Relief camps yesterday, i.e. 3.11.2019. Input received also indicated that a group of Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) members will distribute around 500 – 1000 quintals of rice to inmates of Bru Relief Camps on 5.11.2019 & 6.11.2019.

 

It is also learnt that Advocates of Tripura High Court visited Brus who are currently organising road blockade between Kanchanpur and Ananda Bazar yesterday, i.e. 3.11.2019 and Shri. Alok Kumar, IAS, Principal Secretary and Pu L. Darlong, Joint Secretary, Revenue Department, Govt. of Tripura are intending to visit the same today, i.e. 4.11.2019 and a meeting with leaders of Bru NGOs will be convened. In addition, Advocates of Supreme Court are also intending to visit Bru Relief Camps on 6.11.2019.

The roadblock organised by Bru NGOs have stalled the repatriation process except few families from Kaskau, Khakchangpara and Hamsapara Relief Camps, who have directly reported themselves to Liaison Officer at Kanhmun for repatriation till 2.11.2019. No one reported for repatriation as on today, i.e. 4.11.2019.

 

SP, Dharmanagar has called a meeting with leaders of various Bru NGOs at his office earlier today, i.e. 4.11.2019 @ 12:00 noon in connection with the road blockade by Bru refugees. SP was learnt to have warned the Bru NGO leaders to stop their agitation or else action would be taken against them. Bru NGO leaders however refused to back down despite the SP's warning, and showed no sign of calling off their agitation just yet.

 

Information has also been received that Bru NGO leaders are intending to carry on with the roadblock until and unless their demands are being met. The road blockade has been carried out since 31.10.2019 in protest against the stoppage of ration and cash-dole since 1st October, 2019. At the same time, certain Bru NGOs and individuals of both Tripura and Mizoram are intending to give relief aid to Brus residing in Tripura Relief Camps.

 

Situation is under close watch.
