ROHINGYAS ISSUES

Introduction:

             The Rohingyas crisis has deepened due to the violent acts of the Muslim insurgent group acting under the banner of Arakan Rohingya Salvation Army (ARSA) on 25th August, 2017. The subsequent offensive operation launched by Myanmar Army against this Muslim insurgents resulted in the death of hundreds of people and exodus of a fresh wave of Rohingyas from Rakhine State, Myanmar into the neighboring Bangladesh.

            This has opened a new and complicated dimension to the already vexed issue of the Rohingyas. The vulnerability of the Rohingya migrants to radicalization from forces inimical to India can pose a security threat to the state of Mizoram. 

 

Vulnerability of Mizoram border:

            Mizoram has a long porous international border with two countries (512 kms with Myanmar and 318 kms with Bangladesh).  It is reported that over 2,70,000 Rohingyas refugees have recently entered Bangladesh through border points of Chittagong  Division (Bandarban, Rangamati&Khagrachhari  Cox Bazar, etc).  As Mizoram shares a long porous border with Chittagong Division, further infiltration of Rohingyas migrants/refugees from Bangladesh to India through the border of Mizoram must be prevented at all cost.

Border points i.e B.P.1 to B.P.17 of Mizoram-Myanmar border have also become vulnerable points for infiltration of Rohingyas migrants coming fromRakhine State through Southern Chin State of Myanmar. In addition, big infrastructure project like Kaladan Multi-Modal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP), being implemented under Act East Policy will automatically increase chances of infiltration of foreigners  including Rohingyas across  these border points.

 

Suggested measures to be taken for prevention of illegal entry of Rohingyas

  1. Strengthening of Security and Intelligence Network:

There are 3 (three) Battalionsof Assam Rifles deployed along the Indo-Myanmar border and 3 (three) Battalions of BSF deployed along Indo-Bangladesh border. In addition, the State government has deployed Mizoram Police (MAP/IR Bn.) along the border supplementing the Central Armed Forces.

Apart from strengthening deployment of Border Security Force (BSF) and Assam Rifle along the border. It  is also necessary to set-up new Police Stations/Out post (with the financial assistance of Central government)  within this areas  to act as  a second layer behind the BSF/Assam Rifle for  keeping  effective watch on the infiltration of Rohingyas migrants and other cross border criminal activities.

 

  1. Fencing of Mizoram-Bangladesh border:

In order to prevent illegal migration and illegal activities including anti-national activities from across the border, the Government of India had sanctioned the construction of border fencing with floodlights in two phases. The total length of Indo-Bangladesh border sanctioned for fencing is 3326.14 km; out of which about 2731 km of fencing has so far been completed (up to 31.12.2016). The fencing work should be expedited.

 

  1. Sensitization of Law Enforcement Agencies and Intelligence agencies:

             Mizoram Police, etc should be sensitized about Rohingyas crisis so as to enable them to take prompt steps in identifying the illegal migrants and to initiate the process of deportation, etc. This could be achieved through Seminar, Workshop, etc

 

  1. Detection and deportation of foreigners along Mizoram-Bangladesh/ Mizoram-Myanmar Border :
  1. Special  Task Force (STF)  : In order to detect and deport settling down of  illegal immigrants of Bangladesh nationals in Mizoram , 3(three) Special  Task Force(STF)  had already been set up in three districts of Mizoram namely Lunglei District, Mamit District and Lawngtlai District as desired by Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. Each STP is headed by Deputy Commissioner of the concerned District.

            Similarly, Special Task Force (specially for Rohingyas migrants of Myanmar) may also be set up at the district level to identify and deport illegal staying  of Rohingyas migrants inside the territory of  Mizoram.

 

  1. Arrest of Rohingyas by Mizoram Police:

            Till today i.e, 30.12.2019, no Rohingyas refugees from Rakhine State of Myanmar have taken shelter in any part of Mizoram. However, there are 32 illegal immigrants (ie, Rohingya) apprehended in the state of Mizoram. It may be mentioned that necessary action for their deportation process has already been initiated and instruction from Ministry of Home Affairs and the Ministry of External Affairs, New Delhi is still awaited.
