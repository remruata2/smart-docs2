A. Front page of  NEWSLINK/The Zozam Times/The Aizawl Post/Tawrh Bawm                    newspapers Dt.19.02.2013

      Police officials rap mob attack: Some police officials expressed deep regrets over the

     saturday night's mob attack on the Aizawl police station.

              Talking to reporters, the S.P of of Aizawl LR Dingliana said he could not        understand how  the police station was attacked not for the inefficiency of the       police,         but for wanting to see a criminal. Other police officials also said that they could    not     considered the motive of the mob attack as a good reason and further said that previously angry crowd had gathered in front of the police station but  had never      attack          like on saturday night.

              Dismissing allegations that the police pelted stones first, the police officials said     stones were never the weapons of the police.

     C. Front page of The Aizawl Post newspaper Dt.19 02.2013

     Only three strikes/bandh in Mizoram: According to the reports of Bureau of Police         Research and Development under the Union Home Ministry, the state of Mizoram experiences only three strikes/bandh during the year 2011. The reports indicated the lowest number of  strike was in the state of Sikkim where there were no report of strike          at all within the year. Sikkim is followed by the state of Tripura with two strikes and next          comes Mizoram with a record of only three strikes/bandh. Meanwhile, Tamil Nadu      occupies the highest position with a record of 15,746 of such strikes organized by   various political parties and organizations.
