LOK SABHA MP ELECTIONS BOYCOTTED BY NGOCC

 

*          An emergent meeting of NGO co-ordination committee was held on 4.4.2014 @12.00 noon at Central YMA committee room, Tuikhuahtlang which was attended by representatives from CYMA (Central Young Mizo Association), MZP (Mizo Zirlai Pawl), MSU (Mizo Students Union), MHIP (Mizo Hmeichhe Insuihkhawm Pawl), MUP (Mizoram Upa Pawl) resolved to organize a 72 hours Total Bandh with effect from 5:00 AM of 7th April, 2014 to 5:00 AM of 10th April, 2014 throughout the State in protest against casting of votes by Bru refugees in their respective refugee camps, Tripura and to prevent counting of Bru refugee votes. The meeting further decided to request MP candidates not to accept the result if Bru refugee votes is the deciding factor in the election.

*          The Statewide 72 hours total bandh called by NGO Coordination Committee in Mizoram in demand of ‘complete cancellation of votes already cast by Reang (Bru) at six (6) relief camps in Tripura and re-polling of Bru votes at special booths on the soil of Mizoram’ has been evoking an overwhelming support from the public with business establishments, educational institutions remaining closed in most parts of the State. The Bandh has affected the ongoing electoral poll process as polling parties could not leave their places of duty due to road blockade and stiff resistance from the NGOs volunteers in Aizawl, Mamit, Serchhip, Champhai, Kolasib, Lunglei. However, there was no report of any major untoward incident from any part of the State till 5:00 PM on 7th April 2014. The brief reports from the District headquarters are as follows :-

(i)        Aizawl District :

The 72 hours total bandh has been strictly enforced in Aizawl since 5:00AM and no polling parties could leave for their places of duty so far. NGOs volunteers organized picketing duties at different locations within Aizawl city blocking vehicular movements and other activities. All commercial establishments, educational institutions are closed and the street look deserted due to the total bandh. All the security force/police officials along with Sector magistrates turned up in time however, polling officials, drivers, etc. could not turn up due to strong pressure from the NGOs volunteers.

(ii)       Kolasib District :

The 72 hours total bandh also received an overwhelming support from the general masses with commercial shops, educational institutions remaining closed. All 9 (nine) polling parties are also unable to proceed to their respective places of duty due to the total bandh.

(iii)     Lawngtlai District :

The 72 hours total bandh is not organized throughout the District except in Chawngte village where the bandh is organized during 8:00 AM-12:00 noon. Normal life is not affected in Lawngtlai town.  29 polling parties left Lawngtlai in the morning at 6:00 AM and due to stiff resistance at Khawmawi, Lunglei District by NGOs volunteers, some of these polling parties were asked to change their route via Rulkual and 50 polling parties also left for their places of duties at 10:00 AM. Around 20 polling parties also left at 1:00 PM on this day.

(iv)      Champhai District :

In the district headquarters at Champhai, the total bandh evoked an overwhelming support from the public with commercial shops, educational institutions remaining closed within the town. All the 13 polling parties scheduled to proceed to their respective polling stations today could not leave Champhai due to stiff resistance from the NGO volunteers.

(v)       Mamit District :

Bandh also received a good support from the public and after the 37 polling parties with escort failed to cross the barricade of local NGO volunteers at Mamit Dinthar locality, all the polling parties returned back to the Office of DEO, Mamit. All commercial establishments, educational institutions remain closed due to the bandh.

(vi)      Lunglei District :

The total bandh also received a good response from the public with all vehicles remained off the road and shops, educational institutions remained closed.

In Lunglei District, polling parties for the following 16 (sixteen) polling stations had already reached their destination on the night of 6.4.2014 :- (i) Kawnpui ‘W’ (ii)Marpara S-II (iii) Marpara S-I (iv) Darngawn (v) Terabonia (vi) Bunghmun (vii) New Sachan (viii) Sesawm (ix) Mauzam (x) Laisawral (xi) Tuikawi (xii) Old Sachan (xiii) Sumasumi (xiv) Ukdasury (xv) Kawizau (xvi) Samuksury.

(vii)    Serchhip District :

The total bandh also received an overwhelming support from the public with commercial shops, educational institutions remaining closed within the town. 77 polling parties are scheduled to leave Serchhip town on the next day, ie, 8.4.2014

(viii)   Saiha District :

In the District headquarters at Saiha, the total bandh is not observed on 7.4.2014 and all the 52 polling parties left for their respective places of polling stations on 7.4.2014 as per schedule.

           

*         The NGO Co-ordination Committee has called off the ongoing total bandh on 8.4.2014 at 1:30 PM after getting the following assurance points from Election Commission of India (ECI) as well as from Government of Mizoram :-

(i)          That the Election Commission of India ‘decided that provision would be made during future elections to the Lok Sabha and the Legislative Assembly of the State of Mizoram, for exercise of franchise within the State of Mizoram by Bru (Reang) voters living in Tripura enrolled in voter lists of Mizoram’.

(ii)         That the Election Commission of India ‘decided that the date of poll shall be re-scheduled from 9th April, 2014 to 11th  April, 2014 with the date of re-poll, if any, on 14th April, 2014’.

(iii)        That the Government of Mizoram is committed to take necessary steps for repatriation of genuine Bru refugees residing in Transit Camps, Tripura within 3 (three) months after election process if over i.e. 16th May, 2014.

(iv)        That the Government of Mizoram is committed to take necessary steps for deletion of Bru voters from Mizoram Electoral Roll who refused to return back to Mizoram inspite of appeal made by Government of Mizoram.

PRESENT SITUATION :

Information received indicated that Election Commission of India (ECI) is preparing to set-up Special Polling Stations at Kanhmun for all the voters of Bru of Tripura Rufugee Camps who are enrolled in Electoral Roll in Mizoram to cast their votes for the forthcoming Lok Sabha MP Election, 2019.

It may also be mentioned that NGO Coordination Committee (NGOCC) meeting held on 13.03.2019 @ 1:00 p.m had demanded that Brus in Tripura Transit Camps should cast their votes in their respective Polling Stations in the upcoming MP (Lok Sabha) Election, 2019. The meeting also decided to object any facilitation attempt through arrangement of Special Polling Stations, similar to what was done in the last State Assembly Election held last year, ie, 2018 by setting up Special Polling Stations at Kanhmun, Mamit District. A Press Statement was also released in this regard in which they stated that they will be taking measures so that MP Election will not be conducted in the State.

Information has been received that NGO Coordination Committee (NGOCC) meeting was held at 1.4.2019 @ 11:00 a.m at CYMA Office which was presided over by Pu Vanlalruata, Chairman and attended by 20 (twenty) members from its organisations except representatives of Mizo Students' Union (MSU) Gen. Hqrs. The meeting resolved to organise State-wide Total bandh w.e.f. 8.4.2019 demanding the authority to make Bru voters at Tripura Transit Camps to cast their votes only at their native villages where they are enrolled in the Electoral Roll and to protest against the ongoing arrangement of Special Polling Booths at Kanhmun for Bru voters by the Election officials and the Government.

Information has been received that NGO Coordination Committee (NGOCC) meeting was held on 1.4.2019 @ 11:00 a.m at CYMA Office which was presided over by Pu Vanlalruata, Chairman and attended by 20 (twenty) members from its organisations except representatives of Mizo Students’ Union (MSU) Gen. Hqrs. The meeting deliberated on their future course of action if authority takes any arrangement of Special Polling Booths for Bru voters in Tripura Transit Camps to cast their votes in the upcoming General Election of MP (Lok Sabha), 2019. The meeting resolved the following and issued a Press Release to this effect on 1.4.2019 itself :-

  1. The NGOCC reiterated their demand of making Bru voters in Tripura Transit Camps to cast their votes only at their native villages where they are enrolled in the Electoral Roll and opposed the ongoing preparation of setting up of Special Polling Booths for Bru voters at Kanhmun by the Election officials and the Government. The meeting further resolved to organise State-wide indefinite Total Bandh w.e.f. 8.4.2019 to press the authorities for the implementation of their demand.
  2. The NGOCC further requested commercial and private vehicles already engaged for transporting security personnel for the election duty not to continue their service and further requested the vehicle owners not to let their vehicles in connection with any election matters.
  3. The NGOCC requested the people of Mizoram to support their intention to organise the Total Bandh.

            Consequently, Central YMA convened a meeting on 1.4.2019 @ 1:00 p.m at their office which was presided over by Pu Vanlalruata, President and attended by about 50 (fifty) members including CYMA OBs, leaders of Sub-Hqrs. and Groups which are under direct control of CYMA. The meeting expressed their support of the NGOCC’s decision of organising State-wide indefinite Total Bandh w.e.f. 8.4.2019 and further requested its Sub-Hqrs., Groups and Branches to coordinate with other NGOs at their respective places and chalk out their course of action. A Press Release to this effect was also issued today. Information received indicated that the following Sub-Hqrs. YMA leaders were absent in today’s meeting :-
