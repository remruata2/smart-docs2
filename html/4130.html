A. Front page of  VANGLAINI  newspaper  Dt.24.04.2024

35.20% of post laying vacant under Mizoram Government: As per the 'Statement on Salary & Employment Data in Government, PSUs and aided institutions (2024-2025)' released by Mizoram Government on 27th February 2024, there are currently a total number of 71,682 posts under Mizoram Government which are divided into group A,B,C and D. Out of the total post 46,452 (64.89%) are filled up while the remaining 25,230 (35.20%) are laying vacant. In all the PSU and aided institutions under Mizoram Government, 77.86% of sanction post of group A are filled up while the percentage of post filled up under group B is 66.85%. As for group C and D, the percentage of sanction post filled up are 58.57% and 63.10% respectively.

 

 

 

B. Front page  of  VANGLAINI newspaper  Dt.24.04.2024

456 houses damaged by hailstorm : The recent heavy rainfall accompanied by hailstorm in Mizoram state resulted in the damaged of 456 houses in different parts of the state with one person injured. According to the records of Disaster Management and Rehabilitation (DMR) Department, the destruction caused by the hailstorm were found mainly in the districts of  Aizawl, Kolasib, Champhai and Khawzawl. In Kolasib district, 265 houses were damaged with 13,976 residents affected while the number of houses damaged within Aizawl district were 178. Apart from residences, church buildings, anganwadi and government buildings were also damaged by the rain and storm. As per their requirements, tarpaulins have been distributed to the victims while rehabilitation/assistances are also to be provided under State Disaster Response Fund (SDRF).

 

 

 

C. Front page  of  VANGLAINI newspaper  Dt.24.04.2024

Traffic police statement : In a statement issued recently, Aizawl Traffic Police appealed the public to be careful in parking their vehicles in the streets so as not to disrupt the swift movement of fire engine within the city in case of an emergencies. The statement also requested the public to display their contact numbers on their parked vehicles for easy contact by the police in case of any emergency situations.
