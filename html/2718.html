SEQUENCE OF INCIDENT AT ZOPHAI NEAR BAIRABI

 (ACTIVITIES OF MZP) AT MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER

 

27.2.2018     On 27.2.2018, around 50 (fifty) members of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) from Aizawl and Bairabi constructed "Zofate Chawlhbuk" (Shed) at Zophai near Bairabi located at Mizoram-Assam border. The construction work of 12x6 feet shed was reportedly completed at around 4:00 p.m followed by a short function held at the Shed under the chairmanship of L. Ramdinliana Renthlei, President, MZP. It was also reported that Assam Police, Assam Forest Deptt. Staff and Mizoram Police commanded by Commandant Ist IR Bn., Mualvum were present at the spot during the construction.

 

27.02.2018

9:25 p.m       It has been reported that 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' constructed by Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) on 27.2.2018 at Zophai located at Mizoram-Assam border has been demolished by unknown miscreants reportedly with an excavator on the night of 27.2.2018 at around 9:25 p.m, barely five (5) hours after it was constructed by MZP. In this regard, an FIR has been lodged at Bairabi Police Station by Ricky Zorinsanga, President, MZP Sub-Headquarters, Bairabi on the night itself.

 

02.03.2018   As reported, Zofate Chawlhbuk, constructed by MZP on 27.2.2018 was demolished by some persons reportedly at the instance of Assam Police on the same night it was constructed and the Police Duty Post erected by Assam Police at the same disputed site on 2.3.2018 was also dismantled by some persons reportedly under the aegis of Mizo Zirlai Pawl, a few hours after it was constructed.

 

08.03.2018  

7:00 am        On 8.3.2018 @ 7:00 a.m, members of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) numbering around 70 (seventy) under the leadership of L. Ramdinliana, President, MZP left Aizawl for Zophai near Bairabi, Kolasib District with an intention to reconstruct Zofate Chawlhbuk (Shed) at the disputed site along Mizoram-Assam border.

 

08.03.2018

11:45 am      At around 11:45 a.m, a minor scuffle broke out between the Student union and Assam Police when some members of the student union tried to unload their building materials from the vehicle. The Assam Police had even resorted to Lathi-charge as a result of which around 15 (fifteen) MZP members reportedly sustained minor injuries.

                               The Additional Deputy Commissioner, Hailakandi District, Assam and leaders of MZP hold a brief talk at the site wherein the Student leaders were reportedly asked by the Addl. DC not to go ahead with their intention to reconstruct a Shed at the site but to leave the matter in the hands of Higher officials of the two States.

 

08.03.2018   The Government of Mizoram promptly sent Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, Political Adviser to Chief Minister and other officials on the night of 8.3.2018 itself to prevent further escalation of dispute at the site.

 

 

09.03.2018

6:00 p.m       On 9.3.2018 at around 6:00 p.m, Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, Political Adviser to Chief Minister; Pu Lalbiakzama, Addl. Secretary, Home Department; DC, Kolasib and SP, Kolasib held a meeting with leaders of MZP at the residence of Pu Sanghmingthanga, Bairabi. The Government officials advised leaders of MZP to approach the Court with matters regarding the land dispute to tackle the issue legally. Appeals have been reportedly made to the students not to go ahead with their intention to reconstruct the shed at Zophai in order to avoid further escalation of Law and Order problems and to leave the issue in the hands of higher officials between the two states. In this regard, it has been reported that leaders of MZP appealed and appraised the proposal to their members present at the disputed site. In order to prevent any untoward incident, hundreds of Mizoram Police under the command of SP, Kolasib and Commandant, 1st IR Bn. are performing duty in and around the disputed site.

 

10.03.2018

11:00 a.m     On 10.3.2018 @ around 11:00 a.m, a scuffle broke out again between Assam Police and members of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) when around 100 (hundred) strong MZP members proceeded to the disputed site at Zophai near Bairabi along Assam - Mizoram border with their building materials to reconstruct ‘Zofate Chawlhbuk’ at the site. The students were intercepted by Assam Police before they reached the disputed site and they subsequently used force to disperse them. It has been reported that the Assam Police initially used Lathi but later on resorted to firing and around 8 (eight) shots were reportedly fired as a result of which one Ricky Lalchhandama, Secretary i/c Establishment, MZP reportedly sustained gunshot injury on his right ankle and around 49 (forty nine) others sustained injuries on their persons. Out of 49 (forty nine) injured MZP members, 7 (seven) of them reportedly sustained serious injuries and were referred to other Hospitals for necessary treatment. The Assam Police burnt down the temporary shed constructed by the MZP near the disputed site and the following vehicles belonging to MZP members were also ransacked by Assam Police :-

(1) Sumo - 1 no. (2) Taxi            -1 no. (3) Car (Nano) - 1 no. (4) M/Cycles -3 nos.

 

10.03.2018

12:30 pm     After they were forcefully dispersed by the Assam Police, at around 12:30 p.m, the MZP members gathered in front of Bairabi Police Station wherein they expressed their anger to the Mizoram Police for their inaction in the wake of atrocities committed against them by Assam Police.

                             

10.03.2018

06:00 pm     It has also been reported that MZP members except 5 (five) Office Bearers have left Zophai, Bairabi at around 6:00 p.m in the evening. However, the 5 (five) OBs led by L. Ramdinliana Renthlei, President, MZP were remain stationed at Bairabi reportedly to assess any development along the disputed site. Further input indicated that leaders of MZP have retrieved the damaged vehicles under the protection of Mizoram Police.

 

 

 

11.03.2018   On 11.3.2018, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) held an Emergency Meeting at MZP office, Aizawl under the chairmanship of Pu B. Vanlaltana, Vice President, MZP and was attended by 78 (seventy eight) MZP members. The meeting issued a Press Release wherein they expressed their strong determination to protect the territory of the State. The State Government have been neglecting the border issue and there is no other option left for the MZP but to take such courageous steps towards protection of the State Boundary, the PR added. In order to show their strong intention to protect the boundary of the State, MZP has decided to organise a procession called ‘Ram Humhalh Kawngzawh’ in Aizawl and at all other MZP Hqrs. & Sub-Hqrs. on 12.3.2018.

 

 

12.03.2018

10:30am      Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) organised a procession at Aizawl to express their strong condemnation of the State Government for its lack of action regarding the border issue. The procession began at 10:30 a.m, where around 4000 people marched from Chanmari in the North. At the same time, around 3500 people also marched from Sikulpuikawn in the South and the two parties converged in front of  Vanapa Hall. The following resolutions were passed by the procession :-

                      

                       1) MZP strongly condemned promulgation of 144 Cr.PC on the soil of Mizoram by Government of Assam. MZP strongly condemned Government of Mizoram for turning a deaf ear to them while being attacked by Assam Police.

                      

                       2) Government of Mizoram should safeguard its own territory.

                      

                       3) That the Mizo youths will fight till the end.

                      

                       4) 'Zofate Chawlhbuk’ (Waiting Shed) shall be reconstructed again.

 

                              The procession peacefully dispersed at 1:30 pm in the afternoon.

 

                              Executive Meeting of MZP will be held on 13.3.2018 (Tuesday) to chalk out their future course of actions.

 

                              Information has been received indicated that MZP is intending to reconstruct 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' on 14.3.2014 (Wednesday).

 

                       On 12.3.2018 @ 10:30 a.m, a procession called ‘Ram Humhalh Kawngzawh’ was organised under the aegis of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) in Aizawl and in other towns of the State such as Lunglei, Champhai, Serchhip, Kolasib, Thenzawl, Vairengte, Sairang, Saitual, Tlabung, Bairabi, Bilkhawthlir, Zawlnuam and Mamit wherein a large number of students took to the streets. Situation was peaceful in all the towns and it has been reported that an effigy of Shri. Adil Khan, DC of Hailakandi District, who had reportedly given the firing order against MZP members on 10.3.2018, was burnt by MZP at Tlabung.

 

                                          In Aizawl, the procession began at 10:30 am from Chandmari in the North and from Sikulpuikawn in the South and converged at Vanapa Hall. Around 6,000 (six thousand) students participated in the procession who shouted slogans condemning the State Government for its alleged inaction relating to the border issue and their strong intention to construct Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai. A function was held in front of Vanapa Hall under the chairmanship of Michael Lalramsanga, Treasurer, MZP Gen. Hqrs. wherein  Lalnunmawia Pautu, Gen. Secy., MZP; B. Vanlaltana, Vice President, MZP; R.B. Lalmalsawma, President, MSU and Zorammawia, Vice President, Aizawl City Colleges Joint Students’ Union (ACCJSU) and Pu Vanlalruata, President, CYMA delivered speeches. Adamant with their intention to reconstruct Zofate Chawlhbuk, B. Vanlaltana, Vice President, MZP announced before the gathering that MZP will reconstruct 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' at Zophai on 14.3.2018 (Wednesday). They also appealed all political parties of the State not to politicise the issue in order to gain political advantage.

 

                                          MZP passed the following resolutions on this day :-

                       i)     MZP strongly condemned the Assam Government for invoking Section 144CrPC on the soil of Mizoram and also condemned the State Government for turning a deaf ear to the border issue.

                       ii)    Government of Mizoram should protect the boundary of the State.

                       iii)   MZP will continue to render utmost effort to protect Mizoram and her people.

                       iv)   'Zofate Chawlhbuk' should be reconstructed.

 

                                          The processionists peacefully dispersed @ 1:30 p.m and no untoward incident has been reported from any part of the State today. Further input indicated that MZP will hold an Extended Office Bearers’ Meeting at their office tomorrow, i.e. 13.3.2018 @ 10:30 a.m and they are likely to discuss their future course of action regarding the border issue.

 

 

13.03.2018   An Executive Committee of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) held an Emergency Meeting on the night of 13.3.2018 @ 12:00 midnight at MZP Office, Treasury Square, Aizawl. B. Vanlaltana, Vice President, MZP presided over the meeting which was attended by about 40 (forty) members. The meeting decided to postpone their 'public workcamp' (Hnatlang) (intended to reconstruct on 14.3.2018) to reconstruct Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai, Bairabi to a later date after the meeting of Chief Secretaries of Mizoram and Assam under the chairmanship of Union Home Secretary scheduled on 20.3.2018, to seek an amicable solution regarding the Mizoram-Assam border disputes.

                                          In this regard, MZP issued a Press Release in which leaders of MZP stated that they have received requests from the Hon'ble Chief Minister, Hon'ble Home Minister, leaders of Mizoram Kohhran Hruaitute Committee (MKHC), CYMA, MUP, MHIP and MSU to postpone their plan in order to ease the prevailing tension between the two States. Leaders of MZP also expressed their displeasure over promulgation of prohibitory orders u/s 144 Cr.PC by District Magistrates of Aizawl and Kolasib, and the same was viewed by MZP as a tactic to deter/foil the 'public workcamp'.

                                          The Press Release also highlighted that MZP will call for a 'Total Bandh' in the near future to pressurize the State Government in addressing the issue of Mizoram-Assam border dispute.

 

 

15.03.2018   An Office Bearers’ meeting of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) was held on 15.3.2018 @ 2:00 p.m at MZP Office, Treasury Square, Aizawl. L. The meeting deliberated upon the proposed meeting of Chief Secretaries of Mizoram and Assam under the Chairmanship of Union Home Secretary scheduled to be held on 20.3.2018 to seek amicable solutions regarding the Mizoram-Assam border disputes. In this regard, MZP decided to render assistance to the Government of Mizoram by way of providing relevant documents to the Chief Secretary of Mizoram.

 

 

16.03.2018   Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) General Headquarters, Aizawl issued a Press Release on 16.3.2018 in which leaders of MZP highlighted the following points :-

(i)    MZP stated that MZP Executive Meeting held on16.3.2018 @ 12:00 noon revoked the proposed 'Organizing of a ‘Total Bandh’ in Mizoram'. MZP will wait for the outcome of the proposed meeting of Chief Secretaries of Mizoram and Assam under the Chairmanship of Union Home Secretary scheduled to be held on 20.3.2018 to seek amicable solutions regarding the Mizoram-Assam border disputes.

(ii)   MZP reiterated that they will continue to pursue reconstruction of 'ZofateChawlhbuk' at Zophai, Bairabi.

(iii) MZP stated that Government of Mizoram must not withdraw Mizoram Police deployed at Zophai, Bairabi.

(iv)  MZP will submit a Memorandum to the Hon'ble President of India, Hon'ble Prime Minister of India and Hon'ble Union Home Minister of India with matters regarding the Mizoram-Assam border disputes.

(v)   Government of Assam must take necessary action against the Deputy Commissioner of Hailakandi for his excessive use of power in dealing with the unarmed students.

(vi)  Leaders of MZP expressed gratitude to the Mizo public, especially to the public of Bairabi for their massive support and also appealed the public for their continued support for the cause of Mizo community.

 

 

20.03.2018  The meeting of Chief Secretaries of Assam and Mizoram held under the chairmanship of Union Home Secretary, Govt. of India in New Delhi on 20.3.2018 on the interstate border issue in the context of the recent incidents at Bairabi on 8th and 10th March, 2018 have decided to maintain status quo at the disputed site and it has also been decided that both the Government of Assam and Mizoram will take all measures to maintain peace in their respective areas. To this effect, assembly of people at the site will not be allowed by the two states. The Meeting also unanimously decided that a team comprising Joint Secretary, North East and Principal Secretaries (Home) of Mizoram and Assam will visit the disputed area within a week and the team will be assisted by officials of Survey of India in its work. It has also been decided that the team will submit its findings of the Union Home Secretary which will be discussed in a follow up meeting with the Chief Secretaries of the two states. Thereafter, a meeting will be convened by the Union Home Minister with the Chief Ministers of Mizoram and Assam.

                                          The above decisions seemed to have evoked strong response from the Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP), a student union of Mizoram which had spearheaded an activity to construct Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai, near Bairabi, Kolasib District located along the Assam- Mizoram border as they claimed that the said land is located within the territory of Mizoram and that it belongs to Ch.Chhunga (L), the first Chief Minister of Mizoram. In fact, the MZP had constructed Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai on 27th Feb., 2018 but the shed was demolished on the same day by some persons reportedly at the instance of Assam Police and Forest Officials. It may be pertinent to mention that a Police Duty Post have also been constructed supposedly by the Assam Police at the same site on 2.3.2018 which was also dismantled by some persons reportedly at the behest of Mizo Student Union. At the same time, some advisor of MZP are of the opinion that the decisions taken at the Home Secretary’s meeting with the Chief Secretaries of Assam and Mizoram should be endorsed and the road map prepared therein should also be duly carried out. Input indicated that MZP Gen.Hqrs, Aizawl will convene an Executive Committee meeting at MZP office, Treasury Square, Aizawl on 21.3.2018 @ 1:00 p.m. The meeting will also be attended by Student Unions of various colleges within Aizawl City including Hnampual Student Union (Sub-Tribes of Mizo). They are likely to ponder upon the decisions taken by the Chief Secretaries of Assam and Mizoram and they are also likely to take a decision as to whether they will go ahead with construction of Zofate Chawlhbuk at the disputed site or not.

                                          At the same time, while Central Young Mizo Association (CYMA) are learnt to be in favour of the decisions taken by the Chief Secretaries of Assam and Mizoram to bring lasting solutions to the border disputes, however, in order to meet the aspirations of the MZP and most importantly to soothe their anger in the wake of the alleged atrocities committed upon them by the Assam Police, they are also in favour of construction of Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai by MZP.

 

21.03.2018  An Executive Committee meeting of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP), General Headquarters, Aizawl was held on 21.3.2018 @ 1:00 p.m at MZP Conference Hall. The meeting was chaired by L. Ramdinliana Renthlei, President, MZP and it was attended by 65 (sixty five) members. In this regard, MZP General Hqrs., Aizawl issued a Press Release in which the following resolutions were passed :-

(i)    MZP opined that the meeting of Chief Secretaries of Mizoram and Assam under the Chairmanship of Union Home Secretary   held on 20.3.2018 was futile for the cause of Mizoram. Leaders        of MZP stated that if status-quo is to be maintained by the two   States, Government of Assam should dismantle their buildings constructed within Mizoram such as School, Police Out Post,    Refugee Camp, Religious structures, etc. on or before 15.4.2018 failing which MZP will reconstruct Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai,         Bairabi.

(ii)   MZP disapproved the naming of 'Zophai' to 'Kartuchal' during the meeting of Chief Secretaries of Mizoram and Assam under             the Chairmanship of Union Home Secretary. MZP will not allow Zophai to have another name or appellation.

(iii) MZP will organise a Sit-in demonstration in front of Vanapa Hall, Aizawl on 22.3.2018 @ 1:00 p.m in protest against the           brutal assault on Students and Journalists by Assam Police at             Zophai, Bairabi and in order to show their determination to reconstruct Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai, Bairabi. In this regard,            MZP appealed the public to support and participate in the demonstration.

(iv)  MZP requested the ongoing Assembly Budget Session to pass a         'Resolution' that the 509 Sq.mt. Inner Line Forest Reserve lies        within Mizoram State and efforts should be taken to retrieve the             Inner Line Forest Reserve.

(v)   MZP stated that majority of the residents in Hailakandi and    Garmura are illegal immigrants from Bangladesh. MZP urged those illegal immigrants who are staying in Mizoram to return to their native.

 

22.03.2018  Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP), General Headquarters, Aizawl organised a Sit-in demonstration in front of Vanapa Hall, Aizawl on 22.3.2018 @ 1:00 p.m in protest against the brutal assault on Students and Journalists by Assam Police at Zophai, Bairabi. The demonstration was led by B. Vanlaltana, Vice President, MZP and was attended by about 30 (thirty) persons. The demonstration concluded at 2:00 p.m peacefully.

 

                                          Speaking on the occasion, L. Ramdinliana Renthlei, President, MZP Gen. Hqrs. reiterated MZP’s intention to reconstruct 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' at Zophai, Bairabi. He expressed that MZP is firm on its stand based on Inner Line Permit introduced in 1875.
L. Ramdinliana Renthlei lauded Journalists for their courage and also stated that members of MZP had voluntarily proceeded to Zophai, Bairabi to endure any kind of hurt or pain to protect the interest of Mizoram. He also blamed the Government of Assam for brutal assault on innocent civilians.

 

                                          Pu V.L. Krosshnehzova, Adviser, MZP in his speech, demanded the Central Government and Government of Assam to identify and take appropriate action against Assam Police personnel who brutally assaulted innocent civilians at Zophai, Bairabi. He urged political parties not to take advantage of the on-going situation for political benefit and also urged the political parties to protect, defend and work in the interest of Mizoram. He also requested the MsLA to pass a 'Resolution' that 'Zophai, Bairabi lies within Mizoram'.

 

                                          Pu Lalhruaitluanga Chawngte, Adviser, MZP also delivered a speech in which he highlighted that wars are fought mainly over territory issues by men in uniforms with weapons but for Mizos, men without uniforms went to protect Mizoram. He stated that trespassers built schools, Out posts, etc. within Mizoram and was viewed in silence; while construction of a resting shed within Mizoram by MZP was objected by Assam Government. Firing incident at Zophai, Bairabi by Government of Assam was not properly addressed by Government of Mizoram. He appealed the Mizo public to support the cause of MZP in protecting the territory of Mizoram.

 

                                          Ricky Lalbiakmawia, Fin. Secretary, NESO also delivered a speech in which he stated that Assam is having boundary issues with all its neighbouring states. He also appealed the Mizo public to support the cause of MZP in protecting the territory of Mizoram.

 

                                          It is also reported that the leaders of MZP lauded the support rendered by various Zo-ethnic communities across the world who expressed their support for MZP in their on-going initiatives.
