****SPECIAL REPORT****

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/742

Dated: 1st October, 2019.

Information has been received that Pu A. Sawibunga, President, MBDPF (Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ Forum) and Pu Chauhsarai, Spokesperson of MBIDM (Mizoram Bru Indigenous Democratic Movement) convened a Public Meeting today, i.e. 1.10.2019 @ 7:00 a.m at Naisingpara Multipurpose Hall, and around 350 (three hundred fifty) persons attended the meeting. Pu A. Sawibunga apprised them of several discussions held with the Government of Mizoram and informed that their demands remain unfulfilled. He also informed them of the letter submitted to the Hon’ble Home Minisiter, Government of Mizoram through the District Magistrate &amp; Collector, North Tripura District, Dharmanagar, yesterday, i.e. 30.9.2019. The leaders of MBDPF and MBIDM were learnt to have implored the inmates of the Relief Camps to stand united and not to agree to repatriation and at the same time, stated that no obstruction will be made to those who have decided to be repatriated.

The SDM, Kanchanpur convened a meeting with leaders of MBDPF today @ 11:00 a.m in his office chamber. The meeting was attended by Pu A. Sawibunga, President, MBDPF and Upa Lalfakzuala as well as Bansila and Lalremruatmawia, MBDPF leaders of Hazacherra Relief Camp. The SDM informed them to agree to repatriation process. However, Pu A. Sawibunga reiterated that their demands are still left unfulfilled and due to this reason, they have decided not to agree to repatriation.
Similarly, the SDM, Panisagar, also convened a meeting with MBDPF leaders of Kaiskau, Khakchangpara and Hamsapara Relief Camps. Leaders of MBDPF, such as, Lalbiakthanga, Charlie Molsoi, etc. attended the meeting. No positive outcome emanated from the meeting.

It is also learnt that the Government of Tripura made a public announcement through PA system last evening, i.e. 30.9.2019, at Kaiskau, Khakchangpara and Hamsapara Relief Camps, informing the inmates that those willing to be repatriated should report to the office of SDM, Panisagar today, i.e. 1.10.2019. As per information available, 9 (nine) persons have reported themselves. The SDM, Panisagar was learnt to have given them a copy of the Repatriation Roadmap since the same was not made public by the NGO leaders and further informed them to pass on the information to those who are willing to be repatriated.

As of now, the number of persons willing to be repatriated from various camps is not yet known. However, despite the anti-repatriation appeals made by the NGO leaders, there are people who are willing to be repatriated.

Officials of Mamit, Lunglei and Kolasib districts who are entrusted in repatriation process have reached Kanhmun, Mamit district. It is also learnt that some MBDPF Branch leaders have gone to inspect the Facilitation Centres established at Zamuang and Borai today. The outcome of their visit is not available at the time of writing the report.

Situation is under close watch.
