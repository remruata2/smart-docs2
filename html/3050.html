SPECIAL REPORT

No.MSB/CB/SR/2020/042

Dated: 20th January, 2020.

 

            Information received indicated that Assam Rifles (AR) personnel posted at Zokhawthar resumed detention of trucks carrying areca nut at Zokhawthar w.e.f. 29.12.2019 and as many as 32 (thirty two) trucks including 1 (one) vehicle carrying fertilizers were detained as on 16.1.2020. At the initial period, AR duty intended to handover the seized items to the Custom Department which the latter stated that those items to be disposed off at Aizawl only which was opposed by the residents of Zokhawthar by not allowing the vehicles to move out of their village.

            In pursuance of Zokhawthar All NGOs and Village Council meeting held yesterday at the residence of Pi Pari, about 500 (three hundred) Zokhawthar residents assembled today, i.e. 20.1.2020 @ 8:00 a.m at YMA Hall, which is the opposite of the AR Camp, and chanted slogans condemning the actions of AR personnel. They were also joined by leaders of Melbuk village and Champhai Trade Union. Consequently they organized procession from YMA Hall to Tiau bridge, i.e. near Land Custom station. The gathering was addressed by the following- Pu Zarzoliana, President, YMA; Pi Lalmuanpuii, VCP; Pu Lalhmingliana, VC Vice President; Pi Lalparmawii, MHIP; Pu Lalrammawia, Zokhawthar Welfare Committee and dispersed @ 9:55 a.m. Their slogans, inter alia, included the following :-

  1. Who disturbed the source of income of Zokhawthar residents ? - Assam Rifles.
  2. Who treated us like the disturbed state in the most peaceful state in India ? - Assam Rifles.
  3. AR to leave their camp in Zokhawthar.
  4. AR not to involve in business.
  5. AR to concentrate their duty in the security of the State only.

            A meeting was held today @ 11:00 a.m at Zokhawthar Police outpost under the chairmanship of Pu Rex Zarzoliana Vanchhawng, SP, Champhai, which was attended by magistrate, Custom officials, Assam Rifles officials and leaders of Zokhawthar village. The local leaders demanded unconditional release of the seized items. The meeting, after extensive discussion, reached an agreement i.e. the seized items will be handedover to Custom Department which will be disposed by them at the earliest. They further decided to bring together all the seized vehicles along with the areca nut at one particular place. In pursuance of the agreement, 28 (twenty eight) vehicles along with the seized items were received by Custom officials from AR (on paper) today itself @ 3:00 p.m. Another 4 (four) empty vehicles bearing Myanmar registration will be received by local police after a fresh FIR is lodged by the Assam Rifles stating they have illegally crossed into India.

Situation is under close watch.
