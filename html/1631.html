LOK SABHA UNSTARRED QUESTION DY. NO. 633.

 

(a) the major concerns of protestors across the State of Mizoram in the recent times

           

  PROTEST AGAINST CITIZENSHIP (AMMENDMENT) BILL, 2016

            During the year 2019, there were several instances where state-wide protest against CAB, 2016 were organised in Mizoram as under :-               

 

i)   Total Bandh in protest against Citizenship (Ammendment) Bill, 2016             organised by MZP:

            On 8.1.2019, Mizo Zirlai Pawl (Mizo Students Organisation) organised a total bandh) across the State.

 

ii) Procession in protest against Citizenship (Ammendment) Bill, 2016    organised by MZP:            

            Thousands took to the streets in Aizawl and elsewhere in major towns of different districts across the State in protest against the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 on 23.1.2019. The procession was organised in pursuance of NESO's 'Protest Rally' programme to be carried out in all North-east.

 

iii) Boycott of Republic Day, 2019 by NGOCC (NGO Coordination          Committee) :

            The celebratory function of the 70th Republic Day celebration, 2019 at Aizawl was boycotted by NGOCC in protest against the Citizenship (Ammendment) Bill, 2016 on 26.1.2019. The protestors, prevented civilians, students and others from entering AR Lammual, the venue.

            However, the 70th Republic Day celebration, 2019 was held peacefully on 26.1.2019 @ 8:00 a.m at AR Lammual with Hon’ble Governor of Mizoram, Pu Kummanam Rajasekharan unfurling the National Flag as scheduled. The ceremonial parade was also concluded without any untoward incident. The general public were however absent in the celebration.

 

iv) “Protest Day” against Citizenship  (Ammendment) Bill, 2016 organised       by NGOCC: 

            NGOCC organised “Protest Day” in protest against the Citizenship (Amendment) Bill, 2016 on 12.2.2019 in front of Vanapa Hall, Aizawl and a mass prayer of all the participants was also held.

 

 

 

 

 

PROTEST AGAINST CITIZENSHIP AMMENDMENT ACT

 

i) Burning of posters:

             As organised by MZP General Headquarters, about 300 (three hundred), gathered in front of Vanapa Hall, Aizawl on 12.12.2019 and burnt the effigy of Shri. Amit Shah, Hon’ble Union Home Minister and Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP (LS) in protest against CAA.

 

ii) Sit-in protest:

1. A group known as 'Zoram Revolutionary Movement' (ZRM) staged a Sit-in Protest in front of Vanapa Hall, Aizawl on 13.12.2019.

 

2. Zoram Revolution Movement (ZRM), in continuation of its protest of Citizenship Amendment Act (CAA) called for Sit-in protests in Aizawl and other District Headquarters on 16.12.2019. In this protest, Slogans were raised demanding resignation of Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP (LS) and also displayed placards denouncing the CAA. Demonstrations were also carried out elsewhere in Lunglei, Serchhip and Kolasib.

 

3. A Sit-in demonstration organised by MZU Students’ Council was held at MZU Campus on 18.12.2019. Preamble to the Constitution of India was read out during the protest and slogans were also raised.

 

iii) Submission of letter to the Hon’ble President of India:

             ZPM Youth submitted their disapproval letter of Citizenship Amendment Act, 2019 (CAA) to the Hon'ble President of India through the Hon'ble Governor of Mizoram on 19.12.2019.

 

iv) Received of Pu C.Lalrosanga, MP (LS) :

             A demonstration was  organised by MZP at Lengpui Airport to receive Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP (LS), who arrived from New Delhi on 7.1.2020. The protestors gathered outside the Airport and displayed Placards containing anti-CAA slogans and mockery purportedly directed at Pu C. Lalrosanga, MP(LS) who supported CAA in the Parliament.

 

v)     Displaying of black flags :

             MZP Gen. Hqrs. organized 'MZP Hnatlang' (social work)on 9.1.2020 by displaying black flags at various conspicuous places in Aizawl to show their support and solidarity to fellow students who were being victimized in the nationwide protests against CAB/CAA.

 

(b) the steps being taken, taken by the Government to address these concerns

            The fact that the State of Mizoram has been exempted from CAB/CAA greatly reduces the level of intensity. The Government welcomed exemption of Mizoram from CAA.

 

(c) the total number of casualties as a result of the suppression of these protests

            As a result of the protests mentioned in point (a), there has been no report of any casualty.

 

(d) the number of stakeholders meetings carried out in this regard

1)     Regarding the intention of boycotting 70th Republic Day in connection with the issue of Citizenship Amendment Bill, 2016, a meeting between the Government and NGOCC was held on 25.01.2019 at Secretariat Conference Hall, Aizawl which was chaired by Pu Zoramthanga, Hon’ble Chief Minister.

 

2)     Pu Zoramthanga, Hon'ble Chief Minister, Mizoram convened a meeting with NGOCC leaders on 3.10.2019 @ 3:00 p.m at Chief Minister's Conference Hall, Aizawl. The NGOCC leaders were requested by the Government to cancel their intention to receive Shri. Amit Shah, Union Home Minister, with 'Pawndum' and placards condemning Citizenship Amendment Bill, 2016.

 

3)     On 5.10.2019, leaders of NGOCC met Shri. Amit Shah, Hon’ble Union Home Minister at Raj Bhavan, Aizawl while he visited Aizawl on 5.10.2019 and raised their demands and concern on the issue of CAB.

 

4)     On 29.11.2019, leaders of NGOCC and Political Party leaders of Mizoram met Shri Amit Shah, Hon’ble Union Home Minister at Assam House, Delhi and raised their demands and concern on the issue of CAB.
