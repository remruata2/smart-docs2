27.9.2022 :    Mizoram Taxi Association (MTA) submitted a written petition to Transport Minister on 27.9.2022 wherein they stated that regulation of One Time Replacement of Taxi, which is being in forced, caused complexities to those who are engaging in Taxi business. Such being the case, they requested to review the said regulation and take necessary action so as to enable replacement of taxi permit for more than once.

            In another written petition submitted to Transport Minister the same day, they stated that at present, there are around 3,000 (three thousand) persons who are engaging in Taxi business within Aizawl Municipal area. It also stated that they are not permitted to engage in service for 90-96 days in a year due to traffic rules within AMC area, and at the same time, the tax payable is not reduced. In this connection, they requested to take necessary action for decrease of the amount of tax payable to the state government.

14.10.2022 :  Lawngtlai Auto Rickshaw Owners Association (LAROA) continued picketing of DTO Office, Lawngtlai on Dt. 14.10.2022 @ 8:30 a.m. and around 50 (fifty) members participated in the picketing. In the meantime, they also organized picketing of DC Office, Lawngtlai and around 20 members participated.

            In this connection, a meeting was held yesterday's evening @ 4:30 p.m. at the office chamber of Addl. DC, Lawngtlai and the meeting was attended by Addl. DC, leaders of LAROA and concerned department officials. The meeting reportedly ended without having any conclusive decision. After the meeting, leaders of LAROA decided to continue picketing of DTO Office Lawngtlai and DC, Office Lawngtlai w.e.f., 14.10.2022.

            In this regard, LOROA issued a Press Statement on 14.10.2022, wherein they highlighted their grievances and the reasons for conducting picketing of both of DTO & DC Office, Lawngtlai. It also stated that the present numbers of Auto Rickshaw in Lawngtlai town were sufficient for the time being and if another additional 68 (sixty eight) permits are issued, the same will cause hardship in earning their livelihood.
It further stated that they intend to continue their protest until their demands are fulfilled.

            Leaders of LOROA held a meeting today’s afternoon @ 4:00 p.m. and the meeting decided to hold Executive Meeting this weekend to discuss their further course of action.

17.10.2022 :  (a)       Lawngtlai Auto Rickshaw Owners Association (LAROA) continued picketing of DC, Office, Lawngtlai; DTO Office, Lawngtlai and other important government offices in Lawngtlai town on 17.10.2022 in demand for cancellation of newly issued auto rickshaw permits. In this connection, a meeting was held under the chairmanship of DC, Lawngtlai today i.e., 17.10.2022 @ 12:00 Noon Circuit House, Lawngtlai. The meeting was attended by leaders of LAROA and concerned department officials. After deliberate discussion the meeting resolved the following:

            (i)        Rickshaw Permit issued to government employees should be cancelled.

            (ii)       Rickshaw Permits sold to other should be cancelled.

            (iii)     New Rickshaw Permit may not be issued within 5 years considering the                  population of Lawngtlai town.

           

                                    Subsequently, after the meeting, the picketing was called off this afternoon @ 2:30 p.m.

(b)       Siaha Auto Rickshaw Owners' Association (SAROA) organized picketing of DTO Office, Siaha on 17.10.2022 @ 9:30 a.m. in protest against issuance of
201 (two hundred one) new Auto Rickshaw Permits in Siaha District. In this connection, a meeting was held with leaders of SAROA at the office chamber of Additional DC, Siaha. The meeting, which was chaired by Addl. DC Siaha decided that the newly issued Auto Rickshaw permits will be scrutinized by SAROA so as to find out deserving permit holders and make a list of the same, which will be submitted to the DTO, Siaha for further submission to the government for consideration. After the meeting, the picketing was called off @ 11:30 a.m.

 

21.12.2022 : Aizawl Two Wheeler Taxi Association in its Press Release issued on 20.12.2022 stated that the present rule in force barred two wheeler taxis to engage in service for 1 (one) day per week in view of Traffic congestion within Aizawl city. However, the State Government intends to increase their non-service period to 2 (two) days per week w.e.f. from next year and they strongly opposed the stated initiative. It also stated that they have earned their livelihood only from their two wheeler taxi and their service period will be lesser than private vehicles as per the new rule. Such being the case, they appealed the State Government to review its decision and to revoke the said new rule. It further requested the State Government not to issue additional two wheeler taxi permit due to heavy traffic congestion within Aizawl city.
