****MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER SITUATION REPORT****

No.MSB/CB/B-4/2020/626

Dated 8th November, 2020

In continuation of our report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/625 Dt. 7.11.2020, Situation Report on Mizoram-Assam Border is as under:

1. **Thinghlun, Mamit District Report**-

No new development has been reported during the last 24 (twenty four) hours and situation is normal.

1. **Kanhmun, Mamit District Report–**

There is no movement of vehicles across Medili Bridge, Mizoram-Assam border today i.e., 8.11.2020. Situation is normal.

Information received indicated that w.e.f 7.11.2020 Young Bru Association (YBA), Bru Socio Cultural Organization (BSCO), Bru Sangrongma Mthoh, Damcherra Market Committee, Mog Socio Cultural Organization (MSCO), Young Chorei Association (YCA), Muslim Religious Minority, Rupini Association organized blockage of entry of goods into Mizoram at Damcherra, North Tripura in pursuance of their memorandum submitted to Sub-Divisional Magistrate, Panisagar Sub-Division, North Tripura on 5.11.2020 wherein they stated the following:

1. The government of Mizoram did not allow the people of Tripura to enter into their state even after the inter-state border restriction is lifted by the Central Government while they allowed free entry from Assam and there should be free movement at Mizoam-Tripura border.
2. The government of Mizoram did not open the bridge of the inter-state bordering villages and even did not allow taking bath in the river Longkai under Longkai Valley, Damcherra RD Block.
3. The Government of Mizoram should not close and open the Dasarath Bridge (Damcherra Bridge) whenever they wish, even after opening of Assam-Mizoram road.

It further stated that until amicable solution and agreement is made on the above grievances they declare closure of all border crossing between Tripura and Mizoram.

However, as of now vehicles and pedestrian crossing at the bridge is not possible due to the ongoing renovation work of Damcherra Bridge (a Bridge connecting Tripura to Mizoram) by Mizoram PWD.

Mahila Congress (Ex-MP) intended to visit Saihapui ‘V’ but they were stopped at Assam-Mizoram border and they returned to Assam afterwards. Upon hearing their visit, around 50 (fifty) residents of Saihapui ‘V’ assembled near the border and stated their disappointment regarding frequent attempt to enter into Mizoram territory by residents of Assam and they then dispersed afterwards without any untoward incident. In this connection, i/c Mizoram Police deployed at the area informed the matter and apprised the possible consequences regarding crossing of border by group of civilians to i/c SSB (CAPF) deployed by Assam (newly deployed w.e.f. 6.11.2020) at the Assam side of the border. After deliberation, SSB agreed that henceforth they will not allow entry of civilians from Assam upto Mizoram-Assam border so as to prevent any possible untoward incident.

1. **Bairabi, Kolasib District Report –**

There is no movement of vehicles across Bairabi border today i.e., 8.11.2020. Situation is normal.

1. **Phaisen, Kolasib District Report-**

Blockade of vehicles bound to Assam via Phaisen road at Zukbual Mual, Bilkhawthlir by Buhchangphai MZP is still continuing today i.e., 8.11.2020 and 13 (thirteen) vehicles are stranded due to the said roadblock till today.

1. **Vairengte, Kolasib District Report –**

Levelling work of the proposed place of BSF deployment (to be deployed by Mizoram) which is beyond Vairengte Auto Stand is continued today i.e., 8.11.2020 @ 7:00 a.m. and completed at 5:10 p.m. today’s evening. At the same time, levelling work of proposed place of SSB (CAPF) deployment (to be deployed by Assam) adjacent to proposed BSF place of deployment (around 20 ft) is also continued today’s morning @ 4:00 a.m. In this connection, Vairengte Joint VC will organize community work for construction of BSF Camp at the stated area tomorrow i.e., 9.11.2020.

Today i.e., 8.11.2020 @ 1:20 p.m. around 50 (members) of Assam Congress Workers, Media persons, Police escort led by Smt. Sushmita Dev, President of All India Mahila Congress and Shri. Debabrata Saikia, Leader of Opposition, Assam Legislative Assembly proceeded upto near Vairengte Police Checkgate and conducted short media interview and they then immediately returned to Assam as per direction of Mizoram Police on duty.
