KUKI CHIN NATIONAL FRONT (KCNF/KNF/KNA) :

            This organisation was founded in the year 2008 by elements of Z0-ethnic living in Bangladesh as Kuki-Chin National Development Organisation (KNDO).

            Two years after its establishment, leaders of this organisation sought assistance from Kachin National Army (KNA), a militant outfit operating in Myanmar, for training which resulted in about 20-40 KNDO cadres receiving training from KNA during the year 2017-2020. After these cadres completed their training, their organisation was renamed as Kuki Chin National Front. (KNF).

            The main objective of this organisation is to unite Zo-ethnic tribes including CHIN tribes living in Myanmar-Bangladesh bordering areas into one autonomous region having a development budget of their own from Bangladesh Government.

PEACE DIALOGUE :

            After a series of  online meeting, "First round of Physical Peace Talk" between Kuki-Chin National Front/Army (KCNA) and Government of Bangladesh (GoB) represented by the Peace Committee was held on 5th November, 2023 at Hmunlai village, near Ruma bazar, Bandarbun District, Bangladesh. Although no definite agreements were made, the meeting, agreed to maintained cease-fire between both the parties until the next peace talk. The KNF has submitted a draft of possible and proposed peace agreement with 6(six) point demands to the Prime Minister of Bangladesh through Peace Establishment Committee.

           

            The second round of bilateral physical meeting between the two parties was conducted on 05.03.2024 at Hmunlai Village, near Ruma Bazar, Bandarban District, Bangladesh. In the meeting, representatives of KNF/KNA reiterated their six demands submitted in their previous meeting. However, as the points  require more time, the meeting agreed to discuss and implement them in subsequent dialogues with the next round of Talks scheduled to be held on 22nd April 2024.

 

            Then on 2nd and 3rd April 2024, cadres of KNA/KNF  robbed Sonali Bank (one of the leading banks in Bangladesh) of Ruma Branch, Bandarban, Bangladesh and Sonali Bank and Krishi Bank of Thanchi Branches, Bandarban District respectively and reportedly looted Takka-17 lakhs 50 thousand from the two banks (Tk. 15 lakh from Sonali Bank and Tk. 2 lakh 50 thousand from Krishi Bank).

 

            Bangladesh Government has been conducting combing operation to root out Kuki Chin National Front (KNF) cadres from Chittagong Hills Tract of Bangladesh since 06.04.2024. The combing operations led by the Bangladesh Army were conducted in the aftermath of the robberies of the two banks and looting of arms by suspected KNF Cadres on 02.04.2024 and 03.04.2024. Consequent upon the operation, it has been alleged that many civilians mainly from the Bawm community of Bandarban Districts, Bangladesh were falsely arrested or killed on the pretext of being a sympathizer of KNF. It is reported that many civilians from different villages including Eden Para, Lairunpi Para, Munnuam Para, Artha Para, Bastlang Para and Happy Hill Para of Rumaupazila, Bandarban District had fled their villages, houses and properties reportedly to escape the atrocities of the Bangladesh Army and hide in the forest along the Indo-Bangladesh border. Some of these civilians have entered Mizoram in Lawngtlai District in the past few days to seek shelter and refuge while some are suspected to be still camping in the forest along the border.

 

            Latest information received regarding Bangladesh refugees stated that on the late night of 18.05.2024 (Saturday) and early morning of 19.05.2024 (Sunday), Bangladesh refugees (Bawm community) from several villages of Bandarbans District, Bangladesh had entered Vathuampui Village, Lawngtlai District seeking shelter inside Mizoram. It has also been reported that these Bawm refugees had entered Mizoram at Chhippui, Lawngtlai District and after passing through Mautlang, they finally reached Vathuampui where they are being temporarily accommodated at refugee camps. Another group of Bawm refugees also entered Hruitezawl Village, Lawngtlai District on the early morning of 19.05.2024. This group of refugees however, reportedly entered Mizoram by boat from Paletwa District, Myanmar. With this latest influx of Bangladesh Nationals, the total number of Bangladesh refugees in Mizoram increases to 1535 (one thousand five hundred thirty five) as on 28.05.2024.

MYANMAR  REFUGEES :

       

        The clashes between the Myanmar Army and the civil volunteer which started since the military coup in 01.02.2021 continued till date. Latest report indicated that armed conflict between the Myanmar Army and the People’s Defence Force (PDF) had taken place at Kalaymeo Township as late as 2nd March, 2024 claiming many lives both from the Army and the PDF.

 

            The three Army camps established by the State Administration Council (SAC) at Tidim Township, Chin State were attacked by join forces of ethnic arms group namely CNA Chin Rifles, CDF Halkha, CDF Thlantlang, CDF Hualngoram, CDF KKG, CDF Zanniatram, PDA and TCDF between 12.01.2024-16.01.2024 and were subsequently captured by the ethnic armed groups. However, there is no report of Myanmar Nationals fleeing into Mizoram due to the arm conflict mentioned above

 

Report has been received lately that the number of Myanmar Nationals seeking refuge in Mizoram due to the order of compulsory joining of the Myanmar Army by the State Administrative Council (Myanmar Army) have significantly increase in the recent months. It is reported that as many as 763 (seven hundred sixty three) Myanmar Nationals had fled Myanmar and entered Zokhawthar in the hope of escaping the order as of 18.03.2024. These Myanmar Nationals are mainly from Tahan, Pingkung and Letpanchaung of Myanmar and stayed with their friends and families living in the 5 (five) refugee camps at Zokhawthar. The numbers of these Myanmar Nationals trying to escape compulsory joining of the Myanmar Army and entered Mizoram are likely to increase in the coming days.

 

                Latest inputs regarding the situation in Myanmar indicated that a military operation has been conducted since 15.05.2024 by Chin National Army (CNA) led ethnic armed groups under Chinland Council against the State Administrative Council’s Myanmar Army and the Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA) Eastern Command camps inside Tonzang Township. The CNA led armed groups reportedly captured most of the camps allegedly occupied by Myanmar Army and ZRA Eastern Command including Dampui Camp (ZRA), Nakzaang Camp (Myanmar Army), Chikha Camp (Myanmar Army), Eliza Camp, Suansang (ZRA) and Tonzang Town Camp (Myanmar Army) on 19.05.2024. During these clashes, the Myanmar Army retaliated by conducting airstrikes over Tonzang town, Nakzaang and Lungtak villages which led to desertion of these villages by the civilians. Some of these civilians who had left their villages and houses due to fear of another airstrikes by the Myanmar Army have entered the adjoining villages inside Mizoram for shelter and asylum.

 

            Further, information received also stated that a number of Myanmar Nationals had entered Siaha District during the second week of May 2024 due to the increase tension between Maraland Defence Force (MDF) and Chinland Defence Force (CDF-Mara) in Chin State, Myanmar. These Myanmar Nationals who are seeking temporary shelter inside the Mizoram are mainly from different villages of Myanmar including Vairum, Salachhuah, Laty, Bakla, Piedo, Bakalei and Tisavai villages and are staying with their friends and families.

 

            At the same time, Khumi community of Myanmar’s Paletwa District had entered Khawmawi Village last week allegedly due to the atrocities of the Arakan Army against the Khumi community as well as the conscription law reportedly imposed by Arakan Army upon every man under the age of 40 years of age within the administrative area of Arakan Army.
