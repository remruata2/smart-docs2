**ZOMI REVOLUTIONARY ORGANISATION (ZRO/ZRA)**__

**Activities from 204 till date**

1. On 20.5.2004, 25th AR CPI post arrested one ZRA No. 2856 (Pvt) Thangginsang @ Justin (20) s/o Sinkhotual of Mualkawi village, Manipur alongwith AK-47 amns-2 and SLR amns-2. They were handed over to CPI PS on 20.5.2004 and hence CPI PS C/No.98/04 dt.20.5.2004 u/s 3 of 1 ES was registered.

2. On 27.5.2004, one .22 Pistol (Local made) was recovered from the house of Kapkhanthanga Bethel Veng, CPI which was reportedly kept by Mangkhenkhup, ZRO from the possession of Zochhawna, ZRO. Hence CPI PS C/No.108/04 dt. 28.5.04 u/s 25(1-B)(a) A.Act has been registered.

3. Information stated that on the night of 30.5.04 several masked persons claiming to be ZRA members forcibly took out Ngopauva and Pagina of Ngur Vengsang and allegedly assaulted them near BRTF Camp but fortunately except for some brushes they were not seriously injured.

4. Information received indicated that ZRA volunteers had displayed prohibitory order on the Notice Board of Leisenzo village, prohibiting selling and drinking of liquor in the village sometime in the 3rd week of July, 2004. The local youths who resented the said order had tempered the same by drawing 'X' in the said order and put it upside down. Apparently, ZRA were annoyed of the same and therefore, 10(ten) ZRA volunteers of whom 8(eight) were armed with AK-47 Rifles entered Leisenzo village on 25.7.2004(Sunday) at around 10:00PM and summoned all the available youths of the village to the house of T. Doliana VCM and reprimanded them and also reportedly assaulted some of them. Thereafter, 11 youths of the village, including the LDC of Leisenzo High School were taken to Sesih village on the same night but were released the next morning i.e. 26.7.2004 (yesterday) on being request by VC members of Leisenzo village. Further information received indicated that the youths of Leisenzo village were highly annoyed on the high handedness of ZRA volunteers.

 In this connection, Champhai Police launched combing operation immediately on getting the information and apprehended one ZRA member called Thangtinliana (16) s/o Khamzachin of Churachandpur in Manipur (who has reportedly completed Basic Training recently) from Leisenzo village and is being interrogated.

7.  On 26.4.2005, 20 ZRA volunteers in civil dress entered Hnahlan and demanded Rs. 5000/- from VCP Hnahlan but the VCP did not gave money and told the VCP that they will collect after they return (date and where proceed not known).

ZRA is intending to make recruitment from Paite community from this area. These ZRA cadres are believed to be Capt. Marka's faction ZRA. The ZRA collecting donation was reportedly headed by Mr. Dickon of Suangdawh village, Manipur. They were 5 in numbers and holding the following arms :

 (1) M-16 Rifle  - 2

 (2) US Carbine - 3

 (3) Amns. - 150

9. It has been reported that towards the end of last week of May, 2005, a group of 12 - 15 armed militants reported to be Zomi Re-unification Force a faction of ZRA were active around Khawkawn/Chiahpui areas of Mizoram bordering Manipur. They entered Khawkawn village on the night of 2nd June, 2005 and proceeded to Chiahpui village by hijacking one Sumo and demanded Rs.10,000/- which will be paid on 11.6.2005 at Bukpui village, Manipur and returned to Khawkawn again by forcibly availing one 407 Truck and finally crossed over to Manipur.

10. Reliable information further received that there was an encounter between ZRF and ZRA militant on 5.6.2005 at 10:00 AM of the following day at the outskirts of Sumtuah village about 5/6 Kms. west of Sinzol where 3 (three) ZRF militants were killed and captured 2 - SLRs and 1 - G 2 Rifle at the spot. Casualty on ZRA side is not reported.

11. ZRA a Manipur base insurgency oputfit are regularly collecting tax of Rs. 100/- per trip from the vehicles loading Balu/Sand in the banks of Tuivai river of Mizoram-manipur border near Khawkawn.

 It is further informed that representatives of ZRA outfit at Khawkawn, Mizoram collected the tax which are handed over to the militants later.
