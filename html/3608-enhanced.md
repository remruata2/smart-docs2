A. _Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 25.11.2022_

****Prohibition of fire crackers to be strictly implemented**: In a press conference held at his office chamber yesterday (24.11.2022), SP Aizawl, Pu C.Lalruaia stated that the meeting of government officials held under the Chairmanship of Pu Lalchamliana, Home Minister on 18th October 2022 decided to prohibit firecrackers and its related products during Christmas and New year festive period. While informing the press that this prohibitory order, which was already proclaimed in Aizawl district by the concerned DC on 20th October 2022, would be strictly implemented, the SP also expressed his desire for encountering no firecrackers to be confiscated by the police as it often involves various process to be done which are time consuming for both the police and the accused alike. According to the record of the police, there were 23 cases registered in this connection in the year 2021 out of which 18 cases were disposed by the Court where all the accused were convicted. Pu C.Lalruaia further requested the public to inform the police if they come across any person who are found violating this prohibitory order through telephone calls.**

B. _Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 25.11.2022_

**Lie Detector in Mizoram:******For the first time, Mizoram police will be using Lie Detector to assist them in their investigation process from the month of December, 2022 and will be utilise to examine the accused of the deceased child who was strongly suspected to be sexually abused by her parents during the month of September, 2022. As per information received by VANGLAINI, the parents of this child victim were arrested by the police however, as no concrete evidence has been uncovered till now, they were again released on bail. Speaking to the press in this connection, SP Aizawl, Pu C.Lalruaia stated that prior permission had been obtained from the Court to use lie detector on these suspects and that three experts from Delhi Forensic Science will be arriving soon at Aizawl.

C. _Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 25.11.2022_

**Poppy and Teak seized:**On 23rd November 2022, Kolasib District Forest Officer (DFO), Pi Margareth Lalramchhani and her staffs recovered illegally imported teak timber, which are around 200 cubic meter in all from one warehouse in the outskirt of Vairengte, Kolasib district. While recovering these teak timbers, the Forest department officials also discovered 100 bags of suspected Poppy seeds which can be utilise for processing Opium. The suspected owner along with the poppy seeds were apprehended by local police and were later handed over to Custom officials as no proper charges could be framed under ND &amp; PS Act.
