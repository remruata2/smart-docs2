            (iv)      Sub-contract system was prevalently followed in the stated works and the real contractor/executor of the work received extremely less fund for execution of the work. For instance, third party contractor received around Rs. 10 lakhs out of
the total estimate of Rs. 21.33 lakhs for re-construction of Khawzawl High School.

            (vi)      Most of the real executors/contractors were not aware of project detail and amount as most of the work supervisors appointed by the Consultancy Firms re-sold their contract work.

            It also stated that the matter was discussed yesterday with the Education Minister and concerned officials as MZP viewed the same seriously. It urged for immediate re-construction/repair of all sub-standard works and requested the concerned authorities not to clear the work bills before satisfactory completion of the works.

19.12.2022  : Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 19.12.2022 stated that leaders of MZP General Headquarters led by Pu Chinkhanmanga Thomte, Asst. General Secretary, MZP conducted spot visit of Bangladesh refugees, who are taking shelter at Vathuampui and Parva III village today and took stock of the situation. It also stated that they also visited the refugees accommodated at Chamdur P and Mautlang villages yesterday and handed over assistance.During their visit, a meeting was held with the refugees to discuss the various challenges faced by the refugees. It also stated that donation was collected for zo-ethnic Bangladesh refugees during the recently concluded ZOFEST 2022 at Sialsuk and the fund collected was distributed to the refugees during the stated visit. It further stated that the refugees have been temporarily accommodated at Chamdur P, Mautlang, Parva and Vathuampui villages and 'Village Level Committee on Bangladesh Asylum Seeker' was constituted to look after the refugees in each village. 14 (fourteen) refugee's families are accommodated in Chamdur P, 10 (ten) families in Mautlang, 23 (twenty three) families in Vathuampui and 34 (thirty four) families in Parva-III, added the Press Release.
