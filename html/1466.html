WRITE UP FOR ONLINE LIA MEETING ON 23.06.2021

 

i)  Insurgency

HPC(D)  :When a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) was signed between Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) way back in 2018, Lalhmingthanga Sanate designated his HPC(D) splinter faction as 'HPC(D) (President's Group)' and did not participated in the said MoS. During that time, his group consisted of around 13 cadres with an estimated 4/5 weapons holding. However, by February 2020, 11(eleven) of his cadres deserted him and joined HPC(D)- General Headquarters Sinlung Group, who are under the leadership of Lalkhama @ LK Hmar.

                        At the moment, it is believed that there are only a cadre or two under the command of Lalhmingthanga Sanate, who himself has a great inclination towards surrendering himself to Government of Mizoram

            The other Group under Lalkhama, who designated themselves as 'HPC(D) General Headquarters Sinlung' have an estimated 20-30 and are under the command of their self-style Commander-in-chief, Lalkhama @ LK Hmar, whose family are presently residing in Saikot, Churachandpur, Manipur. Their area of operation is in and around Churachanpur town of Manipur.

BRAU :  A faction of Bru Revolutionary Army of Union, who may be considered as BRAU-J under the leadership of Jeetendro and  Doberam Reang are lingering around the Tri junction border areas of  Assam, Mizoram and Tripura. On 23.12.2020, the two leaders of this group organised a meeting of Bru villagers at Baroghor village, on the Assam-Mizoram border where the said two leaders exaggerate their weapons holding ,thus hoping to attract new cadres .So far, this group had not designated themselves with any specific name or demands and efforts to ascertain the numbers of their arms holding is under way.

CNA: In the wake of the  Military takeover in Myanmar on 1st February 2021  , Pu Hlathun, Army Chief of Myanmar-based armed insurgent group, Chin National Army (CNA), the armed wing of the Chin National Front (CNF) came to Farkawn, ChamphaiDistrict  from  Camp Victoria (CNA Camp, Myanmar) on 2.2.2021 and met PuLalramliana, VCP Farkawn.  He sought asylum for their families in Farkawn VC area in the wake of the military coup in Myanmar and in case, the situation turns violent. PuLalramliana, VCP Farkawn communicated the matter to Pi Maria CT Zuali, Deputy Commissioner, Champhai District over telephone, and she then informed him that she will inform the matter to higher authorities as the matter was beyond her jurisdiction.

Further, so far, there is no report of any action taken by Myanmar Military against CNA and leaders of CNA approached VCP Farkawn and sought asylum in advance in Farkawn VC area, in case the situation turns violent. 

Camp Victoria (biggest Camp of CNA) is located at around 3 (three) Kms on Myanmar side of Tiau River (Indo-Myanmar boundary river) from Farkawn, Champhai District. Dr.Suikhar, Asst. Secretary General, CNA is presently stationed at the Camp along with around 100 (one hundred) CNA cadres, and around 40 (forty) families of CNA cadre are also living in the Camp.

Peace Talks between CNA and Myanmar government were conducted since 5.1.2012, and since then ceasefire has been declared between CNA and Myanmar Army. Further, cadres of CNA are allowed to roam freely anywhere in the country without arms as per their Memorandum signed with Myanmar Union Level Peace Negotiation Team on 7.5.2012. They also set up Liason Office at Tahan and Matupui Township.

So far, no offensive action has been taken against CNA by Myanmar Military and leaders of CNA sought asylum in advance in Farkawn area, in case the need arises in future to take shelter in the Indian soil. Information received indicated that there has been no intention to shift Camp Victoria Headquarters to inside Mizoram territory.

Arakan Army:  Ceasefire between Arakan Army (AA) and Myanmar Army (MA) has been declared in November, 2020 and it is still enforce till today.

Input received indicated that online video meeting was held between top leaders of Arakan Army (AA) and Myanmar Army (MA) to sign formal bilateral Ceasefire recently. However, signing of formal ceasefire agreement could not be reached till today.

PCJSS:  According to enquiry conducted about report on PCJSS having camp near Devasuri Village, Lunglei District, Mizoram , it was ascertained that PCJSS has indeed physical training camp near Devasuri Village, Lunglei District, Mizoram. Around 140-150 PCJSS cadres (some new recruits) had undergone joint refresher course (excluding arms/weapons training) w.e.f. 8th February, 2021 and the training was likely to be completed by the end of February,2021.

            This training camp is located at around 4 kms in the eastern side of Devasuri Village, Lunglei District which is about 1(one) hour walking distance from the said village. Being a temporary camp, it was intended to be  occupied only during the training period i.e. during the month of February and  will be vacated after the training course is over. As such, the camp is not considered as a PCJSS designated camp and no specific name is given.

            Among the instructors of this camp, the most well-known of themincludes S/S Major KetuBabu, S/S Major Jubur, S/S Lt.Soumitra, S/S Lt.Nitia, S/S Capt. Ranjan.

4) Human Trafficking :

                As per the instruction of Govt. of India, four Anti-human Trafficking Units  (AHTU) has been established in Aizawl District, Lunglei District, Champhai District and Kolasib District under Mizoram Police Department.

 

B.        Present Scenario:

 

Action taken by Govt. of Mizoram and Mizoram Police Department in the field of Anti-Human Trafficking are as follows:

 

  1. Superintendent of Police CID (Crime) was appointed as state Anti Human Trafficking Nodal Officer by Police Headquarters, Mizoram vide order No. CB/PHQ/C/307/ 2010/75 dt. 02/11/2010 email ID of Nodal Officer is ahtumizoram@gmail.com.

 

  1. Police Headquarters order No. CB/PHQ/C/307/08/311 dt.12/07/2010 appointed all Addl. SP and Dy. SP as Anti-Human Trafficking Nodal Officers in their respective district.

 

  1. Whenever information is received regarding Human Trafficking, the case will be taken up by Anti-Human Trafficking Unit(AHTU) Team set up at Aizawl, Lunglei, Champhai and Kolasib District along with representative of State Department and NGOs, within their respective jurisdictions under the supervision of concerned District Superintendent of Police and the Nodal Officer, Anti-Human Trafficking, Mizoram. However, in regards to remaining District where AHTUs are not set up viz. (Serchhip, Mamit, Lawngtlai and Siaha, Saitual, Khawzawl and Hnahthial), if such report is received, AHTU Team will be temporarily set up by the  concerned District Superintendent of Police in consultation with the Nodal Officer, Anti-Human Trafficking, Mizoram and the Team so setup will take up the Human Trafficking cases.

 

 

  1. Recently, Mizoram State have some inter-state Human Trafficking cases. The Investigating Officer/Senior Officer along with lady officer is usually dispatched to conduct rescue operation in such cases. Co-operation with NGOs is required for rescue and rehabilitation of trafficked victims and to have a successful combat against Human Trafficking.

 

Workshop for Police Officers and NGOs:

 

            It is proposed to conduct Workshop and Training to Sensitized Mizoram Police Officers and NGOs in connection with Laws regarding Human Trafficking. Fund require for this is being sought from the Central Government. Training/Seminar Proposed to be conducted are as follows:

 

            Awareness campaign : Under the overall guidance of Nodal Officer, State Anti-Human Trafficking Unit, awareness is generated through TV talk shows in local channels, radio talk show and live phone in programmes. Circulars have been issued to the public through Young Mizo Association, the largest NGO in Mizoram and awareness campaigns have been conducted in Schools and Colleges.

 

            Sensitizing police personnel posted on Bus Stands, Bus Terminals and Railway Stations (if any) on human trafficking and also to keep a close watch on suspicious activities have been made recently. Similarly, police personnel posted at Police Stations and at various check posts and borders of the State are being sensitized on issues relating to human trafficking.

 

State Level Conference on Human Trafficking for Judicial Officers and Police Officers was proposed to be held on March 2020 but was cancelled due to Covid-19 pandemic.

MINUTES OF THE MEETING OF STATE LEVEL COMMITTEE ON FAKE INDIAN CURRENCY NOTES (FICN) HELD AT THE OFFICE CHAMBER OF DGP, MIZORAM 0N 8TH MARCH, 2021 @ 11:00 AM.

 

The meeting of State Level Committee on Fake Indian Currency Notes (FICN) was held on 8.3.2021 at 11:00 AM at the Office chamber of DGP, Mizoram, Aizawl. The meeting was chaired by Mr. SBK Singh IPS, DGP, Mizoram and members listed below were present in the meeting.

 

Members present:

 

  1. Miss Veena Bharti, IPS, Addl. Deputy Director, SIB, Mizoram.
  2. Mr. Rakesh Paweriya, IPS, SP CID (Crime), Mizoram.
  3. Mr. Zosangliana, MPS, AIG (Hqrs.), Mizoram.
  4. Mr. K. Lalremsanga, MPS, SP CID (SB), Mizoram.
  5. Miss Mary L. Deng, General Manager, RBI.

 

  1. Organizing training on FICN :-

The meeting decided that training on FICN should be conducted where 2 to 3 Police Officers from each Police Stations should participate in four Phase training. It was also discussed that RBI to deploy personnel for the training through online or if it is not possible either physically or online and to send across training material to IG (Trg)/ Mizoram Police Training Branch.

 

  1. Database of FICN cases :-
  1. It was decided that SP CID (Crime) should compile status on FICN cases and find out how many FICN samples are sent to Nasik to get expert opinion on FICN.
  2. Detail Case Status of FICN ie. Status of Chargesheet, FR, judicial custody, bails, judgement of court should be compiled.
  3. It was decided that previous records should be scrutinized and find out how many habitual offenders are there.
  4. While conducting investigation on FICN cases, all possible routes of FICN, full chain, leading to source of FICN, Carriers, outlets etc should be established.

 

  1. Features of FICN to be circulated to all Police Stations :-

The meeting decided that RBI should provide booklet on features of FICN to SP CID (Crime) for further circulation to all Police Stations so that I.Os will be able to identify FICN in search and seizures.

 

  1. Miscellaneous :-
  1. The meeting discussed that all ATM machines should be incorporated with features to detect FICN and if possible testing of the ATMs whether they are working properly. Same should be done periodically by Branch managers.
  2. Co-ordination of Nodal Officers of FICN at Bank Branches with Police Stations should be maintained.
