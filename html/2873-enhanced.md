**A. Front page of The Aizawl Post newspaper. Dt. 5.10.2012**

**Police safely brought back 2(two) men of Vairengte****:**Vairengte Police brought back 2 men of Vairengte who were kidnapped by some miscreants at Hailakandi last thursday.

Pu Vanlalnghaka and Pu Sukla along with 2 women of Vairengte headed to Hailakandi on Wednesday to buy Gecko ( a typical lizard) which would cost 2 lakhs. According to information gathered, the 4 persons were taken to a nearby village of Hailakandi, in which, the 2 women went back due to the very bad condition of the village road. When the 2 men reached the village both of them were taken to a dark hut and then tied up their hands with ropes, after sometimes, the miscreants asked Pu Sukla, who took his car to go, and bring back the 2 women. Pu Sukla started his car and drove away towards Hailakandi to his known friend's house.

Pu Vanlalnghaka was then taken by the miscreants to find his 3 friends back. Pu Nghaka slipped away into a Muslim gathering place, as he was having some bruises and blood in his body, he was attended by some Muslim gathering people, thereby, the miscreants left him to escape from being noticed by others.

Some locals of Hailakandi took Pu Nghaka to a police station, in which, Pu Sukla also already reported at Lala Police Station, Hailakandi Police informed Vairengte Police and the later Police went to Hailakandi at around 9:00PM on Wednesday and safely brought back Pu Vanlalngahaka and Pu Sukla to Vairengte last Thursday.

**B. Front page of The Zozam Times newspaper. Dt. 5.10.2012**

****Ambush attempt on Union Minister entourage****:** The unknown militants laid an attempt to ambush the entourage of Union Minister of State, KC Venugopal yesterday evening at around 5:30 to 6:30 PM. The entourage included the Mizoram Health Minister Lalrinliana Sailo and other officials. The entourage was on its way back to Aizawl after the Minister visited the site of Tuirial Hydro Electric Project. They were expected to flown back to Aizawl by Helicopter but due to bad weather, the helicopter could not be airlifted.**

It is reported that at around 1 KM from Chemphai, one of the Commandos of the 5th IR Bn. spotted unknown militant with a gun by the road side. The commandos quickly took their position, shield the officials and lights of all the vehicles turned off. After 15 minutes, after the commandos consider it safe to the officials, the entourage returned to Aizawl

**C. Front page of Vanglaini newspaper. Dt. 5.10.2012**

**No nomination was filed for VC by-election****:** The deadline for filing of nomination for Village Council by-election in 15 village councils within Aizawl district and Kolasib District which is to be held on October 18th 2012 has ended 3:00PM yesterday. Meanwhile Home Minister R.Lalzirliana Asked the HPC(D) not to create any trouble for the By-election of Village Council.

State Election Commission said that because of threats received by village leaders no nomination was filed till October 4th 2012, the last date for filing nomination. Those who had taken out the form for nomination returned the same to the Returning Officer.

SEC, however make an effort to conduct Village Council by-election for filing the nomination till October 9th 2012, 3:00PM. Nomination will be scrutinized on October 10th and withdrawal of nomination will be allowed till 4:00PM on the same day. The list of candidates will be published on October 11th, 2012.

All the candidates for by-election of 15 Village Councils will be considered as 'Independent candidate'. A candidate may write 'independent' on a space allotted for the name of his party and he may also write, within bracket, a party that supported him.

SEC asked for the support of all the people within Sinlung Hills Development Council (SHDC) in conducting an election in 15 villages.

Home Minister regretted that the HPC(D) issued threats to the people and threatening them not to file nomination and serious action be taken against those who defy the warning of HPC(D). The Home Minister asked HPC(D) not to disrupt the democracy process of election. He reiterates that the government wants to have a peaceful election.

Pu R. Lazirliana said threats and disruption of election would only result in disturbances in the region. The presence of not just Mizoram Police, but also Central Para Military Force will bring troubles for women and children, and the people in general.
