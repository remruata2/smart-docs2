CHRONOLOGY OF EVENT/ISSUES RELATING TO MIZORAM (SELECTION OF CANDIDATES FOR HIGHER TECHNICAL COURSES) RULES

6.7.1993 :   The State of Mizoram has no facilities for Higher Technical Studies within the State, and whereas, the seats allotted to the State of Mizoram for Higher Technical Studies by the Central Government is insufficient to meet the requirement of technical manpower of the State.

                        Therefore, the Governor of Mizoram is pleased to make rules to ensure fair and just selection of candidates for Higher Technical Studies in respect of Degree Courses of various disciplines in Engineering, Medical, Agriculture, AH and Veterinary etc.,”.

 

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1993 was made on 6.7.1993. Candidates are classified into 3 categories, candidates for MBBS/BDS, etc. will be selected in order of merit or mark percentage in PU Sc. or Class XII (Sc.) exams and marks scored in personal interview. Preference will be given to those who can speak in Mizo language.

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1993 was issued in the Mizoram Gazette.

Rule 5(a) Classified candidates into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats

(i) Children of the local permanent resident of the Mizoram state as category - I.

(ii) Children of the non- local permanent resident of the Mizoram state as category - II.

(iii) Children of the Central/other state Govt employees not permanently serving in Mizoram as category - III.

7.6.1999 :   The seats allotted to the State of Mizoram for Higher & Technical Studies by the Central Government is insufficient to meet the requirement of technical manpower in the State;

                        And whereas the State Government deems it expedient to have fair selection of promising candidates only for those limited seats offered to the State of Mizoram on merit by means of certain aptitude test.

                        Therefore, the Governor of Mizoram is pleased to make the following consolidated Rules for conducting Selection Test Examination to ensure the fair selection of candidates for such higher technical studies in respect of Degree Courses in various disciplines in Agricultural Sciences, Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Sciences, Medical Sciences and all branches of Engineering Science.

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999 was laid on 7.6.1999 which completely repealed Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Couses) Rules, 1993. Written examination i.e., entrance test was to be conducted through this rule and candidates are to be selected in accordance with their performance in the entrance test to qualify for studies in medical and engineering (technical studies).

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999  was  issued in the Mizoram Gazette.

Rule 5(1) All applicants are classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats

(a) Children of the local permanent resident of the Mizoram state as category – I.

(ii) Children of the non- local permanent resident of the Mizoram state as category – II.

(iii) Children of the Central/other state Govt employees not permanently serving in Mizoram as category – III.

 Explanation: For the purpose of this sub-rule, the term ‘Local permanent Residents’ means those who are indigenous people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently  in the state.

On the basis of marks obtained in the Selection Examination, Selection shall be made in order of merit from amongst eligible candidates from category –I first. In case sufficient number of eligible candidates are not available from category-I, the remaining seats shall be filled in, in order of merit by the eligible candidates from Category-II and Category-III likewise.

26.9.2000 : The Hon’ble Guwahati High Court  in the Bhanu Choudhury vs State Of Mizoram And Ors cases(vide Civil Rule(PIL) No.1239 of  1994)   held sub-rule (3) of Rules 5  of  Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999   to be ultra vires and  struck it  down.

 

15.7.2002 :             The seats allotted to the State of Mizoram for Higher and Technical Studies by the Central Government is insufficient to meet the requirement of Technical manpower in the state ;

                        An whereas the Hon’ble Gauhati High Court vide its judgement dt.26.9.2000 in Civil Rule (P.I.L) No.1239 of 1994 struck down the provision of sub-rule (3) of rule 5 of the Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999 ;

                        And whereas the State Government of Mizoram deems it expedient to have fair selection of promising candidates only for those limited seats offered and to strike some reasonable balance of reservation of seats for the different categories of candidates ;

                        Therefore, the Governor of Mizoram is pleased to make the following rules to amend the Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999.

 

Notification issued by Education & Human Resource Department, Government of Mizoram

            As the Hon’ble Gauhati High Court vide its  judgement dt.26.9.2000 in Civil Rule(PIL) No.1239 of  1994 struck  down the provision of  sub-rule (3) of Rule 5 of the Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999, the Governor of Mizoram made amendment  i.e   Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Amendment) Rules, 2002.

 

Amendment to Rule 5(3): The whole portion of Rule 5(3) of the principal rule shall be deleted and replaced by the following

 

‘Allotment of seats shall be made in the following order

(i)Category-I : Children of local permanent resident-85%

(ii) Category-II: Non-Local permanent residents-10%

(iii)Category-III: Children of Central and State Government  employees posted in the state who have  completed  not less than 2 years in Mizoram on the last  day of submission  of application-5%.

The MZP General Headquarters claimed that Chakmas are not indigenous people of the State of Mizoram and therefore should be excluded from the Category-I in the State Technical Entrance Examination (STEE).Due to this protest movement 13 (thirteen) student leaders were arrested by Mizoram Police.    

            On 25.9.2014, thousands of students took out a protest rally within Aizawl City and in different part of Mizoram resulting in the government making agreement with the agitating students’ leaders.

23.4.2015 : In supersession of Notification issued vide even No. dated 24.03.2015, the Governor of Mizoram is pleased to make the following Rules to amend the Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules, 1999 (hereinafter referred and as the Principal Rules) as follows, namely –

 

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Sixth Amendment) Rules, 2015 was issued in the Mizoram Gazette.

 

Amendment of Rule 5

Rule 5 of the Principal Rules shall be substituted by the following,

Sub rule (1 ) namely -(Method of Selection)

“(1) All applicants shall be classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats, viz :-

(a) Children of local permanent residents of the State of Mizoram, as Category – I.

(b) Children of other permanent residents of the State of Mizoram as Category – II.

(c) Children of Central/Other State Government employees staying in Mizoram as Category – III.

 

Explanation :

For the purpose of this sub-rule

(a) The term “Local permanent Residents” means those who are indigenous (Zo-Ethnic people who are native inhabitants) people of the State of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the State.

 

(b) The term “Other Permanent Residents” means those who are Non-indigenous (Non-Zo-Ethnic people who are non-native inhabitants) people of the State of Mizoram and those who are serving under the Government of Mizoram or Government Corporation or Autonomous

Bodies under the State of Mizoram, or other recognised organisations and in whose case, it can safely be inferred that they would reside permanently in Mizoram.

The Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules 2016 was again published on 20th April, 2016 and Zo-ethnic in Mizoram were in Category – I and  95 % of seats were reserved for them, Category – II has non-Zo ethnic and 4 % seats were reserved for them and the remaining 1 % seat was reserved for the children of Central Government Service personal staying in Mizoram ie, Category – III.

(1) All applicants shall be classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats, viz :-

(a) Children of local permanent residents of the State of Mizoram,

as Category - I

(b) Children of other permanent residents of the State of Mizoram

as Category - II

(c) Children of Central/Other State Government employees staying

in Mizoram as Category – III

Explanation :  For the purpose of this sub-rule

  1. The term ‘Permanent residents’ means those who are Zo-ethnic people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the state.
  2. The term ’other permanent residents’ means those who are (i) Children of non-Zo ethnic people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the state.

The notification is meant only for the purpose of State Medical and Technical Entrance Examination (SMATEE), 2017 and it is a temporary measure.

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Sixth Amendment) Rules, 2016 was issued in the Mizoram Gazette.

 

Methods of Selection : Rule 5

(1) All applicants shall be classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats, viz :-

(a)            Children of local permanent residents of the State of Mizoram as Category – I.

(b)            Children of other permanent residents of the State of Mizoram as Category – II.

(c) Children of Central/Other State Government employees staying in Mizoram as Category – III.

 

Explanation :  For the purpose of this sub-rule :

(a) The term ‘Permanent residents’ means those who are Zo-ethnic people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the state.

(b) The term ’other permanent residents’ means those who are (i) Children of non-Zo ethnic people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the state. (ii) Children of parents who are serving under the Government of Mizoram or Government corporation or Autonomous Bodies under the State Government who are regular employees of such organisation and in whose case, it can safely be inferred that they would reside permanently in Mizoram including children of deceased employees of the State Government whose families are living in Mizoram.

(c)  To be eligible to be considered under Category – III, either of the parents of the candidate(s) should have been residing and posted in Mizoram for at least three years.

 

(2) Percentage of seats for Category – I, II and III. 95 % of the total seats shall be observed for Category - I, 4 % of the Total seats shall be reserved for Category - II and 1 % of the total seats shall be reserved for Category - III. Determination of percentage for each Category has been made on the basis of average result of STEE (State Technical Entrance Examination) for the last 5 (five) years.

            The disciplines in which seats are to be allotted to Category – I, II and III will be decided by the Selection Board by preparing seat matrix.

            Vacant seats, if any due to unavailability of suitable candidates from amongst the Category-II and III shall be reverted back to Category – I.
