1. Today, i.e. 12.3.2018 @ 10:30 a.m, a procession called ‘Ram Humhalh Kawngzawh’ was organised under the aegis of Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) in Aizawl and in other towns of the State such as Lunglei, Champhai, Serchhip, Kolasib, Thenzawl, Vairengte, Sairang, Saitual, Tlabung, Bairabi, Bilkhawthlir, Zawlnuam and Mamit wherein a large number of students took to the streets. Situation was peaceful in all the towns and it has been reported that an effigy of Shri. Adil Khan, DC of Hailakandi District, who had reportedly given the firing order against MZP members on 10.3.2018, was burnt by MZP at Tlabung.

 

            In Aizawl, the procession began at 10:30 a.m from Chandmari in the North and from Sikulpuikawn in the South and converged at Vanapa Hall. Around 6,000 (six thousand) students participated in the procession who shouted slogans condemning the State Government for its alleged inaction relating to the border issue and their strong intention to construct Zofate Chawlhbuk at Zophai. A function was held in front of Vanapa Hall under the chairmanship of Michael Lalramsanga, Treasurer, MZP Gen. Hqrs. wherein  Lalnunmawia Pautu, Gen. Secy., MZP; B. Vanlaltana, Vice President, MZP; R.B. Lalmalsawma, President, MSU and Zorammawia, Vice President, Aizawl City Colleges Joint Students’ Union (ACCJSU) and Pu Vanlalruata, President, CYMA delivered speeches. Adamant with their intention to reconstruct Zofate Chawlhbuk, B. Vanlaltana, Vice President, MZP announced before the gathering that MZP will reconstruct 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' at Zophai on 14.3.2018 (Wednesday). They also appealed all political parties of the State not to politicise the issue in order to gain political advantage.

 

            MZP passed the following resolutions today :-

            i) MZP strongly condemned the Assam Government for invoking Section 144CrPC

            on the soil of Mizoram and also condemned the State Government for turning a

            deaf ear to the border issue.

            ii) Government of Mizoram should protect the boundary of the State.

            iii) MZP will continue to render utmost effort to protect Mizoram and her people.

            iv) 'Zofate Chawlhbuk' should be reconstructed.

 

            The processionists peacefully dispersed @ 1:30 p.m and no untoward incident has been reported from any part of the State today. Further input indicated that MZP will hold an Extended Office Bearers’ Meeting at their office tomorrow, i.e. 13.3.2018 @ 10:30 a.m and they are likely to discuss their future course of action regarding the border issue.
