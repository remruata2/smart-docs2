****SPECIAL REPORT****

No. MSB/CB/SR/2021/790

Dated: 29th Oct., 2021

**1.** Information received stated that Pu Lalfakawma (25) S/o Hunlawma of Chuhvel, Mamit District; Pu Lalmuanthanga (20) S/o Lalrinmawia of Chuhvel; Pu Baniram(18) S/o Lalthara and Pu Lalduhawma (28) S/o Ramoni both of Bawngva, Mamit District, left Chuhvel last night. i.e., 28.10.2021 @ around 10:00 p.m. and proceeded to Vandenglui near Thirphun tlang, Mizoram, which is located at around 3 kms from Kolalian village, Mamit District in pursuance of their agreement to purchase cows from 6 (six) non-Mizos (not yet identified) from Assam.

 Upon reaching the stated place early this morning i.e., 29.10.2021 @3:00 a.m., they were viciously attacked by the six non-Mizos from Assam with local made gun (Panghem) and machete and snatched away Rs.1,00,000 and 2 (two) Mobile Phones from their possession. As a result of the stated attack, Lalfakawma (25) S/o Hunlawma of Chuhvel succumbed to his injury at the spot and the dead body is being subjected to autopsy at Kawrthah CHC. Further, Pu Lalmuanthanga (20) S/o Lalrinmawia Chuhvel sustained grievous injuries and he was evacuated to Kawrthah CHC for medical treatment. He is still unconscious and is being referred to Mamit for further medical care and is en-route at the time of writing the report. Fortunately the other two managed to escape the scene unhurt. Information received stated that the identity of the assailants are not yet identified as the person who used to contact and made deal with the above mentioned non-Mizos is Pu Lalmuanthanga who is still unconcious. Investigation of the case is being underway.

 It may be mentioned that all the victims belongs to Bru community and no report of any communal tension or law &amp; order situation has been received due to the above mentioned incident.

****2.** Information received stated that a loud noise/explosion was heard at Zophai, Bairabi area near Pu Ch. Chhunga’s paddy field, early this morning i.e., 29.10.2021 @ 1:25 a.m., which is located around 1 km north of Inter-state COVID Screening Point, Bairabi. Upon immediate enquiry last night, no sign of blast was found within Pu Ch. Chhunga’s paddy field. Further enquiry was done today and it was found that the explosion was found to have occurred at one unoccupied Assam Forest Building, which is around 100 meters southwest of Pu Ch. Chhunga’s paddy field, near NH-154 and the blast is believed to have been caused by gelatine dynamite. No human casualty or destruction of valuable property occurred due to the blast. No further action could be taken up as the P.O. fell outside the jurisdiction of Bairabi PS.**
 Situation is under close watch.
