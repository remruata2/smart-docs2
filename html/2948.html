SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/29

Dated: 12th January, 2019.

 

On 11.1.2019, various Chakma civil society organisations namely, the All India Chakma Students’ Union (AICSU), the Rashtriya Chakma Tribal Sangh (RCTS), the All India Chakma Social Forum (AICSF) and the Central Mizoram Chakma Students’ Union (CMCSU) issued a joint Press Release stating that on 10.1.2019 (Thursday), a team of four youths identifying themselves to be members of Mizo Students’ Union (MSU) visited Tuichawngchhuah village and ordered the demolition of one under-construction house belonging to Puspo Ranjan Chakma, S/o Ranga Soga Chakma, a resident of Tuichawngchhuah in violation of Gauhati High Court judgement vide W.P. (C) No. 134 of 2016. The PR further alleged that these youths threatened to torch the entire village if their diktat was not obeyed and further demanded Rs. 2,000/- from the villagers to cover their conveyance cost. The PR added that the impoverished villagers could collect only about Rs. 300/- which was handed over to the MSU leaders. The PR also accused the Police duty at Tuichawngchhuah of colluding with the student body against the Chakmas. The NGOs demanded immediate action from the Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram, Pu Zoramthanga and the Director General of Police, Pu Balaji Srivastava, IPS for the registration of FIR against the concerned MSU leaders and the Police personnel stationed at Tuichawngchhuah. The PR demanded that appropriate compensation also be paid to the owner. 

On the same day, i.e. 11.1.2019, MSU Sub-Hqrs. Lungsen issued a statement on social media stating that on 10.1.2019, leaders of MSU Sub-Hqrs. Lungsen namely,
C. Laldinsanga, President; Pu Z.D. Vanlalsawia, Adviser; Pu F. Aitluanga, Adviser and Laldinpuia Punte visited Tuichawngchhuah Chakma settlement and found 1 (one) house under construction which was later dismantled on their request. The statement stressed that Tuichawngchhuah is an illegal settlement and strongly object construction of a new house by Chakma(s) in violation of Gauhati High Court order. The statement strongly opposed any illegal Chakma settlement and urged the Government to take appropriate action.

In connection to the above, information received indicated that 4 (four) members from MSU Sub-Headquarters, Lungsen visited Tuichawngchhuah on 10.1.2019 (Thursday) and found that one Puspo Ranjan Chakma S/o Ranga Soga Chakma was constructing a house at Tuichawngchhuah where he had already erected 10 (ten) bamboo poles. The poles were removed by the owner and construction was discontinued at the behest of the MSU. Information further added that Puspo Ranjan Chakma left Tuichawngchhuah 4 (four) years before and settled under Undarmanik village area cultivating land. He then intended to resettle back to Tuichawngchhuah by starting construction at a location which he claimed was his previous house site. Information also
