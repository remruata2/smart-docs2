1. Returnees of Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF) and Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram (BLFM) formed Mizoram Bru Returnees' Peace Accords Implementing Demand Committee (MBRPAIDC) on 31.7.2018. The objective of MBRPAIDC is to pursue for the successful implementation of the provisions of Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed between BNLF and Government of Mizoram on 26.4.2005. The following persons are elected members of MBRPAIDC :-

 

                        Chairman                   :           Pu Surjyamani Reang (Ex-BNLF Leader)

                        Vice Chairman          :           Pu Lalrinkima Molshoy (Ex-BLFM Leader)

                        Gen. Secretary          :           Pu Bruno Meska (Ex-BNLF Leader)

                        Asst. Secretary          :           Pu Chandramony Molshoy (Ex-BNLF Leader)

                        Treasurer                   :           Pu Indiarai Apeto (Ex-BLFM Leader)

                        Fin. Secretary            :           Pu Lalduhawma (Ex-BNLF Leader)

 

MBRPAIDC will also take initiatives on the following points :-

            i)         Implementation of Social Development Project for Brus.

            ii)        Grouping of Bru refugees in villages not less than 500 households.

            iii)       One-time payment of cash assistance for Bru Refugees.

            iv)       Similar cash assistance should be given to the Bru refugees who had already                      been rehabilitated in Mizoram.

            v)        Families of Ex-BNLF and Ex-BLFM should also be given rehabilitation                              packages.

            vi)       Rechristening of 'Reang' to 'Bru' as per MoU.

            vii)      Reservation of jobs under the Central and State Government for the Brus.

9.         As per information received, re-identification of Mizoram Brus in the 6 (six) Refugee Camps in Tripura, viz. Kaiskau, Khakchangpara, Hamsapara, Hazacherra, Asapara and Naisingpara; who were willing to return to Mizoram was conducted from 16.8.2018 to 18.8.2018. 425 families reportedly submitted their willingness to return to Mizoram while 90 families who claimed to be from Mizoram but were not in the Identification List of 2016 and E/Roll of Mizoram reported themselves for identification. After conclusion of the re-identification process for repatriation, officials representing the 3 districts of Kolasib, Lunglei and Mamit proceeded towards Mamit on the evening of 18.8.2018. In the meantime, NGO Coordination Committee had the notion that several number of Form-6 for Summary E/Roll Revision was also collected by these officials while conducting re-identification for repatriation. These returning officials were stopped and checked by Langkaih and Taitesena Group YMA at Zawlnuam area and they were later joined by NGOCC members from CYMA, MZP and MSU headed by Pu R. Lalngheta, Vice President, CYMA who came from Aizawl. No documents relating to Summary E/Roll Revision was found by the NGOCC in the possession of these officials conducting re-identification. 

 

It has been reported that Mizoram Bru Displaced People's Coordination Committee (MBDPCC) leaders headed by their President, T. Laldingliana visited the 3 Bru Refugee Camps at Langkaih Area (namely Kaiskau, Hamsapara and Khakchangpara) on 17.8.2018. These MBDPCC leaders campaigned against returning to Mizoram if their additional demands after the signing of the 'Four Corner Agreement' were not met. They also intended to organise a Sit-in demonstration at Kanhmun on 22.8.2018 to press their demand.

 

Information has been received that Mizoram Bru Displaced People's Forum (MBDPF) submitted their report on the Re-verification/Re-identification of Bru families conducted on 16-18.8.2018 to the Ministry of Home Affairs today, i.e. 20.8.2018.  The report :-

i) Accused the Government of Mizoram (GOM) of attempting to segregate the Mizoram Bru Refugees between those 'willing' and 'unwilling' to return to Mizoram while claiming that everyone was willing to return after completion of necessary documentations.

ii) Claimed that officials of GOM came for collection of names of only those who are willing to be repatriated on 25.8.2018 while they thought that repatriation will start only after completion of works by the GOI, GOT and GOM.

iii)       Accused that the officials intended to identify the left out families from identification held in November 2016 after the completion of the identified families while they claimed that the left out families were to be identified first as per the Agreement.

iv)       Accused the officials of refusing to take spot decisions on new-borns or bifurcation of families and to give out 'identification slip'/'acknowledgement slip' to the identified families and 'verification slip' to the left out families who reported themselves.

v)        Claimed that the inmates were busy with getting necessary requirement for documentary proof, etc. for the repatriation and when they were all willing to turn up for the Re-identification on 18.8.2018, the officials of GOM closed the process at 11:50 a.m and left for Kanchanpur. They also claimed that the leaders of MBDPF met Tripura officials to inform them that the inmates were willing to turn up for verification and also requested the Tripura officials to inform the officials of Mizoram to conduct verification programme.
