A. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES, THE AIZAWL POST, VANGLAINI & NEWSLINK newspapers Dt.8.3.2013

MPSA Special Assembly: A Special Assembly of Mizoram Police Service Association (MPSA) held yesterday at Arya Mess, Police Headquarters, Aizawl was graced by R.Lalzirliana, Home Minister as the Chief Guest. The function was presided over by Lalbiakzama (MPS), President MPSA and inducted 31 new members. The chief guest praised the efficiency of Mizoram police officials in solving difficult cases.

            MPSA is displeased at the Govt. for snatching the duty post of Dy.SP, CPW from MPS, and also at the Govt. notification to delete it from MPS Service Rules. They demand to regain the post, and if that cannot be done, they demand the Govt. to give MPS the rank of Addl. SP. Moreover, while different organised service like MCS, MJS, MF&AS and MMS can hold Secretary level post, MPS, one of the most senior service cadres do not have any Secretary level post. MPS leaders expressed their displeasure at the Govt. for taking 5 posts in ACB from them and they will request the Govt. to compensate.

 

B. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES,VANGLAINI & NEWSLINK newspapers Dt. 8.3.2013

Big arms haul: A joint team of 26th Assam Rifles and Special Narcotic PS Staff CID (Crime)) recovered 23 Nos. of AK 47 rifles, 1 (one) LMG and 1(one) Browning Automatic Rifle from Aizawl to Lengpui Road (near Lengte) on Thursday, ie, 7.3.2013. Three persons, who hailed from North Tripura were arrested in this connection, the three arrested persons are Chakma and appeared to be members of an insurgent group Santi Bahini (A Bangladesh militant outfit).

 

C. Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt.8.3.2013

ACB registered 2 cases: PC Lalremsiama of Zarkawt lodged an FIR to ACB regarding his suspicion on Silpouline supply and its bill payment between 23rd June 2006 and 3rd July 2006; ACB registered it for preliminary enquiry on 4th March 2013. ACB also registered preliminary enquiry on 4th March regarding the misappropriation of 5504.97 quintals of rice (Grade A) at Chawngtlai Supply Centre. PC Vanlalruata, the then storekeeper, is now a pensioner.
