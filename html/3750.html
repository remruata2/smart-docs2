ARAKAN ARMY

  1. The Arakan Army is a Rakhine insurgent group in Myanmar (Burma), founded on the 10th April 2009. It is the armed wing of the United League of Arakan (ULA) and is currently led by Major General Twan Mrat Naing.
  2. The purpose of the AA is to protect Arakan people and to establish peace, justice, freedom and development.
  3. The AA is a participant in the Kachin conflict, fighting alongside the Kachin Independence Army (KIA) against the Tatmadaw (Myanmar Armed Forces). Most AA soldiers were originally trained at the KIA Military Academy; however, the AA has additional training camps in Rakhine State.
  4. The Arakan Army (AA) was founded on 10 April 2009 along with its political wing, the United League of Arakan (ULA), in what it describes as its "temporary headquarters" in Laiza, Kachin State. The group has stated that it advocates for:
  • self-determination for the multi-ethnic Arakanese population.
  • the safeguarding and promotion of the national identity and cultural heritage of the Arakan people.
  • the "national dignity" and best interests of the Arakan people.
  1. Following training, the group had planned to return to Arakan State and fight for self-determination; however, with the outbreak of fighting in Kachin State in June 2011, they were unable to return. As a result, they took up arms against the Myanmar Army in support of the KIA.
  2. In 2014, the AA started a settlement in Rakhine State (home land) near the border with Bangladesh and another near the border of Thai-Myanmar with which it has become much stronger and its combat abilities have been positively impacted.
