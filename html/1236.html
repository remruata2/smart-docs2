i) Security scenario in Barak Valley (Assam) and Mizoram, particularly areas bordering with Bangladesh: The security scenario of the western part of Mizoram bordering with Bangladesh is comparatively peaceful. However, due to the occasional incident of kidnappings/abduction occurred in the border areas because of the porousness of the Indo-Bangladesh border required more attention. The border between Mizoram and Bangladesh, especially Mamit District side of Mizoram and Bangladesh, deserves special attention in and around Dampa Wildlife Sanctuary Abduction cases have had occured in which 5 (five) persons have been abducted by NLFT on the month of Feb.2013. Subsequently, 3(persons) have been abducted by the combined team of NLFT and BDFM in the later part of the same year, out of which 2 persons have been released on 20.01.2014 and the abductors still keep one of the abductees Deep Mandal, Airtel Surveyor in their captivity. However, the Mizoram Government is continuing its efforts to secure his safe and early release. In this regard, an outpost of IR Bn. had been established at Chikha in Dampa Tiger Reserve area, Mamit District. The security condition of the said areas is being kept under constant watch.

iv) Trans-border crimes and illegal movement through border:   

            (a) On 6.6.2011, two employees of ABCI had been abducted by Assam based militant group named UDLA from ABCI camp near Meidum village, Kolasib district. The gang with the abductees left the camp by using the company's vehicle. Hence, Bairabi PS C/No.7/11 dt.6.6.11 u/s 365/386/395/34 IPC r/w 25(1B) (b) Arms Act had been registered.

            The hostages were rescued on 16.6.2011 after a firefight between Joint Operation Team of CID (SB) and 1st IR Bn. RAF of Mizoram Police and the Armed gang at the outskirt of Banglabasha village of Hailakandi district of Assam. After an encounter which lasted for about 30 minutes, the so-called UDLA fled towards the jungle leaving the two hostages behind. The commander of the group, before fleeing, shot at the right abdomen of one of the hostages, namely Premlal Dhiman, Project Manager, ABCI causing serious injury and the abductees were rescued safely. Neither the Police nor the armed group sustained any injury as a result of the encounter. On 26.10.2012, two of the accused persons suspected to be involved in the said abduction case namely, S/S President Doinaram Reang and S/S Pte. Zohmangaiha were arrested who admitted their involvement during interrogation.

 

            (b) On 25.3.2012, 6 (six) employees of ABCI were abducted by about 20 cadres of NLFT militant outfit, who were having permanent hideout inside Bangladesh. The incident took place at a small ABCI designated camp called Lamba Sora located in between New Sachan and Mauzam, Lunglei district and the place of occurrence was not far away from Indo-Bangladesh border. The abductors took the hostages to the nearby Tarunpara village, Rangamati district, Bangladesh. The motive behind the abduction as claimed by NLFT was irregular payment of the so-called taxes imposed to be paid by the company to the outfit. The abductors plainly asserted that any attempt to launch rescue operation would invite instant killing of all hostages without hesitation.

The hostages were released on 30.4.2012 after 35 days long captivity inside Bangladesh. The hostages after their release reached BSF camp at Rajivnagar (Tuipuibari-II), Mamit district. Hence, Tlabung PS C/No.3/12 Dt. 30.3.2012 u/s 363/395 IPC r/w 25(1B) (a) Arms Act had been registered.

           

            (c) On 25.11.2012 @ 8:00pm, five Borok National Council of Tripura (BNCT) cadres headed by Vanlalliana of Chamdur District, Lawngtlai District, Mizoram abducted three Bengali timber traders from Tuipuibari-II (Rajiv Nagar). Two of the abductors were wearing jungle camouflage uniform and armed with carbine while the rest three were in civil dress and carried sticks. The abductors took away teak royalty documents/permits along with cash amounting Rs.30, 000/- and left towards Bangladesh border through the jungle. The abductors demanded payment of ransom money of Rs.15 lakhs each for the release of the hostages. Accordingly, W.Phaileng PS C/No.36/12 dt.26.11.12 u/s 364(A)/171/34 IPC r/w 25(1A) Arms Act had been registered. Further, information received stated that the three abductees Bengali Teak (timber) traders abducted by the combined group of NLFT and BNCT on 25.11.2012 from Tuipuibari-II were held at NLFT camp known as “Hnahbok Camp” located inside Kasalong forest west of Zopui village in Sazek hills of Bangladesh. It is learnt that families of the abductees were last contacted on 02.12.2012 through mobile phone by the abductors urging them to immediately pay up their ransom demand of Rs 30 lakhs. However, the abductees were released on 12.12.2012 after their families reportedly had paid Rs. 9 lakhs of ransom money to the kidnappers.

 

            (d) On the morning of 19.2.2013, 5(Five) employees of Forest Deptt. Namely - (1) Vanlalruatkima @ Nelson-a, Hunthar Veng, Aizawl-JCB Operator. (2) Lalrammawia, Hunthar Veng, Aizawl, JCB Handyman. (3) Vanlalfaka Reang, Damparengpui, MR employee, Forest Deptt. (4) Lalsangmawia, Bethlehem, W.Phaileng, MR employee, Forest Deptt. (5) Lalliansanga, Teirei Forest, MR employee, Forest Deptt. were abducted by NLFT/WNDLFM (Western National Development Liberation Front of Mizoram) from their camp, located in between Tuipuibari and Damparengpui, Mamit District. Hence WPLG C/No.4/13 dt.20.2.13 u/s 364(A)/34 IPC r/w 25(1A) Arms Act was registered.

 

            On 29.3.2013, after an intensive and dedicated pursue of the abductor’s activities especially by SP CID(SB) team, the five hostages were released  without  giving any ransom.

            (e) On 5.6.2013 at around 10:00 AM, three (3) persons of Gautam Construction Company workers namely - (i) Nizam Uddin (28) of Karimganj (Labour Supervisor) (ii) Mitun Bagani (18) of Silchar (Labourer) (iii) Dirgudon Chakma (30) @ Bijoy of Rajiv Nagar (Driver) were kidnapped by militants suspected to be a combined group of NLFT (National Liberation Front of Tripura) and BNDLFM (Bru National Development Liberation Front of Mizoram) from the village of Bodalsuri (4Km from Rajiv Nagar), Mamit District. Sources said that the abductors are around 8 members. As per input shared by Tripura State Special Branch, it has been confirmed that kidnappers were the same group i.e. NLFT who had kidnapped 5 M/R workers of Forest Department on 18th Feb. 2013.

            According to input received, families of two(2) of the kidnapped persons of Gautam Construction Pvt. Ltd. namely (1) Nizam Uddin and (2) Mitum Bagani are negotiating with the NLFT for payment of ransom on 19.7.2013 at an undisclosed location at Kanchanpur, North Tripura through an unidentified middleman. The amount of ransom to be paid though not immediately known appeared to have been realized by families of the two Bengali captives after selling off their properties.

 

(f) On Dt.23.11.2013, member of National Liberation Front of Tripura(NLFT) and Bru National Development Liberation Front of Mizoram(BNDLFM) militants abducted three persons namely (i) Deepak Mandal S/o Nikhil Mandal, Indas,Bakura, West Bengal (ii) Pu Sanglianthanga @ Maliana S/o VL Hmuaka, Mamit Charkawn, Taxi Driver (MZ-08-1308) (ii) Pu Lalzamliana S/o Buanga (L) C/o K.Vanlalchhuanga, Zuangtui Muanna Veng, Aizawl (driver 207 Pickup-MZ-01G-5860) from Old Chika Anti-poaching Camp area within Dampa Sanctuary, Mamit District. The three hostages were taken to the hideout of the militants in Bangladesh.The Abductors had demanded Rs. 5 Crore ransom for the release of the hostages. In this regards, West Phaileng Police Station Case No.24/2013 Dt.25.11.2013 u/s 364 A/379/34 IPC R/w Sec 25(1A) Arms Act has been  registered.

 

On 21.1.2014, two of the three persons abducted by National Liberation Front of Tripura (NLFT) and Bru Democratic Front of Mizoram (BDFM) on 23.11.2013 were handed over to Mizoram Police CID(Special Branch) team near Silsuri in the border with Bangladesh @ 11:30AM. The three hostages were abducted from Chikha Anti-Poaching Camp area inside the Dampa Tiger Reserve in Mamit District. The abductors (NLFT) had handed over Pu Sanglianthanga @ Maliana S/o V.L. Hmuaka, Mamit Charkawn and Pu Lalzamliana S/o Buanga(L) C/o K. Vanlalchhuanga, Zuangtui Muanna Veng, Aizawl/Kolasib to CID (Special Branch) Source stationed at Lungtian, Bangladesh on 20.1.2014 at 3:23 PM and subsequently the CID Source brought them upto the international border.

            The abductors had demanded Rs.5 Crore as ransom for release of the three hostages and after 58 days of intense negotiation by CID(Special Branch) sources stationed inside Bangladesh, the abductors had finally released two of the three hostages without any payment of ransom.  Unfortunately, the abductors still hold Pu Deep Mondal in their captivity. However, the Mizoram Government is continuing its efforts to secure the safe and early release of Shri Deep Mondal.

vii) Miscellaneous/ Any other issues:

 

a)         The Hmar People's Convention ‘Democratic’ (HPC ‘D’), a Manipur based militant outfit is also a matter of concern since it has indulged in felonious activities such as forced collection of money from village authorities, Government employees and Contractors within their demand area which comprises  over 30 villages in the northern part of the State. The HPC ‘D’ issued diktat to boycott the Village Council Election held in February 2012 and as such, 15 Village Councils within its demand area dared not conduct VC election. Moreover, the HPC ‘D’ had also dissolved as many as 37 Branches of YMA within its demand area. All these activities had drawn the attention of the Government resulting in the arrest of 3 (three) top leaders viz. H.Zosangbera, Chairman at IGI Airport, New Delhi on 17.7.2012 and two armed wing leaders S/S Army Chief Ropuia and S/S Dy. Army Chief Biaknunga at Silchar Airport on 10.6.2012.

           

            On 21.11.2012, S/S Army Chief Ropuia was released on bail on medical ground. S/S Chairman H.Zosangbera and S/S Dy. Army Chief Biaknunga were also released on bail on 6.2.2013.

          Input received on 19.02.2014 regarding the presence of a group of Armed HPC (D) cadres numbering approximate 10 to 13 at Parvachawm and Senvawn villages located south-west of Parbung, Churachandpur District had corroborated with debriefing of two HPC (D) deserters who recently surrendered before SP, CID (Special Branch). This HPC’D’ group had been intensifying their unlawful activities such as, Extortion, Intimidation, etc.. ever since electioneering of the recently concluded Legislative Assembly Election in November, 2013. The group is also responsible for the shooting of Pu B. Zahunga of Khawlian on 20.11.2013.

 

After preparing an elaborate plan for operation, a joint team of Mizoram Police assisted by Major H.Lalnunsangi, Officer Commanding of Team 2/1 CTIS Det, Eastern Command Counter Intelligence Unit stationed in Aizawl and 34th Assam Rifles (having AOR of the area in Manipur) conducted simultaneous raids on Parvachawm and Senvawn villages during 2:00 AM – 9:00 AM of 19.02.2014. The following HPC (D) cadres were apprehended and also recoveries of the following items were made during the raid.
