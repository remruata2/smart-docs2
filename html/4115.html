*  Bangladesh Government - KNF dialogue:

            After a series of  online meeting, "First round of Physical Peace Talk" between Kuki-Chin National Front/Army (KCNA) and Government of Bangladesh (GoB) represented by the Peace Committee was held on 5th November, 2023 at Hmunlai village, near Ruma bazar, Bandarbun District, Bangladesh. Although no definite agreements were made, the meeting, agreed to maintained cease-fire between both the parties until the next peace talk. The KNF has submitted a draft of possible and proposed peace agreement with 6(six) point demands to the Prime Minister of Bangladesh through Peace Establishment Committee. The second round of bilateral physical meeting between the two parties is was  conducted on 05.03.2024 at Bethel Para Communiuty Centre in Ruma Upazila of Bandar district. This m,eeting martks a pivotal step towards resolving a conflict that has displaced hundreds and stoked tension in the region. The meeting spearheaded by Zilla Parishad Chairman Kya Shew hla, the 13 member delegation discussed significant issues, including the release of KNF prisoners and the non-return of Bawm community members from Mizoram until a peace agreement is secured. The meeting not only reaffirmed the parties commitment to peace but also illustrated the potential for constructive dialogue in addressing longstanding issues. The next round of talks is not fixed yet till date.

(b) Current trends in smuggling of narcotics drugs, arms, explosives, WLS and other contrabands :

Drug Smugglings

            Mizoram being geographically close to the Golden/Death Triangle and due to the porous unfenced international border with Myanmar became a frequented route for drug smugglers in recent years. Mizoram which also shares inter-state boundary with three other Indian states is also used as a gateway for trafficking of drugs and other contraband items from Myanmar to Mainland India. Also, the recent unrest in Myanmar causing the decline in the number of military check gates on the Myanmar side and the Myanmar Nationals seeking asylum in India acting as a peddlers/dealers to support themselves financially resulted in the increase of drug trafficking in
Indo-Myanmar border. As communal violence occurred in Manipur, which was reportedly one of the major smuggling routes, smugglers could no longer used Manipur as their route; the peaceful state of Mizoram become more vulnerable to such smugglers.
