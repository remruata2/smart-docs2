****MIZORAM AT A GLANCE****

**Introduction:**

** Mizoram is situated in the north eastern part of India and is flanked by Tripura &amp; Bangladesh on the west, Myanmar on the east &amp; south and Manipur as well as Assam in the north. The tropic of Cancer runs just on the southern periphery of the State capital. Although Mizoram is tiny state having an area of only 21,081sq. kms., it has as much as **404 kms/510 kms. of**international boundary with Myanmar and 318 kms with Bangladesh. Aizawl is the capital of Mizoram. There are 8 districts as mentioned below.**

1. Aizawl.
2. Lunglei.
3. Champhai.
4. Saiha.
5. Lawngtlai.
6. Mamit.
7. Kolasib.
8. Serchhip.

As per the census of India 2011, the literacy rate of Mizoram is 91.58 % with a population of 1,091,014. The density of population is 52 persons per square km and the sex ratio is 975.

**Tourists’ attraction :**

The Blue Mountain (Phawngpuitlang) with an altitude of 7100m and situated in Lawngtlai District is the highest mountain in Mizoram. It is covered with blooming evergreen vegetation. Palak Dil is the largest lake in Mizoram and it is situated in Saiha district.

**Political Developments :**

Mizoram attained Autonomous District Council in 1952 under the provision of sub-para of the Sixth Schedule of the Constitution of India. Under the same provision, three tribes viz.Lai (Pawi), Mara (Lakher) and Chakma were grouped together in an Autonomous Region called the Pawi-Lakher Autonomous Region.

In the first Mizo District Council Election, the Mizo Union won the election capturing 23 out of 24 seats.

On 21st January 1972, Mizoram became Union Territory and as per the North East Area (Reorganization) Act, 1971, the Mizo District Council Stood dissolved and the three Autonomous District Councils were upgraded to the status of Autonomous District Councils w.e.f. 29th April 1972. Eventually, Mizoram attained statehood on 21st August 1986.

**Insurgency :**

1. **Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF)** : The BNLF was formed in the month of April 1997 with an objective of creating an Autonomous District Council within the Bru inhabited areas of Mamit District in the western part of Mizoram. The first heinous crime committed by the BNLF was killing of Lalzawmliana, a Forest Game Watcher at Persang village in Dampa Forest Sanctuary in Mamit District. The BNLF thereafter resorted to violent activities such as ambush of Security forces, extortions, kidnappings and arsons. However, to end mistrust amongst different communities and militant activities of BNLF, Govt. of Mizoram initiated Peace Talk with leaders of BNLF w.e.f. 7.9.2001 and after 12 rounds of talks, Memorandum of Understanding was signed on 26.4.2005. The BNLF surrendered 9 different types of arms and 661 nos. of ammunitions.

2. **Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram (BLFM)** : The BLFM was formed in December 2001 due to differences in ideology amongst BNLF members under the auspices of NLFT (BM) while negotiation was going on between Govt. of Mizoram and BNLF. The main objective was repatriation of Bru refugees staying at various Refugee Camps in Tripura. In the middle part of 2002, another outfit called Bru National Front (Independence Movement) was formed by dissidents of BNLF/BLFM. After functioning for over a year, the BNF merged with BLFM in the BLFM in October, 2003. The first crime committed by the BLFM was kidnapping of a Mizo youth F. Lalfamkima, Sumo driver, near West Phaileng, Mamit District. However, after commission several subversive activities, the BLFM numbering 804 members surrendered to the Govt. of Mizoram on 26.10.2006 with 66 arms of different types of and 45 rounds of ammunitions.

 **At present, both the BNLF and BLFM have become defunct organisation.**

3. **Hmar People’s Convention (HPC)**: The HPC was formed on 3.7.1986 with an objective of creating Hmar Autonomous Council in the northeast areas of Mizoram and some portion of Cachar District of Assam. The HPC resorted violent activities like extortion, kidnapping, blasting of bridges and power line and ambush of security forces. The HPC had killed 8 Police personnel between 1989 to 1994. After 13 rounds of Peace Talks 331 cadres of HPC surrendered to Govt. of Mizoram and Memorandum of Understanding was signed on 27.7.1994. The HPC surrendered 40 nos. of different types of weapons and 190 rounds of ammunitions.

4. **Hmar People’s Convention (Democratic) HPC-D** : The HPC-D was formed in 1996 by remnants of the HPC who were disgruntled with the MoU signed between HPC and Govt. of Mizoram in 1994. The HPC-D resorted to violence from the outset of its formation like extortion, kidnapping and ambush of security forces. They had killed 10 Police personnel till 2.9.2008.

 The HPC-D had split into two factions on 29.9.2011. One faction is led by the outset President Lalhmingthanga Sanate and the other faction led by H. Zosangbera. HPC-D H. Zosangbera faction is more active and now in possession of 34 nos. of arms of different types and 950 ammunitions.

 Peace Talk was held between the Govt. of Mizoram and HPC-D on 11.11.2011 but no agreement could be reached. Next round of talk is unlikely at present.

 The HPC-D intensified their criminal activities since early January this year i.e. 2012 by interfering in the V/C election for which State Election Commission was compelled to postpone election for V/C of 13 villages within Aizawl District and 2 others from Kolasib District. Besides, the HPC-D forcibly threatened as many as 37 YMA Branches to dissolve within their demand area. They also collected force donation from VC’s, Govt, Employees and Contractors within their demand area which draw the attention of the Govt. resorting in the arrest of 3 top leaders viz. two armed wing leaders S/S Army Chief Ropuia and S/S Dy. Army Chief Biaknunga at Silchar Airport on 10.6.2012 and H. Zosangbera, Chairman at IGI Airport, New Delhi on 17.7.2012. The three leaders had been kept under judicial custody. S/S Army Chief Lalropuia was released on bail on 21.11.2012 on medical ground.
