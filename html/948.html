Mamit District :

                   Bru Repatriation Process  - Mizoram and Central Government have  repatriated the Bru refugees from Tripura to Mizoram several times without much success. Ministry of Home Affairs has set the deadline for Repatriation of Bru refugees latest by 30th November, 2019.There were 1,681 families (8,869 members) repatriated during 1st to 8th Batch (i.e. 2010 to 2018). And, 280 families (1098 members) were repatriated during  9th Batch and Final  of repatriation process (i.e,  3.10.2019 - 30.11.2019).

                   The main problems which hampered this repatriation process are the Bru refugees themselves. As they are till longing for their free rations and assistance received from the Central Government because of their status as a refugees, they are reluctant to return to Mizoram with a pretext of raising various unacceptable demands.

 

Kolasib District :

                   Assam- Mizoram border issue : There is an ongoing border dispute between Mizoram and Assam in the bordering district of Kolasib, particularly in and around Vairengte and Zophai villages. There has been several instances where scuffles broke out between residents living in these bordering areas. There has also been an  enormous outcry from Students  Organisations and NGOs of Mizoram to the State Government to settle this issue once and for all.

 

Champhai District :

                   The 8 Assam Rifles were checking illegal trade activities in Zokhawthar (a town within Champhai District which is  located in the border with Myanmar) and have stopped the trade of betel-nut and fertilizer across the Mizoram-Myanmar border since 7.9.2019. This heightened tension in the area and further led to discontentment among the people of Zokhawthar since 80% of the total population are engaged as porters.

 

                   Presently, imports of betel nut and export of fertilizers through Zokhawthar area have been stopped to this day as a result of the restriction imposed by the Assam Rifles. The Law and Order situation of the area has also been contained and additional IR police personnel deployed were also withdrawn from the area.

 

 

 

Lunglei District :

                   Pukpui Land dispute - Recently, there is an objection by residents of Pukpui village, Lunglei District against the proposed demarcation of land presently occupied by Indian Air Force. Joint meeting of Pukpui Village Council and all NGOs of Pukpui had decided on 21November 2019 to submit a Memorandum to the President and Prime Minister of India on the subject of objection to permanent land acquisition by the Indian Air Force. This memorandum contains 12 point which highlighted how this process would violate their freedom of religion, that the community would take steps such as protest,/bandh  to show that an Forces camp is unwanted in the region, that the Government should arrange a camp for athe Air Force in a more remote area, etc.
