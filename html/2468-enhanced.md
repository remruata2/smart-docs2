****MIZORAM-ASSAM BORDER SITUATION REPORT****

No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/614

Dated 2nd November, 2020

In continuation of our report vide No. MSB/CB/B-4/2020/612 Dt. 1.11.2020, Situation Report on Mizoram-Assam Border is as under:

1. **Thinghlun, Mamit District Report** **:**

Around 80 (eighty) Assam Police personnel were stationed at Zaite Village and they conducted regular patrolling along Mizoram-Assam border. Situation is normal.

1. **Kanhmun, Mamit District Report :**

Kanhmun Village Council and All NGOs in their press release issued today i.e., 2.11.2020 lauded DC, Mamit for his initiative to avert disruption of supply of essential communities within Mamit District and subsequent released of stranded vehicles carrying essential commodities after a meeting was held with DC, Karimganj. It also stated that it is their stand that Mizoram Police are deployed within Mamit District, Mizoram. The meeting further decided to organize indefinite roadblock at Medili Bridge w.e.f., today i.e., 2.11.2020 due to the following:

1. Blockage of road leading to Vairengte, Saihapui V and Bairabi.
2. Frequent blockage of vehicles carrying essential commodities proceeding towards Kanhmun by Assam Police as per their wishes.
3. No arrest of the accused person(s) at Thinghlun incident has been made till date.
4. Not paying compensation to Pu Zolawma and Pu Ben David of Thinghlun by Assam government.
5. Till safe return of stranded Mizo drivers carrying essential commodities to Mizoram.

The committee further urged the State Government –

1. To convert Temporary IR Posts at Medili and Pu Zolawma’s farm into full fledge Battalion Out Post.
2. To speed up electrification work between Kanhmun to IR Post, Medili Bridge.
3. To amend Mizoram Map.

Today i.e., 2.11.2020 around 50(fifty) residents of Kanhmun organized road block at Medili Bridge Mizoram side. At the same time, residents of Rangamati, Assam organized road block at Assam side near Medili Bridge. There is no vehicular movement across Medili Bridge, Assam-Mizoram border due to the said road block.
A community work was organized for repainting of boundary pillar at Medili Bride, Mizoram-Assam border under the aegis of Hachhek Constituency Development Committee (HCDC) today i.e., 2.11.2020 @ 1:30 p.m. and 20(twenty) volunteers participated.

1. **Saihapui ‘V’, Kolasib District :**

Public meeting was held today i.e., 2.11.2020 @ 7:00 a.m. at VC House, Saihapui ‘V’. Pu Vanlalzawna, VCP, Saihapui ‘V’ chaired the meeting and 50(fifty) members were present. The meeting decided the following:

1. To request the government to set up IR permanent post at Saihapui ‘V’.
2. The concern department be requested to supply the required seeds for their cultivation and 7(seven) water pump machines at subsidised rate.

The meeting further decided to hold community work for collection of required materials for construction of IR Temporary Camp today and a community work for construction of IR Camp will be organized at a convenient time.

Information received indicated that Assam Police are preparing Trench today i.e., 2.11.2020 at northern side of one Hillock at Assam side around 120 meters from Mizoram Police Camp at Saihapui ‘V’ border.

1. **Bairabi, Kolasib District Report :**

Input received indicated that Krishak Mukti Sangram Samiti (KMSS), Hailakandi intended to organize procession tomorrow i.e., 3.11.2020 and urged withdrawal of Mizoram Police deployed in Kachurthal area as claimed by them and reclaim of the already lost Assam land. The procession party may proceed towards Mizoram Police duty post at Zophai, Mizoram-Assam border.

Today i.e., 2.11.2020 there was no inter-state movement of vehicles at Mizoram-Assam border near Bairabi. Situation is normal.

1. **Phaisen, Kolasib District Report :**

****MZP, Buhchangphai Branch in their Press Release issued yesterday i.e., 1.11.2020 stated that it has come to their notice that residents of Dolchorra, Assam intended to remove the wall constructed for blockage of Assam to Mizoram road near Phaisen Village on 2.11.2020 @ 10:00 a.m. It also stated their displeasure regarding the said intention and they will not allow entering of Mizoram without going through Covid-19 screening protocol and if required, they intended to block the said road.

Phaisen Branch YMA in their statement issued today i.e., 2.11.2020 stated that residents of Phaisen Village were not aware of reports regarding residents of Dolchhora, Assam intention to remove Bamboo wall constructed on the road at Assam side today i.e., 2.11.2020 as mentioned in MZP Press Release. It also stated that with the joint initiative of Task Force and Phaisen Branch YMA action has been taken to prevent entry into Mizoram without permission at Phaisen border with the help of Security Forces. It further stated that even if the roadblock is removed they will not allow entering into the state without permission or Medical clearance. It also stated their desire not to take any action which may invite public attention at Phaisen border.

1. **Vairengte, Kolasib District Report :**

****Road block organized by Zoram Drivers’ Union (ZDU) at Tawngtaina Hmun, Outskirt of Vairengte is still continuing today i.e., 2.11.2020. There is no vehicular movement across Mizoram-Assam border at Vairengte and 155 (one hundred fifty five) vehicles are stranded due to the said road block till today. Leaders of MZP who were stationed at Vairengte left for Aizawl yesterday’s evening i.e., 1.11.2020.

Further, Kawngthar Branch YMA, Vairengte apprehended one Imtiaz Ali (48) S/o Khobor Ali, Pesh Nagar, Lailapur, Assam on 1.11.2020 @ 4:30 p.m. at Mautuizau, beyond MVI Checkgate in between Vairengte and Lailapur. They recovered 14(fourteen) Nos. of vials containing brown powder suspected to be Heroin weighting 420 grams and v/o Rs. 35,000/- at the local smuggling rate from his illegal possession. Kawngthar Branch YMA members on duty stated that the accused person first escaped apprehension and he was apprehended after he jumped down from the rock cliff in the area and unable to move further. In this connectioin, Exise and Narcotics Station C/No. 28/2020 Dt. 1.11.2020 u/s 21(a) of ND&amp;PS Act, 1985 was registered. The accused person was evacuated to CHC, Vairengte for Medical Care and examination and unfortunately succumbed at 10:20 a.m. this morning.
Vairengte PS registered an UD case Vide No. 2/2020 Dt. 2.11.2020 u/s 174 CrPC and PME was conducted at CHC, Vairengte. The matter was informed to Dholai Police. The dead body will be handed over to Dholai PS early tomorrow’s morning i.e., 3.11.2020 considering the prevailing situation.

Due to the above incident, so far there is no report of attack of residents of Mizo by non-mizos in Silchar and its surrounding areas. As per report of Revd. Rualthankhuma, Administrative Secretary, Barack Area, Mizoram Synod 4(four) families (16 (sixteen) members) from Synod Mission Compound, Silchar were evacuated to Mizoram House, Silchar, 5 (five) persons to Kahrawt (Cachar Dist, Assam), 9 (nine) persons to Bahadurgram (Cachar Dist, Assam), 3(three) persons to Kamranga (Cachar Dist, Assam), 3(three) persons to Pailapool (Cachar Dist, Assam) and 26(twenty six) persons to Jiribam, Manipur as a precautionary measures. However, 9(nine) mizos are still remained at Synod Mission compound, Silchar along with 13(thirteen) non-mizo workers' families. Mizo Salvation Army workers at Rangirkhari, Silchar and 8(eight) students were also evacuated to Mizoram House, Silchar.As per further report, mizo persons residing in Silchar and its surrounding areas who required safe custody were all evacuated to Mizoram House, Silchar and other areas.
