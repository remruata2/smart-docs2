8.4.2022  : 6 (six) leaders of Federation Of Mizoram Samagra Employees (FEMSE) led by Pu R. Lalnunhlima, President FEMSE met Pu Zoramthanga, Chief Minister at his Office Chamber on 8.4.2022 @ 11:45 a.m. in connection with their standing demand for hike in salary and regularisation of their service. During the meeting, the Chief Minister informed them to discuss the matter with authorities of Finance Department. Information received stated that FEMSE OB and all affiliated members intend to organise meeting on 21.4.2022 to discuss their above mentioned demands.

10.6.2022 :    Federation of Mizoram Samagra Employee (FEMSE) held Special Assembly 2022 on 10.6.2022 @ 11:00 a.m. at Vanapa Hall, Aizawl. Pu Zoramthanga, Chief Minister of Mizoram graced the function as Chief Guest and Pu Lalchhandama Ralte, Education Minister as Guest of Honour. Delivering a speech at the function, Pu Zoramthanga stated that the state government is aware of the various difficulties faced by employees of Samagra, Mizoram regarding increase in their salary, regularization of their service etc. In this regard, he assured to take necessary action to the possible extend. It was followed by discussion on their agendas viz., hike in their salary, regularization of their service etc. and the Assembly is still going on at the time of writing the report.

13.6.2022:     Federation of Mizoram Samagra Employee (FEMSE) Special Assembly 2022 held on 10.6.2022 at Vanapa Hall, Aizawl resolved the following concerning the State government:

            (i)        Necessary action should be taken so as to enable them to avail 100                          percent Earned Leave as per the previous order issued in this regard by                     State Project Office, Samagra.

            (ii)       Necessary action should be taken to expedite the process of                                      Regularization Scheme of employees under Samagra, which is being                        processed and to be considered by the Cabinet Meeting.

            (iii)     FEMSE vehemently disagree with the proposal made by the Finance                        Department for enhancement of their salary, and resolved to take                             necessary action to enhance their salary as per FEMSE proposal. In this                         connection, a written representation should be submitted to the Chief               Minister of Mizoram within 10 (ten) days. Strong
                        ultimatum should be made, if no favourable information is received                                     during the stipulated period.

 

16.6.2022 : Mizoram Teachers' Federation (MTF) held a meeting on 16.6.2022 @ 2:00 p.m. at the Top Floor of Govt. Mizo High School Hall, Aizawl. Pu Rosangzuala Ralte, President, MTF presided the meeting and 80 (eighty) members attended. The meeting expressed their surprise over the issuance of advertisement for recruitment of Circle Education Officer (CEO) under School Education Department by the Mizoram Public Service Commission (MPSC). In this regard, the meeting resolved that the State government should revoke the said advertisement. It also demanded that the State government should fulfil their long standing demand for abolition of CEO post. Further, they decided to take severe necessary action, if no favourable information is received regarding their demand before the end of June, 2022.

 

7.7.2022: As per the decision of Federation of Mizoram Samagra Employees (FEMSE) OB meeting held on 5.7.2022, all employees of Samagra Mizoram wore black badge as no action has been taken on their demand for revision of their pay. The black badge agitation was supported by employees of Samagra across the State.

            In this connection, FEMSE Council Extended Meeting was held 0n 7.7.2022 @ 12:00 Noon at Chanmari YMA Hall, Aizawl. A Press Release in this regard was issued which stated that they intend to resort to pen-down strike w.e.f., 11.7.2022 if the ongoing black badge agitation yields no fruitful result. It also stated that 'Study Team' was constituted by the state government following agitation organized by employees of Samagra in November, 2019. The Study Team Recommendation was placed before the Cabinet Meeting after prolonged consideration of the same by various concerned departments. However, the same was not approved by the Cabinet Committee and it expressed strong disappointment in this regard. Such being the case, they decided to take extreme measures i.e., Pen-down strike, and if the same yield no fruitful result, FEMSE Council Meeting will be convened on 15.7.2022 to discuss their future course of action.

11.7.2022 :    Dr. Lalzirmawia Chhangte, Secretary Education Department and Pu Lalhmachhuana, State Project Director, Samagra Shiksha held a meeting with 4 (four) leaders of Federation of Mizoram Samagra Shiksha Employees (FEMSE) led by Pu R. The meeting discussed the various demands and difficulties faced by FEMSE and leaders of FEMSE agreed to indefinitely cancel their intended pen-down strike, scheduled to be held w.e.f., 11.7.2022 and to await the ongoing steps taken on their demands by the authorities.

30.8.2022 :    FEMSE General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 30.8.2022 stated that the Council of Minister Meeting held today approved enhancement of the salary of employees under Mizoram Samagra. In this connection, they expressed gratitude to Pu Zoramthanga, Chief Minister; Pu Lalchhandama Ralte, Education Minister and concerned officials of Education Department.

29.9.2022 :    Leaders of 2006 SSA Upgraded Teachers submitted a written petition to Chief Minister on 29.9.2022 wherein they expressed gratitude for the action being taken by the state government for regularisation of Teachers under Samagra and at the same time, expressed regret for not approving the said initiative by the Law Department. It also stated that there are around 200/220 Upgraded Teacher (PS & MS under SSA), which were recruited in 2006. They requested the Chief Minister to take necessary action for regularisation of at least SSA Upgraded Teacher recruited in 2006 through the authority of the Cabinet.

12.10.2022:   Federation of Mizoram Samagra Employee (FEMSE) General Headquarters in its Press Release issued on 10.10.2022 expressed disappointment regarding addressing of their pay anomalies by the state government. While lauding the efforts of the Chief Minister and Education Minister for redressal of their grievances, the press release stated that FEMSE failed to understand the redressal mechanism adopted which includes limiting dearness allowances to only 15% without increasing their basic salary, which is not at par with their fellow employees under the state government. It urged and requested the state government to address their grievances by rectifying their pay anomalies including their basic pay. It further resolved that it would continue to render its earnest efforts for its members in terms of pay and service benefits.
