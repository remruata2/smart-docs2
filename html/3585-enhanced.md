A. _Front page of THE AIZAWL POST newspaper Dt. 16.6.2022_

**Registration of Myanmar refugees virtually compleated****:******High Level Committee on Myanmar Refugees, a committee constitute by Mizoram Government under the Chairmanship of Mizoram Home Minister intended to registered all Myanmar refugees entering Mizoram. According to the records of this Committee, till 4 June 2022, there were 29,964 Myanmar refugees where identification has been completed on 24,751 which is 99.28%. ID cards have also been issued to them accordingly.

 The identification process was scheduled to be completed by the month of February this year. However, the Covid-19 pandemic, movements of these refugees from place to place, arrival of additional refugees and other factors prevented its completion in time.

 Presently, as per the Committee, there are 149 relief camps inside Mizoram -Siaha, Lawngtlai,Champhai and Hnahthial districts where a total of 11,833 refugees are accommodated while the remaining 18,131 settled in other locations.

****Campaign organised by Police :******As part of their ongoing Awareness Campaign, Serchhip District police on 14 June 2022 organised a Special Drive programme at Chawngbuangi Memorial School, Serchhip. In this programme, workshop on Road safety &amp; Traffic rules, Wildlife Protection Act, Arms Act, MV Act and POCSO Act along with awareness on alcohol/drug abuse and Crime Against Women were conducted and was attended by 438 students and 13 teachers.**

C. _Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 16.6.2022_

**Delimitation Committee without the consent of Election Department:******Government of Mizoram created a delimitation committee which is headed by Dy.Chief Minister to study in depth the areas, its borders and suitable name for various MLA constituencies. Election department in Mizoram, however express that they had not received any instruction from Election Commission of India (ECI) regarding this.

 This committee, which also consisted of representatives from various political parties of the state had one meeting so far, with the next meeting scheduled to be held on 17 June 2022 where the members are expected to put forward their respective suggestions for discussions.
