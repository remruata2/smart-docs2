A. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES, THE AIZAWL POST & VANGLAINI newspapers Dt. 1.7.2013

YMA in HPC demand area: After the revocation of prohibition of establishing YMA in their demand area by HPC(D) on 25th June, 2013, leaders of Tuisual group YMA discussed about reforming YMA on Saturday at New Vervek.

            The meeting was led by LK Mawia, President of Tuisual Group YMA, they decided that branch YMA seal which was submitted to Central YMA should be taken back on Monday (ie, today) for re-forming YMA. The meeting later decided that leaders of YMA branches under Tuisual Group should first have a meeting on 6th July, 2013 to consider and discuss steps to be taken for reforming YMA.

            After YMA under Tuisual Group was dissolved, Youth Association was formed in several villages, and leaders were elected. The meeting will discuss if this Youth Association  is to be accepted as YMA by Central YMA or should this Youth Association be dissolved and YMA branch be newly formed.

 

B. Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES newspaper Dt. 1.7.2013

Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe in Mizoram: According to the Census in 2011, there are 1,218 Scheduled Caste (SC) and 10,36,115 Scheduled Tribe (ST) in Mizoram. The most member of SC is in Aizawl district with 627 and the least in Champhai district with 17. There are 373,542 ST in Aizawl district which is the most in all the districts while Saiha district has the least number of ST with 54,642 in number.

 

C. Front page of NEWSLINK newspaper Dt. 1.7.2013

More polling stations:  Election Commission of India approved formation of six more polling stations under Lunglei district, in addition to the existing 175 polling stations as per the appellation of the concern district election officer. The new polling stations are - Bazar Veng-III, Electric Veng-III, Venghlun-II, South Khawlek, Lalnutui and Andermanik Village.
