REPORT ON ACTIVITIES OF ZOMI REVOLUTIONARY ARMY (ZRA), ARAKAN LIBERATION ARMY (ALA) / ARAKAN LIBERATION PARTY (ALP) ANDREPORT ON SEIZURE OF EXPLOSIVE SUBSTANCES AT FARKAWN, CHAMPHAI DISTRICT/SEIZURE OF ILLICIT ARMS AND AMMUNITIONS AT SERCHHIP DISTRICT.

No.MSB/CB/B-104/477

Dated: 23rdJune, 2021

 

1.       Activities of Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA):        

          6 (six) cadres of Zomi Revolutionary Army (ZRA) carrying amrs entered into the Diltlang North VC area, Champhai district, Mizoram on 16.6.2021. They chased away 58 (fifty eight) Myanmar refugees (believed to be People Defense Force members) residing in a temporary refugee camp set up at the bank of Tiau river at Tuithoh forest area and burnt down their camps and other properties. The 58 refugees fled to Myanmar and they are presently seeking shelter at Ngente and Darkhai village of Myanmar.

          Before their initial entry into Mizoram, the above 58 refugees sought permission from PuTawnga, VCP of Ngur village to allow their stay under Ngur village area, but the latter denied citing Covid-19 pandemic and as per his direction they set up temporary camp at Tuithoh Kai (Crossing point).

          As per input, the above ZRA cadres and Diltlang North VLTF also had an altercation on 16.6.2021, but ended without any physical violence and ZRA cadres subsequently withdrew and entered into Myanmar. Diltlang North YMA and VLTF on Covid-19 are strongly against entry of ZRA cadres into their area and they want Mizoram government to take necessary action to prevent their entry in future. While top Leaders/OBs of ZRA are residing in Manipur and the above cadres have their base in Myanmar at Darkai village area.

          ZRA are a member of United People Front (UPF), a union of different insurgent groups of Manipur, and they are currently having Suspension of Operation (SoO) with Indian government. Mr.Thanglianpauva, President of ZRA also used to be the Chairman of UPF and he is residing at ZRA designated camp at Songtal, Manipur.

         

          It is strongly believed that the above action of ZRA cadres was due to their support to Myanmar military government as they believed that the 58 persons are PDF members who are to undergo training to fight Myanmar military government. UNLF, which is the biggest insurgent group of Manipur, are having base inside Myanmar territory under the patronage of Myanmar military at Senam and Chikha area and they supported the Myanmar military and similarly ZRA also opposed any act against Myanmar military.

2.       Arakan Liberation Army(ALA)/Arakan Liberation Party(ALP):

          Arakan Liberation Army(ALA) is a Rakhine insurgent group in Myanmar. It is the armed wing of the Arakan Liberation Party (ALP). The ALA signed a ceasefire agreement with the government of Myanmar on 5th April 2012. At present, their cadre strength is expected to be around 2,000 to 3,000 and their Headquarters are located in (1) Sittwe, Arakan State, Myanmar (2) Kheyaktoe and (3) Marau.

          ALA cadre are stationed along Bangladesh and Indian borders. Around 20 (twenty) ALA outfits led by Commander SS SurgeantHleichho are stationed in BandukBanga village, along the Indo-Myanmar border. Since 2017, due to fear of Arakan Army(AA) many ALA families sought asylum in Azasora village, Mizoram, India, which is located around 3 km from BandukBanga village and at present around 15 (fifteen) families are still stationed in the area and ALA cadre sometimes took shelter in thisvillage.

          Acting on input received, Lawngtlai Police and 3rd Assam Rifles apprehended the following 5 (five) suspected Arakan Liberation Army (ALA) outfits at Kakichhuah, Lawngtlai District, (around 105 km from Lawngtlai) near Indo-Myanmar border on Dt. 11.6.2021 @ 4:45 p.m.

2)      KhainZawZay Ray HtatPaine(23) S/o NyoHtunPhya of Sittwe, Myanmar

          1 (one) 9 mm. pistol, 1 (one) .38 revolver,  55 rounds of ammunitions and 4(four) hand grenades were recovered from the possession of the above suspected ALA cadres. A case was registered vide Lawngtlai PS C/No.29/21 Dt.12.06.21 U/S 25(1-B)(a)Arm Act R/w 14 Foreiner Act and they are placed under judicial custody.

          As per report, the above arrested suspected ALA members demanded Rs. 10,000 each from PuSangkima, Pu C. Buatsaiha and PuLalnghaka, all residents of Hruitezawl, Lawngtlai District on 10.6.2021. They  also asked donations from selected villagers of Kakichhuah and other villages who are from Rakhine, Myanmar. But they were arrested before receiving any money.

          It is reported that Assam Rifles has good coordination with ALA/ALP. Informations related to Bangladesh and Myanmar are constantly provided to Assam Rifles by ALP/ALA. ALA SS Major MyoHeinn alias Mahesh is presently staying at AR Camp, Lawngtlai at the time of writing the report. Arms and ammunitions are normally procured from Karen National Union (KNU), one of the insurgent outfits in Myanmar.

          Information received also stated that there have been inputs of ALP cadres establishing medical camps in nearby villages of Kakichhuah and distributing COVID-19 essential items to the locals as part of their propaganda. However, it has come to light that the medical camp was just a temporary base with intent to earn goodwill and acceptance of the locals.

3.       Report on Seizure of Explosive Substances at Farkawn, Champhai District:

          8th Assam Rifles personnel, who were on duty at Farkawn Mobile Check Post seized the following explosive substances on 21.6.2021 @ 10:00 a.m. and in this connection, FIR was lodged at Dungtlang Police Station.

1)      Special Detonator          -        3,000 Nos. (packed in 6(six) Cartoons)

2)      Electric Detonator          -        925 Nos. (37 packs containing 25 each of                                                  electric detonators)

3)      Safety Fuse                    -        2000 Mtrs (4(four) boxes, each box containing                                          50 coils of 10 mtrs each)

4)      Explosive powder          -        63 sacks of explosive powder (13,104 Tonnes).

 

          The above explosive substances were seized from TATA 207 B/R No. MZ-01 M-9258 driven by Zothanpuia (48) S/o Lenghnuna of Farkawn, Champhai District, while en route towards Tiauriver, Indo-Myanmar border. Later, PuLallawmawma (43) s/o Vanlalsawma of Kelsih (accused as owner of the seized items), who was stationed at Farkawn was apprehended by Assam Rifles for interrogation. In this connection, Dungtlang PS case No. 5/2021 Dt. 21.6.2021 u/s 5(a) Explosive Substances Act was registered. Dungtlang Police released the two accused persons after executing Bail Bond as the Hon’ble Court granted anticipatory bail to them. It is reported that the seized explosive substances are intended to be delivered to Camp Victoria (Chin National Army (CNA) Camp), Myanmar.

          The seized items along with ration supply for refugees (dal, potatoes, edible oil, rice bags etc.) were transported to Farkawn by 5 (five) CNA members on the evening of 19.6.2021 by using 2 (two) vehicles viz. SML Truck and Mahindra Pik Up. The items were arranged by PuRokhawma (CNA member) presently staying at Bawngkawn, Aizawl. Due to heavy rainfall, they had a night halt at the outskirt of Khawlailung and arrivedFarkawn the next evening i.e., 20.6.2021 and stayed at Tourist Lodge, Farkawn as per arrangement of VCP, Farkawn. The next day i.e., 21.6.2021 the consignments were loaded into TATA 207 B/R No. MZ-01 M-9258 driven by Zothanpuia (48) S/o Lenghnuna of Farkawn and they were intercepted and seized by Assam Rifles en route  to Tiau river, Indo-Myanmar border.

          Information received also stated that PuLallawmawma of Kelsih, one of the accused and his wife Lalrampari possessed Shot Firer's Certificate (valid upto 25.5.2023) and Explosive License (valid upto 31.3.2022) issued by Petroleum & Explosive Safety Organization (PESO), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Guwahati Assam. They used to procure explosive substances from Guwahati and stored at their Godown, located at Lengpui. They normally received order through phone as they have not yet opened their explosive shop at Kulikawn, Aizawl due to enforcement of lockdown.

PuLallawmawma’s Shot Firer’s Certificate was issued on 25.5.2018 by Petroleum & Explosive Safety Organization (PESO), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Guwahati Assam after clearing interview and other formalilities conducted by PESO. NOC was issued by DC, Aizawl and recommendation was given by Ajay Kodal (explosive distribiutor), North East Expo, Narangi, Guwahati.

Pi LalrampariSailo’s (wife of PuLallawmawma) Explosive License was issued on 6.2.2018 by Ministry of Home Affairs, GOI. Her application was recommended by Deputy Conroller, PESO and NOC was given by DC, Aizawl.
