SPECIAL REPORT

 

No.MSB/CB/SR/2019/63

Dated: 29th January, 2019.

 

Information has been received that leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ Forum (MBDPF) namely, A. Sawibunga, President; R. Laldawngliana, Vice President and Bruno Msha, General Secretary had been visiting Bru Transit Camps in Tripura during last week in  which they conducted public meetings where they gave report of their meeting with
Pu Mahesh Kumar Singla, Adviser (Bru), submission of Memorandum to the Hon’ble Prime Minister of India and the Hon’ble Union Home Minister and their meeting with
Pu Zoramthanga, Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram during their recent visit to Delhi. The leaders of MBDPF explained before the Brus, the 10 (ten) demands in their Memorandum submitted to the Hon’ble Prime Minister and the Hon’ble Union Home Minister and reported that they also had a positive discussion with Pu Mahesh Kumar Singla, Adviser (Bru) for the resumption of repatriation process. They also stated that they were very pleased with the positive response they received from the Hon’ble Chief Minister of Mizoram. Their audience had also expressed their desire for repatriation at the earliest while stressing that they wanted to receive advance payment of housing assistance and completion of construction of houses before actual physical repatriation. Information received indicated that majority of the Mizoram Brus expressed strong desire to return to Mizoram as soon as possible.

On 25.01.2019, MBDPF leaders submitted a Memorandum to the Chief Electoral Officer (CEO), in response to the CEO’s letter dated 18.01.2019, stating that MBDPF will not be able to perform their advice to download statutory forms and distribute it to those bonafide/ eligible persons which may be submitted with supporting documents in connection with Summary Roll Revision in the 7 (seven) Bru camps in Tripura citing limited stipulated time period for the conduct of the said Special Summary Roll Revision. They requested the CEO to send a team to Tripura to conduct Special Summary Roll Revision in the 7 (seven) Bru Transit camps so that new eligible persons may be enrolled in the Electoral Roll in time so that they can exercise their franchise in the coming Lok Sabha Election, 2019. They also requested the CEO for extension of stipulated time frame for the Special Summary Roll Revision.
