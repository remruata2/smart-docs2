CHRONOLOGY OF EVENT/ISSUES RELATING TO MIZORAM (SELECTION OF CANDIDATES FOR HIGHER TECHNICAL COURSES ) RULES

 

     Date

 

      Events/Developments

 

 

 

   6.7.1993

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1993 was  issued  in the Mizoram Gazette.

Rule 5(a) Classified candidates into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats

(i)Children of the local permanent resident  of the Mizoram state as category I

(ii) Children of the non- local permanent resident  of the Mizoram state as category II

(iii) Children of the Central/other state Govt employees not permanently serving in Mizoram as category III

 

   

   7.6.1999

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999  was  issued in the Mizoram Gazette.

Rule 5(1) All applicants are classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats

(a) Children of the local permanent resident  of the Mizoram state as category I

(ii) Children of the non- local permanent resident  of the Mizoram state as category II

(iii) Children of the Central/other state Govt employees not permanently serving in Mizoram as category III

 Explanation: For the purpose of this sub-rule, the term ‘Local permanent Residents’ means those who are indigenous people of the state of Mizoram and have been residing permanently  in the state.

(3) On the basis of marks obtained in the Selection Examination ,Selection shall be made  in order  of merit  from amongst eligible candidates from category –I first. In case sufficient  number of eligible candidates are not available  from category-I,the remaining seats shall be filled in, in order of merit by the eligible candidates from Category-II and Category-III likewise.

Note:  With the commencement of Rule,1999, Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1993 was repealed 

 

 

26 .9. 2000

The Hon’ble Guwahati High Court  in the Bhanu Choudhury vs State Of Mizoram And Ors cases(vide Civil Rule(PIL) No.1239 of  1994)   held sub-rule (3) of Rules 5  of  Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999   to be ultra vires and  it had  struck  down.

 

15.7.2002

(Notification issued by Education & Human Resource Department, Government of Mizoram)

 

As the Hon’ble Gauhati High Court vide its  judgement dt.26.9.2000 in Civil Rule(PIL) No.1239 of  1994 struck  down the provision of  sub-rule (3) of Rule 5 of the Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999 , the Governor of Mizoram made amendment  i.e   Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Amendment) Rules,2002

   Amendment to Rule 5(3): The whole portion of Rule5(3) of the principal rule shall be deleted  and replaced by the following

 

‘Allotment of seats shall be made in the following order

(i)Category-I : Children of local permanent resident-85%

(ii) Category-II: Non-Local permanent residents-10%

(iii)Category-III: Children of Central and State Government  employees posted in the state who have  completed  not less than 2 years in Mizoram on the last  day of submission  of application-5%

 

 

25.9.2014

 

Mizo Zirlai Pawl (MZP) General Headquarters organized a series of protest against the inclusion of Chakmas and other non-indigenous students under Category-I under the State Technical Entrance Examination (STEE). The MZP General Headquarters claimed that Chakmas are not indigenous people of the State of Mizoram and therefore should be excluded from the Category-I in the State Technical Entrance Examination (STEE).Due to this protest movement 13 (thirteen) student leaders were arrested by Mizoram Police.           

On 25.9.2014, thousands of students took out a protest rally within Aizawl City and in different part of Mizoram forcing the government to reach an agreement with the agitating students’ leaders.

 

 

3.4.2015

 

 

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Sixth Amendment) Rules, 2015 was issued in the Mizoram Gazette.

Amendment of Rule 5

Rule 5 of the Principal Rules shall be substituted by the following,

Sub rule (1 )namely -(Method of Selection)

“(1) All applicants shall be classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats, viz :-

(a) Children of local permanent residents of the State of Mizoram,

as Category - I

(b) Children of other permanent residents of the State of Mizoram

as Category - II

(c) Children of Central/Other State Government employees staying

in Mizoram as Category – III

Explanation :

For the purpose of this sub-rule

(a) The term “Local permanent Residents” means those who are indigenous (Zo-Ethnic people who are native inhabitants) people of the State of Mizoram and have been residing permanently in the State.

(b) The term “Other Permanent Residents” means those who are Non-indigenous (Non-Zo-Ethnic people who are non-native inhabitants) people of the State of Mizoram and those who are serving under the Government of Mizoram or Government Corporation or Autonomous

Bodies under the State of Mizoram, or other recognised organisations and in whose case, it can safely be inferred that they would reside permanently in Mizoram.

 

16.7.2015

Mizoram Chakma Students Union(MCSU) had filed PIL(No.39/2015.Dt 23.6.2015) in Guwahati High Court challenging the  Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Sixth Amendment) Rules, 2015. On 16.7.2015, the hon’ble Guwahati High Court disposed of the PIL after the Government of Mizoram withdrew  the Sixth Amendment Rules of 2015  and put the Chakmas under Category I.

22.4.2016

 

Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) (Sixth Amendment) Rules, 2016 was issued in the Mizoram Gazette.

 

Methods of Selection : Rule 5 (1)

       All applicants shall be classified into three categories for the purpose of allotment of seats, viz :-

(a) Children of local permanent residents of the State of Mizoram,

as Category - I

(b) Children of other permanent residents of the State of Mizoram

as Category - II

(c) Children of Central/Other State Government employees staying

in Mizoram as Category – III

Explanation :  For the purpose of this sub-rule

  1. The term ‘Permanent residents’ means  those who are Zo-ethnic people of the state of Mizoram and have been  residing permanently in the state.
  2. The term ’other permanent residents’ means those who are (i)Children of non-Zo ethnic people of the state of Mizoram  and have been residing permanently in the state.

 

(2) Percentage of Seat for Category I is 95%, Category II is 4% and Category III is 1%.

 The disciplines in which seats are to be allotted  to Category –I, II and III will be decided  by the Selection Board  by Preparing seat matrix.

 

With the commencement of this Rule, Mizoram (Selection of Candidates for Higher Technical Courses) Rules,1999 and  its subsequent amendments  stand repealed .
