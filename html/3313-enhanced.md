A. _Front page of THE ZOZAM TIMES, THE AIZAWL POST & VANGLAINI newspapers Dt. 8.5.2013_

**Procession held by Mosolman in Mizoram**: A procession was held yesterday ie, 7.5.2013 at Kanhmun by around 300 persons of Muslim community from Kanhmun PS to Damcherra. The procession was organised due to the incident on dt. 8.4.2013 at the Farmland of Bungthuam where Abdul Manaf and his family were robbed and attacked, and Abdul Manaf’s wife and 9 year old daughter were raped. One of the attackers Sebaram Reang was arrested by the police but he ran away and was later found dead hanging in the Tripura area at their village; Bru's alleged Mizoram police responsible for his death and organised bandh which is supported by traders at Damcherra. In return, the Mizo community also closed Langkaih Bridge indefinitely.

 Muslims at Nagra and Rangamati in Karimganj district organised procession to show anger at Tuikuk people and condemned the raping of mother and daughter of their community. They demanded Tripura government to give 10 lakh to Manaf and his family for compensation and demanded Bru people to apologise to Mizo people for wrongly accusing them on the death of Sebaram Reang. They also demanded Tripura government to arrest the offenders as quickly as possible. Meanwhile, they praised Mizoram police and YMA as to how they are dealing with the issue. Meanwhile, sources said that Damcherra Bridge is now being closed from the Tripura side as well.

B. _Front page of VANGLAINI newspaper Dt. 8.5.2013_

**Stay order of KMMTTP retracted**: Yesterday ie, 7.5.2013, Kaladan Multi Modal Transit Transport Project (KMMTTP) Land owners’ Association withdrew stay order given to the contractors of the Multi Modal road.

Discussion was held at Lawngtlai DC Lalhmingthanga’s office yesterday ie, 7.5.2013; and he promised to talk and meet with higher authority so that Land Owners get compensation. Various topics were discussed at the meeting and KMMTTP Land Owners’ Association decided to withdraw the stay order they had given to contractors of the road.

The meeting was attended by leaders of KMMTTP Land Owners’ Association, SP Lawngtlai, Mizoram government officials and leaders of MADC.
