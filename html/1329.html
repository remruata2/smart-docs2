Brief Note on Activities of NGOs’ in Mizoram in reaction to  Election Commission of India (ECI)’s direction to conduct Special  Summary Revision w.r.t 01.01.2016  - Bru/Reang voters of Mizoram residing in Tripura.

 

Introduction:

            Leaders of Mizoram  Bru Displaced People’s Forum(MBDPF) namely  Pu.A. Sawibunga, President, MBDPF and Pu.Bruno Msha, General Secretary, MBDPF had submitted a representation to Chief Election Commissioner, Election Commission of India(ECI) on 21st August, 2015 in which they quoted the news item of ‘The Mizoram Post’ dated 19th August,2015 regarding the intention of Election officials of Mizoram not to conduct the ongoing Special electoral roll revision of Reang(Bru) living in Tripura without the instruction received from the ECI.  The   leaders of MBDPF also requested the ECI to instruct Chief Electoral Officer of Mizoram to conduct special summary revision of electoral rolls in all the Bru relief camps in Tripura so as to ensure that all the eligible displaced Brus are able to enrol themselves into the Electoral Rolls of Mizoram. (Copy of Representation of MBDPF enclosed in Annexure –I)

            The Election Commission of India (ECI) discussed the matter on 11.09.2015 and forwarded the copy of representation (submitted by MBDPF) to Chief Electoral Officer, Mizoram on 14th September,2015 with the instruction that the Special Summary revision of electoral roll for Bru/Reang relief camps in Tripura  be conducted  as well.(Copy of ECI letter enclosed in Annexure-II)       

 

Chronology of Reactions of  NGO’s & Political Parties of Mizoram

 

Date

Activities

23.9.2015

 

 

           On 23.9.2015, Mizo Students’ Union (MSU) held an Executive Meeting and the meeting strongly criticised the decision of Election Commission of India (ECI)  to conduct special summary revision of electoral rolls of Brus in Tripura  and the MSU also decided to oppose such  exercise at any cost.

 

           Mizo Zirlai Pawl(MZP) General Headquarters also issued a Press Release on 23.9.2015 in which  the MZP stated that it cannot accept in any way the ECI’s brazen bias for the displaced Bru Community who refused to return to Mizoram in spite of numerous attempts to repatriate them back in Mizoram. In a statement the MZP  expressed  its strong determination to protest /oppose  any move to conduct special summary revision of electoral rolls of Brus in Tripura.( Copy of Press Release of MZP   enclosed in Annexure-III)

 

 

24.9.2015

 

 

           NGO Joint Committee (CYMA,MUP,MHIP,MZP, MSU) comprising of the three largest NGOs like  Central Young Mizo Association(CYMA), Mizo Hmeichhe  Insuihkhawm Pawl(Mizo Women Organisation), Mizoram Upa Pawl(Senior Citizens Association) and two students’ bodies Mizo Zirlai Pawl(MZP) and Mizo Students’ Union  issued a Joint Statement on 24.9.2015. in which it stated that the ECI’s instruction contradicted its(ECI) own commitment made  before the 2014 Lok Sabha elections.

          The Joint statement also stated that the Civil societies and public of Mizoram always welcome the return of those Brus who are bonafide citizen of Mizoram and however, despite several attempts to repatriate them, they are still reluctant to return, making lame excuses. NGO Joint Committee demanded the ECI to rollback its decision and take measure for deleting the names of those Brus (who refused to be repatriated) from Electoral Roll of Mizoram. The NGO Joint Committee also urged all the public of Mizoram to remain united in safeguarding the interest of Mizoram.(Copy of Statement of NGO Joint Committee enclosed in Annexure-IV)

 

 

29.9.2015

  

          Central Young Mizo Association (CYMA) held its Central Executive  Committee (CEC) meeting on 29.9.2015  and discussed the following points

         (i)Representative of NGO Joint Committee be sent to New Delhi to meet officials of Election Commission of India (ECI) and take up their grievances with Election Commission of India (ECI).

         (ii)To widely disseminate information about their opposition, etc to leading National Newspapers.

         (iii)To seek cooperation from Political parties of Mizoram.

 

 

30.9.2015

 

          NGO Coordination Committee was convened today @12:30 PM at Central YMA Committee Room. The meeting resolved the following points with regards to conduct of Special Summary Revision of Electoral Roll for Bru refugees at Transit Camps in Tripura:

(i)To urge the authority not to conduct Special Summary Revision of Electoral Roll for Bru refugees at Tripura Tansit Camps.

(ii)To seek cooperation from political parties on Bru issues.

(iii)Appreciated the initiative of the Government of Mizoram to withhold conduct of Special Summary Revision of Electoral Roll for Bru refugees.

(iv)To take measures for removal of Bru refugees (who were reluctant to be repatriated to Mizoram) from Electoral Roll of Mizoram.

 

 

3.10.2015

 

        Revolutionary Democratic Party (RDP) burnt a copy of Election Commission of India(ECI)  letter no.30/MIZ/2015 Dated 14.9.2015 regarding Special Summary revision of electoral roll for Brus living in relief camps in Tripura as mark of protest on 3.10.2015 and RDP also sent a memorandum to ECI demanding  the ECI to revoke the said order and to refrain from hurting the sentiments of the Mizos. (Copy of Memorandum enclosed in Annexure-V)

 

5.10.2015

 

          All political party (MPCC, MNF, MPC, ZNP & BJP) and NGO Coordination Committee (CYMA, MUP, MHIP, MZP& MSU) meeting was held today @1:00 PM at Central YMA Committee Room under the chairmanship of Pu Lalbiakzuala, President, CYMA and the following resolutions were passed:

  1. Condemned the proposed conduct of Special Summary Revision of Electoral Roll by Election Commission of India for Bru refugees at Tripura Transit Camps and appealed the officials not to undertake the task of revision of voter’s list in the Bru relief camps in Tripura.

 

  1. Appreciated the initiatives taken by Government of Mizoram to withhold conduct of Special Summary Revision of Electoral Roll for Bru refugees. In the meantime condemned the distribution of Electoral Roll Forms to Mizoram Bru Displaced People Forum (MBDPF) by some interested persons and maintained that such Form should not be processed by Election Officials.

 

(iii) To submit a Joint Memorandum in this regard to Election Commission of India and Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India within this month i.e. October, 2015.

 

 

Conclusion:

            In light of the above stated voices  of  various NGOs and Political parties of Mizoram  against the direction of the Election Commission of India (ECI) to conduct a Special Summary Revision of Electoral Rolls for Bru/Reang in relief camps in Tripura; it is strongly anticipated that if the Election Commission of India(ECI)  go ahead with  this arrangement, there is every possibility  of breakdown of law and order  in the state of Mizoram and smooth conduct of Special Summary Revision of Electoral Roll for Brus in Relief camps in Tripura would definitely be disrupted. It is pertinent to reiterate that CYMA is the main NGO with member of 3,50,000(three lakhs fifty thousand) while the two student bodies MZP & MUP together account for 1,00,000(one lakh) members approximately. In addition, MHIP & MUP account for nearly 2,40,000(two lakh forty thousand) and 58,000(fifty eight thousand) respectively. While the total population of Mizoram is approximately 12,00,000(twelve lakhs) and the said NGOs  are having bases across the state of Mizoram. 

 It may be mentioned here that the NGO Co-ordination Committee of Mizoram under the aegis of CYMA had organized Total bandh(72 hours) before polling date of 2014 Lok Sabha election  in Mizoram on 7.4.2014  in protest against the ECI’s decision to allow the Brus/Reangs  living in Tripura to cast their votes at six(6) relief camps in Tripura as the NGO claimed that the Brus  refused to return to Mizoram despite several repatriations attempts. The Total Band was lifted on 8.4.2014@1:30 PM after the Election Commission of India (ECI) committed that provision would be made during future elections to the Lok Sabha and Legislative Assembly of the State of Mizoram, for exercise of franchise within the state of Mizoram by Bru(Reang) voters living in Tripura enrolled in voter lists of Mizoram.( Copy of ECI letter enclosed in Annexure-VI)
