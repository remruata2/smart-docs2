As remarked, the town of E.Lungdar was enquired in case there might be any person engaging or dealing with radiation emitting objects. However, when prominent citizens of E.Lungdar were consulted, it was found that no persons were known by them who could be connected with collection of these types of pillars. The name- Mawia Hmar and the house no. given were out of question as far as this town is concerned.

 

            As the investigation could not see much progress, the village of N. Lungleng was again scrutinized (in case there is spelling mistake on the part of the given address).Even former inhabitants, who had left the village were also considered. This resulted in the uncovering of one person – Chawiliana, former residence of N. Lungleng village.

 

            Chawiliana S/o Lalhleia was born in N.Lungleng village. He left the village  more than 20 years ago to pursue further studies and professions. He served as an evangelist in Tripura under Seventh Day Adventist for a couple of years before studying and completing B.D from ATS, Jowai, Meghalaya. However, as he had been already over-age, he could not pursue pastoral job under Seventh Day Adventist Church and now runs a small business shop in Vaivakawn. Presently, he and his family are residing in a rented flat belonging to Pastor Biakthansanga at Vaivakawn.

 

            During the course of an interview, Mr. Chawiliana claimed that, while he was serving in Tripura as an evangelist, he once came across this type of radiation emitting items, probably left behind by Imperial British during their occupation. According to him, being an evangelist, he collected all the relevant informations to curb villagers from scaring each other with tales relating to the pillar. He became extremely interested in this issue. He even mentioned that he had visited different places in Mizoram where these pillars are said to exist, but without any success.

 

            Although no evidence could be established to lay accusation to Mr.Chawiliana for the application, there is a possibility that he is using part of his younger brother name's H.Lalrimawia (as Mawia Hmar since H stands for Hmar).Besides, both of them are hailing from N.Lungleng village,which is the nearest possible name to the village given in the address. On the other hand, it can only be a mere coincidence, as the house No. given i.e. 91 (assuming it is from N.Lungleng) belongs to Mr.Lalzidinga while the House No. of Chawiliana (presently occupied by his parents)  is 146.

           

            When the subject of an application, submitted by a person with a vaguely mentioned address, for permission to collect radiation emitting objects was raised, he strongly denied knowing the person. He also totally denied submitting any such application himself or using parts of his brother's name. He however, added his willingness to pursue this business if such permission could be granted by the government.

 

            His brother, H. Lalrimawia when contacted, denied any involvement or knowledge regarding this issue, In fact, he is presently residing in Kohima, Nagaland, practicing law after marrying one Naga lady.

 

 

            It is evident that, the applicant, who ever he is, cannot be considered as genuine since his name and address cannot be ascertained at the moment. He has given an entirely wrong address using a village in Aizawl district which does not exist. It is likely that any acknowledgement which may be forwarded will not reach him at all in his given address. Due to the above circumstances, the applicant may be considered a fraud or an imposter which cannot be taken as legitimate.
