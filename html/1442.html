BRU REFUGEES

 

I.     GENESIS

 

                  The BNU conference held in September, 1997 at Saipuilui village in Mamit District adopted a resolution for creation of Autonomous District Council for Brus in the western part of Mizoram which was strongly objected by the Mizo community in the concerned area. At the same time a militant outfit called the Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF) which was formed in 1996 under the leadership of Vanlalliana, Tuirum village in Mamit district became more and more active and on 21.10.1997 Lalzawmliana, Forest Game Watcher in Dampa Forest Sanctuary was killed and two of his friends were also abducted by the BNLF. The news about the killing had spread like wild fire throughout Mizoram and ignited the already tension in the area due to the demand of an Autonomous District Council by BNU. Tension between the two communities escalated and communal violence broke out and being lesser in numbers, the Bru people started fleeing Mizoram at the instance of the BNLF for their operational convenience.

                  The Bru people who fled Mizoram in the wake of the incident were given refugees status in Tripura. They were given shelter in six refugee camps and were also given free rations and other essential commodities at the rate fixed by the Union Government.

                  The Bru National Liberation Front(BNLF) who took arms against the state of Mizoram since 1996 laid down their arms to the Govt. of Mizoram on 25th July, 2005 after 13(thirteen) rounds of Peace Talks between Govt. of Mizoram and BNLF. As many as 195 BNLF surrendered. The other Bru militant outfit called Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram(BLFM) formed in the year 2000 after they break away from parent outfit BNLF, took arms against Mizoram till they surrendered to the Govt. of Mizoram on 26th October, 2006. As many as 804 BLFM cadres surrendered and were given same package as a rehabilitation plan with their parent body BNLF.

 

 

II. special polling station at kanhmun and tuipuibari in MLA general election – 2003

                          Special Polling Stations was made at Kanhmun and Tuipuibari for the Bru refugees who were staying at refugee camp in Tripura.

                          Out of 361 voters from Kaikau, Hmasapara and Khakchangpara, 213 Bru refugee voters turned up for exercising their franchise at Kanhmun Special Poling Station. Out of 213, there were 176 eligible voters and those 176 Bru refugee voters from the said 3 refugee camp cast their votes at Kanhmun Special Polling Station. The Bru refugees left Kanhmun at 2:00 pm on that day after casting their votes.

                          About 100 members of MZP get together and shouted slogan against the Bru voters  but peaceful poll was ensured by the security personnel deployed there. Information received indicated that members of the agitating public may make an attempt to obstruct polling party of Kanhmun while coming back to Mamit somewhere near Zawlnuam. Therefore, the polling party were provided sufficient escort while proceeding to Mamit from Kanhmun.

 

                          Polling at Tuipuibari has also been ended at 5:00 pm. Naisingpara, Asapara and Hazachera camps cast their votes at Tuipuibari and altogether, 462 Bru refugee voters exercise their franchise and cast their votes. After the poll, the polling party of Tuipuibari proceeded to Mamit under proper escort. Polling ended peacefully and no report of incident has been received.

V. DEMAND OF NGOs

1. To delete those who does not want to be repatriated to Mizoram from the Electoral Roll.

2. Casting of vote by Brus at their respective Polling Booth in their respective villages.

3. No more repatriation for those who does not want to repatriate to Mizoram as the quadripartite agreement  signed by Govt. of India, Govt. of Mizoram, Govt. of Tripura and Mizoram Bru Displaced People Forum (MBDPF) on 3.7.2018 is already over on 30th September, 2018.

 

VI. NO. OF VOTERS OF BRU REFUGEE AT 3 DISTRICTS        

              Latest input revealed that there are about 11,232 Bru voters in Tripura out of which 8,777 are from Mamit District, 1,732 are from Kolasib District and 723 are from Lunglei District.

              There are around 15,000 Bru voters inside Mizoram in 4 (four) districts such as Kolasib, Mamit, Lunglei and Lawngtlai. In the same way, the rough population ratio of Brus in Mizoram and Tripura refugee camp is about 60:40 as per latest input received.
