****CTU, CHAMPHAI Vrs JOINT NGO, ZOKHAWTHAR****

The main reason behind the present confrontation between Champhai Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) and Zokhawthar Joint NGO involves two points :

1. SENIORITY: Goods coming from Myanmar are stock in a transit points in Zokhawthar Town before they are transported to various destination inside India. These Goods are carried inside Indian Territorry using all sorts of transport vehicles, which were supposes to be done on seniority basis, i.e., vehicles arriving first should be allotted first the goods to be carried. However, CTU alleged that the supervising authority â€“ Zokhawthar joint NGO practice nepotism as some of the owners of these vehicles belong to Zokhawthar town.

1. RATE OF COLLECTING DONATION: For transportation of these goods, Zokhawthar Joint NGO collected donation from each vehicles depending on the type of the commercial vehicles. These rates, according to CTU are far too high and must be reduced if not completely stopped.

****CHRONOLOGY OF EVENTS ON MISUNDERSTANDING BETWEEN****

****CTU, CHAMPHAI Vrs JOINT NGO, ZOKHAWTHAR****

**25.3.2019**

Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) started their blockade of vehicles transporting goods and commodities coming from Zokhawthar demanding Zokhawthar to have proper management on the seniority of vehicles carrying goods from Zokhawthar.

**27.3.2019**

Various transport vehicle associations under the banner of Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) continued their blockade of vehicles transporting goods &amp; commodities coming from Zokhawthar on _27.3.2019_. The blockade was joined by Champhai to Zokhawthar Maxi Cab Welfare from that day due to their discontentment on the service rate of their Maxi Cab and blocked the Maxi Cab service plying between Zokhawthar to Champhai. Information received indicated that the agitation is organised due to disagreement between CTU and Zokhawthar NGOs on the issue of supervision of seniority of vehicles which transport goods &amp; commodities from Zokhawthar. The supervision is being carried out by Zokhawthar NGO which various transport vehicle associations from Champhai alleged that there is some improper management on their seniority.

**28.3.2019**

Information received indicated that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) continued the blockade of all vehicles transporting Goods &amp; Commodities from Zokhawthar on _28.3.2019_ and around 30 (thirty) members participated at their duty point, i.e. Mualkawi. _In the meantime, Champhai to Zokhawthar Maxi Cab Welfare, which blocked Maxi Cab service from Zokhawthar to Champhai on 27.3.2019 demanding increase of Maxi Cab fare, resumed their service from today with the existing rate, i.e. Rs. 100/-._

**29.3.2019**

Information received indicated that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) continued the blockade of all vehicles transporting Goods &amp; Commodities from Zokhawthar on _29.3.2019_ and around 20 (twenty) members participated at their duty point, i.e. Mualkawi. Information received further indicated that 5 (five) Trucks carrying fertilizers and grocery items of Indian products plying towards Zokhawthar were stranded at Mualkawi. In a Press Release issued on 29.3.2019, CTU alleged Zokhawthar NGOs of collecting donation from their vehicles by their own fixed rates which they cannot accept and expressed their intention to continue their blockade until the impasse is settled.

**1.4.2019**

Information received indicated that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) continued the blockade of commercial vehicles transporting Goods &amp; Commodities from Zokhawthar till _1.4.2019_ and its members continued their duty day &amp; night at their duty point, i.e. Mualkawi. Information received further indicated that 5 (five) vehicles from Zokhawthar and 7 (seven) vehicles going towards Zokhawthar were stranded at Mualkawi.

**2.4.2019**

Information received indicated that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) continued their blockade of commercial vehicles transporting goods &amp; commodities plying between Zokhawthar and Champhai on 2_.4.2019_ and about 30 (thirty) members participated at their duty point, i.e. Mualkawi.

**3.4.2019**

Information has been received that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) continued blockade of commercial vehicles transporting goods &amp; commodities, plying between Zokhawthar and Champhai, on 3.4.2019 and about 20 (twenty) members participated at their duty point, i.e. Mualkawi. Information received indicated that 9 (nine) vehicles were stranded on the way.

 Information has been received that Village Councils and all NGOs of 3 (three) villages namely, Zokhawthar, New Hruaikawn and Melbuk, held a meeting on 1.4.2019 and issued a joint Press Release. In the Press Release, they criticised the CTU for organising the vehicle blockade and lauded Zokhawthar NGOs in managing commercial vehicles seniority at Zokhawthar. The Press Release further expressed their readiness for negotiation to settle the long pending impasse.

**4.4.2019**

Information has been received that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) continued blockade of commercial vehicles transporting goods &amp; commodities, plying between Zokhawthar and Champhai, till 4.4.2019 and about 15 (fifteen) members participated at their duty point, i.e. Mualkawi. Information received indicated that 15 (fifteen) vehicles were stranded.

 Information has been received that Vehicle Owners' Associations held a meeting on 4.4.2019 @ 10:50 a.m at Mualkawi and highlighted the PR issued by Zokhawthar Joint NGOs on 3.4.2019. The meeting expressed their willingness to settle the impasse and expected Sub-Hqrs. YMA, Champhai to mediate between the two parties to settle their issues.

**5.4.2019**

_Information has been received that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) called off their blockade of commercial vehicles transporting goods & commodities, plying between Zokhawthar and Champhai, from 5.4.2019._

**15.4.2019**

_Information received indicated that Champhai District Border Trade Transport Union (CTU) resumed their blockade of commercial vehicles plying between Zokhawthar and Champhai on 15.4.2019 at Mualkawi_ in which about 30 (thirty) members participated at their duty point, i.e. Mualkawi.

 In the meantime, an Emergency Meeting of Vanhnuailiana Group YMA was held on 15.4.2019 @ 11:00 a.m. at YMA Hall, Mualkawi which was presided over by Pu HC Lalthankhuma, President and attended by 33 (thirty three) members. The meeting resolved that:

i) To implore higher authority to settle the dispute between CTU and Zokhawthar.

ii) To exclude items of Indian products e.g. Rice, oil etc in the blockade.

iii) To consult VC, Mualkawi and leaders of Mualkawi NGOs in connection with  the road blockade.

iv) To take necessary initiative for the implementation of their request.

 _Earlier, the two organisations, i.e. CTU and Zokhawthar Welfare held a joint meeting on 8.4.2019 and 10.4.2019 under the guidance of MJA, Champhai but in vain._

1. Zokhawthar welfare alleged CTU of violating their agreement, but CTU refuted the allegation stating that they objected the point allowing only Zawkhawthar vehicles to carry goods &amp; commodities during the blockade called off period.

  The CTU further criticised Zokhawthar welfare of being absent from a joint meeting scheduled to be held on 13.4.2019 @ 10:00 am at MJA Conference Hall, Champhai.

2. The contention by Zokhawthar claiming that the agreement made under the guidance of Champhai MJA does not include the management of vehicles seniority by Zokhawthar, is false.

3. Road blockade will be resumed w.e.f 15.4.2019 (Monday) and even vehicles carrying vegetable items will be included.

4. CTU will not be responsible to any incident to the violators.
