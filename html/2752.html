BRU REFUGEES

 

2.7.2018         Bru Refugee Repatriation agreement was made by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and the Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) on 2.7.2018.  

 

12.7.2018       In a Press Note issued on 12.7.2018 leaders of NGO Coordination Committee (CYMA, MUP, MHIP, MZP and MSU) lauded Bru Refugee Repatriation agreement made by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and the Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) on 2.7.2018 and also appealed the Mizo public to support the agreement. Leaders of NGO Coordination Committee is optimistic that all the 1995 Electoral Roll registered Bru Refugees and their descendants lodged at various Transit Camps in Tripura will peacefully return to Mizoram and also lauded the decision to permanently close down Refugee Camps in Tripura. NGO Coordination Committee also stated that the Committee will oppose conduct of Elections or its related matters outside Mizoram.

 

13.7.2018      Information has been received that Rosnojoy (56) of Naisingpara Refugee Camp, P/A Saihatlangpui K, Mizoram staged a Hunger Strike w.ef. 2.7.2018 till today at Naisingpara Mandir in protest against the agreement made by the Joint Meeting of Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF). Rosnojoy demanded implementation of the following points :-

                        (i)    Central Security Forces must be deployed for providing security to repatriated Bru refugees.

                        (ii)  Demanded Bru refugees to be accommodated in Cluster Villages.

                        (iii) Rs. 4 Lakhs rehabilitation package to be deposited in Savings            Account of the beneficiaries.

                        (iv) Establishment of Schools in Bru villages.

                        (v)   Establishment of Hospitals at Bru villages.

 

                           Leaders of MBDPF reported that activists from Human Rights Commission, Vishwa Hindu Parishad and RSS are likely to visit Rosnojoy (56) at Naisingpara Refugee Camp in the near future. Leaders of MBDPF are also reportedly stating that the inter-village feud between Chuhvel and Zamuang could cause fear among the Bru refugees who are on the verge of returning to Mizoram.

 

16.7.2018      It has been reported that Young Bru Refugees, Naisingpara Branch organised a procession on 16.7.2018 @ 8:30 a.m. Around 3,000 (three thousand) Refugees of Naisingpara Refugee Camp participated in the demonstration. The demonstrators urged leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Forum (MBDPF) to withdraw from the ‘Agreement’ made with Central Government, Government of Mizoram and Government of Tripura until the following points were implemented :-

 

 

                        i)                        Central Security Forces must be deployed for providing security to                          Repatriated Bru refugees.

                        ii)           Demanded the Repatriated Brus to be accommodated in Cluster Villages.

                        iii)          Rs. 4 lakhs rehabilitation package to be deposited in Savings account of the
               beneficiaries.

                        iv)          Establishment of Schools in Bru villages.

                        v)           Establishment of Hospitals at Bru villages.

 

                                       It has also been reported that the demonstrators have restrained Sub Divisional Magistrate, Kanchanpur ; Deputy Collector, Kanchanpur and Sub Divisional Police Officer, Kanchanpur and leaders of MBDPF including A. Sawibunga, President; R. Laldawngliana, Vice President; Bruno Masha, General Secretary, etc. at MBDPF Office, Naisingpara. The demonstrators in the presence of Tripura Officials forced A. Sawibunga to sign a document containing the above highlighted demands and subsequent withdrawal from the ‘Agreement’ made with Central Government, Government of Mizoram and Government of Tripura with regard to Bru Repatriation process. It has also been reported that Rosnojoy (56) of Naisingpara Refugee Camp, P/A Saihatlangpui K, Mizoram called off his hunger strike on this day, i.e. 16.7.2018.

 

16.7.2018      Information has been received that leaders of Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples’ Forum (MBDPF), viz. A. Sawibunga, President; Bruno Msha, Gen. Secretary and R. Laldawngliana, Vice President met District Magistrate, Kanchanpur in his office chamber on 16.7.2018 at around 5:00 p.m with regard to their displeasure with the agitators of the Bru refugees at Naisingpara Refugee Camp. Leaders of MBDPF expressed their support of the scheduled repatriation process. It is reported that leaders of MBDPF are likely to resign from their leadership in protest against the agitators forcing Pu A. Sawibunga to withdraw from the four corner ‘Agreement’ signed by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and MBDPF. It is also reported that MBDPF will not take any further initiative regarding the scheduled repatriation process.

20.7.2018      Information has been received that Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples Forum (MBDPF) held public meetings simultaneously at 3 (three) Bru refugee camps today, i.e. 20.7.2018 @ 8:00 a.m as follows :-

                           i) Khakchangpara Camp     -           A. Sawibunga, President, MBDPF

                           ii) Kaiskau Camp                 -           R. Laldawngliana, V. President, MBDPF

                           iii) Hamsapara Camp           -           Bruno Msha, Gen. Secretary, MBDPF

 

                                       It is reported that majority of the Bru refugees of the aforesaid 3 (three) camps supported the demands made by the Bru All NGOs and were not in favour of the four-corner agreement signed by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and MBDPF. At the same time, it is reported that some of the refugees favoured the four-corner agreement and as such, no concrete conclusion could be made at present.

 

24.7.2018      Information has been received that the Bru NGO’s Joint Action Committee comprising of :-

                           i)         Bru Welfare Union, India               ii)        Bru Christian Fellowship

                           iii)       Bru Students’ Association              iv)       Bru Women Federation

                           v)        Bru Drivers’ Association                vi)       Bru Cultural Organisation

 

                        which was formed on 23.7.2018 under the auspices of MBDPF had voiced dissent on the recently signed Agreement for Bru Repatriation. The Bru NGO’s JAC are learnt to have had an interaction with the MBDPF leader, A. Sawibunga who visited Aizawl yesterday after withdrawing from the Agreement, and are intending to meet the Hon'ble Governor of Mizoram at an early convenient time. Information received suggests that the Bru NGO’s JAC may urge for a review of the Agreement as desired by the refugees.

 

                                       In the meantime, the Equal Package Demand Committee (EPDC) submitted a Memorandum to the Hon'ble Union Home Minister on 24.7.2018 by speed post demanding grant of packages to those already repatriated at par with the provisions of the Agreement. Their main demands are :-

                           i)         Free Ration for another one year in addition to one year ration already                                received.

                           ii)        Rs.70,000/- per familily in addition to Rs.80,000/- already received

                           iii)       Rs.5,000/- per family for a period of two years

                           iv)       Rs. 4 Lakhs per family

                           v)        Providing same assistance to self-repatriated families who have not yet                               received any assistance.

 

                                       The EPDC in its Memorandum also threatened to boycott the the forthcoming repatriation if their demands are not conceded before 14th August, 2018.

 

31.7.2018     Returnees of Bru National Liberation Front (BNLF) and Bru Liberation Front of Mizoram (BLFM) formed Mizoram Bru Returnees' Peace Accords Implementing Demand Committee (MBRPAIDC) on 31.7.2018. The objective of MBRPAIDC is to pursue for the successful implementation of the provisions of Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed between BNLF and Government of Mizoram on 26.4.2005. The following persons are elected members of MBRPAIDC :-

                          Chairman                   :           Pu Surjyamani Reang (Ex-BNLF Leader)

                          Vice Chairman          :           Pu Lalrinkima Molshoy (Ex-BLFM Leader)

                          Gen. Secretary          :           Pu Bruno Meska (Ex-BNLF Leader)

                          Asst. Secretary          :           Pu Chandramony Molshoy (Ex-BNLF Leader)

                          Treasurer                   :           Pu Indiarai Apeto (Ex-BLFM Leader)

                          Fin. Secretary            :           Pu Lalduhawma (Ex-BNLF Leader)

 

 

                          MBRPAIDC will also take initiatives on the following points :-

                       i)            Implementation of Social Development Project for Brus.

                       ii)           Grouping of Bru refugees in villages not less than 500 households.

                       iii)          One-time payment of cash assistance for Bru Refugees.

                       iv)          Similar cash assistance should be given to the Bru refugees who had                                                  already been rehabilitated in Mizoram.

                       v)           Families of Ex-BNLF and Ex-BLFM should also be given rehabilitation                                packages.

                       vi)          Rechristening of 'Reang' to 'Bru' as per MoU.

                       vii)         Reservation of jobs under the Central and State Government for the Brus.

6.8.2018         A meeting of NGOs and Religious leaders from 6 (six) Bru Refugee Camps was held on 28.7.2018 at Multipurpose Centre, Naisingpara under the chairmanship of Pu A. Ramliana. The meeting reviewed the Four Corner Agreement signed at New Delhi by the Central Government, Government of Mizoram, Government of Tripura and MBDPF. Consequent upon the meeting, NGOs and Religious leaders from 6 (six) Bru Refugee Camps formed the Mizoram Bru Displaced Peoples' Coordination Committee (MBDPCC) on 2.8.2018. MBDCC also strongly criticized the initiatives of leaders of the already repatriated Brus under the aegis of Equal Package Demand Committee (EPDC) in their demand for uniform financial repatriation packages. The following are the Office Bearers and Senior Advisers of MBDPCC :-

                           President                    :           Pu Laldingliana

                           Vice President          :           Pu M. Vanlalhriata

                           Gen. Secretary          :           Pu Rokhuma

                           Asst. Secretary          :           Pu Lalbiaka

                           Treasurer                   :           Pu T. Malsawma

                           Senior Adviser          :           i)         Pu A. Ramliana                    

                                                                           ii)        Pu Ronjit Reang

                                                                           iii)       Pu Ramprosat

                           MBDCC will take initiatives on the following points in addition to the agreement made under the Four Corner Agreement :-

                          i)           Formation of Area Development Council (ADC) for the Brus.

                        ii)           Allotment of 5 hectares of land to each repatriated Bru family.

                        iii)          Formation of Cluster/Model villages.

                        iv)          Upfront payment of Cash Assistance before Repatriation.

                        v)           Updating of Electoral Roll before Repatriation.

                        vi)          Conduct re-identification and inclusion of approximately 1,000 families left out for Repatriation.

                        vii)         Granting of General Amnesty.
