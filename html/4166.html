Security scenario of Mizoram in connection with 2024 Mizoram MP election :

 

Insurgency:

            After Mizoram gained statehood in 1987, it had experienced low level insurgencies in the North Eastern regions . Government of Mizoram and HPC(D) (the insurgent group from the northern parts) signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) on 2nd April 2018 which end the over two-decade armed insurgency of the HPC(D). However, a faction of HPC(D) led by Lalhmingthanga Sanate along with a handful of his followers, who refused to come over ground during the Peace Accord signed by HPC(D) and government of Mizoram in the year 2018 have started resurfacing and reorganise themselves in recent years.

            Prior to the Mizoram Members of Legislative Assembly election 2023, there were certain cases where Village Council Presidents and political party leaders of the north eastern parts of the state received telephonic calls from unknown persons who claimed themselves to be members of HPC(D). The callers in some cases threatened the call receivers to persuade the villagers not to cast their votes for some particular political parties while demanding money from others.

            To avoid the repetition of such unwanted incidents and to have a free & fair  2024 Lok Sabha election in the state, especially in the north eastern regions, adequate deployment of CAPF and state police force may be require to instil confidence in the heart of the local population.

 

Refugees :

            Due to the political turmoil in the neighbouring state and countries of Mizoram (Manipur, Bangladesh and Myanmar), a considerable numbers of IDPs and refugees, particularly Zo-ethnic tribes have been entering Mizoram in the last two/three years, seeking asylum. One consequence of these refugees entering Mizoram is the possibilities of these refugees/foreigners obtaining fake Indian documents such as voters ID cards, Aadhar cards etc. through illegal means as they are, as mentioned before, Zo-ethnic tribes, speaking the same dialect and following the same traditions/customs.

            Regarding fake Indian IDs or real Indian IDs, procured through illegal means, CID (SB) has been playing a pro active role. So far, two enquiries had been conducted by CID (SB) in alleged procurement of illegal Indian IDs.

            One of the enquiry revealed that one Myanmar National had acquired Indian Voters ID (EPIC Card) which was eventually surrendered by the defaulter after being exposed.

            The other enquiry revealed that PAN Cards and Aadhar Cards were procured through illegal means in Aizawl and other places. In this connection, a case vide Kawnpui PS Case Number 13/23 dated 15.06.2023 under section 468/180 (B) IPC r/w 14 Foreigners Act was registered which was later re-registered by Crime and Economic Offences Wing Police station as C&EO PS Case Number 14/23 dated 30.08.2023 u/s 468/120 (b) IPC r/w 14 Foreigners Act.  Investigation of the case had  led to the arrest of 6 (six) accused persons.

            All possible efforts have to be taken to prevent the issuance of illegal Indian documents to these refugees by the state government in collaboration with the Local Councils/Village Councils  and the local community leaders. This issue has to be taken seriously not only for the 2024 Parliamentary election, but for any future elections to be faced by the state

 

Other influencers:

            Representatives of different Church denominations and prominent NGOs of the Mizoram held a deliberation on 5th July, 2005 wherein they decided on the need to improve the state of politics in Mizoram and adopted a proposal for the same. After each church denomination and each group held a discussion on the proposal within their own respective Church or group, on September 1st, 2005, their respective representatives came together and formally accepted the formation of the Mizoram People Forum(MPF) so that a greater initiative can be taken towards the improvement of politics within the state. After careful consideration deliberation and assessment of the objectives, it was deemed satisfactory and so the Mizoram People Forum (MPF) was formally formed on 21st June, 2006.

            Ever since its establishment, MPF has taken great efforts for holding clean, free and fair election in various elections within Mizoram. For this, an agreement, commonly known as “Articles of Agreement” was signed between MPF and all the political parties of Mizoram. The joint efforts of these political parties and the Mizoram People Forum in implementing this agreement have been a great success in the past elections.

            The MPF is an organisation in Mizoram which all political parties/candidates dare not cross openly. As such, although it is a non government organisation, proper utilisation of its man-powers/ members can greatly reduce the burden of shortages of police force in the state after election has been declared as most of the MPF members are prominent and locally respected persons in their respective community.
