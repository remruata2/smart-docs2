HMAR PEOPLES CONVENTION (DEMOCRATIC) [HPC(D)]

 

1.                     Background :

                        The Hmars, an ethnic group of Mizoram mostly settled in the North Eastern part of the State have been aspiring for a separate administrative set up mainly due to the instigation of the elite and educated members of the community who propounded that the Hmars are a separate ethnic community with distinct culture as compared to the Mizo speaking populace of Mizoram. However, it may be mentioned here that the Hmars were never regarded as a distinct ethnic community, historically and culturally. Their only distinctiveness is that they spoke the Hmar dialect which is however having very close affinity to Mizo - a dialect of the Lushei - which is now accepted as the official language of Mizoram.  The Hmars also spread-out as far as Haflong in North Cachar Hills District of Assam and the Churachandpur District of Manipur. It may be mentioned here that the settlement of the Hmars in the adjoining areas of Manipur and Assam among other ethnic groups with distinct cultures and customs gradually made them ethnically self-conscious and vulnerable. This sentiment of alienation gave birth to political aspirations among the elite section of the community culminating in the formation of political platform such as the Hmar National Union (HNU) etc. in 1957 with the proclaimed objective of preserving the democratic rights, culture and ethnic identity of the Hmars within their dominated areas of Manipur and Assam.

 

2.                     Demand for Autonomous District Council by HPC :

                        The Hmars being a dominant ethnic group of Mizoram have never felt the need nor aspired for any special political status for their community in Mizoram. They were a secured and contented community. However, mainly due to the influence of the Hmars living outside the State, politically ambitious section of the community started propounding the need for preserving their democratic rights, culture and ethnic identity. In 1986, the Hmar Peoples Convention (HPC) was formed with the sole objective of the creation of an Autonomous District Council (ADC) for the Hmars within Mizoram. Simultaneously, extreme elements of the HPC cadres took up arms and resorted to violent and unlawful activities like abduction, extortion of money, attack on Mizoram Police personnel etc. and thus jeopardizing the law and order situation of the State particularly in the Hmar dominated area.

 

 

3.                     Signing of Peace Accord :

                        The Government of Mizoram eventually managed to contain the activities of the HPC militants through the courageous and dedicated efforts of Mizoram Police which resulted into the signing of a Peace Accord between the Government of Mizoram and HPC on 27th July 1994. The HPC militants surrendered formally to the Govt. of Mizoram as per the provision of this Accord and these persons were given financial grants for their rehabilitation etc. The Govt. of Mizoram also agreed to set up the Sinlung Hills Development Council (SHDC) for the socio-economic upliftment of the people living in the Hmar dominated areas. Accordingly, Adhoc Chairman and members constituting the council were even appointed by the Govt. of Mizoram from among the leaders of the erstwhile HPC set up. Peace was thus restored in Mizoram after the signing of the Accord.

 

 

4.                     Formation of HPC-'D' :

                        However, in spite of signing of the Peace Accord,  there were still a disgruntled section of the HPC set up, led by Lalhmingthanga Sanate, the then General Secretary of the HPC who did not accept the Peace Accord. Lalhmingthanga Sanate formed a militant outfit called the Hmar Peoples Convention -Democratic (HPC-D) in 1995 with its nerve centre at Hmarkhawlian (Lakhipur) in Cachar District of Assam. The HPC(D) was formed with the political objective of renewing demand for ADC under the active patronage of NSCN(I-M). In 1997, about 15-20 cadres under the command of S/S Commander-in-Chief Ropuia were sent to NSCN(IM) base camp in Bangladesh for training. They also received arms assistance from them.

                        In the meantime, a new underground outfit called the Hmar Revolutionary Force (HRF) was formed in 1996 with LZ Hrangchal, former S/S Chief Marshal of HPC as its leader. It may be noted that the HPC did not surrender all their arms to the Government of Mizoram in 1994. They have secretly kept about 14 (fourteen) numbers of sophisticated weapons un-surrendered which they claimed were borrowed from the NSCN(I/M), and that the weapons were already returned to the NSCN(IM). The facts remained, however, that these weapons were meant for future use in the pretext of safeguarding the Hmar tribes. They were also in friendly terms with the PREPAK and UNLF. The group however, surrendered before Govt. of Mizoram in 1999 after experiencing armed conflict with rival HPC (D).

 

5.                     Objective :

                        The purported objective of the HPC (D) over the years has changed from an autonomous district council covering the north and north eastern Mizoram to an independent Hmar State consisting of the Hmar inhabited areas of Mizoram, Manipur and Assam.

 

 

6.                     Areas of Operation:

                        The HPC (D) are active primarily in the north eastern areas of (i.e. some portions of Kolasib District and Aizawl District which are dominated by the Hmars) Mizoram, the Hmar dominated areas of Churachandpur District in Manipur and the Cachar and North Cachar Hill Districts of Assam.

            The 4th round of Peace Dialogue was held on 28th April, 2017, and the 5th Round of Peace Dialogue was held on 21st & 22nd September, 2017 where in the framework of agreement was worked out. The Peace Dialogue was concluded under conducive atmosphere. As scheduled, the Political Level Peace Talk between Mizoram State Government and HPC-D was held under a cordial environment at the State Guest House, Aizawl during 7th & 8th March, 2018 and the Peace Talk ended with a logical conclusion on 8.3.2018. The State Government and the HPC-D were represented by 10 (ten) members each. While the delegates of Mizoram Government were led by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Political Adviser to Chief Minister; Pu L.T. Hmar, Working Chairman, HPC-D led the HPC-D delegates during the two-day Peace Dialogue.

 

            On 2.4.2018 @ 4:30 p.m, Government of Mizoram and HPC-D signed a Memorandum of Settlement (MoS) at the function held at the State Guest House, Aizawl. Shri. Arvind Ray, Chief Secretary of Mizoram and Pu H. Zosangbera, Chairman, HPC-D signed the MoS on behalf of the State Government and HPC-D respectively. Pu Lalbiakzama, Addl. Secretary, Home gave reports on the peace process and speeches were delivered by Pu Lalrinmawia Ralte, MLA and Pu Lalthalien, Spokesperson, HPC-D during the function. The signing of MoS will bring to an end, the over two-decades armed conflict of the HPC-D.
